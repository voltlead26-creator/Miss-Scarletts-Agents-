# Cohorts and Stickiness

Cohorts help you study groups of users with shared characteristics or behavior.

## Core facts

- Cohorts can be based on actions, properties, or timing.
- Stickiness helps describe how often users return or interact.
- Cohorts are useful for comparing users who share a lifecycle moment.

## Practical guidance

- Use cohorts to understand repeat behavior and retention.
- Group users by acquisition, activation, or churn-related characteristics when relevant.
- Compare like with like.

## Common mistakes

- Comparing cohorts that are not meaningfully similar.
- Using a cohort definition that changes too often.
- Reading stickiness without understanding what behavior it measures.
