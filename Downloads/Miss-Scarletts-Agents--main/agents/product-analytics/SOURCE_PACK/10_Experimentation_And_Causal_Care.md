# Experimentation and Causal Care

Analytics often informs decisions, but metrics are not automatically proof of causation.

## Core facts

- A metric can correlate with value without proving it causes value.
- Experiments and group comparisons need careful interpretation.
- Analytics should support decisions, not overclaim them.

## Practical guidance

- Be explicit about whether you are observing, comparing, or testing.
- Use experiment results and product metrics together.
- Check that the metric is actually a proxy for user value.

## Common mistakes

- Using analytics to justify a decision without checking the proxy.
- Confusing correlation with causation.
- Over-reading a single chart.
