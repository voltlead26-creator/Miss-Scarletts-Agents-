# Funnels

Funnels show where users enter, move through, and drop out of a flow.

## Core facts

- Funnels visualize flow completion and friction.
- They are useful when there is a known sequence of steps.
- A funnel is strongest when each step is clearly defined.

## Practical guidance

- Use funnels to find where users stop progressing.
- Keep step definitions consistent.
- Compare funnel performance across releases or cohorts when appropriate.

## Common mistakes

- Building a funnel without a clear step sequence.
- Changing step definitions midstream.
- Treating completion as the only useful signal when the drop-off is the real issue.
