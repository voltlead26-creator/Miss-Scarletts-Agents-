# Dashboards and Reporting

Dashboards should answer recurring questions quickly and consistently.

## Core facts

- PostHog insights include trends, funnels, retention, user paths, stickiness, lifecycle, and SQL.
- Dashboards should not mix unrelated questions without structure.
- Reports are most useful when the metric definitions are stable.

## Practical guidance

- Organize dashboards by question type.
- Keep traffic, engagement, and conversion sections separate.
- Use dashboards for monitoring and reports for deeper investigation.

## Common mistakes

- Building dashboards that answer everything and nothing.
- Recreating reports because definitions drifted.
- Treating a dashboard as a substitute for analysis.
