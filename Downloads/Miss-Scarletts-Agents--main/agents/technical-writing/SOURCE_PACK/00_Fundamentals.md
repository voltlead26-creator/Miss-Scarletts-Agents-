# Technical Writing Fundamentals

Technical writing is about helping readers complete a task with confidence and without unnecessary ambiguity.

- Good technical writing is clear, concise, and audience-aware.
- Documentation needs structure that helps scanning and task completion.
- Error messages should help the reader recover, not just report failure.
- Accessible writing helps readers with disabilities and improves comprehension for everyone.
- The reader's task should drive the document structure.

## Core facts to keep in mind

- Clarity comes from audience awareness, explicit terminology, and consistent naming.
- Active voice usually makes instructions easier to follow than passive voice.
- Headings, lists, and short sections help readers find what they need quickly.

## Practical implications

- Write for the person who is stuck.
- Keep the next action obvious.
- Use complete, non-placeholder outputs.
- Prefer short sentences when precision matters.
