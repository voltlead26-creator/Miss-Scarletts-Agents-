# Motion, Responsive, and Device Accessibility

Accessibility is affected by animation, timing, layout, and device differences.

## Core facts

- Motion can distract or harm users who are sensitive to it.
- Content should remain usable at different viewport sizes and zoom levels.
- Touch and pointer targets need enough size and spacing to be used comfortably.

## Practical guidance

- Avoid motion that is decorative but necessary for understanding.
- Keep layouts stable when content changes.
- Make sure controls remain usable at small widths and under zoom.
- Do not depend on hover alone for essential information.

## Common mistakes

- Animating large interface regions without a clear reason.
- Hiding crucial actions behind hover-only interactions.
- Letting responsive layouts collapse controls into unusable clusters.
