## Overview
Designs a full-funnel B2B paid media strategy — from channel mix and budget allocation to campaign architecture, audience segmentation, and bid strategy — engineered to generate qualified pipeline rather than vanity metrics. Use this when launching a new paid program, restructuring underperforming accounts, or aligning paid media spend directly to pipeline and revenue targets.

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Create custom properties: `Paid Source`, `Paid Campaign`, `Paid Ad Group` mapped from UTM parameters
- Build contact lists by paid source for reporting: "LinkedIn Ads MQLs Q2", "Google Search MQLs Q2"
- Use HubSpot's Ad Attribution reporting to track LinkedIn and Google spend directly against pipeline in Deals

**Salesforce:**
- Use Campaign Hierarchy: Paid Channel → Paid Campaign → Ad Group with budget and ROI fields
- Enable Campaign Influence (Customizable Campaign Influence) to track multi-touch paid attribution on Opportunities
- Build Salesforce reports: "Cost per Opportunity by Paid Source" using Campaign Cost + Opportunity count

**LinkedIn Campaign Manager:**
- Connect LinkedIn Insight Tag to your website for retargeting and conversion tracking
- Set up LinkedIn Lead Gen Forms as a parallel conversion path (pre-filled forms = 2-3× higher CVR)
- Use LinkedIn's Revenue Attribution Report (RAR) if Salesforce-integrated — tracks pipeline from LinkedIn touchpoints

**Google Ads:**
- Import Salesforce/HubSpot opportunity created events as Google Ads conversions (not just form fills)
- Use Customer Match to upload CRM suppression lists (current customers, lost deals) as negative audiences
- Set up automated rules to pause keywords with CPL > 2× target after 50+ clicks

**Zapier / Make (Automation):**
- Trigger: New MQL created in CRM with paid source → Action: Slack alert to SDR with lead details + paid context
- Trigger: Opportunity created from paid source → Action: Update Google Ads/LinkedIn offline conversion (closes attribution loop)
- Trigger: Deal closed-won from paid source → Action: Log revenue in campaign cost spreadsheet for ROAS calculation

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High CPL but good CTR — ads are clicking but not converting**
Fix: The issue is landing page, not the ad. Audit the LP for ICP/message match — does the page speak to the same pain the ad promised? Test removing form fields (reduce to 3-4 fields max for TOFU offers). Add social proof above the fold (customer logos, G2 badge, specific outcome stat). Run heatmaps (Hotjar/Microsoft Clarity) to identify where visitors drop off.

**Problem: LinkedIn costs are 3-4× higher than benchmarks with no pipeline to show**
Fix: LinkedIn CPLs vary dramatically by audience size and ICP specificity. If audience is under 50,000 members, costs spike — broaden targeting or layer job seniority instead of specific titles. Also check: Are you sending LinkedIn traffic to a generic homepage? LinkedIn audiences need persona-specific landing pages. Finally, switch from Sponsored Content to Lead Gen Forms — pre-filled forms typically cut CPL by 40-60% for LinkedIn campaigns.

**Problem: Paid MQLs are converting at lower rates than organic — sales team says lead quality is poor**
Fix: This is an audience or offer misalignment problem. Audit which specific campaigns, ad sets, and audiences are producing low-quality MQLs. Common culprits: TOFU guide offers attracting non-buyers (add qualifying questions to form), overly broad job title targeting (tighten to director+ seniority), or geographic expansion beyond core market. Implement a quality score for MQLs (use firmographic fit score) and report by paid source — don't let blended MQL metrics hide channel-specific quality issues.
