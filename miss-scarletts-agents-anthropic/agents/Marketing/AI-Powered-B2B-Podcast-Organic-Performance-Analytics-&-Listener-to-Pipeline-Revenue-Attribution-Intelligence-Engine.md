This engine builds a complete, AI-automatable analytics system that connects B2B organic podcast performance to pipeline influence and closed revenue — solving the hardest attribution problem in audio marketing: proving that a show your prospects listen to while driving to work is genuinely influencing enterprise deals that close 4–9 months later. ## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Vanity URLs show clicks in GA4 but no corresponding demo requests are attributed to podcast source — the attribution seems to disappear after the landing page visit.**
Solution: This is almost always a form source attribution failure. When a listener visits yourcompany.com/listen (which redirects with UTM parameters), the UTM data is captured in the GA4 session. However, if your demo request form is embedded on a different page or subdomain (e.g., app.yourcompany.com/demo or a third-party form tool like Typeform) without passing session source data through, the CRM contact gets created without podcast attribution. Fix this in two steps: (1) Ensure your GA4 → HubSpot integration is passing utm_source to the CRM contact's "Original Source" or a custom "GA4 Source" property at the moment of form submission — test by submitting a test form via a podcast vanity URL and checking the resulting HubSpot contact record; (2) If your form tool doesn't natively pass UTM data, use hidden form fields that capture the UTM parameters from the page URL using JavaScript — your developer can implement this in under 2 hours, or use HubSpot native forms which capture UTM data automatically when configured correctly.

**Problem: Sales team isn't consistently logging podcast references from discovery calls — the CRM activity type has almost no entries despite SDRs verbally reporting frequent podcast mentions.**
Solution: The root causes are nearly always behavioral, not technical: reps don't log because it adds 45 seconds to a workflow they're already trying to compress. Attack from three angles simultaneously: (1) Configure Gong or Chorus to automatically detect and flag calls where your podcast name or episode topics are mentioned — remove the need for manual logging for auto-detectable mentions; (2) Share a monthly "Podcast Influence Report" in your sales all-hands that shows the dollar value of deals where podcast influence was logged, with explicit acknowledgment of which SDRs and AEs generated the data — social recognition creates logging motivation more reliably than process mandates; (3) Reduce logging friction: create a one-click CRM macro or HubSpot sequence enrollment trigger tied to a single checkbox field ("Prospect mentioned podcast on this call: Y/N") rather than requiring reps to navigate to a custom activity type — the 3-second checkbox version gets completed; the 90-second activity type version doesn't.

**Problem: Podcast subscriber email list is growing, but CRM matching shows less than 5% of captured emails match existing CRM contacts — the show seems to be reaching people outside our ICP.**
Solution: A low CRM match rate reveals one of two problems that require different fixes. First, diagnose which: export your podcast subscriber list → enrich with Apollo or ZoomInfo → check the job titles and company sizes. If enrichment shows your audience is mostly practitioners outside your ICP (e.g., individual contributors when you sell to VPs, or SMBs when you sell to enterprise), your guest selection and topic strategy is attracting the wrong audience. Fix by auditing your top 20 most-downloaded episodes against your ICP criteria — is your content accidentally speaking to an audience 2–3 levels below your buyer? Adjust upcoming episode themes to focus on challenges and vocabulary your economic buyer uses, not the end-user. If enrichment shows your audience IS your ICP but they're not in your CRM, your sales and marketing team hasn't reached them yet — this is actually a positive signal. Flag these enriched ICP listeners as a priority prospecting segment for your SDR team, with the outreach hook: "I noticed you subscribe to [Podcast Name] — I wanted to personally reach out because [episode topic] directly relates to what we help [their job title]s at [company type] accomplish."
