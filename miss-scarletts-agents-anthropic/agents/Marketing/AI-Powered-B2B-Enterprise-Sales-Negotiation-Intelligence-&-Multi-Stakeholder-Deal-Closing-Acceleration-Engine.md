## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The champion says they support the deal but keeps "not having time" to present the business case to the economic buyer.
**Solution:** Champion engagement fatigue is a leading indicator of low political capital or waning confidence. Don't push harder — lower the bar. Offer to co-present with them, convert the business case into a 3-slide deck they can forward, or propose an async video recorded by your CEO/CSO. If none of those work, this is a championing problem, not a scheduling problem — identify a secondary contact with more urgency.

**Problem:** Procurement demands a price reduction that would make the deal uneconomic.
**Solution:** Never negotiate price without getting something of equal or greater strategic value in return. Use the Give-Get Matrix: offer a 10% discount in exchange for a 3-year commitment and a reference customer agreement. If they require more than 15% off list, escalate to your executive and reframe the conversation around strategic partnership value — request an executive-level conversation to discuss "the right commercial structure for a long-term relationship." This reframes discount-seeking into partnership negotiation.

**Problem:** IT security review has been in queue for 6+ weeks with no movement.
**Solution:** This is an escalation opportunity, not a waiting game. Have your champion identify who owns the IT security review queue and whether there's a way to get a "conditional approval" that allows contract execution while security review completes in parallel. If your product has SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, or similar certifications, send a pre-completed security questionnaire with a summary page that security can approve in 30 minutes. Offer a direct call between your CISO/VP Security and their IT security lead to address top concerns in real time.
