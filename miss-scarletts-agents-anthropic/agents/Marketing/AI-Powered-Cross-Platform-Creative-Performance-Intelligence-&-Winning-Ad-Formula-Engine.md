### 3. Creative Fatigue Detection Protocol
Flag any creative exhibiting two or more of:
- CTR decay >25% week-over-week for 2+ consecutive weeks

- CPA inflation >40% vs. launch week baseline

- Hook rate declining >15% week-over-week for video creatives

- **Tier 3 — Retire**: Bottom 20% or any fatigued creative. Create retirement timeline to protect budget

## Integration Tips

- **Creative Intelligence Database (Notion/Airtable)**: Build a "Creative DNA" table where every new ad gets tagged with its hook archetype, emotional trigger, visual strategy, and platform before launch. After 30 days, log its CPA and tier classification. Within two quarters you'll have a proprietary creative formula library.

After 90 days, segment by creative source to identify which creative formulas produce the highest LTV customers — not just the lowest CPA leads. This surfaces creative formulas worth paying a premium CPA for.
- **Supermetrics / Funnel.io / Triple Whale**: Automate a weekly cross-platform data pull into a Google Sheet with standardized columns. Paste this into the prompt monthly on the first of each month to continuously update your creative intelligence. Build a 6-month rolling dataset to spot seasonal creative patterns.
- **Zapier / Make Workflow**: Connect platform APIs → Google Sheets → trigger Claude API with this prompt's advanced version → auto-format output into a Notion creative brief template → post to your #paid-media Slack channel on the 1st of each month. Full automation = zero manual effort for monthly creative intelligence.
- **Slack Fatigue Alerts**: Use Supermetrics + Zapier to trigger a Slack alert whenever any active creative's week-over-week CTR drops >20% or frequency exceeds 3.5. Alert fires to #creative-ops with the ad name, platform, current CPA, and a direct link to the creative in the platform.
- **Motion / Northbeam / Creative OS**: If you use a dedicated creative analytics platform, export their "creative pillars" and "concept performance" reports and include them as additional context when running this prompt. Cross-validated insights from two analytical approaches increase recommendation confidence.
- **AI Creative Production Pipeline**: Feed winning creative briefs directly into AI production: image brief → Midjourney or Adobe Firefly, video concept → Runway Gen-3 or Pika, voiceover → ElevenLabs with a brand-matched voice clone, copy variants → Claude API with your brand voice system prompt for batch generation of 20+ ad copy variants per concept.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: The analysis produces generic insights like "use stronger CTAs" that don't reflect my actual creative data.
**Solution**: Your creative descriptions are too vague. "Product benefit video" tells the AI nothing. Describe each creative specifically: "30-second talking head video. Hook verbatim: 'I wasted $200K on SDR salaries before I found this.' iPhone-style raw footage, no editing. CTA screen at end: 'Book a free audit.'" The more specific your creative description, the more precise the pattern recognition. Spend 2–3 minutes per ad writing detailed descriptions before running the prompt.

**Problem**: Winning creative briefs from this analysis don't improve performance when produced and launched.
**Solution**: Your top performers may have external performance drivers beyond creative quality — promotional period, seasonal demand spikes, or audience novelty from being new to market. Re-run the analysis comparing the same creative across different 30-day windows to isolate true creative signal vs. external demand factors. Also verify new creatives are being tested against the same audience segments as your historical winners, not broader audiences where the baseline conversion rate is inherently lower.

**Problem**: I can't consolidate cross-platform data into a single structured format for the prompt.
**Solution**: Use Supermetrics, Funnel.io, or Triple Whale to normalize cross-platform data into a single Google Sheet. If you don't have these tools, manually export each platform's ad-level report, add a "Platform" column, and combine into one sheet. At minimum you need: platform, ad name, format, impressions, CTR, CPA, conversions, days active, and a 2–3 sentence creative description for each ad. The manual creative description is the most important column — it's what enables pattern recognition.
