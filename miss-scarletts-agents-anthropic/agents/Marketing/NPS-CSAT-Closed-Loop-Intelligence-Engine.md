## Overview
Transform raw NPS and CSAT survey responses into automated intervention playbooks, product insight reports, and advocacy pipelines. Use this when your company collects customer feedback but struggles to act on it systematically — turning detractors into saves, passives into engaged accounts, and promoters into case studies and referrals before sentiment shifts.

## Integration Tips

- **Gainsight:** Use Timeline entries to log every closed-loop action; set C360 NPS field to auto-update from Delighted webhook; build a Cockpit playbook that fires on detractor score with renewal date <90 days
- **Salesforce:** Map NPS score to Contact and Account objects; create a "Feedback Risk" field on Opportunity that flags active detractors with open renewals; use Flow to auto-create a follow-up Task assigned to the Account Owner
- **HubSpot:** Use custom properties for NPS score, survey date, and follow-up status; build a workflow that enrolls detractors in a CSM notification sequence and promoters in an advocacy sequence
- **Delighted / Medallia:** Use webhook integrations to push responses in real-time to Slack and CRM; use response tags in Delighted to implement the taxonomy before automating to Jira/Salesforce
- **Jira:** Create a dedicated NPS Feedback project with issue types matching your taxonomy; use Zapier to auto-create tickets when AI classifier tags a response as "Product Bug" or "Feature Request" with severity 3+
- **Slack:** Create dedicated channels: #nps-alerts (real-time detractor pings), #voice-of-customer (weekly digest), #competitive-intel (any competitor mentions); use Zapier or native integrations to route by tag
- **Zapier / Make.com:** Core automation: Delighted new response → filter by score → branch logic → Salesforce task + Slack message + email draft in Gmail/Outlook; build separate zaps for competitive mention routing

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Response rate is below 15% and declining.**
Fix: Audit your survey frequency — quarterly relationship NPS plus in-app CSAT is already two touchpoints; adding more causes survey fatigue. Reduce survey length to 2 questions maximum (score + one open-text). Test sending from the CSM's personal email address rather than a no-reply domain. Add a pre-survey email from the CEO ("We want to hear from you") 48 hours before the survey link goes out. Target only economic buyers and power users — not all contacts on an account.

**Issue: Detractors receive the acknowledgment email but won't book a discovery call.**
Fix: Remove the calendar link from the first email — it signals "sales call" to detractors who are already frustrated. Instead, ask one simple question in the email body: "What's the one thing that's most frustrating right now?" Once they reply, you've opened a text conversation that's easier to transition to a call. For high-ARR accounts, have the VP of CS call directly without sending an email first — the call feels more sincere than a templated email sequence.

**Issue: Open-text responses are too vague to classify accurately ("It's fine" / "Good product").**
Fix: Add a follow-up question that appears only after they submit their score: "What's the main reason for your score?" with 6 radio button options matching your taxonomy categories. This structured branching captures specific categories even when customers won't write long responses. For promoters who give vague positive feedback, follow up with: "Which specific feature has saved your team the most time?" — this surfaces specific testimonial material even from low-effort responders.
