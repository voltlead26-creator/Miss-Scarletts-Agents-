## Troubleshooting
- **Problem:** Prospects get defensive when asked about budget authority or who the economic buyer is.
  **Solution:** Never ask "What's your budget?" or "Are you the decision-maker?" — both signal transactional intent. Use indirect access questions instead: "Who else in your organization has visibility into this initiative?" or "When your team has made similar technology investments, who typically sponsors that conversation?" Frame every question as helping the prospect build their internal business case, not as vendor qualification.

- **Problem:** AEs complete the scorecard but scores are artificially inflated — team average is consistently 26+ yet win rates are not improving.
  **Solution:** Institute a calibration rule: "If you cannot quote the prospect's exact words for a MEDDIC element, the score for that element is 1." Have managers independently score two recorded discovery calls per rep per month. When manager score and rep score diverge by more than 5 points, address in 1:1 coaching. Run monthly pipeline reviews where all deals scored 25+ are stress-tested out loud.

- **Problem:** Discovery calls run long because reps work through MEDDIC questions linearly and it feels like an interrogation.
  **Solution:** Teach hypothesis-driven discovery. Enter every call with a pre-built pain hypothesis based on account research and confirm or refute it with 2–3 targeted questions rather than starting from zero. Prioritize Metrics and Identify Pain in the first 20 minutes — Economic Buyer and Decision Process can be filled in via champion calls, email follow-up, or pre-demo prep without burning discovery call time.
