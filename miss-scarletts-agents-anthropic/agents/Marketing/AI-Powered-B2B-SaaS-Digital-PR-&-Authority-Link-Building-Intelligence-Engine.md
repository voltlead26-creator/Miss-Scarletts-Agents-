## Overview

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Outreach emails are being sent but response rate is under 5%**
Solution: The pitch is likely too long, too promotional, or targeting the wrong person. Rewrite to under 75 words, lead with what's in it for the recipient (their readers, their content, their credibility), and verify you're emailing an editor who actually manages the page you're targeting. Test a more direct subject line (e.g., "Quick question about your resources page" vs. "Guest post pitch").

**Problem: Digital PR campaign launched but earned fewer than 5 backlinks in 30 days**
Solution: The story angle likely lacks a sharp news hook or the data isn't surprising enough. Reanalyze: Is there a counterintuitive finding you can lead with? Can you localize the data (regional cut, company size cut)? Did you pitch the right reporters — those who've covered similar data stories before? Consider repackaging for LinkedIn as a thought leadership post to drive secondary coverage from industry newsletters.

**Problem: HARO response rate is high but backlinks aren't materializing**
Solution: Journalists are using your quotes without linking. Two fixes: (1) In your response, include your website URL naturally ("…as we detailed in our recent research at fenixrevops.com") to make it easy to attribute; (2) After the article publishes, email the journalist politely noting the quote was used and asking if they can add a source link. Most will comply — it takes 30 seconds on their end and you have a 40-60% success rate on follow-up requests.
