## Overview
Applies behavioral psychology frameworks — Cialdini's influence principles, Kahneman's dual-process theory, status quo bias, loss aversion, and committee consensus dynamics — to build B2B messaging architectures that systematically trigger purchase decisions. Use this when your pipeline stalls at specific stages, when technically superior products lose to inferior competitors, or when rational-sounding messaging fails to generate urgency.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Loss framing feels too negative and sales team pushes back**
Solution: Pair every loss-framed message with a risk-reversal mechanism (trial period, success guarantee, or rollback option). The frame is "You're already losing X, and here's how we make it safe to stop." Test loss-framed messages in email subject lines first where they're easiest to A/B test with low risk.

**Issue: Messaging feels manipulative or inauthentic**
Solution: Every psychological trigger in this framework must be grounded in genuine truth — the losses must be real and quantifiable, the scarcity must be legitimate, the social proof must be from actual customers. If a psychological lever feels forced, it means the underlying proof point doesn't exist yet. Build the proof point first, then apply the frame.

**Issue: One buyer type responded well but other committee members are resistant**
Solution: This is the committee consensus problem — you've activated one psychological profile but haven't addressed others. Run Deliverable 3 (Buying Committee Psychology Map) for every role, identify the blockers' specific fears, and create targeted content for each. Most deals that stall in committee have one unaddressed stakeholder whose psychological needs haven't been met.
