## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Equity partners ignore all marketing content and emails — only IT Director responds**
Solution: Stop targeting equity partners with marketing automation. Partners respond only to peer-to-peer introductions, practitioner-authored research they actually use, and event formats where a known peer facilitates the conversation. Shift 60% of partner-targeting budget to roundtable event program, LinkedIn Thought Leader Ads (amplifying a peer partner voice, not your brand), and mutual connection introduction requests via Sales Navigator. The IT Director is a champion, not a blocker — equip them with the committee presentation kit and champion enablement email templates to build internal consensus themselves.

**Problem: ROI model lands well with COO but equity partners say "interesting, but not urgent" in committee**
Solution: Your ROI model is calculating in the wrong currency. Equity partners are evaluated on partner drawings and book of business — not operational efficiency. Rebuild the ROI model to calculate: (hours recaptured per professional × effective billing rate × utilization rate × attorney headcount) = annual revenue impact on partner earnings. Then divide by total partners to show per-partner earnings improvement. A $2.4M firm-level efficiency gain sounds abstract; "$7,800 per equity partner in additional annual drawings" creates urgency. Layer in the competitive threat angle: "The three firms that lost partners to AI-native boutiques in the last 12 months all had the same operations profile as you."

**Problem: Deals stall after demo — no follow-up timeline from the firm's side**
Solution: Professional services firms stall because no one owns the timeline. At the end of every demo, establish a Mutual Action Plan with specific dates: "When does your next partner committee meet? Can we get on the agenda for [date]? What do you need from us to be ready?" Then build a Proof-of-Value offer with a defined 30-day pilot scope that creates forward momentum without requiring full committee approval. A pilot can often be approved by the COO alone — which builds internal evidence before the equity partner vote.
