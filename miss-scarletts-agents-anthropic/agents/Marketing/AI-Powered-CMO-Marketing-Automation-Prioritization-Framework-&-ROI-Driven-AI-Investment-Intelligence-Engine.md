# AI-Powered CMO Marketing Automation Prioritization Framework & ROI-Driven AI Investment Intelligence Engine

## Overview
Helps CMOs systematically audit every marketing workflow, score and rank automation opportunities by ROI potential and implementation effort, build a phased AI investment roadmap with concrete financial projections, and create a board-ready business case for marketing AI spend. Use it when you're building your annual marketing AI strategy, preparing a budget defense to the CFO, or need to answer "where should we start with AI automation?" with data, not instinct.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The ROI projections are too speculative and the CFO rejects the business case**
*Likely cause:* The savings estimates are based on assumed hours rather than measured time-tracking data. CFOs distrust "we estimate X hours/week" without evidence.
*Fix:* Before building the business case, run a 2-week time audit using Clockify or Toggl where every marketing team member logs time spent on manual tasks by category. Use real data to populate the model. This typically takes 10 days and produces numbers the CFO can't dispute because they're measured, not estimated. Alternatively, run one Quick Win first (e.g., the dashboard automation), measure actual time saved at 30 days, and use that proof point to anchor the full business case.

**Problem: Phase 1 deployments stall because IT won't prioritize the security reviews**
*Likely cause:* Phase 1 was designed assuming no IT dependency, but one or more tools triggered IT's standard security review process (common with any tool that connects to Salesforce or accesses customer data).
*Fix:* Re-audit your Phase 1 initiatives against your company's IT review trigger criteria BEFORE the roadmap is published. Any tool that: (1) accesses PII, (2) integrates with CRM/MAP, or (3) processes customer data likely requires IT review regardless of marketing's classification. Move those initiatives to Phase 2 and substitute truly IT-independent Quick Wins (e.g., internal-only tools, tools that only process public web data, or AI writing assistants that don't integrate with any system). Submit IT review requests for Phase 2 tools on Day 1 of Phase 1 — don't wait.

**Problem: Team adoption stalls after initial training — people fall back to manual habits**
*Likely cause:* The AI tools were trained as an option, not embedded as a requirement. When people are busy, they default to familiar workflows. Adoption collapses unless the AI workflow is the path of least resistance.
*Fix:* Rebuild the workflow so the manual version is harder than the AI version. Example: For content briefs, remove the old blank-template Google Doc from the shared drive and replace it with a link to the AI brief generator. For weekly reporting, cancel the manual dashboard build meeting and replace it with a "review the AI-generated draft" meeting. Change management insight: you don't change behavior by adding a better option — you change it by making the old option unavailable or inconvenient.
