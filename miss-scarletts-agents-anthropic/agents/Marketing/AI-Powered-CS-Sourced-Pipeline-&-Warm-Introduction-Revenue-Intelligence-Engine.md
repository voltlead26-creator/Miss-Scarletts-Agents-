### SECTION 1: REFERRAL READINESS SCORING MODEL (RRS)

**Referral Readiness Score (RRS) — 0 to 100**

Calculate weekly per account via CS platform formula. Review in CS Manager 1:1s monthly. Trigger referral ask workflow when RRS exceeds threshold.

| Signal | Weight | Scoring Logic |
|---|---|---|
| NPS Score | 30% | 9–10 = 100pts; 8 = 70pts; 7 = 40pts; 0–6 = 0pts (never ask detractors or passives) |
| Documented Outcome Achieved | 25% | Customer has quantified ROI documented in Success Plan = 100pts; Verbal outcome only = 60pts; No documented outcome = 0pts |
| Relationship Tenure & Engagement | 20% | 12+ months with consistent CSM engagement = 100pts; 6–12 months = 70pts; <6 months = 30pts; <3 months = 0pts |
| Champion Seniority & Network Breadth | 15% | Director+ with visible industry network (LinkedIn connections in ICP profile) = 100pts; Manager with limited network = 50pts; Individual contributor = 20pts |
| Recent Positive Sentiment Signal | 10% | Positive QBR/EBR outcome, unprompted praise in meeting notes, G2 review submitted, or case study participation in last 60 days = 100pts; No recent signal = 20pts |

**RRS Thresholds:**
- **RRS 80–100:** Priority referral candidate — trigger Peak Moment ask within 14 days of next positive interaction
- **RRS 65–79:** Warm candidate — embed referral ask in next QBR agenda or value check-in email
- **RRS 50–64:** Developing — cultivate 60–90 more days; focus on outcome documentation to raise RRS
- **RRS 0–49:** Not ready — do not ask; focus on value delivery and health improvement

**When NOT to Ask (Override Conditions — suppress referral workflow regardless of RRS):**
- Open support escalation or unresolved technical issue (any priority)
- Contract renewal negotiation in progress
- Champion departure detected in last 90 days
- NPS submitted in last 90 days was below 7
- AE is actively working an expansion deal — coordinate timing to not create noise

### SECTION 2: REFERRAL ASK PLAYBOOK — 3 APPROACHES

**Approach 1: The Peak Moment Ask**

*Trigger:* Within 72 hours of a documented positive outcome — ROI confirmed in a QBR, case study approved, NPS 9–10 submitted, major milestone hit, or champion receives internal recognition tied to your product.

*Why it works:* The endowment effect + peak-end rule. People are most willing to give when they feel they've just received. The moment a customer says "this saved us $180K" is when they're most likely to say "let me introduce you to my old colleague."

*Owner:* CSM (personal, not automated — this moment requires human authenticity)

*Channel:* Direct email (not Slack or LinkedIn — creates a record and signals appropriate gravity)

*Email template:*

Subject: [First Name] — thank you, and one small ask

Hi [First Name],

I just got off our QBR and I'm still thinking about what you shared — [specific outcome, verbatim from meeting, e.g., "cutting your month-end close from 11 days to 3 days is genuinely remarkable, especially in your first 8 months"].

I want to be direct: I'm so proud of what your team has built here. And selfishly — I'd love to help other finance leaders like you get to the same place.

Do you know 1–2 peers — VPs or CFOs at [target company type, e.g., manufacturing companies, similar size to yours] — who are still dealing with [primary pain point, e.g., the manual AP nightmare you solved]? If anyone comes to mind, I'm not asking for a sales pitch — just "I know someone doing interesting work, want me to connect you?" is all it takes.

If no one comes to mind, that's completely fine. But if someone does, your introduction would mean everything — and I'll make sure the conversation is worth their time.

[CSM Name]

*Expected conversion rate:* 32–45% (vs. 8–14% for generic referral program asks)
*Why it converts:* Specific, personal, low-pressure ask; framing around their peer's pain (not your quota); explicit permission to say no

**Approach 2: The QBR Embedded Ask**

*Trigger:* Quarterly or semi-annual business review with RRS 65–79 accounts.

*Why:* QBRs create a natural moment to review outcomes (building gratitude) and look ahead (building partnership mindset). The ask lands as a natural extension of the strategic conversation.

*Agenda placement:* Second-to-last agenda item, after ROI review and before "next steps."

*Talk track (CSM-delivered, in QBR):*

"Before we go to next steps — I want to ask you something that's a bit outside our normal agenda.

What you've built here in [X months] is exactly the kind of story that other [VP Finance / CFOs] in [industry] need to hear. We're finding that a lot of companies your size are still managing [pain point] the way you were at the start of last year.

Do you ever cross paths with peers in similar roles at other companies — whether that's through [industry association, peer CFO group, past company, LinkedIn, wherever]? If there's anyone you'd feel comfortable making a brief, informal introduction for, I'd love to follow up after today's call. No hard sell, no awkward sales process — I'd just love to have a 20-minute conversation with them."

*Follow-up email (same day as QBR):*

Subject: Great QBR today — and the referral ask I mentioned

Hi [First Name],

Thank you for the conversation today — the [specific outcome or metric discussed] numbers are genuinely impressive and your team should be proud.

On the referral question I raised: absolutely zero pressure. But if someone does come to mind over the next week or two — even a vague "I know someone who might benefit" — just reply to this email with their name and I'll handle everything else.

If it comes to nothing, no worries at all. I just wanted to make the ask once, explicitly, so the door is open.

[CSM Name]

**Approach 3: The Executive Sponsor Direct Ask**

*Trigger:* Accounts where:
- Executive sponsor (C-suite) is actively engaged with your company
- NRR from account is $100K+ ARR
- Executive sponsor has visible peer network (board memberships, industry associations, speaking engagements)
- RRS 80+

*Who makes the ask:* CS Manager, VP CS, or company CEO — not the frontline CSM. Executive-to-executive ask carries 3x the weight.

*Email template (CS Manager or VP CS to Executive Sponsor):*

Subject: [Executive First Name] — a personal ask from me

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to reach out directly — not as a formal business communication, but as a personal note.

What [customer company] has built with [your product] over the last [X] months has been remarkable. [Specific outcome, e.g., "The 73% reduction in AP processing time your team achieved is one of the strongest results we've seen in [industry] — and it's a direct result of how seriously your team took the implementation."]

I have a personal ask: I'd love your help introducing us to 1–2 other CFOs or finance executives in your peer network — people dealing with the same pressures you were facing [X months ago]. We're not looking for sales leads; I'm looking for conversations with thoughtful executives who could benefit from what you've built.

If you're open to it, I'd suggest we get on a brief call and I'll walk you through how we'd handle any introduction — so your name and relationship are always protected.

Thank you for everything you've built with us. I hope we've earned the right to ask.

[VP CS / CEO Name]

*Expected conversion rate on executive ask:* 55–70% when relationship is strong and outcome is documented

### SECTION 3: WARM INTRODUCTION WORKFLOW

**Step 1: Referral Agreement & Target Confirmation**

When a customer agrees to make an introduction, immediately:

1. **Reply within 4 hours** — thank them and confirm the process
2. **Ask for the target's name, company, and email** (if not already offered)
3. **Research the prospect** (AI prompt below) and send back a 3-bullet "intro briefing" to the customer — what to say when they make the intro, so the referrer looks knowledgeable and the intro is warm

*Briefing email to referrer (template):*

Subject: Thank you — here's exactly what to say

Hi [First Name],

Thank you! Here's a quick cheat sheet on [Prospect Name] so your intro lands perfectly:

**What to say when you reach out to them:**
"I've been working with [Your Company] for [X months] — they helped us [one-sentence outcome, e.g., 'cut our AP processing from 14 days to 2 days']. Given what I know about [Prospect Company's] operations, I thought it might be worth a conversation. Want me to make an intro?"

**What [Prospect Company] is likely dealing with:**
[AI-generated pain point hypothesis based on company profile — 1–2 sentences]

**What I'll say in the introduction email:**
I'll keep it brief, mention your name and outcome only (nothing commercial), and ask for 20 minutes. You'll be CC'd on my first email so you can see exactly what I send.

Does this feel right? Any adjustments before I reach out?

[CSM Name]

---

**Step 2: Double Opt-In Introduction Protocol**

Never cold-contact a referral without the prospect's consent. This protects the referrer's relationship and dramatically improves conversion.

*Sequence:*

**Phase A — Referrer initiates contact with prospect** (they forward the briefing or send their own message)

**Phase B — Prospect replies yes to referrer**

**Phase C — Referrer sends three-way email (template below)**

*Three-way introduction email (written by CSM, sent by referrer — or CSM sends with referrer's approval):*

Subject: [Prospect First Name] — connecting you with [CSM First Name]

Hi [Prospect First Name],

[Referrer Name] here. I've been meaning to do this for a few weeks.

I've been working with [Your Company] for [X months]. They helped us [specific outcome — 1 sentence, e.g., 'eliminate 120+ hours per month of manual invoice processing']. Given what I know about [Prospect Company], I thought the conversation might be valuable for you.

I'm looping in [CSM First Name] who runs our account. She'll take it from here — I just wanted to make the introduction personally so you know it's coming from a real experience, not a sales list.

[Referrer Name]

REFERRAL HANDOFF: [Prospect Name] at [Prospect Company]

Next step: AE follows up within 24 hours with a note that references the referrer relationship naturally — NOT a standard SDR template.

### SECTION 4: REFERRAL INCENTIVE PROGRAM DESIGN

**Incentive Structure Selection Matrix**

| Customer Segment | Recommended Incentive | Why It Works | What to Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Enterprise (>$100K ACV, C-suite sponsor) | Executive experience (exclusive dinner, industry event, private advisory access) — NOT cash | Cash feels transactional at this relationship level; experiences reinforce strategic partnership | Cash, gift cards, obvious "affiliate" framing |
| Mid-Market ($25K–$99K ACV, VP-level champion) | Service credit, professional development (conference sponsorship, training credits), or product feature early access | Ties incentive to product value; helps champion justify their advocacy internally | Cash paid to individual (creates compliance issues at many companies) |
| SMB (<$25K ACV, manager-level champion) | Amazon gift card, account credit, swag bundle — announced upfront as a formal referral program | More transactional relationship; explicit referral program feels appropriate and appreciated | Over-complicated tiered systems; anything requiring legal review |

---

**Incentive Delivery Templates**

*Incentive announcement (embed in QBR Ask or Peak Moment Ask email — use only for Mid-Market/SMB):*

"One more thing: we do have a formal referral program. If an introduction leads to a customer, you'll receive [specific incentive]. I didn't want to lead with that because I hope you'd make the intro regardless — but it's there, and I want to be transparent about it."

*Incentive fulfillment email (sent at close, not at intro):*

Subject: Thank you — and a small token of appreciation

Hi [First Name],

[Prospect Company] just signed as a customer — and your introduction made it possible.

[Incentive delivery: gift card / account credit / event invitation — specific details]

More than the referral program: thank you for trusting us with your professional relationships. That means more to us than any deal.

[CSM Name]

*Critical rule:* Never mention incentives in the initial referral ask email if the customer is enterprise or C-suite. Introduce incentives only after they've agreed in principle, or omit entirely for high-value relationships.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CSMs feel uncomfortable making referral asks — it feels like they're using the customer relationship for quota purposes.**
Fix: Reframe the referral motion as customer service, not sales. A CSM who introduces a CFO to a peer who solves their biggest problem is providing value to both parties. Train the team on the "peer introduction" framing — you're helping your champion look good by connecting them to a relevant resource. Role-play the Peak Moment Ask script in team meetings until it feels natural. Track referral ask activity as a leading CS metric so it's a professional expectation, not an uncomfortable exception.

**Problem: Referral introductions are being made but prospects aren't converting to meetings.**
