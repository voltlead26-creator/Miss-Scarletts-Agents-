## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Customers who hit usage limits reduce usage instead of upgrading — the limit acts as a ceiling, not an upgrade trigger.
**Solution:** Shift the trigger threshold from 80% to 60% of limit, giving customers 4–6 weeks of runway before the constraint becomes painful. Reframe the message from "you're near your limit" to "you're on a growth trajectory — here's what uncapped looks like." Add a 30-day "free capacity boost" trial that lets them exceed the limit temporarily, making the value of the higher tier tangible before the ask.

**Problem:** Can't attribute expansion ARR to specific marketing programs in the CRM — it looks like CS-driven expansion.
**Solution:** Implement a dedicated `Expansion Source` field in your CRM mirroring your lead source taxonomy. Add UTM parameters (`utm_source=expansion_email&utm_campaign=limit_alert&utm_content=email_d1`) to all upgrade CTAs and map them to contract change records via your marketing automation platform's revenue attribution rules. Run a monthly deconfliction meeting with CS to agree on multi-touch credit allocation when both CS and marketing touched the same account.

**Problem:** Downgrade signals are identified too late — the customer has already made the decision and is in the contract change process.
**Solution:** Move from reactive to predictive detection: implement a weekly "Usage Cliff Detection" query that flags accounts where usage has dropped >30% versus the prior 4-week rolling average. This captures disengagement 45–60 days before formal downgrade requests. Pair with champion job-change monitoring (tools like Salesforce native alerts, UserGems, or Keyplay) since champion departure precedes 60% of Enterprise downgrades.
