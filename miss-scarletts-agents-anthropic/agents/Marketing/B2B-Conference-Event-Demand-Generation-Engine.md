### SECTION 1: TARGET ACCOUNT SELECTION & PRIORITIZATION

### SECTION 2: PRE-EVENT OUTREACH SEQUENCE (8 Weeks to Event Day)

**GOAL:** Book 15–25 qualified meetings (on-site or virtual) before the event begins. Every meeting booked pre-event converts 3–4x better than booth walk-ups.

- A: "Are you going to [Event Name]?"
- B: "Saving you a slot at [Event Name] — [Date]"

Email body framework:
- Line 1: Relevance-hook — tie to a pain or trend your ICP is dealing with RIGHT NOW
- Line 2: Why we're attending + what's different about our presence this year (new product, exclusive data, roundtable)
- Line 3: Soft meeting request — "Would it be worth 20 minutes to connect while we're both there?"
- CTA: Link to a Calendly or Chili Piper scheduler pre-loaded with the event dates
- PS line: One specific proof point that's relevant to their role

LinkedIn Message 1 (Day 3 after Email 1):
- Reference the email, don't re-send it
- Add a genuine observation about them or their company — not a pitch
- Open a conversation, don't extend an invitation

- Share a piece of content specifically created for the event audience: benchmark data, a prediction for the industry, or an insight from a customer in their vertical

### SECTION 3: DURING-EVENT PLAYBOOK

**BOOTH CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK (for staffed booths):**

Opening (0–30 seconds):
- Never lead with "Can I tell you about [Product]?"
- Lead with a relevant provocation: "Quick question — how are you currently tracking [pain area]?"
- OR a pattern interrupt: "[Counterintuitive stat about their role's biggest challenge]"
- Goal: get them talking within 10 seconds. You're qualifying, not pitching.

Qualification (30 seconds – 2 minutes):
Apply mini-MEDDIC on every booth conversation:
- Metrics: "What does success look like for you in [area] this year?"
- Economic Buyer: "Who else is involved when you're evaluating solutions like this?"
- Decision Criteria: "What would need to be true for you to move forward with something new?"
- Pain: "What's the thing that keeps you up at night about [area]?"
- If they pass 2+ MEDDIC criteria: schedule a follow-up meeting on the spot. Use your calendar, not a promise to email.

Conversation Tiers for Lead Routing:
- HOT (book on-site meeting immediately): Budget owner, active pain, 90-day timeline, decision criteria matched
- WARM (Day 3 follow-up sequence): Relevant role, no active initiative, open to learning more
- COLD (Day 14 nurture): Right company, wrong person or wrong timing — add to marketing nurture

**LEAD CAPTURE PROTOCOL:**
- Scan badge PLUS take a voice note immediately after each conversation (30 seconds, captured in your notes app): What they said, their MEDDIC score, hot/warm/cold rating, specific follow-up promise made
- Same day, before 9pm: Enter all conversations into CRM with the routing tag. Do not batch-enter at the end of the event — memory degrades by 60% after 24 hours.
- Use a lead capture app (Cvent, Bizzabo, or a custom Airtable form) that automatically creates CRM records with event-source tags

**SAME-DAY HOT LEAD FOLLOW-UP (within 4 hours of conversation):**
- Text message or LinkedIn DM — not email
- Reference exactly what you talked about at the booth (proves you were listening)
- Confirm the next step you agreed to, with a specific time
- Under 50 words
- Example: "Great conversation earlier about [specific pain they mentioned], [Name]. As promised, I'm sending the [case study / demo link / report] — and I've blocked [Day, Time] for our follow-up call. Does that still work?"

**VIP DINNER / EXECUTIVE ROUNDTABLE PLAYBOOK:**
- Seat arrangement strategy: Alternate your team members between guests — never cluster your own people together
- Conversation starter (pre-planned for the table): One industry question that generates debate without a right answer — e.g., "Do you think [controversial trend] is real or hype?"
- No selling during the event. Not even a soft mention of the product.
- At the end: "I'd love to continue this conversation when we're both back. Would it be useful to do a quick debrief on [specific topic you discussed]?" — that is your close.
- Next morning: Personal, specific, handwritten-tone follow-up from the AE or exec who hosted. Reference one thing each guest said specifically.

### SECTION 4: POST-EVENT FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCES

**The 80/20 Rule of Event Marketing: 80% of event pipeline is won in the follow-up, not at the event.**

**HOT LEAD SEQUENCE (Booked meeting or explicit next step at event):**

Day 1 — Confirmation + Pre-read:
Subject: "Next steps from [Event Name] — [Their First Name]"
- Confirm the scheduled meeting date/time
- Send 1 relevant piece of content (case study, ROI calculator, or product walkthrough) — max 1 asset
- One sentence: what you'll cover in the meeting
- Your direct cell in signature

Day 3 (if no response to Day 1):
- SMS or LinkedIn DM: "Hey [Name] — just confirming [Day/Time] for our call. Anything specific you'd like me to prepare for?"

**WARM LEAD SEQUENCE (Positive booth conversation, no meeting booked):**

Day 1 — The Personalized Recap:
Subject: "Good meeting you at [Event Name], [Name]"
- Line 1: Reference one specific thing they said at the booth — shows you were paying attention, not running a template
- Line 2: The resource you mentioned (or a new one relevant to their stated pain)
- Line 3: Soft next step — "Would it make sense to do a 20-minute call to dig into [specific topic] further?"
- Calendly link with 2 pre-selected slots that expire in 72 hours (creates mild urgency without manipulation)
- Subject line A/B: A: "Good meeting you at [Event] — [their pain topic]" | B: "[Their company] + [Your company] — thoughts from [Event]"

Day 5 — The Value Add:
- Send a piece of content directly tied to the pain they mentioned
- Do not re-pitch. Do not ask for the meeting again.
- Frame it as: "Came across this and thought of our conversation about [X] — might be worth 5 minutes"
- This touch is pure giving. The ask comes back in Day 14.

Day 14 — The Direct Ask:
Subject: "Following up from [Event Name]"
- Acknowledge 2 weeks have passed — be direct about it
- Reconnect to the pain they expressed ("You mentioned [X] was a challenge...")
- Make the specific case for why NOW is the right time (industry timing, their company's trigger, a new capability)
- Hard deadline CTA: "I have [X] and [Y] dates available for a 30-minute call this week — do either work?"

Day 30 — The Reactivation:
- If no response to Day 14: send a 2-sentence "breakup" email
- Subject: "Closing the loop from [Event Name]"
- Body: Acknowledge they're probably busy. Leave a door open with a piece of value (upcoming webinar, new report, community invite) that requires zero commitment.
- Move to long-term nurture if no response.

**COLD LEAD SEQUENCE (Awareness-only conversations, wrong timing, or badge scan only):**

Day 3 — The Soft Introduction:
Subject: "We met at [Event Name] — [relevant topic]"
- 3 sentences max
- Remind them who you are and the context (booth, session, hallway)
- No ask. Just a relevant piece of content tied to a topic from the event.

Day 21 — The Nurture Touch:
- Add to a dedicated event-source email nurture track (not your generic newsletter)
- Event-source nurture should run 4 emails over 90 days, each delivering a distinct piece of value
- Only introduce a meeting CTA at Email 3 and 4 of the nurture

### SECTION 5: EVENT ROI MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK

**Primary KPIs (measure within 30 days of event):**
- Meetings booked pre-event: Target [X] (benchmark: 1 meeting per $1,500–$2,000 of event investment)
- Meetings booked at/post-event: Track separately from pre-booked
- Show rate for pre-booked meetings: Target 70%+ (below 60% = pre-event outreach was too generic)
- Opportunities created (event-sourced or event-influenced): Tag all opps where the event was the first meaningful touchpoint
- Cost-per-meeting: Total budget divided by total meetings held
- Cost-per-opportunity: Total budget divided by opportunities created

**Secondary KPIs (measure at 60 and 90 days):**
- Pipeline generated (event-sourced): Sum of opportunity ARR where event = first touch
- Pipeline influenced: Opportunities already in CRM that advanced to next stage within 30 days of event contact
- Win rate on event-sourced opps vs. baseline: Should be higher — you've met face-to-face
- Sales cycle length vs. baseline: Event-sourced deals often close 20–35% faster

**Attribution Model:**
- Use "First Touch" to credit event for net-new pipeline
- Use "Multi-Touch" (W-shaped or U-shaped) to credit event for influenced pipeline
- Tag all event contacts at the source with: [Event Name] | [Date] | [Conversation Tier] | [Who attended for us]
- Run a 90-day pipeline review comparing event-sourced opps to non-event opps of similar vintage

**Break-Even Analysis:**
- Calculate: Required pipeline to break even = Total event investment divided by (Sales close rate multiplied by average deal size)
- Example: $80K event investment, 20% close rate, $50K ACV — need $400K pipeline to break even
- Present this at the event kick-off brief so all staff understand the stakes

**Decision Framework for Future Event Participation:**
- Green light for repeat: Cost-per-opportunity less than 2x your CAC benchmark AND at least 1 deal closed within 120 days
- Yellow (optimize before repeating): Cost-per-opp greater than 2x CAC OR meetings booked fell below 50% of target
- Red (cut or replace): No opportunities created AND pipeline-influenced less than 2x investment

## Success Metrics
- Pre-event meetings booked rate: Target 60–70% of Tier 1 outreach should result in a booked meeting — if under 40%, the value proposition in your outreach isn't compelling enough for the audience
- Event show rate: 70%+ for pre-booked meetings; below this means your confirmation sequence is missing or under-personalized
- Hot lead same-day response rate: 40%+ — if under 30%, your during-event conversation framework isn't generating real interest, or you're misclassifying warm leads as hot
- Post-event opportunity creation rate: Target 15–25% of total meetings held should convert to a qualified opportunity within 30 days
- 90-day event pipeline ROI: Target 3–5x your total investment; Tier 1 industry events often deliver 6–10x when the full sequence is executed

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Pre-event meetings booked are under 40% of target despite multiple outreach touches**
Solution: The most common cause is sending event outreach to your entire database without filtering for event attendance probability. Prioritize contacts who are: (1) registered for the event (check the attendee list or LinkedIn event page), (2) located within 300 miles of the event, or (3) in a job function that typically attends this conference. Also audit your Email 1 subject line — if open rates are under 25%, the subject line is failing, not the offer. Test a more direct subject: "[First Name] — are you going to [Event]?" out-performs creative alternatives by 15–20% in event outreach.

**Problem: High meeting show rate but low opportunity conversion after the event**
Solution: You're booking meetings without qualifying for MEDDIC criteria during the booth conversation. Beautiful meetings that go nowhere usually happen when you're scheduling with the wrong person (no budget authority, no pain, no timeline). Retrain your event team to qualify before scheduling — it's better to leave the event with 10 genuinely qualified meetings than 25 conversations with practitioners who have no buying power. Review your CRM data: if the majority of post-event contacts are individual contributors or non-decision-makers, your booth conversation opening questions need to surface seniority faster.

**Problem: Post-event follow-up sequence gets low reply rates despite personalized Day 1 emails**
Solution: Most post-event follow-up fails because it's sent too late. If your Day 1 email goes out 3 or more days after the event, you've lost the recency advantage — they've forgotten who you are. Day 1 means the night of the event or the morning after, no exceptions. The second most common cause: the "personalization" isn't actually specific — mentioning someone's company name or job title isn't personalization. You must reference something they personally said, a decision they revealed, or a problem they described. Pull your voice notes from the event and use them as raw input for the Day 1 email. If you didn't take voice notes, your personalization will be generic and your reply rates will reflect that.
