## Troubleshooting

**Issue: AI output is generic and not calibrated to PE firm expectations**
Solution: Include the specific PE firm name and any known value creation frameworks they use (e.g., Vista uses "VEP" — Vista Excellence Program; Thoma Bravo uses specific ARR efficiency benchmarks). Adding "The PE sponsor is [firm] which uses [framework] to measure portfolio performance" dramatically sharpens recommendations to match real sponsor expectations.

**Issue: ARR impact projections feel vague or unsubstantiated**
Solution: Add explicit benchmark anchors directly to the prompt: "SaaS Capital 2024 data shows median CAC payback for $15-25M ARR healthcare SaaS is 16 months" and "OpenView 2024 benchmarks show top quartile NRR for vertical SaaS is 118%." When the AI has specific benchmarks to compare against, projections are grounded in market reality rather than estimates.

**Issue: 100-day plan is too aspirational and doesn't account for team capacity constraints**
Solution: Add hard constraints to the prompt: "The marketing team is 5 FTEs (1 demand gen manager, 1 content writer, 1 marketing ops, 2 general marketers). Zero budget increase is available. All Phase 1 recommendations must be executable within existing team capacity." Explicit resource constraints force the AI to prioritize ruthlessly rather than producing an aspirational wish list.
