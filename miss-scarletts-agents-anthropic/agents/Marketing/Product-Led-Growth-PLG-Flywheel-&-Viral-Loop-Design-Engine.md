## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Viral K-factor is near zero despite in-product sharing features being built.
**Solution**: The sharing mechanism isn't surfaced at the moment of maximum emotional resonance. Map where in the product user satisfaction is highest (post-Aha Moment, post-first-win) and move the share CTA to that exact moment. Also audit whether the sharing experience delivers value to the *recipient* — if the recipient gets nothing useful, the loop won't close.

**Problem**: Free-to-paid conversion is below 2% and freemium tier feels too generous.
**Solution**: Run a "reverse trial" test — give all new users full paid access for 14 days, then auto-downgrade. This inverts the psychology from "trying a limited product" to "losing something valuable." Typically lifts conversion 40-80% in B2B SaaS (Loom, Calendly case studies). The key: Day 12 email must be loss-aversion framed, not feature-benefit framed.

**Problem**: PQLs are handed to sales but close rates are no better than cold outbound.
**Solution**: The PQL definition is likely too loose (triggering on low-intent signals) or the sales handoff timing is wrong. Require at least one collaboration signal (invited teammate, shared output) in the PQL definition — solo users without team expansion intent rarely convert at rates that justify sales resources. Also, sales first touch should reference the specific in-product action: "I saw you ran 7 calls through Fieldnote this week — what's your team's biggest friction in updating the CRM afterward?"
