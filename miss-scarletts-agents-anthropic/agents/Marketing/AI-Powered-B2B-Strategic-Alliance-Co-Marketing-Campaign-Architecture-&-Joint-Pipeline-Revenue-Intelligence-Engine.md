## Integration Tips

**Crossbeam / PartnerTap:**
Export account overlap data weekly as CSV → import into HubSpot/Salesforce as "Partner Overlap" field → trigger co-marketing nurture sequences automatically for Tier 1 overlapping accounts.

**HubSpot:**
- Create co-marketing campaign in HubSpot with partner-specific UTM parameters (utm_source=partner, utm_medium=co-marketing, utm_campaign=[partner]-[campaign-name]-[quarter])
- Use "Associated Company" to tag deals as "Partner Influenced" or "Partner Sourced"
- Build shared dashboard with partner contact access (HubSpot Reports → Share via link)

**Salesforce:**
- Add "Partner Campaign Influence" as a campaign type in Campaign object
- Create opportunity field: "Partner Co-Sell Status" (Not Involved / Supportive / Actively Co-Selling / Joint Presentation)
- Set up Salesforce flow to notify partner contact when opportunity in Tier 1 account moves to Stage 3+

**Impartner / Allbound / PartnerStack:**
Publish co-marketing assets to partner portal with automatic notification to partner's marketing contact. Track asset downloads and portal engagement as early signal of partner activation.

**Slack / Teams:**
Create dedicated Slack channel (#veridian-snowflake-comkt) with both teams for real-time campaign coordination. Use Zapier to auto-post new MQL alerts and pipeline milestones to the channel.

**Zapier Automation:**
Trigger: New MQL from co-marketing campaign (HubSpot) → Action: Post to shared Slack channel + Create task in Asana for partner co-marketing contact + Log in shared Google Sheet dashboard.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner marketing team is unresponsive or deprioritizes joint campaigns**
Fix: Escalate to partner's alliance/channel manager (not marketing) and reframe as a co-sell motion, not a marketing project. Show pipeline data from comparable co-marketing programs. Offer to do 80% of the work—ask for brand approval and list access only. Start with a small, low-lift activation (a joint blog post or a 30-min webinar) to build trust before proposing larger campaigns.

**Problem: Lead routing disputes—both teams claiming pipeline credit**
Fix: Pre-agree on attribution rules before campaign launch, in writing. Use the "originating source" model: whoever first touched the account (in CRM data) gets 60% credit; the other party gets 40% influence credit. Document this in the co-marketing agreement and configure CRM campaign attribution accordingly before the campaign goes live.

**Problem: Campaign assets stuck in legal/brand approval for weeks**
Fix: Build a "pre-approved template" library with each partner that handles the most common asset types (co-branded email template, solution brief template, webinar slide deck template) with approved logo placement and legal disclaimers. Reserve full legal review only for net-new asset categories. Most co-marketing delays happen because teams start from scratch—pre-approved templates cut approval cycles by 70%.
