## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Low advocate program enrollment despite healthy NPS scores**
*Fix:* Review the invitation email tone — if it reads as a demand instead of an exclusive opportunity, open rates drop. A/B test "invitation from CSM" vs. "invitation from Product team" vs. "peer recommendation." Also audit timing: sending the invite too early in the customer lifecycle (< 90 days post-onboarding) reduces response rates by 40% on average. Wait until your first major customer success milestone before the ask.

**Issue: Expansion campaigns generating opens but no pipeline conversations**
*Fix:* The economic buyer sequence is likely hitting the wrong contact (power user) or arriving at the wrong time in their fiscal calendar. Check your CRM contact role fields — expansion emails to economic buyers (CFO, VP Legal, COO) must use role-based enrollment, not account-level. Also confirm the expansion campaign is paused during active renewal negotiation windows (30 days pre/post renewal date), as pricing conversations during that window create confusion.

**Issue: CSM team resisting customer marketing program enrollment ("these are my accounts")**
*Fix:* This is cultural, not technical. Solve it by building CSM co-ownership into program design: (1) Make CSMs the "from" name on high-touch advocate emails, (2) Send CSMs a pre-send notification 48 hours before any email goes to their accounts with a one-click "pause this send" option, (3) Share CSM-level attribution reporting showing how customer marketing is driving expansion pipeline credit for their book — most CSMs become advocates once they see pipeline attribution with their name on it.
