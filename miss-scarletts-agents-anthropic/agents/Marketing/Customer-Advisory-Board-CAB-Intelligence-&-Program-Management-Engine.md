## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot**: Create a "CAB Member" account tag and track CAB participation, session attendance, and follow-through commitments as CRM activities. Build a CAB NRR dashboard comparing cohort expansion vs. non-CAB accounts monthly.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Build a CAB Intelligence Hub — a living database of all session insights tagged by theme, signal type, and member. Link directly to product Jira tickets and content briefs created from CAB outputs. Update it within 48 hours of every session.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Pull the last 3 call recordings for each CAB member before a session to refresh on their current concerns, language patterns, and recent questions — surfaces intelligence the CS team may have missed in written notes.
- **Gainsight / ChurnZero**: Sync CAB membership and attendance into your CS platform so health scores automatically account for CAB engagement. Members who miss two consecutive sessions and haven't sent an explanation are a churn signal worth investigating.
- **Zoom / Hopin**: For virtual CABs, use breakout room transcripts and auto-summarization to capture 100% of small-group conversations — not just the plenary session. Enable AI meeting notes and export directly into your insight synthesis workflow.
- **Jira / Linear**: Create a saved filter for issues tagged "CAB-sourced" so the product team can pull a single report before each CAB session showing what they built based on previous input — closing the feedback loop visibly.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate the post-session workflow — when session notes are uploaded to Notion, trigger: (1) thank-you email sequence draft sent to marketing for review, (2) Salesforce tasks created for each commitment, (3) Slack notification to CS leads with churn risk flags.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: CAB members give polished, safe answers instead of honest critical feedback — the session feels like a PR exercise rather than strategic dialogue.
**Solution**: Redesign the facilitation approach: (1) Use anonymous real-time polling (Mentimeter, Slido) before full-group discussion so members see aggregate sentiment before committing to a position publicly — this dramatically reduces social desirability bias. (2) Ask "What's the one thing we're doing that you wish we'd stop?" as a structured exercise — giving explicit permission to criticize makes members more comfortable using it. (3) Rotate the facilitator out of the room for one breakout segment and have a neutral CS manager run it — members are more candid without the product/marketing team listening.

**Problem**: CAB insights don't make it into the product roadmap — the team says "we already knew that" or ignores the input entirely.
**Solution**: Quantify every CAB insight with revenue impact before presenting to product. Instead of "7 members mentioned they want better reporting," say "7 members representing $620K ARR said inadequate reporting is actively preventing them from giving additional seats to business partners — this is blocking $187K in identified expansion." Revenue-weighted insights get into backlogs. Abstract feedback does not. Also require product leads to attend at least one CAB session per year — not as observers, but as active participants in a breakout. Nothing accelerates roadmap prioritization like a product manager hearing a $90K customer say a feature is unusable.

**Problem**: You're struggling to recruit qualified CAB members — customers are reluctant to commit the time.
**Solution**: CAB is a value exchange, not a favor. Make the value explicit in your recruitment pitch: members get early access to the product roadmap, direct access to leadership, peer benchmarking with similar companies, and documented first-mover advantage on features they helped shape. Create a "CAB Alumni" case study highlighting how a previous member's input led to a specific product improvement that directly benefited their company. Offer a "CAB Preview" — a 30-minute leadership call before formal membership — so prospects can evaluate whether the program is worth their time before committing.
