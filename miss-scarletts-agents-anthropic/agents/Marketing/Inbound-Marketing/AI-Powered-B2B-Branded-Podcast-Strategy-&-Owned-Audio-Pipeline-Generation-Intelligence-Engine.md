## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Downloads are flat or declining after 6 episodes**
Solution: Diagnose sequentially — (1) Check episode completion rate: if < 40%, the content format or length is wrong; try shorter episodes with more specific topics. (2) Check subscriber sources: if 80%+ of downloads come from your own email list, you have a distribution problem, not a content problem — invest in guest amplification and SEO optimization. (3) Check guest selection: if guests have < 5,000 LinkedIn followers, they can't amplify. Upgrade to Tier 1 guests with 10,000+ followers.

**Problem: Zero pipeline attribution after 3 months**
Solution: The CTA architecture is broken. Diagnose: (1) Are show notes pages getting traffic? (Check Google Analytics.) (2) Are visitors clicking CTAs? (Check heatmaps with Hotjar.) (3) Are forms converting? (Check form completion rate — should be > 15%.) Most common fix: the lead magnet doesn't match the episode content. Redesign CTAs to be hyper-specific to each episode topic, not a generic "Subscribe to our newsletter" offer.

**Problem: Top guests are declining or ghosting booking requests**
Solution: Your guest value proposition is weak. Add three elements: (1) Send a "listener intelligence brief" — a one-page document showing who your audience is with job titles, company sizes, and industries. Executives want to know who they're speaking to. (2) Offer to write their guest bio, episode summary, and LinkedIn post copy — remove their effort. (3) Share episode analytics from recent episodes to prove the show has real reach. Never cold-book a Tier 1 guest without social proof of audience quality.
