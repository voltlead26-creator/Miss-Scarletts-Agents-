## Integration Tips

- **Buffer/Hootsuite:** Use the content calendar output as a CSV import. Map pillar names to campaign tags for performance filtering by content type.
- **HubSpot:** Create a "Social Media" campaign for each content pillar. Add UTM-linked forms to track social-sourced contacts. Build a workflow: `if contact source = LinkedIn organic → enroll in Social Warm Lead sequence`.
- **Salesforce:** Add "Social Influenced" checkbox to Opportunity object. Create a report: `Opportunities with Contact Role who has LinkedIn activity in last 30 days before Stage 2`.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Use TeamLink to identify when target accounts engage with your company posts. Trigger SDR outreach within 24 hours of a VP-level prospect commenting on a post.
- **Zapier/Make:** Automate: when LinkedIn post exceeds 3% engagement rate → notify SDR team via Slack with list of commenters to connect with → log to Google Sheet for weekly review.
- **Notion:** Use the 90-day calendar output to create a Notion database with properties: Platform, Pillar, Format, Status (Draft/Scheduled/Published), Engagement Rate (post-publish). Link to published URLs for easy performance tracking.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** LinkedIn posts getting low impressions despite strong content.
**Solution:** Algorithm prioritizes posts that receive engagement in the first 60 minutes. Schedule posts when your ICP is most active (Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am or 12-1pm in their primary timezone). Ask 3-5 internal advocates to comment within the first hour — substantive comments, not just likes.

**Problem:** Content calendar exists but execution stalls — posts aren't getting written or published consistently.
**Solution:** The calendar is too granular and aspirational. Reduce to a "minimum viable social" plan: 3 posts per week on LinkedIn only. Create a repeatable production system — one ideation session (30 min, Monday), one writing session (60 min, Tuesday), schedule through Thursday. Batch-produce 2 weeks of content in one sitting monthly.

**Problem:** Social engagement is high but CRM shows zero pipeline influenced by social.
**Solution:** UTM tracking is broken or not implemented. Audit: (1) Check UTM parameters on every link shared in last 30 days. (2) Confirm HubSpot/Salesforce is reading UTM parameters into contact source fields. (3) Many B2B buyers don't click links from social — they Google your company name instead. Set up a branded keyword attribution rule: any contact that arrives via branded search within 7 days of a high-engagement post gets "social-influenced" tag.
