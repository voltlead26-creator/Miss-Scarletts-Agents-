## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom contact property `newsletter_sponsor_source` to capture self-reported attribution from form submissions. Build a workflow that tags contacts arriving via newsletter UTMs into a dedicated newsletter sponsorship list for 90-day pipeline tracking.
- **Salesforce:** Build an `Influence Source` campaign member record for newsletter-attributed contacts. Use Salesforce Campaigns to track multi-touch pipeline influence — create one Campaign per newsletter property and flight date.
- **HockeyStack / Dreamdata:** Enable self-reported attribution surveys and map newsletter sponsorship as a channel. These tools natively de-duplicate UTM-tracked vs. self-reported pipeline, making them ideal for this channel.
- **Google Sheets / Looker Studio:** Build a monthly CPL & pipeline scorecard by property. Use IMPORTRANGE to pull UTM-tracked conversions from GA4 into a master scorecard alongside CRM pipeline data.
- **Zapier:** Automate promo code redemption tracking — when a CRM contact's `Promo Code Used` field is populated, trigger a Zap to tag the contact with the relevant newsletter property and flight date for attribution.
- **Typeform / Google Forms:** Set up a 1-question post-demo survey ("Where did you first hear about us?") with a multi-select list that includes your active newsletter properties. Route responses to CRM via Zapier.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** UTM tracking shows very few clicks from newsletter placements despite publisher reporting high open rates.
**Solution:** Newsletter email clients (Gmail, Outlook mobile) often strip UTM parameters or prevent tracking pixels from firing. Assume UTM data captures only 40–70% of actual traffic. Always pair UTMs with promo codes and post-conversion surveys. Cross-reference with direct traffic lift in GA4 during active flight windows. Ask publishers for click-verified reporting using their own link tracking (not just open rates).

**Problem:** Lead quality from newsletter sponsorships is poor — high CPL, low SQL conversion rate.
**Solution:** Usually indicates audience-ICP mismatch. Run the 10-point due diligence checklist on the media property — specifically request subscriber demographic breakdown (job title distribution, company size). Shift budget toward Tier 2 niche properties with tighter ICP alignment. Also evaluate your landing page — cold newsletter traffic needs more trust-building above the fold before conversion; reduce friction and add social proof before the form.

**Problem:** Publisher can't provide transparent engagement metrics or audience demographics.
**Solution:** This is a major red flag. Require a media kit that includes verified open rates (not just claimed), click rates for comparable sponsors, and audience survey or firmographic data. If they can't provide it, negotiate a performance guarantee (minimum clicks) into the contract with a refund clause, or run a very small test placement before committing to a larger flight. Never commit to a quarterly contract without seeing at least 3 months of historical performance data for comparable placements.
