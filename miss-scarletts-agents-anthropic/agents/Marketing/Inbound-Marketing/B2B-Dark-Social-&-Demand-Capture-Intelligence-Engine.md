## Overview

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Survey completion rate below 20% — not enough self-reported data**
Fix: Move the primary attribution question to the demo confirmation page (not email follow-up) and make it a single-question embed. Reduce options from 10 to 6 to lower cognitive load. Add social proof: "847 customers told us how they found us — your answer helps us serve communities like yours better." Target: 40%+ completion when the question appears immediately post-form-submit.

**Problem: Community seeding posts feel promotional and get flagged by moderators**
Fix: Apply the "80/20 value rule" — 80% of community posts should be genuinely helpful with zero brand mention, 20% can reference your brand's perspective or data. Lead with a question or contrarian data point rather than a "here's what we do" framing. Have a non-marketing employee (practitioner) post rather than the company account. Read the community's posting guidelines and match tone exactly before posting.

**Problem: CFO won't fund dark social investment because ROI is "unprovable"**
Fix: Frame dark social as a "brand tax multiplier" rather than a standalone channel — every $1 spent on traceable channels is worth 1.3x more when dark social brand awareness already exists in the ICP (supported by Nielsen, LinkedIn B2B Institute research). Use your own data: if branded search is up 40% but trackable inbound is up 15%, the gap is your dark social ROI floor. Model conservatively: "If even 20% of our unattributed closed-won deals came from community influence, that's $Xm in pipeline this year. Our community investment is $Y, which is a Z:1 return floor."
