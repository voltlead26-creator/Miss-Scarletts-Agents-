## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Build as a Workflow triggered by "Demo Booked" deal stage change. Use personalization tokens for company, rep name, and pain points captured in intake form. Branch logic for deal size (Enterprise vs. SMB) using Company Properties.
- **Salesforce + Outreach**: Create an Outreach Sequence triggered via SFDC workflow rule on Opportunity Stage = "Demo Scheduled." Use Outreach Variables to pull in Opportunity fields (company, rep, date).
- **Chili Piper**: Use Instant Booker form fields (pain points, contract volume, team size) to feed into the confirmation email personalization. Pass form data as UTM parameters or hidden fields into HubSpot/Salesforce.
- **Calendly**: Set up Calendly invitee questions ("Top challenge," "Contract volume") and push responses to HubSpot via native integration. Use answer data in Email 1 personalization.
- **Loom/Navattic/Arcade**: Pre-record an async demo version for no-show recovery emails. Create role-specific versions (legal vs. sales buyer) for higher relevance.
- **Slack + CRM**: Set up Slack alerts to the AE when a prospect opens the same-day recap email 3+ times — strong buying signal to prioritize outreach.
- **Zapier**: If show rate drops below threshold in any week, trigger a Slack alert to the demand gen manager with that week's show rate data from Salesforce/HubSpot.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Show rate isn't improving despite sending confirmation emails.**
Solution: Check email deliverability first — if open rate on confirmation Email 1 is below 60%, your domain reputation may be suppressing delivery. Second, test SMS timing: move SMS 1 to 26-28 hours before (not 24 exactly) to catch buyers during work hours vs. late evening. Third, add a pre-demo prep question that requires a reply — demos where prospects reply have 40%+ lower no-show rates.

**Problem: No-show recovery emails get ignored (< 10% reply rate).**
Solution: The no-show Email 1 subject line is doing the heavy lifting — A/B test at minimum 3 subject variants. Best-performing format tends to be empathetic + very low-friction subject: "Missed you — here's a 4-min version" outperforms "Let's reschedule." Also ensure the async demo link (Loom/Arcade) is genuinely tailored to their ICP, not a generic product walkthrough.

**Problem: Post-demo sequence generates opens but no replies or next meetings.**
Solution: Each email likely has multiple CTAs or a weak single CTA. Audit every email for exactly one ask. The Day 9 ROI email tends to be the highest-converting — if it's not generating responses, the ROI calculator numbers may not feel credible to the buyer. Add a specific customer company reference (anonymized if needed) with their actual ROI outcome rather than generic ranges.
