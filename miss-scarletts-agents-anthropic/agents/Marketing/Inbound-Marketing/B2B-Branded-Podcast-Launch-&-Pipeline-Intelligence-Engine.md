## PART 3: EPISODE-TO-PIPELINE CONVERSION ARCHITECTURE

### 3.1 Episode Structure Engineered for Conversion
**Minutes 0–3: The Cold Open Hook**
- Start with the guest's most provocative statement (edit in post-production)
- Never start with "Thanks for having me"
- Formula: "Today we're going to challenge [common assumption] — [guest name] is going to show you [specific transformation]"

**Minutes 3–8: Credibility + Context**
- Guest backstory — NOT a resume recitation; instead: "Tell me about the mistake that taught you [key lesson]"
- Anchor their experience to YOUR ICP's reality: "You've seen this at [# companies] — what's the most common mistake?"

**Minutes 8–40: The Intellectual Core**
- 3–4 structured questions tied to the episode's lead magnet topic
- Use the STAR-C framework: Situation → Task → Action → Result → **Context** (what should listeners do with this?)
- Probe for specifics: "Can you give me a real number? What was the before and after?"

**Minutes 40–45: The Rapid Fire + Soft CTA**
- 3 quick-hit questions (favorite tool, contrarian take, what they'd do differently)
- Soft CTA: "For listeners who want to go deeper on [topic], we've built a [lead magnet name] — link in the show notes"

**Minutes 45–47: The Hard CTA**
- Host solo close: "If today's episode resonated, [Company Name] helps teams like [guest company] to [outcome]. The fastest way to see if we're a fit: [specific low-friction action, e.g., 'grab a 15-minute intro call at [URL]']"

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Low episode completion rates (< 50%) — listeners drop off before your CTAs
**Solution:** Move your hardest-hitting insight to Minutes 5–10 instead of burying it mid-episode; A/B test episode lengths (30-min vs. 45-min for same topic type); ensure your cold open hook is edited in post-production from the episode's best 30-second moment

**Problem:** Podcast generating listeners but zero pipeline attribution
**Solution:** Every lead magnet needs a dedicated landing page (not a generic resources page) with UTM-tagged links in every show notes; add a "How did you hear about us?" field to your demo request form and include "Podcast" as an option; create a Podcast Listeners audience segment in HubSpot and retarget on LinkedIn

**Problem:** Guests agreeing to record but not promoting their episodes
**Solution:** Send a "Guest Amplification Kit" 48 hours before launch: pre-written LinkedIn posts (3 variants at different lengths), a quote card image sized for LinkedIn, a 60-second audiogram, and direct links with their personal UTM tag so they can see their own downstream traffic — make sharing easier than not sharing
