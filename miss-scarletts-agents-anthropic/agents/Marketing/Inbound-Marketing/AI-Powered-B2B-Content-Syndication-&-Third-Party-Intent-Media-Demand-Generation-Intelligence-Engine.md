## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High lead volume but SQL rate is below 5%**
*Root causes and fixes:*
- **Intent data absent:** You're buying broad CPL with no demand signal. Fix: Add TechTarget Priority Engine or Bombora topic filter to every vendor contract. Require vendors to restrict delivery to accounts showing intent signals for your product category.
- **ICP filtering too loose:** Your qualification questions aren't filtering aggressively enough. Fix: Add a company size hard filter on the vendor's form platform, not just in your CRM post-delivery — leads that don't match ICP should never be delivered.
- **Content asset mismatch:** You're syndicating late-stage content (case studies) to top-of-funnel audiences who aren't ready for vendor evaluation. Fix: Match asset stage to audience intent stage. Use benchmark reports and industry research for awareness-stage audiences; reserve case studies for intent-enriched vendors with proven decision-maker audiences.

**Problem: Vendors keep delivering duplicate leads or recycled contacts already in CRM**
*Fix:* Implement a suppression list upload to every vendor before each delivery cycle (monthly minimum, weekly ideal). Most Tier 1 vendors support suppression list uploads via their portal or API. For vendors that don't: add a CRM deduplication workflow that checks every inbound lead against existing contacts before routing. File replacement claims for duplicates within your contract's claim window (typically 30 days). If a vendor consistently delivers >15% duplicates, renegotiate the contract to include a duplicate guarantee or find a replacement vendor.

**Problem: SDRs won't work syndication leads — they claim they're "low quality"**
*Fix:* This is almost always a segmentation and context problem, not a lead quality problem. SDRs are receiving leads without intent context and treating them like cold list pulls. Fix in three steps: (1) Surface intent data on every lead — include the lead's Bombora surge topics and score in the CRM record so SDRs see *why* this person is a priority. (2) Create a separate SDR sequence specifically for syndication leads — shorter, more empathetic, acknowledging the content they downloaded. (3) Start a "Syndication lead closed won" Slack thread celebrating wins from the channel to rebuild SDR trust in the source.
