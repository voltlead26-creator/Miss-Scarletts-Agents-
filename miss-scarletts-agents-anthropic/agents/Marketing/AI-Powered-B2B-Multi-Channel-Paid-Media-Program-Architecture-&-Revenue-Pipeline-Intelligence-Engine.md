MODULE 1: STRATEGIC CHANNEL ARCHITECTURE & BUDGET ALLOCATION
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

**A. Channel-by-Channel Role Definition**

Assign each channel a specific job in the revenue engine:

LinkedIn Ads (Recommended: 35–45% of budget for most B2B companies):
- Primary role: Professional identity targeting — the only channel where you can target by job title + company size + seniority + skills simultaneously
- Best for: Decision-maker awareness, content offer distribution, retargeting buying committee members
- Campaign types: Thought Leadership Ads (native LinkedIn posts boosted to targeted audiences), Document Ads (gated content), Message Ads (InMail for high-value accounts), Conversation Ads (multi-branch nurture sequences), Video Ads (executive testimonials, product explainers)
- Benchmark CPL range: $180–$380 for qualified B2B leads; $95–$180 for content downloads
- Audience size guardrail: Minimum 50,000 matched audience members for stable delivery; 300,000+ for awareness campaigns

Google Paid Search (Recommended: 20–30% of budget):
- Primary role: Intent interception — capture buyers actively researching solutions in your category
- Best for: Bottom-funnel high-intent queries ("best [category] software," "[competitor] alternative," "[your brand] demo"), comparison queries, feature-specific searches
- Campaign architecture: Brand defense (own your name), Category capture (intent keywords), Competitor displacement (terms targeting competitor brand searches, carefully worded), Problem-aware queries ("how to [solve the problem your product solves]")
- Bidding strategy: Target CPA (tCPA) for conversion campaigns once you have 30+ conversions in 30 days; Maximize Conversions for launch phase
- Quality Score optimization: Landing page relevance, expected CTR, ad relevance — minimum target QS of 7+

Google Display & YouTube (Recommended: 10–15% of budget):
- Primary role: Awareness and retargeting — reach ICP audiences at scale and re-engage website visitors
- Display targeting: Customer Match (upload CRM contact list), Similar Audiences, In-Market audiences (B2B software buyers), Affinity + contextual overlays
- YouTube: 15-second non-skippable for brand awareness, 30-second skippable for storytelling, TrueView for Action for direct response
- Programmatic Display via DV360 for brand-safe premium inventory with ABM audience overlays

Programmatic ABM Platforms — Demandbase / 6sense / RollWorks (Recommended: 15–20% of budget for companies with ICP account lists):
- Primary role: Named account saturation — serve coordinated ads to all known contacts at target accounts simultaneously
- Account-level targeting: Upload tiered account list, platform matches to IP addresses and cookied profiles across the web
- Intent data activation: 6sense/Bombora intent scores trigger increased bid intensity for accounts showing active research behavior
- Cross-channel orchestration: Coordinate ad exposure timing with SDR outbound sequences — accounts see ads the week before/after first SDR touch
- Reporting: Account-level engagement scoring, account pipeline influence, account-level reach and frequency metrics

Meta/Instagram (Recommended: 0–15% depending on ICP — higher for SMB-focused products, lower for enterprise):
- Primary role: Retargeting and lookalike expansion — cost-efficient reach for known audiences
- Only viable for B2B when: ACV < $30K (SMB/mid-market focus), or when retargeting warm audiences (website visitors, email list, existing customers for expansion)
- Meta advantage: Lowest CPM of any channel for retargeting; strong lookalike audiences from customer email lists

**B. Budget Allocation Framework**

Apply the Demand Waterfall Budget Model — allocate backwards from pipeline targets:

Monthly Pipeline Target ÷ Average Deal Size = Opportunities Needed
Opportunities Needed ÷ MQL-to-Opportunity Rate = MQLs Needed  
MQLs Needed × Blended Target CPL = Required Budget

Distribute budget across funnel stages:
- TOFU (Awareness/Education): 20–25% — optimized for reach, frequency, CPM
- MOFU (Consideration/Lead Gen): 50–55% — optimized for CPL and lead quality score
- BOFU (Decision/Demo): 20–25% — optimized for CPA (demo booked, trial started)
- Always-On Retargeting: 10% allocated across channels — non-negotiable floor

**A. Audience Tier Definitions**

Build a tiered audience architecture across all channels:

Tier 1 — High Intent (BOFU): Most valuable, smallest audiences, highest CPM acceptable
- CRM contacts: Marketing-qualified, sales-accepted, opportunities not yet closed
- Website visitors: Pricing page, demo page, competitor comparison pages (last 30 days)
- Engaged content consumers: Downloaded 2+ content assets, watched >50% of product video
- Account-level: 6sense/Demandbase accounts with Active or Decision-stage intent score

Tier 2 — Mid-Funnel Warm (MOFU): Moderate CPM ceiling, strong message-to-stage alignment
- Website visitors: Product pages, blog posts, resource pages (31–90 days)
- Email subscribers: Non-MQL active subscribers
- LinkedIn Page engagers (last 180 days)
- Video viewers (25–75% completion on paid video ads)

Tier 3 — Cold ICP (TOFU): Broadest reach, lowest CPM priority, awareness-stage messaging
- LinkedIn: Job title + seniority + company size + industry targeting
- Google: Customer Match lookalikes, In-Market audiences for business software
- Programmatic: Named account list from Tier 1 + 2 account universe (named accounts not yet engaged)

**B. Audience Exclusions (Non-Negotiable Budget Protection)**

Always exclude:
- Current customers (upload suppression list from CRM monthly)
- Competitors (upload competitor company list — especially for LinkedIn)
- Job titles not in ICP buying committee (e.g., individual contributors when targeting Director+ only)
- Churned accounts (within 12 months of churn — let Customer Success handle reactivation separately)
- Employees (LinkedIn: exclude your own company followers from cold prospecting campaigns)

**C. Buying Committee Coverage Strategy**

B2B deals involve 6–10 decision-makers. Design audience stacks to reach all roles:
- Primary Buyer (e.g., VP Revenue Operations): Full-funnel campaign exposure
- Economic Buyer (e.g., CFO): ROI/financial-impact-focused creative, selective LinkedIn Message Ads
- Champion (e.g., Director of RevOps): Technical depth content, product-focused creative
- Influencer (e.g., VP Sales who uses product daily): Social proof and peer testimonial creative

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

**A. Message Architecture by Funnel Stage**

TOFU creative principles:
- Lead with a counterintuitive insight or data point that challenges conventional wisdom
- Avoid product mentions — educate on the problem, not your solution
- Hook formula: "[Industry-specific pain] is [getting worse/misunderstood] because [unexpected reason]"
- Example hook: "73% of revenue forecasts miss by more than 15% — and it's not a CRM data problem"

MOFU creative principles:
- Lead with proof of transformation (customer outcome, benchmark data, ROI figure)
- Offer content that helps them solve the problem without requiring your product (build trust)
- Hook formula: "How [company like theirs] achieved [specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]"
- Offer types ranked by conversion performance: ROI calculator > benchmark report > webinar > template > ebook

BOFU creative principles:
- Direct response — every ad must have one clear CTA (Book Demo / Start Free Trial / Get Pricing)
- Lead with risk-removal messaging (free trial, no credit card, 30-day money-back, setup in 48 hours)
- Use social proof at maximum density: customer logos, G2 badges, review quotes, case study stats
- Urgency triggers: limited cohort, end-of-quarter pricing, implementation timeline commitments

**B. Creative Testing Matrix (90-Day Roadmap)**

Month 1 — Hook Testing: Hold format and CTA constant, test 4–6 different opening lines/value propositions
Month 2 — Format Testing: Hold best hook constant, test image vs. video vs. carousel vs. document ads
Month 3 — Offer Testing: Hold best hook + format, test 3 different CTAs/offers (Demo vs. Assessment vs. Report)

Testing rules:
- Minimum 200 clicks per variant before declaring a winner
- Test one variable at a time — never change hook AND format simultaneously
- Kill underperformers at 150% of target CPA without improvement
- Refresh creative every 6–8 weeks regardless of performance — B2B audiences are small and frequency fatigue is real

**C. Ad Format Performance Benchmarks by Channel**

LinkedIn Ads (B2B benchmarks):
- Single Image: CTR 0.4–0.8%, CPL $150–$280 for gated content
- Carousel: CTR 0.3–0.6%, higher brand recall, good for feature storytelling
- Video (15s): View rate 15–25%, CPV $0.12–$0.35, strong for brand awareness
- Document Ad: CTR 1.2–2.8% (highest of all formats — native reading experience)
- Thought Leadership Ads (boosted employee/exec posts): CTR 0.8–2.5%, lowest CPL when organic post already has engagement

Google Search:
- Responsive Search Ads: 3–5% CTR for branded, 1–3% for non-branded
- Performance Max: Use only for retargeting and customer match audiences — not cold prospecting for B2B (poor targeting control)

**A. Landing Page Type by Campaign Objective**

Content Download (MOFU):
- Above fold: Headline (outcome-focused, not topic-focused) + 3 bullet proof points + form
- Form length: 4–6 fields maximum (name, email, company, role, phone optional)
- Social proof: Customer logos + 1 quote from similar-role testimonial
- Expected conversion rate benchmark: 18–35% (visitors to form fills)

Demo Request (BOFU):
- Hero: Specific outcome claim + "See it in 20 minutes" framing
- Evidence: 3 customer logos in ICP industry + G2 rating badge + 1 ROI stat
- Form: Name, work email, company, role, phone (all required — demo is high-value, tolerate form friction)
- Calendly embed below fold as alternative conversion path (reduces form anxiety)
- Expected conversion rate benchmark: 8–18%

Personalized Account Landing Pages (ABM BOFU):
- Dynamic content showing account name, industry-specific case study, and relevant use case
- Built in Demandbase Personalization, Mutiny, or HubSpot Smart Content
- Expected conversion rate lift vs. generic page: 35–65% improvement

**A. Attribution Model Selection Framework**

For B2B paid media with 90–180 day sales cycles, use multi-touch attribution:

Recommended: W-Shaped (or custom data-driven if volume allows)
- First touch: 30% credit — which channel created awareness
- Lead creation touch: 30% credit — which campaign converted the lead
- Opportunity creation touch: 30% credit — which campaign triggered sales engagement
- All middle touches: 10% credit shared — supporting influence

Why not last-touch only: Severely under-credits awareness and education campaigns, causes budget to collapse into BOFU only, destroys funnel health over 18–24 months.

Why not first-touch only: Blindly rewards brand awareness with no pipeline accountability.

**B. Metrics Hierarchy (Report This Order to Leadership)**

Tier 1 — Revenue Metrics (Monthly/Quarterly to CMO, CFO, Board):
- Pipeline Sourced by Paid Channel ($): Opportunities where paid was first/primary touch
- Pipeline Influenced by Paid Channel ($): All open opps where paid touched at least one contact
- Closed Won Revenue Sourced by Paid ($): Trailing 12-month closed revenue attributable to paid
- Cost Per Pipeline Dollar: Total paid spend ÷ pipeline sourced (target: <$0.15 per $1 of pipeline)

Tier 2 — Program Health Metrics (Weekly to Demand Gen Team):
- MQL Volume by Channel and Campaign
- MQL-to-SQL Conversion Rate (signals lead quality — if <35%, something is wrong with targeting or offer)
- Cost Per MQL by channel (blended target: varies by ACV; rough rule: CPL < 10% of ACV)
- Cost Per SQL
- Cost Per Opportunity Created

Tier 3 — Campaign Optimization Metrics (Daily/Weekly — demand gen team only):
- CTR, CPC, CPM by ad set
- Landing page conversion rate
- Form completion rate
- Video view rate / completion rate
- LinkedIn Lead Gen Form fill rate (benchmark: 11–14% for quality form placements)

**C. Tech Stack for Revenue Attribution**

Minimum viable attribution stack:
- UTM parameter taxonomy: utm_source / utm_medium / utm_campaign / utm_content / utm_term — apply to every paid URL without exception
- CRM integration: All leads must flow into Salesforce/HubSpot with channel/campaign stamp intact
- MAP integration: Marketo/HubSpot Marketing — tracks multi-touch across email, paid, organic before CRM handoff
- LinkedIn Insight Tag + Google Ads Tag + Meta Pixel — installed with consent compliance (GDPR/CCPA)

Advanced attribution for $500K+ paid programs:
- Bizible (Adobe Marketo Measure) or Full Circle Insights: Multi-touch attribution inside Salesforce
- 6sense or Demandbase: Account-level journey analytics and pipeline influence reporting
- Northbeam or Triple Whale (if significant DTC overlap): Cross-channel revenue attribution

**A. Weekly Budget Reallocation Protocol**

Review every Monday:
1. Identify campaigns spending >120% of weekly budget with CPL <80% of target → increase budget 20%
2. Identify campaigns with CPL >150% of target for 2 consecutive weeks → reduce budget 30% or pause
3. Reallocate freed budget to top 2 performers first before testing new campaigns
4. Maintain minimum spend on all retargeting campaigns regardless of CPL — retargeting converts later in cycle

**B. Seasonality and Demand Surge Playbook**

Pre-event demand surges (conferences, product launches):
- Increase BOFU/MOFU spend 30–50% in the 2 weeks before and 1 week after major industry events
- Activate LinkedIn Event Ads tied to conference content
- Create conference-specific landing pages ("Are you going to [Event]? Book a meeting with our team")

Quarter-end acceleration:
- Shift 15% of TOFU budget to BOFU in weeks 11–13 of each quarter
- Increase retargeting budgets — these audiences are warmest and most ready to convert
- Launch "end-of-quarter offer" campaigns with time-boxed incentives for demo bookings

## Success Metrics

**Campaign Quality Indicators:**
- Blended CPL below 10% of ACV within 90 days (e.g., <$9,500 CPL for $95K ACV product)
- LinkedIn Lead Gen Form fill rate > 10% on Document Ads and Content Offer campaigns
- Google Search CTR > 3% for branded and > 1.5% for category keywords
- Landing page conversion rate > 20% for content offers and > 10% for demo requests
- MQL-to-SQL conversion rate > 35% (below this indicates targeting or offer quality problem, not volume problem)

**Program Health Indicators:**
- No single channel representing > 50% of total pipeline contribution (diversification prevents single-channel collapse)
- Retargeting CPL < 40% of cold prospecting CPL (warm audiences should convert significantly cheaper)
- Cost per pipeline dollar < $0.15 (every $1 in paid media budget generating > $6.67 in pipeline)
- Month-over-month pipeline growth > 15% while maintaining or reducing CPL (scale efficiency improving)

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot / Salesforce:**
- Create custom campaign source fields that persist through the entire lead lifecycle — paid campaign name, ad set, ad creative should be visible on every deal record
- Build a "Paid Pipeline Dashboard" in Salesforce showing pipeline by UTM campaign — share with CMO and CFO monthly
- Set up automatic lead scoring adjustments: +20 points for BOFU paid form fills, +10 for MOFU content downloads, +5 for TOFU ad engagers

**LinkedIn Campaign Manager:**
- Sync LinkedIn Matched Audiences directly with HubSpot/Salesforce lists using LinkedIn's native CRM sync or a tool like Zapier, Dreamdata, or Metadata.io
- Enable LinkedIn Insight Tag on all web properties — provides demographic overlay on all organic and paid website traffic
- Use LinkedIn's Revenue Attribution Report to see which campaigns influenced closed-won opportunities (requires >10 closed deals for statistical validity)

**Google Ads:**
- Import Salesforce or HubSpot offline conversion events back into Google Ads (offline conversion import) — teach Google's Smart Bidding to optimize for MQL/SQL creation, not just form fills

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CPL is on target but MQL-to-SQL rate is below 25% — leads aren't converting to pipeline.**
Fix: The targeting is generating quantity but not quality. Audit the audience: are you reaching actual decision-makers or individual contributors? Check the form fields — add "company size" and "role/seniority" qualifiers. Review the offer — if a content piece converts well but leads don't fit ICP, the content is attracting the wrong audience. Add a "qualifying question" to the form (e.g., "How many sales reps does your team have?") to self-select quality over volume.

**Problem: LinkedIn CPL keeps rising month-over-month despite no changes to campaigns.**
Fix: Audience fatigue. LinkedIn audiences are small — a 200,000-person audience can become saturated at 8–10 frequency exposures per month. Check your campaign frequency report: if average frequency exceeds 4x per month for the same audience, creative fatigue is likely. Solutions: rotate in 2–3 new creative assets, expand the audience by loosening 1 targeting dimension, or introduce a new audience segment entirely. Also check if LinkedIn's algorithm has shifted — CPM inflation on LinkedIn has been 15–25% YoY since 2022.

**Problem: Google Search converting at great CPL but LinkedIn showing zero pipeline influence in CRM.**
Fix: Attribution is broken, not the channel. Check that LinkedIn's Insight Tag is firing on all pages including post-conversion thank-you pages. Verify UTM parameters are persisting through your form submission and being captured in the CRM. LinkedIn's native conversion tracking uses a 30-day view-through and 7-day click window — make sure your CRM attribution window matches. B2B buyers often see a LinkedIn ad, don't click, then search Google days later — LinkedIn gets no click credit but influenced the search. Consider adding a "How did you first hear about us?" form field to capture this dark attribution.
