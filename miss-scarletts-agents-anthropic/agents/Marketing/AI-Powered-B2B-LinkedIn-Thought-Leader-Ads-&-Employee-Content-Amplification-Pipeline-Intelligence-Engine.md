## Integration Tips

- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Thought Leader Ads require the post author to grant your company page permission to sponsor their posts. Set this up in Campaign Manager → Creatives → Sponsored Content permissions before launch. Authors must have "Super Admin" or "Content Admin" access to approve.
- **HubSpot:** Add UTM parameters to all TL Ad landing page links (utm_source=linkedin, utm_medium=paid_social, utm_campaign=tl_ads, utm_content=[creator_name]_[post_topic]). HubSpot automatically ingests these into contact records — build a HubSpot list of TL Ads-touched contacts and review conversion rates vs. non-touched.
- **Salesforce:** Create a custom field "LinkedIn TL Ads Influenced" on the Opportunity object. Use HubSpot-Salesforce sync or a Zapier workflow to flag opportunities where the contact engaged with a TL Ad before the opportunity was created. Review in Salesforce reports quarterly.
- **LinkedIn Insight Tag:** Deploy on all key pages (website, demo request, content download confirmation) to enable LinkedIn conversion tracking. Required for CPL-optimized campaigns and for building website visitor retargeting audiences.
- **Slack workflow automation:** Build a Monday morning Slack alert (via Zapier or Make) that pulls the previous week's top 5 organic LinkedIn posts from tracked creators (using LinkedIn Sharing API or manual monitoring sheet) and sends to the performance marketing manager for weekly content review.
- **6sense / Demandbase:** If you have an ABM platform, export your engaged account list weekly and upload to LinkedIn Matched Audiences (Company list CSV) to create account-level targeting for your BOFU TL Ads campaigns. Refresh the audience weekly for accounts in Active Buying Stage.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Creator won't give permission to sponsor their posts**
Solution: Frame it as "we want to pay to extend the reach of content you already worked hard to create — you keep full ownership, we just cover the distribution cost." Offer to show them a preview before any ad goes live. Start with the CEO or a brand ambassador who's already bought in before expanding to the broader team. Never amplify without explicit per-post permission.

**Problem: TL Ads CTR is lower than standard Sponsored Content**
Solution: This usually means you're amplifying the wrong content type. Promotional posts, product announcements, and company-centric content consistently underperform personal narratives, data insights, and how-to frameworks. Re-run the AMPLIFY content scoring rubric on your current ads — any post with an Authenticity Score below 6 should be paused immediately. The most common mistake is amplifying a post the company loves rather than the post the audience engages with.

**Problem: Pipeline attribution is showing zero TL Ads influence despite strong click volume**
Solution: Check UTM parameter passthrough — many LinkedIn ads drop UTMs on mobile clicks. Add the LinkedIn Insight Tag to all thank-you / confirmation pages and use LinkedIn's native conversion tracking as your primary attribution source. Cross-reference with Salesforce by comparing deal close rates for contacts who have a LinkedIn interaction record (visible in HubSpot contact timeline) vs. those who don't. Also check that your CRM campaign attribution window (typically 30-90 days) matches your average sales cycle length.
