## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Open rates declining month-over-month**
Root causes (in order of likelihood):
1. Deliverability degradation — check sender reputation in Google Postmaster Tools and Microsoft SNDS. If spam rate >0.05%, pause promotional sends, run re-engagement, suppress non-engagers.
2. Subject line fatigue — you're using the same formula. A/B test curiosity-gap vs. direct-benefit vs. question formats. Benchmark: 25-40% open rate indicates strong deliverability + relevance.
3. List aging — if growth has slowed, your list skews older (more disengaged). Relaunch lead magnet acquisition to inject fresh subscribers.
4. Cadence mismatch — sending too frequently. Try reducing frequency 30% for your lowest-engaged segment and measure impact.

**Problem: High open rates but no conversions**
Diagnosis: The email is doing its job (compelling subject) but the content doesn't match buyer stage.
Fix: Audit your email-to-CTA alignment. TOFU subscribers shouldn't be getting BOFU offers. Check segmentation tags — ensure welcome sequence has fired and subscribers are in the correct nurture stream. Run a segmentation fork email immediately to re-sort your list.

**Problem: Strong initial engagement but list growth has plateaued**
Root cause: You're not acquiring new subscribers at the rate you need; existing acquisition infrastructure is tapped.
Fix: (1) Launch a referral program — ask existing subscribers to share with one colleague in exchange for exclusive content. Estimated lift: +15-30% growth rate. (2) Audit your top-traffic pages — if opt-in conversion rate on your top 5 organic pages is under 1.5%, you have a major growth lever waiting. Add content upgrades specific to each page's topic. (3) Launch a LinkedIn newsletter parallel to email list — cross-promote with opt-in gate to convert LinkedIn followers to email subscribers.
