## Success Metrics

**Sequence-level targets (B2B SaaS, 60-90 day sales cycle):**
- Email 1 open rate: ≥ 42% (warm lead, highly relevant send)
- Email 2-4 open rates: ≥ 28% (normal drop-off is acceptable)
- Email 5-6 open rates: ≥ 35% (executive sender lifts opens)
- Click-to-open rate: ≥ 6% across sequence
- MQL-to-demo conversion: ≥ 8% of leads entering sequence
- Unsubscribe rate: < 0.5% per email (above 1% means subject lines are misleading or list is wrong)
- Sales-accepted leads from sequence: track separately; target ≥ 60% SAL rate on booked demos

**Content quality signals:**
- At least 1 reply to the sequence (often email 5 or 6 generates replies even without clicking)
- Forward rate on email 1 or 3 (data-heavy emails get forwarded to colleagues)

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot Workflows:**
- Create a Workflow triggered by Contact Property "Lead Source = [content asset]" AND "Lifecycle Stage = MQL"
- Use HubSpot's "Branch" step on email open/click to implement the behavioral branching logic
- Map each email's dynamic tokens to Contact Properties: `{{contact.firstname}}`, `{{contact.company}}`, `{{contact.jobtitle}}`
- Set the goal as "Meeting Booked = true" to auto-exit leads who convert and trigger Sales notification

**Marketo Engagement Programs:**
- Build as a Nurture Program with separate Streams per engagement level (Cold / Warm / Hot)
- Use Smart Campaign triggers to move leads between streams based on email engagement scores
- Connect to Salesforce via native sync — create a Task for SDR when lead hits "Hot" stream

**Outreach / Salesloft:**
- Emails 5-6 (executive touch) work better when sent from the AE's mailbox via Outreach, not your marketing ESP — use a webhook from HubSpot to trigger an Outreach sequence step
- Track email 5+6 reply rates separately in Outreach; these feed SDR call prioritization

**Google Sheets (lightweight teams):**
- Export HubSpot/Mailchimp sequence performance daily via Zapier → Google Sheets
- Build a simple cohort table: Week entered sequence → emails sent → opens → clicks → demos booked
- Use this to calculate your sequence's contribution to monthly pipeline

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Open rates are high but no one is clicking the CTA**
- The email body is delivering value but the CTA is asking for too large a commitment too soon. Diagnose by checking which email in the sequence has the highest open-to-click gap. Fix: replace a "Book a Demo" CTA in emails 1-3 with a lower-friction ask (watch a 90-second video, download a one-pager, run a self-assessment). Reserve demo CTAs for emails 4-6.

**Issue: High unsubscribe rate on email 2 or 3 (above 1%)**
- The sequence is losing relevance fast. Most common cause: the lead magnet topic doesn't connect logically to the product pitch. Fix: audit the "bridge" between what the lead downloaded and what your first 3 emails offer. Each email should feel like a natural extension of why they downloaded the report — not a pivot to a product pitch. Lead with insight and education for the first 3 emails before introducing any product CTA.

**Issue: Sequence producing demos but a low percentage are sales-accepted**
- The sequence is attracting the wrong segment or creating unrealistic expectations about the product. Fix: add a qualification layer before booking. In email 4 or 5, include a brief "Is this a fit?" self-qualification prompt (e.g., "We work best with teams of X or companies using Y — if that's you, here's how to get started"). This pre-qualifies intent and sets accurate expectations before the demo, improving SAL rate dramatically.
