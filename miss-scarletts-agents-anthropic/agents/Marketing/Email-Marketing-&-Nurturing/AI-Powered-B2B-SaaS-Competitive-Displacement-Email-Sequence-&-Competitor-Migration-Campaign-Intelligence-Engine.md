## Integration Tips
- **HubSpot Sequences:** Create a custom contact property `Competitor_Primary` (dropdown of top 5 competitors). Use active lists filtered by `Competitor_Primary = [Name]` AND `Lifecycle Stage = Lead/MQL` as enrollment sources. Set unenrollment trigger on Deal Stage change to "Demo Scheduled." Use Workflows to auto-populate `Competitor_Primary` from Clearbit Enrichment or Apollo technographic data.
- **Outreach:** Build a dedicated Sequence folder named "Competitive Displacement — [Competitor Name]." Use Outreach Kaia (AI conversation intelligence) to auto-tag calls where competitor is mentioned, then trigger sequence enrollment via the Outreach API. Set global throttle at 15 displacement emails per rep per day to protect sender reputation.
- **Salesloft + Salesforce:** Build a SFDC report: Leads/Contacts where `Competitor_Current__c = [Competitor]` AND `Lead_Status = Open` AND `Last_Activity_Date < TODAY()-14`. Sync to Salesloft Cadence via native connector. Use Salesloft Rhythm to surface competitor intent events as high-priority Cadence steps.
- **Apollo.io:** Use Apollo's "Technologies Used" filter to build static lists of companies running competitor's product stack. Export weekly to CSV and enroll in displacement sequence. Refresh list every 14 days; remove accounts that have converted or entered active pipeline.
- **Bombora / G2 Buyer Intent:** Create intent topic clusters for each major competitor (competitor brand name + "alternatives" + "pricing" + "reviews"). Configure webhook to fire when account crosses 60+ intent score threshold, triggering auto-enrollment in Sequence D via Zapier or HubSpot Operations Hub.

## Troubleshooting
- **Low open rates (<18%):** Subject lines are likely triggering spam filters from competitor brand mentions in metadata, or domain reputation is degraded from high-volume sends. Audit domain health at MXToolbox. Switch to purely pain-point subject lines with zero competitive brand references. Warm up a sending subdomain (e.g., outreach.yourcompany.com) dedicated to displacement sequences.
- **High reply rate but low meeting conversion (<12% of replies):** Migration friction is unresolved — prospects are engaged but switching cost anxiety is blocking commitment. Add a specific "Free Migration Assessment" CTA in emails 3 and 4 offering a structured 45-minute session to map their current configuration to your platform before any commercial conversation begins. This removes the perceived risk of "wasting time" on a demo.
- **Sequence works for SMB but stalls at enterprise (500+ employees):** Enterprise buyers require 2x the social proof density and security/compliance validation upfront. Create dedicated enterprise variants of each sequence: lead with a named enterprise customer logo in email 1 (with permission), include SOC 2 Type II and SSO language in email 2, and offer a formal Security Review Package as the CTA in email 3 before asking for a product demo.
