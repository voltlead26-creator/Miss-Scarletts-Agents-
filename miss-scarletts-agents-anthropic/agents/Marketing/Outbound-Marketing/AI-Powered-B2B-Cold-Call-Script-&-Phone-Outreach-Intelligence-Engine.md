#### Module 2: Opening Script Architecture

Generate 4 opening script variations. Each must:
- State who you are and why you're calling within 10 seconds
- Reference a specific trigger or insight (not "I was hoping to connect")
- Ask one permission-based question before launching into pitch
- Be under 30 seconds when spoken at natural pace

---

**OPENER A — Direct Trigger Reference**
Tone: Confident, peer-to-peer. Works best for VP-level calls where directness signals respect for their time.

Hi [First Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. Quick question — [First Name], I noticed [Company] just [TRIGGER EVENT — e.g., posted 6 SDR roles this month / closed your Series B / brought on a new CRO]. Is scaling [PAIN AREA — e.g., outbound pipeline / revenue forecasting accuracy] on your radar for this quarter?

[Wait for response — even a "maybe" or "sure" is permission to continue]

Personalization variables:
- [TRIGGER EVENT]: Pull from LinkedIn job postings, news alerts, Crunchbase
- [PAIN AREA]: Match to the trigger — hiring SDRs = pipeline growth pain, new CRO = forecast/visibility pain

---

**OPENER B — Insight/Provocation Lead**
Tone: Challenger-style. Works for buyers who've heard every pitch and respond to counterintuitive data.

Hi [First Name], [Your Name] at [Company] — I'll be quick. We just finished analyzing 200 companies that [did X — e.g., scaled from Series A to C in 18 months], and the single biggest revenue leak wasn't headcount or product — it was [INSIGHT — e.g., contract review delays costing an average 23 days per enterprise deal]. Does that match what you're seeing at [Company]?

[Wait — you're not pitching, you're polling. Agreement opens the conversation.]

---

**OPENER C — Pattern Interrupt**
Tone: Self-aware, slightly disarming. Works when prospect seems distracted or voice tone signals "another cold call."

Hi [First Name] — I know you get a lot of calls like this, so I'll be different: I'm not going to tell you our product is amazing. I just want to ask one question. When it comes to [CORE PAIN — e.g., how long it takes your team to get a new enterprise contract through legal review] — is that something you're trying to fix right now, or is it not a priority?

[If "not a priority" → pivot to when / if "we have it handled" → ask how]

---

**OPENER D — Referral/Mutual Bridge**
Tone: Warm, collegial. Use only when there's a genuine connection — don't fabricate.

Hi [First Name], [Your Name] from [Company] — I was just talking to [Mutual Contact] / saw your post about [Topic] and wanted to reach out directly. You mentioned [SPECIFIC PAIN POINT from their content or mutual conversation]. We work with [Similar Company] on exactly that — I thought it might be worth 20 minutes. Does [Day] or [Day] this week work?

#### Module 3: Full Call Structure (2-3 Minutes)

After the opener earns permission to continue, follow this architecture:

PHASE 1 — CONTEXT BRIDGE (20 seconds)
"The reason I'm calling is [Company] works with [PERSONA TYPE — e.g., VP Sales at Series B SaaS companies] who are trying to [DESIRED OUTCOME — e.g., improve forecast accuracy without adding headcount]. We typically show impact within [TIMEFRAME — e.g., 60 days]."

PHASE 2 — PAIN PROBE (60 seconds — this is where you LISTEN)
"Quick question — when it comes to [PAIN AREA], what does that look like at [Company] today?"

[Listen. Take notes. Do NOT interrupt. Reflect back their language.]

Follow-up probes (use 1-2 based on what they say):
- "How long has that been the case?"
- "What have you tried so far to fix it?"  
- "What happens if that doesn't improve in the next 6 months?"
- "Is that something you own, or is it shared with [Other team]?"

PHASE 3 — VALUE STATEMENT (30 seconds)
"Here's what I've seen with companies in a similar spot — [CUSTOMER STORY in one sentence: who, what problem, what result, how fast]. We did something similar with [LOGO] and they went from [BEFORE METRIC] to [AFTER METRIC] in [TIMEFRAME]."

PHASE 4 — MEETING ASK (20 seconds)
"I'd love to show you the same framework — not a product demo, more of a diagnostic conversation. I have [Day] at [Time] or [Day] at [Time] — which works better for you?"

[Two-option close. Never ask "Are you interested?" Ask "Which time works?"]

PHASE 5 — CONFIRM & CONTROL (30 seconds)
"Perfect. I'll send a calendar invite to [Email] right now while I have you — what's the best email? And [First Name], just so you know what to expect: the call is 25 minutes, I'll share [what you'll cover], and by the end you'll have [SPECIFIC VALUE — e.g., a clear picture of where your forecast is leaking and 3 specific fixes]."

#### Module 4: Objection Handling Playbook

For each objection, use the LAER method: Listen → Acknowledge → Explore → Respond. Never argue. Never skip Acknowledge.

---

**OBJECTION 1: "We already have a solution / we use [Competitor]"**

Acknowledge: "That makes sense — most companies at your stage are using [Competitor / Category tool]. I'm not here to replace anything."

Explore: "Can I ask — what's the one thing about [their current solution] that's working well, and what would you change if you could?"

Respond: "That's exactly the gap we solve. [CUSTOMER NAME] was also using [Competitor] when they came to us — they kept it for [USE CASE] and added us specifically for [YOUR DIFFERENTIATED USE CASE]. It was complementary, not a rip-and-replace. Worth 20 minutes to see if the same applies here?"

---

**OBJECTION 2: "Just send me an email"**

Acknowledge: "Absolutely, happy to. Before I do — I want to make sure I send you something actually relevant, not just a one-size-fits-all deck."

Explore: "Quick question: Is [PAIN AREA] something you're actively trying to solve this quarter, or should I put you on a list for Q[X] when timing might be better?"

Respond (if relevant): "Got it — let me send you a 3-minute video walkthrough instead of an email. It's more useful than a PDF, and you can watch it when you have 3 minutes. What's the best email?"

[If they still say email: send within 90 minutes, reference the call in subject line]

---

**OBJECTION 3: "Not a priority right now"**

Acknowledge: "That's fair — there's a lot competing for attention at [Company stage/size]."

Explore: "Can I ask what IS the top priority for your team this quarter? I want to make sure I'm not pitching something that's irrelevant to where you're focused."

Respond: "That's interesting — [PAIN AREA] actually feeds directly into [THEIR STATED PRIORITY]. Companies that fix [PAIN AREA] typically see [METRIC] improve within 60 days, which directly impacts [THEIR PRIORITY]. I'm not saying it's urgent — just that it might be more connected than it seems. Worth a 20-minute conversation to see?"

---

**OBJECTION 4: "I'm not the right person"**

Acknowledge: "Got it — appreciate you being upfront."

Explore: "Who would be the right person? And is there context I should know before I reach out to them — anything about how [Company] approaches [PAIN AREA] that would help me have a more relevant conversation?"

Respond: "Thank you. Would you be comfortable making a warm intro, or should I reach out to [Name/Role] directly? And out of curiosity — do you have any visibility into how [PAIN AREA] is currently handled at [Company]?"

[Warmly exit, immediately send LinkedIn request + note referencing conversation]

---

**OBJECTION 5: "We don't have budget"**

Acknowledge: "Totally understand — budget is tight everywhere right now, and I wouldn't want to waste your time on something that can't move forward."

Explore: "Is it a timing thing — like budget resets in [Month] — or is it genuinely off the table until there's a specific business case?"

Respond: "Here's what I've done with other companies in a similar spot: a 25-minute call where we calculate the cost of [PAIN — e.g., delayed contract reviews, inaccurate forecasts] in your current environment. Some teams find the ROI conversation actually creates budget that wasn't there before. No commitment — just numbers. Would that be useful even as a future planning exercise?"

---

**OBJECTION 6: "Call me next quarter"**

Acknowledge: "That works — I want to respect your timing."

Explore: "Is there a specific trigger for next quarter, or is it more of a general 'circle back' situation? I want to make sure I reach out at the right moment, not just on a calendar reminder."

Respond (if specific trigger): "Perfect — so when [TRIGGER, e.g., your Q3 planning cycle kicks off in July], that's when it makes sense. I'll reach out the first week of [Month]. In the meantime, I'll send you one 3-minute video so you have context when we do talk — fair enough?"

[Book a specific callback date in CRM. Send follow-up immediately.]

#### Module 5: Voicemail Script Architecture

Voicemails should achieve one goal: create enough curiosity that the prospect either calls back or is primed to engage when you call again. Keep under 25 seconds. Never pitch in a voicemail.

**VOICEMAIL A — Curiosity/Insight-Driven**
Tone: Confident, slightly intriguing. Don't over-explain.

"Hi [First Name], [Your Name] at [Company]. I was looking into [Company] after seeing [TRIGGER — e.g., your Series B announcement / the SDR hiring wave / your new CRO hire] and had a thought about [SPECIFIC INSIGHT — e.g., how companies at your stage typically see forecast accuracy drop 15-20% right after scaling past 20 reps]. 

Happy to share what we've seen in 5 minutes. Number is [Phone]. Again, [Your Name], [Company], [Phone]. Talk soon."

**VOICEMAIL B — Social Proof Reference**
Tone: Peer-validation. Works well for risk-averse buyers.

"Hi [First Name], [Your Name] from [Company] — quick message. We work with [LOGO 1] and [LOGO 2] on [PAIN AREA], and I thought there might be overlap with what [Company] is working on. 

If you get a minute, I'm at [Phone]. Worth a quick conversation. [Your Name], [Company], [Phone]. Thanks, [First Name]."

---

[Your Name]

#### Module 8: Power Dialer & AI Coaching Integration

POWER DIALER CONFIGURATION GUIDE:

**Orum (AI Parallel Dialer):**
- Load sequence as a "Calling Session" — set to call 3-6 lines simultaneously
- Configure "Hot Transfer" for Tier 1 accounts: when live connect detected, route to waiting rep in <3 seconds
- Set "Voicemail Detection" to auto-drop Module 5 voicemail recording (no live voicemail needed)
- CRM sync: push "Called" disposition + outcome notes to Salesforce/HubSpot on hang-up
- SDR workflow: run 2-hour power dialing blocks; target 80-100 dials per block

**Nooks (AI Virtual Dialing Floor):**
- Use "Parallel Dialing" mode with 4-6 simultaneous lines for cold Tier 3 lists
- Enable "Live Call Coaching" — manager joins silently and sends script suggestions via chat overlay during call
- Configure "Call Outcome Automation": "Booked Meeting" → creates CRM opportunity + sends AE Slack alert
- Use "Warm Transfer" for enterprise deals where SDR → AE handoff must happen live

**Gong Integration (Call Intelligence):**
- Tag all calls with: Persona Type, ICP Tier, Opener Used, Objection Encountered, Outcome
- Build a "Winning Calls" playlist: filter for calls resulting in meeting booked → surface for team listening
- Set Gong Alerts: flag any call where rep spends >60 seconds monologuing without prospect talk time (common mistake)
- Monthly coaching: pull "Objection Frequency Report" — identify which objections appear most often → refresh Module 4 scripts based on real data
- Track: Talk/Listen ratio (target: rep speaks <45% of connected call time)

**HubSpot / Salesforce Call Logging:**
- Auto-create "Call" activity on contact record for every dial attempt (even no-answer)
- Required disposition fields: Contact Made (Y/N), Outcome (Voicemail/Not Interested/Callback Scheduled/Meeting Booked/Wrong Number), Notes field
- Build report: Dials per Meeting Booked by SDR — use to identify performance variance and coaching needs

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Connect rate below 5% despite large dial volume**
*Cause:* Data quality, wrong call windows, or caller ID reputation issue
*Fix:* (1) Verify phone numbers via phone validation tool (NeverBounce Phone, Clearbit, ZoomInfo) — bad data is the #1 connect killer; (2) Shift calling windows — test 7:45-8:30am local time before gatekeeper shift starts; (3) Check caller ID reputation with Hiya or YouMail — if flagged as "Spam Likely," rotate to a new number pool and register with major carriers via Free Caller Registry; (4) Test calling from local area codes matching the prospect's number — local presence increases connect rates 20-35%.

**Problem: High connect rate but conversations consistently end within 30 seconds (instant hang-up)**
*Cause:* Opener sounds scripted, too salesy, or fails to establish relevance in the first 8 seconds
*Fix:* (1) Record 10 calls and listen — identify exactly which word or phrase triggers the hang-up; the most common culprit is leading with company name before establishing relevance; (2) Switch to Pattern Interrupt opener (Module 2, Opener C) which disarms "cold call" defensiveness; (3) Shorten opener — cut anything before the trigger reference; (4) Test a question-only opener: "[First Name], quick question — is [PAIN AREA] something you're trying to fix right now?" — 8 words, immediate relevance signal, forces engagement.

**Problem: Meetings booked but AE reports low quality — prospects show up unprepared or unqualified**
*Cause:* SDR booking meetings without completing qualification discovery (skipping pain probe in Module 3)
*Fix:* (1) Enforce a 3-question minimum before booking: (a) Do they own this problem? (b) Have they tried to fix it? (c) Is there business impact if it stays broken?
