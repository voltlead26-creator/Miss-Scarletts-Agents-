━━━ MODULE 2: SEARCH INTENT & KEYWORD QUALITY ANALYSIS ━━━

**Search Term Intent Classification:**
Categorize every significant query from the search term report into:
- *Transactional / Commercial Intent:* [e.g., "buy CRM software," "salesforce alternative pricing"] — should capture and convert
- *Informational / Research Intent:* [e.g., "what is CRM software," "how to manage sales pipeline"] — evaluate whether to keep or exclude

- *Irrelevant / Waste Queries:* [e.g., job seekers, students, wrong industry] — immediate negatives

**Negative Keyword Gap Report:**

**Match Type Audit:**
- Assess the current broad/phrase/exact distribution
- Identify where broad match is expanding into low-quality territory vs. where it's finding legitimate search demand
- Recommend match type adjustments by ad group with rationale

**Long-Tail Opportunity Analysis:**
- Identify high-intent, lower-competition long-tail queries in the search term report that deserve dedicated ad groups or keywords for better QS and lower CPCs
- Calculate estimated CPC reduction if these terms were moved to exact match targeting

**Quality Score Deep Dive:**
For the 20 highest-spend keywords:
- Diagnose each low QS keyword (below 6/10) using the three QS components:
  - *Expected CTR:* Below average = headline/description doesn't match searcher intent → rewrite ads
  - *Ad Relevance:* Below average = keyword doesn't fit ad group theme → restructure into tighter SKAGs or ad groups
  - *Landing Page Experience:* Below average = post-click content doesn't match keyword intent → fix landing page or route to better URL
- Estimate CPС reduction from QS improvement: +1 QS point typically = 8–15% CPC reduction
- Priority QS fix order: Rank by (monthly spend × QS gap × estimated CPC improvement)

━━━ MODULE 3: AUCTION INTELLIGENCE & COMPETITIVE POSITIONING ━━━

**Impression Share Loss Diagnosis:**

*Lost to Budget (critical: fix first):*
- Which campaigns are budget-constrained and missing impression volume?
- Estimated revenue missed due to budget caps: If campaign X spends $8,000/month with 35% lost IS (budget) at current CPA, estimated missed conversions = [X] at [$X revenue]
- Budget increase recommendation with expected incremental CPA impact

*Lost to Rank (fix with QS and bids):*
- Campaigns losing >20% IS to rank signal structural issues (low QS or insufficient bids)
- For Search campaigns: Is lost IS (rank) caused by low Ad Rank (QS × bid × context) or incorrect bid cap constraints?
- Recommended bid adjustments or QS improvements to recapture lost rank

**Auction Insights Competitive Analysis:**
For each major competitor appearing in auction insights:
- *Impression Share Gap:* If competitor has 72% IS vs. your 41%, what does it cost to close the gap?
- *Position Above Rate:* Competitors consistently appearing above you signals their bids or QS exceed yours — diagnose which
- *Overlap Rate:* High overlap means you're in direct head-to-head bidding; low overlap may indicate different keyword coverage
- *Top-of-Page Rate:* Competitors consistently at top of page for your branded terms are poaching intent — immediate response plan
- **Specific competitor displacement recommendations:** For the #1 competitor threatening your most valuable keywords, provide 3 tactical responses

**Ad Rank & Position Analysis:**
- Absolute top impression share vs. top impression share — are we appearing above organic results or below?
- CPC premium being paid for top position vs. 3rd–4th position — is the position premium delivering proportional conversion lift?
- Position bid adjustment recommendations by campaign type and conversion intent

━━━ MODULE 8: SMART BIDDING ALGORITHM HEALTH ASSESSMENT ━━━

**Bidding Strategy Fit Analysis:**
Diagnose whether each campaign's Smart Bidding strategy is properly configured for the data available:
- *Conversion volume thresholds:* Target CPA requires 30–50+ conversions/month per campaign to optimize effectively; below this threshold, maximize conversions (unconstrained) or manual CPC may outperform
- *Target CPA calibration:* Is the target CPA set within 20% of current achieved CPA? Targets set too aggressively cause under-delivery; targets too loose waste budget
- *Target ROAS calibration:* Similar — targets should be within 15–20% of current ROAS to allow algorithm flexibility
- *Learning period status:* Any campaigns in "Limited by bid strategy" or showing volatility from recent setting changes? Learning periods require 2–4 weeks of stable conversion data before optimization

**Conversion Signal Quality:**
The #1 factor in Smart Bidding performance is conversion signal quality:
- Are offline conversions (closed deals, SQLs) being imported? If not, Google optimizes for MQLs — which may not correlate with revenue
- Are micro-conversions (pricing page visits, demo page views) set as secondary conversions and excluded from Smart Bidding primary signals?
- Enhanced conversions status: If not enabled, estimate the % of conversions being missed due to cookie/tracking limitations (typically 15–25% in a post-iOS 14 world)
- Conversion lag: If average sales cycle is 60 days, are conversion windows set to capture these delayed conversions?

**Portfolio Bidding Strategy Opportunities:**
- For accounts with 5+ campaigns, identify where portfolio bid strategies (shared target CPA/ROAS across campaigns) would improve efficiency vs. individual campaign targets
- Particularly valuable for: Multiple non-brand Search campaigns targeting similar audiences, Shopping campaigns split by product category

## Troubleshooting
- **Problem:** Performance Max campaign shows strong CPA in Google Ads, but CRM pipeline from PMax-attributed leads is low quality — sales rejects most as non-ICP.
  **Solution:** PMax's CPA reporting measures whatever conversion actions are configured, including secondary micro-conversions (content downloads, chatbot interactions) if they're accidentally included in Smart Bidding signals. Audit your conversion action settings: only primary revenue-generating actions (demo requests, purchase completions) should be set as "Primary" and included in bidding. All others should be "Secondary" and excluded from bidding. Additionally, strengthen PMax audience signals — upload your CRM customer list (past closed-won accounts) as a customer match signal so the algorithm learns to find similar companies. If the problem persists after 60 days, test running a parallel Standard Search campaign for your highest-intent keywords and compare SQL rates.

- **Problem:** Smart Bidding (Target CPA) consistently underspends — campaigns are "Limited by bid strategy" and not delivering full budget.
  **Solution:** This signals the algorithm cannot find conversions at your target CPA at the available volume. Three causes and fixes: (1) Target CPA is set too low relative to achievable market CPAs — increase target by 20–30% for 4 weeks to exit learning; (2) Conversion volume is too low (<30/month/campaign) for Smart Bidding to function — consolidate campaigns or switch to Maximize Conversions without a CPA target until volume builds; (3) Audience or keyword pool is too narrow — expand match types or add audience segments to give the algorithm more signals. Use the Google Ads Bid Simulator to see the conversion volume available at various CPA targets before changing settings.

- **Problem:** CTR is strong (>5%) but conversion rate is under 1% — Google Ads is generating clicks but no pipeline.
  **Solution:** High CTR with low conversion rate almost always points to a post-click problem, not a campaign problem. Diagnose in this order: (a) Landing page speed — run Google PageSpeed Insights on your destination URLs; sub-3-second mobile load time is the single biggest conversion killer; (b) Message match — does the landing page headline directly mirror the ad headline and keyword that triggered the click? If ad says "HR Software for Mid-Market" but the page says "The #1 HR Platform for Any Business," there's intent mismatch that kills trust; (c) Offer friction — is the conversion action too demanding for the traffic temperature? Cold non-brand searchers rarely convert on a "Talk to Sales" CTA; offer a lower-friction gate like a product demo video, ROI calculator, or benchmark report; (d) Form fields — every additional field reduces conversion rate 10–15%; B2B SaaS forms should ask for email, company name, and phone only at the top-of-funnel stage.
