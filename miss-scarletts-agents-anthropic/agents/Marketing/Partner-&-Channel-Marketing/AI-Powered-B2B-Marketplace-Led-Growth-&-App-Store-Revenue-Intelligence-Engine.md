## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce AppExchange:** Use Salesforce's Partner Community portal to access co-marketing funds (MDF) and co-sell dashboards. Connect AppExchange install events to Salesforce CRM via ISVForce managed package telemetry.
- **HubSpot App Marketplace:** Use HubSpot's Partner Portal to access install analytics and sync app install events as HubSpot Custom Events for revenue attribution in attribution reports.
- **Zapier:** Build a Zap that fires on marketplace install webhook → creates HubSpot/Salesforce contact → enrolls in onboarding sequence → notifies SDR Slack channel if install score meets threshold.
- **Mixpanel / Amplitude:** Instrument your marketplace app with product analytics to track time-to-activation, feature adoption by cohort, and expansion signals (adding teammates, connecting additional data sources).
- **Notion / Confluence:** Use the co-marketing campaign briefs as living documents shared with your platform partner team. Notion's integration with Slack makes async partner collaboration frictionless.
- **Google Sheets / Looker Studio:** Build a weekly marketplace performance dashboard pulling from AppExchange analytics API, HubSpot partner dashboard, and CRM pipeline data for unified attribution reporting.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Listing gets views but installs are low (under 1.5% conversion rate)**
Solution: The disconnect is almost always in the first screenshot or the hero tagline. Run a 5-second test: show 5 people in your ICP only the hero image and ask "what does this product do?" If they can't answer clearly, redesign the visual. Rewrite the tagline to lead with the outcome ("Automatically sync your Salesforce pipeline to Google Sheets — live") not the feature ("Real-time data connector"). Also audit your primary category — if you're miscategorized, high-intent buyers won't find you.

**Problem: High installs but low activation (under 20% reach first value moment)**
Solution: Activation failure is a product problem disguised as a marketing problem. Audit the first 5 minutes of the install experience — most users abandon at authentication friction, permission scopes that feel invasive, or a blank state with no guided setup. Fix the onboarding UX first. As a marketing lever: replace your Day 0 email's feature tour CTA with a single, pre-built template specific to their job title (detected from their signup or CRM source). One concrete next action always outperforms "explore the product."

**Problem: Platform partner team is unresponsive to co-marketing pitches**
Solution: Platform partner teams receive hundreds of inbound requests. The ones that get responses lead with mutual customer data ("We share 340 joint customers and the ones using both platforms have a 94% retention rate vs. 81% average") and propose the smallest possible first campaign (a 500-word joint blog post, not a 3-month webinar series). Lead with value they can say yes to in one email. Once you have one successful joint initiative with a named customer story, larger co-marketing opportunities open automatically.
