## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partners complete training but still don't sell — "certification theater" with no revenue impact**
Root cause: Training is knowledge transfer without behavioral change. Partners know the product but haven't internalized how to find opportunities in their existing client base. Fix: Add a mandatory "ICP mapping exercise" before Level 1 assessment — partners must identify 5 real accounts in their book that match your ICP and explain why. This converts passive learners into active pipeline identifiers. Pair with a PAM-facilitated account mapping call within 7 days of passing the assessment.

**Problem: Battlecards aren't being used — partners revert to "I'll ask my vendor rep"**
Root cause: Battlecards are written in vendor voice, not partner voice. A partner rep can't use language that sounds like they work for you. Fix: Rewrite every battlecard from a neutral consultant's perspective. "My clients have found that..." reads better than "At Fieldbit, we believe...". Also — make battlecards accessible from mobile. If a partner can't pull it up in 10 seconds standing in a parking lot before a meeting, it won't get used.

**Problem: Re-activation sequence fails — dormant partners go quiet or unsubscribe**
Root cause: Re-activation emails lead with what the vendor needs (more deals) rather than what the partner needs (easy wins, warm leads, less work). Fix: Week 2's account mapping offer is the highest-performing lever — start there if open rates are low on Week 1. Consider replacing Week 1 with a direct phone call from the PAM instead of email. Voice dramatically outperforms email for dormant partner re-engagement. Target: call first, email to confirm.
