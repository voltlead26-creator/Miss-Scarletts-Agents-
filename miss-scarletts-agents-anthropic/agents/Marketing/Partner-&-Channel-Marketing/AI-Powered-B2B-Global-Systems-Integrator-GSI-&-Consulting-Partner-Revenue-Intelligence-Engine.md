## Troubleshooting

**Problem 1: GSI Alliance Director is responsive, but practice leaders are not engaging**
- Root cause: Alliance Directors manage the relationship, but practice leaders control client recommendations. Alliance Directors have no revenue incentive to activate the practice.
- Fix: Bypass the Alliance team and directly engage practice leaders through warm introductions from your own clients or mutual contacts. Frame every conversation around practice economics, not partnership terms. The Alliance Director is a facilitator, not a champion.

**Problem 2: GSI partner registers deals but rarely converts — partner win rate is low**
- Root cause: Partner is registering deals opportunistically (spraying and praying) rather than qualifying fit. GSI consultants are not enabled to qualify.
- Fix: Tighten deal registration requirements — require answers to 5 qualification questions before registering. Co-develop a one-page deal qualification checklist with the GSI partner manager. Offer co-investment (technical resources, executive access) only for deals that meet minimum qualification threshold.

**Problem 3: GSI keeps positioning our product incorrectly to clients — losing deals to competitors**
- Root cause: Insufficient enablement. GSI consultants have 20+ technology partnerships and can't remember differentiators under pressure.
- Fix: Create a "30-second elevator pitch card" for every common competitive situation. Run quarterly 60-minute battle card refresh sessions (record and share). Build a mobile-accessible quick reference guide GSI consultants can pull up during client meetings. Consider a Slack channel where they can ask real-time competitive questions and get responses within 2 hours.
