## Integration Tips
- **HubSpot:** Create a custom deal property "Partner Influenced" and "Partner Sourced" to track partner attribution. Use HubSpot workflows to trigger the integration-install email sequences automatically when a webhook fires from your app.
- **Salesforce:** Use Campaign Influence (multi-touch) to measure partner-influenced ARR. Create a Partner object and associate it with Opportunities where the integration was used.
- **Zapier / Make:** Build zaps that fire when your product's integration webhook triggers (install, disconnect, usage milestone) and route to HubSpot/Salesforce + Slack.
- **Notion:** Use Notion as your partner wiki — one database per partner with listing copy, campaign assets, metrics, and QBR notes all linked.
- **Segment / Amplitude:** Track integration_connected, integration_disconnected, and integration_event_fired as analytics events to feed your integration activation rate dashboard.
- **Figma Community / HubSpot App Marketplace / Salesforce AppExchange / Slack App Directory:** Each marketplace has different listing requirements, review systems, and SEO rules. Treat each as its own distribution channel with a tailored strategy.
- **Partner Slack Connect channels:** Create a shared Slack channel with each Tier 1 partner's marketing and partnerships team to coordinate co-marketing campaigns in real time.

## Troubleshooting
**Problem:** Partner is interested in co-marketing but keeps deprioritizing it internally.
**Solution:** Reduce friction to near-zero. Offer to do 80% of the work (write all the copy, design all the assets, build the landing page) and ask only for their logo, a quote, and a send from their newsletter. Give them a ready-to-go email template they can approve in 5 minutes. Reciprocity accelerates execution.

**Problem:** App marketplace listing is getting installs but few trial conversions.
**Solution:** The intent gap is likely between "install" and "value realization." Add an instant onboarding email that fires within 60 seconds of install with a single action CTA. Reduce the steps between install and first value moment to ≤3 clicks. Review drop-off in your analytics to identify where users abandon.

**Problem:** Can't measure partner-influenced ARR because attribution data is messy.
**Solution:** Start with proxy metrics before building a full attribution model: (1) tag all leads who came via integration landing pages in your CRM, (2) ask every new customer in onboarding survey "How did you hear about us?", (3) track which customers have integrations connected vs. not, and compare their ACV and NRR. These three data points give you a compelling enough story for your first partner QBR.
