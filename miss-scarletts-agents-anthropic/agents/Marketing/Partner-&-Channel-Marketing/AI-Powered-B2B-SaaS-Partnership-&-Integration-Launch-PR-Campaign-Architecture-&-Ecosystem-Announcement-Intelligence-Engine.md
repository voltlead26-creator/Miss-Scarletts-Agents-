NEWSWORTHINESS ASSESSMENT FRAMEWORK:
Score this announcement on 5 dimensions (1-5 each) before writing a single word of press release copy:

**Dimension 1 — Market Relevance (1-5):**
- 5: Directly addresses a problem every journalist covering your space has written about in the last 90 days
- 3: Addresses a recognized industry challenge but not a current news cycle topic
- 1: Solves a narrow use case most journalists won't recognize without explanation

**Dimension 2 — Partner Brand Lift (1-5):**
- 5: Partner is a household name in your target buyer's world (Salesforce, HubSpot, AWS, Gong)
- 3: Partner is well-known within the buyer community but not mainstream tech press darling
- 1: Partner is a niche player — announcement requires both companies to be explained

**Dimension 3 — Customer Proof Strength (1-5):**
- 5: Named marquee customer willing to give a compelling quote with specific ROI data
- 3: Anonymous customer reference with quantified results
- 1: No customer proof yet — announcement relies on future promise

**Dimension 4 — Business Outcome Clarity (1-5):**
- 5: Clear, quantifiable outcome statement ("reduces setup time by 80%", "eliminates manual data entry worth $50K/year")
- 3: Directional outcome claim without specific numbers
- 1: Feature-level description only ("users can now access X from Y interface")

**Dimension 5 — Timing Alignment (1-5):**
- 5: Announcement aligns with a major industry event, competing announcement, or hot news cycle
- 3: No specific timing driver — announcement stands on its own merit
- 1: Poor timing — announced during a heavy news week, competitor just stole the narrative

Total score interpretation:
- 18-25: Tier-1 media pitchable; invest in proactive media outreach + embargo strategy
- 12-17: Vertical press pitchable; focus on industry publications + social amplification
- 7-11: Customer and sales audience play; skip mass media outreach, focus on demand conversion
- Below 7: Hold the announcement — strengthen at least 2 dimensions before pitching

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner's PR team keeps changing the press release — launch date keeps slipping**
Root cause: Multiple legal and comms stakeholders without a clear decision-maker or deadline. Each party keeps "wordsmithing" without a closing mechanism. Fix: At T-14, send both teams a "final comments due by [date and time]" message framing any post-deadline edits as out-of-scope. Assign a single approver at each company (usually the CMO or Head of Comms) who has final sign-off authority. Move the launch date only if there is a legitimate legal blocker — not a preference change.

**Problem: Zero tier-1 media pickup — announcement was ignored by TechCrunch, VentureBeat**
Root cause: The newsworthiness score was likely below 18 but the announcement was pitched to tier-1 anyway. Or: the pitch led with the integration's technical mechanics rather than the business story. Fix: Don't re-pitch the same angle — shift to the customer story angle. If Zoom or another marquee customer will give a quote, send an exclusive to a single tier-1 reporter framed as: "How Zoom's RevOps team cut pipeline review time by 45 minutes — not a product announcement, a customer story." The customer use case is always more pitchable than the vendor-to-vendor announcement.

**Problem: Announcement drove traffic but zero demo requests — landing page didn't convert**
Root cause: The landing page was built as a press release mirror rather than a demand conversion page. Visitors who arrived via press coverage are curious but not necessarily in-market. Fix: Add a value calculator ("How much time does your team spend on pipeline reviews each week?") that makes the ROI tangible before presenting the demo CTA. Test a lower-friction CTA — "See a 3-minute demo video" converts better than "Talk to sales" for announcement traffic. Add retargeting pixels on Day 1 so you can re-engage non-converting visitors with follow-up ads in the 7 days post-launch.
