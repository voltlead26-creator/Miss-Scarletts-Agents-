# Partner Recruitment Qualification Engine - Ideal Partner Profile Builder & Scoring System

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** partner-marketing, channel-marketing, b2b, saas, partnerships, revenue-operations, abm, icp

## Overview
Builds a data-driven Ideal Partner Profile (IPP), generates a scored qualification framework for evaluating inbound and outbound partner candidates, and produces a prioritized shortlist of target partners to recruit — fully ready to hand off to a partner development team or run autonomously in a CRM workflow. Use it when launching a new channel program, expanding into new geographies, or when your existing partner roster is underperforming.

### COMPONENT 4: Partner Outreach Sequencing Strategy

**Sequencing logic — recruit in this order for maximum program credibility:**

Tier 1 — Warm Referral Partners (Week 1-4):
These are partners referred by your existing top customers. They have the highest conversion rate (typically 60-70% from outreach to signed agreement) and the fastest time to first deal. Start here to build early wins and social proof for the program.

Tier 2 — Established Market Leaders with ICP Overlap (Week 2-8):
Well-known agencies, SIs, or VARs whose name will attract Tier 3 partners. Signing one recognized player early makes recruiting mid-tier partners significantly easier. Invest disproportionate effort in these — offer enhanced terms for program launch partners.

Tier 3 — High-Potential Emerging Partners (Week 4-12):
Growing boutique firms with strong ICP fit but smaller footprint. Easier to activate than large firms because they're more motivated by margin and co-sell support. These often become your highest-performing partners within 18 months.

Tier 4 — Inbound Partner Applicants (ongoing):
Prioritize by score, not by enthusiasm. Enthusiastic but low-scoring partners consume disproportionate enablement resources with low return. Use the scorecard as a filter, not enthusiasm or persistence.

**Outreach timing rules:**
- Never approach a partner cold with a commission offer first — lead with customer insight about their market
- Ideal outreach timing: immediately after a trigger event (partner's new hire in partnerships role, company funding, conference where both companies exhibited)
- Avoid outreach in Q4 (partner sales teams are closing their own year-end deals and have zero bandwidth)
- Re-engagement cadence for declined candidates: 6 months minimum before re-approach

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High-scoring partners sign agreements but go inactive — no deals registered after 90 days.**
Solution: The scoring model is capturing intent but missing activation capacity. Add two questions to your discovery call: "Who specifically on your sales team will introduce our product to clients — can I meet them today?" and "Can you name three clients right now that you'd introduce us to in the first 30 days?" Partners who can't answer these concretely are capacity-constrained, not ICP-misaligned. Add an "Activation Readiness" criterion to the scorecard (weight: 10%) and reduce weight on more abstract criteria.

**Problem: The scorecard is producing too many Gold-tier candidates and your team can't resource them all.**
Solution: Raise the Gold threshold to 90+ points and introduce a "Strategic Fit" knockout criterion — only candidates where a VP-level executive will personally sponsor the partnership qualify for Gold. Gold designation should mean premium investment from your team, not just a high score. Alternatively, introduce a "Cohort Launch" model: recruit Gold partners in groups of 5-8 per quarter so onboarding resources are not spread thin.

**Problem: Recruited partners source deals outside your ICP, creating high churn from partner-referred customers.**
Solution: The ICP Overlap scoring is based on self-reported data from the partner, which they have incentive to inflate. Add an account-mapping step using Crossbeam or a shared customer list before signing — require at least 10 named accounts that match your ICP before a partner is classified as Gold. Partners that cannot produce 10 named ICP-fit accounts are speculating, not qualifying.
