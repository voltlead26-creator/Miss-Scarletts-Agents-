## Overview
Transforms marketing from a cost-center/MQL-generation model into a revenue-accountable function that owns pipeline creation, coverage ratios, and marketing-sourced ARR. Use when a CMO needs to redesign team KPIs, negotiate binding revenue SLAs with the CRO and CEO, restructure compensation around pipeline outcomes, and build the board-ready business case for marketing as a primary revenue driver.

Build a workflow to auto-stamp deals at creation. Build a CMO Revenue Dashboard with: Marketing-Sourced SAO count (weekly), Marketing-Sourced Pipeline Value, Coverage Ratio widget, and SAO-to-Close win rate split by source.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue 1: CRO refuses to negotiate, says "attribution is just marketing spin" and won't sign any SLA**
Solution: Stop negotiating and start proving. Pull 12 months of won deal data from HubSpot/Salesforce and answer three questions: (1) What % of won deals had at least one marketing touch before opportunity creation? (2) What is the average ACV for marketing-touched vs. never-touched accounts? (3) What was the sales cycle length for marketing-influenced vs. cold-outbound opportunities? In most B2B SaaS companies with active marketing, 50–70% of won deals have marketing touches — they're just not tracked. Bringing data to a second meeting, rather than a concept, changes the negotiation entirely. Offer to use the most conservative attribution model possible (direct-source only, no assists) to start.

**Issue 2: Marketing team morale drops when MQL targets disappear and pipeline targets feel impossible and unfair**
Solution: Run a 60-day parallel tracking period where both MQL metrics and the new pipeline metrics are reported simultaneously. This removes the "ripping away" feeling. Hold a team workshop where you show the math in both directions: convert the new pipeline target back into MQL-equivalent to show the workload is similar, just measured differently. Emphasize that the new model gives marketing more power and budget justification, not more blame. Acknowledge that the first 90 days will feel harder because infrastructure is being built — frame this as a temporary investment period, not a permanent state.

**Issue 3: CRM attribution data is too dirty or incomplete to prove marketing sourcing, undermining the entire transformation**
Solution: Do not attempt to retroactively clean all historical data — it will take months and may never be credible. Instead, declare a "Clean Slate Date" (today + 14 days to configure CRM). All opportunities created after that date are tracked under the new attribution rules, with proper source stamping. Run the new model in parallel with old reporting for 60 days. Use the first 30-day cohort of clean data as the proof-of-concept for the CRO, not historical data. Clean historical data only for the prior 90 days (won deals), which is a manageable audit to support the initial negotiation conversation.
