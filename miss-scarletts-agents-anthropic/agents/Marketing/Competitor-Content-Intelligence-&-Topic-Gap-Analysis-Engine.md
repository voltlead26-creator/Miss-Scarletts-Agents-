# Competitor Content Intelligence & Topic Gap Analysis Engine - AI-Powered Content Gap Discovery & Strategic Opportunity Mapping

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** content-strategy, competitive-intelligence, seo, content-gap-analysis, demand-generation, content-operations

## Overview
Systematically reverse-engineers competitor content strategies, identifies high-value topic gaps your brand is missing, and produces a prioritized content roadmap with business impact scores. Use when you want to outrank and outmaneuver competitors on high-intent search terms, fill buyer-journey blind spots, or reallocate content budget toward topics that drive pipeline — not just traffic.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior content strategist and SEO intelligence analyst. Conduct a comprehensive competitor content gap analysis for [Your Company Name].

COMPANY: [Your Company Name] — [1-sentence description of what you do and who you serve]
INDUSTRY: [Your industry/market category]
PRIMARY COMPETITORS: [List 3-5 direct competitors by name]
ICP (Ideal Customer Profile): [Job title, company size, industry, key pain points]
CURRENT CONTENT FOCUS: [What topics/formats you already publish — e.g., "B2B SaaS ops, implementation guides, ROI calculators"]
GOAL: [Drive organic traffic / Fill pipeline / Establish category authority / Displace competitors in search]

Deliver a complete competitor content intelligence report with:

1. COMPETITOR CONTENT AUDIT
   - For each competitor, identify their top-performing content categories (infer from their site structure, blog, resource hub)
   - Estimate their primary content themes, buyer journey coverage, and SEO keyword clusters
   - Identify their "signature content plays" — formats or angles they consistently win with
   - Flag any topic areas they over-index on vs. ignore

2. CONTENT GAP MATRIX
   - Topics competitors rank for that you don't address at all (Tier 1 priority: high volume + high commercial intent)
   - Topics where competitors have thin/weak coverage that you could dominate with depth (Tier 2: quick wins)
   - Topics no competitor addresses that represent category-defining opportunities (Tier 3: blue ocean)
   - Buyer journey stage for each gap: TOFU (awareness) / MOFU (consideration) / BOFU (decision)

3. KEYWORD OPPORTUNITY CLUSTERS
   - 15 specific high-priority keyword targets your content should capture
   - For each: estimated search intent, competitive difficulty (low/medium/high), buyer journey stage, recommended content format
   - Group into 3-5 topic clusters with a pillar page strategy

4. CONTENT DIFFERENTIATION ANGLES
   - 5 specific positioning angles you can take to win on contested topics (e.g., "Gartner-style benchmark data," "practitioner-first tutorials," "anti-[competitor] contrarian take")
   - For each angle: which competitor it displaces and why your take wins

5. 90-DAY CONTENT BATTLE PLAN
   - Priority 1 (Month 1): 3 high-ROI content assets to create immediately with full briefs
   - Priority 2 (Month 2): 5 supporting content pieces
   - Priority 3 (Month 3): Ongoing cluster expansion strategy
   - Each asset: title, target keyword, format, word count, CTA, and expected outcome

## Integration Tips

**Semrush / Ahrefs:**
- Run the Keyword Gap tool with your domain vs. 3-5 competitor domains before using this prompt — paste the top 50 overlapping and unique keywords into the Advanced Version's competitor input to ground the AI output in real data
- Export competitor top pages by traffic and paste the top 20 URLs per competitor into the prompt context to get URL-level displacement strategies

**HubSpot / Salesforce:**
- Tag all leads from new content assets with UTM parameters (utm_source=organic, utm_content=[asset-name]) to track MQL and pipeline attribution per content gap you fill
- Build a HubSpot workflow that auto-tags contacts who convert on gap-filling content for sales context: "This lead found us searching for [keyword] — they're evaluating [competitor]"

**Google Search Console:**
- Export your top 100 queries where you're ranking position 4-20 — these are "near-miss" opportunities that a gap-filling or content refresh strategy can immediately improve
- Paste these queries into the prompt to identify which represent genuine competitor displacement opportunities vs. informational traffic

**Notion / Confluence:**
- Create a Content Intelligence Dashboard in Notion with columns: Gap Topic | Target Keyword | Assigned Writer | Status | Published Date | Current Ranking | MQL Count
- Update monthly after running this prompt to maintain a live content opportunity backlog

**Zapier / Make:**
- Build an automation: New first-page ranking detected (via Semrush API alert) → Slack notification to content team → Auto-update Notion dashboard row → Trigger weekly performance summary email
- Connect Google Search Console → Google Sheets → scheduled Claude API call to re-run gap analysis monthly and flag new competitor content

## Troubleshooting

**"The gaps identified are too broad — I need specific keyword-level opportunities, not general topic areas"**
→ Add more precision to your prompt: Include 5-10 specific URLs from each competitor's blog/resource hub, paste their top navigation categories, and specify "I need keyword-level specificity — every gap must include a primary keyword with estimated monthly search volume and a specific competing URL to displace." The more concrete competitor data you provide, the more actionable the output.

**"The differentiation angles feel generic — they're not specific enough to our product"**
→ Add product-specific context: List your top 5 unique features or data assets no competitor has (e.g., "We have 2 years of anonymized benchmark data from 800 customers"), describe your customer success stories in 1-2 sentences each, and specify your brand voice constraints. Then ask the AI to "generate differentiation angles that only Fieldstitch (or your company) can credibly execute — not angles any competitor could copy."

**"The 90-day plan is too ambitious for our team capacity"**
→ Add resource constraints to the Advanced Version: "Our team capacity is 4 blog posts per month maximum, 1 long-form guide per quarter, no developer resources for interactive tools." Ask for a scaled-down version with "highest ROI per content hour invested" as the primary optimization criteria, then use the remaining gaps as a 6-12 month roadmap backlog.
