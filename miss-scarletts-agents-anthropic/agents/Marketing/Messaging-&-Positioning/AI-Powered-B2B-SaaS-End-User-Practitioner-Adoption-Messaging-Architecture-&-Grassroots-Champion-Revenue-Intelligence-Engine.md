## Overview
Builds a complete messaging system targeted at the people who actually use your product — not the buyers — translating features into daily workflow wins, career advancement, and professional identity. Use it when adoption is lagging despite a closed deal, when renewal risk is high because end users aren't engaged, or when you want to create a grassroots champion movement that accelerates expansions and referrals from the bottom up.

## Integration Tips

**Customer Success Platforms (Gainsight, ChurnZero, Totango):**
- Load the Adoption Journey messaging sequence as a triggered playbook: Day-1 email fires on account activation, Day-14 message fires when usage milestone isn't hit, Day-60 champion ask fires when usage crosses power-user threshold
- Build a "champion identified" health score dimension — accounts with a confirmed grassroots champion get a +15 health score bonus, automatically reducing at-risk tagging
- Auto-assign CSMs to "champion activation" tasks when product analytics signal a power user (12+ briefs/week) who hasn't been formally engaged

**In-App Messaging (Pendo, Appcues, Intercom):**
- Trigger the Day-1 activation in-app modal on first login, not in onboarding email — the message lands better in the context where the action happens
- Use behavioral triggers (idle time >3 days) to fire the "habit formation" nudge copy directly in the product UI
- Personalize the Day-60 champion message using actual usage data: "You've generated 47 research briefs. Your colleagues average 12. You're in the top 8% of users on our platform."

**Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:**
- Create a "Grassroots Champion" field on the Account object — when a user is nominated as a champion, it flags for CSM follow-up and triggers the expansion conversation workflow
- Store the manager's one-pager template as a Salesforce asset linked to renewal opportunities, so CSMs can share it directly from the CRM during renewal conversations
- Build an "adoption score" dashboard that correlates license-level usage data to renewal probability, giving CSMs a data-backed conversation starter

**Slack / Microsoft Teams Integration:**
- Deploy the "team ritual" concept as a Slack bot: every Monday morning, the bot posts that week's most interesting insight from a randomly selected power user's recent briefs — making advanced use visible without feeling like surveillance
- Create a #product-wins channel specifically for power users to share outputs — the social validation of peers seeing real results is the most effective adoption driver in team cultures

**Gong / Chorus (Conversation Intelligence):**
- Tag calls where the rep demonstrates research depth as "Catalyst-assisted" to build a data set correlating product use to call quality scores
- Build a "discovery quality" tracker that shows the difference in discovery question depth between high-usage and low-usage reps — this is the manager data point that drives internal advocacy more than any other metric

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: End users activate but don't return after day 3 — the habit doesn't form.**
*Fix:* The most common cause is that the day-1 experience didn't produce a visible result they could connect to a real outcome. Run the activation message audit: did the first session produce a concrete output (a briefing, a call insight, a prospect action item)? If the day-1 experience is still abstract ("explore the platform"), redesign it to force a single, specific, immediately useful output. The habit forms around a result, not around the product.

**Problem: Power users love it but aren't converting to internal champions — adoption stays at 40% on the team.**
*Fix:* The champion activation ask is likely too formal or too soon. Most power users need to have a personal win story before they'll advocate publicly. Add a "tell me your story" step before the "tell your manager" ask — have a CSM or automated in-app message ask the power user to describe a specific call or deal where the product made a difference. Once they've articulated it out loud (or in writing), the conversion to advocacy is nearly automatic. The ask becomes: "Can I help you share that story with your team?"

**Problem: The manager is neutral or negative — they don't reinforce the tool in 1:1s, so team adoption stays low.**
*Fix:* Managers don't reinforce tools they don't understand or haven't seen results from themselves. Target the manager with a specific data package before your 90-day check-in: pull the top 3 performers on the team by usage frequency and show the manager their quota attainment vs. the bottom 3 users. Don't make it about the tool — make it about the pattern. "I noticed your top users of the platform are averaging 104% of quota this quarter vs. 83% for the bottom users. Wanted to share that with you before renewal." That conversation makes managers into advocates without asking them to become product champions.
