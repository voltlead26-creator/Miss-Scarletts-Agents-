## Troubleshooting

**Issue: The AI messaging feels generic even after running this prompt**
*Solution:* The prompt requires specific AI capability descriptions, not vague ones. Replace "AI-powered analytics" with the exact mechanism — e.g., "uses gradient boosting trained on 5M+ closed-won/lost deals to predict close probability." The more specific your input, the more differentiated your output. Run the prompt again with 3x the technical specificity.

**Issue: Economic buyers still glaze over during AI pitches**
*Solution:* You may be leading with the AI mechanism instead of the business problem. Take Module 1's core value proposition and test whether you can describe your entire product value in 30 seconds without mentioning AI at all. If you can't, your messaging is too technology-centric. Rebuild starting from the business outcome, and introduce AI as the mechanism only after establishing the problem cost.

**Issue: Trust messaging feels defensive and actually raises more concerns**
*Solution:* Trust messages work best when they're proactively embedded in value claims, not isolated as objection responses. Instead of "Don't worry, our AI is accurate," restructure as: "Every recommendation shows the exact signals that triggered it and the historical accuracy rate for that pattern — so your team always has full context for the call." Confidence, not defensiveness.
