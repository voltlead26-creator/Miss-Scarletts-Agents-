## Overview
Constructs a complete, proof-backed messaging architecture — from company narrative to persona-level value propositions — that aligns sales, marketing, and content teams around a single authoritative story. Use it when launching a product, repositioning against a competitor, or rebuilding scattered messaging into a coherent system.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Messaging pillar claims are too generic — could apply to any SaaS tool**
Solution: Add this constraint to the Advanced prompt: "For each pillar claim, ask: could [COMPETITOR 1] paste this exact sentence onto their homepage without changing a word? If yes, rewrite with a specific mechanism, named timeframe, or quantified outcome that only we can own." Force the model to anchor every claim to a proof point before writing the pillar.

**Problem: Persona matrix outputs feel interchangeable — different titles but same message**
Solution: Before running the prompt, for each persona write one sentence in first person describing their single biggest fear (not their professional goal — their fear). Example: "CFO: I'm terrified of presenting a missed quarter to the board and having no explanation." Feed these fear statements as additional persona context. The model will generate fundamentally different emotional entry points for each persona.

**Problem: Competitive differentiation grid is too diplomatic — doesn't give sales usable ammunition**
Solution: Add this instruction: "For the 'where we win' column, write each point as if you are a confident, well-prepared sales rep who genuinely believes the buyer is making a mistake by choosing the competitor. Be direct and specific. If we're faster, say by how many days. If we're cheaper at mid-market, give a % estimate. Don't hedge with 'it depends' language." Directness in competitive messaging is a strategic choice, not a risk.
