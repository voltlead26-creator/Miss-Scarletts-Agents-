## Overview

Use it when deals stall at the executive level, when sales reps can't articulate ROI to the C-suite, or when your champion needs ammunition to get budget approved.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The financial model feels generic — the CFO isn't engaging with the numbers.**
*Fix:* The model is likely using industry averages instead of prospect-specific inputs. Before running this prompt, gather 3 pieces of real data from discovery: (1) how many people are doing the manual work today, (2) what tool/process they're replacing, and (3) one specific business pain the CFO has mentioned in the past 90 days. Personalized cost models based on the prospect's own data outperform generic ROI calculators by 3-4x in CFO engagement.

**Problem: The champion is using the email template but getting no response from the CFO.**
*Fix:* The email is likely too long or too feature-focused. CFO emails should be under 120 words, lead with a dollar figure in the first sentence, and end with one ultra-specific ask. Also check the subject line — "quick note on the EBITDA initiative" outperforms "Meridian AI ROI follow-up" because it signals relevance to an existing priority rather than a vendor follow-up.

**Problem: The objection responses sound like sales scripts — the CFO pushes back harder.**
*Fix:* Each objection response should end with a question, not a statement. "The comparison isn't $96K vs zero — it's $96K vs your current $285K spend" closes the door. "What would make this feel like an obviously better use of that $285K budget you're already spending?" opens a conversation. CFOs are trained to defeat sales arguments; they're not trained to answer sincere diagnostic questions.
