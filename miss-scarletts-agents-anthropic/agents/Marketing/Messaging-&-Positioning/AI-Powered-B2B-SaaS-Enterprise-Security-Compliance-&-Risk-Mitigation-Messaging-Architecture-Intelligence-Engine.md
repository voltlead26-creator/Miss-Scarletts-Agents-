## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The CISO is blocking the deal with vague concerns ("we just can't approve this right now") that don't map to specific objections.

**Solution:** The vague block usually means they don't have enough information to say yes, so they default to no. Schedule a 30-minute "Security Architecture Walk-Through" directly between your Head of Security/CTO and their security team — not a sales call. The goal is to answer every technical question, not to sell. CISOs respond to peer-level technical conversations, not vendor pitches. Come prepared with your shared responsibility model, your incident response SLA, and your last pen test executive summary.

---

**Problem:** Sales team is bypassing security messaging and going straight to champions who don't have security authority, causing deals to stall at procurement/legal.

**Solution:** Build security messaging into the discovery call script — specifically the qualification questions in the Advanced Version Section 7. Train AEs to identify whether a CISO or IT Security team will be involved before the deal is in late stage. The cost of a late-stage security surprise is 6-8 weeks; the cost of early security qualification is one extra question in discovery. Create a "Security Involved" deal stage that triggers automated security content delivery and alerts PMM/security team to provide real-time support.

---

**Problem:** Competitors are claiming equivalent security certifications but you know their implementation is weaker.

**Solution:** Certifications are binary (you have it or you don't) but implementation is a spectrum. Train your sales team to ask the following questions in competitive situations: "Does their SOC 2 cover their AI processing, or just their core application?" / "Do they offer dedicated tenant architecture or shared multi-tenant?" / "What's their breach notification SLA — 24 hours, 72 hours, or 'as required by law'?" These questions reveal implementation depth without requiring you to disparage competitors directly. Let the prospect's security team ask the questions — your job is to arm the champion with the right questions to surface real differences.
