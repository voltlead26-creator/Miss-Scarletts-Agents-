## Overview
Builds a complete messaging system for the "we could build this internally" objection — one of the top-3 deal killers in B2B SaaS, now amplified by AI-assisted development tools that make engineering teams believe they can replicate commercial software faster than ever. Use it when prospects go quiet after a great demo, when deals stall with "we need to evaluate an internal build option," or when your engineering-friendly champion returns from an internal conversation saying "the CTO thinks we can do this ourselves."

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Engineering team builds a working prototype in 6 weeks and uses it to argue they can complete the full build**
*Fix:* Validate the prototype genuinely ("that's impressive velocity — your team is exactly as good as we thought"). Then redirect to the question the prototype doesn't answer: "A prototype handles the straightforward path. The question is what happens when [specific edge case relevant to their use case — multi-tenant data isolation, GDPR right-to-erasure requests, SOC 2 audit trail for enterprise deals]. How will the prototype handle those?" Offer to run a technical workshop with their engineering team to scope what full production readiness actually requires. This positions you as a partner in their decision and often surfaces the gap between prototype and production.

**Issue: The "build advocate" is an engineer who genuinely wants to build it as a career development project and has the CTO's ear**
*Fix:* Don't fight the career motivation — redirect it. The most powerful reframe: "The build is a great opportunity to learn [related technology]. What if we partnered so your team extends and customizes our platform instead of rebuilding what we've already built? Here's our API documentation and what companies your size are building on top of us." Turning an internal build advocate into a platform extensibility champion neutralizes the threat while creating a deeper customer relationship.

**Issue: Prospect goes completely dark after the build-vs-buy objection surfaces, even after multiple follow-ups**
*Fix:* Send one final message to the champion with a "permission to close your file" structure: "We haven't connected since you mentioned the internal build option — I don't want to keep reaching out if the direction is clear. If you're moving forward with an internal build, I'd love to send you our internal-build guidance document — it's the checklist we wish every company had before they started. If timing just isn't right, a quick reply is all I need and we'll reconnect when the moment is right." This creates a low-friction re-engagement path and the free guidance offer often surfaces latent doubt about the build decision.
