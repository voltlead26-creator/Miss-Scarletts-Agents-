## Overview
Builds a complete messaging system for competitive evaluation scenarios — the phase when a prospect has shortlisted 2–4 vendors and is actively comparing. Use it when deals stall in evaluation, when win rates against specific competitors are below 40%, or when your sales team lacks persona-specific "why us" narratives for the final stages of a deal.

## Integration Tips

Use the persona messaging cards as custom fields on opportunity objects — "primary competitor," "champion coaching status," "evaluation criteria locked." Trigger automated reminders to AEs when deal stage reaches "Evaluation" to pull the champion kit.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Competitive trap questions feel adversarial and damage the champion's internal credibility**
*Fix:* Reframe trap questions as "evaluation diligence questions any serious buyer should ask every vendor" — position them as procurement best practice, not competitive warfare. Provide your champion with a 5-question evaluation checklist that applies equally to all vendors (including you), where only your competitor will struggle with 3 of the 5.

**Issue: Prospect's technical evaluator is already friendly with Competitor X's SE team and dismisses our proof points**
*Fix:* Don't fight the relationship — reframe the evaluation as "our job is to validate their recommendation, not replace their judgment." Use the success criteria architecture (Component 3) to shift the conversation from "who do you prefer" to "what will success look like in 12 months" — a question where personal relationships are irrelevant and outcomes data speaks loudest.

**Issue: Win rate against competitor improves but deal size shrinks due to pricing pressure in competitive evaluations**
*Fix:* Add a "competitive discount defense" section using the cost-of-switching-cost reframe: if the prospect chooses the cheaper option but switches back in 18 months (as 34% of competitive losses do, per Gartner), what does the full switching cost look like including implementation, retraining, and productivity loss? This reframes price comparison from ACV to 3-year TCO.
