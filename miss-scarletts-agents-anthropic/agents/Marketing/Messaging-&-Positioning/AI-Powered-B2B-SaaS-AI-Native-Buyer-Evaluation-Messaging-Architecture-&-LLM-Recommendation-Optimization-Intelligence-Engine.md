## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI systems consistently describe us as a generic alternative to a competitor, not as a distinct category.**
Solution: The root cause is usually that your company's public content contains more comparative language ("better than X", "alternative to X") than category-defining language. AI systems learn brand positioning from the most common patterns across your public content. Add 3–5 long-form content pieces that define your category from first principles — explaining why your approach is fundamentally different, not just comparatively better. Publish an original point-of-view piece titled "Why [Old Category Name] Is Dead and What [New Category Name] Means for [ICP]" and distribute it through channels that carry authority weight with AI systems (your blog, a major industry publication, LinkedIn articles).

**Problem: Our proof points aren't being cited in AI responses even though they're on our website.**
Solution: AI systems weight proof points by source authority, specificity, and cross-reference frequency. A metric that appears once on your website in marketing copy is rarely cited. The same metric cited in a press release, an analyst report, a case study PDF, a G2 review, and a journalist's article is highly likely to be cited. For your top 3 proof points, build a "multi-source citation stack": (1) publish the data in a named report, (2) issue a press release referencing the report, (3) pitch the stat to 3 journalists for inclusion in category coverage, (4) reference it in your G2 company description, (5) train your customer community to echo the metric in their reviews.

**Problem: AI systems produce accurate descriptions of our product but always list us 3rd or 4th in category recommendations.**
Solution: AI category ranking correlates strongly with review volume, media velocity, and content depth — not just accuracy. Run a competitor benchmarking audit: how many G2 reviews do your top competitors have vs. you? How many media mentions? How many third-party category guide inclusions? The gap between you and the #1 ranked brand in AI responses is almost always a review volume gap, a media coverage gap, or a third-party endorsement gap. Build a 90-day sprint to close the specific gap — typically: a G2 review generation campaign (targeting 30–50 new reviews), 3 earned media placements in category-relevant publications, and inclusion in 5 "best of" listicles that AI systems consistently cite in category recommendation responses.
