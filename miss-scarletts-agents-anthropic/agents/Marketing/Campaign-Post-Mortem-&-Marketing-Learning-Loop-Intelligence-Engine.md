## Overview

Systematically deconstructs completed campaigns to extract root-cause insights, transferable learnings, and predictive patterns — then packages them into a structured knowledge base that makes every future campaign smarter. Use this after any significant campaign (launch, demand gen, ABM push, event, product announcement) to move beyond surface-level reporting and build true institutional marketing intelligence.

   - Specific recommendations for: audience targeting, messaging, channel mix, timing, offer, and budget allocation
   - Changes to make immediately vs. changes to A/B test

7. LEARNING LIBRARY ENTRY
   - A 3-sentence summary of this campaign's key learning, written to be retrieved and applied by any marketer 12 months from now

## Example Input/Output

**Example Input:**

Company: Meridian Analytics — Series B SaaS, $8M ARR, sales intelligence platform for B2B sales teams
Campaign: Q4 2025 Pipeline Sprint — targeting VP Sales at 200-2,000 employee SaaS companies
Goal: $1.2M pipeline in 45 days
Channels: LinkedIn Ads ($22K), email to 3,400 contact database, SDR outreach to 120 Tier-1 accounts
Results:
| Metric | Goal | Actual |
|---|---|---|
| Pipeline created | $1.2M | $710K |
| MQLs | 90 | 112 |
| Opportunities | 15 | 8 |
| LinkedIn CTR | 0.7% | 1.1% |
| Email open rate | 26% | 38% |
| MQL → Opp conversion | 17% | 7% |
| Cost per opportunity | $2,933 | $5,500 |

Sales feedback: "Leads are engaging with content but not booking demos. They're consuming the ROI calculator but ghosting on follow-up."

---

**Example Output (Abbreviated):**

**EXECUTIVE VERDICT:** Partial success. The campaign generated strong top-of-funnel engagement (LinkedIn CTR exceeded goal by 57%, email open rates beat benchmark by 46%) but collapsed at MQL-to-Opportunity conversion (7% vs. 17% target). The campaign produced awareness and interest but failed to convert that intent into sales conversations. The $710K pipeline represents 59% of goal — insufficient to justify the 45-day sprint investment.

**ROOT CAUSE (5 Whys on MQL-to-Opp conversion):**
- Why did MQL → Opp conversion collapse? Prospects aren't booking demos after engaging with content
- Why aren't they booking demos? SDR follow-up isn't converting interest into conversations
- Why is SDR outreach failing to convert? The ROI calculator leads are in early research mode, not purchase consideration
- Why are we generating research-stage leads? The LinkedIn creative drove clicks on curiosity (cost headlines) rather than urgency (competitive loss content)
- Why did we use curiosity-based creative? The brief prioritized reach and CTR, not opportunity quality
- **ROOT CAUSE:** Success metric misalignment — optimizing for MQL volume rather than MQL quality created a funnel filled with early-stage researchers

**TOP HYPOTHESIS:**
H1: Switching LinkedIn targeting to accounts showing intent signal (Bombora topics: "Sales Intelligence," "CRM Integration") will improve MQL-to-Opp conversion from 7% to 14%+ in Q1 2026, because intent-data-qualified leads have demonstrated purchase research behavior vs. cold audience

**LEARNING LIBRARY ENTRY:**
LEARNING ID: 202512-PipelineSprint-01
HEADLINE: Optimizing for MQL volume with curiosity-based creative produces high-volume, low-quality leads that collapse at conversion
CONTEXT: Q4 2025 pipeline sprint generated 25% more MQLs than goal through strong LinkedIn creative and email engagement. However, MQL-to-Opp conversion fell to 7% (target: 17%) because the audience was in early research mode, not purchase consideration. SDR follow-up on ROI-calculator leads consistently met "just researching" objections.
APPLICABILITY: When running pipeline sprint campaigns with volume goals, add a qualification signal requirement (intent data overlay, direct competitor mention, explicit role-based signals) to LinkedIn targeting before prioritizing creative optimization.
COUNTER-CASES: Brand awareness campaigns where top-of-funnel volume is the legitimate goal.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** "The post-mortem analysis is too generic — it says things like 'improve targeting' without specific recommendations."
**Fix:** Paste actual metric data in table format rather than summarizing. Include specific creative headlines/subject lines tested, exact audience targeting parameters used, and day-by-day or week-by-week performance breakdowns. The more granular the input, the more specific the root cause analysis. If you're getting generic output, ask: "For each recommendation, specify the exact parameter to change, the expected metric impact, and how to test it in isolation."

**Problem:** "The 5 Whys stops at 3 levels and doesn't reach actionable root causes."
**Fix:** After the initial output, prompt: "The root cause for [specific metric] still feels symptomatic. Push one level deeper — what process, decision, or resource gap caused the execution failure you identified?" Continue until you reach something a team member can directly change (a process, a template, a criterion, a workflow).

**Problem:** "I don't have historical benchmarks to compare against."
**Fix:** Use industry benchmarks as a proxy. Provide: "Use B2B SaaS industry benchmarks for [channel] as the comparison baseline. Assume LinkedIn Ads: 0.45% CTR, $7-11 CPL for demand gen; email marketing: 22% open rate, 3.5% CTR; inbound MQL-to-Opp: 20-25% for enterprise, 10-15% for SMB." After 3 campaigns, your own historical data will be a better benchmark than industry averages.
