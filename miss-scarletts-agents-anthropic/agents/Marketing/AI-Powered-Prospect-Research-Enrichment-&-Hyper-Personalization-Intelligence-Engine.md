## Overview
Builds a complete AI research waterfall that automatically generates prospect intelligence dossiers — pulling company news, LinkedIn activity, job postings, tech stack signals, and executive triggers — then translates raw data into hyper-personalized outreach hooks, conversation starters, and pain-point angles for every contact. Use it before any outbound sequence to arm SDRs or AI agents with the specific, relevant context that makes a cold message feel like a warm, researched conversation.

- **Time-to-dossier**: A well-structured Clay waterfall or AI research prompt should generate a complete dossier in under 4 minutes per prospect — if it takes longer, the input data collection workflow needs optimization

- **Meeting show rate**: Prospects who received hyper-personalized outreach referencing specific signals should show at 75%+ vs. 60% industry baseline

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Personalization hooks sound generic even after filling in all the data**
Solution: The specificity problem almost always traces back to vague input signals. "They visited our pricing page" produces a generic hook; "they visited our pricing page 4 times in 3 days including once on a Sunday evening" produces a sharp one. Audit the signal quality before the research prompt — the output can only be as specific as the input. For company-level signals, pull from LinkedIn company posts, job posting *requirements* (not just titles), and G2 reviews mentioning specific pain. For contact-level signals, pull the exact words they used in a LinkedIn post or comment, not just the topic.

**Problem: Research dossiers are taking too long to produce at scale**
Solution: You are manually collecting inputs that should be automated. Build a Clay waterfall that auto-populates 70-80% of the input fields before the research prompt runs. The prompt is the synthesis layer — data collection should be automated. Start with these three automations: (1) LinkedIn company post scraper for recent news, (2) Job posting API (Coresignal or Proxycurl) for active listings, (3) Clearbit/Apollo for tech stack signals. With these running automatically, the AI research prompt runs on structured data, not manual input.

**Problem: SDRs are not using the dossiers — they are still sending generic emails**
Solution: The dossier is not integrated into their workflow touchpoint. The research must appear at the exact moment the SDR is drafting the email — not in a separate document they have to context-switch to. Two fixes: (1) Populate the top hook directly into the email sequence as a merge field — no extra steps required. (2) For call prep, add a 30-second "Research Brief" card in your sales engagement platform that shows only the 3 hooks and the pain-point hypothesis — not the full dossier. Behavioral change happens when the new action is easier than the old habit.
