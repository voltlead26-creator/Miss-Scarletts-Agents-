## 3. Ad Format Strategy

For each funnel stage, specify the optimal YouTube ad format:

**TOFU — Skippable In-Stream (15-60 seconds) + Bumper Ads (6 seconds):**
- Skippable in-stream: Use for storytelling and problem agitation; must deliver value in first 5 seconds before skip button appears
- Bumper ads: Use as sequential companion to in-stream, reinforce brand/message with frequency at low CPM
- Why: Widest reach, lowest CPM, builds awareness efficiently at scale

**MOFU — Non-Skippable In-Stream (15 seconds) + In-Feed Video Ads:**
- Non-skippable: Use for high-impact differentiation messaging with guaranteed delivery of full message; reserve for high-quality creative only
- In-feed (YouTube search + related videos): Appears in YouTube search results and next to related content; targets active searchers, typically higher intent
- Why: Captures buyers in research mode, positions brand alongside competitor content

**BOFU — Video Action Campaigns (VAC) with skippable in-stream + companion banner:**
- Use Google's Video Action Campaign type (now feeds into Performance Max as well)
- Includes CTA overlay and companion banner that persist after skip
- Optimizes automatically for your conversion goal (demo request, form fill)
- Why: Highest direct conversion efficiency; Google's algorithm optimizes toward your CPA target

**Retargeting — Skippable In-Stream with personalized messaging:**
- Separate creative per audience segment (pipeline stage, persona, competitor consideration)
- Sequence creative: view TOFU → serve MOFU → view MOFU → serve BOFU (story arc approach)
- Why: Context-aware messaging dramatically outperforms generic retargeting

## 9. Failure Modes & Prevention

**Failure Mode 1: Reaching non-ICP audiences and burning budget on irrelevant views**
- Symptom: High view count, very low conversion rate, demo requests from SMBs when targeting enterprise
- Prevention: Layer Custom Intent + Customer Match exclusions (exclude current customers, personal email domains) + channel-level exclusions; review audience insights report weekly for seniority and industry distribution
- Fix: Add negative affinity audiences (Consumer interests, Personal Finance), tighten to custom intent-only audiences for BOFU

**Failure Mode 2: Creative with poor 5-second hook causing high skip rates and wasted spend**
- Symptom: VTR below 15% for skippable ads, high CPV, low earned actions
- Prevention: Test multiple hooks in first 30 days before scaling budget; hook must state the buyer's problem or outcome in first 3 words; avoid logo or brand name as first visual
- Fix: Recut existing video to lead with strongest moment; use in-feed format (no skip option) while testing new hooks

**Failure Mode 3: Attribution gap — YouTube influence invisible in CRM, creating "prove it" conversations with finance**
- Symptom: YouTube shows conversions in Google Ads but CRM shows 0 pipeline attributed; leadership questions YouTube's ROI
- Prevention: Set up UTM tracking before launch, verify GA4 → CRM pipeline, implement view-through conversion import from CRM
- Fix: Pull YouTube audience overlap report (% of pipeline accounts that were served YouTube impressions in prior 90 days) as proxy attribution evidence; use Google's Brand Lift Study for awareness campaigns

## Integration Tips

- **Google Ads + Salesforce/HubSpot:** Use Google Ads offline conversion import to push Salesforce opportunity creation events back to Google Ads — this allows the algorithm to optimize toward qualified pipeline, not just form fills. Set up via Zapier or native Salesforce/HubSpot Google Ads integration.
- **6sense or Demandbase overlay:** Import your target account list from 6sense/Demandbase into Google Ads as a Customer Match audience. Use this as a positive audience layer on TOFU campaigns to bias impressions toward ICP accounts identified as "in-market" by your ABM platform.
- **Google Analytics 4:** Link GA4 to Google Ads and enable Audience Export — build GA4 audiences (e.g., "Visited Pricing Page + Watched Demo Video + Not a Customer") and import to Google Ads for precision retargeting unavailable via standard remarketing.
- **YouTube Analytics → CRM enrichment:** Use YouTube Analytics API (or a tool like Tubes2CRM) to extract the YouTube channel IDs and company signals from engaged viewers — cross-reference with Clearbit or ZoomInfo to identify which company accounts are watching your content.
- **Slack alerting via Zapier:** Set up a Zap that fires when a YouTube-influenced lead converts to a demo request in HubSpot/Salesforce, posting a Slack notification to the demand gen team with the UTM source and account details.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Budget depletes before end of day with few conversions on BOFU Video Action campaigns.**
Solution: Limit your Video Action campaign to the highest-intent placements using placement targeting (target your own YouTube channel, competitor YouTube channels, specific B2B playlist URLs) rather than running broad. Also, set an hourly impression cap at campaign level and shift 20% of BOFU budget to in-feed video formats, which have lower CPMs and higher intent for B2B searches.

**Problem: Custom Intent audiences are too small to spend budget (estimated reach <100,000).**
Solution: Expand your keyword list for Custom Intent — add broader category terms, related job titles, and industry publications to your URL-based custom intent. Supplement with Google's built-in in-market segments (layer as "observation" initially to gauge performance before restricting). For very niche ICPs (e.g., defense software buyers), accept that YouTube reach is limited and allocate a larger % of budget to LinkedIn Ads.

**Problem: View-through conversions are inflating reported CPA, making results look better than pipeline data supports.**
Solution: Separate your reporting view — pull "click-through conversions only" as your primary success metric. Use view-through conversions as a secondary signal for brand awareness campaigns, not as primary CPA optimization data. Cross-reference every week with CRM pipeline data: if Google Ads shows 50 conversions but CRM shows 5 YouTube-attributed MQLs, your form tracking may be double-counting or your demo landing page has a tracking gap.
