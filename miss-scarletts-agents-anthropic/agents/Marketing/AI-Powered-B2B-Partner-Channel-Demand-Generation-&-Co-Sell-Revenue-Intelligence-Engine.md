## Success Metrics

**Program-Level KPIs:**
- Partner-sourced pipeline as % of total pipeline (target: 20-30% for scaled programs)
- Partner-sourced ARR as % of new ARR (target: 15-25%)
- Partner-influenced ARR (broader definition, target: 30-40%)
- MDF ROI: partner pipeline generated per $1 of MDF spent (healthy ratio: 5:1 minimum)

**Partner Health Indicators:**
- Partner activation rate: % of recruited partners who register a deal within 90 days (target: >40%)
- Deal registration rate: % of partner opportunities formally registered (target: >65%)
- Partner win rate vs. direct win rate (partner-assisted deals should win at equal or higher rates)
- Partner portal engagement: monthly active partners as % of total partner base (target: >35%)

**Operational Efficiency:**
- MDF reimbursement cycle time: submission to payment in <21 business days
- Deal registration response SLA: partner notified of approval within 2 business days
- Time from partner recruitment to first deal registered (target: <60 days)

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Set up a custom "Partner" pipeline stage in HubSpot CRM; add "Deal Source = Partner" as a required field at deal creation; build a deal registration workflow that auto-notifies the owning partner manager and stamps registration date
- Use HubSpot Lists to segment partner contacts by tier; automate tier upgrade/downgrade emails based on deal revenue custom properties
- Connect PartnerStack or Impartner to HubSpot via Zapier or native integration to sync partner deal data and MDF requests without manual entry

**Salesforce:**
- Activate Partner Community Cloud or a PRM app (Allbound, Salesforce PRM) to give partners a portal with deal registration, content, and MDF requests
- Create a "Partner Influenced" opportunity field with a picklist (Partner Sourced / Partner Assisted / Partner Referenced) for attribution reporting
- Build a Salesforce report for the monthly channel dashboard: Partner by Tier → Pipeline by Stage → MDF Spend → Win Rate → Partner ARR

**Notion/Google Sheets:**
- Export the QBR template as a Notion database with partner-specific pages; use Notion's relational databases to link partner contacts, opportunities, and MDF requests
- Use Google Sheets for MDF budget tracking: one tab per partner tier, with conditional formatting flagging utilization above 80% of annual allocation

**Zapier/Make:**
- Trigger: partner submits MDF request (via Typeform or HubSpot form) → Zapier creates approval task in Asana → notifies channel marketing manager via Slack → logs to Google Sheet budget tracker
- Trigger: new deal registered in PRM → Zapier creates opportunity in HubSpot/Salesforce with partner name, tier, and registration date → assigns to partner manager's queue

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partners recruit to the program but never register a deal — low activation rate**
Solution: Activation failure is almost always an enablement gap, not a motivation gap. Audit the first 30-day partner experience: is the partner portal confusing, are sales plays unclear, or is the certification path too long? Implement a mandatory 90-day activation plan with a named partner manager check-in at days 15, 30, and 60. Add a "first deal bonus" — extra MDF or a SPiFF for the first closed deal — to create immediate incentive.

**Problem: Direct sales team registers deals before partners can, creating channel conflict**
Solution: Implement deal registration protection windows (30-60-90 days by tier) enforced in your CRM with automated conflict detection. Create a clear rules-of-engagement document co-signed by VP Sales and VP Alliances specifying who owns what territory and when partner protection applies. Run a joint training session so direct AEs understand the partner program economics and stop cannibalizing partner deals.

**Problem: MDF is being spent but partner pipeline isn't materializing — poor MDF ROI**
Solution: Most MDF waste comes from funding activities (sponsorships, swag, webinars) with no pipeline follow-up. Shift from pre-approval based on activity to post-approval based on proof of pipeline. Require partners to submit the list of leads generated within 14 days of campaign completion; block reimbursement until leads are entered into CRM with follow-up activity. Redirect MDF away from awareness-only spend toward co-sell campaigns with direct pipeline conversion potential.
