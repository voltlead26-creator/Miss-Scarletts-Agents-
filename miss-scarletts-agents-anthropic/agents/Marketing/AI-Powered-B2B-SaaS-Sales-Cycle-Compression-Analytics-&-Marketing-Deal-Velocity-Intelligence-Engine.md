## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: CRM doesn't track content consumption — no data to build the velocity correlation matrix.
**Fix**: Implement a minimal content tracking layer in 48 hours: (1) tag all Highspot/Seismic/Google Drive shared links with UTM parameters, (2) log content share events as CRM activities using Zapier (HubSpot) or Flow (Salesforce), (3) for deals already in motion, have AEs retroactively log content shared in the past 30 days. Run the analysis on a 60-day cohort once data collection is live.

**Problem**: Sales resists attribution — AEs say marketing content doesn't influence their deals and the data is wrong.
**Fix**: Don't lead with attribution; lead with the "deal stall rescue" angle. Show AEs a specific stall situation from the past quarter and which content asset unblocked it. Build a "Stall Rescue Kit" — a curated set of 3–5 assets for each common stall scenario — and give it to AEs as a tool they asked for, not a marketing attribution exercise. Once adoption is high, attribution data becomes accurate automatically.

**Problem**: The analysis shows marketing-influenced deals are faster, but causality is unclear — maybe better-fit deals just happen to consume more content.
**Fix**: Control for deal quality by segmenting the analysis by ICP fit score (high-fit vs. low-fit accounts) and running the velocity comparison within each segment. If marketing-influenced high-fit deals still outpace non-influenced high-fit deals by 10+ days, that's strong evidence for marketing's causal contribution. Supplement with a small cohort experiment: for 30 days, deliberately remove content from a random 20% of deals in one segment and measure velocity difference.
