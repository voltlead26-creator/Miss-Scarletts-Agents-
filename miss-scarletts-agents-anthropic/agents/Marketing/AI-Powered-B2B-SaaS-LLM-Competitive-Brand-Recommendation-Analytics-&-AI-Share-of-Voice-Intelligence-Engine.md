## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI models give different answers each time I run the same query, making it hard to track consistently.**
Solution: This is expected — LLMs are non-deterministic. Use two tactics to create consistent measurement: (1) Run each query 3 times and use the modal/majority response as your baseline. (2) Track the *minimum* score (worst case) rather than average — this represents your floor and is the metric you're working to improve. For automation, use a temperature setting of 0 via API to maximize response consistency when building your tracking system.

**Problem: I don't have G2 review volume data for competitors to assess the review gap.**
Solution: Review counts are publicly visible on G2, Capterra, and Gartner Peer Insights without login. Simply visit each competitor's profile and record the number. For a quick proxy, use G2's "Compare" feature which shows side-by-side review counts. Review count is one of the highest-correlation signals with LLM citation frequency — it's worth tracking manually even before you have automation in place.

**Problem: My brand has very low AI Share of Voice (<15%) and the 90-day roadmap feels overwhelming — where do I start?**
Solution: If starting from near-zero AI visibility, follow this 3-priority sequence before the full roadmap: (1) Ensure Wikipedia article exists and has sourced facts including funding, customer count, and founding year — this single action improves brand entity recognition across all models within 30 days. (2) Run an aggressive G2 review generation campaign to reach 500+ reviews — models heavily weight review site presence. (3) Create ONE flagship "best [your category] software" SEO piece targeting your highest-volume discovery query with full structured FAQ section and comparison tables. These three actions typically produce a measurable 10-15% AI SoV improvement before tackling the full roadmap.
