## SECTION 2: MASTER VIDEO SCRIPT FRAMEWORK
Build scripts for each trigger type. Each script must be 60-90 seconds when read at natural conversational pace (≈130-150 words).

SCRIPT STRUCTURE (non-negotiable):
[0:00-0:05] PATTERN INTERRUPT HOOK
  → Start mid-sentence or with a bold claim. Never "Hi [Name], my name is..."
  → Reference their trigger in the first breath

[0:05-0:20] TRIGGER-TO-PAIN BRIDGE  
  → Connect what you observed (trigger) to the downstream business impact
  → Use "which means" language: "[Trigger] → which typically means [business pain]"
  → Make them feel seen, not stalked

[0:20-0:45] CREDIBILITY ACCELERATOR
  → One ultra-specific customer story from a similar company
  → Format: "[Similar company type] was dealing with [exact same pain] → they did [specific action with your product] → result: [specific metric] in [specific timeframe]"
  → Zero vague language ("some of our customers..." is banned)

[0:45-1:10] MICRO-COMMITMENT CTA
  → Ask for something tiny, not a demo — make saying yes feel costless
  → Options ranked by conversion rate:
    1. "Does this resonate? Just reply with a thumbs up and I'll send you [specific asset]"
    2. "Is [specific pain] something you're actively working on this quarter?"
    3. "Worth a 10-minute call this week? I'll do the homework beforehand."
  → End with energy — upswing in voice, not trailing off

## Integration Tips

**Loom / Vidyard / BombBomb:**
- Use the "Custom Thumbnail" feature to upload a graphic with the prospect's name or company — this alone can lift open rates 20-30% vs. auto-generated thumbnails
- Enable watch notifications so reps can follow up within 5 minutes of a prospect opening the video (reply rate spikes 4x with sub-5-minute follow-up)
- Set up Vidyard or Loom webhooks to push watch events directly to Salesforce Activity or HubSpot Timeline

**Clay (AI enrichment for personalization at scale):**
- Build a Clay table pulling LinkedIn job postings, funding data, and tech stack signals
- Use the AI column in Clay to auto-generate the trigger-to-pain bridge sentence for each prospect
- Output the complete personalized email body from Clay directly into Outreach or Salesloft as a sequence step

**Outreach / Salesloft / Apollo:**
- Insert the video email as Step 1 of a dedicated "Video Prospecting" sequence
- Use A/B test mode to split-test 2 subject line variants against each other for the first 50 sends
- Map the {{trigger_event}} and {{peer_company}} variables to custom prospect fields populated by Clay or ZoomInfo enrichment

**HubSpot / Salesforce:**
- Create a custom "Video Prospecting" activity type to log all video sends separately from standard emails
- Build a dashboard tracking: videos sent → opened → watched (50%+) → replied → meeting booked → opportunity created
- Set up automated task reminders for Day 3, Day 7, and Day 14 follow-up based on video send date

**Synthesia / HeyGen (AI-generated personalized video at scale):**
- Use AI avatar videos for high-volume prospecting (50+ videos/day) where personal recording isn't feasible
- Personalize the script with dynamic variables (prospect name, company, trigger) while keeping the background and avatar consistent
- Test AI video against human-recorded video — at most B2B segments, human-recorded outperforms AI-generated by 15-25% on reply rate, but AI-generated scales 10x faster

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low video open rate (<30%) despite compelling thumbnail**
Solution: The issue is almost certainly the subject line, not the thumbnail. Test 5 subject lines on batches of 20 sends each before scaling. Prioritize subject lines that reference a specific trigger event (e.g., "your Series B announcement") over generic curiosity lines. Also check email deliverability — video emails with embedded thumbnails can trigger spam filters if your domain health is poor.

**Problem: High open rate but low watch rate (prospects opening email but not clicking play)**
Solution: Your thumbnail or opening thumbnail frame isn't creating enough curiosity or relevance to earn the click. Test: (1) add the prospect's company name or logo to the thumbnail graphic, (2) use a whiteboard with a handwritten note rather than a face-cam thumbnail, (3) add the text "60 seconds" or "quick watch" next to the play button to reduce perceived time commitment.

**Problem: Strong watch rate but no replies**
Solution: Your CTA is broken — either too vague ("let me know if you want to chat"), too large an ask ("book a full discovery call"), or delivered with low energy at the end of the video. Rewatch your video ending. The ask should be a micro-commitment: a binary yes/no question, a request to reply with a thumbs up, or a link to a 1-question survey. Test replacing your CTA video section without re-recording the whole video.
