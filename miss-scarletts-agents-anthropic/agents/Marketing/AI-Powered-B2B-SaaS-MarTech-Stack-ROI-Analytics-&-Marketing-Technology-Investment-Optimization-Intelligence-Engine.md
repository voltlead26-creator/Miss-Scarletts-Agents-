# AI-Powered B2B SaaS MarTech Stack ROI Analytics & Marketing Technology Investment Optimization Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** b2b, martech, analytics, roi, marketing-operations, stack-optimization, budget

## Overview
Transforms your marketing technology spend data, tool utilization metrics, and pipeline contribution signals into a board-ready MarTech ROI report — mapping every tool, license, and platform to business outcomes, surfacing underutilized investments, and producing a data-driven rationalization roadmap. Use this prompt quarterly or during annual budget planning to justify your stack, eliminate waste, and build the business case for strategic technology investments.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Pull "last marketing touchpoint" and "all marketing touchpoints" fields into your stack analysis to build true multi-touch attribution by tool. Create a custom report: "Opportunities by First MarTech Tool Touched" to see which tools generate pipeline vs. just influence it.
- **Vendr / Zluri / Zylo:** If you use a SaaS spend management platform, export the utilization dashboard as CSV and paste directly into this prompt. These tools already have seat utilization % and cost-per-active-user data pre-calculated.
- **Google Sheets / Notion:** Build a live MarTech stack registry as a shared Google Sheet with columns: Tool | Cost | Seats | Owner | Renewal Date | ROI Grade. Run this prompt quarterly and update the sheet. Share with your CFO as your "stack audit log."
- **Gong / Chorus Conversation Intelligence:** Pull "deals where Gong was used in deal review" vs. "deals where it wasn't" to measure Gong's actual win-rate impact — this is your killer data point for Gong renewal justification.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** You don't have clean utilization data — you only know what you pay, not how many people use each tool.
**Solution:** Email each tool's CSM and request a "utilization report" — all enterprise tools (Salesforce, Marketo, 6sense, Gong, etc.) have this in their admin console and CSMs will share it on request. For tools without a CSM, check: Settings → Admin → Users → "Last login" date for each user. This gives you rough MAU. Alternatively, survey your team: "Which tools do you use daily? Weekly? Never?" — a 10-question Google Form takes 20 minutes and gives you directionally accurate utilization data.

**Problem:** You can't attribute pipeline to specific MarTech tools because your CRM doesn't track which tools were involved in a deal.
**Solution:** Start with a proxy metric: "campaigns run in Tool X that influenced opportunities." Every MAP (Marketo, HubSpot) tracks this. For tools without direct pipeline attribution, use a correlation proxy: "deals where a Gong call was recorded in the last 30 days" vs. overall win rate. This isn't perfect attribution, but it's directionally accurate and defensible for budget conversations.

**Problem:** Your CFO doesn't believe the "pipeline influenced" number because "influenced" is fuzzy.
**Solution:** Switch your framing from "influenced" to "pipeline where [tool] was the ONLY touchpoint before form fill." This is a much smaller but ironclad number. Present two figures: (1) conservative attribution (last-touch or sole-touch) and (2) full-assist attribution (any touchpoint). The conservative number wins CFO arguments; the assist number wins CMO arguments. Use both.
