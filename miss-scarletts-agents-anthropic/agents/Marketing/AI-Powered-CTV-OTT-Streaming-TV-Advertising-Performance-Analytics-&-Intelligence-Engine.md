━━━ MODULE 2: REACH, FREQUENCY & DELIVERY ANALYSIS ━━━

**Household Frequency Distribution Audit:**

CTV campaigns fail most often not from weak creative or poor targeting — but from frequency abuse against an undersized audience pool. Diagnose:

- *Optimal frequency curve:* What frequency level correlates with peak site visit rates? Benchmark: frequency 1–2 builds initial recall; frequency 3–5 drives peak consideration; frequency 6+ shows diminishing returns; frequency 10+ often triggers avoidance behavior (viewers switching content, minimizing screen)
- *Frequency cap gap:* If [X%] of reached households have received 10+ exposures in 30 days, the frequency cap is too loose or the targetable audience pool is too small for the budget level
- *Audience pool tension calculation:* If your target segment has [X million HH] nationally and you're spending [$X]/month at [$X CPM], the minimum forced frequency is (budget ÷ CPM × 1000) ÷ (target HH pool × reach rate). If forced frequency exceeds 12 in a 30-day period, expand audience definition or reduce budget concentration
- *Cross-platform frequency deduplication:* Are households receiving CTV impressions from BOTH your DSP programmatic buy AND your direct publisher IO simultaneously? This effectively doubles frequency without your awareness — fix by sharing household suppression lists across all buying paths or using a unified frequency management layer

**Reach Extension Opportunities:**
- Which demographic or behavioral sub-segments are under-reached relative to their index in your customer database?
- DMA concentration: Which markets show high frequency but low unique reach — signaling over-concentration in a small local household pool?
- Cord-cutter vs. cord-never segmentation: Are you reaching the highest-value streaming-native households (those who have never subscribed to linear TV) or primarily reaching hybrid viewers who also consume linear?

**Pacing & Delivery Quality:**
- Impression pacing pattern: Front-loaded delivery (burns 70% of budget in first half of flight) creates frequency spikes followed by reach gaps — flag if this pattern exists
- Under-delivery tracking: If publisher X has delivered only 60% of contracted impressions at flight midpoint, initiate make-good conversation and prepare budget reallocation to over-performing publishers
- PMP deal win rate: Win rates below 40% in Private Marketplace deals indicate your floor bid is below market clearing price — either raise bid price or negotiate guaranteed deal structures for priority inventory access

━━━ MODULE 3: AUDIENCE SEGMENT PERFORMANCE ANALYSIS ━━━

**Segment Efficiency Matrix:**

For every active audience segment, calculate and compare:
- *CPCV by segment:* Which segments watch ads to completion most consistently? High VCR = higher audience engagement and attention quality
- *Site visit lift by segment:* Which segments show the highest incremental visit rate post-exposure vs. control? This reveals which audiences are actually influenced by CTV exposure vs. those who would have visited anyway
- *Cost per conversion by segment:* Apply (visit rate × on-site conversion rate) to determine which segments generate acquisitions within target CPA
- *Segment overlap:* Are behavioral segments like "In-Market: Home Furnishings" and "Lifestyle: Interior Design Enthusiasts" pointing at the same 80% of households? High overlap wastes budget — merge overlapping segments or apply household deduplication at the DSP level

**Audience Quality Signals:**
- First-party data match rate: If you uploaded a customer suppression list or lookalike seed, what is the household match rate? Benchmark: 40–70% for IP-based matching; 20–50% for email hash matching. Low match rates signal list quality issues or data partnership gaps
- Third-party segment accuracy: Run a segment validation test — pull the post-exposure brand lift split by audience segment from your measurement vendor. If "In-Market: Mattress Shoppers" shows no measurable brand lift while "Lifestyle: Home Improvement Enthusiasts" shows +14% recall lift, the first segment is likely stale or miscategorized
- Lookalike vs. curated third-party segment comparison: Are model-built lookalike audiences (built from your CRM best customers) outperforming purchased third-party behavioral segments? This gap quantifies the value of your first-party data in CTV targeting

**Suppression Gap Analysis:**
- Recent converters: Are customers who purchased in the last 90 days suppressed from prospecting campaigns? Showing brand awareness ads to recent buyers wastes impressions and dilutes efficiency metrics
- Zero-conversion segments: Which audience segments have 0 attributed conversions after [1M+] impressions and multiple flights? Build exclusion lists for these segments and redirect budget to proven performers
- Geographic waste: Are households in states or regions where you don't ship, operate, or serve being exposed? National CTV buys for regional businesses commonly waste 20–35% of impressions on unreachable geography

━━━ MODULE 5: ATTRIBUTION & REVENUE IMPACT ANALYSIS ━━━

**Attribution Methodology Transparency Audit:**

CTV attribution is the most contested measurement space in digital advertising. Be explicit about what your measurement actually proves vs. implies:

*IP-Based Household Attribution (most common in DSPs):*
- A household sees your CTV ad → their IP address is logged → if the same IP visits your website within [X] days, the visit is "attributed" to CTV
- Accuracy limitations: IP matching is 65–80% accurate due to shared IPs (apartment buildings, university campuses, corporate offices); dynamic IPs that change daily or weekly break the match chain
- Window inflation test: Run the same data with 3-day, 7-day, and 30-day windows — the attribution count difference reveals how much "CTV attribution" is organic behavior coinciding with ad exposure. If 30-day count is 4× the 7-day count for the same exposure cohort, your 30-day window is capturing primarily organic visits

*ACR (Automatic Content Recognition) Panel Attribution:*
- Available via Samba TV, Inscape (Vizio data), or Comscore CTV — measures actual TV ad exposure via pixel recognition on smart TVs
- More accurate than IP matching but limited to panel size (10–30M US smart TVs depending on vendor)
- Best use: Upper-funnel brand lift validation and reach/frequency measurement; not suited for conversion attribution

*Geo-Holdout Incrementality Testing (Gold Standard):*
- Identify 6–10 matched DMAs; run CTV in markets A–D, withhold from E–G (holdout group); measure conversion rate difference during and after flight
- True incremental lift = [(exposed market conversion rate - holdout market conversion rate)] ÷ (holdout market conversion rate) × 100
- Statistical requirements: Minimum 6-week test duration, 90% confidence threshold, ≥1,000 conversions in each market group for significance
- Recommended holdout: 25–30% of planned markets withheld — sufficient holdout without sacrificing too much reach

**Cross-Channel Conversion Path Analysis:**
- *CTV → branded search spike:* If branded search volume in your CTV-targeted DMAs increases [10–25%] vs. non-targeted control DMAs during the flight, this is strong evidence of ad recall even when direct IP attribution is limited
- *CTV → social retargeting → conversion:* Map what % of CTV-exposed households also clicked a Meta or Google retargeting ad before converting — true attribution credit should be shared; the CTV delivered brand awareness that made the retargeting click possible
- *New vs. returning visitor split:* CTV's job is reaching net-new households — if ≥65% of CTV-attributed site visitors are first-time visitors vs. returning, the campaign is successfully expanding reach rather than just re-engaging known customers

**Full-Funnel Revenue Impact (B2B SaaS / Enterprise):**
If using CTV for B2B account-based brand awareness:
- Branded search volume lift within target account headquarters DMAs (pull from Google Search Console geo reports)
- MQL quality signal: Do MQLs sourced from CTV-targeted markets enter sales calls with higher brand recognition (measured via discovery call recordings in Gong/Chorus)?
- Pipeline velocity: Do accounts in CTV-heavy markets show shorter time-to-demo or higher win rates, controlling for account characteristics? This is the downstream B2B CTV value case

**Creative Fatigue Detection & Response:**
- VCR declining >3% week-over-week for a specific creative = fatigue signal — rotate to a new creative or apply a per-creative frequency cap (separate from campaign-level cap)
- Completion rate curve analysis: If VCR drops sharply in the final 5 seconds of a 30s spot, the closing CTA or end-card is causing abandonment. Restructure so your brand mark and core message appear within the first 5–7 seconds — critical for brand recall even when viewers skip or change content
- Per-creative frequency floor: If the average household has seen Creative A 8+ times in 3 weeks, introduce Creatives B and C to reset novelty without reducing household reach. Creative rotation restores completion rates to initial levels within 1–2 weeks

**Sequential Storytelling Performance:**
If running a sequential narrative series (Ad 1: Problem awareness → Ad 2: Solution introduction → Ad 3: Proof + CTA):
- Sequence progression rate: What % of target households received exposure to all 3 sequential ads within the intended pacing window?
- Lift by sequence depth: Compare conversion rates for households who saw 1 ad vs. 2 ads vs. all 3 — the incremental lift at each stage quantifies sequential storytelling ROI
- Optimal inter-ad pacing: Benchmark 3–7 days between sequential exposures to allow message processing without losing narrative continuity; gaps >14 days typically break the story thread

**15-Second vs. 30-Second Format Economics:**
- 30-second spots deliver 30–40% higher brand recall vs. 15-second spots but cost 2× more per completed view
- 15-second spots deliver superior CPCV economics and work well for retargeting audiences already familiar with the brand — they need a CTA reminder, not a full brand introduction
- Decision framework: Use 30s for awareness/new-market entry and unknown brand campaigns; use 15s for retargeting warm audiences and promotional offers to brand-familiar households

## Troubleshooting
- **Problem:** CTV attribution shows strong site visit lift (4.2% exposed vs. 2.1% control) but ROAS is negative — CTV-attributed visitors are not converting at the rate needed to justify CPM investment.
  **Solution:** Applying direct-response ROAS expectations to a brand awareness CTV campaign is a measurement category error. Restructure success metrics: CTV's job is to (1) expand reach to net-new households who don't know your brand — target ≥65% first-time visitors among CTV-attributed traffic; (2) build brand recall that reduces friction in the conversion funnel downstream — measure branded search lift in targeted DMAs during flight; (3) grow the retargeting pool — how many CTV-exposed households entered your Meta/Google retargeting audience for the next conversion stage? The correct CTV attribution story is: awareness exposure → branded search or social retargeting engagement → conversion. Measure CTV's contribution to pipeline entry and retargeting audience growth, not just last-touch attributed conversions.

- **Problem:** Video Completion Rate is 82% — below the 92% CTV benchmark — and publisher-level reporting doesn't identify which placements are dragging the average down.
  **Solution:** VCR below 90% in CTV almost always traces to one of three causes: (1) Mobile in-app video inventory is being classified as "OTT" or "CTV" by your DSP — filter your supply path to CTV device types only (Smart TV, streaming stick) and explicitly exclude mobile and desktop in your inventory controls; (2) Low-quality open-exchange programmatic inventory where CTV fraud (bot traffic, apps misclassified as TV apps, device spoofing) inflates impression counts with near-zero human completion — run a supply path audit and build a publisher whitelist; shift all open exchange spend to PMPs or direct publisher buys immediately; (3) Creative quality issue — if audio or visual fidelity drops after the first 5 seconds (common with compressed assets or improper spec delivery to different publisher environments), viewers switch content. Pull the completion rate curve frame-by-frame from your DSP creative report to identify the exact dropout second and fix the creative asset.

- **Problem:** Your geo-holdout incrementality test shows only +4% incremental conversion lift — well below the 10% benchmark — but a brand lift survey simultaneously shows +12% aided recall lift. These results seem contradictory.
  **Solution:** This is a valuable and common finding — CTV is successfully building brand memory (recall) but not yet converting that recall into near-term purchase behavior. This typically occurs when: (1) The creative builds awareness but lacks a compelling reason-to-act-now — add a 15s DR companion spot to your 30s brand spot rotation; the brand spot builds recall and the DR spot converts it within the same session; (2) The purchase cycle is longer than the incrementality measurement window — if NestWell's average decision cycle from first brand exposure to purchase is 45–90 days (researching mattresses is a high-consideration purchase), a 30-day incrementality test will undercount true incremental conversions; extend the measurement window to 90 days and re-evaluate; (3) The conversion action measured in your incrementality test is too downstream — measure mid-funnel proxy events (product page visits, comparison page views, email signups) in addition to purchases to capture the full range of CTV-influenced behaviors that eventually convert.
