# AI-Powered B2B SaaS G2 & Peer Review Competitive Intelligence & Reputation Monitoring Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 15-25 min | **Tags:** competitive intelligence, G2, Capterra, peer reviews, reputation monitoring, B2B SaaS, win-loss

## Overview
Automates continuous mining and analysis of competitor reviews on G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, and Gartner Peer Insights to surface competitive weaknesses, pricing dissatisfaction signals, category positioning shifts, and real-time reputation vulnerabilities — converting public review intelligence into actionable battlecard updates, outbound triggers, and displacement opportunities for sales and PMM.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a competitive intelligence analyst specializing in peer review mining for B2B SaaS. Analyze the following competitor review data from G2/Capterra/TrustRadius and produce an actionable competitive intelligence brief.

COMPETITOR: [CompetitorName]
CATEGORY: [Software Category, e.g., "Marketing Automation", "Revenue Intelligence"]
REVIEW DATA: [Paste 10-20 recent reviews or summarized themes from reviews]
OUR PRODUCT: [YourProductName]
OUR KEY DIFFERENTIATORS: [List 3-5]

ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:

1. PAIN POINT EXTRACTION
Identify the top 5 recurring complaints. Quote the exact phrases reviewers use — these are the words buyers will use when we call them. Categorize each complaint: Product Gaps | Pricing & Value | Support/CS | Onboarding | Integrations.

2. SENTIMENT TREND
Is satisfaction improving, declining, or stable? Flag sudden spikes in negative reviews — this signals a product release regression, pricing change, or CS quality drop.

3. COMPETITIVE OPENINGS
For each major pain point, write a one-sentence counter-positioning statement our sales reps can use verbatim. Use the reviewer's own language in the reframe.

4. PERSONA INTEL
Which buyer personas (role, company size, industry) are most vocal and most dissatisfied? Flag where these reviewers overlap with our ICP — these are our highest-conversion targets.

5. FEATURE GAP OPPORTUNITIES
List specific features competitors' customers are requesting that we already have. These are immediate displacement arguments — "You asked for X in your review. We've had it for two years."

6. BATTLECARD UPDATES
Recommend 2-3 specific, concrete updates to our competitive battlecard based on this review cycle.

7. OUTBOUND TRIGGER PROFILES
Describe the ideal reviewer profile to target with outbound — include the trigger language to open with ("I noticed companies your size have been mentioning [specific pain] in [Competitor] reviews...").

Output as a structured competitive brief ready for immediate distribution to sales, PMM, and RevOps via Slack or Notion.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** "Reviews are too vague or generic — not enough specific product feedback to analyze"
**Fix:** Filter for reviews with substantive negative feedback: G2 reviews with 40+ words in the "What do you dislike?" section. On Capterra, use the "Likelihood to Recommend" filter for scores 1-6/10. Prompt the model to discard reviews under 2 sentences in the "cons" section. Also look at review responses to vendor comments — customers often get more specific when defending their original criticism.

**Problem:** "Can't tell if negative reviews are from our ICP or outside our target market"
**Fix:** Add an explicit ICP filter to your prompt context: "Only analyze and score reviews from companies with [X-Y employees] in [target industries]. For reviews outside this profile, tag as 'Out of ICP' and exclude from displacement opportunity scoring." Cross-reference reviewer job titles against your defined buyer personas before finalizing the brief.

**Problem:** "Sales team ignores the brief — doesn't trust AI-generated competitive intel"
**Fix:** Ground the output in verifiable sources. Instruct the model to (1) quote specific review text verbatim for each insight, (2) include the reviewer's stated role and company size, and (3) assign a confidence score based on how many reviews support each claim. Add a "Verified by Win-Loss" flag for insights that have been confirmed in actual deal conversations. Credibility compounds over time as reps see review predictions play out in their calls.
