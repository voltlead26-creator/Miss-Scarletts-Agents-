## Troubleshooting

**Issue 1: Always-on campaigns generate volume but pipeline quality is low (low MQL-to-opportunity rate)**
Solution: Audit your intent signal tiers — most teams set intent thresholds too low, flooding the funnel with low-quality accounts. Tighten Tier 3 and 4 to require 2+ intent signals (e.g., intent score + specific page visit) before triggering pipeline acceleration actions. Also verify your ICP filter is applied before any account enters the always-on system.

**Issue 2: Automation rules fire correctly but human review bottlenecks kill velocity**
Solution: Categorize actions by impact × risk. Low-impact / low-risk automations (bid adjustments, email sends, retargeting pixel additions) should require zero human approval. Reserve human review only for actions that commit >$1,000 in budget or send outbound to C-suite contacts. Document approved thresholds in your marketing automation platform settings.

**Issue 3: Pipeline is inconsistent month-to-month despite the always-on architecture**
Solution: Check if your "always-on" campaigns actually have date-specific dependencies (event promos, seasonal offers, quarterly pushes). True always-on demand gen uses evergreen offers (ROI calculators, benchmark reports, free trials, product tours) not time-sensitive CTAs. Audit every active campaign and replace time-sensitive offers with evergreen equivalents. Also check budget pacing — ensure campaigns are set to "standard" or even delivery, not accelerated.
