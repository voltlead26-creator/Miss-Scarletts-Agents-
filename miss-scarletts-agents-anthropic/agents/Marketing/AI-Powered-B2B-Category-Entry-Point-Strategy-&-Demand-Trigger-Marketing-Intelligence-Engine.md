# AI-Powered B2B Category Entry Point Strategy & Demand Trigger Marketing Intelligence Engine - Own the Buying Moments That Define Your Category

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** positioning, brand-strategy, demand-generation, b2b, buyer-psychology, always-on-marketing

## Overview
This engine identifies and maps the specific situational triggers — Category Entry Points (CEPs) — that activate a B2B buyer's consideration of your product category, then generates a systematic always-on marketing program that embeds your brand into those precise moments. Use it when you need to build durable pipeline through memory-structure ownership rather than interrupt-based demand generation.

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Create a custom property "CEP Trigger" on Contact and Deal records; train SDRs to tag which CEP fired when creating an opportunity
- Build smart lists segmented by CEP trigger for automated email nurture sequences
- Use workflow triggers: if contact downloads a CEP-specific asset → enroll in corresponding 4-touch nurture → flag for SDR outreach after 2 engagements

**Salesforce:**
- Add "CEP Trigger" picklist field on Opportunity (populated at Stage 1); use in reporting to correlate CEP with win rate and deal velocity
- Build a CEP attribution dashboard: pipeline by CEP source, win rate by CEP, average deal size by CEP

**Bombora / G2 Buyer Intent:**
- Map CEP-relevant intent topic clusters to each priority CEP; configure alerts when a target account spikes on ≥2 CEP topics simultaneously

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CEP Discovery produces generic triggers that every competitor could claim ("they want to save money," "they need more efficiency").**
Solution: Push the prompt to be more specific by providing 3-5 verbatim quotes from recent sales discovery calls or customer interviews. Generic inputs produce generic CEPs. Force specificity by asking: "What exact words did the last 5 customers use the week before they started evaluating us?" Feed those quotes directly into the prompt.

**Problem: The CEP content plan requires too many assets to produce given team bandwidth.**
Solution: Start with ONE hero CEP asset per priority CEP — the awareness asset — and repurpose it into all other formats (LinkedIn posts, email sequence, paid ad variants, sales one-pager). The prompt's content suite is the ideal state; the practical minimum is one differentiated piece per CEP that you can atomize across channels.

**Problem: Sales team won't tag CEP triggers consistently in CRM, making measurement impossible.**
Solution: Don't ask reps to interpret CEPs — give them a pre-populated picklist with 5-6 plain-English trigger names (e.g., "Supplier Price Shock," "CFO Visibility Demand," "New VP Procurement Hire") and require selection at Stage 1 as a mandatory field. Run a 15-minute enablement session showing how CEP data improves their deal coaching. Measurement only works if the tagging habit is built from day one.
