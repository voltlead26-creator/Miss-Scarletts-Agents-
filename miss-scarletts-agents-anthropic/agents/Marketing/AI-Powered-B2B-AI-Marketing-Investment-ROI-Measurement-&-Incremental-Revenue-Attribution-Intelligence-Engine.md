**Phase 2: Tool-Level Incrementality Measurement**

For each AI tool category, apply the appropriate causal methodology:

INTENT DATA TOOLS (e.g., 6sense, Bombora, G2 Buyer Intent):
- Experiment Design: Matched account pair analysis — for every account flagged as "high intent" by the tool, identify a near-identical account (same firmographic tier, same industry, same lifecycle stage) that was NOT flagged, then compare pipeline generated and win rates at 90/180/365 days
- Control for: SDR coverage, deal size, competitive landscape
- Primary metric: Win rate lift for intent-flagged vs. non-flagged accounts (in %-points, not relative lift)
- Secondary metrics: Sales cycle length delta, pipeline velocity improvement
- Confidence threshold: Minimum 100 account pairs per segment before drawing conclusions

ENRICHMENT & PROSPECTING AI (e.g., Clay, Apollo AI, Clearbit):
- Experiment Design: Random assignment holdout — route 20% of new MQLs through manual enrichment process, 80% through AI enrichment; measure SDR connect rate, meeting booked rate, and opportunity creation rate at 30/60/90 days
- Control for: SDR skill variation (use per-rep analysis, not team aggregates)
- Primary metric: Meetings booked per 100 outreach attempts (AI enrichment vs. manual)
- Revenue metric: Pipeline generated per $1 of enrichment cost (AI vs. manual baseline)

CONVERSATIONAL AI / CHATBOTS (e.g., Drift, Intercom AI, Qualified):
- Experiment Design: Time-of-day and day-of-week geo-matched holdout (chatbot on/off during controlled windows while tracking conversion patterns)
- Alternative: Direct comparison of chatbot-engaged vs. chat-declined visitors with propensity score matching
- Primary metric: Qualified meeting booked rate (visitors who engaged chatbot vs. didn't)
- Guardrail: Form conversion rate must not decline in chatbot-active periods (replacing form = bad; augmenting form = good)

AI CONTENT & PERSONALIZATION TOOLS (e.g., Jasper, Mutiny, Optimizely AI):
- Experiment Design: Classic A/B test — AI-generated content vs. human-written control, with proper sample sizing for statistical significance
- Primary metric: Downstream pipeline influence (not just clicks/opens — requires CRM tracking of content consumption to opportunity creation within 90 days)
- Caution: Creative quality confound — ensure human-written control is your actual best work, not the average

ABM / ORCHESTRATION AI (e.g., Demandbase, Terminus, RollWorks):
- Experiment Design: Account-level randomization — 30% of ICP accounts receive full AI-orchestrated ABM treatment, 30% receive standard demand gen, 30% receive no marketing (pure holdout)
- Measurement period: Minimum 2 full sales cycles (e.g., 180 days for 90-day average close)
- Primary metric: Pipeline created per ICP account (not pipeline per dollar — must normalize by account coverage)
- Board metric: Incremental pipeline attributable to ABM AI above baseline (in $ with confidence interval)

**Phase 3: Portfolio-Level Attribution Model**

AI tools rarely touch deals in isolation. Build a portfolio attribution model:

MULTI-TOOL TOUCH SEQUENCE ANALYSIS:
For closed-won deals in the last 12 months, map AI tool touchpoints:
- Which AI tools were active on the account BEFORE opportunity creation?
- Which tools influenced during active sales cycle?
- What is the average number of AI tool touchpoints per won deal vs. lost deal?

SHAPLEY VALUE ATTRIBUTION (Preferred for Multi-Tool):
Using the game theory Shapley value method, calculate each tool's marginal contribution across all deal permutations where it participated. This eliminates the double-counting that plagues first-touch/last-touch models.

Simplified Shapley Calculation Process:
1. Segment all closed-won deals by AI tools that were "active" (touched account) during the deal
2. For each possible combination of tools, calculate average win rate
3. Each tool's credit = average marginal contribution across all deal combinations it appears in
4. Output: Revenue credit per tool (in $ and %)

INCREMENTALITY SANITY CHECK:
Sum of all tool-level incremental revenue attributions should NOT exceed total marketing-sourced revenue × (1 - organic/brand baseline rate). If attribution sum exceeds this, you have over-attribution — scale down using a proportional correction factor.

**Phase 4: Financial ROI Framework**

DIRECT ROI FORMULA:
- Gross Revenue Attributable = (Incremental win rate lift × Avg deal size × Deals touched per month) × Gross margin %
- Net Revenue ROI = (Gross Revenue Attributable − Tool Cost − Implementation/Ops Cost) / (Tool Cost + Implementation/Ops Cost)
- Payback Period = Tool Cost / Monthly incremental revenue generated

- Internal ops time to manage/tune the tool: [Z hours/week × internal hourly cost]
- Data costs (enrichment credits, API calls): $[W]/mo
- Total fully-loaded monthly cost: $[Sum]

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM**: Create a custom "AI Tool Touchpoints" object that logs which AI tools were active on each account during the sales cycle — this is the foundation of all attribution calculations; without it, you're guessing
- **6sense / Bombora**: Export weekly account-level intent scores and upload to CRM via Zapier or native integration; maintain 12-month history for trend analysis and cohort comparison
- **Google Sheets / Looker Studio**: Build the AI ROI dashboard using a Google Sheet that auto-pulls CRM data via the Salesforce/HubSpot API connector — runs weekly, produces a PDF summary for the CFO the first Monday of each month
- **Notion / Confluence**: Maintain your experiment registry as a Notion database with Status (Planning / Active / Concluded), Hypothesis, Start Date, Primary Metric, Current Result, and Decision fields
- **Clay**: Export enrichment coverage rates and bounce rates by week to track data quality over time; deteriorating data quality is the #1 silent killer of enrichment tool ROI
- **Segment / Rudderstack**: If using a CDP, create AI tool interaction events (e.g., "chatbot_qualified", "intent_flagged", "content_personalized") as first-class events — this enables cohort analysis by AI touchpoint without manual CRM tagging

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: No clean pre-AI baseline data — adopted all tools simultaneously 6-12 months ago, making before/after comparison impossible**
Solution: Use matched cohort analysis going forward rather than retrospectively. Identify accounts currently in your ICP that have NOT been touched by any AI tools yet (often exists in lower-priority segments or geographic markets), use them as your ongoing control group, and build your baseline from current performance with controls, not historical performance without them. Alternatively, use a synthetic control: find 2-3 comparable companies (same size, industry, GTM motion) who haven't adopted AI marketing tools and benchmark your performance trajectory against theirs using public data (G2 review velocity, LinkedIn follower growth, Similarweb traffic).

**Problem: Sales team keeps overriding AI tool recommendations (ignoring 6sense scores, not using Clay sequences), making it impossible to measure AI impact cleanly**
Solution: This is a process fidelity problem before a measurement problem. First, instrument compliance: create a CRM field "AI Tool Guidance Followed (Y/N)" that SDRs fill in. Segment your analysis by compliant vs. non-compliant reps. Present the per-rep ROI data showing that reps following AI guidance consistently outperform those who don't — this creates the internal case for adoption discipline. Then rerun your incrementality study using only compliant reps as your treatment group.

**Problem: CFO doesn't trust "incremental pipeline" as a metric — insists on measuring only closed revenue, but our 90-day sales cycle makes this impossible to show within a quarter**
Solution: Bridge the credibility gap with a staged proof structure. Month 1-3: Show leading indicators (intent-flagged accounts entering pipeline at higher rates, enriched outreach converting to meetings at higher rates). Month 3-6: Show pipeline-stage velocity (intent-flagged deals reaching proposal stage faster). Month 6-12: Show closed revenue correlation (cohort of deals touched by AI tools closing at higher rates). Present this as "the 12-month AI revenue proof" timeline — CMOs who want quarterly proof of a tool affecting 90-day sales cycles are measuring on impossible timelines, and framing this as "here's our committed proof schedule" builds CFO trust better than defending inadequate data.
