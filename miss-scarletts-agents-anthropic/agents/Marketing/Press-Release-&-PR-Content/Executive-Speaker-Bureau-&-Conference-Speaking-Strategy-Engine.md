## Success Metrics

- **Abstract acceptance rate:** 15-25% is excellent for cold submissions; 30%+ if you have existing organizer relationships
- **Stage appearances per quarter:** 2-3 appearances per executive is a healthy cadence without speaker fatigue
- **Post-talk MQL rate:** 3-8% of talk audience should convert to a meaningful action (QR code scan, meeting request, content download)
- **Pipeline influenced:** Each tier-1 talk should influence $50K-$300K in pipeline within 90 days, depending on ACV and audience ICP match
- **Content amplification:** Each talk should generate a minimum of 10 derivative content pieces with combined reach 5-10x the in-room audience
- **Organizer relationship score:** Measure by % of target organizers who respond to outreach, % who invite you back proactively

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Abstracts keep getting rejected despite strong content**
Solution: Most rejections happen because the abstract sounds like a product pitch disguised as education. Run a "sponsor test": read your abstract and ask "could a competitor company give this same talk?" If no, it's too product-specific. Rewrite to focus on the industry problem and methodology, not your solution. Aim for 80% practitioner insight, 20% company credibility. Also check that your title uses active, specific language — vague titles like "AI in HR: What You Need to Know" are rejected at a much higher rate than specific claims like "The 3-Year Recruiting Data That Proves Job Descriptions Are Killing Your Pipeline."

**Problem: Executive speakers are too busy / deprioritize the speaking program**
Solution: Build the system so speaker preparation requires < 2 hours of executive time per talk (not the 8-10 hours most speakers spend). Create a "talk factory" template where PMM or comms team does 90% of the work: research, abstract drafting, slide deck first draft, pre-talk content calendar. The executive reviews, personalizes, and delivers. Run a quarterly "speaker brief" meeting (30 min) where the exec reviews upcoming opportunities. Show pipeline influenced data — executives re-engage immediately when they see a $200K deal in the pipeline that started with a talk they gave.

**Problem: No measurable pipeline from speaking despite good audiences**
Solution: The issue is almost always the lack of a clear post-talk capture mechanism. A generic "visit our website" CTA generates near-zero leads. Fix: (1) Create a talk-specific lead magnet (the research report or framework slides from the talk), (2) Use a unique QR code per event linked to a dedicated landing page with the lead magnet offer, (3) Have 2 SDRs working the event floor immediately after the talk to approach people who watched — strike while attention is hot. Most companies skip step 3 and wonder why the talk generated no pipeline.
