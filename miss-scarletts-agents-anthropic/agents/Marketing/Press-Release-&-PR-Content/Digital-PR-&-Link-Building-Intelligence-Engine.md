## Integration Tips

- **Ahrefs / Semrush**: Export competitor backlink profiles monthly to identify resource pages and journalist sources who have linked to competitors but not you. Feed this list directly into Phase 3 outreach templates.
- **HARO / Connectively / Qwoted**: Set up keyword alerts for your top 10 category terms. Use the HARO Response Template from Phase 2 as a saved snippet in your email client — most winning responses go out within 2 hours of query publication.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Create UTM parameters for all digital PR referral links (utm_source=digital-pr, utm_medium=referral, utm_campaign=link-building). Track MQL conversions from PR referral traffic separately to calculate true link-building ROI.
- **Notion / Airtable**: Build a link tracker database with columns: Publication, DR, Anchor Text, Date Earned, Referral Traffic (monthly), Pipeline Influenced. Share with CMO as part of monthly marketing performance report.
- **Google Search Console**: Set up DR correlation tracking — export monthly impressions and click data for target keywords and overlay against link acquisition timeline to demonstrate causality (not just correlation) to stakeholders.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator**: Use to find and follow target journalists covering your beat before pitching. Track their content for 30 days, engage authentically, then reference their work in your outreach.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate HARO/Connectively query alerts → Slack notification → pre-filled response template using your expert bio and key stats. Reduces response time to under 45 minutes.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Outreach emails get no response even from high-quality publications**
*Solution:* The most common cause is pitching the wrong person — most link-building emails go to editors who don't handle inbound pitches. Use LinkedIn to identify the specific reporter who covers your beat, not the generic "editorial@" address. Also audit your subject lines — if they sound like PR, they get treated like PR. Use sentence-case, no buzzwords, and lead with the specific data insight. If response rate is still under 5%, your linkable asset itself may lack a genuinely counterintuitive hook — the data must surprise or contradict a widely held belief.

**Problem: Earned links aren't moving domain rating or keyword rankings**
*Solution:* Link equity takes 6-12 weeks to propagate in Google's index. Use Ahrefs' "New Backlinks" report to confirm links are being crawled. More importantly, audit whether links are coming from topically relevant pages — a DR 70 link from a page about irrelevant topics passes less authority than a DR 50 link from a page that's directly about your category. Refocus outreach on publications your ICP actually reads and that cover your exact subject matter.

**Problem: HARO/Connectively response rate is below 20%**
*Solution:* Three fixes: (1) Speed matters enormously — queries get 50-200 responses, and journalists often pick from the first 10-15 they receive. Set up real-time alerts and respond within 60 minutes. (2) Credential specificity — "marketing expert" gets ignored; "author of the 2025 B2B Forecasting Report, cited by Forbes and SalesHacker" gets read. Lead with your most impressive, specific credential. (3) Avoid generic advice — journalists need a quotable insight with a surprising angle, not a list of best practices. Every response should contain one statement the journalist hasn't heard a hundred times.
