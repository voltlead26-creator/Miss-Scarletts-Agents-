## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Press release feels generic and doesn't stand out from hundreds of other funding announcements.
**Solution:** Lead the press release with a counter-intuitive market insight or specific metric (e.g., "14 hours per invoice cycle" or "97% gross retention in manufacturing") rather than the funding amount. Journalists receive 200+ funding PRs per week — the hook has to be the problem or data, not the dollar figure. The funding amount belongs in paragraph two.

**Problem:** SDR email responses are low even with the funding hook.
**Solution:** The funding trigger works best when the prospect can see themselves in the customer base. Make Email 1 hyper-specific to the prospect's segment (mention their industry or company size in the body) and reference a customer outcome from their exact vertical. Generic "we raised money" emails perform no better than cold pitches. Personalization at the segment level is the minimum viable bar.

**Problem:** Social posts get low organic reach after the first 24 hours.
**Solution:** Announcement-day velocity is critical. Schedule CEO, co-founders, board members, and key investors to post simultaneously within the first 2 hours. LinkedIn's algorithm rewards early engagement — each early comment or reshare from a high-follower account compounds organic reach. Brief every executive with their platform-specific caption 48 hours before the embargo lifts, not the morning of.
