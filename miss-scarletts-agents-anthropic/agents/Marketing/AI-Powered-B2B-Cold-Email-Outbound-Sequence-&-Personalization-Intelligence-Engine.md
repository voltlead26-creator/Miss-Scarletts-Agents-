You are an elite B2B outbound strategist with 15+ years writing cold emails that book enterprise meetings. Your sequences average 8-12% reply rates and 4-6% meeting rates.

Build a complete 6-touch cold email sequence for the following campaign:

**Product/Solution:** [Your Product — e.g., AI-powered revenue forecasting platform for B2B SaaS]
**Target Persona:** [Job title + seniority — e.g., VP of Sales at Series B-D SaaS companies, 50-500 employees]
**ICP Trigger/Signal:** [What makes this prospect in-market — e.g., recently hired a new CRO, raised Series B in last 90 days, using Salesforce + HubSpot simultaneously]
**Core Pain:** [Primary business problem — e.g., forecast accuracy below 70%, reps sandbagging pipeline]
**Differentiator:** [What makes you uniquely credible — e.g., 94% forecast accuracy guarantee, 45-day implementation]
**CTA:** [Desired next step — e.g., 20-minute discovery call, product demo, async video review]

For each of the 6 touches, provide:
1. Channel (email, LinkedIn, phone voicemail drop)
2. Day in sequence
3. Subject line (email only) — A/B variant included
4. Full message body
5. Personalization hook using the ICP trigger
6. One-line rationale for timing/angle

Apply these frameworks:
- Touch 1: Pattern interrupt opening + insight-led value prop (not feature dump)
- Touch 2: Social proof + peer reference (name a competitor they beat or a similar company)
- Touch 3: Pain amplification using a specific business impact metric
- Touch 4: LinkedIn connection + soft ask (no pitch)
- Touch 5: Case study snippet + ROI anchor
- Touch 6: Breakup email with a genuine door-open

Keep emails under 80 words for touches 1-3. Use conversational, direct language. Never use "I hope this email finds you well." Never open with your company name. Never use passive voice.

After the sequence, output:
- Personalization formula: What data points to pull per prospect (from LinkedIn, news, tech stack, etc.)
- A/B testing plan: Which variables to test first and why
- Deliverability checklist: 5 technical hygiene items before sending

## Integration Tips

**Apollo.io / Clay:**
- Export sequence templates to Apollo's sequence library with merge field mapping
- Use Clay to auto-enrich prospects with LinkedIn headline, funding data, and recent news for Tier 1 first-line personalization
- Clay + GPT-4 formula: `=AI("Write a 1-sentence cold email opener for a CISO at " & [Company] & " that just completed " & [Trigger] & ". Under 20 words. No fluff.")` → auto-generates personalized first lines at scale

**Outreach / Salesloft:**
- Import sequence as multi-step workflow; map email touches to "Email" steps and LinkedIn touches to "LinkedIn" task steps
- Set auto-pause rules: pause sequence on reply, out-of-office, or bounce
- Trigger sequence enrollment from Salesforce Campaign Member Status = "Outbound Target"

**HubSpot Sequences:**
- Use HubSpot's sequence enrollment triggers tied to Contact Property "ICP Tier" = 1 or 2
- Connect sequence reply → deal creation automation via HubSpot Workflows
- Sync meeting booked events to Contact Timeline for SDR-AE handoff visibility

**Salesforce:**
- Create custom Campaign Type = "Outbound Sequence" with member statuses: Enrolled → Opened → Replied → Meeting Booked → SAL
- Track pipeline sourced via Campaign Influence (First Touch model)
- Build dashboard: Outbound Meetings per SDR per Week (leading indicator) + Outbound Pipeline Created (lagging indicator)

**Zapier / Make:**
- Trigger: New row in Google Sheet (prospect list) → Enroll in Apollo/HubSpot sequence
- Trigger: Reply detected in sequencer → Create Salesforce Task for SDR follow-up within 4 hours
- Trigger: Meeting booked in Calendly → Create Salesforce Opportunity + notify SDR + AE via Slack

**Gong:**
- Tag all outbound-sourced meetings with "Outbound" deal source in Gong
- Analyze first call transcripts to find which pain points resonate most → feed back into sequence copy refinement monthly

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Open rates below 30% despite strong subject lines**
*Cause:* Deliverability issue — emails landing in spam or promotions folder
*Fix:* Run GlockApps inbox placement test immediately. Check: (1) DMARC policy — must be set; (2) sending volume — reduce to 30/day/mailbox and ramp slowly; (3) content scan — run through Mailmeteor spam score tool; (4) sending infrastructure — switch to dedicated IP if on shared pool with high-volume senders. Allow 7-10 days of re-warm before re-launching.

**Problem: High open rate (>50%) but reply rate below 2%**
*Cause:* Messaging-to-market mismatch — subject line sells the open, body doesn't sell the reply
*Fix:* Audit touch 1-3 bodies for: (1) feature-led vs. outcome-led copy — rewrite to lead with business impact metric, not product feature; (2) CTA friction — test "Worth a 2-line reply?" as micro-CTA instead of direct calendar booking; (3) social proof — add one specific customer name + result in touch 2 if not already present; (4) email length — if body >100 words, cut to 60 and measure.

**Problem: Meetings booked but show rate below 55%**
*Cause:* ICP mismatch, low urgency, or no confirmation sequence
*Fix:* (1) Add automated confirmation email 24 hours before meeting with agenda + "what to have ready" to anchor commitment; (2) Add SMS reminder via Calendly/Chili Piper integration; (3) Review reply text from booked prospects — if "sure, why not" language predominates, qualification bar is too low; tighten ICP trigger criteria. Target >3 qualifying signals before enrolling in Tier 1 sequence.
