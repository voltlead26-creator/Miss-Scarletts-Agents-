## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Champions aren't sharing the toolkit with committee members**
Solution: The toolkit is likely too long or too sales-y. Champions share content they'd be embarrassed NOT to share — it must look like objective market research, not a vendor brochure. Redesign as "third-party framed" assets (benchmark reports, industry frameworks) where your company is cited as best practice rather than the explicit subject. Test with 5 champions before broad rollout; ask them "would you share this with your CFO?" and observe their reaction.

**Issue: Content is being consumed but deals aren't accelerating**
Solution: Content is reaching the wrong people at the wrong time. Audit your distribution logic — are Technical Validation assets being served to Economic Buyers? Are business case assets being delivered before technical evaluation is complete? Restructure activation rules to require stage-sequencing: content gates should only open based on CRM opportunity stage OR engagement with prerequisite content. Also check whether content is surfacing too late — if buyers are already 60 days into evaluation before discovering your technical docs, the delay itself is the problem.

**Issue: Security/Legal/Procurement blocking content before it reaches the committee**
Solution: They're blocking because the content feels like a sales tactic, not a buyer resource. Reframe all assets as "vendor-neutral evaluation resources" — your comparison guide should help buyers evaluate any CSPM vendor, not just you. Include competitors. Make it genuinely useful as an independent resource, and Procurement will share it because it makes their job easier. The paradox: the less self-promotional your buyer enablement content, the more powerful it becomes.
