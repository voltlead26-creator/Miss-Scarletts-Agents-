Generates complete "[Your Product] vs [Competitor]" pages, "Best [Competitor] Alternatives" landing pages, and competitive displacement content that captures buyers at peak purchase intent — when they're actively comparing you against rivals — and converts them to pipeline without a single sales touch.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Tag comparison page visitors with a `competitive_intent` contact property; route them to a specialized SDR queue trained on competitor objections; suppress them from generic nurture sequences that would lower the conversation
- **6sense / Demandbase / Bombora:** Set up a competitor intent topic cluster (add competitor brand name, "[Competitor] alternatives," "[Competitor] pricing" as intent topics); trigger the displacement email sequence when accounts hit tier-3+ intent score
- **Google Analytics 4:** Create an audience for comparison page visitors + a conversion event for comparison page CTA clicks; build a lookalike audience in Google Ads for prospecting; feed back into LinkedIn Matched Audiences for retargeting
- **Clearbit / ZoomInfo Enrich:** Enrich comparison page form submissions automatically — company size, tech stack, recent funding — and route accordingly (enterprise stack to AE, SMB stack to self-serve trial)
- **Gong / Chorus (ironically):** Record every competitive sales call and build a library of verbatim objections; feed new objections back into the ASSET 5 cheat sheet quarterly; the best competitive content comes from real buyer language
- **Webflow / Unbounce:** Build the comparison page as a CMS collection so non-technical marketers can update the comparison table when competitive intel changes; no engineering ticket required for routine updates
- **Notion / Confluence:** Store the competitive content program as a living document with a "last verified" date on every competitive claim; assign ownership to a competitive intelligence DRI who reviews accuracy quarterly

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The comparison page ranks well but doesn't convert — high bounce rate, low CTR on CTAs**
Solution: The most common cause is a trust gap — buyers sense the page is written by the company being compared, so all claims feel biased. Fix this by leading with third-party evidence (G2 ratings, analyst quotes, customer testimonials) before making any first-party claims. Move the comparison table above the fold. Add a "We'll be honest with you" section near the top that explicitly names 2–3 areas where the competitor is stronger — this counterintuitive transparency dramatically increases credibility and time-on-page.

**Problem: Legal or executive leadership is uncomfortable naming a competitor directly on a public page**
Solution: Use a two-track approach: (1) Create a gated "versus" PDF that can be shared 1:1 in sales but doesn't live on the public site; (2) On the public page, name the competitor's category instead of the brand ("legacy call intelligence platforms" instead of "Gong") and let SEO schema markup and anchor text do the targeting work. Many top-ranking competitive pages avoid direct competitor naming in their H1 while still ranking for competitor keywords.

**Problem: The competitive displacement email sequence gets flagged as aggressive or triggers brand protection responses from the competitor**
Solution: Never make unsubstantiated claims about a competitor's product — every competitive claim in the email sequence must be traceable to a public source (G2 review, the competitor's own pricing page, a press release). Focus outreach on the buyer's problem, not the competitor's failure. "Your pipeline reviews might be running on delayed data" is defensible and buyer-centric; "[Competitor] has a broken data sync" is libelous and counterproductive.
