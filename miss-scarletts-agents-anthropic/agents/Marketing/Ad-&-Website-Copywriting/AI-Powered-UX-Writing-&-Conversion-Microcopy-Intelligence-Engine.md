TOUCHPOINT 1: EMPTY STATES
Framework: AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action)
Empty states are the #1 silent conversion killer in SaaS — most teams ship a blank screen with a gray illustration and lose 40%+ of new users at this moment.

Generate copy for all three empty state types:

A) ZERO-DATA STATE: Brand new user, nothing configured yet
   - H1 (max 6 words, benefit-forward, not feature-forward)
   - Subhead (1–2 sentences, acknowledge where the user is, show them where they're going)
   - Primary CTA (action verb + outcome, not just "Get Started")
   - Optional secondary CTA (for the hesitant user who isn't ready to connect data)

B) POST-DELETE STATE: User deleted their content and knows what's missing
   - Shorter, warmer — they know the product, they just made a choice
   - Do not guilt them; give them a single path forward

C) ZERO-SEARCH-RESULTS STATE: User searched and found nothing
   - Acknowledge the intent, offer an alternative path
   - H1 + 1 sentence + 1 CTA

TOUCHPOINT 2: PROGRESSIVE ONBOARDING SEQUENCE (3-step guided flow)
Framework: Fogg Behavior Model — Motivation × Ability → Trigger
High motivation + low effort = successful onboarding step completion

Map the 3 most critical steps to reach the activation moment, then write:
- Step 1: [FIRST_ACTION e.g., "Connect your primary data source"]
- Step 2: [SECOND_ACTION e.g., "Configure your first report"]
- Step 3: [ACTIVATION_MOMENT e.g., "View your first automated insight"]

For each step write:
— Progress indicator: "Step X of 3 · ~Y minutes remaining" (always show light at the end of the tunnel)
— Headline (benefit-first: what the user gains from completing this step, not what the feature is)
— Body (max 25 words — show, don't tell; use "you" not "users")
— Primary CTA (action verb + specific outcome, not generic "Next" or "Continue")
— Skip/back text (reduce guilt; preserve goodwill for users who need to come back later)

TOUCHPOINT 3: ERROR RECOVERY MESSAGES
Framework: Error Anatomy — What happened → Why (in plain English) → What to do next
Rule: The user is never the villain. Every error is a product failure, not a user failure.

Write compassionate, action-oriented error messages for:
a) Network/API connection failure
b) Permission or access denied
c) Form validation — email field
d) Form validation — file upload (wrong type or too large)
e) Data sync failure or timeout
f) Rate limit or usage cap reached

Constraints for every error message:
— Max 20 words
— Zero technical jargon (or define it immediately in plain language)
— End with a specific next action the user can take right now

TOUCHPOINT 4: SUCCESS & DELIGHT MOMENTS
Framework: Peak-End Rule — Users remember the peaks of an experience more than the average
This is where most SaaS products leave emotion on the table.

Write copy for three milestone moments:
A) First activation: Completing the key activation action for the first time
   — Celebration headline (human, specific — reference what they just did)
   — Contextual next-step CTA (what's the obvious "wow, what do I do with this?" action?)
   — Optional social share prompt (only if brand-appropriate and genuinely earned)

B) First meaningful output: First report generated, first export saved, first insight surfaced
   — Show the user the magnitude of what they just unlocked
   — Suggest a high-value next action that deepens engagement

C) 30-day milestone: Habit has formed — honor the relationship
   — Acknowledge consistency without being creepy
   — Offer a power-user tip or unlock that rewards loyalty

TOUCHPOINT 5: FEATURE DISCOVERY & UPGRADE FRICTION REDUCTION
Framework: Loss Aversion + Endowment Effect
Users must feel they're losing something by not using a feature, not just gaining something by upgrading.

— Paywall subheadline (make the risk feel low — trial, demo, or guarantee language)
— Upgrade CTA (specific to the outcome — not "Upgrade Now" but "[Unlock X]" or "[Start seeing Y]")
— Objection handler micro-copy (1 sentence addressing the #1 hesitation: cost, time to value, or risk)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI-generated copy sounds generic and doesn't match the product's brand voice**
Solution: Provide 3–5 verbatim samples of existing copy you approve of (from your product or a brand you admire) at the top of the prompt. Add explicit constraints: "Never use the word 'leverage'" or "Always write in second person using 'you,' never 'users.'" Running the Advanced version with your actual voice & tone documentation pasted in full produces dramatically better output.

**Problem: Error messages are too technical or too long for non-technical users**
Solution: Add the constraint "Write for a 10th-grade reading level (target Flesch-Kincaid score: 70–80). Never use a technical term without an immediate plain-English parenthetical. Test every error message by reading it aloud — if it sounds like a robot, rewrite it." Also cap error messages at 15 words, not 20, for interfaces where users are already stressed.

**Problem: Upgrade prompts generate user resentment or feel manipulative**
Solution: Apply the "value before restriction" rule without exception — lead with the specific outcome the user gains, not the feature name or plan tier. Replace "Upgrade to Pro to unlock Advanced Cohort Analysis" with "See which cohorts are shrinking your NRR — available on Pro." Always include a soft-exit option ("Learn more" or "Remind me later") alongside the conversion CTA to serve users still evaluating — this reduces resentment and preserves trust for a future conversion.
