## Overview
Architects a complete, psychologically-grounded B2B copy system that aligns message, channel, and buyer stage — from first impression to signed contract. Use this when you need to stop writing isolated copy pieces and instead build a coherent persuasion architecture where every touchpoint does deliberate conversion work, each stage building on the last, and the full system generating measurable pipeline.

## Success Metrics

**Copy System Quality Indicators:**
- TOFU CTR benchmark: LinkedIn Sponsored Content 0.4–0.8% (industry avg); target 0.9%+ with well-tested hooks
- Landing page above-fold CVR: B2B SaaS benchmark 2–5%; strong copy systems should achieve 6–12% for warm traffic
- Demo request page CVR: benchmark 20–35% for BOFU landing pages; target 40%+ with friction-reduction copy
- Email nurture open rate: benchmark 22–28% for B2B; target 32%+ with A/B-tested subject lines
- Demo-to-close rate uplift: track whether BOFU copy changes reduce time-from-demo-to-contract by ≥15%

**Qualitative Quality Checks:**
- The "2am test": would a VP Engineering read this at 2am when they're stressed about headcount? If not, it's not specific enough
- The "could your competitor say this?" test: if a competitor could run identical copy with their logo, it's too generic
- The "so what?" test: after every headline, ask "so what?" — if the answer isn't immediately obvious, rewrite it
- The "jargon sweep": remove every word that requires marketing knowledge to understand

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: TOFU ads get clicks but MOFU landing page conversions are low (under 3%)**
Fix: This is almost always a message-match failure. The ad made a specific promise (e.g., "60% close-time reduction") that the landing page doesn't immediately confirm. Audit your ad-to-landing-page message match: the landing page H1 should echo the exact language of the ad headline that drove the click. Run a 48-hour heatmap (Hotjar/Clarity) to see where visitors drop — above-the-fold drops indicate headline failure; below-the-fold drops indicate proof or CTA failure.

**Problem: Copy is converting demo requests but demos aren't converting to opportunities**
Fix: BOFU copy is over-promising. When copy creates unrealistic expectations about what the demo will deliver, buyers show up skeptical and leave disappointed. Add an expectation-setting statement to your demo confirmation page and pre-demo email: "In 30 minutes, you'll see [specific thing], get [specific asset], and know whether [your product] is worth a deeper conversation — no pressure." This filters for intent and primes the buyer correctly.

**Problem: Copy variants all sound the same and tests aren't producing meaningful lifts**
Fix: You're testing copy tweaks, not copy strategies. Meaningful A/B tests require testing fundamentally different psychological angles — not just word swaps. If Control is a pain-focused headline ("Stop Losing 3 Weeks to Manual Close") and Challenger is a slightly different pain headline ("Manual Close Is Stealing 3 Weeks From Your Team"), you're testing word preference, not psychology. Test Hook A vs. Hook B vs. Hook C as defined in Section 2 — curiosity vs. fear vs. aspiration are genuinely different persuasion strategies and should produce 15–30% CVR differences when copy is well-executed.
