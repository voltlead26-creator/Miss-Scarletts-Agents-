## Overview
Generates a systematic creative matrix of high-performing ad variations across headlines, body copy, CTAs, and value propositions — then predicts performance, prioritizes test order, and outputs a creative rotation and fatigue management strategy. Use this when scaling paid media spend, combating creative fatigue, or launching new campaigns that need 30+ validated creative variants from day one.

**2. HEADLINE GENERATION ENGINE**

- **Urgency/scarcity**: Time-bound or supply-based limitation (only use if genuinely true)
- **Empathy mirror**: Open by describing their current painful situation so accurately they feel understood
- **Authority transfer**: Borrowed credibility through customers, media, analysts, certifications

- Low commitment (awareness): "See how it works" / "Watch the 90-second demo"

- Urgency-amplified: "Claim your spot" / "Join before [date/milestone]"

## Integration Tips

**Meta Ads Manager (Advantage+ Creative):**
- Upload all headline, body, and CTA variants as individual assets in Advantage+ campaigns — Meta's AI will dynamically assemble the best combination per user
- Use the "Creative Combinations" report to see which asset combinations Meta is auto-optimizing toward — these are your winners to double down on manually
- Set asset labels (e.g., "Angle: Fear", "Angle: Social Proof") to track performance by messaging angle in custom breakdowns

**Google Ads (Responsive Search Ads + Performance Max):**
- Enter all 8 headline variants and 6 body copy variants into a single RSA — Google will test combinations automatically
- Pin your highest-confidence headline to Position 1 to protect brand safety; let all others rotate freely
- In Performance Max, upload full creative matrix as asset groups segmented by audience signal (retargeting vs. prospecting)

**HubSpot / Salesforce Campaign Tracking:**
- Append UTM parameters with creative angle codes (e.g., `utm_content=angle_fear_headline_v3`) to attribute downstream pipeline to specific creative angles
- Build a dashboard tracking: creative angle → landing page CVR → MQL rate → pipeline → close rate. This reveals which angles attract highest-quality buyers, not just highest volume

**Zapier / Make.com Automation:**
- Trigger: Weekly ad performance export from Meta/Google → Google Sheets
- Logic: If CTR drops >20% week-over-week OR frequency >8 → auto-tag ad as "fatigued" → alert team to queue next variant
- Output: Populate a creative production Asana/Notion brief for next batch of variants using winning patterns

- Use this as the single source of truth for all creative learnings — feed patterns back into each new prompt cycle

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High CTR but low CVR (ads get clicks but no conversions)**
Fix: Your creative is creating curiosity but the landing page doesn't deliver on the promise. Audit for message match — the landing page headline should mirror the ad headline verbatim or near-verbatim. Also check if you're attracting wrong audience: if "free trial" CTA gets lots of clicks from users who then bounce immediately, you're over-promising ease of access. Switch to "See a 2-minute demo" CTA to filter for genuine intent.

**Problem: Creative fatigue happening too fast (CTR drops after 7-10 days)**
Fix: You're not producing enough variants to sustain rotation. You need minimum 8-10 unique creative combinations per ad set for a campaign spending $5k+/month. Additionally, segment your audiences more granularly — if one ad is showing to both cold prospecting AND retargeting pools, it fatigues faster. Separate audiences into distinct ad sets with tailored creative.

**Problem: Winning variant stops performing when budget is scaled**
Fix: This is classic audience saturation, not creative failure. When you scale spend on a winning creative, you exhaust the best-matched audience first and start serving to lower-quality segments. Solution: expand your audience (broaden targeting, add lookalikes) at the same time you scale spend. Also introduce 2 new creative variants alongside the winner to give the algorithm more to work with at higher spend levels.
