## Overview
Generates persuasive, jargon-free copy for AI and ML-powered products targeting non-technical executive buyers (CFOs, CHROs, COOs, CEOs, VPs of Operations) — translating technical capabilities into measurable business outcomes, proactively addressing AI adoption skepticism, and producing ready-to-deploy assets across website, email, paid ads, and sales materials. Use this when your product is AI-powered but your buyer is not an AI practitioner.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The output still sounds too technical even after using this prompt.**
Solution: Add a constraint to your input: "Assume my buyer has never heard of machine learning, LLMs, or AI agents. They have an MBA, 20 years of business experience, and zero CS background. Every sentence must pass the 'would my non-technical CFO friend understand this at a dinner party?' test." Also, explicitly list the technical terms you need to avoid (e.g., "do not use: neural network, vector database, fine-tuned model, inference, embeddings").

**Problem: The copy feels generic — it could apply to any AI company.**
Solution: Your input proof points are probably too vague. Go back and replace every generic claim with a specific number and context: not "reduces manual work" but "eliminates 140 hours/month of manual reconciliation for a 10-person finance team." The more specific your inputs, the more differentiated the copy output. Also add 2–3 direct competitor names and their primary messaging claim so the prompt can write against them.

**Problem: Sales reps aren't using the generated email templates.**
Solution: The copy is likely too polished and doesn't match the rep's natural voice. After generating the templates, run a second prompt: "Rewrite this email to sound like it was written by a human who is good at their job but not a professional copywriter. Remove anything that sounds like marketing copy. Make it conversational, slightly imperfect, and 30% shorter." Then share both versions and let reps pick the one that fits their voice.
