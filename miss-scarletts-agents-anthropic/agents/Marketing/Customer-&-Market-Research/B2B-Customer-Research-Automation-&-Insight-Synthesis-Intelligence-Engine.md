# B2B Customer Research Automation & Insight Synthesis Intelligence Engine - Turn Customer Conversations Into Revenue-Ready Insights at Scale

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** customer-research, jtbd, voice-of-customer, market-research, b2b-saas, persona, insight-synthesis, qualitative-research, interview-automation, product-marketing, messaging, icp

## Overview
Automates the entire B2B customer research lifecycle — from designing research instruments (survey screeners, interview guides, NPS follow-up questions) to synthesizing raw qualitative data into structured insight frameworks that marketing, product, and sales can immediately act on. Use this when you need to run customer discovery, validate messaging, uncover unmet needs before a product launch, or build a research program from scratch without a dedicated researcher on staff.

**Section 3: Interview Guide — Full JTBD Switch Interview Protocol**

Structure as a complete 45-60 minute guide with:

*Opening (5 min):*
- Context-setting statement (what this is NOT — not a sales call, not support)
- Consent and recording permission script
- 2 warm-up rapport questions that reveal company context without leading

*The Timeline Reconstruction (15 min) — Switch Interview core:*
- "Walk me through the moment you first thought you needed [category of solution]"
- First thought probes: what were you doing, who else was involved, what was the precipitating event
- "What was the first thing you did?" — maps the discovery phase
- "How did [Company Name] get on your radar?" — source attribution
- "What made you put off the decision earlier?" — reveals passive looking vs. active looking triggers
- "Who else was involved in the decision?" — buying committee mapping

*Functional Job Probes (10 min):*
- "What were you hired to accomplish in your role before you found [product]?" — JTBD framing
- "What does success look like 6 months after implementing [product]?" — outcome metric elicitation
- "What would have to be true about [product] for you to feel confident recommending it to your CEO?"
- "What did you use before [product]? What did that solution do well that you wish [product] did?"

*Emotional and Social Job Probes (5 min):*
- "How did [the problem] make you feel at the time?" — emotional job
- "Who in your organization would notice most if this problem was solved?" — social job / status driver
- "When you told a colleague you were implementing [product], what did they say?" — social proof and fear signals

*Competitive and Switching Intelligence (10 min):*
- "What other options did you consider? Walk me through why you eliminated each one."
- "What almost made you NOT choose [product]?" — objection and anxiety mining
- "If [product] didn't exist, what would you do instead?" — alternative behavior mapping
- "What would have to change for you to consider switching to a competitor?"

*Closing — Unmet Need Surfacing (5 min):*
- "If you had a magic wand, what's the one thing [product] could do that it can't do today?"
- "What would make you a promoter — someone who proactively recommends [product] to peers?"
- "Is there anything I haven't asked that you think would be important for our product team to know?"

*Probing follow-up techniques for every question:*

- Interview thematic saturation reached: new interviews stop surfacing new themes by interview 8-12 (for qualitative programs)
- At least 3 research insights directly influence a shipped marketing deliverable within 60 days of readout
- Campaign copy written with customer verbatim quotes outperforms control copy in A/B test (target: +15% CTR or +10% conversion rate)
- Sales team reports using insights from research readout in at least 30% of discovery calls (track via Gong keyword analysis)
- At least 2 "surprises" — findings that contradicted your pre-research assumptions — are surfaced and acted on
- Insights are referenced in at least 1 board-level marketing update within 2 quarters

- Participants self-identify problems in their own words before you introduce solution framing (≥70% of interviews)

- Synthesis produces at least 3 messaging hypotheses your team would not have generated without research

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Can't recruit enough churned customers — they won't respond or ignore outreach**
*Fix:* The framing of your outreach is the problem, not the channel. Churned customers have one fear: being sold to or guilted. Reframe completely — subject line: "Not asking you to come back — just want to understand what we got wrong" — body: 2 sentences acknowledging they left, 1 sentence explaining this is confidential product research (not re-activation), 1 sentence offering a $50 Amazon gift card. Send from the CEO or CTO email, not customer success. Response rate for churned outreach jumps from ~5% (standard CSM re-engagement) to ~22-28% when framed as genuine learning and sent from a founder. If direct outreach still fails, recruit churned-equivalent participants via UserInterviews.com by filtering for: "[your category] user, switched solutions in last 12 months."

**Problem: Interviews produce generic, surface-level answers — customers say things like "it just wasn't a fit" or "we needed more features"**
*Fix:* The problem is chronological — you're asking evaluation questions before establishing emotional context. Do not ask "why did you choose us" until 20+ minutes into the interview. Spend the first 15 minutes exclusively on timeline reconstruction (Trigger → First thought → First action → Discovery → Evaluation) using the Switch Interview protocol from Section 3. Generic answers emerge when participants feel they're being evaluated or are trying to be polite. The timeline reconstruction puts them in storytelling mode, where specificity and emotion emerge naturally. Also: use silence deliberately — after a generic answer, wait 4-6 seconds before probing. Most people fill silence with the more specific, authentic follow-up they were editing out.

**Problem: Research produces interesting insights but nothing changes — findings sit in a deck nobody reads**
*Fix:* The readout format and distribution are the problem. Three structural fixes: (1) Replace the 20-slide deck with a one-page "Research Brief" that leads with the 3 most surprising findings (not the most confirming ones), formatted as: [Assumption we held] → [What customers actually said] → [What we're going to do differently]. Surprises drive action; confirmations do not. (2) Assign a named owner to each insight-to-action item in the Research-to-Action Roadmap (Section 7) before the readout meeting — not "PMM team" but "[Name] by [Date]." (3) Book a 30-minute "Research Activation Check-in" 6 weeks after the readout where each owner reports on their action item. The existence of the check-in alone increases action completion rates by ~40%.
