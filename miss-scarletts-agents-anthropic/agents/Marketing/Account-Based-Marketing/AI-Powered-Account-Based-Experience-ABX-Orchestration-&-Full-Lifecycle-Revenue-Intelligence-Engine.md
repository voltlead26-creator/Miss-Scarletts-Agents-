# AI-Powered Account-Based Experience (ABX) Orchestration & Full-Lifecycle Revenue Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** abx, abm, b2b, enterprise, orchestration, personalization, revenue, sales-alignment

## Overview
ABX (Account-Based Experience) is the evolution of ABM — it orchestrates every touchpoint across the full account lifecycle (pre-sale through expansion and renewal) with a unified, AI-coordinated experience. Use this when you want to align marketing, sales, and customer success around a single account strategy that drives new logo acquisition, deal acceleration, and expansion revenue simultaneously.

## Integration Tips
- **6sense / Demandbase**: Load all Module 5 trigger rules as automation workflows. Sync account engagement scores to Salesforce daily. Use the intent signal API to dynamically adjust content in Module 3 based on real-time keyword spikes.
- **HubSpot**: Create a custom "ABX Playbook" deal property set with fields for each module output. Use HubSpot Sequences for Track B plays. Connect LinkedIn Ads via HubSpot's native integration for Track A targeting by account name.
- **Salesforce**: Create a custom "ABX Playbook" object linked to the Account record. Log all touchpoints as Activities with a custom "ABX Track" field (Marketing / Sales / CS). Build a dashboard tracking engagement score progression and stage advancement across all active ABX accounts.
- **Outreach / Salesloft**: Import Track B sequences directly as account-level "plays." Use dynamic merge fields to pull account-specific data from Salesforce into each touchpoint automatically.
- **Notion**: Build a shared ABX workspace with the account brief pinned. Marketing, sales, and CS each maintain their track's status column. Replaces weekly email status updates with a single source of truth.
- **Slack**: Create an account-specific channel (e.g., #abx-meridian-logistics). Connect CRM, intent platforms, and LinkedIn alerts to the channel via Zapier so the full team sees signals in real time without logging into multiple tools.

## Troubleshooting
- **Problem**: The buying committee map is incomplete — we don't know who the economic buyer is.
  **Solution**: Run the Buying Committee Intelligence Engine prompt first to identify the org chart. Then deploy a low-friction, high-value content piece (like a personalized benchmark report) that requires a business email to access — this surfaces unknown stakeholders organically. Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator's "TeamLink" feature to find warm introduction paths to unidentified executives.

- **Problem**: Marketing and sales keep contacting the same stakeholder on the same day without knowing it.
  **Solution**: Enforce Module 7's coordination protocol immediately. Create the shared Slack account channel and require every outreach to be logged there before it goes out. Set a firm rule: no ABX account outreach is sent without a 24-hour heads-up in the channel. In Salesforce/HubSpot, build a "Last Contacted By" field that flags if the same contact was touched by two different team members within 72 hours.

- **Problem**: Account engagement score is high (lots of content consumed) but no meetings are being booked.
  **Solution**: High digital engagement without meetings signals the content is relevant but the call-to-action is misaligned with the buyer's stage. If they are reading pricing pages and ROI content, the CTA should be "Schedule a 15-minute ROI review" not "Download our whitepaper." Activate the champion play immediately — have your internal advocate request the meeting on your behalf rather than marketing or sales making the cold ask. Peer-to-peer meeting requests convert at 3-5x the rate of vendor outreach.
