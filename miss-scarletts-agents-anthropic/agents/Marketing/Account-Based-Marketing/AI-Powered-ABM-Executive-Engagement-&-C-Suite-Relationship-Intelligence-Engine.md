## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Executive won't respond to any outreach, even after warm intro attempts.
**Solution:** Don't increase outreach frequency — that destroys the relationship before it starts. Instead, activate your champion to request a 15-minute "perspective check" between the exec and one of your executives (CEO / CPO / CSO) framed as peer advice, not a sales call. Or find a third-party venue (industry event, peer roundtable) where you can get in the same room without the vendor dynamic. Never go around the champion without their knowledge.

**Problem:** Executive agreed to an EBC but sent their deputy at the last minute.
**Solution:** Run the EBC for the deputy at full quality — they now own the account relationship internally. Debrief with them immediately after: "What would make this conversation more relevant if Sarah joined the follow-up?" Then design a second, 30-minute executive summary session specifically for Sarah based on that debrief. The deputy becomes your internal champion for getting Sarah into the room.

**Problem:** Executive engagement score is high but deal isn't moving.
**Solution:** High engagement without deal movement usually means you have a relationship but haven't created urgency. Diagnose: (1) Is there a real budget cycle, or are they exploring without authority to buy? (2) Is there an undisclosed incumbent relationship or internal politics blocking the decision? (3) Are you solving a priority-3 problem for an exec who needs priority-1 solved first? Use the EBC discovery questions to surface the real constraint — then reframe your entire engagement program around their actual decision criteria, not your product's strengths.
