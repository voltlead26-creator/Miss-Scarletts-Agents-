## Overview

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Gifts are being delivered but getting zero response — follow-up emails still going unanswered**
Fix: The note personalization is likely too generic. "I saw you're in cybersecurity" is not personalization — it's spam with a stamp. Require a minimum of 2 research sources per note (e.g., LinkedIn post + company news item). The opening sentence must be specific enough that the recipient knows you looked them up as a person, not a title. If personalization research yields nothing (ghost executive with no LinkedIn presence), escalate to a champion-to-economic-buyer warm introduction play instead of cold gifting.

**Issue: Finance is pushing back on the gifting budget — hard to show ROI**
Fix: Pull a 90-day pipeline influenced report from CRM: filter opportunities with "Gift Sent" activity → compare average days-to-close vs. control group that received no gift. If you don't have enough data yet, use the benchmark: gifted Tier 1 accounts close 20-30% faster with 2-3x higher response rates. At a $150K ACV, even one accelerated deal covers an entire quarter of gifting program costs. Build a simple ROI calculator: (Gifts Sent × Response Rate × Meeting-to-Opportunity Rate × ACV × Close Rate) - Program Cost = Pipeline ROI.

**Issue: Recipients are reporting gifts under company gift policies (>$50 limit at their employer)**
Fix: Two options: (1) Switch to Alyce's choice-gifting model where recipients can accept a lower-value item or donate to charity — this keeps the gesture while respecting their policy. (2) For strictly regulated contacts (public sector, Fortune 50 procurement teams with zero-gift policies), replace physical gifts with "experience gifting" like a virtual expert session, a sponsored conference pass, or a co-branded report in their name — these typically fall outside standard gift policy definitions.
