### Step 1: Buying Committee Architecture (MEDDIC Framework)

For each stakeholder type below, provide a structured profile:
- **Likely Titles at This Specific Company**: 3–5 job titles that typically hold this role given their industry and size
- **Primary JTBD**: Functional job (what they're trying to accomplish), social job (how they want to be perceived), emotional job (fear they're trying to avoid)
- **Top 5 Decision Criteria**: What they evaluate vendors on — in priority order
- **Decision Weight**: Estimated % influence on final purchase approval
- **Risk Profile**: What would make them block or delay — and how to preempt it
- **Preferred Engagement Channel**: Where they consume content and respond to outreach

**Stakeholder Types to Map:**
1. Economic Buyer — controls and approves the budget
2. Technical Buyer / DRI — evaluates fit and makes formal recommendation
3. Internal Champion — emotionally invested in solving this problem
4. End User Buyers — will use the product daily and influence adoption success
5. Legal / Procurement / InfoSec — must approve before contract execution
6. Executive Sponsor — required for C-suite signoff on strategic purchases
7. Influencer / Trusted Advisor — external consultant or board member shaping the decision

### Step 2: Personalized Multi-Channel Outreach Sequences

For each identified stakeholder type, generate:

**Cold Email Sequences (3 emails per persona):**
- **Email 1 — Pattern Interrupt**: Reference a specific, verifiable trigger event (new executive hire, earnings call comment, press release, LinkedIn post) with a provocative question. Under 100 words. No attachments.
- **Email 2 — Value Hypothesis**: Deliver a single, role-specific insight — a benchmark stat, a 1-slide framework, or a finding from a comparable company's experience. Include a soft CTA (reply yes/no). Under 120 words.
- **Email 3 — Social Proof + Direct Ask**: One sentence from a peer at a comparable company (same industry, similar size) plus a direct calendar link ask. Under 80 words.

**Formatting rules for all emails:**
- First-person, conversational — never corporate or passive voice
- Subject lines under 40 characters with no punctuation gimmicks
- Avoid spam trigger words (free, guaranteed, limited time, act now)
- Personalization token in first 10 words — not just first name

**LinkedIn Outreach (3 messages per persona):**
- **Connection Request** (300 character max): Reference a specific post, article, or shared professional interest — never "I came across your profile"
- **Acceptance Follow-Up** (sent day 2 after connection): Value-add only — share a relevant insight, article, or benchmark with zero ask
- **Engagement Message** (sent day 9): Soft ask anchored to mutual connection, industry event, or timely trigger

**Content Asset Recommendations (per persona):**
- 1 ungated asset (calculator, benchmark tool, or data-driven blog post) for immediate sharing
- 1 gated asset (original research report or ROI playbook) for mid-funnel nurture
- 1 customer story from a company in their exact sub-vertical with quantified outcomes

### Step 3: 90-Day Account Orchestration Playbook

Design a week-by-week playbook specifying for each week:
- **Primary Objective** (awareness / engagement / evaluation / decision)
- **Persona Being Targeted** (which stakeholder type gets the most attention this week)
- **Channel Mix** (email, LinkedIn, phone, direct mail, paid display retargeting, field event)
- **Content Deployed** (which asset, in what format, through what distribution)
- **Success Signal** (what behavioral response advances the account to the next stage)
- **CRM Stage Update** (what Salesforce field changes trigger based on this week's activity)

**Phased Orchestration Structure:**
- **Weeks 1–3 (Awareness)**: Cold outreach to Champion and Technical Buyer; LinkedIn engagement warm-up; content syndication targeting account domain
- **Weeks 4–7 (Engagement)**: Follow-up sequences for non-responders; executive-to-executive outreach from your CEO/VP; direct mail to Tier 1 accounts; virtual event or roundtable invitation
- **Weeks 8–11 (Evaluation)**: Multi-stakeholder discovery calls; custom ROI model delivery; technical deep-dive with DRI; executive briefing center invitation
- **Weeks 12–13 (Decision)**: Mutual action plan (MAP) activation; commercial proposal delivery; legal/procurement pre-engagement; champion enablement kit for internal selling

### Step 4: Objection Mapping & Consensus-Building Rebuttals

For each major persona (Economic Buyer, Technical Buyer, Legal/Procurement), provide:
- **Top 3 Objections**: Price/ROI uncertainty, incumbent vendor loyalty, internal priority competition, technical fit concerns, resource/implementation risk
- **Pattern Interrupt Rebuttal**: One sentence that reframes the objection rather than defending against it
- **Proof Point**: A specific data point, case study reference, or third-party validation (G2, Gartner, IDC)
- **Escalation Path**: Who to bring into the conversation if the objection persists — and what they should say

## Integration Tips
- **Salesforce**: Map each buying committee member as a Contact Role on the Opportunity object (Economic Buyer, Technical Buyer, Champion, etc.); use account plan custom fields to store the 90-day playbook milestones; trigger stage advancement via workflow rules when success signals are logged
- **Outreach / Salesloft**: Import email sequences as multi-step cadences with persona-based tags; enable A/B testing on subject lines for Email 1; use "out of office" triggers to automatically pause cadences and resume after the trigger event
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator**: Create a saved lead list for each buying committee role using the generated title filters; set job-change alerts to detect when a Champion leaves or a new Economic Buyer joins; use InMail credits on VP/C-level personas who don't respond to email
- **6sense / Bombora**: Configure intent topic clusters from the generated signal monitoring list; set account segment rules to trigger SDR alert when account reaches "Active Intent" or "Decision" stage; sync intent data to Salesforce for AE visibility
- **HubSpot**: Build a workflow that enrolls contacts into the correct email sequence based on their job title matching the buying committee role taxonomy; adjust lead score based on content engagement per persona type
- **Gong / Chorus**: Tag discovery calls with the buying committee persona for each contact; track which objections surface most by persona to refine the objection map over time; create call scorecards aligned to MEDDIC criteria

## Troubleshooting
- **Can't find buying committee contacts in LinkedIn or ZoomInfo**: Start with the Champion persona — they're most accessible and will typically identify other stakeholders if asked directly ("Who else on your team would evaluate a decision like this?"); cross-reference with the account's job postings for titles actively being hired that signal infrastructure investment
- **Low email reply rates (<5% after full sequence)**: Audit trigger event references for accuracy — fabricated or stale events destroy credibility immediately; switch Email 1 from trigger-event angle to peer benchmark angle; test sending from a personal email domain instead of sales tool IP address
- **Multi-stakeholder meeting stalls after Champion engagement**: The Economic Buyer has not been engaged — Champion-only deals die in late stages when budget approval escalates; use the Champion to request a joint working session framed as "internal alignment" rather than a vendor demo
