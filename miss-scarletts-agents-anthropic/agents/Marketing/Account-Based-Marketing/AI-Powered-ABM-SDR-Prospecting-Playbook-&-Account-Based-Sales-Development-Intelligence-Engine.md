## Overview
Generates account-specific SDR prospecting playbooks that translate marketing's ABM investments into precision sales outreach. Automates buying committee research, tier-based sequence design, and personalized messaging for each target account—enabling SDRs to execute like strategic account managers rather than generic prospectors. Use this when launching a new ABM program, onboarding SDRs onto target accounts, or systematizing how sales engages ABM-qualified accounts.

## Success Metrics

- Meeting booked rate from completed sequences: Target 4–6% Tier 1, 2–3% Tier 2, 0.5–1% Tier 3 (industry average is ~1–2% for generic outbound — ABM sequences should 2–3x this)
- Buying committee coverage at opportunity creation: At least 3 distinct buying committee contacts identified and engaged before AE creates an opportunity
- Sequence completion rate: 80%+ of planned touches sent per account (most SDRs abandon after 2–3 touches — completion is the highest-leverage behavior to improve)
- Marketing + sales co-influenced pipeline: Target 60%+ of ABM opportunities having both marketing engagement (ad, content, event) AND SDR outreach before first meeting
- MEDDPICC qualification at discovery: By end of first call, M (metrics), I (pain), and C (champion) must be partially confirmed or the meeting was not productive
- Time-to-opportunity: Tier 1 accounts should either enter CRM as opportunities or be recycled to Tier 2 nurture within 45 days — avoid dead-account limbo

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce + Outreach**: Create custom Salesforce fields for ABM tier, buying committee coverage score, and MEDDPICC completion percentage. Map each account tier to a dedicated Outreach sequence with locked step cadences—SDRs can customize messaging but cannot skip steps without manager approval. Configure trigger-based Outreach tasks when marketing engagement events fire: pricing page visit → Priority 1 task created for SDR within 15 minutes via Salesforce Flow.
- **HubSpot**: Build a custom pipeline stage called "ABM Active" triggered when an account is added to a target list. Auto-enroll in a HubSpot workflow that notifies the SDR, launches parallel marketing nurture, and creates a task checklist from the 5-question opportunity gate. Use HubSpot's company scoring to auto-promote accounts between tiers as engagement signals accumulate above a threshold.
- **Clay**: Build a Clay table that ingests your target account list and auto-enriches each account with Clearbit firmographics, Apollo contact data, LinkedIn recent activity scrape, Crunchbase funding history, BuiltWith tech stack, and open job posting signals from Greenhouse/Lever. Output a pre-populated Account Intelligence Brief per account that the SDR reviews, validates, and uses to personalize outreach — eliminating 2–3 hours of manual research per Tier 1 account and enabling SDRs to work 3x as many accounts at the same quality level.
- **Gong / Chorus**: After every discovery call, configure Gong to auto-populate MEDDPICC fields in Salesforce using conversation intelligence extraction. Set Gong alerts for buying signal language ("budget approved," "we're evaluating," "my VP wants to see this") to trigger same-day SDR and AE tasks. Use Gong's trackers to monitor what % of ABM calls contain value-based language vs. product feature pitching — the ratio predicts deal velocity.
- **Slack + Salesloft**: Create a dedicated Slack channel per Tier 1 account (e.g., #abm-veridian-software) that pipes in marketing engagement events via Zapier, SDR sequence activity from Salesloft, Gong call summaries, and CRM stage changes. The AE, SDR, demand gen manager, and (for expansion accounts) CSM all participate. This eliminates the email thread coordination failure that causes sales and marketing to send conflicting messages to the same prospect in the same week.

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem:** SDRs complete 2–3 touches and abandon the account, citing "no response means they're not interested" and moving to easier targets.
  **Solution:** Reframe the outcome metric. A 4–6% meeting booked rate from a 12-touch Tier 1 sequence means the majority of accounts that eventually convert respond between Touch 6 and Touch 11. Abandoning at Touch 3 means the SDR has done 25% of the work and captured 0% of the pipeline. Run a weekly ABM sprint review where SDRs report sequence completion rate alongside meetings booked. Celebrate and reward touch completion as a leading indicator, not just meetings as the lagging metric.

- **Problem:** The personalization reads as fake or hollow — the SDR adds a company name and recent funding round but the email still feels like a template blast.
  **Solution:** Genuine personalization requires a specific, verifiable fact that the recipient would recognize as real research — not a fact available in a CRM enrichment tool. Run the Advanced Version of this prompt and specifically require 3 opening line variants where the SDR must manually confirm the specific detail before sending (e.g., read the actual LinkedIn post, verify the exact job posting language, confirm the exact technology from BuiltWith rather than CRM guess). If the SDR cannot confirm the specific detail, the line is not personalized enough to send.

- **Problem:** Marketing and sales are messaging the same contacts with conflicting angles — marketing ads are running an awareness play about industry trends while the SDR is running urgent ROI-based sequences, creating confusion and diluting both.
  **Solution:** Implement the Day 0 Sales-Marketing Alignment Checklist from Deliverable 5 as a mandatory gate before any SDR sequence launches on a Tier 1 account. The SDR, AE, and demand gen manager must align in a 15-minute sync on: what message angle marketing is running, what the SDR's unique added value is beyond what the ads say, and what the marketing-to-sales handoff trigger looks like. Document the agreed angle in the CRM account record so both teams have a shared record.
