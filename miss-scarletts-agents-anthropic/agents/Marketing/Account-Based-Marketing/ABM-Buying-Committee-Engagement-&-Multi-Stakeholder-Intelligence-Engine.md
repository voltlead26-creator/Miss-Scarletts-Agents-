## Overview

Use this when running enterprise ABM plays where 6–10 decision-makers must reach consensus before a deal closes.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion is engaged but won't introduce us to the Economic Buyer.**
- Root cause: Champion lacks confidence or internal political capital to sponsor a $300K+ decision; may fear looking bad if implementation fails
- Fix: Shift focus to champion enablement — help them build their internal business case first. Run a free "business case workshop" where you help them prepare the financial model. Once they own the numbers, they have the confidence to present internally. Do NOT try to bypass the champion — it destroys trust and kills deals.

**Problem: Technical Evaluator is creating blockers (security concerns, integration complexity, compliance questions).**
- Root cause: Technical Evaluators are measured by risk avoidance, not revenue generation. Their job is to say no unless given a clear path to yes.
- Fix: Engage the Technical Evaluator early and directly — don't let them learn about the product from the champion's secondhand summary. Send a dedicated technical brief (security whitepaper, SOC 2 report, integration architecture doc). Schedule a "technical deep-dive" call with your solutions engineer before the full demo. Make them feel like a partner in the evaluation, not an afterthought.

**Problem: Buying committee is stuck in evaluation limbo — multiple meetings but no progression.**
- Root cause: Classic "group consensus" stall — Gartner research shows 77% of buying committees include at least one "consensus seeker" who delays decisions until everyone agrees. Without forcing a decision framework, committees drift.
- Fix: Introduce a Mutual Success Plan (MSP) — a co-created document that outlines evaluation criteria, timeline, milestones, and each stakeholder's sign-off role. This externalizes the timeline from you to the committee's own stated process. Ask the champion to help build it, which forces an internal alignment conversation. Set a clear decision date tied to a real business event (budget deadline, renewal date, new fiscal year).
