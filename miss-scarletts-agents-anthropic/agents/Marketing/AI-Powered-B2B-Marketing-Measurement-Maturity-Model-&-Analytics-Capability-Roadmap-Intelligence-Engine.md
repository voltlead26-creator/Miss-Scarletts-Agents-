## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce**: The roadmap will almost always require Salesforce campaign field standardization. Run the Campaign Source / Campaign Type audit first — it typically takes 2-3 weeks and unblocks all downstream attribution work. Use Salesforce Reports to validate stage conversion rates before investing in a BI tool.
- **HubSpot**: If on HubSpot Marketing Hub Professional or Enterprise, enable Multi-Touch Revenue Attribution Reports before buying a dedicated attribution tool. HubSpot's built-in W-shaped and time-decay models are sufficient for Stage 3 maturity and can be implemented in days.
- **Dreamdata / Factors.ai / Rockerbox**: Export the maturity assessment output and use it as the requirements brief when evaluating B2B attribution tools. Each vendor will map their solution against your gaps — having the scorecard prevents over-buying on capabilities you won't use for 12+ months.
- **Snowflake / BigQuery**: The roadmap's Phase 2 often includes a data warehouse decision. Use the unified customer ID requirement (connecting web, MAP, CRM, and product data) as the primary evaluation criterion — not pricing. A half-built data warehouse with poor ID resolution is worse than no warehouse.
- **Looker / Tableau / Google Looker Studio**: Start with Looker Studio for Phase 1 dashboards (free, fast to build, shareable). Graduate to Looker or Tableau only when self-serve analytics access becomes a bottleneck (typically at 15+ marketers or $50M+ ARR).
- **Zapier / Make**: Use automation to trigger Slack alerts when pipeline forecast deviates >15% from plan — turns the forecast model into an active early warning system without a data team.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The maturity scores feel inaccurate — the AI rated us higher/lower than reality.**
Solution: Rerun the assessment and add concrete examples. Instead of "Do you use multi-touch attribution? PARTIAL," specify: "We have HubSpot's first/last touch reports but no true multi-touch model — we manually adjust in spreadsheets quarterly." The more specific the input, the more calibrated the output. Also answer the question as your CRO would answer it, not as your MOps team would — the real test is whether the data is trusted by decision-makers, not whether it technically exists.

**Problem: The recommended roadmap budget is 3x what we have available.**
Solution: Ask for a "budget-constrained variant" with a $[YOUR_BUDGET] ceiling. The prompt will reprioritize around the single highest-ROI initiative (usually multi-touch attribution or UTM governance) and defer Phase 2-3 investments. In most cases, Phase 1 alone (foundation cleanup + free/included tool activation) delivers 60% of the value at 15% of the cost.

**Problem: The CRO or CFO won't engage with the measurement maturity concept — they just want dashboards.**
Solution: Skip the maturity framing entirely. Reuse Deliverable 4 (tool recommendation matrix) and Deliverable 5 (investment business case) and present them as a specific proposal: "We need $X to fix these 3 specific measurement gaps, and here's the revenue impact." Maturity models are valuable for planning but can feel abstract to non-marketing stakeholders — lead with the revenue impact and use the roadmap as the supporting detail.
