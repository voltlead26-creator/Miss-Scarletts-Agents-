# AI-Powered Account Research & Prospect Intelligence Synthesis Engine - Automated Pre-Outreach Account Brief Builder

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 15 min | **Tags:** outbound, abm, prospecting, b2b, account-intelligence, personalization, automation

## Overview
Automates deep account research across 10+ data sources—LinkedIn, Crunchbase, G2, job postings, news, SEC filings, tech stack detection, and social signals—to produce a comprehensive, outreach-ready account brief with ranked personalization hooks. Use this before any outbound sequence, ABM campaign, or executive engagement to replace 45 minutes of manual research with a 90-second AI synthesis.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version
You are a senior B2B sales intelligence analyst. Research the following account and produce a complete outreach brief.

ACCOUNT: [Company Name]
WEBSITE: [URL]
TARGET PERSONA: [Job Title]
MY PRODUCT: [Your Product — 1 sentence description]
MY ICP: [Ideal customer profile — 2-3 sentences]

Research and synthesize the following into a structured account brief:

1. COMPANY SNAPSHOT (5 bullets max)
   - Industry, size, funding stage, ARR if public, headcount
   - Recent growth signals (hiring surges, funding rounds, expansions)
   - Tech stack (inferred from job posts, BuiltWith, G2 reviews)
   - Key business initiatives and strategic priorities (from press releases, blog, LinkedIn posts)
   - Pain points and challenges signals (from G2 reviews, Glassdoor, job descriptions)

2. BUYING COMMITTEE MAP
   - Primary champion (most likely title, what they care about)
   - Economic buyer (who approves spend, what they optimize for)
   - Technical evaluator (likely title, technical concerns)
   - Potential blockers (who might oppose this purchase and why)

3. TRIGGER EVENTS (ranked by recency and relevance)
   - List 3-5 specific events that create urgency for my product right now
   - For each: event type, what happened, why it's relevant, confidence score (High/Medium/Low)

4. PERSONALIZATION HOOKS (ranked by quality)
   - 5 specific, research-backed hooks to open outreach
   - Each hook: the observation, the implied pain, the natural bridge to my product
   - Avoid generic compliments — every hook must reference a specific data point

5. OUTREACH STRATEGY
   - Recommended first touch channel and why
   - Key message angle that resonates with this account's specific situation
   - 3 conversation starters for LinkedIn connection request, cold email opener, and phone opener
   - Suggested sequence: touch 1 / touch 2 / touch 3 cadence with timing

6. ACCOUNT SCORE
   - ICP fit score (1-10) with reasoning
   - Timing/urgency score (1-10) with reasoning
   - Overall priority tier: Tier 1 (immediate), Tier 2 (this quarter), Tier 3 (nurture)

Output as structured markdown, ready to paste into a CRM or Notion page.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Hooks are generic despite research instructions**
*Fix:* Add a constraint: "Every hook must reference the company by name AND include one specific data point (date, dollar amount, person's title, product name, or verbatim quote). Reject any hook that could apply to 10 other companies."

**Issue: Tech stack inference is wrong or too speculative**
*Fix:* Specify sources: "Infer tech stack ONLY from job postings (require the tool to be named in the JD), BuiltWith data if available, or G2 profile integrations. Do not speculate. Mark unconfirmed tools as 'Possible — unconfirmed.'"

**Issue: Buying committee roles don't match actual org structure**
*Fix:* Add known contacts as input: "These are the LinkedIn profiles of people I've identified: [list]. Use them to anchor the buying committee map and infer others from org chart patterns."
