## Integration Tips
- **Gartner/Forrester portals:** Pull raw analyst report excerpts and feed directly into the "Analyst & Research Signals" input section. Set Google Alerts for your identified trend keywords to auto-feed new signals monthly.
- **SparkToro / Audiense:** Use audience intelligence tools to validate which trends your ICP actually cares about before investing in content.
- **Brandwatch / Mention:** Configure social listening alerts for your trend terminology and competitors'. Feed weekly signal reports into the Quick Version monthly.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Add a "Trend Mentioned" field to deal records. Track which thought leadership content pieces appear in buyer journeys of closed-won deals using UTM parameters and multi-touch attribution.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Create dark posts targeting your ICP with trend-related content to A/B test which trend narrative generates the highest CTR before committing to a full sprint.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build a "Trend Intelligence Dashboard" page that aggregates monthly signal inputs — use this as the standing input for quarterly runs of the Advanced Version.
- **Zapier automation:** Connect Google Alerts → Notion/Airtable to auto-log trend signals weekly; connect CRM "Trend Mentioned" field → Slack to notify the PMM team when deals reference your trend narrative.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The trend priority matrix produces high scores across the board, making prioritization difficult.
**Solution:** Add a tie-breaker dimension: "Founder/CEO conviction" — a trend your leadership team isn't personally excited to evangelize will stall in execution. Also add a "10x content" test: could you publish 10 pieces of genuinely differentiated content on this trend? If not, it's not rich enough to own.

**Problem:** Competitors copy your trend narrative within weeks of launch.
**Solution:** This is a signal you've identified a real trend — congratulations. Your response: accelerate original research publication (primary data is uncopyable), book every major speaking slot, and immediately brief 3-5 analysts to establish timestamps of your thought leadership. Emphasize your product's direct connection to the trend vs. their opportunistic positioning.

**Problem:** The analysis generates a strong trend POV but internal stakeholders (CEO, sales, product) don't align on it.
**Solution:** Run the "enemy" test in a group workshop: ask "What status quo or approach does this trend make obsolete?" If the room can't agree on the enemy, the POV isn't sharp enough. Rerun Section 3 with more specificity on the contrarian angle. A good thought leadership POV should make at least some people uncomfortable.
