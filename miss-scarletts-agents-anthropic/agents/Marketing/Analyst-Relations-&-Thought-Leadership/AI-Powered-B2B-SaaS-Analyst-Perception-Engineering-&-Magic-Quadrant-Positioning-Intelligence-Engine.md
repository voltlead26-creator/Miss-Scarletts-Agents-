## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Analysts ignore outreach or decline briefings**
Solution: Stop cold-emailing analysts. Use warm introductions through mutual customers or investors who know the analyst. Lead with a specific research question ("We're seeing X trend in our customer base — is this consistent with your research?") rather than a sales pitch. Analysts respond to peers with data, not vendors with products.

**Problem: Included in evaluation but scored low on Ability to Execute**
Solution: Analysts score Ability to Execute primarily on customer evidence — referenceable logos, published case studies with hard metrics, NPS/CSAT scores, and customer retention data. Audit what customer evidence you've shared in briefings vs. what competitors submit. Typically the gap is that competitors provide structured evidence packages (100-page RFI responses with appendices) while challengers provide slide decks. Build a formal evidence data room for your next evaluation cycle.

**Problem: Achieving good product scores but weak on strategy/vision criteria**
Solution: Vision criteria are driven by narrative, not product. Analysts award vision scores based on how well you articulate your market view, where the category is going, and how your roadmap responds to macro trends. Have your CEO or CPO lead analyst briefings (not just VP Product or PMM). The conversation must happen at the industry thesis level, not the feature level. Commission original research that advances a specific market narrative you own — and brief analysts on the findings before publication.
