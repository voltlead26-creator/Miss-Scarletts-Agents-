## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Survey findings are boring — every stat says "most companies do X, which is fine"**
Fix: Redesign questions to force ranking and prioritization rather than agreement scales. Add "forced choice" questions like "If you could only improve ONE metric this year, which would it be?" Also add the "confidence gap" question structure: first ask "How important is X?" then ask "How good is your company at X?" — the gap between importance and performance is almost always the most shareable finding.

**Problem: No press coverage despite compelling findings**
Fix: Your stats may be interesting to marketers but not newsworthy to journalists. Reframe findings through an economic or workforce angle ("B2B companies wasting $X billion annually on misattributed spend" or "1 in 3 marketing jobs at risk as AI attribution replaces manual reporting"). Pitch to HR/business press in addition to marketing trade press. Offer an exclusive to one major outlet 48 hours before the embargo lifts.

**Problem: Research generates downloads but no pipeline**
Fix: The landing page is likely not collecting enough qualification data. Add 2-3 progressive profiling fields (company size, primary challenge, timeline to evaluate solutions). Implement an instant "you qualify for X" trigger: if downloaders indicate they have the pain your product solves and the budget to fix it, route them to a fast-track SDR sequence rather than standard nurture. Also ensure sales can see exactly which contacts downloaded the research in their CRM dashboard.
