# AI-Powered B2B Earned Media Authority & Speaker Bureau Pipeline Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** earned media, thought leadership, speaker bureau, PR amplification, pipeline attribution, B2B authority building

## Overview
Systematically identifies, pursues, and measures earned media opportunities — media placements, podcast guest slots, conference speaking, and expert commentary — then connects each appearance to pipeline influence and revenue impact. Use when you need to justify thought leadership investment to the CFO, scale your executive speaker bureau, or build an always-on earned media engine that generates compounding authority and inbound pipeline.

## Success Metrics

A high-quality output should include:
- **Specificity**: Named publications, actual podcast titles, real conference names — not generic categories
- **Differentiated positioning**: Each authority territory should be clearly distinct from competitor POVs
- **Pitch quality**: Pitches should read like they were written by a seasoned PR professional — hook, angle, credibility, CTA in that order
- **Attribution realism**: Measurement recommendations must work without an enterprise analytics stack
- **Execution clarity**: A marketing coordinator should be able to pick up the plan and execute without additional guidance

Green flags: pitch templates that make you want to actually send them, authority territories that expose genuine competitor gaps, measurement frameworks that answer the CFO's "so what" question

Red flags: generic pitches ("our company is disrupting X"), vague authority claims, attribution models requiring custom data engineering

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Pitch open rates are high but response rates are near zero**
Fix: Your pitches are likely leading with the company/product instead of the journalist's or host's audience needs. Rewrite the opening 2 sentences to start with a provocative insight or data point your audience would immediately recognize as relevant. The pitch should feel like a gift to the journalist, not a request.

**Problem: Getting placements but seeing no measurable pipeline movement**
Fix: Three likely causes — (1) you're targeting the wrong publications where your ICP doesn't actually read, (2) there's no CTA or follow-up mechanism linking the media appearance to a next step, or (3) your tracking is broken. Audit: are your UTMs correctly implemented? Are reps checking contact history for earned media touches before discovery calls? Add a dedicated UTM-tagged landing page for every appearance.

**Problem: Executives are too busy to pitch and prepare for appearances**
Fix: This is a scaling problem, not a willingness problem. Build a "Speaker Prep in 30 Minutes" kit: a 1-page brief with key talking points, 3 data points to cite, 2 stories to tell, and 2 things NOT to say. Use AI to convert existing blog posts and sales decks into podcast talking points, so preparation is curation not creation. Set a quarterly cadence (4 appearances/quarter per executive) as a target rather than reactive requests.
