## MODULE 3: CO-CREATION CONTENT FRAMEWORKS

**Format A: Practitioner-Authored Original Research**

Structure:
- YOU provide: Research funding ($5K-$20K survey panel cost), data collection infrastructure, distribution platform, PR promotion
- PRACTITIONER provides: Survey design, analytical framing, written conclusions, their byline and promotion to audience
- GUARDRAIL: You see data before publication and can request methodology changes — you CANNOT edit their conclusions
- OUTPUT: "State of [Category] Report" with practitioner byline — your brand as presenting sponsor
- REACH: Practitioner's audience + your amplification (email, paid promotion, PR)
- ATTRIBUTION MECHANISM: Gated download requires ICP email — captured leads tagged "practitioner-research-[NAME]" in CRM

**Format B: Live Product Evaluation Session**

Structure:
- YOU provide: Sandbox environment with realistic (non-customer) demo data, 90-min facilitator, recording infrastructure
- PRACTITIONER provides: Evaluation criteria from their expertise, live commentary, authentic reactions (positive AND negative)
- GUARDRAIL: You do NOT see questions in advance, you do NOT edit the recording, you can ask them to blur customer names only
- OUTPUT: Unedited video (YouTube/LinkedIn) + practitioner's written summary
- CRITICAL: If they find real problems, they say so. The authenticity is the product.
- REPURPOSE: Transcript → blog post, highlights → social clips, quotes → sales enablement

**Format C: Community Benchmark Report**

Structure:
- PRACTITIONER provides: Access to their community audience for survey, analytical credibility, distribution
- YOU provide: Survey infrastructure (Typeform, etc.), data analysis support, design/formatting, co-promotion
- METHODOLOGY: Community members answer practitioner's questions — you have no influence on questions asked
- OUTPUT: "Practitioner Community Benchmark: [Topic]" — joint publication
- LEAD GENERATION: Registration gate captures ICP audience email addresses
- INSIGHT LOOP: Data insights feed your product roadmap conversations AND your analyst briefing narratives

**Format D: Expert Practitioner Interview Series**

Structure:
- YOU create the publishing platform (podcast, YouTube series, newsletter interview slot)
- PRACTITIONER owns the guest selection and questions — you suggest guests but don't control selection
- EDITORIAL INDEPENDENCE: You cannot reject interview content after recording unless it contains factual errors
- YOUR ROLE: Distribution, production support, light editing (not content editing), promotion
- COMMERCIAL LINK: Natural product mentions only when directly relevant — never forced
- MONETIZATION: Subtle sponsorship disclosure ("This series is presented by [Company]") — not product ads

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Practitioner publishes critical or negative content about your product after agreeing to partnership**
Solution: This is actually a credibility signal — do NOT request removal or relationship termination. Respond publicly and professionally ("We appreciate the honest take — here's how we're addressing [specific issue]"). Practitioners' audiences trust them precisely because they give honest opinions. Suppressing criticism destroys both the relationship and your credibility with their audience. Discuss privately, improve the product, and give the practitioner a follow-up opportunity to cover the improvement.

**Problem: Competitors approach the same practitioners with higher compensation, creating a bidding war**
Solution: Competing purely on price with well-funded competitors is a race to the bottom. Instead, differentiate on access and relationship quality: NDA roadmap previews, direct CPO/CEO access, co-ownership of research IP, genuine product influence. The best practitioners choose relationships where their expertise is valued, not just rented. Build this through the quality of quarterly advisory conversations, not the size of the check.

**Problem: Legal/compliance team rejects practitioner program due to FTC disclosure concerns**
Solution: Proactively build FTC-compliant disclosure language into all agreements. All paid or compensated content must be labeled as such (#sponsored, #ad, or explicit disclosure in copy). The disclosure requirement is non-negotiable — but compliant disclosure doesn't meaningfully reduce content performance if the content is genuinely credible. Brief practitioners on disclosure requirements at program kickoff; include it as a contractual obligation. Buyers are increasingly savvy about sponsored content and respond better to transparent sponsorship than discovered undisclosed sponsorship.
