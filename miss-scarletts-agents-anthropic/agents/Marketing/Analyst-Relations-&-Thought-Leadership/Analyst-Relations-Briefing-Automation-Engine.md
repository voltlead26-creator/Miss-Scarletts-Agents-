# DELIVERABLES

## 1. ANALYST POSITIONING NARRATIVE
Create a 3-paragraph positioning narrative optimized for analyst conversations. Structure using the "Context → Conflict → Resolution" framework:
- Context: market problem as analysts define it in their research
- Conflict: why current solutions (including market leaders) fail at this problem
- Resolution: how our approach uniquely solves it with defensible proof

## 2. MAGIC QUADRANT / WAVE SCORING STRATEGY
For the target report, provide:
- Analysis of likely "Completeness of Vision" criteria and how we score on each (1-5 scale with rationale)
- Analysis of likely "Ability to Execute" criteria and how we score on each (1-5 scale with rationale)
- Top 3 gaps to address before next evaluation cycle
- Specific evidence packages to submit for each criteria where we score below 4

## 3. BRIEFING DECK STRUCTURE
Create a 10-slide briefing deck outline with:
- Recommended slide title
- Key message to convey
- Proof points / data to include
- What NOT to include (common mistakes that annoy analysts)

Slide sequence: Market Problem → Our Unique Perspective on the Market → Product Demo Flow → Customer Outcomes Data → Competitive Differentiation Map → Vision & Roadmap → Ecosystem & Integrations → Financial Traction → Customer Reference Availability → Ask of the Analyst

## 4. ANALYST INQUIRY PREPARATION
Generate 8 inquiry questions to ask the analyst to:
a) Understand how they're scoring our competitors
b) Learn what "good" looks like on criteria where we're weak
c) Get their perspective on emerging market trends we should address in our roadmap
d) Understand what customer references they want to speak with and why

## 5. INFLUENCE CONTENT CALENDAR
Create a 90-day content cadence to increase analyst mindshare:
- Week 1-2: Initial briefing + follow-up collateral
- Week 3-4: Customer reference calls (which analyst, which customer, what topics)
- Month 2: Thought leadership content submission (research sponsorship, contributed data)
- Month 3: Pre-evaluation check-in + updated evidence package

For each item: owner, format, key message, success metric.

## 6. ANALYST RELATIONSHIP SCORING MATRIX
Score our current relationship with each target analyst (1-5 on: familiarity with our product, recency of interaction, sentiment based on past coverage, strategic importance to our goals). Output as a prioritized engagement list with recommended next action for each.

## 7. COMPETITIVE RESPONSE PREPARATION
For each top competitor in the target report, create a 3-sentence "contrast narrative" we can use when analysts compare us: what they do, what we do differently, why it matters to the buyer.

# OUTPUT FORMAT
Structure as a complete AR briefing preparation document with numbered sections. Use tables for scoring matrices. Include specific, actionable language — not generic frameworks. Every recommendation should be directly executable by a PMM or AR manager.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Tag analyst interactions as a contact type; log briefing dates, inquiry topics, and sentiment scores; build an AR pipeline view with next-touch dates and objectives
- **Notion / Confluence:** Store the briefing deck outline, evidence packages, and competitive contrast narratives as living documents; version-control each analyst report cycle
- **Google Sheets:** Maintain an analyst scoring matrix (Vision criteria × Ability to Execute criteria) as a shared doc; update scores quarterly as product ships and competitive landscape shifts
- **Klue / Crayon (competitive intel):** Feed competitive positioning data directly into your contrast narratives; set alerts for when competitors publish new analyst-facing content
- **Chorus / Gong:** Pull customer success stories and outcome metrics cited in calls to populate your evidence packages automatically
- **Krow AR / ARInsights:** Sync analyst contact records, interaction logs, and sentiment scores to your dedicated AR platform if budget allows; otherwise replicate the logic in a CRM deal stage workflow

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: The analyst keeps writing about competitors despite our briefings.**
Fix: Analysts write what their clients ask about. Request a copy of the inquiry questions their clients are asking about your category, then reverse-engineer content that answers those questions with your product as the solution. Produce a "client-facing" research note draft they can use as a template — analysts will often adopt your framing if it's well-researched and client-centric.

**Issue: We don't have enough customer data/metrics to back our claims.**
Fix: Run a 30-day customer evidence sprint: (1) survey your top 20 accounts for outcome metrics using a 5-question NPS+ROI survey, (2) ask your CS team for anonymized before/after metrics from 10 QBRs, (3) generate a third-party validated ROI study with a firm like Forrester Consulting (TEI study) — this is the gold standard and analysts cite these frequently.

**Issue: Our briefing goes well but the analyst doesn't change their written position.**
Fix: Written positions change at scheduled report cycles (typically annual). Between cycles, focus on getting cited in "Quick Takes," Hype Cycle entries, and inquiry responses. Ask specifically: "What 1-2 things, if we shipped them, would change how you'd position us in the next MQ?" Then ship those things and brief them again before the evaluation window opens.
