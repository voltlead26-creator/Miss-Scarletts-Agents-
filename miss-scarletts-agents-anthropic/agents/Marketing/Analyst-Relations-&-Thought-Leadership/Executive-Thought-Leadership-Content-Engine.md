## Integration Tips

- **LinkedIn Scheduling:** Export posts into Buffer, Hootsuite, or LinkedIn's native scheduler. Set a recurring Monday/Wednesday/Friday cadence. Use LinkedIn's native analytics to A/B test hook variations.
- **CRM Tracking:** Add a "Content-Sourced" source in HubSpot/Salesforce. Ask inbound leads "how did you hear about us?" on demo request forms — track content attribution over 90 days.
- **Notion Content Hub:** Create a Notion database with columns: Post Type | Draft | Published Date | Engagement Rate | Inbound Leads Generated. Review monthly.
- **Employee Advocacy via Slack:** Use a Zapier flow — when a post is published (via Buffer webhook), auto-send a Slack message to #marketing with the LinkedIn URL and a template comment for team members to use.
- **Speaker Pipeline in HubSpot:** Create a "Speaking Opportunity" deal type in HubSpot. Log each submission, track response status, and set a 21-day follow-up task if no reply.
- **Podcast Booking:** Use Calendly + a dedicated landing page (e.g., `yourcompany.com/podcast`) to capture inbound podcast requests generated by the pitch campaign.
- **Google Analytics / UTM:** For byline articles, create UTM-tagged CTAs in the author bio (e.g., `utm_source=hbr&utm_medium=byline&utm_campaign=exec-tl-q2`). Track in GA4 under Acquisition → Campaigns.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn posts get low engagement despite good content.**
Solution: The hook is almost always the issue. Test 3 different first-line styles: (1) bold claim, (2) specific number, (3) "I made a $X mistake." Never start with a question — LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes it. Also check posting time — B2B peaks are Tuesday-Thursday, 7:30–9 AM and 12–1 PM in the executive's local time zone.

**Problem: Conference submissions get no response.**
Solution: Cold submissions rarely work. Build the relationship first: comment thoughtfully on the conference organizer's LinkedIn for 30 days, email the programming lead 8-10 weeks before their submission deadline (not after), and reference a specific talk from last year's event. Offer a free 30-minute preview session. A warm submission converts 5x better than cold.

**Problem: Executive voice feels off — content sounds like marketing copy.**
Solution: Record the executive speaking for 10 minutes on the topic — just talking, stream-of-consciousness. Transcribe it (use Otter.ai or Claude). The transcript is your voice calibration. Have the AI rewrite the post using 3 direct quotes from the transcript as anchor phrases. The real voice shows up in the spoken word first.
