## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** We don't know which specific criteria Gartner/Forrester will use for our category's next evaluation.
**Solution:** Request a "Category Definition" inquiry with the lead analyst for your space — frame it as wanting to understand how the market is evolving, not as evaluation prep. Also review the last 2-3 published evaluations in your category; criteria change incrementally. Subscribe to Gartner Research Circle or Forrester's B2B Summit to hear criteria previews before evaluations open.

**Problem:** Our customer reference pool is too small — we have fewer than 10 referenceable customers.
**Solution:** Don't wait until the evaluation window opens. Start the reference program 6-12 months early. Use Customer Success to identify "promoter" customers (NPS 9-10), then run a structured reference recruitment campaign: personal outreach from CEO or VP CS, offer tangible benefits (early product access, co-marketing, speaking opportunities at your user conference). Even 15 well-prepared references across diverse segments can outperform 50 unprepared ones.

**Problem:** Analysts have a strong bias toward incumbent Leaders and keep dismissing our vision narrative.
**Solution:** Shift from asking analysts to change their perception to giving them the data that forces it. Commission a third-party research study that validates your market thesis (e.g., "The State of Real-Time Revenue Intelligence") and share it in your next briefing. Analysts can dismiss your claims, but they cannot easily dismiss their own methodology when faced with market evidence. Also recruit 2-3 marquee enterprise customers (Fortune 500) to speak directly to analysts — peer testimony from respected brands carries more weight than any vendor briefing.
