## Overview
Design and deploy a complete milestone-triggered customer lifecycle marketing system that runs autonomously from day one of onboarding through year five of tenure — turning every product adoption event, success milestone, and lifecycle stage transition into a personalized marketing moment that deepens engagement, drives expansion revenue, and builds customer advocacy. Use this when your company markets aggressively to acquire customers but has no structured marketing program once the contract is signed, or when your CS team is the only team engaging customers between onboarding and renewal.

**8 Lifecycle Marketing KPIs:**

   Alert: <55% → audit activation campaign engagement and CSM handoff process
   Cadence: Weekly (new cohort tracking); Monthly (rolling 90-day rate)

2. **Lifecycle Marketing NRR Contribution**
   Definition: NRR delta between accounts enrolled in lifecycle marketing vs. control group (10% of similar accounts excluded from campaigns)
   Calculation: NRR (Marketing-enrolled cohort) - NRR (Control cohort)
   Benchmark: 8–12pp NRR advantage for marketing-enrolled accounts within 12 months
   Alert: <4pp delta → lifecycle marketing is not moving the needle; audit stage assignment accuracy and trigger calibration

3. **Milestone Trigger-to-Action Rate**
   Definition: % of accounts that receive a milestone trigger campaign and take the intended action (feature adoption, expansion inquiry, advocacy participation)
   Calculation: Accounts taking target action / Accounts enrolled in trigger campaign × 100
   Benchmark: 18–28% for product milestone triggers; 35–45% for advocacy triggers (already warm)
   Alert: <10% on any trigger → campaign content or timing is misaligned with the trigger signal

4. **Expansion Signal-to-Close Rate**
   Definition: % of expansion signals that result in closed expansion ARR within 90 days
   Calculation: Expansion deals closed / Expansion signals fired × 100 (by signal type)
   Benchmark: 22–32% for seat utilization signals; 14–20% for feature depth signals

5. **Advocacy Conversion Rate**
   Definition: % of NPS Promoters (9–10) who complete at least one advocacy action (G2 review, case study, reference call) within 90 days
   Calculation: Promoters completing advocacy action / Total NPS Promoters × 100

   Alert: <20% → advocacy ask timing or friction level needs adjustment

6. **CSM Time Saved — Lifecycle Marketing Automation**

   Calculation: Average monthly CSM interactions logged in CS platform per account, before vs. after program
   Benchmark: 25–35% reduction in routine CSM touches within 6 months
   Alert: No reduction → lifecycle marketing not deflecting routine communication; review overlap between CS plays and marketing sequences

   Calculation: Average (date of 70% feature activation - contract start date) for activated accounts

   Alert: No improvement → feature discovery campaigns are not reaching users or the in-app guidance is not effective

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Product analytics data isn't making it into marketing automation reliably — lifecycle stage transitions aren't firing, or data is stale.**
Fix: Audit your data pipeline in three stages: (1) Is the product event being tracked correctly in Amplitude/Pendo/Mixpanel? Use the platform's "Live Events" feature to watch for your activation event in real-time with a test account. (2) Is the event reaching your CDP (Segment/Rudderstack)? Check the CDP debugger for event delivery confirmation. (3) Is the CRM field updating? In Salesforce or HubSpot, check the field update history for a test account. Most lifecycle automation failures are data pipeline issues, not marketing automation issues. If you don't have a CDP, build a lightweight daily sync: schedule a data warehouse query that exports key usage metrics to a Google Sheet, then use Zapier Tables to sync that Sheet to CRM contact properties. Not elegant, but it works until you invest in a proper CDP.

**Issue: Lifecycle marketing campaigns are sending to customers who already had those conversations with their CSM — creating awkward overlaps and CSM complaints.**
Fix: Build a 72-hour marketing suppression window that triggers whenever a CSM logs any interaction in the CS platform (call, email, meeting, note). Use a Zapier automation: when a ChurnZero/Gainsight activity is logged for a contact, set a HubSpot/Marketo contact property "Recent CSM Touch = True" with a timestamp; build a filter into all lifecycle workflow enrollment steps that excludes contacts where "Recent CSM Touch = True within 72 hours." Additionally, build a weekly Slack digest for each CSM showing which lifecycle marketing emails went to their accounts that week — CSMs who know what marketing is sending will stop feeling blindsided and start using the campaigns as conversation starters rather than competitors.

**Issue: NRR isn't improving despite high campaign engagement rates — customers are reading the emails but not taking action that translates to retention or expansion.**
Fix: Engagement metrics (opens, clicks) measure attention, not behavior change. Audit the gap between engagement and action in three places: (1) Are expansion signal campaigns creating CRM opportunities? If accounts are clicking expansion campaign emails but no opportunities are being created, the campaign CTA is too soft or CSMs aren't following up on the signal. Add a direct calendar booking link and make the CRM opportunity creation mandatory when a signal fires. (2) Are activation campaigns leading to activation? Compare activation rates for accounts who opened activation emails vs. those who didn't — if there's no difference, the email content is not actually guiding them to the activation milestone; add a direct in-app deep link that drops them exactly at the activation action, not the product homepage. (3) Are re-engagement campaigns actually rescuing accounts? If open rates for rescue campaigns are high but churn is not improving, the content is not addressing the real reason they disengaged — run 5 customer interviews with re-engaged accounts and 5 with churned accounts to understand what actually moves the needle vs. what feels good to send.
