## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales cycle is stuck at IT security review and GxP validation — deals sit for 6–9 months in IT procurement with no forward momentum**
Solution: This is the most common pipeline killer in pharma commercial technology, and it is almost entirely preventable if addressed proactively. The core failure is reactive compliance documentation — you are waiting for IT to ask for security questionnaire responses, SOC 2 reports, and validation documentation instead of proactively delivering them before IT gets involved. Implement the "Day 1 Procurement Package" protocol: the moment an opportunity reaches SQL stage (before the security review is even requested), your sales engineer delivers the complete package: pre-populated CAIQ/SIG questionnaire, SOC 2 Type II certificate, 21 CFR Part 11 compliance matrix, GxP validation documentation template, and reference IT contacts at customers who have completed validation. In parallel, request a dedicated 30-minute "IT Introduction Call" with the customer's IT security and validation lead — not to sell, but to map their validation timeline requirements onto your standard process. This single intervention consistently compresses pharma IT review from 7–9 months to 2–3 months. Also consider offering a "Validation as a Service" component in your contract — your team executes the IQ/OQ/PQ protocols using pre-approved scripts and delivers the complete validation package to the customer's QA team for review and sign-off. This removes the customer's primary resource constraint and transforms validation from a blocker into a competitive differentiator.

**Problem: JP Morgan Healthcare Conference is generating 80% of pipeline but the 10 months between JP Morgan cycles create a pipeline desert — quarterly targets are consistently missed in Q2 and Q3**
Solution: JP Morgan dependence is a common structural problem in pharma B2B marketing and it signals that your demand generation motion is conference-driven rather than trigger-driven. The fix requires activating the pipeline milestone monitoring system described in Layer 1. Start with a one-time ClinicalTrials.gov audit: export all Phase 2 and Phase 3 studies with your ICP therapeutic areas (oncology, rare disease, immunology), match against your target account list, and segment by estimated PDUFA timeline. This audit will typically identify 30–60 accounts in your active buying window that you are not currently engaging between conferences. For each, configure automated monitoring for Phase 3 completion and PDUFA date assignment. The goal is to have a systematic monthly trigger flow generating 15–25 new MQLs per month independent of conference cycle — making JP Morgan an amplifier of an existing pipeline motion, not the primary source. Additionally, activate ASCO (oncology), ISPOR (market access), and DPharm (digital health) as Tier 2 sources to distribute pipeline generation across the calendar year.

**Problem: Losing competitive evaluations to Veeva Commercial Cloud despite a clearly superior product — deals that started with enthusiasm are eventually awarded to incumbent**
Solution: Veeva incumbency is one of the most formidable competitive dynamics in pharma commercial technology — it is not primarily a product battle, it is a risk management battle. Pharma buyers default to Veeva in ambiguous situations because the career risk of a failed technology implementation in a commercial launch is severe and personally career-damaging. Your competitive displacement strategy must neutralize this risk narrative before the final decision. Specifically: (1) Introduce CCO-to-CCO peer reference calls at the evaluation stage — a 20-minute call with a current customer CCO who has successfully launched on your platform is worth more than any product demo or ROI calculation; (2) Build and distribute a "Migration Risk Mitigation Guide" that proactively addresses every concern a pharma CCO would have about switching from Veeva — data migration, field rep re-training, validation re-do, integration continuity — and show how you have solved each in previous implementations; (3) De-risk commercially by offering a launch-outcome guarantee: "If field force productivity benchmarks are not met at Month 6 post-launch, we will credit [specific dollar amount] against Year 2 contract" — Veeva does not offer outcome guarantees; (4) Engage at PDUFA minus 18 months, not PDUFA minus 6 months — if Veeva is already in a paid pilot when you arrive, you are fighting an uphill battle. The win rate differential for companies engaged 18+ months vs. 6 months before PDUFA is documented in your win/loss data and should be prominently featured in your sales process coaching.
