## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Customers are enthusiastic about referring but few introductions actually materialize.
**Solution:** The friction is in the mechanics of making the intro, not the willingness. Implement a "forward this email" template the CSM writes on behalf of the customer — the customer just clicks forward. Alternatively, use Intro.co or a personal Loom video the customer can share. Every additional step you require cuts follow-through by ~50%. Make the ask + the delivery a single 30-second action.

**Problem:** Referral asks feel awkward or transactional — customers don't respond well to incentive-first framing.
**Solution:** Lead with peer value, not reward. "I wanted to ask if you knew anyone struggling with [exact problem you solved for them] — I thought of you because you solved it so well, and I'd love to help someone in your network the same way." Mention the incentive only after they express interest. B2B buyers have strong anti-sales-pitch reflexes; activate their helper identity instead.

**Problem:** Referral pipeline isn't converting at the expected rate — referral deals are dropping off at demo or evaluation stage.
**Solution:** Referrals convert higher because of trust at entry, but they can stall if the referred prospect feels the deal is being rushed because "their colleague vouched for you." Add a "Social Proof Acceleration" step: ask the referring customer to send a short 2-minute Loom reinforcing why they use your product after the referred prospect completes a demo. This recency-of-trust effect increases close rates by 15-25% in B2B contexts.
