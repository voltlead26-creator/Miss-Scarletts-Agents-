**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, competitive-intelligence, positioning, displacement, switcher-acquisition, product-marketing, win-rate, messaging

## Overview
This prompt engineers a complete competitive displacement program — from identifying accounts ripe for switching to creating tailored anti-incumbent messaging, automating competitive battlecards, and designing the full-funnel campaign to win customers away from a specific rival. Use it when you need to systematically take market share from a named competitor or defend against competitive encroachment on your installed base.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Displacement messaging feels aggressive or makes prospects uncomfortable; they respond defensively about their current vendor choice.
**Solution:** Shift the framing from "Competitor X is bad" to "your requirements have evolved beyond what any first-generation platform can handle." The displacement narrative should attack the category, not the competitor — make it about the buyer's growth outpacing legacy solutions, not about vendor failure. Leads with "you've outgrown it" not "they've failed you."

**Problem:** Win rate in competitive deals improves, but the displacement program is attracting price-sensitive switchers who churn within 12 months.
**Solution:** Add ICP qualification signals to your displacement scoring model — weight toward accounts that match your expansion ICP (company growth rate, tech stack sophistication, marketing team size). Add a "switcher qualification" step to your discovery call: ask for current usage depth, integrations in place, and business goals for next 12 months. Discount-driven switchers are a churn liability; outcome-driven switchers become your best advocates.

**Problem:** The "comparison page" drives traffic but doesn't convert — visitors bounce after reviewing the table.
**Solution:** The comparison table alone rarely converts. Add "proof of switch" content immediately below — 3 micro case studies of companies that switched from the specific competitor, each with a 30-day outcome metric. Then add an embedded migration calculator that shows the prospect's estimated time-to-value and migration timeline based on their company size. The goal is to make switching feel concrete and fast, not abstract and risky.
