# CMO 90-Day Onboarding Playbook Generator - First 90 Days Diagnostic & Action System

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** cmo, leadership, onboarding, strategy, b2b, saas, organizational-audit, marketing-operations

## Overview
Generates a structured, prioritized 90-day marketing leadership playbook for a new or transitioning CMO — covering org assessment, quick wins, stakeholder alignment, positioning gaps, and a 30/60/90-day milestone roadmap. Use it when stepping into a CMO role, inheriting a marketing org after an acquisition, or resetting strategy after a significant pivot.

## Example Input/Output

**Input Example:**

Company: Meridian AI
Stage/ARR: Series B, $22M ARR
Business model: B2B SaaS, sales-led with light PLG (freemium tier launched 6 months ago, not yet converting)
Primary ICP: Head of Data / VP Analytics at 500-5,000 person financial services companies
Known marketing problems: (1) Pipeline is 80% outbound/SDR-generated, inbound almost nonexistent. (2) Positioning is vague — described differently by every sales rep. (3) CMO seat was empty for 8 months, team ran on autopilot.
Team: 5 people — 2 content writers, 1 demand gen manager, 1 SDR (yes, in marketing), 1 graphic designer
CMO background: Came from Palantir (enterprise analytics), deep in analyst relations and thought leadership, weaker in performance marketing

---

**Output Example (Module 1 — CEO Listening Tour Questions):**

Meeting goal: Understand what the CEO actually wants from marketing vs. what they say they want — and identify where marketing has failed to earn trust internally.

Questions:
1. "If marketing delivered exactly what you needed this year, what would be different about the company at the end of it — not in marketing metrics, but in business outcomes?" *(Reveals real success criteria, not proxy metrics)*
2. "When a sales rep loses a deal, what do they typically blame? And how much of that do you think is actually a marketing problem?" *(Surfaces the sales-marketing tension without confronting it directly)*
3. "What's your current hypothesis on why the freemium tier isn't converting? And what have you tried?" *(Tests whether CEO has a theory — and whether it conflicts with reality)*
4. "What would a board member have to see from marketing at the Day 90 review to say you made the right hire?" *(Reverse-engineers the success criteria so you can design toward it)*

Red flag answer: "I just need more leads." Marketing is seen as a volume engine, not a revenue engine. Reframe quickly or the CMO will spend all their time on MQL quantity.

Green flag answer: "I need buyers to come to us already convinced we're the right choice, so sales can close faster." CEO understands marketing's role in conviction-building. You have permission to invest in brand and content.

---

**Output Example (Module 3 — Quick Win #1):**

**Win: Positioning One-Pager — Standardize the Company Story in 30 Days**

Rationale: When every sales rep describes the product differently, every piece of marketing content is built on a different foundation. Fixing positioning first makes every other marketing investment more effective — and sales will immediately feel the difference.

Execution:
- Week 1: Interview 5 customers using the Jobs-to-be-Done framework. Ask "what were you hiring this product to do?" Record exact language.
- Week 2: Audit current website, sales deck, and top 10 email templates for consistency. Map the gaps.
- Week 3: Draft a 1-page Positioning Brief: category, primary ICP, top 3 differentiated outcomes, proof points per outcome, elevator pitch (2 sentences). Validate with CEO and top sales rep.
- Week 4: Distribute to full sales team in a 30-min Slack-first launch. Update homepage hero and sales deck intro slide with new language.

Success signal: VP Sales forwards the positioning brief to their team unprompted and says "finally."

Who to celebrate: Credit the sales team's input publicly — frame it as "you told us, we listened." This builds goodwill and increases adoption.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales leadership is dismissive of the listening tour — "just focus on pipeline, we don't need another audit."**
Solution: Schedule the VP Sales interview as an "I need your help" conversation, not a "tell me what's broken" audit. Lead with: "I know you've been waiting for a real marketing partner. Help me understand exactly what you need so I don't waste your team's time on the wrong things." Frame the audit as working for sales, not evaluating them. Show them the maturity scorecard rubric before you score it and let them co-score their own section — participation creates ownership.

**Problem: The Day 90 board presentation becomes a moving target because the CEO keeps changing what they want.**
Solution: Use Module 4's "Day 30 CEO Report" as a forcing function. Get the CEO to explicitly sign off on the 90-day success criteria in writing at the 30-day mark — what they want to see, what questions they need answered, what success looks like. Reference this document at Day 60 and Day 90. If the target moves, surface the original agreement and have a direct conversation about the trade-off before the board meeting.

**Problem: Quick wins feel forced or superficial — leadership isn't impressed.**
Solution: Quick wins must solve a pain that leadership already articulated during the listening tour, not pains the CMO invented. If leadership is unimpressed, it means the wins solved problems they don't care about. Go back to the listening tour notes and find the top complaint from the CEO or VP Sales, then design a quick win that directly addresses it, even if it's small. A fixed MQL definition that sales asked for twice is more impressive than a redesigned email template no one requested.
