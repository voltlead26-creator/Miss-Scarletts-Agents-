## WEEKLY OPERATING LAYER

MONDAY: Marketing Leadership Standup (30 minutes — CMO + function leads)
Format: Standing room only. No slides. Screen share of live dashboard only.

Agenda Template:
- 0:00-0:05 | Pipeline score for the week — green/yellow/red vs. weekly target
- 0:05-0:15 | Exception reporting ONLY — which channels are off-track and why (leads discuss pre-prepared analysis, not discovery)
- 0:15-0:25 | Decision queue — 2-3 decisions that need CMO input to unblock this week
- 0:25-0:30 | Priority alignment — each lead states their top 3 priorities for the week

Non-negotiable rules:
- No status updates on things that are on track — those are in the dashboard
- Every problem raised must come with a proposed solution and resource ask
- Decisions made in this meeting are final for the week (no re-litigating in Slack)

WEDNESDAY: Marketing-Sales Pipeline Sync (45 minutes — CMO + CRO/VP Sales + Rev Ops)
This is the single most important recurring meeting. Design it around these principles:
- Marketing leads the agenda (not sales) — CMO presents pipeline contribution data
- Focus on current-quarter pipeline coverage and next-quarter pipeline build
- No blame — only shared accountability for conversion rates

Agenda Template:
- 0:00-0:10 | Marketing pipeline report: MQLs created, pipeline sourced, pipeline influenced this week vs. last week vs. target
- 0:10-0:20 | Sales pipeline review: Which marketing-sourced opportunities are progressing / stalling / needs support
- 0:20-0:30 | MQL quality feedback: Sales shares conversion data on last month's MQLs — which converted and why, which didn't and why
- 0:30-0:40 | Campaign performance vs. pipeline impact: Review any campaigns that launched last 2 weeks — early signals
- 0:40-0:45 | Action items: Specific commitments from both marketing and sales for the next 7 days

FRIDAY: Campaign & Content Review (60 minutes — Demand gen + Content + PMM)
Internal team review. No cross-functional attendance.

Agenda:
- Campaign performance review: Any campaign running that week — results vs. benchmark
- Content pipeline: Assets completing vs. assets needed for next 2 weeks of campaigns
- Experiment results: Any A/B tests, new channel tests, copy variations — read and decide
- Next week readiness: Confirm all campaigns for next week are built, approved, and scheduled

MONTHLY MARKETING BUSINESS REVIEW (MBR) — held on Day 3-5 of new month
Attendees: CMO, function leads, CRO, CEO (optional), CFO (optional)
Duration: 90 minutes
Format: Pre-read deck sent 48 hours before meeting. Meeting is for decisions only, not information sharing.

PRE-READ DECK STRUCTURE (AI-generated on Day 1 from CRM + MA data):

Section 1: Executive Scorecard (1 slide)
- MQLs created vs. target: [Actual] / [Target] / [% attainment]
- Pipeline created vs. target: [Actual] / [Target] / [% attainment]
- Pipeline influenced vs. target: [Actual] / [Target] / [% attainment]
- Closed-won from marketing-sourced pipeline: [Actual] / [Target]

Section 2: What Happened (2 slides — facts only)
- What drove overperformance in channels that beat target (specific campaigns, audiences, offers)
- What drove underperformance in channels that missed (root cause analysis, not theory)

Section 3: Forward View (2 slides)

Section 4: Strategic Decisions Required (1 slide)
- List exactly 3-5 decisions that require CMO or executive input (budget reallocation, new channel investment, campaign pause/acceleration, headcount)
- Each decision framed as: [Decision] → [Option A with tradeoff] vs. [Option B with tradeoff] → [CMO recommendation]

MBR MEETING RULES:
- First 20 minutes: Questions on data only — no opinions until everyone has shared interpretation
- Middle 50 minutes: Decision discussion — only decisions that require this group to resolve
- Last 20 minutes: Action items and owners, confirmed before leaving room

# DELIVERABLE 2: PIPELINE EARLY WARNING SYSTEM

Design an AI-powered leading indicator system that predicts next-quarter pipeline problems 6-8 weeks before they materialize.

LEADING INDICATOR FRAMEWORK:

Indicator 1: MQL Velocity Trend (6-week rolling)
- Metric: MQLs created per week over the trailing 6 weeks
- Warning signal: 3 consecutive weeks below 90% of weekly target
- Prediction: If this trend holds, you will miss next quarter's pipeline coverage by [X]%
- AI action: Generate remediation options with 4-6 week lead time (campaigns take time to build pipeline)

Indicator 2: Pipeline Coverage Ratio Trajectory
- Metric: Open pipeline coverage ratio for next quarter (pipeline / quarterly target)
- Warning signal: Coverage drops below 3.0x for enterprise / 2.5x for mid-market / 2.0x for SMB
- Industry benchmark: Enterprise companies need 4x+ coverage to reliably hit quarterly targets at typical conversion rates
- AI action: Alert + calculate how many net-new MQLs are needed at current conversion rates to restore coverage

Indicator 3: Channel Efficiency Degradation
- Metric: Cost per MQL by channel vs. 90-day trailing average
- Warning signal: Any primary channel's CPL increases more than 25% vs. trailing average
- Interpretation: Rising CPL often precedes a volume cliff (budget gets exhausted faster, fewer leads)
- AI action: Flag for reallocation analysis — is the budget better spent elsewhere?

Indicator 4: Sales Velocity Slowdown (marketing-sourced)
- Metric: Average days-to-close for marketing-sourced opportunities vs. prior quarter
- Warning signal: Velocity slows more than 15% — deals taking longer to progress from stage to stage
- Interpretation: Often signals competitive pressure, economic headwinds, or ICP messaging misalignment
- AI action: Cross-reference with win/loss data to identify if specific personas or segments are stalling

Indicator 5: SDR Meeting Acceptance Rate (for outbound-heavy GTMs)
- Metric: Meeting booked rate per 100 outbound sequences initiated
- Warning signal: Rate declines more than 20% vs. prior month
- Interpretation: Message-market fit erosion, contact data quality degradation, or ICP shift
- AI action: Flag for immediate message testing and ICP validation — generate 5 hypothesis-driven sequence variants

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales leadership won't commit to the marketing-sales alignment protocol — they see it as marketing trying to hold them accountable**
Solution: Don't frame it as accountability — frame it as joint forecasting. Position the operating rhythm as "we need the same data to make the same plan." Start with one shared metric that both teams already care about (MQL-to-opportunity conversion rate) and build trust through a single joint review before expanding the cadence. Bring the CRO into the Monday standup for the first 4 weeks so they see marketing's self-accountability in action — this disarms the adversarial framing faster than any process document.

**Problem: The early warning system generates too many alerts and the CMO starts ignoring them (alarm fatigue)**
Solution: Recalibrate thresholds — if you're getting more than 2-3 alerts per week, your yellow thresholds are set too sensitive. Alerts should require action, not awareness. Reduce alerts to only situations where a decision must be made this week to prevent a miss. For general awareness items, use a weekly digest instead of real-time pings. The rule: if the alert doesn't require a decision in the next 72 hours, it doesn't need to be an alert — it belongs in the Friday review dashboard.

**Problem: The quarterly QBR takes 4 hours but no decisions actually get made — it turns into a presentation session**
Solution: Send all data in the pre-read 48 hours before, and open the meeting with the rule: "We are not reviewing data. We are making decisions. If you want to understand how we got to this data, ask during the 20-minute Q&A. After that, we decide." Restructure the QBR so the first 30 minutes are explicitly a rapid-fire decision sprint on the 5-8 biggest calls for next quarter. Once decisions are locked, the rest of the session is execution planning — which is fast and energizing, not slow and political.
