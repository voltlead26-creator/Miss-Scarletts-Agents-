# Marketing Scenario Planning & Stress-Testing Engine - CMO-Level Contingency Intelligence for Volatile Markets

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25-30 min | **Tags:** cmo, strategy, scenario-planning, budget, risk-management, b2b, forecasting, leadership

## Overview
Transforms your annual marketing plan into a resilient, scenario-hardened playbook by stress-testing current investments against 5 market scenarios — from hypergrowth to severe contraction — and generating trigger-based contingency responses for each. Use it at the start of each fiscal year, before board presentations, or any time market conditions shift materially.

My current marketing targets: [MQLs: X, Pipeline: $X, New Logos: X, NRR contribution: X%]

Primary channels: [e.g., paid LinkedIn, SEO/content, outbound SDR, events, partner/channel]

Now build me a 5-scenario stress test:

**Scenario 1 — Bull Case:** Market accelerates 20%+ faster than plan. Competitors pull back. Buyers are aggressive. What do I do with the extra capacity and any budget flex? Which channels get doubled? What new bets do I make?

**Scenario 2 — Base Case:** Plan holds. No major surprises. What are the 3 biggest execution risks that could still derail me even in a "normal" year?

**Scenario 3 — Bear Case:** Pipeline coverage drops to 1.5x. CPL increases 35%. Two key hires fall through. What do I cut first, what do I protect at all costs, and how do I hit a revised number with 70% of current budget?

**Scenario 4 — Competitive Disruption:** A well-funded competitor launches a free tier or acquires our #1 partner. Or a new AI-native competitor enters and begins stealing deals in the bottom of our funnel. How do I respond marketing-wise within 90 days?

**Scenario 5 — Macro Shock:** CFO implements a 30% budget freeze mid-year with 2 weeks notice. Hiring freeze kicks in. How do I protect pipeline, retain the team I have, and position for recovery?

For each scenario, give me:
1. The 3 leading indicators I should monitor NOW to know which scenario is playing out
2. The trigger threshold that activates the contingency response (e.g., "if MoM pipeline coverage falls below 2x for 6 consecutive weeks")
3. The specific marketing actions to take in the first 30 days of that scenario
4. Which budget line items to protect vs. cut vs. accelerate
5. The key message to bring to the CEO/board when presenting this scenario

Format as a decision-ready playbook I can share with my leadership team.

### PART 1: SCENARIO MATRIX

For each of the 5 scenarios below, complete the full analysis:

**SCENARIO DEFINITIONS:**

- **S1 — Hypergrowth (15% above plan):** Market conditions accelerate. ICP buyers increase budget and urgency. Your pipeline coverage hits 5x+ and close rates improve 20%. Hiring market opens up.

- **S3 — Soft Market (20-30% miss):** Buyer sentiment softens. Sales cycles extend 30%. CPL increases 25-40%. 1-2 key hires fall through. You'll miss MQL target by 25% without adjusting.
- **S4 — Competitive Disruption:** A direct competitor makes a move that materially impacts your demand funnel (free tier launch, acquisition of a key partner, category re-definition by a well-funded player, AI feature parity achieved by incumbent). Win rates drop 15 points in 90 days.
- **S5 — Budget Shock (30%+ budget freeze mid-year):** CFO implements emergency budget cuts. Marketing budget reduced by 30% with 2-4 weeks notice. Headcount freeze. All discretionary spend suspended.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Pull the 5 early warning metrics into a weekly dashboard view. Set automated alerts when any metric crosses the threshold you defined in each scenario's trigger criteria. Use workflow automation to notify CMO + RevOps lead via Slack when 2+ signals breach threshold simultaneously.
- **Google Sheets / Notion:** Build the Scenario Matrix Table (Part 1 summary) as a live spreadsheet with color-coded RAG indicators that update from CRM data pulls. Share with CEO and CFO as a standing weekly artifact.
- **Tableau / Looker / Metabase:** Create a "Scenario Health Dashboard" with one panel per scenario, tracking live metrics vs. trigger thresholds. Update weekly in Monday morning team standup.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate a weekly "Scenario Check-In" that pulls 3-5 key metrics from HubSpot, LinkedIn, and Google Ads and delivers a formatted Slack summary to #marketing-leadership every Monday at 9am, flagging any metrics approaching threshold.
- **Lattice / 15Five:** Add scenario status as a standing check-in question for the CMO's direct reports: "Which scenario does the current data most resemble?" — builds team awareness and distributed signal detection.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Monitor sales calls for language indicating S3 or S4 signals (buyer says "budget freeze," "procurement on hold," "our CFO needs to approve now") — train a Gong tracker for these phrases and route alerts to CMO.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Scenario triggers are too vague — team argues about whether the threshold has been breached.
**Solution:** Revise triggers to require data from a single source of truth (always Salesforce, never "the SDR team's gut feel"). Set a rule: if two people looking at the same dashboard disagree on trigger status, the threshold is not specific enough. Rerun the Advanced prompt with the instruction "make every trigger observable from a single data source within 30 seconds."

**Problem:** The 30-day playbooks feel generic — cutting events and shifting to content is always the answer.
**Solution:** The prompt needs more specificity about your channel mix and fixed vs. variable cost structure. Add to the Advanced prompt: "Flag which line items have contractual lock-in of 60+ days, which can be paused within 2 weeks, and which have exit clauses. This is a key constraint — the playbook must work within these realities."

**Problem:** Board pushback — "You're planning for failure."
**Solution:** Reframe the scenario plan as a "CMO Readiness Assessment" not a contingency plan. Emphasize that S1 (hypergrowth) is the most likely outcome given current pipeline trends, and that the stress-test work has identified 3 fragile investments that are being de-risked proactively — increasing confidence in the plan, not signaling fear.
