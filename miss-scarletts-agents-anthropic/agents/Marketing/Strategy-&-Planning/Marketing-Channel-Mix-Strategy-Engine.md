## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom "Channel Attribution Source" property mapped to each recommended channel; build a Pipeline by Channel report to track monthly contributions vs. projections. Pull the output KPI targets directly into HubSpot Goals.
- **Salesforce:** Build a Channel Mix Dashboard using Salesforce Reports; create Opportunity Source fields matching your recommended channels; set up Pipeline Inspection view filtered by channel to track velocity per channel.
- **Google Sheets / Looker Studio:** Paste the Budget Allocation table into a Google Sheet with actual spend columns; connect to Google Analytics 4 and LinkedIn Campaign Manager via Looker Studio for a live channel ROI waterfall.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Filter call recordings by opportunity source to validate channel quality — do deals from Channel A close faster than Channel B? Feed back into channel scoring quarterly.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate the monthly channel performance pull: trigger on first of month → pull data from Google Ads, LinkedIn, HubSpot → format into channel scorecard → send to Slack #marketing-ops channel for CMO review.
- **6sense / Bombora:** If running ABM channels, connect intent data feed to automatically update the Channel Scoring Matrix's ICP Fit score monthly as intent signal concentration shifts.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Budget allocation outputs feel generic and don't match our specific market.**
Solution: Re-run the Advanced Version with richer competitor channel data. Use SimilarWeb to identify traffic sources for 3-5 competitors, add their dominant channels to the "Competitors' dominant channels" input, and explicitly state your market's seasonality (e.g., "Q4 is dead for manufacturing — no events, low email open rates"). The more market-specific constraints you add, the more differentiated the output.

**Problem: The recommended channels require capabilities/headcount we don't have.**
Solution: Add a constraint to the Advanced Version: "We have [X] full-time marketers available. Each channel must be operable with ≤ [Y] hours/week of headcount. Flag any channel requiring a dedicated specialist hire." The engine will auto-filter to channels executable with your current team and flag where hiring unlocks scale.

**Problem: Channel performance projections seem unrealistically optimistic.**
Solution: Add your historical performance data explicitly: "Our actual Google Ads CPL is $340, LinkedIn CPL is $890, and SDR-sourced SQL rate is 18%. Use these as baseline benchmarks, not industry averages." Grounding projections in your actual unit economics produces conservative, board-defensible forecasts.
