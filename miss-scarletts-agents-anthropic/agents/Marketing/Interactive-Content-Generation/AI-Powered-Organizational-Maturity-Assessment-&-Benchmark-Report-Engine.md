Add a custom field "RevOps Maturity Stage" to the Lead and Contact objects. Create a Salesforce report filtered by stage to give AEs a daily view of high-intent Stage 4-5 completers. Use Process Builder or Flow to trigger a Slack notification to the assigned SDR within 5 minutes of a Stage 3+ completion. **Typeform / Jotform / Outgrow:**
Build the assessment in Typeform (best UX, native conditional logic) or Outgrow (purpose-built for calculators and assessments with built-in benchmarking display). Use Typeform's Hidden Fields to pass UTM parameters through the assessment so you can attribute maturity completions back to the traffic source in your analytics stack.

**Zapier / Make:**
Build a Zap that fires on assessment completion → creates/updates contact in CRM → adds to stage-specific email sequence → notifies SDR in Slack → logs completion event in your data warehouse (Snowflake, BigQuery). This entire flow runs in under 60 seconds with no manual touches.

**Notion / Google Sheets:**
Maintain the benchmark dataset in a shared Notion database or Google Sheet. As assessment responses accumulate, run a quarterly recalibration of percentile benchmarks — even 200 responses gives you statistically meaningful benchmarks for 3-5 company size segments. Share the raw (anonymized) dataset with your analyst relations contacts annually; this is the raw material for a Forrester or IDC co-branded study.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Assessment completion rate is below 55%**
Root cause is almost always question count or cognitive load. Audit the assessment: if any question has more than 5 answer options or uses two-concept answers ("we do X and Y"), restructure it. Run a 5-person cognitive walkthrough — time each person and note where they pause or re-read. The fix is usually cutting 3-4 questions (get to 10-12 max) and simplifying answer language to plain first-person statements. Also check mobile rendering — 60%+ of B2B professionals now complete assessments on mobile; long answer options truncate on small screens and destroy completion rates.

**Problem: Stage distribution is skewed — 70%+ of respondents land at Stage 2-3**
This is a scoring calibration issue, not a data problem. If your question weights are too conservative, most respondents cluster in the middle. Recalibrate by adding 2-3 "Stage 5 indicator" questions that only the most advanced organizations can answer truthfully (e.g., "Our AI system proactively surfaces anomalies before leadership asks for them"). This spreads the distribution and makes Stage 4-5 scores feel genuinely earned.

**Problem: High completion rate but low email capture (below 40% gate conversion)**
The gate friction is too high relative to the perceived value of the full report. Two fixes: (1) Show more of the report before the gate — reveal the headline score and one dimension breakdown before asking for email; blur the remaining three. (2) Strengthen the gate value copy — replace "Get your full report" with "See how you compare to 1,400+ companies your size — full benchmarks unlocked." Specificity in the value promise dramatically increases gate conversion.
