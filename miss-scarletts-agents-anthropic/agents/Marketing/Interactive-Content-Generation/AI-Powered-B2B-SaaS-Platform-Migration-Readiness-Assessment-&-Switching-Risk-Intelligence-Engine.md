# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Platform Migration Readiness Assessment & Switching Risk Intelligence Engine - Convert Fence-Sitters Into Committed Switchers

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, enterprise, interactive-content, migration, switching-cost, sales-enablement, lead-generation, change-management, competitive-displacement, procurement

## Overview
Generates a complete platform migration readiness assessment framework — including organizational, technical, and risk scoring logic — that qualifies how ready an enterprise buyer is to switch platforms, produces a personalized migration roadmap, and quantifies the risk of switching versus staying. Use this when enterprise deals stall because prospects fear migration complexity, when you want to displace incumbents by making the switching decision feel manageable, or when you want to build a high-converting interactive assessment as a gated demand generation asset.

### SECTION 1: MIGRATION READINESS SCORECARD (MRS)

Design a weighted 25-question assessment across five domains. For each domain include:
- The 5 assessment questions (each scored 1-5)
- Scoring weight (% of total MRS)
- What a high score indicates vs. a low score
- Red flag indicators that require immediate attention

**Domain 1: Technical Readiness (25% weight)**
Assessment dimensions:
- Integration complexity: How many active integrations does the current platform have? (1=20+, 5=fewer than 5)
- Custom code/configuration: How extensively has the current platform been customized? (1=heavily customized, 5=standard configuration only)
- Data volume and structure: How clean and structured is the data in the current platform? (1=inconsistent/poorly structured, 5=clean, well-documented schema)
- API availability: Does the current platform have full API export capability? (1=no/limited, 5=full API with documentation)
- IT/DevOps resource availability: Does the company have dedicated technical resources for the migration? (1=no dedicated resources, 5=dedicated migration team ready)

Red flag threshold: Domain score below 12/25 → flag as "Technical Pre-Migration Work Required" with specific remediation steps

**Domain 2: Organizational Readiness (20% weight)**
Assessment dimensions:
- Executive sponsorship: Is there a C-suite champion actively driving the platform switch? (1=no champion, 5=CEO/COO personally sponsoring)
- Change management capacity: Does the organization have experience managing large software transitions? (1=first major migration, 5=experienced with multiple platform transitions)
- End-user adoption track record: How successfully has the organization adopted past technology changes? (1=historically high resistance, 5=strong adoption culture)
- Internal project management: Is there a dedicated project manager or PMO for this initiative? (1=no PM assigned, 5=dedicated PM with migration PM experience)
- Stakeholder alignment: Are all affected departments aligned on the switch decision? (1=active internal opposition, 5=unanimous buy-in across all departments)

**Domain 3: Data Readiness (20% weight)**
Assessment dimensions:
- Data governance maturity: Is there a documented data governance policy for the current system? (1=no documentation, 5=fully documented with data dictionary)
- Data deduplication status: Has the data been deduped and validated recently? (1=never cleaned, 5=cleaned within last 6 months)
- Historical data requirements: How much historical data must be migrated vs. archived? (1=100% of historical data required in new system, 5=only last 12 months required)
- Custom field complexity: How many custom fields, picklists, and lookup tables exist in the current system? (1=100+ custom configurations, 5=fewer than 20)
- Data compliance requirements: Are there regulatory requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, SOX) that affect data handling during migration? (1=complex multi-regulation environment, 5=no regulated data in scope)

**Domain 4: Process Readiness (20% weight)**
Assessment dimensions:
- Workflow documentation: Are current platform workflows documented in writing? (1=undocumented/tribal knowledge only, 5=fully documented process maps)
- Training program capacity: Can the organization run a structured user training program during migration? (1=no training infrastructure, 5=dedicated L&D team ready)
- Business continuity plan: Is there a plan for maintaining operations during the migration period? (1=no continuity plan, 5=detailed parallel-running plan with rollback procedure)
- Process ownership clarity: Is there clear ownership of each process that will be migrated? (1=shared/unclear ownership, 5=named process owners for every workflow)
- User community size: How many end users will need to be trained and onboarded? (1=500+ users, 5=fewer than 25 users)

**Domain 5: Business Readiness (15% weight)**
Assessment dimensions:
- Budget certainty: Is migration budget approved or in final approval stage? (1=not yet in budget, 5=fully approved and released)
- Timeline urgency: Is there a compelling event or deadline driving the switch? (1=no urgency, 5=hard deadline with business consequence if missed)
- Vendor relationship status: Is the relationship with the current vendor terminated or in termination notice? (1=actively renewing current vendor, 5=contract terminated, hard stop date confirmed)
- Internal champions: How many internal champions are actively promoting the switch? (1=zero champions, 5=three or more champions across different departments)
- Board/leadership visibility: Is this migration project on the executive team's radar? (1=not visible, 5=on Q1 board agenda with success metrics)

**MRS INTERPRETATION GUIDE:**
- 85-100: Migration-Ready — Full speed ahead. Recommend 90-day implementation start.
- 70-84: Almost Ready — 2-3 specific preparation steps before launch. Timeline: 30-60 day pre-work then migration.
- 55-69: Conditionally Ready — Address 2 domain-level gaps first. Timeline: 60-90 day preparation, then migration.
- 40-54: Preparation Required — Significant pre-migration work needed in [flagged domains]. Build a 90-day readiness plan before committing to migration.
- Below 40: Not Yet Ready — Major organizational or technical barriers. Recommend a readiness accelerator program before migration begins.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The prospect's MRS comes out very low (below 55) and it feels like it's pushing the deal away rather than accelerating it.
**Solution:** Reframe the low MRS as a competitive advantage. A low score means they have more exposure to migration risk with ANY vendor — not just yours. Use the readiness gap analysis to position a "Readiness Accelerator" pre-engagement: a structured 60-day program (consulting hours, data cleanup support, stakeholder alignment workshops) that brings them to MRS 70+ before migration begins. This turns a "not ready" into a paid pre-implementation engagement and deepens the relationship before the main contract. The key message: "You're not migration-ready yet — but you will be by [date], and we're the only vendor offering a structured path to get you there."

**Problem:** Prospect's IT team disputes the integration migration complexity estimates and insists their environment is unique.
**Solution:** Ask IT to walk you through their integration map in a 30-minute call. Use Section 4's integration architecture table as the meeting agenda — go line by line. This call serves two purposes: (1) you get accurate data to update the MRS with real numbers, and (2) the IT team realizes as they narrate the complexity out loud that it's more manageable than they feared. Bring in your implementation engineer or solutions architect for this call — a peer-to-peer technical conversation between their IT director and your implementation lead changes the dynamic from vendor pitch to collaborative scoping.

**Problem:** The champion is using the assessment to build their internal case but is getting pushback from their CFO on the inaction cost calculations.
**Solution:** The CFO is right to scrutinize the numbers — your job is to make them audit-proof. For each inaction risk dollar estimate, replace AI-generated assumptions with sourced inputs: (1) pull the competitor's actual renewal escalation clause from the prospect's existing contract, (2) use the prospect's actual campaign velocity data (from the discovery call) for the opportunity cost calculation, (3) source the migration complexity increase from a named analyst report or your own implementation data. Send the champion a "CFO-Defensible Version" of the inaction cost analysis with every number traced to a named source. The goal is for the champion to be able to say in their CFO meeting: "Every number here came from our own data or a named external source — not the vendor."
