## Overview
Generates a complete AI automation maturity assessment framework — including scoring logic, question architecture, personalized report templates, and CTA sequences — that attracts mid-to-bottom-funnel buyers, self-qualifies them by maturity tier, and delivers personalized recommendations that position your solution as the natural next step. Use this when you want to build a high-converting interactive lead magnet, when you need to segment prospects by readiness before handing to sales, or when you're competing in a market where buyers don't yet know where they fall on the AI adoption curve.

Define four maturity stages for AI automation adoption in my product category:
- Stage 1: Manual/Reactive (no automation, everything done by hand)
- Stage 2: Partial Automation (some tools, disconnected workflows)
- Stage 3: Integrated Automation (connected stack, AI-assisted decisions)

- Use radio buttons or 1-5 scales (no open text)

- Cover: current tooling, team workflows, data integration, decision-making processes, and strategic AI investment

3. SCORING LOGIC
Define the scoring algorithm:
- Points per answer choice (1-4 scale mapping to maturity tier)
- Category weighting (which dimensions matter most)
- Tier thresholds (score ranges that map to each of the 4 stages)
- Tie-breaking rules for respondents near boundaries

4. PERSONALIZED REPORT TEMPLATES (one per tier)
For each maturity tier, write a personalized report that:
- Validates where they are (no shame, just facts)
- Quantifies the cost of their current stage (use industry benchmarks)
- Shows the specific 3-step path to the next maturity level
- Positions [Product Name] as the enabling technology for the next step
- Includes 2-3 peer company examples at their same stage who advanced

5. CTA SEQUENCE
For each tier, write 3 CTAs ranked by conversion probability:
- Primary CTA (highest intent)
- Secondary CTA (nurture)  
- Tertiary CTA (early-stage education)

Output as a structured document I can hand directly to a developer or Outgrow/Ion builder.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Respondents are clustering at Tier 2-3 and very few fall into Tier 1 or Tier 4 — the distribution doesn't match your designed tier prevalence.
**Solution:** Recalibrate the question difficulty. Tier 1 answers should describe behaviors 100% of Tier 1 buyers actually exhibit (e.g., "we use spreadsheets" — not "we have no automation at all"). Tier 4 answers should require something genuinely rare (e.g., "AI agents complete this workflow with no human involvement"). Run a 20-person internal calibration test: have your team assign companies they know to tiers, then run those companies through the assessment and check agreement rate. If calibration is off by >15%, adjust the answer choices for the 5 highest-volume questions.

**Problem:** Sales team doesn't trust the tier scores and is ignoring the CRM enrichment data.
**Solution:** Run a 90-day cohort analysis showing conversion rate, deal size, and sales cycle length by maturity tier. Present to sales leadership with specific examples of Tier 3 respondents who converted and Tier 1 respondents who churned after 3 nurture emails. Maturity scores gain trust when they demonstrably predict deal quality — if the initial correlation isn't strong, review whether the scoring dimensions align with your actual ICP definition and adjust. Consider building a joint calibration session where AEs score 10 accounts by hand and compare to the assessment-derived tiers.

**Problem:** Conversion from assessment completion to booked demo is below 10% for Tier 3 respondents, who should be high-intent.
**Solution:** The problem is almost always the CTA architecture, not the assessment quality. Tier 3 respondents know they have a problem — they don't need more education. Audit the primary CTA copy: is it framed around their specific bottleneck (the weakest dimension) or is it generic ("See a demo")? Replace with a high-specificity CTA that mirrors language from their report: "You scored 12/20 on Data Integration — see how [Product] closes that specific gap in 20 minutes." Tier 3 buyers respond to precision, not breadth.
