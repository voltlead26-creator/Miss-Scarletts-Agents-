## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Completion rates are below 50% — prospects are dropping off mid-survey.
**Solution:** Run a drop-off analysis in your survey tool to identify exactly which question is causing abandonment. Common culprits: (1) Questions asking for revenue ranges or headcount appear too early — move company profile questions to Sections 2-3 after establishing value; (2) Survey length is visible and looks intimidating — use a progress bar and rewrite section headers to show time remaining ("Section 2 of 4 — 3 minutes"); (3) Questions feel like vendor qualification, not peer benchmarking — reframe from "What is your monthly marketing budget?" to "How many programs does your team run per quarter?" which captures the same signal with less friction.

**Problem:** AEs aren't using the benchmark data in outreach — they're still sending generic cold outreach.
**Solution:** The benchmark data is only useful if AEs can access it at the right moment. Solve this by: (1) Creating a Slack notification that fires when a named account completes the benchmark — include the top 3 findings in the alert, not just the score; (2) Adding benchmark score to the Salesforce "Account Intelligence" page layout so it's the first thing AEs see when researching a prospect; (3) Running a 30-minute AE enablement session showing one example of how referencing a benchmark finding in outreach doubled response rates — concrete case study beats abstract instruction.

**Problem:** We don't have enough benchmark data to make peer comparisons meaningful in the first 60 days of launch.
**Solution:** Use a three-stage data seeding approach: Stage 1 (Pre-launch, Weeks 1-4) — run the benchmark with your existing customer base. Their responses seed the peer comparison dataset. This also surfaces product gaps and customer advocacy opportunities. Stage 2 (Soft-launch, Weeks 5-8) — invite warm prospects and newsletter subscribers first. Keep the report live but mark comparisons as "Early Access Data — growing daily." Stage 3 (Full launch, Week 9+) — once you have 100+ responses, comparisons are statistically meaningful enough for full distribution. Relaunch with updated stats and an updated PR angle: "100 [ICP title]s have taken the benchmark — here's what we found."
