## Overview
Ingests CRM activity data, training completion records, deal history, and manager coaching logs to identify exactly which onboarding activities, content touchpoints, and skill milestones drive fastest ramp to full quota — then outputs a predictive ramp model and prescriptive playbook to cut time-to-productivity by 20–40%. ## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce:** Build the Ramp Health Score as a calculated field on the User/Rep object, pulling from Activity metrics, Opportunity stage progression, and a custom LMS completion sync field. Use Flow automation to trigger Chatter alerts when RHS drops to Red.
- **Mindtickle / Highspot / Seismic:** Connect LMS completion data via API to Salesforce custom fields. Set automated nudges at Day 21 for reps below 50% certification completion. Use Scorecard features to tie skill scores to deal outcome data.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Export Discovery Call Scorecards weekly to a shared Enablement dashboard. Tag calls by rep hire cohort for longitudinal analysis. Use Gong's Team Analytics to compare call behavior of fast-rampers vs. slow-rampers by Day 30.
- **People.ai / Clari:** Use AI activity capture to auto-populate CRM activity data (removing manual entry as a confounding variable for the Activity Score component of RHS). Clari's Forecast module can be used to track pipeline creation velocity by rep cohort.
- **Google Sheets / Looker / Tableau:** Build the Cohort Performance Curve visualization as a running line chart by hire quarter. Add a "Program Version" variable to isolate the impact of onboarding changes. Share as a live dashboard for VP Sales and Directors.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate the Day 21 / Day 45 / Day 60 escalation notifications — when a rep's RHS drops to Red, auto-create a Slack alert to the rep's manager and Director, and add a task to Salesforce for a required check-in within 48 hours.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** CRM data is inconsistent — reps log activities late or not at all, making Activity Score unreliable.
**Solution:** Implement People.ai or Clari Groove for automated activity capture from email and calendar. As a short-term fix, normalize Activity Score to *call recordings in Gong* (which are system-generated and not subject to manual logging bias) instead of CRM-logged activities.

**Problem:** Only 12–18 months of rep data available, making cohort analysis statistically weak.
**Solution:** Supplement historical cohort analysis with a 60-day sprint of intensive RHS monitoring on current reps hired in the last 6 months. Use their data to validate predictor correlations forward-looking rather than backward-looking. Even 15–20 reps with complete Day 30/60/90 data is sufficient to calibrate the model.

**Problem:** Manager resistance to coaching compliance tracking ("feels like surveillance").
**Solution:** Frame the RHS as a REP support tool, not a manager performance tool. Present it as "early warning system for reps who need help" — not "manager accountability tracker." Share individual rep RHS dashboards WITH the rep themselves so they can self-advocate for help. Tie manager coaching compliance to 1 of 10 criteria in manager OKRs (not a major weighting), focusing the conversation on rep outcomes rather than manager activity.
