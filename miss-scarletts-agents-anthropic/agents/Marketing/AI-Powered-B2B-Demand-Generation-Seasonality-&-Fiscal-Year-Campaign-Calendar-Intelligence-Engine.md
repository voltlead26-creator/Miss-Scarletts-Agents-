## Overview
Map your target accounts' fiscal buying cycles onto a full-year demand generation campaign calendar — then auto-generate channel activation sequences, spend pacing curves, and pipeline coverage targets for each quarter. Use this when planning annual marketing strategy, aligning campaigns to sales quota cadences, or recovering mid-year pipeline gaps caused by misaligned campaign timing.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The fiscal year calendar looks right in theory but campaign execution never matches the plan — teams are always reacting, never proacting.
**Solution:** The calendar is a tool, not a commitment device. Add a recurring quarterly planning meeting 8 weeks before each quarter start where this prompt's output becomes the literal agenda. Ask: "Are we on track to have the flagship campaign live by the Week 1 target?" If not, the bottleneck is usually content production lag. Use the AI agent automation calendar in Module 6 to auto-generate the content production task list 10 weeks before each quarter — not 4.

**Problem:** The end-of-quarter push creates a "lumpy" pipeline — massive spike at quarter end followed by a dead first two weeks of the next quarter.
**Solution:** This is the quarter-end hangover problem. Two fixes: (1) Run a parallel "Q+1 pipe-gen" track starting in Week 4 of the current quarter — even while accelerating current deals, maintain 30% of team capacity on new pipeline. (2) Set a rule: no "urgency" offers (discounts, fast-track onboarding) in the last 3 weeks — offers in this phase should add value (implementation support, risk removal), not manufacture urgency. Artificial urgency compresses one quarter's pipeline at the expense of the next.

**Problem:** The seasonal model breaks down because our buyers are in multiple industries with different fiscal years — we can't optimize for one calendar.
**Solution:** Segment your target account list by fiscal year type. Build parallel campaign tracks — one for calendar-year buyers (peak in Jan, Dec), one for April-fiscal-year buyers (peak in April-May, March), one for government/public sector (peak Sept-Oct). Each track uses the same campaign creative but different timing and urgency triggers. Your ABM platform's account-level fiscal year field (populate via enrichment with Clearbit or ZoomInfo) drives which track each account enters. The Dead Zone and End-of-Quarter modules are then run per-segment, not globally.
