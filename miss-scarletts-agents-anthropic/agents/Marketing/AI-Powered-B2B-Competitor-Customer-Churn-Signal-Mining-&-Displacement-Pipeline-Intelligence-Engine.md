**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** b2b, enterprise, competitive-intelligence, displacement, demand-gen, ABM, automation, signal-based-marketing

## Overview
Systematically mines competitor customer dissatisfaction signals across review sites, news events, LinkedIn, and technographic data to identify high-intent switchers and automatically trigger personalized competitive displacement campaigns. Deploy when a major competitor raises prices, suffers a major outage, gets acquired, or accumulates mounting negative reviews—converting their unhappy customers into your pipeline.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Displacement email sequences generate low open rates (<15%) despite strong signal data.
**Solution:** The subject line is likely too direct or uses competitor branding. Test subject lines that lead with the buyer's outcome ("Before you renew at the new rate") or curiosity ("What 47% of [Competitor] customers did last quarter") rather than explicit "switch from [Competitor]" framing. In most B2B tests, competitor-name subject lines underperform pain-first subject lines by 30–45% due to defensive psychological triggers. Also audit deliverability — ensure the sending domain is warmed and the sequence is excluded from accounts who have previously opted out.

**Problem:** High-scored accounts (80+) do not respond to SDR outreach despite confirmed signals.
**Solution:** Signal recency is almost certainly decaying — scores above 80 are only valid for the first 21 days after signal detection. After that, apply a 5-point weekly decay. SDRs should never reference a signal older than 30 days without first confirming it is still relevant via a fresh LinkedIn check. Also verify the SDR is opening with the specific signal ("I noticed [Company] mentioned the support response times on G2 in April") rather than a generic competitive opener. Specific signal references generate 3–5x higher reply rates than generic openers in signal-based outreach.

**Problem:** Displaced customers show higher churn rates at 6 months compared to other acquisition cohorts.
**Solution:** Customers who switched primarily due to competitor dissatisfaction (push motivation) rather than your product's distinctive value (pull motivation) have shallow loyalty anchors. Solve this by adding a mandatory "value discovery" call at Day 30 to co-define success metrics specific to their business, not just replicate what they had before. Ensure the migration team proactively rebuilds their top 3 reports within the first 10 days — delayed value realization is the primary predictor of displacement-cohort churn at 6 months. Track time-to-first-value separately for this cohort and set an internal SLA.
