## Overview
Designs and operationalizes a complete LinkedIn Thought Leadership Ads (TLA) program — from employee content selection and amplification scoring to buying committee targeting, creative governance, and pipeline attribution. Unlike standard company-sponsored LinkedIn ads, TLAs sponsor organic posts from individual employees and executives, combining authentic personal credibility with precise B2B audience targeting. Use this when you want to scale executive/employee content into a systematic paid pipeline engine without destroying the organic feel that drives high engagement.

B) CONTENT SELECTION SCORING RUBRIC
Score each employee post 0-10 on these dimensions before boosting:
1. Organic engagement signal (0-3 pts): Comment velocity in first 2 hours (3=10+ substantive comments, 2=5-9 comments, 1=1-4 comments, 0=zero engagement)
2. ICP relevance (0-2 pts): How directly does this address a top-3 pain point of the buying committee? (2=directly addresses core pain, 1=tangentially related, 0=not relevant)
3. Business outcome clarity (0-2 pts): Does the post create a "I need to solve this" moment? (2=clear urgency + solution direction, 1=raises awareness only, 0=no business impact)
4. Authenticity score (0-2 pts): Does this sound like the employee's real voice? (2=clearly personal and credible, 1=slightly polished, 0=sounds like brand copy)
5. CTA alignment (0-1 pt): Is there a natural next step for engaged readers? (1=yes, 0=no)

Minimum TLA threshold: 6/10. Posts scoring 8-10 get priority budget allocation.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Advocates won't approve TLA requests in time (LinkedIn requires advocate approval before the ad can run)**
Solution: Set a company policy that TLA approval requests must be reviewed within 24 business hours. Use the LinkedIn app notification as the trigger and create a shared Slack channel `#linkedin-tla-approvals` where the content team pings the advocate directly with a 1-click preview of exactly how the boosted post will appear. If approval isn't received in 24 hours, the TLA request expires — include this in advocate onboarding so they understand urgency.

**Problem: TLA engagement rates are high but no leads or pipeline being generated**
Solution: This usually means top-of-funnel awareness TLAs are working but mid/bottom-funnel conversion paths are missing. Audit whether you have: (1) Lead Gen Form TLAs for bottom-of-funnel content, (2) CTA in boosted posts pointing to a conversion page, (3) Retargeting campaigns re-engaging TLA engagers with higher-intent offers. Also check: are TLA-engaged contacts being followed up by SDRs? Often the pipeline is created by SDR outreach triggered by TLA engagement, not by TLA direct conversion.

**Problem: Advocates stop posting consistently after 4-6 weeks (advocacy program dies)**
Solution: This is the #1 TLA program killer. Root causes: (a) posting feels like extra work with no visible reward, (b) advocates don't know what to post about, (c) they don't see their own results. Fix: (a) Assign a content team member as a dedicated "advocate success manager" who sends each advocate a monthly personalized report showing their LinkedIn follower growth, TLA reach, and any pipeline influenced by their content; (b) Provide 5 pre-drafted post starters every Monday via Slack so posting is a 5-minute edit-and-publish task, not a blank-page creative challenge; (c) Share specific wins in all-hands: "Marcus's post about forecast accuracy reached 38,000 RevOps leaders and directly influenced 3 demos this month."
