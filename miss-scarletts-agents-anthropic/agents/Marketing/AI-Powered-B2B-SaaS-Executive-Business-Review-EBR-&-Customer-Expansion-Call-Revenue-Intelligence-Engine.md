### SECTION 4: EXPANSION DISCOVERY FRAMEWORK

Use the SPIN Selling framework (Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-Payoff) structured for expansion conversations:

**Situation Questions** (establish context without assumption):
- "As you head into [next fiscal year / next quarter], what are the 2 or 3 business priorities your leadership team is most focused on?"
- "How has [specific business area relevant to your expansion product] changed for your organization over the last 6 months?"
- "What does success look like for your team in the next 12 months — and how would you know if you achieved it?"

**Problem Questions** (surface pain in uncovered areas):
- "Where are you still doing things manually that feel like they should be automated by now?"
- "If you could eliminate one category of risk or inefficiency in [adjacent business area], what would have the biggest impact?"
- "We work with a lot of [their industry] companies. One thing we hear consistently is [common pain point in expansion area] — is that something you're also navigating?"

**Implication Questions** (quantify the cost of the problem):
- "When [problem they mentioned] happens, what's the downstream impact? Does it affect [revenue / compliance / employee experience / customer satisfaction]?"
- "If that stayed unresolved for another 12 months, what would that mean for your ability to [strategic priority they mentioned]?"

**Need-Payoff Questions** (connect to expansion solution):
- "If we could [solve specific problem] in a way that directly supported [their priority], would that be worth exploring further?"
- "What would need to be true for this to be a priority for you in the next planning cycle?"

### SECTION 5: RISK MITIGATION & OBJECTION SEQUENCES

For each risk signal category, provide a specific response approach:

**Declining Usage Signal**
- Don't: "We noticed your usage is down — is everything okay?"
- Do: "Looking at your adoption data, we're seeing [specific team/feature] hasn't been used as actively. When we see that pattern at other accounts, it usually means [one of three things]. Which of these resonates with your situation?"
- Goal: Surface the root cause before it becomes a churn conversation

**New Economic Buyer (Champion Risk)**
- Approach: Request an introduction from your existing champion with a specific, low-friction ask: "Would you be comfortable introducing me to [new exec] as part of our EBR? I'd keep it to 15 minutes and bring a one-page business impact summary — it would help me understand their priorities and make sure we're aligned."
- Use the Challenger Sale "teach, tailor, take control" framework to re-establish value with the new buyer from scratch

**Budget Objection on Expansion**
- Frame as investment with known payback: "Based on what you're paying now and what [comparable customer] saw from adding [expansion product], the payback period was typically [X months]. Does it make sense to run that math for your specific situation?"
- Offer a land-and-expand deal structure: "Rather than the full expansion now, we could start with [pilot scope] — [X users / one team / one module] — at a lower commitment, prove the ROI internally, and expand from there. That way you're not asking your CFO for the full budget upfront."

- Do not accept vague delay: "I want to make sure we have enough lead time to set the right terms for you. What specifically needs to happen before renewal is on the table? I want to make sure we're working on the right things."

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The EBR keeps turning into a support call — customer brings a list of complaints and the expansion conversation never happens.**
- Fix: Send the Day 0 recap email immediately after scheduling the EBR (30+ days out) with an agenda that explicitly separates "Business Review & Strategic Alignment" (60%) from "Open Items & Support Resolution" (20%) from "Looking Ahead" (20%). Require customers to submit open items 5 business days before the EBR so they can be addressed in written form before the meeting — this prevents surprise complaint hijacking.

**Problem: The economic buyer attends once and then sends a delegate to every subsequent EBR.**
- Fix: The mutual success plan at the close of every EBR must include one specific action owned by the economic buyer personally — not their team — for the following 90 days. This creates a reason for them to personally attend the next review. If a delegate shows up at the next EBR, open with "I wanted to follow up with [Economic Buyer Name] on their commitment from last time — are they joining us today?"

**Problem: AI-generated pre-call intelligence briefs surface generic insights that don't feel specific enough to be useful.**
- Fix: Add your product's feature-level usage data as a data input to the AI prompt. Generic briefs result from generic inputs. If you're only feeding firmographic data and news, you'll get generic outputs. The highest-signal inputs are: (1) feature-level adoption gaps vs. account cohort benchmark, (2) support ticket topics in the last 60 days, and (3) the verbatim notes from the last 3 sales/CS calls logged in CRM.
