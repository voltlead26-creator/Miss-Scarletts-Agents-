## Success Metrics

- **Registration rate:** Target 3-8% of email database; above 5% indicates strong topic/ICP fit
- **Attendance rate:** 35-50% of registrants attending live; below 30% indicates promotion timing or topic mismatch
- **Engagement score:** More than 60% of live attendees staying past 45 minutes (platform data)
- **Post-event MQL rate:** At least 10% of live attendees converting to MQL within 7 days
- **Pipeline influenced:** Webinar-sourced or webinar-influenced pipeline at least 5x event cost within 90 days
- **Replay completion rate:** More than 40% of replay viewers watching more than 50% of content
- **Email reply rate:** Post-event email sequence generating at least 3% reply rate (indicating sales conversations opening)

## Integration Tips

- **Zoom Webinars + HubSpot:** Use the native Zoom-HubSpot integration to sync registrant and attendee data automatically. Set workflow triggers based on attendance duration to fire lead score updates and enrollment into post-event sequences.
- **ON24 + Marketo:** ON24's Marketo integration pushes engagement scores (poll responses, CTA clicks, dwell time) directly into Marketo fields — use these as scoring inputs in your Program Member Status workflow.
- **Salesforce Campaigns:** Create a Salesforce Campaign for each webinar. Log registrant and attendee records as Campaign Members with statuses (Registered / Attended / No-Show / Replay). This enables proper multi-touch attribution in Salesforce reporting.
- **Demio + Zapier:** For teams using Demio without a native MAP integration, use Zapier to catch Demio webhooks (registered, attended, left) and update HubSpot or Salesforce contact records in real time.
- **Google Sheets pipeline tracker:** Export attendee and engagement data post-event into a shared Google Sheet for the SDR team. Include: Name, Company, Title, Score, Poll Answers, CTA Clicks, Recommended Action. Use Zapier or Make to auto-populate from your MAP.
- **Slack SDR alerts:** Configure a Zap or Make scenario to post to a #webinar-hot-leads Slack channel in real time when a contact clicks the in-event CTA — giving SDRs live visibility to act within minutes of the signal firing.
- **Notion event playbook:** Store this prompt's output in a Notion page as your webinar SOP. Link it to a Notion calendar database to track upcoming events and post-event pipeline contribution by month.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Registration numbers are strong but attendance rate is below 25%**
Solution: Low attendance typically means the registration-to-event window is too long (more than 3 weeks), reminder sequences are too few, or the event is scheduled at a low-engagement time. Move webinars to Tuesday-Thursday, 10-11am or 12-1pm in the audience's primary timezone. Add a T-1hr SMS reminder via Twilio if your MAP supports it. Send a "what to expect" email at T-7 days with a teaser insight to rebuild anticipation.

**Problem: Post-event email sequences generate opens but no replies or demo bookings**
Solution: The offer is likely too generic ("Book a Demo") for cold-to-warm contacts. Replace demo CTAs in emails 1-2 with lower-friction next steps: a free assessment, a personalized benchmark report, or a "strategy call" framing. Reserve the direct demo CTA for email 3 and for contacts who scored sales-ready. Personalize subject lines with specific session content (a poll answer or question they asked) to break through inbox noise.

**Problem: Lead scores show many MQLs but SDR team reports quality is low**
Solution: Re-calibrate scoring to weight intent signals more heavily over passive attendance. Remove points for simply registering (too low-intent). Add negative scoring for competitor job titles, student email domains, or companies outside ICP firmographic range. Add a 2-question post-event survey ("What's your biggest challenge with X?" and "Are you actively evaluating solutions?") — use survey responses as a hard gate for Sales-Ready status, overriding score alone.
