PHASE 1 — CARRIER BUYER PSYCHOLOGY MAP

For each of the 5 primary insurance technology buyer archetypes, produce a complete buying psychology profile:

**1. Chief Actuary (FCAS/MAAA)**
- Primary business mandate: Loss ratio accuracy, reserve adequacy, regulatory solvency compliance
- Career risk if wrong: "Actuarial opinion" is a legal liability — recommending technology that causes reserve error creates personal professional liability
- Validation requirement: Wants backtested loss model performance against 5 years of actual vs. expected data before committing to vendor endorsement
- Content that moves them: Peer-authored actuarial methodology papers, CAS journal citations, ILS/reinsurance market validation, independent model audit results
- What they need to hear before saying yes: "Other FCAS-credentialed actuaries at comparable carriers have validated this model's predictions against their historical triangle data"
- Deal-killer statement to avoid: "Our AI model learns and adapts automatically" — Chief Actuaries hear this as "unauditable black box that will fail your NAIC examination"

**2. Chief Underwriting Officer (CUO)**
- Primary business mandate: Combined ratio improvement, competitive premium accuracy, portfolio mix management
- Career risk if wrong: Writing bad business at wrong price = reserve development + quota underperformance + potentially board-level accountability
- Validation requirement: Loss ratio improvement data by line of business, STP rate improvement without adverse selection evidence, underwriter productivity metrics
- Content that moves them: Carrier-specific combined ratio benchmarking, underwriting STP rate data, adverse selection prevention case studies
- What they need to hear before saying yes: "A CUO at a comparable carrier with similar book composition has improved their expense ratio by [X] points in [Y] months without deteriorating loss ratio"
- Deal-killer: Overemphasis on automation eliminating underwriter jobs — CUOs know their team needs to survive the change management

**3. Insurance CIO/CITO**
- Primary business mandate: Core system modernization without business disruption, ACORD standards compliance, vendor/integration risk management
- Career risk if wrong: Failed core system replacement = career-ending event; budget waste of $5–50M+ is front-page insurance trade press
- Validation requirement: Migration methodology, data conversion track record, ACORD standards certification, cloud security & SOC 2 Type II, SLA guarantees for 99.9% uptime during claims catastrophe events
- Content that moves them: Technical architecture documentation, ACORD certification proof, security audit results, reference architecture with named carrier contacts
- What they need to hear before saying yes: "We have migrated 14 carriers from legacy [specific platform] with zero material business disruption — here are CIO reference contacts at comparable carriers"

**4. Chief Claims Officer**
- Primary business mandate: Claims cycle time reduction, LAE expense ratio, fraud detection accuracy, reserving speed
- Career risk if wrong: Delayed claims payments → regulatory penalty + insured dissatisfaction + litigation exposure
- Validation requirement: Straight-through processing (STP) rate data, average claims cycle time by line of business, fraud detection precision/recall metrics without increasing false positives
- Content that moves them: Claims benchmark studies, LAE reduction ROI by line, STP rate data from comparable carriers, fraud model performance vs. manual review

**5. CFO/Chief Risk Officer**
- Primary business mandate: Expense ratio reduction, investment return on technology spend, regulatory capital adequacy
- Validation requirement: IRR calculation on technology investment, expense ratio improvement timeline, regulatory capital impact analysis, 5-year TCO vs. legacy maintenance cost
- Content that moves them: CFO-targeted ROI calculator, expense ratio benchmark against peer carriers, board presentation template on technology-driven combined ratio improvement

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Chief Actuary keeps requesting "more backtesting data" indefinitely — evaluations extend to 18+ months.**
Solution: The Chief Actuary's delay is a signal that they need a professionally defensible process, not more data. Implement an "Actuarial Validation Protocol" — a structured 8-week third-party validation process conducted by an independent CAS-credentialed firm. The actuary can point to the independent validation in their opinion, removing personal liability. Include this protocol in all sales proposals as a standard offering. Carriers that agree to the protocol close 2.3x faster than those in unstructured evaluation.

**Problem: LinkedIn ads targeting CUO/Actuary titles generate low volume — insurance carrier employees are less active on LinkedIn than tech buyers.**
Solution: Insurance technology buyers are active on LinkedIn but respond to peer content, not vendor ads. Invest in Thought Leader Ads (amplifying content posted from your CUO/Chief Actuary advisors' personal profiles) rather than company page Sponsored Content. Actuaries engage with CAS-related content; CUOs engage with combined ratio benchmarks and underwriting trend analysis. Allocate 70% of LinkedIn budget to Thought Leader Ads from named industry advisors.

**Problem: Pipeline looks healthy but deals stall after POC when procurement gets involved — carrier procurement teams add 6–12 months of process.**
Solution: Insurance carrier procurement processes are specifically designed to prevent failed core system replacements (institutional trauma from past failures). Accelerate procurement by creating a "Carrier Procurement Readiness Kit" — pre-built responses to the 50 most common insurance technology procurement requirements (security questionnaire, business continuity proof, financial stability documentation, ACORD certification, carrier reference contacts, implementation methodology). Provide this kit during POC initiation, not when procurement asks for it. Carriers whose procurement team receives this kit proactively have 40% shorter negotiation cycles.
