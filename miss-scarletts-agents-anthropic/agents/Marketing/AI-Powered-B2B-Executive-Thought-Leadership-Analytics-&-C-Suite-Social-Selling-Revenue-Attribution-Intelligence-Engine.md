## Integration Tips
- **Shield Analytics**: Third-party LinkedIn analytics tool providing historical data, personal profile analytics, executive performance benchmarking, and content type breakdowns unavailable in native LinkedIn Analytics — connect via LinkedIn API; exports to Google Sheets, CSV, or Notion
- **Gong/Chorus**: Query call transcripts using executive name keywords to surface self-reported attribution; set up automated deal tags when executive name appears in transcript — exports to Salesforce/HubSpot as activity; creates a closed-loop attribution signal that CRM first/last-touch misses entirely
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator**: Enable "TeamLink" feature to surface which accounts in pipeline are connected to company executives; use "Persona" filters to identify ICP-matching followers of executive profiles; exports account lists to Salesforce/HubSpot via native integration
- **HubSpot/Salesforce**: Create a custom CRM property "Executive LinkedIn Touchpoint" that sales reps populate when a prospect cites executive content in discovery; track this property on opportunity records to build longitudinal attribution data for executive program ROI reporting
- **Sprout Social or Oktopost**: Schedule and publish executive content, track link clicks with UTM parameters, and generate multi-executive performance comparison reports — both integrate directly with Salesforce and HubSpot to close the attribution loop
- **LinkedIn Revenue Attribution**: Free LinkedIn Campaign Manager feature that maps company page AND organic post impressions to CRM accounts in pipeline — activate for all executives' company page activity and use as a proxy for organic executive reach influence on target accounts
- **Google Looker Studio (formerly Data Studio)**: Build a live executive thought leadership dashboard pulling from Shield Analytics API, HubSpot deal data, and Gong attribution tags — creates a single CMO view of the full executive program with auto-refreshing pipeline attribution numbers

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Executive LinkedIn activity shows high impressions but zero measurable pipeline or attributed demos**
Solution: This is almost always an attribution instrumentation gap, not a performance gap. Three immediate fixes: (1) Activate LinkedIn Revenue Attribution (free, 30-minute setup) — this maps organic impressions from executive posts to accounts in your CRM pipeline and will reveal hundreds of thousands in influenced pipeline within 30 days; (2) Set up Gong keyword tracking for each executive's name — prospects regularly cite executive content in calls but it goes unlogged; (3) Add UTM-tracked links in each executive's LinkedIn "Featured" section and include a CTA in the first comment of every post (not the post body — LinkedIn suppresses external links in post body algorithmically). Most companies running this analysis discover they've been generating $500K-$2M in quarterly pipeline influence with zero credit in the CRM.

**Problem: One executive dramatically outperforms others but leadership wants to invest equally across all executives**
Solution: Present the data as an optimization argument, not a ranking argument: "Executive A generates 8x the pipeline influence of Executive B per dollar invested — this doesn't mean we stop investing in Executive B, it means we identify what A is doing differently and apply it to B's strategy." Specifically, analyze whether the gap is (1) audience size — if so, invest in audience growth for B; (2) content format — if so, shift B's content mix toward A's winning formats; (3) topic fit — if so, align B's content themes more closely to ICP pain points. Rarely is the gap about the executive as a person — it's almost always about content strategy and audience composition.

**Problem: C-suite executives are reluctant to invest time in LinkedIn because they don't see personal benefit or don't want to post about business publicly**
Solution: Reframe the conversation using two data points specific to their role: (1) Show them the deal acceleration data — deals where a prospect follows the executive close faster; executives respond to personal impact on their quota/revenue targets more than brand metrics. (2) Show them the inbound warm pipeline data — a single viral executive post generates warm inbound from prospects who already know, like, and trust the company before a sales rep ever reaches out. Frame their LinkedIn as a pre-sales motion that makes every SDR's job easier, not a marketing vanity project. For resistant executives, start with a ghost-written LinkedIn newsletter (10 minutes/month to review and approve) that builds audience with minimum time investment before expanding to regular posting.
