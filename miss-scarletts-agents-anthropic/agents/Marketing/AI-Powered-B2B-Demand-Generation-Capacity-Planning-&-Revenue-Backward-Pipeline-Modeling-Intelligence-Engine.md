## Overview
Builds a complete, data-driven demand generation capacity plan by working backward from a revenue target — calculating exactly how many MQLs, SQLs, opportunities, and pipeline dollars are required at each funnel stage, what budget and headcount you need to hit them, and how to model conservative/base/aggressive scenarios for leadership. Use it when planning annual or quarterly demand gen budgets, requesting headcount, defending marketing investment to CFO, or redesigning your GTM motion after missing pipeline targets.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM**: Pull actual stage conversion rates directly from your CRM using pipeline reports segmented by lead source and quarter. Most CRMs have out-of-the-box funnel analytics — use 12-month trailing data to get statistically meaningful rates. Export the waterfall model as a Google Sheet and link live CRM data to it with a Zapier or native connector.
- **Marketo / HubSpot Marketing Automation**: Configure lifecycle stage tracking to fire timestamps at each funnel stage transition. This gives you the actual MQL-to-SQL conversion time (in addition to rate), which feeds your quarterly timing model in Module 6.
- **Google Sheets / Excel**: The waterfall and channel model tables in this prompt are structured for direct copy-paste into a spreadsheet. Build sensitivity sliders for win rate, ACV, and MQL-to-SQL rate so finance and leadership can stress-test assumptions in real time during planning reviews.
- **6sense / Demandbase**: Use account engagement scoring data to model intent-qualified MQL (iMQL) rate separately from form-fill MQL rate. Intent-qualified leads convert to SQL at 2-4x the rate of raw MQLs — including this split in your model dramatically improves forecast accuracy.
- **Looker / Tableau**: Build the 12 leading indicators from Module 7 as a live dashboard with red/yellow/green thresholds visible to the CMO and CRO weekly. Embed the pipeline coverage ratio as the hero metric on the executive marketing dashboard.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Document the assumptions behind every conversion rate in your capacity plan in a shared wiki. This creates accountability and prevents "the model was built on bad assumptions" post-mortems when targets are missed.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My conversion rates are wildly inconsistent quarter-over-quarter, making it impossible to build a reliable model.**
Solution: Segment your funnel by lead source (inbound content, paid, events, outbound) and ICP tier (ideal vs. acceptable vs. out-of-ICP). Lumped rates hide massive variance — an ICP-matched lead from a target account converts to SQL at 3-5x the rate of a mis-matched inbound lead. Build separate mini-models for your top 2-3 segments and weight them by volume. The inconsistency usually disappears when you stop blending apples and oranges.

**Problem: My model requires 4x MQL volume growth but my team is already at capacity — the plan is technically correct but unexecutable.**
Solution: Separate the capacity model (what's required) from the execution plan (what's achievable). Present both to leadership: "To hit $11M in marketing-sourced ARR requires 607 MQLs/month. Our current capacity is 152 MQLs/month. Closing this gap requires either $3.6M in additional program budget + 3 FTEs, OR reducing the marketing-sourced pipeline target from 65% to 35% of total with a corresponding SDR/outbound investment to compensate." Giving leadership a clear trade-off choice is more powerful than either refusing to build the plan or committing to an unrealistic one.

**Problem: Finance keeps challenging my CPL and CPO benchmarks as too low (optimistic).**
Solution: Pull 90-day trailing actuals from your CRM and ad platforms — not 30-day, which includes anomalies, and not 12-month, which may include campaigns you've already discontinued. Apply a 15% inefficiency buffer to paid channels (costs always drift up as you scale, due to audience saturation and ad fatigue). For organic/content channels, use a fully-loaded cost model (content production + distribution + team time) rather than just direct program spend. Showing finance you've built in a conservative buffer builds credibility and prevents mid-year re-forecasts.
