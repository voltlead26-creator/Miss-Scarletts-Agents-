## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The CHRO persona isn't engaging with outbound sequences despite following the playbook.
**Solution:** CHROs are typically reached via warm introductions, board connections, and peer referrals — not cold email. Shift strategy to: (1) target their VP HR or People Ops Director as a champion first, (2) activate LinkedIn thought leadership to build visibility before SDR outreach, (3) use executive roundtable invitations as the primary outbound hook rather than product-focused pitches. CHROs respond to peer learning opportunities, not vendor demos.

**Problem:** Generated messaging feels generic and doesn't differentiate from competitors.
**Solution:** Feed actual win/loss interview transcripts or Gong call recordings into the prompt's CONTEXT section before running Deliverable 2. The more specific your competitive differentiation inputs (including exact competitor weaknesses you've heard from prospects), the sharper the output. Also run the prompt specifically for your top 3 competitive displacement scenarios rather than general positioning.

**Problem:** The 90-day plan is too ambitious given current team size or budget.
**Solution:** Re-run Deliverable 3 with explicit constraints: "We have a team of 2 marketers and $25K/month budget. Reduce the plan to the highest-ROI 3 programs only, with specific weekly tasks." The engine will reprioritize to the most efficient pipeline programs for your resource level — typically a focus on outbound sequence activation, one strong lead magnet, and LinkedIn advertising to the target account list.
