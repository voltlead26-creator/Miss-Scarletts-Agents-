## Overview
Builds a fully autonomous AI system that mines competitor, customer, and prospect quarterly earnings calls, 10-K/10-Q filings, and annual reports within hours of release — extracting competitive signals, market validation quotes, customer pain points, and budget intelligence, then transforms them into ready-to-deploy marketing assets. Use this when you want to convert public financial disclosures into pipeline-generating content before your competitors' marketing teams finish their first cup of coffee.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The earnings transcript is too long to paste into a single prompt, causing the AI to miss key sections**
Solution: Use a hierarchical analysis approach. Pass the transcript in chunks with a priority order: (1) CEO prepared remarks first — this sets the strategic narrative; (2) CFO metrics section — revenue health signals; (3) Q&A — the most commercially revealing section, where analysts probe competitive pressure points. For each chunk, ask the AI to tag each intelligence extract with a confidence level (High = directly stated, Medium = implied, Low = inferred) so you know which signals to act on immediately vs. investigate further.

**Problem: The content produced sounds generic and could apply to any company, not specifically tied to your competitive position**
Solution: The prompt's "My company context" section is doing too little work. Add three specific elements: (1) Your three most recent competitive win reasons (from CRM win/loss data); (2) Your three most recent competitive loss reasons; (3) The exact language your ICP uses to describe their pain (from recent discovery calls or Gong recordings). The AI will then generate content that specifically connects earnings signals to your actual competitive differentiation, not a generic "we're better" narrative.

**Problem: Sales team isn't using the earnings intelligence — Slack alerts are being ignored**
Solution: The intelligence is failing the "so what for my deal" test. Restructure the Slack alert to lead with a specific account name: "If you have an open deal at [Account], read this" — pulled from your CRM by cross-referencing the monitored company's customer list against your open opportunities. Personalizing to their actual pipeline converts the alert from ambient information to urgent deal intelligence. Follow up in your weekly deal review meeting by asking one AE to share how they used the earnings intelligence in a recent call — social proof within the team builds adoption faster than top-down mandates.
