## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce:** Create a custom "Campaign" with type "Roadshow" and add sub-campaigns per city stop. Use Campaign Influence (Pardot or native) to attribute pipeline. Add a custom field "Roadshow Touched" to Contact and Account objects. Build a Salesforce report comparing deal velocity for roadshow-touched vs. untouched accounts in same segment.
- **HubSpot:** Use "Events" in Marketing Hub (Enterprise) to manage registrations. Tag contacts with "Roadshow 2026 - [City]" list. Build a workflow that auto-enrolls attendees in post-event sequence and notifies AE via Slack/email when attendee opens Day 1 follow-up email.
- **Marketo:** Build a program template with tokens for city, date, venue. Clone per stop. Use Interesting Moments to log "Attended [City] Roadshow" on lead activity timeline visible to AEs in Salesforce.
- **6sense / Bombora:** Run intent surge filter for your target account list 2 weeks before each city stop — if an account shows 3+ intent topics, escalate to Tier 1 and have AE make personal phone call to follow up on invite. Post-event, monitor if attendee accounts' intent scores increase.
- **Outreach / Salesloft:** Build a dedicated post-event sequence cadence for each attendee status (attended / no-show / not-invited). Use dynamic fields to reference the city, date, and a quote from their conversation if your AE captured it in notes.
- **Calendly:** Embed a "Book a follow-up" Calendly link in Day 3 post-event email with pre-populated 30-minute "Revenue Intelligence Audit" context. Track conversion from email to booked meeting.
- **Notion / Airtable:** Build a tour operations tracker: one row per city stop, columns for venue, catering, speaker, budget, registration count, attendance count, pipeline created. Share with sales ops for weekly revenue review updates.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low registration rates from Tier 1 accounts (< 30% accepting invites)**
Solution: The invite is likely too broad or not personalized enough at the executive level. Switch from marketing-sent invites to AE-or CEO-sent for all Tier 1 accounts. Remove any language that sounds like a product pitch. Add a specific peer reference: "I'm also inviting [peer company in same vertical] — thought it would be a valuable conversation for both of you." Create urgency through genuine scarcity, not false countdown timers.

**Problem: High registration, low attendance (> 30% no-show rate)**
Solution: Executive no-shows are usually caused by last-minute calendar conflicts and insufficient personal relationship with the host. Fix: (1) AE calls or texts registered attendees 48 hours before to confirm and share a "teaser insight" that makes them regret missing it. (2) Make the event non-transferable to prevent low-value substitutions. (3) Consider shifting from morning breakfast (first to get cancelled) to evening dinner (harder to cancel). (4) Send a "we're saving your seat" note from the AE 24 hours before.

**Problem: High attendance but low pipeline conversion (< 15% attendee-to-opportunity rate within 30 days)**
Solution: The event experience or follow-up is failing to create urgency. Audit three things: (1) Was the event too vendor-focused? Peer exchange should dominate 60%+ of agenda time. (2) Did AEs capture specific pain points per attendee during the event? If the Day 3 follow-up is generic, it won't move. (3) Is the next step offer too high-commitment? A free "Revenue Intelligence Benchmark Report" for their specific company context converts better than "schedule a demo." Use a value-first offer to lower the activation barrier.
