## Overview
Systematically analyzes closed-won and closed-lost deals to surface the exact patterns, competitive dynamics, and buyer objections driving your win rate — then generates prioritized playbooks to replicate wins and eliminate loss patterns. Use this when win rate drops unexpectedly, a competitor is taking deals, or you need to tell leadership exactly why Q2 underperformed.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't have enough deal data — only 5-8 closed deals per month."**
Solution: Expand the analysis window to 6 months instead of 90 days. Flag patterns as "directional hypotheses" rather than statistically significant findings. Supplement closed deal data with 3-5 post-loss customer interviews (even email-based) — one quote from a lost prospect is worth 10 deals of CRM notes.

**Problem: "Our loss reasons in CRM are all 'price' or 'timing' — the data is useless."**
Solution: Don't use stated CRM loss reasons as inputs. Instead, feed in the raw deal notes, Gong summaries, and email thread context. Ask the prompt to infer root causes from behavioral signals (e.g., "economic buyer never engaged," "proposal sent without discovery call," "demo request came from junior persona"). CRM hygiene is a downstream problem — work around it by using richer unstructured data sources.

**Problem: "The competitive analysis output is generic — it says 'improve messaging' without specifics."**
Solution: Add verbatim prospect quotes from post-loss emails or call transcripts to the Advanced Version inputs. The more specific your input (e.g., "Prospect said: 'Clari showed us a 12-week implementation timeline vs. your 6-month estimate'"), the more specific the competitive counter-play output. Also paste your current battlecard for the competitor so the engine can identify its gaps rather than regenerating from scratch.
