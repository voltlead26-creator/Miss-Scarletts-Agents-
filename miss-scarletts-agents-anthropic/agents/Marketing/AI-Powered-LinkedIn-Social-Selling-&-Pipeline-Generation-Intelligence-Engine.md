## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Connection acceptance rate below 25%**
*Solution:* Your request templates are too generic or ICP targeting is off. Audit the last 20 sent requests — are you referencing something specific to each person? Run the "conference test": would this message make sense as something you'd say to a stranger you met at an industry event? If not, rewrite. Also audit your own profile — if your headline reads "Sales Rep at [Company]," prospects won't accept. Update to outcome-focused positioning first.

**Problem: Sequence reply rate below 8% despite high acceptance rate**
*Solution:* You're pitching too early or your "value" touches aren't actually valuable to this ICP. Audit Touch 2 and 3 — are you sharing something genuinely useful to a VP of Sales, or something that feels like a sales asset in disguise? Move Touch 5 (problem agitation + social proof) later. Add a curiosity-gap question in Touch 3 that only a practitioner in their role would find interesting. Test replacing one touch with a loom video message — video DMs get 3–5x higher reply rates.

**Problem: LinkedIn restricting your account for "unusual activity"**
*Solution:* You're sending too many connection requests too fast. LinkedIn's limit is approximately 100–150/week for personal accounts. Warm up new automation tools slowly: 20/day week 1, 30/day week 2, 40/day week 3. Never use automation for personalized DM content — only for initial connection requests. If restricted, take a 48–72 hour break, then resume at half volume. Long-term, invest in a Sales Navigator Team license which has higher activity thresholds and official CRM integration.
