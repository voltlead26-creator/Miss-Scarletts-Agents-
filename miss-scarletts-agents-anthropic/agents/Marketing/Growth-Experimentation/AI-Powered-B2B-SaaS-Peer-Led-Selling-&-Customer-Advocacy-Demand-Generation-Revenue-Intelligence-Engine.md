## Overview

This prompt enables B2B SaaS demand generation and customer marketing teams to design and operate a full-funnel peer-led selling program — transforming satisfied customers into an autonomous, AI-orchestrated demand generation channel. Use it when your CAC is rising on paid channels, when buyers tell you "I trusted a peer's recommendation more than your sales team," or when you want to build a compounding referral flywheel that generates influenced pipeline without proportional spend increase.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Advocates accept requests but participation drops after the first activity.
**Solution:** Audit your ask-to-give ratio. Advocates who wait 45+ days between enrollment and their first meaningful benefit (roadmap access, recognition, community value) show 60% lower 90-day retention. Prioritize delivering a high-value benefit within 14 days of tier assignment — don't start with an ask. Also run a quarterly "Advocate Experience NPS" (separate from product NPS) to catch burnout early. Target Advocate Experience NPS of 70+; if below 50, pause all activation and conduct 1:1 feedback calls with churned advocates before re-engaging.

**Problem:** Sales team bypasses the program and still sends ad hoc CSM reference requests with no tracking.
**Solution:** This is a process adoption problem, not a product problem. Create a formal "Reference Request Policy" (co-signed by VP Sales and CMO) requiring all reference requests to be submitted via Salesforce form or ReferenceEdge. Track the delta between structured requests and ad hoc CSM emails quarterly. Present win rate data at sales QBR: deals using the structured program close 18% more often and 20% faster — the numbers convert skeptics. Consider a Sales SPIFF for reps who submit requests through the system for the first 90 days.

**Problem:** RevOps and Finance dispute how to define "peer-influenced pipeline" and won't accept the numbers.
**Solution:** Align before launch. Create a written Attribution Policy Doc in week 1, defining: (1) what constitutes an advocacy touchpoint, (2) minimum time window for influence credit (advocacy touchpoint must occur ≥14 days before close date to qualify), (3) how credit is split in multi-touchpoint deals, (4) who has authority to adjust attribution. Get VP of Sales, CMO, and CFO to co-sign. Operationalize in CRM immediately, run one quarter in parallel with existing attribution before presenting to leadership. Starting with clean, agreed-upon definitions prevents the "whose number is it?" conflict that kills program credibility.
