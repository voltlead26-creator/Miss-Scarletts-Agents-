## Integration Tips
- **Gainsight**: Create a custom CTA type called "Referral Program — Invite" triggered when CARE score exceeds 75. Assign to CSM with pre-populated invite email template. Track advocate program membership as a custom field on the Company object. Use Gainsight's "Timeline" entries to log referral submissions and status updates.
- **Salesforce**: Add a "Referral Source" picklist to the Lead and Opportunity objects. Create a custom object "Advocate" linked to the Account object to track enrollment status, referrals submitted, referrals closed, and total reward value earned. Build a dashboard with the 7 KPIs from Component 4 as report tiles.
- **HubSpot**: Use the Deals pipeline with a dedicated "Referral" pipeline source property. Create a workflow that automatically enrolls referred contacts into a "Referral Fast Track" sequence with compressed sales cycle SLAs. Build a custom report comparing close rate and velocity by lead source (referral vs. inbound vs. outbound).
- **PartnerStack / Rewardful / Friendbuy**: For programs requiring formal referral link tracking, use a dedicated referral platform. PartnerStack works well for B2B with CRM integration; Rewardful is simpler for SaaS with Stripe integration. Connect referral platform data to your CRM via native integration or Zapier.
- **Zapier / Make**: Build a trigger that fires when a Gainsight milestone event (vendor onboarding completed, renewal closed, NPS response of 9-10) hits a webhook. Route to a Google Sheet log and trigger a personalized Outreach/Salesloft task for the CSM with the pre-written "referral moment" message template.
- **Notion / Google Sheets**: Maintain your advocate roster in a shared Notion database with columns for CARE score, program tier, enrollment date, referrals submitted, referrals closed, and rewards earned. Update monthly during CS team reviews.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Advocates enroll but submit zero referrals — program has dead members.**
The most common cause is a friction-heavy referral submission process or vague instructions on who to refer. Audit your submission flow: if it takes more than 2 clicks or requires the advocate to write anything from scratch, rebuild it. Send a "referral story" email at Day 30 showing a real peer-to-peer introduction that worked — advocates need social proof too. Then at Day 60, give them a specific, named ask: "Is there anyone at [Company Type] you've spoken with recently who is dealing with [Pain Point]?" Concrete beats open-ended every time.

**Problem: Referred prospects are not converting to qualified pipeline despite strong referral volume.**
Check whether the referral incentive is attracting the wrong advocate behavior — if you're paying for introductions, some advocates will introduce everyone they know regardless of fit. Tighten eligibility: a "qualified referral" should require the referred prospect to meet 2-3 ICP criteria (company size, industry, job title) before the incentive triggers. Add a brief qualification call with the referred prospect before logging as qualified. Also review whether advocates understand your ICP clearly — send a one-page "who we're best for" card with every enrollment welcome email.

**Problem: Champions are enthusiastic but keep forgetting to make introductions — no follow-through.**
The problem is not motivation, it is memory and timing. Build referral moments into your CS cadence: add "referral opportunity" as a standing agenda item in every QBR. Send advocates a quarterly "peer list" — 3-5 companies in their network that match your ICP (sourced from LinkedIn mutual connections via Sales Navigator or Clay) — and ask if they know anyone there. Specific names outperform open invitations by 4-5x. Also shorten the referral window: a program with no urgency creates indefinite delay. Add a quarterly "double reward" period to create time-bound activation.
