## Overview
Designs and executes a full-stack, AI-powered demand generation program for B2B SaaS companies entering or scaling within a specific industry vertical (Healthcare, FinServ, Manufacturing, Legal, etc.). Use this when you need pipeline from a specific vertical that requires distinct messaging, channels, proof points, and buyer journeys—not when a horizontal, one-size-fits-all demand gen motion is sufficient.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Vertical program generates high MQL volume but low SQL conversion (< 20%)**
Solution: This usually signals a targeting precision problem — your vertical audience is too broad. Audit the last 20 MQLs: if company size, sub-segment, or job title is misaligned with your ICP, tighten LinkedIn and programmatic targeting parameters. Add a mandatory qualification question to all vertical content downloads ("How many beds does your facility have?" or "What is your organization's IT headcount?"). Review whether your vertical messaging is attracting the wrong sub-segment — too many small accounts? Refine to midmarket-only messaging and remove "any size" language.

**Problem: Vertical pipeline stalls and deals die in procurement/legal phase without closing**
Solution: This is a vertical-specific bloat issue at the decision stage, not an awareness or lead gen problem. Build and deploy "procurement acceleration" content: vendor security questionnaire pre-fill (common in healthcare, finance, government), standard BAA/DPA templates, reference customer list by sub-segment size, and implementation case studies showing similar-sized deployment timelines and costs. Brief your SDR/AE team on vertical procurement cycles and set appropriate sales cycle benchmarks so deals aren't prematurely flagged as lost.

**Problem: Content investment in the vertical isn't generating organic search traffic or inbound**
Solution: Vertical content requires patience (6-12 months for SEO) but 30 days should show syndication traction. If no traction after 8 weeks: (1) Check whether you're targeting highly competitive head terms—shift to long-tail intent-heavy keywords ("HIPAA-compliant project management software for hospitals" outperforms "project management software"); (2) Ensure content is being submitted to vertical-specific publications and associations for syndication, not just sitting on your blog; (3) Invest in vertical-specific backlink building via expert contributor placements in HIMSS Insights, Becker's Hospital Review, HealthTech Magazine, etc. These syndication and backlinks compound SEO authority faster than waiting for Google to discover your content organically.
