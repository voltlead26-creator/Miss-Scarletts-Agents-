## Troubleshooting

**Problem: You hit 200 upvotes by 9 AM but stall through the afternoon, losing your top-5 ranking**
Solution: PH's algorithm heavily weights comment velocity in addition to upvote velocity in the afternoon window. Deploy your "Day supporters" (Tier 2 contacts) to comment — not just upvote — between 12–3 PM PST. A single substantive comment generates a notification to every prior commenter in the thread, re-activating dormant supporters. Simultaneously, post a "mid-launch update" on LinkedIn and Twitter: "Halfway through our Product Hunt launch day — [X] upvotes, and some fascinating feedback from the community. Here's what we've learned: [1–2 genuine insights from comments]." This drives a new wave of click-throughs.

**Problem: High upvotes but low conversion rate from PH traffic to signups**
Solution: The PH audience is browsers-first. If conversion is below 4%, the landing page has a friction or relevance problem. Diagnose in order: (1) Does the page say "Welcome, Product Hunt community" or acknowledge the source? If not, visitors don't feel "seen" and bounce immediately. (2) Is the primary CTA a free trial or instant product access? Anything requiring a sales call as the first step will kill PH conversion — this audience self-selects for self-serve first. (3) Is pricing visible? PH visitors want to know if this is affordable before they explore. Show a clear pricing tier or "pricing from $X/month" on the landing page. Add a "Founding Member Deal" timer countdown with the launch special.

**Problem: You get great launch day traction but email signups don't convert to paid within 30 days**
Solution: PH signups have fundamentally different buyer intent than inbound or paid leads — they're early adopters who want to explore, not buyers with immediate budget approval. Your email sequence needs to reflect this: (1) Extend the "exploration" phase — give 30-day free access instead of 14-day (PLG products). (2) Focus Day 7–14 emails on "have you gotten value yet?" rather than "upgrade now." (3) Deploy a human outreach (not automated) at Day 10 for any signup that has logged in 3+ times — these are engaged users who need a conversion conversation, not another drip email. Frame it: "I noticed you've been using [Product] regularly — what would need to be true for this to become a permanent part of your workflow?"
