STAKEHOLDER 1: ECONOMIC BUYER
Typical title: CFO, COO, CEO, CRO, divisional VP with P&L ownership
Core motivations: Revenue impact, cost reduction, competitive advantage, risk mitigation, board narrative
Messaging framework: Use ROI framing, peer benchmarks, strategic timing, board-level language
Key insight to weave in: They approved this budget category to solve a business problem — remind them of the cost of inaction

STAKEHOLDER 2: CHAMPION (Internal Sponsor)
Typical title: Director or VP-level owner of the problem you solve
Core motivations: Internal credibility, being seen as innovative, career advancement, making their team more effective
Messaging framework: Equip them to sell internally. Give them the narrative, the proof, and the talking points they need to get executive buy-in
Key insight: Champions lose deals when they can't answer "why now?" and "why you specifically?" — answer both for them

STAKEHOLDER 3: TECHNICAL EVALUATOR
Typical title: VP/Director of IT, Security Lead, Solutions Architect, CTO of a division
Core motivations: Implementation risk, integration complexity, security/compliance, support quality, avoiding surprises
Messaging framework: Specificity wins. Reference their stack, use their terminology, lead with security certs and architecture details
Key insight: Technical evaluators kill deals by raising late-stage blockers — engage them early and arm them with technical proof

STAKEHOLDER 4: END USER / PRACTITIONER
Typical title: Manager, individual contributor, the person who will use the product daily
Core motivations: Reducing manual work, avoiding frustration, making their job easier, not losing their job to automation
Messaging framework: Show the "day in the life" transformation. Use before/after comparisons. Validate that their current pain is real
Key insight: Users are silent deal-killers if they fear the change. Make them advocates, not blockers

STAKEHOLDER 5: PROCUREMENT
Typical title: VP of Procurement, Vendor Manager, Sourcing Manager
Core motivations: Vendor risk reduction, price benchmarking, process compliance, avoiding maverick spend
Messaging framework: Lead with vendor legitimacy, reference customers they'll recognize, make their job easier by providing documentation preemptively
Key insight: Procurement delays deals by 30-60 days — engage them before they engage you

STAKEHOLDER 6: LEGAL / COMPLIANCE
Typical title: General Counsel, VP of Legal, Data Protection Officer, CISO (if compliance-heavy)
Core motivations: Data privacy (GDPR, CCPA, SOC2), liability exposure, contractual clarity, regulatory compliance
Messaging framework: Proactive documentation. Send your security posture, DPA, subprocessors list, and SLA terms before they ask
Key insight: Legal's silence is not approval — proactively arm them with answers to avoid last-minute contract negotiations

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Map each stakeholder email to a contact record tagged by role. Use deal stage as a trigger to auto-enqueue the right email sequence via Salesloft, Outreach, or Apollo. Set task reminders when a stakeholder hasn't opened in 7 days.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Cross-reference buying committee members. Pull recent activity (posts, job changes, comments) to personalize the opening hook in real time before sending.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Feed call transcripts into the Advanced prompt's "Deal Context" section. Extract exact language prospects use and mirror it in the email body for dramatically higher resonance.
- **Clay:** Use Clay to auto-enrich stakeholder profiles (job history, LinkedIn activity, company news, tech stack) and inject them as personalization variables into the prompt before generating email content.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build a buying committee tracker that logs which stakeholder received which email, open/reply status, and deal stage progression. Link each email to the relevant stakeholder record.
- **Zapier / Make:** Trigger the buying committee prompt automatically when a deal enters "Technical Evaluation" stage in CRM — generate drafts and queue them in Outreach or Salesloft for rep review.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Emails feel generic even with the advanced prompt — stakeholders aren't responding.
**Solution:** The most common failure is skipping the "Account Profile" field or filling it with vague information. Invest 10 minutes researching the target account before running the prompt: pull their last press release, last funding announcement, their CEO's LinkedIn posts, and any recent G2 reviews. Paste specific details into the context field. Specificity is the single biggest driver of reply rates.

**Problem:** The champion loves the emails but the economic buyer never responds.
**Solution:** Economic buyers get 200+ emails a day. If your subject line doesn't lead with a business outcome tied to something they care about THIS quarter (board meeting, funding round, audit, competitive threat), it gets ignored. Replace abstract value language ("streamline your process") with specific outcome language ("cut your Q2 close cycle before the board meeting"). Reference a named peer company they respect.

**Problem:** Procurement and Legal are blocking the deal after all other stakeholders are aligned.
**Solution:** The prompt includes a "Procurement" and "Legal" stakeholder email for exactly this reason — but the key is to send these BEFORE the champion asks you to. Run the prompt at the start of evaluation, not the end. Send procurement your security documentation and vendor risk questionnaire proactively. Send legal your DPA and standard contract redlines before they ask. Being one step ahead of their process is what turns 60-day legal reviews into 2-week sign-offs.
