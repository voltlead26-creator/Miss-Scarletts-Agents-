## Success Metrics

- **Open rate:** Launch day email should hit 35–55% for active customer segments (industry average: 25–30%)
- **Click-through rate:** 8–15% for active customer emails; 3–6% for prospect emails
- **Feature activation rate:** Track % of email recipients who activate the feature within 7 days — target 20–30% for active customers
- **Upgrade conversions (if paid add-on):** Track upgrade rate within 14-day launch window; benchmark 2–5% of eligible users
- **Reply rate on Segment 3 (dormant):** 2–4% reply rate signals copy resonance before CTA click
- **PS line click attribution:** Tag PS CTAs separately — should drive 10–25% of total email clicks
- **Unsubscribe rate:** Flag if >0.3% per email — signals messaging misalignment with segment

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a Smart List for each segment using contact properties (last login date, plan tier, lifecycle stage). Use Workflows to enroll contacts into the appropriate sequence on launch day. Use "Goal" enrollment to suppress contacts who convert early. Tag all emails with a campaign UTM (`utm_campaign=launch-[feature-name]&utm_content=[segment]`) for revenue attribution in HubSpot reporting.
- **Marketo:** Build a Program per launch. Use Smart Campaigns with filters on "Last Interesting Moment" to separate active from dormant users. Use "Email Suspended" suppression for high-NPS customers who are already in a relationship nurture track.
- **Customer.io:** Use Broadcast + API-triggered campaigns. Pipe product usage data (last login, plan tier, feature flags) from your data warehouse to Customer.io using Segments or a direct API call. Trigger Segment 3 (dormant) separately via a manual broadcast vs. automated workflows.
- **Salesforce Marketing Cloud:** Create a Journey with Decision Splits based on engagement (opened / clicked / converted). Use Contact Builder to pull product usage attributes from your data model.
- **Notion (for content operations):** Build a Launch Email Brief template that maps: launch tier → segments → email count → approval owners → send dates. Attach final copy to the launch project page before handoff to marketing ops.
- **Zapier / Make.com:** Automate post-send reporting by piping email metrics (opens, clicks, conversions) into a Google Sheet dashboard. Set up Slack alerts for anomalies: >0.5% unsubscribe on any single email, or <2% open rate on launch day for active customer segments.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Open rates are below 25% for active customer segments**
Solution: Subject line is the issue, not the list. Run a 3-way A/B test (curiosity gap / direct benefit / social proof) on your next send to the same segment. Also check send time — Tuesday–Thursday at 9–10am local time typically outperforms Monday or Friday. Confirm that the "From" name uses a real person (e.g., "Sarah from Callibri") rather than a company brand name; personal senders generate 20–30% higher open rates for customer communications.

**Problem: High opens but low CTR (clicks under 3% for customers)**
Solution: The email body is not connecting feature to outcome quickly enough. Audit the first two sentences — if they don't state a specific outcome the reader cares about within 25 words, rewrite the opener. Also check CTA copy: "Learn more" and "See what's new" are graveyard CTAs. Replace with action-outcome phrasing: "See my time savings", "Activate in Salesforce", "Watch the 90-second walkthrough."

**Problem: Segment 3 (dormant customers) not responding**
Solution: Dormant customers have immunized themselves to product emails. Do NOT increase email frequency — it will accelerate unsubscribes. Instead: (1) Switch the FROM address to the CSM or account owner, not a marketing alias. (2) Use a plain-text email format — no images, no HTML template — it reads like a personal note. (3) Change the subject line family entirely to question format: "Is [Company] still worth it for you?" or "What would bring you back?" Direct honesty outperforms feature promotion for win-back.
