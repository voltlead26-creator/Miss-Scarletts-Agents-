## Overview
Generates a complete, behavior-triggered 30-day onboarding email sequence for new B2B SaaS users — from the welcome moment through first value milestone — designed to maximize activation rates, time-to-value, and trial-to-paid conversion using engagement signals, product usage data, and persona-specific messaging.

Create a 10-email new user onboarding sequence that:
1. Delivers a personalized welcome that sets clear expectations
2. Guides the user to their first "aha moment" within 72 hours
3. Teaches one high-value feature per week with a specific use case
4. Re-engages users who go inactive after Day 5
5. Converts trial users to paid in the final 7 days of trial

For each email provide:
- Subject line with an A/B variant
- Preview text (under 90 characters)
- Full email body (under 200 words, conversational, value-first)
- Trigger condition (time-based or behavior-based)
- Primary CTA with a single in-product action
- Personalization variables to pull from your product database

Write as a helpful guide, not a salesperson. Every email should teach or enable, never just pitch.

## Integration Tips

**Customer.io:**
- Build each email as a Campaign within a single onboarding Workflow
- Use Customer.io's event-triggered branching to route users to EMAIL 3 (celebration) or EMAIL 2 (quick win nudge) based on aha moment completion event
- Set up `people.attributes.days_since_signup` as a computed attribute for time-based triggers
- Use Customer.io's Liquid templating to inject product telemetry into EMAIL 9 (ROI Preview): `{{ customer.reports_created }}`, `{{ customer.sessions_this_month }}`

**Intercom:**
- Use Intercom Series to build the full sequence as a connected campaign
- Leverage Intercom's "Event triggers" for behavior-based branching (e.g., trigger Email 3 when `aha_moment_completed = true`)
- Use Intercom's "Snooze" feature so Email 8 (check-in) surfaces as an in-app message + email simultaneously
- Connect Intercom's product tours to Email 4 (Feature Spotlight) — link directly to a Product Tour step from the email CTA

**HubSpot:**
- Build as a HubSpot Workflow triggered on contact property `Trial Start Date`
- Use HubSpot's "Custom behavioral events" to branch on aha moment completion
- Sync product telemetry via HubSpot's custom object API to populate Email 9 personalization tokens
- Use HubSpot's A/B testing within Workflows (available on Marketing Hub Professional+) to test subject line variants at scale

**Klaviyo (for PLG / e-commerce-adjacent SaaS):**
- Use Klaviyo Flows with metric-triggered splits for behavioral branching
- Build suppression lists for each email based on completion events (suppresses Email 2 when aha moment fires)
- Use Klaviyo's predictive analytics to flag high-conversion-probability users and route them to a faster upgrade path in Emails 10–12

**Amplitude / Mixpanel → Email Platform:**
- Use Amplitude's Cohort Sync or Mixpanel's Messaging feature to push behavioral cohorts (e.g., "Day 7 active, no integration connected") directly to HubSpot, Customer.io, or Braze for real-time email triggering
- Set up conversion funnels in Amplitude to measure email → in-product action → retention correlation

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Aha moment completion rate stays below 20% despite Email 2 delivery**
Fix: The activation blocker is likely upstream of the email — users aren't logging in to see it. Check Email 2's open rate: if it's above 50%, the email is delivering but users aren't acting, meaning your product path to the aha moment has friction. Run a session recording audit (Hotjar, FullStory) on the path from Email 2's CTA click to aha moment completion. If open rate is below 30%, fix deliverability: move Email 2 to plain text, change the sender to a real name, and A/B test a subject line that calls out the specific action rather than generic "getting started."

**Issue: Trial-to-paid conversion from Email 10–12 is under 10%**
Fix: Email timing may be misaligned with trial engagement. Run a cohort analysis: compare conversion rates for users who received Email 10 while "active" (logged in in the last 3 days) vs. "dormant." If dormant-user conversion is dragging the average, build a re-engagement path at Day 18 before the conversion sequence — win them back first, then convert. Also audit Email 10's plan recommendation: if users feel mis-matched to the suggested plan, they stall. Add a "Not sure which plan?" CTA that routes to a 1-question survey or live chat.

**Issue: Team invite rate stays below 10% after Email 7**
Fix: The invite prompt may feel like a chore rather than a benefit. Reframe Email 7 around a specific collaborative outcome, not the feature itself. Test a version that shows a screenshot of what a shared Loopfeed dashboard looks like in action — concrete social evidence is more persuasive than abstract benefit statements. If your product supports it, send Email 7 with a pre-populated invite link that defaults to the teammate's likely role (e.g., "Invite your content lead" rather than "Invite a teammate").
