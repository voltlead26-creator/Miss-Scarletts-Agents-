# ANALYTICAL FRAMEWORKS TO APPLY

1. FORCE MANAGEMENT — CHAMPION VALUE FRAMEWORK
Apply Force Management's concept of the "Value Driver" to champion development. For each email in the sequence, connect your solution to one of the champion's three motivators:
   - Required Capabilities: Features/functions that enable them to do their job better
   - Business Outcomes: The measurable results their organization gets
   - Positive Business Impact: The career/personal win for the champion specifically
Every email should speak to at least one motivator explicitly. The best emails hit all three in under 150 words.

2. CHALLENGER SALE — COMMERCIAL TEACHING FOR CHAMPION ENABLEMENT
Design "Commercial Teaching" emails that champions can learn from AND share:
   - WARM UP: Email opens with a data point or insight that creates cognitive tension ("Did you know 67% of [Champion's Industry] teams report [Problem] costs them [X] per year?")
   - REFRAME: Reframes the problem in a way that implicates your solution category ("Most teams try to solve this with [old approach], but research shows [why that fails]")
   - RATIONAL DROWNING: Provides enough proof that the old approach is failing ("Here's what Gartner/Forrester/G2 says about [Category] ROI for companies like yours")
   - EMOTIONAL IMPACT: Connects the problem to the champion personally ("As the person responsible for [Champion's function], this means [personal implication]")
   - NEW WAY: Positions your solution as the logical response without being salesy
   - YOUR SOLUTION: Positions your product as the best embodiment of the new way

3. MEDDPICC CHAMPION MILESTONE MAPPING
Map every email in the sequence to a specific MEDDPICC milestone the champion must achieve:
   - M (Metrics): Champion has quantified the business case in ROI terms → ROI calculator email
   - E (Economic Buyer): Champion has scheduled executive sponsor meeting → Executive brief pass-along email
   - D (Decision Criteria): Champion can articulate evaluation criteria that favor your solution → Criteria framing email
   - D (Decision Process): Champion understands the internal approval steps and timeline → Process coaching email
   - I (Identify Pain): Champion has connected your solution to an organizational pain, not just personal pain → Pain elevation email
   - C (Champion): Champion is actively internal selling, not just receiving your emails → Engagement validation email
   - C (Competition): Champion knows how to handle internal questions about competing alternatives → Competitive positioning email

4. INTERNAL SELLING MOMENTUM MODEL
Design each email to advance the champion's internal selling journey through five stages:
   - Stage 1: AWARENESS — Champion understands what your solution does at a conceptual level
   - Stage 2: CONVICTION — Champion believes your solution solves a real, significant organizational problem
   - Stage 3: PREPARATION — Champion has the materials and confidence to pitch internally
   - Stage 4: ACTIVATION — Champion has initiated internal conversations with key stakeholders
   - Stage 5: SPONSORSHIP — Champion has secured at least one executive sponsor who supports moving forward

5. COGNITIVE LOAD REDUCTION DESIGN
Champions fail internally not because they don't believe in your solution, but because the internal selling task feels too hard. Every asset you send should reduce friction:
   - ONE THING emails: Each email has exactly one primary action or insight
   - PASS-ALONG READY: At least one email per week includes a fully written, editable asset the champion can forward with minimal personalization
   - OBJECTION PRE-LOADING: Deliver objection responses BEFORE the champion encounters the objection — not after deals stall
   - SOCIAL PROOF BY TITLE: Case studies and proof points must match the exact titles of people the champion needs to convince

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Build the Champion Activation Sequence as a Contact-based Workflow with enrollment trigger "CRS score ≥ 61 AND Deal Stage = Demo Completed." Use Contact Properties for Champion Persona and Economic Buyer Title to dynamically swap subject lines and body copy. Connect to Deal record to attribute champion emails to open pipeline.
- **Marketo:** Use Engagement Programs with 3 streams (Curious Evaluator → Engaged Prospect → Confirmed Champion) and transition rules based on CRS score changes. Set up Interesting Moments for each green-signal trigger and route to Salesforce as "Marketing Activities" on the opportunity record.
- **Salesforce:** Create a custom "Champion Health Score" field on the Contact object synced from MAP. Build a Lightning report showing Champion Email Engagement by Opportunity Stage. Configure opportunity alerts when CRS drops below 40 mid-cycle.
- **Outreach/Salesloft:** Use MAP webhook to trigger AE sequence "Champion Alert - Executive Meeting Not Scheduled" when a champion reaches Day 14 of activation sequence without booking executive briefing. AE sequence should reference specific content champion has engaged with.
- **Highspot/Seismic:** Upload all 5 pass-along assets to your content management system with champion-specific metadata tags. This reveals who inside the account is reading champion-forwarded content.
- **Google Sheets + Zapier (no MAP required):** For lean teams — use Zapier to watch Gmail/HubSpot email opens on tagged champion emails → append data to Google Sheet → send Slack alert to AE channel when ≥ 3 champion emails opened in one week.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champions engage with email 1–2 but drop off before email 4 (the Executive Ask).**
Solution: The jump to "asking champion to involve their CTO" feels too large too fast. Insert a lower-stakes milestone between email 3 and 4 — such as a "share this with a colleague" asset or a "reply with your biggest internal concern" email. The goal is to get one active response before asking champions to make an internal move. Alternatively, check if your ACV triggers a budget approval level that makes champions reluctant to escalate — if so, reframe the executive ask as "a 10-minute informational call" rather than a "decision meeting."

**Problem: Champions forward the executive brief but economic buyers don't respond.**
Solution: The brief itself is likely too long or too product-focused. Economic buyers at director+ level respond to 3-bullet briefs that lead with a financial outcome and a competitor risk. Redesign the CFO Business Case Brief to follow this structure: (1) "Companies like yours are [losing/missing/risking] $X because of [Problem]" → (2) "[Company Name] solved this in [X weeks], resulting in [Outcome]" → (3) "15 minutes to show you why [Champion's Name] believes this is worth a conversation." Remove all product screenshots and feature lists.

**Problem: Champion re-engagement sequence isn't working — champions don't respond to email 1 (value-first).**
Solution: The insight in re-engagement email 1 is probably not novel enough to interrupt a pattern of disengagement. Upgrade the re-engagement trigger to an external event signal: use intent data (6sense, Bombora) or trigger re-engagement when a new competitor funding round, a new regulatory announcement, or a relevant industry event occurs. A champion who ignored a general "new research" email often responds to "I saw [Competitor] just raised $50M — here's why this changes the calculation you showed your team last quarter."
