## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Carrier evaluations progress through technical validation but stall at CTO approval for months — deals in "internal budget prioritization" indefinitely**
Solution: CTO budget approval stalls in telco almost always mean one of three things: (1) the CTO's internal champion hasn't built a compelling enough business case to beat competing priorities, (2) the CTO is waiting for an external event (an analyst report, a competitor's deployment announcement, a board mandate) to justify the investment, or (3) there are political dynamics within the carrier IT/network organization that your account team hasn't mapped. Diagnose: (1) Ask your internal champion directly: "What specifically does the CTO need to see to make this decision now? What objection has she raised internally?" — champions often know the real objection but haven't told you; (2) Deliver a CTO-ready business case package to your champion: board-level one-pager (one page, strategic framing, no technical detail), CFO TCO model (carrier-specific $ numbers), and analyst endorsement excerpt from Heavy Reading or Analysys Mason — give your champion the materials to make the internal case; (3) Offer a "CTO Peer Reference Call" — 20-minute conversation between your reference carrier CTO and their CTO, peer-to-peer, no vendor involved — CTOs trust other CTOs more than any vendor or analyst; (4) Monitor for external trigger events (competitor deployment announcement, analyst report, board technology review) and be ready to re-engage immediately when the trigger occurs.

**Problem: Every PoC conversation ends with "we need to check our incumbent vendor can't do this first" — deals stall indefinitely while carrier checks with Ericsson/Nokia/Amdocs**
Solution: This objection reflects that your champion hasn't established internal permission to evaluate alternatives. The carrier procurement process typically requires a "make vs. buy vs. extend" decision before approving new vendor evaluation — your champion needs to complete that internal analysis. Address this before it kills PoC conversations: (1) Proactively provide a "Capability Gap Analysis" document — a structured assessment of what your target product category requires vs. what the incumbent OSS/BSS platform can realistically deliver (use publicly available incumbent product documentation + known limitations from analyst research — no unsupported claims); (2) Offer a "Vendor Evaluation Framework" — a structured RFI scorecard template your champion can use internally to assess the incumbent vs. alternatives on objective criteria; (3) Provide case studies specifically about carriers who extended their existing platform vs. brought in a specialist solution — show the pattern of outcomes; (4) Suggest a "parallel PoC": run your PoC simultaneously with the incumbent assessment rather than sequentially — this prevents indefinite delay while preserving the incumbent relationship check; (5) Set a PoC conversation deadline: "We can hold the PoC environment for your team until [date 30 days out] — after that we allocate it to another carrier in your region" — urgency framing helps champions get internal approval rather than letting the process drift.

**Problem: Strong technical credibility and positive analyst reviews, but SDRs can't get meetings with carrier CTOs/VPs — cold outreach response rate below 2%**
Solution: Direct cold outreach to carrier C-suite has extremely low effectiveness — carrier executives receive hundreds of vendor outreach messages per month and have strong filters. The path to carrier CTO access is almost never cold outreach. Fix this: (1) Industry event introductions: A warm introduction from a mutual contact at MWC/DTW converts at 40–60% vs. <2% for cold email — invest in event meeting programs over SDR cold sequencing for CTO-level targets; (2) Analyst-brokered introductions: Ask your Heavy Reading or Analysys Mason analyst contacts to introduce you to CTO-level contacts at target accounts — analysts frequently facilitate vendor-carrier introductions, especially when they believe the technology is genuinely relevant; (3) SI partner pathway: Ask your SI partner's account team at the target carrier to introduce you — the SI account lead can open a door in 48 hours that an SDR can't open in 6 months; (4) TM Forum working group participation: CTOs and CDOs send their top architects and program managers to TM Forum working groups — engage with these working group participants first (they are accessible and technically motivated), earn their technical endorsement, and let them facilitate the CTO introduction; (5) LinkedIn thought leadership: CTO-level carrier executives are active on LinkedIn — engage authentically with their posts (substantive technical comments, not empty praise) over 60–90 days before any direct outreach; (6) If SDR outreach is used at all, target VP/Director level (not CTO level) with a very specific technical insight relevant to their specific carrier's known transformation challenges — one sentence of research showing you understand their specific situation converts at 5–8×.
