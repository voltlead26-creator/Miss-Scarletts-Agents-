## Deliverable 1: Campaign Architecture & Budget Allocation

Design a 5-campaign structure:

**Campaign 1 — Branded Defense (10-15% of budget)**
- Goal: Own branded SERP, prevent competitor conquest, capture high-intent brand traffic
- Keyword approach: Exact + phrase match branded terms, branded + product modifier terms, branded + competitor comparison terms (e.g., "[Brand] vs [Competitor]")
- Bid strategy: Target Impression Share (90%+ top of page) or Maximize Conversion Value
- Expected metrics: CTR 15-40%, CPC $0.50-$3, conversion rate 8-20%

**Campaign 2 — Competitor Conquest (15-20% of budget)**
- Goal: Capture buyers actively evaluating competitor products
- Keyword approach: Competitor brand names (exact + phrase), competitor product names, competitor + "alternative/comparison/vs" modifiers — note: do NOT use competitor names in ad copy per Google policy
- Ad copy angle: Lead with your differentiator, proof point, or G2/Gartner positioning
- Landing page: Dedicated comparison page per competitor (e.g., "[Your Brand] vs [Competitor]")
- Expected metrics: CTR 2-6%, CPC $5-$25 (competitive), conversion rate 3-8%

**Campaign 3 — Category/Solution Keywords (30-35% of budget)**
- Goal: Capture buyers who know the solution category but haven't chosen a vendor
- Keyword approach: Solution category terms (e.g., "endpoint security platform," "HR analytics software"), job-to-be-done terms (e.g., "automate employee onboarding"), outcome terms (e.g., "reduce cloud infrastructure costs")
- Bid strategy: Target CPA based on blended demo + trial conversion rate
- Expected metrics: CTR 3-8%, CPC $8-$40, conversion rate 2-6%

**Campaign 4 — Problem-Aware/Pain Keywords (20-25% of budget)**
- Goal: Intercept buyers who know their problem but haven't identified your category
- Keyword approach: Pain-point terms (e.g., "why are our cloud bills so high," "HIPAA audit failed," "sales reps not hitting quota"), symptom terms, "how to fix/solve/reduce [pain]" patterns
- Offer: Gated asset (benchmark report, ROI calculator, diagnostic tool) — lower friction than demo request
- Bid strategy: Maximize Conversions with Target CPA set to gated content value
- Expected metrics: CTR 1-4%, CPC $4-$15, conversion rate 4-10% to asset download

**Campaign 5 — RLSA + Remarketing Overlay (10-15% of budget)**
- Goal: Re-engage searchers who visited key pages (pricing, demo, product) but did not convert
- Structure: Broad match keywords on converted search campaigns with RLSA audience layer (bid +30-60% for warm audiences, or targeting-only for non-converter remarketing)
- Audience segments: Pricing page visitors (last 30 days), demo page visitors (non-converters), case study readers (last 14 days), competitor comparison page readers

## Deliverable 2: Keyword Architecture

**Keyword Research Framework:**
- Use Google Keyword Planner + SEMrush/Ahrefs + SpyFu (competitor gap) + internal search query reports from existing organic traffic
- Organize keywords into tightly themed ad groups (5-20 keywords per group) to maximize ad relevance and Quality Score
- Map match types: Exact match for high-intent, high-CPC terms; Phrase match for variant capture; Broad match ONLY when Smart Bidding has 30+ conversions/month (algorithm requires volume for effective broad match)

**Negative Keyword Master List (minimum 50 terms — examples to build from):**
- Employment/hiring intent: "jobs," "careers," "salary," "internship," "hiring," "resume"

- Competitor names in non-conquest campaigns: [Add each competitor brand as negative in non-conquest campaigns]
- Modifier negatives: "cheap," "free," "DIY," "open source" (if your product is enterprise-priced)
- Geography negatives: [Specific countries/cities outside target market]

**Search Term Monitoring Cadence:**
- Weekly: Review search term reports, add new negatives for irrelevant queries, promote high-converting search terms to exact match
- Monthly: Identify new keyword opportunities from search term patterns, adjust match types based on Smart Bidding performance data
- Quarterly: Full keyword audit — pause low-QS terms, expand themes showing CPA efficiency

## Deliverable 4: Smart Bidding Strategy

**Bidding Ladder by Account Maturity:**

*New Account (0-30 conversion events/month):*
- Start with: Maximize Clicks (build impression share, gather search term data)
- Transition to: Maximize Conversions (once 30+ micro-conversions tracked per month)
- Graduate to: Target CPA (once 50+ primary conversions/month with consistent CPA)

*Established Account (50+ conversions/month):*
- Category campaigns: Target CPA set at [ACV × pipeline conversion rate × acceptable CAC ratio]
- Branded campaigns: Target Impression Share 90% (top of page)
- Conquest campaigns: Target CPA at 1.5× category CPA (conquest traffic converts lower)
- RLSA campaigns: Target CPA at 0.7× category CPA (warm audiences convert higher)

**Bid Modifier Recommendations:**
- Device: Desktop +20% (B2B research heavily desktop), Tablet -30%, Mobile -50% (unless mobile conversion tracking confirmed)
- Day of week: Monday-Thursday +10%, Friday -15%, Saturday/Sunday -60% (B2B purchase decisions happen during work week)
- Time of day: Business hours 8am-6pm in target time zones +20%; nights/weekends -50%
- Geography: Your top-converting metro areas +15%; low-converting regions -25%
- Audience (RLSA): Pricing page visitors +50%; site visitors 1-7 days ago +30%; customer match suppression list (existing customers) — exclude from acquisition campaigns

## Deliverable 5: Quality Score Optimization Plan

**Quality Score Components and Targets:**

| Component | Poor | Below Average | Average (Goal Minimum) | Above Average (Goal) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Expected CTR | <1% | 1-2% | 3-5% | 6%+ |
| Ad Relevance | Keyword ≠ Ad | Partial match | Keyword in headline | Keyword pinned H1 |
| Landing Page Experience | Generic homepage | Product page | Campaign-specific LP | Personalized LP with query matching |

**Landing Page Alignment Protocol:**
- Each ad group's keyword theme must match the landing page headline (H1) — Google crawls LP for relevance
- Include primary keyword in page title, meta description, H1, and first paragraph
- Page load speed: <2.5 seconds on mobile (use Google PageSpeed Insights to audit; slow pages tank QS)
- Include trust signals above the fold: customer logos, G2 badge, certifications
- Conversion form: 3-5 fields maximum for initial capture (name, email, company, phone — do NOT require 15-field enterprise intake forms)

**QS Improvement Actions:**
1. Pause keywords with QS ≤ 3 after 200+ impressions (low QS keywords increase CPCs for entire account)
2. Restructure ad groups where keyword themes are too broad — tighter theming improves ad relevance
3. A/B test landing pages: headline variants, social proof placement, form field count
4. Review Search Impression Share lost to rank (if lost IS >20%, QS or bid is suppressing volume)

## Deliverable 7: Attribution & Pipeline Tracking Setup

**Conversion Action Hierarchy (configure in Google Ads):**

*Primary conversions (used for Smart Bidding optimization):*
- Demo request form submit (import from GA4 goal or CRM webhook)
- Free trial account created (server-side conversion if possible — not pixel-dependent)
- Contact sales form submit

*Secondary conversions (observe-only — do NOT include in Smart Bidding):*
- ROI calculator completion
- Gated content download
- Pricing page visit (micro-conversion)
- 3+ minutes on site (engagement signal)

**CRM Pipeline Attribution Requirements:**
- UTM parameter structure: utm_source=google / utm_medium=cpc / utm_campaign=[campaign-name] / utm_content=[ad-group-name] / utm_term={keyword}
- Pass UTM data into CRM lead/contact record on form submit (use hidden form fields)
- Map Google Ads click ID (GCLID) to CRM opportunity record for enhanced conversions
- Configure Google Ads enhanced conversions: import CRM opportunity stage changes back into Google Ads as offline conversion events (triggers Smart Bidding on pipeline-level outcomes, not just top-of-funnel form fills)
- Revenue attribution model: Time-decay (appropriate for 30-180 day B2B sales cycles); avoid last-click for search which overvalues branded terms

**Weekly Reporting Metrics:**
- Impression Share (IS) — branded: target 90%+; category: target 40-60%; conquest: target 20-40%
- Average Quality Score by campaign
- Cost Per Demo Request (CPDR) — track separately from blended CPL
- Pipeline Influence: Opportunities where Google Ads touch point appears in CRM multi-touch attribution
- Search Term Efficiency: % of spend on exact match vs. phrase vs. broad (broad should not exceed 30% in early months)

**Days 1-30 — Foundation & Data Collection:**
- Week 1: Launch branded and category campaigns; install conversion tracking and UTM infrastructure

**Days 61-90 — Scale & CRM Integration:**
- Connect Google Ads enhanced conversions to CRM opportunity data (offline conversion import)

- Launch Performance Max campaign with search themes as complement (NOT replacement) to standard search
- Milestone: Pipeline attribution visible in CRM, CPA at or below target, Search IS >50% for top category terms

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Smart Bidding is underdelivering — campaigns are not spending budget**
Solution: Smart Bidding algorithms require sufficient conversion volume to model effectively (minimum 30-50 conversions per month per campaign). If you have low conversion volume, use micro-conversions (pricing page visits, time-on-site) as Smart Bidding signals while accumulating demo/trial conversion data. Alternatively, expand match types and reduce CPA targets temporarily to generate volume before tightening.

**Problem: Competitor conquest campaigns have very high CPCs but low conversion rates**
Solution: Conquest traffic fundamentally converts at lower rates than branded or category traffic — buyers searching for a competitor are already partially committed to another vendor. Improve conquest ROI by: (1) sending traffic to a dedicated comparison landing page (not your homepage), (2) leading with your strongest differentiation proof point (G2 rating, customer ROI stat, faster deployment), (3) offering a lower-friction CTA (free comparison guide download vs. demo request), and (4) following up conquest conversions with a longer nurture sequence that addresses switching costs.

**Problem: Quality Scores are stuck at 4-5 despite good CTR**
Solution: Low QS despite good CTR almost always indicates a landing page experience issue. Google's landing page crawler evaluates: (a) keyword relevance — is the search term present in the page H1, title, and first paragraph? (b) page load speed — use Google PageSpeed Insights; score below 50 on mobile will suppress QS regardless of relevance; (c) mobile usability — conversion forms must render correctly on all devices. Run a QS diagnostic by reviewing the three-component breakdown in Google Ads (expected CTR / ad relevance / landing page experience) for your lowest-QS keywords, then address the weakest component first.
