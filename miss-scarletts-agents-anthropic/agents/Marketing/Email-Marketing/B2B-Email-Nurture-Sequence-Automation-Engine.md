## Overview
Generates complete, stage-aware B2B email nurture sequences — from top-of-funnel awareness through closed-won — including subject lines, preview text, body copy, CTAs, and send-timing logic. Use it when launching a new campaign, refreshing a dead nurture track, or building sequences for a new ICP segment. Outputs are ready to paste directly into HubSpot, Marketo, or Salesforce Marketing Cloud without editing.

## Success Metrics

- **Open rate benchmark:** 28–38% for B2B nurture (below 22% = subject lines need A/B testing)
- **Click-through rate:** 3–6% per email; Email 1 and Email 3 should outperform (value-first emails)
- **Sequence completion rate:** Track % of contacts who reach Email 5 without unsubscribing
- **Sequence-to-meeting rate:** 2–5% of contacts who enter the sequence should book a call
- **Revenue influenced:** Track opportunities where the contact received ≥3 emails before opportunity creation in CRM
- **Reply rate:** A healthy nurture sequence generates 0.5–2% direct replies — replies signal high intent and should route to sales immediately
- **Unsubscribe rate:** Flag if >0.5% per email — usually signals misaligned persona targeting or wrong entry trigger

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Build each email as a workflow action inside a contact-based workflow
- Use "Contact property is known" + "Last form submission = [lead magnet name]" as enrollment triggers
- Add `{{ contact.firstname }}` and `{{ contact.company }}` personalization tokens
- Connect workflow to deal pipeline stage to auto-unenroll when deal moves to "Discovery Booked"
- Use HubSpot's A/B email testing module for subject line variants on Emails 1 and 3

**Marketo:**
- Create as an Engagement Program with a 5-step cadence stream
- Use Smart List filters to gate emails by score thresholds (e.g., only send Email 4 if score ≥ 40)
- Enable "Communication Limits" at the program level to prevent overlap with SDR outreach sequences
- Use Velocity scripting for advanced personalization (inject industry-specific benchmark data dynamically)

**Salesforce Marketing Cloud (SFMC):**
- Build in Journey Builder using "Email Send" activities with wait periods
- Use Einstein Engagement Scoring to dynamically reorder email sequence for low-engagement contacts
- Connect to Sales Cloud via "Update Contact" activity to push email engagement signals to lead record
- Use AMPscript to pull CRM data (account industry, ARR tier) into email body dynamically

**Clay + Zapier (AI-personalized sequences):**
- Use Clay to enrich lead records with LinkedIn bio, recent company news, and tech stack data
- Pass enriched data to a Claude API call that rewrites Email 1 body copy with account-specific hooks
- Trigger the HubSpot or Outreach sequence via Zapier once enrichment is complete

**Notion (content management):**
- Store all sequence variants (persona × funnel stage × vertical) in a Notion database
- Tag each email with persona, stage, version, and performance metrics for easy retrieval and iteration

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Open rates below 20% across the sequence  
**Solution:** The entry trigger is too broad. Segment more tightly by behavior (e.g., split "downloaded any ebook" into intent-specific sub-sequences by which ebook). Test 3–5 subject lines per email before scaling. Verify your sender domain has DKIM, DMARC, and SPF records configured — deliverability issues masquerade as engagement problems.

**Problem:** Strong opens but near-zero clicks (CTAs not converting)  
**Solution:** You're likely asking for too much too soon. Audit the CTA-to-stage ratio: Emails 1–2 should have zero-friction CTAs (read a blog post, watch a 90-second video). Reserve demo/meeting CTAs for Emails 4–5. Also check if your CTA button is landing on a page with a friction-heavy form — replace with Calendly direct-link or a 1-field "notify me" capture.

**Problem:** High unsubscribe rate (>1% per email)  
**Solution:** The sequence is either mis-targeted (persona doesn't match content), too frequent (reduce cadence to every 5–7 days for cold audiences), or repetitive in messaging (each email must deliver standalone value, not just "circling back"). Re-read the sequence as the recipient: could you unsubscribe from Email 1 without feeling like you missed something useful? If yes, rewrite the hook.
