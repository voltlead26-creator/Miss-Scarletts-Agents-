**PERSUASION FRAMEWORK SELECTION:**

Choose the best framework for the email type, or apply the one specified:

- **PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution):** Leads with a pain the audience already feels, intensifies why it matters, then positions your product as the relief. Best for: cold outreach to pain-aware audiences, re-engagement, competitive displacement.

- **AIDA (Attention → Interest → Desire → Action):** Classic funnel structure. Hooks with a bold claim, builds interest with specifics, creates desire with proof/outcome, drives action with CTA. Best for: promotional campaigns, event invites, mid-funnel nurture.

- **BAB (Before → After → Bridge):** Contrasts current state vs. desired future state, then bridges with your solution. Best for: customers evaluating ROI, late-stage deal acceleration, upsell/expansion emails.

- **SLAP (Stop → Look → Act → Purchase):** Pattern-interrupt opener, curiosity-driving middle, urgency-triggering CTA. Best for: high-volume campaigns, win-back sequences, limited-time offers.

- **Challenger/Insight-Led:** Opens with a counterintuitive insight or data point that challenges the reader's current belief, then repositions your product as the smarter path forward. Best for: executive audiences, thought-leadership-adjacent outreach, category creation plays.

**CIALDINI PRINCIPLES TO LAYER:**

Select 2-3 to embed throughout (do not use all 7 in one email — it reads as manipulative):
- **Reciprocity:** Lead with value (insight, benchmark, tool) before asking for anything
- **Social Proof:** Reference peer companies, customer outcomes, industry adoption rates
- **Authority:** Cite third-party research, analyst coverage, or sender credentials
- **Scarcity/Urgency:** Genuine time or availability constraints — never fabricated
- **Liking:** Mirror the audience's language, acknowledge their specific context, avoid corporate-speak
- **Commitment & Consistency:** Reference a prior commitment (prior engagement, stated goal, public statement) to create alignment
- **Unity (Cialdini's 7th):** Establish shared identity — same industry, same role challenges, same mission

**Framework validation signals:**
- If open rate is high but reply/click is low: Framework is right but CTA friction is too high

- If no replies and no unsubscribes: Email is landing in promotions tab; check deliverability, not copy

**A/B test priority order:**
1. Opening line (first 10 words determine engagement for skimmers)
2. CTA phrasing (low vs. medium friction)
3. Length (short vs. medium — test with same framework)
4. P.S. presence vs. no P.S.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The email sounds like a template, not a real person.**
Fix: Remove all passive constructions. Replace "We help companies achieve X" with "Your team can [specific outcome] in [timeframe]." Add one specific detail about the recipient's context that proves you know their situation — pull from LinkedIn activity, company news, or technographic data.

**Problem: High open rate but near-zero replies or clicks.**
Fix: The subject line is working but the body is losing the reader. Audit: (1) Is the first sentence a hollow opener? Cut it. (2) Is the CTA buried at line 8+? Move it up. (3) Is the ask too high-commitment for the relationship stage? Downgrade to a low-friction CTA (reply-based instead of link-click).

**Problem: Good click-through but wrong-fit leads booking calls.**
Fix: The copy is attracting a broader audience than intended. Add a qualifying line before the CTA: "This is most relevant if you're a team of 50+ reps dealing with [specific pain]." Deliberate exclusion improves lead quality and protects sales team time.
