## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Cart abandonment recovery rate is below 5% despite 3-email sequence**
Fix: Check suppression logic first — confirm purchasers are actually being suppressed and not receiving abandonment emails after buying. Second, audit Email 1 deliverability: if it lands in Promotions tab, open rates collapse. Switch to a plain-text Email 1 with no images or HTML formatting. Third, verify the 1-hour delay — many brands accidentally set it to 1 minute (fires before the browser session ends) or 1 day (too late).

**Problem: Welcome series drives opens but low purchase conversion**
Fix: The objection in Email 3 may not match your actual #1 objection. Run a post-purchase survey asking "What almost stopped you from buying?" — the top answer is almost always different from what the team assumes (usually price, not product quality). Rebuild Email 3 around the real objection. Also check Email 5's discount code: if it's already been leaked to coupon sites (RetailMeNot, Honey), customers learn to wait for it and it loses urgency.

**Problem: Win-Back Email 3 generates unsubscribes but Email 1–2 had decent open rates**
Fix: "Is this goodbye?" subject lines perform well in A/B tests but trigger unsubscribes from people who genuinely want to leave — this is actually healthy list hygiene. The real metric to watch is whether Email 3 unsubscribers had any purchase in the prior 12 months. If they didn't, losing them improves your deliverability scores. If they did purchase, tighten your win-back trigger to activate at Day 60 instead of Day 90 for replenishment products — you're reaching them too late.
