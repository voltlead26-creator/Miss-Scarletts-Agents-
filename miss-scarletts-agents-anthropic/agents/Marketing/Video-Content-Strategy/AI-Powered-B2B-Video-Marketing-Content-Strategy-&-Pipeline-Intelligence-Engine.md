## DELIVERABLE 1: VIDEO CONTENT STRATEGY FRAMEWORK

### Buyer Journey Video Map
Design a video content portfolio mapped precisely to buyer awareness stages. For each stage:

**TOFU (Awareness) — Reach & Education:**
- Primary objective: Earn trust from in-ICP buyers who don't yet know your company
- Required video types: Thought leadership interviews with industry experts or executives; "State of [Industry]" data-driven explainers with original insights; pain-point explainer videos that name the problem your ICP lives with; executive speaking clips (conference keynotes, podcast appearances, short LinkedIn opinions)
- Platform priority: LinkedIn organic first (largest B2B professional audience), YouTube second (search-captured demand), TikTok only if your ICP buyer personas are demonstrably active there
- Optimal length: 45-90 seconds for LinkedIn native clips; 8-15 minutes for YouTube educational deep-dives
- CTA strategy: Soft — subscribe/follow, download lead magnet, no demo push

**MOFU (Consideration) — Evaluation & Differentiation:**
- Primary objective: Help in-market buyers evaluate your approach, compare alternatives, and build internal justification for your solution
- Required video types: Product deep-dives and use-case walkthroughs; customer success mini-documentaries (2-3 min, specific outcomes with metrics); "our approach vs. the alternative approach" comparison videos; ROI calculator walkthroughs; webinar replays edited to 20-minute highlight reels; live Q&A recordings with timestamps
- Platform priority: YouTube (searchable discovery), website product/solution pages, LinkedIn Showcase Pages for specific verticals or use cases
- Optimal length: 3-8 minutes
- CTA strategy: Demo request, free trial, relevant downloadable asset

**BOFU (Decision) — Conversion & Purchase Validation:**
- Primary objective: Remove final purchase friction; provide validation that reduces buyer risk perception
- Required video types: Customer testimonial videos (60-90 sec, specific ROI outcomes, named companies, recognizable logos); use-case-specific product demo videos; "implementation in 30 days" walkthroughs that address time-to-value concerns; executive trust-building videos (CEO product vision, CISO security overview); honest competitive comparison demonstrations
- Platform priority: Website pricing page and demo request pages; sales email sequences via Loom or BombBomb; deal room (Aligned, Notion, Highspot)
- Optimal length: 60-90 seconds for testimonials; 8-15 minutes for full product demos with narration
- CTA strategy: Direct demo booking with specific calendar link; contact sales with urgency framing

### Content Pillar Architecture
Identify 3 thematic pillars that anchor the entire video program. For each pillar:
- Pillar name and strategic rationale (specifically why this topic cluster earns trust with your economic buyer, not just your end user)
- 10 video episode ideas within the pillar, ordered by production difficulty (easiest first)
- Differentiated angle: why your company's point of view on this topic is distinctive vs. competitor videos on the same topic
- YouTube keyword opportunity: primary keyword phrase to rank for with estimated monthly search volume and competition level
- Pipeline conversion hypothesis: the specific buyer action this pillar is designed to trigger (subscribe → download resource → book demo)

### Production Priority Matrix
For every video type you've identified, score on:
- Pipeline impact potential (1-10): direct likelihood this video type drives demo requests or opportunities
- Production effort per video (1-10): total hours of content manager + editor time, including scripting, recording, editing, and publishing
- Reusability multiplier (1-5): how many derivative content pieces this produces (short clips, blog post, email insert, quote graphics)
- Time-to-pipeline estimate: average weeks from video publish date to first attributable demo request

Produce a ranked table from highest to lowest ROI priority. This becomes the production queue.

### Script Templates by Video Type

**Thought Leadership Interview (8-12 min YouTube):**
HOOK (0-30 sec): Open mid-conversation with the most provocative or counterintuitive insight from the interview. Cut to title card. Never open with "Hi, I'm [Name], today we're talking about..."

GUEST INTRO (30-60 sec): 2-3 sentences on guest's credibility and the specific reason they're worth listening to on this topic. Skip irrelevant resume items.

THE BIG CLAIM (60-180 sec): Guest's primary contrarian or data-backed claim about [topic]. Why is this different from conventional wisdom?

THE ROOT CAUSE (180-300 sec): Why does the problem guest is describing actually happen? Who suffers most? Guest should use specific examples, not abstractions.

THE FRAMEWORK (300-480 sec): Guest's 3-part methodology, model, or approach. Should be memorable and ownable (e.g., "The Three Layers of X," "The Rule of Y").

THE PROOF (480-600 sec): One specific case study, data point, or customer example that validates the framework. Numbers required.

THE FUTURE STATE (600-660 sec): What does success look like if you get this right? What becomes possible?

RAPID FIRE / LIGHTNING ROUND (660-720 sec): 3 quick questions with short answers. Creates a natural energy shift and produces highly shareable clips.

CTA + SUBSCRIBE (720 sec+): Summarize 3 key takeaways in one sentence each. Soft CTA to subscribe or download companion resource. Hard CTA only if this is a MOFU/BOFU topic.

**Customer Testimonial (60-90 sec):**
RESULT FIRST (0-10 sec): Lead with the quantified outcome. "[X]% increase in [metric] in [timeframe]." Never start with customer intro.

CONTEXT (10-25 sec): "Before [Product], we were [specific painful situation]. [The thing we were manually doing / the result we were missing]."

THE TURNING POINT (25-45 sec): "What changed was [specific capability or workflow]. Instead of [old way], now we [new way]."

THE OUTCOME (45-65 sec): "[Primary metric improvement]. But what surprised us was [secondary benefit they didn't expect]."

THE ENDORSEMENT (65-90 sec): "I'd recommend [Product] to any [ICP job title] who [trigger condition — e.g., 'is managing a team of 20+ reps and losing deals to [competitor]']."

**Product Demo Video (5-8 min):**
PROMISE (0-30 sec): "In the next [X] minutes, I'll show you exactly how [ICP job title] at companies like [recognizable company type] use [Product] to [specific outcome with a number if possible]. No fluff."

BEFORE STATE (30-90 sec): Visually show the painful before-state. A messy spreadsheet, a manual process, a missed alert. Make the viewer feel the pain before you show the solution.

CORE WORKFLOW (90-300 sec): Walk through the 3 most important actions in the product. "Step one is... Step two is... Step three is..." Use a real (or realistic demo environment) account, not a fake demo with obviously fake data.

PROOF MOMENT (300-360 sec): Show real data, a real report, or reference a real customer outcome tied to what you just demonstrated. "Customers using this workflow typically see X."

OBJECTION HANDLING (360-420 sec): Address the #1 concern your ICP has in the buying process. "I know what you're thinking — 'how long does implementation take?' Here's what that looks like..."

NEXT STEP (420+ sec): Direct, specific CTA. "If you want to see what this looks like for your team, click the link in the description to book a 30-minute personalized walkthrough. I'll show you your accounts, your data."

### Pipeline Attribution Setup

**Step-by-Step Configuration:**
1. Install Wistia or Vidyard on your website and connect to HubSpot/Salesforce via native integration
2. Configure "Video Viewed" contact activity: auto-log when any contact watches ≥40% of any video
3. Apply UTM parameters to every external video link: `utm_source=[platform]_video&utm_medium=[organic/paid]&utm_campaign=[video-slug]`
4. Create a HubSpot/Salesforce report: "Video-Influenced Pipeline" = all opportunities where associated contact has ≥1 Video Viewed activity logged before the opportunity creation date
5. Track three attribution models in parallel:
   - First Touch: video was the first-ever marketing touchpoint before conversion
   - Last Touch: video was the final touchpoint before demo request
   - Multi-Touch (U-Shaped): video influenced somewhere in the buyer journey

**Video ROI Formula:**
Video CAC = Total Quarterly Video Program Cost ÷ (Video-Influenced Opportunities × Average Close Rate)

Video ROAS = Video-Influenced Closed Won ARR ÷ Total Quarterly Video Program Cost

Video Payback Period (months) = Total Video Program Investment ÷ Monthly Video-Influenced ARR

Content Efficiency = Total Content Pieces Published ÷ Raw Videos Produced (target: ≥3.5x)

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot + Wistia**: Install the Wistia HubSpot integration (native, no code required). Configure it to auto-log a "Video Viewed" activity on a contact record whenever someone watches ≥40% of a hosted video. Create a HubSpot smart list: "Watched >50% of product demo video" and enroll in a 3-email BOFU sequence with a direct Calendly link. Add +25 lead score points for any contact who watches a full product demo. Sales team sees video watch history in the contact sidebar — use as a conversation starter: "I saw you watched our billing walkthrough — which part resonated most?"

- **Salesforce + Vidyard**: Use Vidyard's Salesforce managed package (free tier available) to surface video view data in the opportunity and contact records. Create a custom field "Video Touchpoints Count" that auto-increments. Add this field to the opportunity layout so reps see it during pipeline reviews. Reps who reference specific videos in follow-up calls close at 12-18% higher rates according to Vidyard's own research.

- **Zapier + Loom**: Build a Zap: when a Loom video is viewed by someone for >60 seconds → look up their email in HubSpot → if they are a known contact → create a task for the assigned sales rep saying "Prospect [Name] at [Company] watched your video — follow up within 2 hours." Hot leads get instant human follow-up.

- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager (Video Boosting)**: After an organic LinkedIn video post performs well (>500 views, >1% engagement rate), boost it to your target account list as a Sponsored Content dark post. Budget $75-150 per boost. LinkedIn users who see the same content organically and then paid show 3-5x higher conversion rates than cold paid audiences. This is the single highest-ROI paid tactic in a B2B video program.

- **Opus Clip + Buffer**: Set up a workflow: publish long-form video to YouTube → Opus Clip automatically generates 5-8 short clips → clips route to Buffer queue for LinkedIn and Instagram Reels scheduling. One long-form video per week becomes 5-8 short-form posts. A single 10-minute YouTube video can fuel 2 weeks of LinkedIn content.

- **Notion or Airtable Video Hub**: Build a master video asset database with fields: Title, Platform URL, Publish Date, Video Type (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU), 30-Day Views, Watch Time %, Leads Generated (with tracking link), Deals Influenced ($), Short Clips Extracted (#), Blog Post Published (Y/N), Email Used (Y/N), Status (planned / scripting / recording / editing / published / archived). Review monthly to retire underperforming videos and double down on top performers.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Videos accumulate views but generate no demo requests or inbound leads.**
Solution: The conversion gap is almost always one of three root causes. First, mismatched CTA — TOFU explainer videos should not ask for a demo; they should offer a lead magnet (template, checklist, benchmark report). Only MOFU/BOFU videos should hard-sell the demo. Audit your CTA vs. funnel stage alignment. Second, wrong audience reaching the video — check LinkedIn video analytics for seniority and function of viewers. If junior ICs are watching instead of VP-level economic buyers, adjust your hashtags, post copy framing, and content topics to appeal to seniority (use language about "your team," "your budget," "your board" rather than "how to do X yourself"). Third, insufficient volume — a video program needs 60+ days and 12+ published videos before the compound reach effects kick in. Single-video experiments don't generate statistically meaningful pipeline data. Commit to 90 days before drawing conclusions.

**Problem: LinkedIn videos plateau at 400-700 views and can't break through.**
Solution: LinkedIn's algorithm is heavily weighted toward early engagement velocity — comments, shares, and reactions in the first 60-90 minutes after posting determine whether the post gets pushed to a wider audience. Three fixes: (1) Build an internal activation system — notify 8-12 employees (executives, sales reps, customer success) via Slack 30 minutes before a video goes live. Ask them to watch and leave a genuine comment (not "Great post!"). Their comments expose the video to their networks. (2) Post time matters more than most marketers realize — Tuesday through Thursday, 8-10am in your primary market timezone consistently outperforms other windows for B2B content. (3) Use LinkedIn document posts (carousel-style PDF) as a complement to native video. LinkedIn's algorithm currently favors document posts at comparable or higher reach than native video. Use documents as teasers with a "full video in comments" CTA to combine both format's reach benefits.

**Problem: Can't measure whether video is influencing pipeline — leadership doesn't believe the ROI.**
Solution: The attribution gap is a setup problem, not a measurement problem. Three sequential fixes: (1) Switch video hosting for your website from YouTube embeds to Wistia or Vidyard — YouTube embeds don't log individual viewer data. Wistia identifies viewers by email when they're a known contact in your MAP/CRM and logs the view automatically. (2) Apply strict UTM governance to every external video link: `utm_source=linkedin_video&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=[video-slug-date]`. GA4 and your CRM will then show you the downstream path from video click to lead to opportunity. (3) Build the "Video-Influenced Pipeline" report in your CRM before you publish your next video. The report query: "Show me all open and closed opportunities where the associated primary contact has a 'Video Viewed' activity logged within 90 days before the opportunity creation date." Run this report monthly and present it in your pipeline review meeting. Once stakeholders see the first $200K in pipeline influenced by video, budget objections disappear.
