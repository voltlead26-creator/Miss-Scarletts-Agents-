## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom property "Video Content Engager" and use a workflow to tag contacts who click through from YouTube CTAs. Build a dedicated nurture sequence triggered by this property. Track video-sourced MQLs in campaign reports.
- **YouTube Studio + Notion:** Build a video production database in Notion that mirrors your YouTube Studio analytics. Use Zapier to auto-populate view counts, watch time, and subscriber data weekly so you can make programming decisions from a single dashboard.
- **Loom/Vidyard:** Use your YouTube content as the "value bomb" in cold outreach — embed episode clips in Vidyard sequences to warm prospects before first contact. Sales reps can send the most relevant episode to a prospect's specific pain point.
- **LinkedIn:** Schedule LinkedIn native video uploads 48-72 hours after YouTube publish (prevents YouTube SEO cannibalization). Use the LinkedIn post as the primary comment-harvesting mechanism; route best comments back to YouTube as engagement bait.
- **Zapier + Google Sheets:** Auto-export YouTube analytics every Monday into a Google Sheet. Use conditional formatting to flag episodes underperforming on CTR (<3%) or watch time (<35%) for immediate thumbnail/title A/B testing.
- **Calendly / Chili Piper:** For demand capture episodes with direct demo CTAs, embed a Calendly link in the video description and pin it as a comment. Track conversions with UTM parameters specific to each episode type.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Episodes get views but generate zero leads**
Solution: The CTA architecture is broken. Audit each episode: Is the CTA delivered verbally on camera (not just in the description)? Is the linked asset directly relevant to the episode topic (not generic)? Is the landing page optimized for video traffic (headline references the video, form is ≤3 fields)? Run an A/B test: spend one month adding a verbal mid-roll CTA at the 40% mark of your top-viewed episodes and measure conversion lift.

**Problem: Show launches but views plateau at 200-300 per episode with no growth**
Solution: You're likely missing search discovery. Audit your last 8 titles: Are they optimized for how your ICP searches on YouTube (problem-first, specific numbers, "how to" or "why")? Run your titles through YouTube search autocomplete. If you're not appearing in suggested results, shift from brand-voice titles to search-intent titles for 60 days and measure the difference in impression volume.

**Problem: Production quality is inconsistent batch-to-batch**
Solution: Create a single-page "show bible" document that travels to every production day. It should include: approved backdrop/set configuration photo, lighting setup diagram, approved on-camera outfits, intro/outro script word-for-word, segment time targets, and a 5-point quality checklist the editor uses before delivering a cut. Inconsistent quality is almost always a missing pre-production documentation problem.
