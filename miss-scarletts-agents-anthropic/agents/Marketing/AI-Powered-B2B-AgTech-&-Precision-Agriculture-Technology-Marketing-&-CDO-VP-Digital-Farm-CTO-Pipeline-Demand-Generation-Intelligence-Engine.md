## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Outreach gets ignored because farmers/agribusiness executives "don't trust tech vendors"**
Solution: Lead with peer proof, not product. Every outreach touch must reference a named customer (with permission) or aggregate data from "operations like yours in [state]." Replace "our platform" language with "what we're seeing in the field" language. Consider sending physical mail — a 2-page field report in an envelope gets opened at 60%+ rates in agriculture, where physical mail stands out vs. digital clutter. When possible, have your champion agronomist or a customer speak first on your behalf.

**Issue: Long sales cycles (9-14 months) make pipeline feel empty and ROI hard to demonstrate**
Solution: Map every campaign to multi-stage pipeline movement, not just MQL creation. Track SAL (Sales Accepted Lead) velocity, Opportunity creation rate, and "pipeline contribution" metrics to show marketing's influence at every stage. Build a "pipeline velocity" dashboard showing average days between stages — use it to identify where deals are stalling and create specific nurture content for that stage. For board reporting: show "influenced pipeline" (deals where marketing had ≥2 touchpoints) alongside "sourced pipeline" to capture full marketing contribution.

**Issue: Seasonality creates demand feast-or-famine — Q4/Q1 overwhelm, Q2/Q3 drought**
Solution: Use the off-season (May-August) to build your content library, deepen customer relationships for case studies/testimonials, run field day events that create visual proof assets, and warm prospects for the November-March decision window. Build an "always-on" nurture track that delivers value every 3-4 weeks year-round — market insights, crop performance data, regulatory updates — so that when the buying window opens, you're already the trusted advisor in the inbox. Score engagement across 12 months, not just campaign response, to identify prospects heating up before they raise their hand.
