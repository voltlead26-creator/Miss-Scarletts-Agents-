## Integration Tips

- **6sense / Bombora → Apollo / Outreach:** Use 6sense's Salesforce integration to write intent scores to a custom field; build a Salesforce workflow rule that triggers Apollo/Outreach sequence enrollment when score crosses threshold and account is in your ICP filter
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator → HubSpot:** Use Sales Navigator's CRM sync to push job change notifications as HubSpot contact property updates; trigger workflow to create a sequence task for the SDR and pre-populate the signal context in the task body
- **BuiltWith / Datanyze → Zapier → Slack:** Set up a BuiltWith monitor on your ICP account list; pipe new tech stack additions via Zapier into a #signal-alerts Slack channel with pre-filled Apollo sequence link
- **G2 Buyer Intent → Salesloft:** G2's native CRM integration can push competitor review activity into Salesforce; create a cadence in Salesloft that auto-assigns the "competitor displacement" sequence variant when this field populates
- **Crunchbase → Notion signal log:** Use Crunchbase's funding alerts to populate a Notion database; have SDRs review it daily and mark accounts for sequence activation with one click
- **First-party web analytics (Clearbit Reveal / Warmly / Koala):** For accounts visiting your pricing or comparison pages, set real-time Slack alerts to SDRs; these contacts should skip to Touch 4 of the sequence (they've already self-selected as interested)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Sequences feel generic despite filling in signal details — prospects reply saying "this feels like a template."
**Fix:** The issue is usually that the signal reference is surface-level (e.g., "I saw you raised funding — congrats!"). Go one level deeper: what does the funding *specifically* mean for their role? A new VP RevOps joining post-funding faces a specific set of problems (fixing forecasting, rationalizing the tech stack, proving CAC efficiency to board) — reference one of those explicitly rather than the funding event itself.

**Problem:** Reply rates are high but meeting conversion is low — prospects reply but don't book.
**Fix:** Your CTA is likely asking for too much too soon. Replace "book a 30-minute demo" with "would it be worth 10 minutes to compare notes on how other [job title]s at [company stage] are solving this?" — a peer conversation framing books 2–3x more meetings than a demo ask.

**Problem:** Signals are firing but sequences aren't producing pipeline — the accounts just aren't buying right now.
**Fix:** Audit your signal threshold — you may be triggering sequences on low-intensity signals (e.g., a single intent topic spike, a hire that's been in role 180+ days). Tighten the trigger criteria: require 2+ signals simultaneously, cap job change triggers at 60 days post-hire, and require intent scores above 70 (not 40) for anonymous signals. Quality of signal timing matters more than quantity of sequences launched.
