## Overview
Design and deploy a complete marketing-led renewal and expansion revenue motion that runs automatically in the 90 days before each customer renewal date. Use this when your company relies solely on CSMs to drive renewals, has no structured marketing program for existing customers, or is losing expansion revenue because no one is systematically marketing to accounts between QBRs. This engine transforms renewal from a reactive sales conversation into a proactive, data-driven marketing campaign that makes customers feel the value — before your CSM ever asks for the signature.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Usage data isn't available at the account level, so personalized emails are impossible.**
Fix: Start without usage personalization — run the campaign with company-level tokens only (company name, industry, CSM name, customer since date) and outcome-focused copy that doesn't reference specific metrics. Parallel track: work with your engineering or data team to create a simple Salesforce custom object or field that captures 3 product usage KPIs updated weekly via a scheduled export from your product database. Even a Google Sheets → Zapier → Salesforce pipeline gets you usage data in email tokens within 2 weeks. Until then, the champion enablement kit and ROI framework in the prompt can be completed by CSMs from customer-reported data rather than product telemetry.

**Issue: CSMs feel the automated marketing emails are taking away their relationship and they're pushing back on the program.**
Fix: Position the program explicitly as "marketing does the heavy lifting so CSMs can focus on strategic conversations." Involve CSMs in the email review process before launch — have them approve the Day 85 and Day 30 emails as "coming from their desk" even if automated. Make CSM name and photo the sender on all automated emails (not a no-reply address). Share the CSM time-savings metric monthly: "Your renewal touchpoints this month were reduced by X hours — here's what your accounts engaged with instead." Most CSM resistance dissolves when they see customers responding positively to marketing-automated emails and the renewal conversations become shorter.

**Issue: The renewal marketing campaign is running but renewal rates aren't improving after 90 days.**
Fix: Audit the sequence in three areas: (1) Engagement — are accounts opening emails? If open rate is <25%, the subject lines or sender identity are failing; test CSM-name sender vs. marketing@, and A/B test 3 subject line variants per email. (2) Action — are accounts clicking and consuming content? If open rate is fine but CTR is <3%, the content isn't compelling — switch from generic case studies to vertical-specific proof; generate ROI reports even if manual for the first 30 days. (3) Correlation — compare renewal outcomes for engaged vs. non-engaged accounts in the campaign; if engaged accounts renew at the same rate as non-engaged, your campaign is hitting the wrong accounts (healthy accounts who would renew anyway) — adjust Health Score threshold to focus campaign energy on Yellow accounts (score 40–69) where marketing can actually move the needle.
