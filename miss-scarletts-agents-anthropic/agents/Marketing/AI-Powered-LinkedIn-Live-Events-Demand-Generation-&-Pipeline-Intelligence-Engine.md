## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low registration rates despite consistent posting (< 50 registrants)**
Solution: The promotional post on Day 14 is doing too much selling. LinkedIn's algorithm punishes external links in the main post body — move your Event link to the first comment. Also A/B test your event title: the hook should agitate a specific pain, not describe a format. "How 3 Operators Cut CAC by 40% (Live)" beats "LinkedIn Live: Marketing Strategy Session" every time.

**Problem: High registration but low live attendance (< 25% show-up rate)**
Solution: LinkedIn Events does not send robust reminder notifications compared to email. Add a DM reminder workflow: the day before and 2 hours before the event, send a personal DM from the host (or automate via LinkedIn helper tools like Dripify or Expandi) with the direct join link. This alone typically lifts attendance 12-18 percentage points. Also ensure your replay promise is in the event description — paradoxically, promising a replay increases live attendance because it signals the event is worth attending.

**Problem: Post-event DMs feel awkward and get ignored**
Solution: Your Tier 1 DMs must reference something hyper-specific from the event — a question the prospect asked, a comment they made, or a reaction they gave. Generic "Thanks for attending!" DMs perform identically to cold outreach. If your event platform does not expose individual attendee engagement signals, ask a qualifying question in the event description ("What's your biggest challenge with X?") via a pre-event form — then reference their answer in the DM.
