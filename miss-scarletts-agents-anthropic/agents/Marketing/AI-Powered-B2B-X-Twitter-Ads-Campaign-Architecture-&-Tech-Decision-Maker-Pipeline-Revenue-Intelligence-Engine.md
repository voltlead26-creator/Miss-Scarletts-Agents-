**A. Full-Funnel Campaign Structure**

- Objective: Tweet Engagements
- Target Audience: Tier 2 (handle lookalikes) + keyword audiences showing research intent

- Bid Strategy: Automatic bidding for engagements, cap CPE at $2.00 for B2B
- Budget Allocation: 25% of monthly budget
- Success Metric: Engagement rate (target >1.5% for promoted content), reply quality (are ICP buyers engaging?)

*Campaign 3 — Direct Response & Pipeline Capture (Bottom Funnel):*
- Objective: Website Traffic or Conversions
- Target Audience: Tier 3 (warm retargeting) + highest-intent keyword clusters
- Ad Format: Single image or carousel with strong offer (free assessment, ROI calculator, limited-time trial)
- Bid Strategy: Automatic bid for website clicks (target $8–$20 CPLC for B2B tech ICP)

- Target Audience: Handle lookalikes of top industry accounts + keyword audiences

- Budget Allocation: 10% of monthly budget
- Success Metric: Cost per follower, follower quality (check new followers' bios for ICP fit)

- Objective: Website Traffic + Engagements (run simultaneously during events)
- Target Audience: Active event hashtag keyword targeting (real-time conversation participants)
- Ad Format: Time-sensitive single image ads with event context
- Bid Strategy: Manual CPC with elevated CPM bids to win impressions during high-competition event windows
- Budget Allocation: 10% of monthly budget (concentrated into 3–5 day event windows = 3–4 events/year)
- Success Metric: Impression share during event window, engagement rate relative to non-event benchmarks

**B. Ad Format Specifications & Best Practices**

For each X ad format, provide: ideal use case, creative specs, character limits (tweet copy max 280 chars including URL), and B2B-specific creative best practices.

- Single Image Ad: Best for direct response offers with clear CTA. Image 1200x628px, max file size 5MB. First line must function as standalone hook (users see tweet text before scrolling to image). Never lead with a logo — lead with a stat, a provocative question, or a pain point statement.
- Multi-Image Ad (up to 4 images): Best for showing product screenshots, before/after comparisons, feature showcases. Each image must work individually in case the carousel isn't swiped.
- Video Ad: 15–30 seconds optimal for B2B. Add captions — 80%+ of X video is watched without sound. Hook must land in first 3 seconds. End with a clear CTA screen (URL or verbal CTA).
- Carousel Ad (up to 6 cards): Best for multi-step process explanations, feature walkthroughs, or "5 signs you need [product category]" formats.
- Promoted Organic Tweets: Leverage tweets that already have organic traction (>2% engagement rate). The social proof of real replies and retweets makes paid amplification more credible than pure dark posts.

- Frequency cap: 3 impressions per user per 7 days to avoid overexposure for a single post

**C. Coordinated Organic + Paid Cadence**
- Executive posts organically → Marketing team monitors for posts hitting the engagement threshold → Amplification campaign launched within 48 hours → Engagement retargeting audience built from amplified post → Bottom-funnel Website Traffic campaign retargets engaged users with direct offer

**D. Employee Advocacy Integration**
- When executive posts go viral organically and the company account retweets → amplify the company retweet rather than the original to keep budget in the company's ad account while capitalizing on the executive's credibility signal

- Export demo request and SQL conversion data from CRM on weekly cadence
- Match against X click IDs (twclid parameter) captured via UTMs in your CRM's lead source field
- Upload as offline conversions in X Ads Manager to train X's bidding algorithm on actual pipeline quality, not just form fills
- This allows X to optimize for deal-quality leads, not just raw conversion volume — critical for B2B where lead quality variance is high

**Weeks 1–2 (Foundation & Baseline)**
- Launch all 4 core campaigns with conservative budgets ($200/day total)
- Install X Tag and verify conversion event firing
- Establish baseline CTR, CPE, and CPLC by audience tier and ad format
- Use X's audience insights to validate that reached audience matches ICP demographics (age, gender, geography, device type breakdown)
- Decisions: If Tier 1 keyword audiences are delivering <0.2% CTR for direct response → review keyword cluster relevance; if @handle lookalike audiences deliver engagement but not clicks → shift to Engagements objective instead of Website Traffic for those audiences

**Weeks 3–4 (Audience Refinement)**
- Add negative keyword exclusions based on tweet engagement quality (are the right people engaging? Review engager bios manually)
- A/B test two creative angles for primary direct response campaign (pain-point-led vs. proof-led copy)
- Activate engagement retargeting campaign for users who engaged with Tier 2 (Consideration) content
- Build first promoted tweet amplification campaign if organic content hits threshold

**Month 2 (Scale & Diversify)**
- Creative refresh: rotate ad copy to prevent frequency fatigue (X creative fatigue is faster than LinkedIn — refresh every 10–14 days for high-frequency campaigns)
- Launch lookalike expansion from CRM custom audience if matched audience exceeds 2,000 users
- A/B test keyword targeting vs. handle lookalike targeting on same campaign objective to determine which delivers lower cost-per-qualified-click
- Activate offline conversion upload to train bidding algorithm on pipeline-quality conversions

**Month 3 (Event Integration & Full Attribution)**
- Deploy first event-burst campaign around next major industry conference (3-phase pre/during/post structure)
- Complete first 90-day pipeline attribution report: X-sourced pipeline value, X CAC vs. other channels, X ROAS
- Optimize budget allocation based on CRM pipeline data (shift budget toward audiences and campaigns producing qualified pipeline, not just lowest CPLC)
- Scale winning campaigns by 20–30% and pause underperformers using the three-signal kill switch: CPLC >2x target AND engagement rate <0.5% AND zero CRM pipeline contribution after 30 days

**Three Key Scale/Pause/Pivot Signals:**
1. SCALE: CPLC within target range AND post-click conversion rate >2% AND at least 2 CRM-qualified leads attributed in 30-day window → increase budget 25% and expand to broader audiences
2. PAUSE: CPLC >2.5x target for 14+ days with no improvement trend → pause ad set, audit audience-to-creative fit
3. PIVOT: High engagement rate but zero website conversions → landing page mismatch, not audience mismatch — test new landing page before changing targeting

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High impression volume but low link click-through rate (<0.2%)**
Solution: X audiences consume content without clicking — this is normal X behavior. Check if your ad copy is being read as a standalone tweet (valuable in itself without clicking) vs. designed to create a gap that requires a click to resolve. Rewrite using the "incomplete information" hook formula: "We found the #1 reason B2B SaaS security audits fail — and it's not what you'd expect [link]." Also check audience relevance: use X's performance breakdown to see if you're reaching the right job titles/locations. If engagement rate is high but CTR is low, shift some budget to Tweet Engagements objective to build audience for retargeting rather than forcing premature bottom-funnel conversion.

**Problem: Keyword audiences delivering high volume but low lead quality (ICP mismatch)**
Solution: X keyword targeting is broad by default — it matches users who have mentioned the keyword, not just active buyers. Add audience narrowing layers: combine keyword targeting with interest category targeting (Technology, Business & Finance), add demographic filters (narrow age range typical of VP/CTO buyers, typically 30–55), and set a minimum follower count threshold (X allows this in some campaign types, typically 100+ followers signals an active X user more likely to be a professional). Also review which specific keywords are driving the most clicks and exclude keywords driving irrelevant traffic (e.g., "AWS security" might pull in students and hobby coders, while "cloud security posture management" is more qualified).

**Problem: Low match rate on CRM custom audiences (<15%)**
Solution: X's match rate for B2B email lists is typically 25–45% — significantly lower than LinkedIn (50–70%) because X accounts are often registered with personal emails. To improve: upload both work and personal emails if available, include phone numbers as an additional identifier (X can match on phone), segment your list to exclude very old contacts (pre-2020) as X accounts may have been deactivated, and ensure your list has at least 1,000 contacts before expecting reliable matching (X requires minimum 100 matched users per audience). As an alternative, prioritize @handle lookalike audiences and keyword targeting, which don't depend on CRM list match rates.
