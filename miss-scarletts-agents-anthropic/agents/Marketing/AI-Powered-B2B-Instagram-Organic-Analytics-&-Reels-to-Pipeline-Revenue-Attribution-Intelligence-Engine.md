## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Instagram Insights shows dramatically different numbers than GA4 for Instagram traffic.**
Solution: This is normal and expected. Instagram Insights tracks in-app behavior (saves, shares, profile visits) while GA4 tracks website sessions. The gap represents the dark funnel — in-app activity that never generates a website session. Stop trying to reconcile the numbers; instead, use Instagram Insights for content performance decisions and GA4 for pipeline attribution. The self-reported attribution survey bridges the two.

**Problem: Save rates are high but no pipeline is being generated — lots of saves, zero demo requests.**
Solution: High save rates without pipeline typically means your content is attracting practitioners (ICP-adjacent) rather than buyers (ICP decision-makers). Audit the last 50 accounts who saved your top Reels — what are their job titles? If you're attracting junior practitioners instead of VP/Director-level buyers, shift your hook language from tactical how-to ("Here's how to run a 1:1") to strategic ("Why your retention numbers won't improve until you change this one thing"). Senior buyers save content about strategic problems; practitioners save tactical how-tos.

**Problem: Leadership wants a single "Instagram pipeline number" but the dark funnel makes this hard to defend.**
Solution: Present a pipeline range rather than a single number: "Instagram organic contributes an estimated $X–$Y in pipeline per quarter. The lower bound ($X) is UTM-tracked and verified in HubSpot. The upper bound ($Y) adds self-reported attribution and applies our 2.7x dark funnel multiplier. We recommend using $X as the conservative figure in board reporting and $Y as the internal planning figure." This framing acknowledges the measurement limitation while making the contribution undeniable even at the low end.
