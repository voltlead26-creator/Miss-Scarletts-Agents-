## Overview
Design and operationalize a complete AI-powered executive sponsor program that assigns your company's C-suite and senior leaders as named sponsors to strategic customer accounts, automates briefing preparation, detects relationship health signals, and triggers executive-level interventions before churn reaches a critical threshold. Use this when your company has enterprise accounts ($50K+ ACV) where CSM-level relationships aren't sufficient to protect ARR, when you've lost strategic accounts because the relationship was too narrow (champion-only), or when you need a systematic way to deploy executive attention at scale across a portfolio of named accounts.

## SECTION 3: EXECUTIVE ENGAGEMENT PLAYBOOKS

**Playbook 1: Scheduled Relationship Touchpoint (No Crisis, Healthy Account)**

Objective: Build strategic rapport, identify expansion signal, reinforce value at the executive level.

Cadence: Tier 1 accounts — every 6 weeks. Tier 2 — every quarter. Tier 3 — every 6 months.

Touchpoint options (in order of relationship depth):
1. Strategic call (30 minutes, video or phone)
2. Conference meeting (when both attend the same industry event)
3. Executive dinner (for Tier 1 accounts, once per year)
4. Written note (email or LinkedIn — for quarter when no call)

**30-Minute Strategic Call Script:**

Opening (5 minutes):
> "[Contact first name], thanks for making time today. I've been following [something specific from their company's recent news or initiative] — sounds like [your team / the business] has been [moving fast / navigating real change / building something impressive]. I wanted to catch up and hear more from your perspective before we dive into the rest of this year."

Transition to listening (15 minutes — sponsor asks, does not present):
> "I want to make sure the time I spend with you is actually useful for your business — not just another vendor check-in. So: as you're heading into [Q3 / H2 / your planning cycle], what are the one or two challenges that are keeping you up at night that [Product] could be doing more to help with? And equally — where do you feel like we've really delivered for your team?"

[Sponsor listens, takes notes, asks follow-up questions. The goal is to surface: (1) unmet needs = expansion opportunity, (2) executive perception of value = renewal confidence signal, (3) internal dynamics = relationship risk flags]

Close with a commitment:
> "This has been really helpful — I want to make sure I act on what I just heard. I'm going to [specific action: share a resource / connect you with our [CPO / Head of Product] who is working on exactly that / have [CSM name] follow up on the [specific thing] you mentioned]. Can I follow up in [2 weeks] to close the loop?"

Post-call action (within 24 hours):
- Log call summary in CRM (sponsor or EA enters within 24 hours)
- Write follow-up email (sponsor sends personally): [Template — 60 words, 2 specific things that were said in the call, one action the sponsor is taking, one ask for information that benefits the customer]
- Update CS ops: flag any expansion signals, health score inputs, or risk flags surfaced in the call

## SECTION 5: PROGRAM MEASUREMENT & ROI REPORTING

**Core Metrics Dashboard — Executive Sponsor Program:**

**Metric 1: Enterprise Renewal Rate — Sponsored vs. Non-Sponsored Accounts**
Definition: Gross renewal rate for accounts in executive sponsor program vs. accounts without a sponsor
Calculation: Separately track renewal outcomes for each cohort; report delta
Benchmark: Expect 6–12 percentage point improvement within 12 months
Alert threshold: If sponsored accounts renew at same rate as non-sponsored — program execution, not design, is failing
Review cadence: Quarterly
Owner: VP Customer Success + CRO

**Metric 2: Executive Touchpoint Completion Rate**
Definition: % of scheduled executive touchpoints that actually occurred (scheduled vs. completed)
Calculation: Completed touchpoints / Scheduled touchpoints × 100
Benchmark: >75% completion rate; below 60% = sponsor bandwidth problem
Alert: <60% → CRO or CEO must address sponsor time availability
Review cadence: Monthly
Owner: CS Operations

**Metric 3: Executive Relationship Score — Portfolio Average**
Definition: Average executive relationship score across all accounts in the program
Calculation: Sum of individual scores / Total accounts × 100
Benchmark: Portfolio average >65; below 50 = systematic relationship deterioration
Alert: 3+ accounts with score <30 in the same month = program crisis indicator
Review cadence: Monthly
Owner: CS Operations

**Metric 4: Expansion Pipeline Generated via Exec Relationships**
Definition: Pipeline from expansion opportunities where executive sponsor was involved in surfacing or initiating the conversation
Calculation: Sum of expansion opportunities in CRM where "Source = Executive Sponsor" or CSM notes document exec involvement
Benchmark: 20–30% of enterprise expansion pipeline should have exec sponsor involvement within 18 months of program launch
Alert: <10% — program is operating as a retention tool only, not expansion driver
Review cadence: Quarterly
Owner: CRO + CS Leadership

**Metric 5: Champion Depth Score (Multi-Threading Index)**
Definition: Average number of executive-level contacts (Director+) per sponsored account
Calculation: Total executive contacts across all sponsored accounts / Total sponsored accounts
Benchmark: Target 2.5+ executive contacts per account; below 1.5 = dangerously single-threaded portfolio
Alert: If average drops below 1.5 — launch systematic multi-threading campaign across portfolio
Review cadence: Quarterly
Owner: CSM team + CS Operations

**Metric 6: Churn Saves Attributed to Executive Intervention**
Definition: Number and ARR value of accounts where churn was prevented and executive intervention was documented as a contributing factor
Calculation: Track all accounts that entered risk intervention, document exec involvement, track outcome
Benchmark: Sponsor intervention should show >65% save rate for accounts where exec engaged (vs. ~40% without exec involvement)
Alert: <50% save rate for exec-intervened accounts — review intervention playbook quality
Review cadence: Quarterly
Owner: VP Customer Success

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Executives commit to the program but don't follow through on touchpoints — completion rate stays below 50%.**
Fix: The root cause is almost always friction, not intention. Reduce friction in three ways: (1) Make the briefing come to them — CS Ops pushes the 5-minute briefing to the exec's email 48 hours before every scheduled touchpoint; do not require the exec to pull it. (2) Make scheduling frictionless — CS Ops owns scheduling by providing 3 specific time options to the customer and handling all back-and-forth; the exec just shows up. (3) Make the calls 25 minutes, not 60 — executives will do a 25-minute strategic conversation quarterly; they will not do a 60-minute QBR. If these three changes don't improve completion, escalate to the CEO: "Our exec sponsor program is protecting $[X]M in ARR. We need [X hours/month] committed from each sponsor or we redesign the portfolio assignments."

**Issue: The executive sponsor outreach to economic buyers gets ignored — no responses to warm outreach attempts.**
Fix: Three possible issues: (1) The executive's message reads like a vendor email, not an executive peer reaching out — rewrite using the templates in this playbook, emphasizing peer-to-peer tone with a real insight, not a check-in. (2) The outreach timing is off — avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons; mid-week mid-day (Tuesday–Thursday, 10am–2pm recipient local time) consistently outperforms. (3) Email isn't the right channel — if the exec sponsor knows anyone in their network connected to the economic buyer, use a warm LinkedIn introduction instead. One warm introduction from a mutual connection outperforms 10 cold executive emails.

**Issue: The program is protecting retention but sponsors are not surfacing expansion opportunities — metric 4 (expansion pipeline from exec relationships) stays below 10%.**
Fix: Sponsors are defaulting to relationship maintenance and avoiding anything that sounds like selling — which is the right instinct. The fix is to equip them with a non-salesy framework for surfacing expansion naturally. Add to every briefing: "Expansion conversation starter." When the account's usage data shows an expansion signal (seat utilization >80%, new department onboarding, feature depth milestone), give the sponsor a specific, data-backed observation to share: "We're seeing [Company]'s team at 88% seat capacity — before you hit that ceiling, I wanted to flag it personally. [CSM name] can walk you through what adding [X] seats looks like — it's designed to be straightforward." The sponsor shares a business observation, not a pitch. When the conversation shifts to specifics, the sponsor hands off to CSM and AE.
