**Value Metric Decision:** Price on active workflows + base platform fee. Employees as the billing unit creates anxiety and discourages adoption (customers hide users). Active workflows directly correlate to value delivered — every workflow running = time saved = willingness to pay.

## Success Metrics

Your pricing architecture is working when:
- **Tier distribution:** 60–70% of new customers land on the middle tier (if <40% land on middle, the anchoring is wrong; if >80%, the enterprise tier is under-priced)
- **Freemium/trial conversion rate:** >25% of trial starters convert to paid within 30 days
- **Usage-based expansion rate:** >30% of Growth customers exceed their tier limit within 6 months (triggering upgrade conversations)
- **NRR improvement:** Packaging restructure should improve NRR by 8–15 points within 12 months

- **Churn reduction:** If churned customers were consistently on the wrong tier (features they didn't use), churn should drop 15–25% post-restructure as better tier-fit improves

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI produces generic tier names (Basic/Pro/Enterprise) and generic feature lists without psychological rationale.**
Fix: Add this constraint to the prompt: "For each tier boundary, explain the specific psychological mechanism (loss aversion, social proof threshold, status signal, productivity cliff, sunk cost) that makes this feature gate effective. Name each tier based on the customer identity/aspiration at that stage, not the feature depth."

**Problem: Output pricing numbers feel arbitrary (e.g., $99/$299/$999) without grounding in value delivered.**
Fix: Before running the prompt, calculate your customer's ROI from the product (time saved × hourly rate, or revenue impact). Add to context: "Customers report saving [X hours/month] at an average fully-loaded cost of [$Y/hour], meaning the product delivers [$Z/month] in quantifiable value. Price tiers should capture 10–20% of delivered value." This grounds the AI's pricing recommendations in willingness-to-pay reality.

**Problem: Migration plan output is too gentle — it doesn't account for customers who will churn on price increase.**
Fix: Add to the prompt: "Segment migration approach into three cohorts: (1) customers whose new price is lower or equal — send a win notification; (2) customers whose new price increases <30% — send value reinforcement + grandfather period; (3) customers whose new price increases >30% — flag for CSM manual outreach with a retention offer. Produce separate messaging for each cohort."
