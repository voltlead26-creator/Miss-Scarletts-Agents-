## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI generates a pricing model that's theoretically correct but operationally impossible for our billing infrastructure.**
Solution: Add a constraint to the prompt: "Our billing system can handle [flat subscription / per-seat / metered usage / one-time charges]. Design the model within these constraints and flag any infrastructure gaps we'll need to address."

**Problem: Output pricing tiers feel generic — not differentiated enough from competitors.**
Solution: In the CONTEXT section, add 3-5 specific customer anecdotes (anonymized) that illustrate the range of value delivered. For example: "Our smallest customer saves 2 hours/week; our largest saves 40 hours/week across a 12-person legal team." This forces the AI to design tiers around real customer segments, not hypothetical ones.

**Problem: The ROI proof architecture produces numbers that don't match our actual customer data.**
Solution: Provide your actual customer data in the prompt: "Our median customer reports [X outcome] in their first 90 days. Our top quartile reports [Y]. Use these as the basis for the ROI model and do not extrapolate beyond 2x our reported median."
