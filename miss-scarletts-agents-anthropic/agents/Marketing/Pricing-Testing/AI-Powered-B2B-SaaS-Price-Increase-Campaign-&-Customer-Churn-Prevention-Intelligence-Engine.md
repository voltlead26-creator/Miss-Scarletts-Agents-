## Overview
Designs the complete marketing and customer communication program for a B2B SaaS price increase — segmenting customers by churn risk and expansion potential, generating the full communication sequence, building champion enablement assets to secure internal buy-in, and deploying retention campaigns for at-risk accounts. Use it whenever you're raising prices on existing customers, migrating from legacy pricing, or transitioning pricing models (per-seat to usage-based, flat-rate to tiered).

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Create a custom object or deal stage called "Price Increase Campaign" to track each account's tier, communication touchpoints, and renewal outcome. Set workflow triggers to auto-enroll accounts in the appropriate email sequence based on health score.
- **Gainsight / ChurnZero / Totango:** Sync customer health scores directly into the segmentation model. Use the CS platform to auto-assign CSM tasks for Tier 3 and 4 intervention calls with pre-populated account context.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Train a Gong tracker for price increase calls to surface common objection patterns in real time. Analyze post-increase call recordings to refine objection scripts for future pricing events.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build the champion enablement one-pager as a templatized Notion document that customer-facing teams can duplicate and populate per account in under 10 minutes.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion is supportive but can't get internal budget approval in time.**
Solution: Offer a 30-day extension on the grandfathering offer contingent on written internal approval (email from their CFO/VP confirming review is underway). This maintains urgency while removing the champion from an impossible position. Document the extension and set a firm final deadline.

**Problem: Multiple customers from the same investor portfolio or the same Slack community are coordinating pushback.**
Solution: Get ahead of this with a proactive group webinar for that customer segment — "Investing in DataStream Pro: What's Next" — led by the CPO. Community-coordinated pushback collapses when customers hear from the product team directly, see the roadmap, and realize peers are renewing. Never respond to coordinated pushback with a unilateral policy exception — it spreads immediately.

**Problem: The AI generates generic value statements that don't resonate with specific accounts.**
Solution: Feed account-specific usage data into the Advanced Version before running: last 90-day workflow run count, top 3 integrations in use, number of team members active, and any outcomes the account has shared in QBRs or NPS responses. The more specific the input, the more compelling the output. For Tier 1 accounts, run the prompt individually per account rather than as a batch.
