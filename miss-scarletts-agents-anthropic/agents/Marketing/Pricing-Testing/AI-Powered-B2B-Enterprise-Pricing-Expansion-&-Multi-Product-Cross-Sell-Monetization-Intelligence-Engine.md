## Integration Tips

- **Gainsight / ChurnZero:** Create Health Score rules that fire when usage signals cross expansion thresholds. Route "marketing expansion eligible" accounts into Marketo or HubSpot for automated campaign enrollment rather than manual CS outreach — this scales expansion without adding CS headcount.
- **Salesforce:** Create a custom Opportunity Type = "Expansion" with sub-types (Tier Upgrade, Module Add-On, Seat Expansion). Map marketing campaign source to expansion opportunities using a Campaign Influence model. This lets you report marketing-attributed NRR to the board.
- **HubSpot / Marketo:** Build a dedicated Expansion Revenue workflow list with enrollment trigger = "Usage signal threshold met" (synced from product via API or Segment). Suppress accounts that are already in active expansion conversations with CS or AE to avoid conflicting outreach.
- **Stripe / Chargebee:** Configure in-app upgrade prompts to fire when self-serve customers hit 85% of their tier limit. Use Stripe Checkout for frictionless self-serve upgrades under $5K ACV — remove the sales call requirement for this segment. Track Stripe upgrade events back to Salesforce via Zapier or a native connector.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Mine expansion conversations for objection patterns. Feed common objections back into the business case template and campaign copy quarterly. - **Segment / Amplitude:** Use product analytics to build cohort analyses showing which feature adoption patterns predict 12-month NRR > 120%. Feed these cohort definitions into your CRM as a dynamic "Expansion Ready" segment that auto-enrolls in marketing sequences.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Expansion campaigns feel like generic upsell emails and champions are ignoring them.**
Solution: The issue is almost always that the trigger signal is not personalized enough in the email copy. Make the first line of every expansion email reference the specific usage milestone that triggered it — "You've reviewed 47 deals in Helix this month" beats "We noticed you're getting value from our platform." Pull the actual usage number from the product data layer into the email via personalization token. This one change typically improves open rates by 35-50% on expansion sequences.

**Problem: Economic buyers aren't engaging even when the business case is sent.**
Solution: Economic buyers respond to peer benchmarks, not product features. Replace the feature list in your Touch 3 email with a single sentence: "Companies at your stage ([ARR range], [vertical]) that added [module] saw [specific outcome] within [timeframe] — here's the data." Source this from your customer success team's QBR notes or from a quick survey of 5 expansion customers. One real data point from a peer is worth 10 feature bullets.

**Problem: CS team says marketing expansion campaigns are creating confusion with their own renewal outreach.**
Solution: Build a shared "Account in Flight" suppression list between marketing and CS. Any account where CS has logged a renewal or expansion activity in Gainsight in the past 30 days is suppressed from marketing-triggered expansion campaigns. Create a weekly sync meeting between CS ops and marketing ops to align on account ownership. Establish clear rules: marketing owns accounts with <$50K ACV expansion potential; CS + AE jointly own accounts above that threshold. Document this in a Revenue Expansion Rules of Engagement document and get VP CS and CMO sign-off.
