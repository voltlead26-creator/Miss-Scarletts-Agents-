## Overview
Designs a research-backed product packaging architecture that segments features across tiers, engineers feature gates that create organic upgrade pressure, and builds an expansion revenue flywheel. Use this when your current pricing tiers feel arbitrary, when free or starter users aren't converting to paid, or when enterprise ACV is flat because deals aren't expanding post-land.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Gates are triggering but conversion to paid is below 5%**
Solution: The gate is creating friction but not articulating value. Audit the in-app upgrade message — it likely describes the missing feature but not the business outcome the feature enables. Rewrite using the formula: "You've hit [limit]. Users at your stage who upgrade to [tier] see [specific business outcome, e.g., '23% more pipeline generated from automation']. Upgrade now for $[incremental cost]/month."

**Problem: Enterprise buyers are landing on Business/Scale tier and never signing MSAs**
Solution: This is the "enterprise cannibalization" packaging flaw. Move SSO/SAML, SCIM provisioning, audit logs, and DPA behind a hard Enterprise-only gate with no self-serve option. Enterprise buyers will not go live without these — they will have to engage sales to get access, which is the conversion event you need.

**Problem: Existing customers are churning after packaging changes because they lost features they had**
Solution: Never remove features from existing customers without a grandfather period and clear communication. Run a "grandfather + upgrade incentive" motion: keep existing customers on their current entitlements for 6-12 months, send a proactive email at month 3 explaining the new value they'd get by upgrading, and offer a 20% first-year discount if they upgrade before the grandfather period ends. This turns a churn risk into an expansion event.
