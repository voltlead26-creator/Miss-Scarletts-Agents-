# DELIVERABLES

## 1. COMMUNICATION TIMELINE & CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE
Design a 90-day communication sequence with:
- Day 0: Internal team briefing (what to say, what not to say, FAQ for CS)
- Day 1: Tier 3 proactive outreach (CS-led calls before announcement)
- Day 7: Official announcement email to all affected customers
- Day 30: Feature spotlight email — reinforce value added since last price change
- Day 60: Reminder email + FAQ link
- Day 75: Final notice + "lock in current pricing" call-to-action for annual upgrade
- Day 90: Price goes live — transition acknowledgment email

For each touchpoint: sender, subject line, key message, CTA, and which customer tiers receive it.

## 2. ANNOUNCEMENT EMAIL SEQUENCE (3 VERSIONS)
Write complete emails for three tiers:

**Version A — Tier 1 Champions:**
Lead with expansion opportunity. Acknowledge their partnership. Present price increase as investment in their growth. Offer multi-year pricing lock with added value (bonus seats, priority support, executive QBR).

**Version B — Tier 2 Standard:**
Professional, transparent, value-forward. Lead with what's new in the product. Acknowledge the increase is meaningful. Provide clear effective date and FAQ link. No retention offer unless they ask.

**Version C — Tier 3 At-Risk:**
Personalized (CSM-sent, not marketing blast). Acknowledge their account status. Lead with "we want to make sure you're getting full value." Book a call before the announcement. Retention offer inside the call, not in email.

For each email: Subject line (A/B option), opening paragraph, value narrative (3 bullet points), price change disclosure, CTA, signature block.

## 3. RETENTION OFFER MENU (5 OPTIONS)
Design 5 retention offers in order of margin impact. For each offer:
- Offer name and positioning statement
- What we give up (cost) vs. what we gain (retention/expansion value)
- Which customer tier it's designed for
- How to present it verbally and in writing
- Guardrails (when NOT to offer this — protecting margin)

Options should include:
- Multi-year lock-in at pre-increase pricing (no discount — just grandfather)
- Seat reduction + success plan (right-size rather than churn)
- Feature upgrade at same price (move to higher tier without price increase)
- Payment plan flexibility (quarterly billing for annual ARR accounts)
- Co-marketing / case study partnership trade for pricing concession

## 4. CS TEAM CALL SCRIPT & OBJECTION HANDLING
Create a complete call guide for customer success managers:

**Opening script** for proactive Tier 3 calls (before announcement goes out)

**Objection response matrix** — for each objection, provide:
- What the customer says (verbatim)
- What they REALLY mean (emotional subtext)
- Ideal response (acknowledge → reframe → resolve)
- Escalation path if unresolved

Required objections to address:
1. "This is too expensive — we're already stretched"
2. "We're not using all the features we're paying for now"
3. "Your competitor [X] is cheaper"
4. "We just renewed 6 months ago — this feels unfair"
5. "I need to take this to my CFO/VP — it's a budget conversation"
6. "We'll just cancel and come back later at new customer pricing"
7. "What did you actually ship to justify this?"

## 5. FAQ DOCUMENT (CUSTOMER-FACING)
Write a complete FAQ for your help center / customer portal. Include:
- Why are you raising prices?
- When does the new pricing take effect?
- How does this affect my current contract?
- What new features/value have you added?
- Can I lock in my current rate?
- What if I want to reduce my seats instead?
- How do I contact you with questions?
- What happens if I don't respond before the effective date?

Tone: Transparent, direct, empathetic. No corporate doublespeak.

## 6. INTERNAL ENABLEMENT PACKAGE
Create the internal brief for CS, sales, and support:
- Key messages and approved language
- Things NOT to say (and why)
- Competitive talking points if customers threaten to switch
- Escalation matrix (who approves what exceptions)
- Win/loss tracking template for monitoring churn during transition
- Weekly metrics to report to leadership (churn risk accounts, save rate, ARR at risk)

## 7. WIN-BACK CAMPAIGN (POST-CHURN)
For customers who churn during the transition:
- 30-day post-churn email (acknowledge, don't grovel)
- 90-day check-in (product update, no pressure)
- 6-month re-engagement offer (time-limited return incentive)

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Customers are threatening mass cancellation and it's going viral on social/G2/Glassdoor.**
Fix: Price increase backlash follows a predictable pattern — it's loudest in days 1-14 and fades by day 45 if you respond well. Do NOT discount publicly or it signals that the first price was wrong. Instead: (1) Post a transparent "why we raised prices" blog post signed by your CEO with specific numbers — customers respect honesty more than apology; (2) Offer a 1:1 call with any customer who posts publicly — resolution calls almost always end positively; (3) Add a visible product changelog so customers can see what they're getting for the new price; (4) Never price-match publicly in response to complaints — handle exceptions privately and only for accounts worth saving.

**Issue: Our CS team is too small to handle proactive Tier 3 outreach before the announcement.**
Fix: Prioritize by ARR-at-risk × churn probability × strategic value. If you have 50 at-risk accounts and 2 CSMs, focus the top 20 accounts (likely 70-80% of the at-risk ARR). For the remaining 30, use a highly personalized email template that mimics a CSM-written note — segmented by use case and referencing their specific product usage. Automate the send via your CRM but write it to sound 1:1. Pair it with a Calendly link for self-serve call booking. Track open rate and click rate; CSMs follow up manually on anyone who clicks but doesn't book.

**Issue: Our CFO wants to accelerate the timeline from 90 days to 30 days to hit Q2 revenue targets.**
Fix: A 30-day notice period for significant price increases (>20%) creates legal exposure in some geographies (UK, EU) and destroys customer trust. Negotiate internally: offer a middle path of 60 days with a "lock in current pricing" annual upgrade offer that accelerates the revenue impact within 30 days. Model it out — if 15% of ARR converts to annual at current pricing, the NRR impact often exceeds what you'd gain from forcing the 30-day increase on everyone. Present the comparison to your CFO with specific numbers.
