## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Use Contact-based multi-touch attribution reports under Reports → Attribution to see which content assets are generating most first-touch and last-touch contacts. Build custom dashboards with "Content Asset" as the dimension. Set up Revenue Attribution Reports under CRM → Reports to connect content to closed deals.
- **Salesforce**: Create a custom "Marketing Content Touches" related list on the Opportunity object. Use Salesforce Campaign Influence to track which Campaigns (each major content asset should be a Salesforce Campaign) are influencing open and closed opportunities. Build Pipeline Influence reports filtered by Campaign Type = Content.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Set up Content Groups to segment performance by content type (blog, case study, webinar, comparison). Use Explorations → Funnel Exploration to visualize the path from content consumption to conversion event. Export to Looker Studio for custom ROI dashboard.
- **Looker Studio / Google Data Studio**: Connect GA4 + HubSpot (via Supermetrics or HubSpot connector) + Salesforce (via Coefficient or native connector) to build the executive content ROI dashboard that auto-refreshes daily.
- **Ahrefs / SEMrush**: Export top-ranking pages monthly, cross-reference with HubSpot to see which top-traffic pages are generating MQLs — this surfaces the "hidden value" SEO content that drives pipeline but doesn't get credited in last-touch models.
- **Notion / ClickUp**: Use the Content Portfolio Matrix as a Notion database with formula columns that auto-calculate ROI for each asset based on monthly pipeline data pulled from CRM.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: HubSpot shows content-sourced MQLs but Salesforce shows different pipeline numbers, creating a discrepancy the CFO questions.**
Solution: The disconnect happens because HubSpot and Salesforce count revenue attribution differently. Align on a single system of record for content ROI reporting (we recommend Salesforce for revenue numbers, HubSpot for contact-level content attribution). Build the bridge by exporting HubSpot content attribution by Contact, then joining to Salesforce Opportunity via Contact ID. Use this joined dataset as the source of truth, not either system individually.

**Problem: Most pipeline appears as "direct / none" or "unattributed" in both GA4 and HubSpot, making it impossible to credit content properly.**
Solution: This is the dark funnel problem. First, audit your UTM implementation — most "direct" traffic is actually misattributed paid or social traffic. Second, implement self-reported attribution: add a "How did you first hear about us?" field on all demo request and contact forms, with options that map to content types (organic search, read your blog, saw a LinkedIn post, colleague recommended, watched your webinar). Third, use HubSpot's Original Source data (not just latest source) and build reports on first-touch vs. last-touch separately. Accept that 20–30% of pipeline will remain unattributed and model it as "proportional attribution" based on the distribution of what you can attribute.

**Problem: The content team feels demoralized because TOFU content (which builds brand and drives long-term organic) never shows up in pipeline reports, creating pressure to only produce BOFU content.**
Solution: Create a separate "content brand equity" metric that captures the long-term value of awareness content: (branded search volume growth + organic traffic growth + referring domain growth) vs. prior year, translated into equivalent paid media cost (if you had to buy this traffic at your average paid CPC, what would it cost?). Report this alongside pipeline attribution so that awareness-building content gets credit for the "free pipeline" it's generating over its lifetime — not just the MQLs it creates directly.
