## Integration Tips

**Salesforce Integration:**
- Build a "Waterfall Audit Dashboard" in Salesforce Reports using the standard Campaign Influence and Lead Conversion reports — set up a rolling 90-day view that refreshes weekly
- Use Salesforce Flow to auto-log a "Waterfall Audit" custom object whenever an MQL is rejected, capturing the rejection reason, time-to-contact, and lead score at rejection — this creates an audit trail for diagnosing scoring problems
- Enable "Lead Conversion Analytics" in Salesforce to track the exact path from lead creation to closed revenue — requires Lead Source field integrity governance

**HubSpot Integration:**
- Create a HubSpot Custom Report: "MQL-to-SAL Pipeline Waterfall" → plot Contact Stage → MQL Date → Sales Activity Date → Opportunity Created Date → Deal Closed Date on a single timeline per contact
- Use HubSpot Workflows to set a "First Contact Timestamp" custom property that fires when an SDR logs the first call or email — this lets you calculate speed-to-lead programmatically without manual data entry
- Build a HubSpot Dashboard with four tiles: (1) Weekly MQL volume, (2) MQL→SAL % trend, (3) Speed-to-first-contact histogram, (4) Pipeline-per-MQL rolling average

**Outreach/SalesLoft Integration:**
- Pull sequence reply rates by MQL lead score decile — this reveals whether high-scored leads actually respond better (if not, your scoring model is broken)
- Use Outreach Analytics to identify which SDRs have the highest MQL→SAL rates — interview them to systematize their qualification approach into training for the team

**Marketo Integration:**
- Run Marketo's Program Performance Report filtered to MQL-tagged leads — cross-reference which programs produce the highest MQL-to-SAL conversion rates (not just MQL volume)
- Set up Marketo Smart Campaigns to recalculate lead scores nightly and trigger Salesforce task creation the moment a score crosses MQL threshold — eliminates the lag between score threshold and SDR notification

**BI/Data Warehouse:**
- If you have Snowflake or BigQuery, build a waterfall cohort table that tracks every lead from source through close by week-of-MQL-creation — this is the cleanest way to see whether waterfall efficiency is improving over time, not just snapshot-to-snapshot

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Sales team argues every rejected MQL was legitimately unqualified**
Root cause: No objective definition of what "qualified" means — SDRs using subjective judgment.
Fix: Define MQL criteria as a binding SLA in the Marketing-Sales Agreement. Require SDRs to select from a dropdown of pre-approved rejection reasons (not free text). Add a "Disputed MQL" process: if Marketing disagrees with a rejection, the deal goes to a third-party review (RevOps). Run quarterly MQL audit: pull a random sample of rejected leads 6 months later — how many ended up closing with a competitor? That's your cost-of-over-rejection.

**Issue: Waterfall metrics look healthy but revenue isn't meeting targets**
Root cause: Volume at top of funnel is disguising a quality collapse — more leads are entering the funnel but fewer of them have real purchase intent. Total conversion rates average out, hiding the deterioration.
Fix: Segment your waterfall by lead source and calculate pipeline-per-MQL by channel. You'll find that one or two channels (often paid social or content syndication) are generating high MQL volume but near-zero revenue. Their "healthy" volume is masking the low performance of high-value channels. Cut or reallocate budget from low-revenue-per-MQL channels and re-examine what those MQLs actually look like vs. your ICP.

**Issue: Attribution data contradicts what SDRs say is generating pipeline**
Root cause: CRM lead source field is being overwritten when leads convert or when SDRs update contact records — marketing attribution history is being destroyed.
Fix: Immediately lock the Lead Source field in Salesforce/HubSpot so only Marketing Ops can modify it after creation. Add an "Original Lead Source" field (write once, never overwrite) that preserves the first-touch attribution. Add a separate "SDR Sourced" boolean field for deals originated through outbound — this creates additive attribution (both marketing and SDR get credit) rather than overwriting marketing source with outbound.
