# Product Launch Go-To-Market Blueprint - Full-Stack Launch Orchestration Prompt

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** gtm, product-launch, b2b, b2c, strategy, cross-functional

## Overview
Orchestrates a complete product or feature launch from positioning through post-launch optimization. Use this when you have a launch date on the calendar and need to align messaging, channels, audiences, and timelines into a single coherent plan that won't embarrass you in front of the board.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a seasoned go-to-market strategist with 15+ years launching B2B SaaS and consumer products. I am launching [Product/Feature Name] on [Launch Date].

Here is what I know:
- Product: [2-3 sentence description of what it does]
- Primary buyer: [Job title / persona]
- Core problem solved: [Pain point in one sentence]
- Pricing: [Free / $X/mo / Enterprise]
- Competitors: [2-3 main alternatives]
- Our differentiator: [Why us, not them]

Build me a complete go-to-market launch plan that includes:
1. POSITIONING STATEMENT using the Geoffrey Moore template (For [target customer] who [has this problem], [Product] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [competitor], our product [differentiator].)
2. LAUNCH TIER classification (Tier 1 = major public launch, Tier 2 = feature release, Tier 3 = beta/soft launch) with rationale
3. AUDIENCE PRIORITY MATRIX: rank these segments by conversion probability for Day 1 — existing customers, warm prospects, cold ICP, press/analysts, partners
4. 30/60/90-DAY MESSAGING ARC: what story do we tell in each phase and why it changes
5. CHANNEL PLAYBOOK: for each channel (email, social, paid, PR, in-app, partner), give the specific action, timing relative to launch day, and one success metric
6. LAUNCH DAY MINUTE-BY-MINUTE CHECKLIST: first 4 hours only
7. RISK REGISTER: top 5 launch risks with mitigation tactics
8. SUCCESS DEFINITION: primary KPI at Day 7, Day 30, and Day 90

Format as a structured document with clear headers. Be specific — no vague advice.

DELIVERABLES REQUIRED:

**Section 1: Strategic Foundation**
- One-sentence positioning statement (Moore template)
- Three proof points that validate the positioning (must be verifiable, not aspirational)
- Category naming recommendation: enter existing [category] OR create new category called [X]
- Persona priority order with reasoning (which segment converts fastest given our GTM motion)

**Section 2: Messaging Architecture**
- Hero headline (10 words max, outcome-focused)
- Sub-headline that handles the #1 objection before it's raised
- Three-tier message hierarchy: (1) What it is, (2) Why it matters, (3) Why us
- Before/After narrative using the Jobs-to-be-Done framework
- Three customer segments x three messages = message matrix (9 cells)

**Section 3: Channel Execution Plan**
For EACH channel below, provide: Action, Timing (T-30, T-7, T-1, Launch Day, T+7, T+30), Owner (role), KPI, and Example copy/creative brief:
- Email (existing customers, trial users, cold outbound)
- LinkedIn (organic + paid)
- Twitter/X
- Product Hunt (if applicable)
- PR/Media outreach (top 5 target publications)
- In-app notifications / onboarding flow
- Partner/reseller communications
- Community (Slack communities, Reddit, Discord)
- Paid search (branded + competitor terms)
- Retargeting

**Section 4: Launch Sequence**
- T-30 days: Internal alignment checklist (sales, CS, support, product)
- T-14 days: Asset production checklist
- T-7 days: Soft launch/embargo lift activities
- T-1 day: Final go/no-go criteria (what would make you delay?)
- Launch Day: Minute-by-minute schedule for first 4 hours
- T+7 days: Momentum maintenance plan
- T+30 days: Retrospective framework

**Section 5: Sales Enablement Package**
- One-page battle card (us vs. top 2 competitors)
- Top 5 objections with tested responses
- Discovery questions that surface the buying trigger
- Demo flow recommendation (problem to solution to ROI)
- Pricing conversation guide

**Section 6: Metrics & Decision Framework**
- North Star metric for this launch
- Leading indicators (Week 1 signals that predict 90-day success)
- Lagging indicators (what we will measure at Day 90)
- Decision tree: if [metric] is below [threshold] at Day 7, then [specific action]
- Board-ready launch summary template (5 slides max)

CONSTRAINTS:
- Every recommendation must be executable by the team size specified
- Do not recommend tactics that require more than 2 weeks to implement unless flagged as "post-launch investment"
- Prioritize ruthlessly — if budget is constrained, tell me what to cut first
- Flag any assumption you are making about the market or the company

OUTPUT FORMAT: Use clear headers, numbered lists for sequences, and tables for the message matrix and channel plan. Include one "CMO Hot Take" callout box per section with the strategic insight most marketers miss.

**Day 1 Audience Priority:**
1. Warm pipeline — 23 deals in "legal review" stage in CRM; SDR sequence starts T-3 days

## Success Metrics
- Launch week: demo request volume vs. target, share of voice in target community channels, press pickup count
- Day 30: pipeline generated (attributed), trial-to-paid conversion rate, win rate on competitive deals since launch
- Day 90: ARR added from launch cohort, NPS of customers acquired in launch quarter, sales cycle length vs. pre-launch baseline
- Qualitative: did sales actually use the battle cards? (track in CRM deal notes)

## Integration Tips
- **HubSpot:** Create a launch campaign in HubSpot with UTM parameters for every channel. Use the workflow builder to route demo requests from launch traffic to a dedicated SDR queue with a 15-minute SLA.
- **Salesforce:** Add a "Launch Source" field to opportunities to track pipeline attribution 90 days out. Build a launch dashboard with pipeline by source, stage velocity, and win rate.
- **Notion:** Use the channel playbook output as a Notion database with Owner, Status, Launch Date, and KPI columns. Assign DRIs in the first team sync.
- **Slack:** Create a #launch-war-room channel and post the minute-by-minute checklist as a Slack canvas. Use a launch-day bot to ping owners at each milestone.
- **Product Hunt:** Schedule the submission for 12:01 AM PST and prepare a maker comment thread with answers to the top 10 anticipated questions. Secure the hunter relationship T-14 days minimum.
- **Zapier:** Automate: new demo request form submission creates a HubSpot contact, notifies the SDR in Slack, and adds a row to the launch tracking spreadsheet in Google Sheets.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The positioning statement sounds like every competitor's positioning.**
Solution: You have not found your real differentiator yet. Run the "5 Whys" on your unfair advantage. Ask your 3 best customers: "Why would you be devastated if we disappeared?" Their answer is your positioning. Do not write copy until you have that answer.

**Problem: The launch plan is too ambitious for the team size.**
Solution: Run the "launch MVP" cut. Identify the one channel that reaches the most ICP with the least effort — usually email to warm list plus one social channel plus PR pitch to top 3 journalists. Execute those three flawlessly. Add channels at T+30 when you have momentum data.

**Problem: Sales is not using the battle cards or launch messaging.**
Solution: Launch materials fail when sales is not in the room when they are created. Run a 30-minute "message co-creation" session with your top 3 AEs before finalizing anything. Get their objection list. Use their exact language in the battle card. They will use something they helped write.
