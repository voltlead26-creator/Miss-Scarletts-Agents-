## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Output produces generic security/compliance advice without AI-specific concerns**
*Fix*: Add specific context to the prompt: "Our buyers are in [regulated industry] and have [specific AI governance policy — e.g., 'a formal AI review committee that evaluates all models against the NIST AI RMF']. Include concerns specific to this regulatory environment and governance structure." The prompt must know the regulatory and policy context to produce AI-specific (not generic vendor risk) objection handling.

**Problem: The "native AI" competitive positioning feels weak or unconvincing**
*Fix*: Add your actual accuracy benchmark data: "In head-to-head testing vs. [Competitor]'s AI on [specific metric], our AI achieves [X%] vs. their [Y%]. Base the competitive positioning on this specific benchmark." Generic "we're more specialized" arguments are weak — the output needs real, defensible performance claims to be credible with technical enterprise buyers.

**Problem: The pilot program design is too complex for a resource-constrained customer success team to actually deliver**
*Fix*: Add a constraint: "We have [N] Customer Success Managers and [N] implementation engineers. The pilot program must be executable with [X hours/week of CS time] per customer. Scale down the program accordingly and prioritize the 2-3 milestones with the highest conversion impact." The prompt will produce a leaner pilot design that maximizes value per CS hour.
