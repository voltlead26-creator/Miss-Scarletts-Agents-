## Overview
Audits what a founder has built in go-to-market, extracts and codifies the winning patterns, and produces a complete blueprint for transitioning from founder-sourced pipeline to a scalable, marketing-led revenue motion. Use this when you've just hired (or are about to hire) your first marketing leader, when founder deal-sourcing is becoming the growth bottleneck, or when you need to turn founder market knowledge into repeatable marketing playbooks before it walks out the door.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot CRM:** Set up lead source tracking from Day 1 with these specific values: `founder-sourced`, `marketing-inbound`, `marketing-outbound`, `referral-partner`, `referral-customer`, `event`. This is the only way to measure the transition — without it you're flying blind. Create a custom property `pipeline_source_detail` that captures the specific touch (e.g., "CEO LinkedIn post → demo request").
- **LinkedIn:** Use LinkedIn's built-in UTM tracking on all posts (using link shorteners with tracking) to attribute website visits and form fills to specific LinkedIn content pieces. This lets you prove which topics and formats generate MQLs, not just engagement.
- **Salesforce:** If using Salesforce, build a custom pipeline source dashboard that shows pipeline $, win rate, and average ACV by source — updated weekly. Share this with the board to make the transition progress visible and reduce pressure to revert to founder-led selling.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Create a "Founder GTM Knowledge Base" — a living document that captures: the founder's best sales stories, objection-handling scripts, relationship maps (which VCs, analysts, and partners to tap), and market insight memos. This knowledge base is the foundation for all content, sales enablement, and positioning work.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Record all founder sales calls from Month 1 onward. Use Gong's Topic Tracker to identify the exact words, questions, and framings that correlate with "Meeting Scheduled" and "Verbal Commit" outcomes. This is the raw material for scalable sales enablement.
- **Loom:** Have the founder record short "founder knowledge capture" videos (10-15 minutes each) on: why they started the company, how they think about the market, the top 3 customer success stories, and how they handle the top 3 objections. These videos become training assets for new sales reps and input for content marketers.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate founder LinkedIn follower → CRM enrichment pipeline: when someone follows the founder AND visits the website, automatically create a CRM record with company-level intent data appended via Clearbit or Apollo. This captures dark social pipeline that currently goes untracked.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The founder says "I'll step back from sales when we have enough pipeline" — but the pipeline never feels like enough, and the transition never happens.**
Solution: This is the most common failure mode. Fix it with a "transition trigger" agreement, not a vague promise. In your first 90-day plan, define the specific metric that triggers the transition: "When marketing-sourced pipeline reaches $200K/month for two consecutive months, the founder will stop taking first demos for deals under $30K ACV." Put this in writing, shared with the board. Without a predefined trigger, the founder's identity and risk aversion will always find a reason to stay involved.

**Problem: Sales rejects marketing-sourced MQLs at a high rate (>40%), saying "these aren't real leads," and the marketing team gets demoralized.**
Solution: This rejection rate is usually a symptom of an undefined MQL standard, not bad leads. Run a "lead audit" — pull 20 rejected MQLs and have the sales leader score them against the ICP criteria in Section 8 of this prompt. In most cases, 30-50% will actually be good leads that were rejected due to SDR laziness or poor CRM hygiene. For the truly bad leads: identify the specific ICP mismatch (wrong company size? wrong title? wrong industry?) and update the MQL qualification criteria. Then rebuild the MQL-to-SQL SLA document together in a joint sales-marketing workshop — sales ownership of the criteria dramatically increases acceptance rates.

**Problem: The first marketing hire is producing content and campaigns but cannot demonstrate pipeline impact after 60 days, and the CEO is questioning the investment.**
Solution: A marketing hire's pipeline impact is almost never visible in 60 days for B2B with a 60-180 day sales cycle. The solution is leading indicators, not lagging ones. Establish these Month 1 metrics: number of MQLs generated, website sessions from target ICP accounts (using Clearbit or 6sense reveal), LinkedIn organic reach increase, and email open/click rates on outbound sequences. Present these to the CEO weekly. By Month 3, you should start seeing SQLs and pipeline; by Month 6, you can calculate a preliminary marketing CAC. If there are zero leading indicator improvements at 60 days, the issue is strategy (wrong channels, wrong ICP targeting), not time horizon.
