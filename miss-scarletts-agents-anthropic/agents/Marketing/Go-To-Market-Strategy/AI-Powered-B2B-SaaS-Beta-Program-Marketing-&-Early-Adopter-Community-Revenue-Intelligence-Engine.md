## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Create a custom "Beta Candidate" object with scoring fields (advocacy score, use case alignment, implementation velocity). Use workflow automation to route candidates above threshold to design partner pipeline and track beta program status as a custom deal stage with conversion metrics.
- **Customer.io / Braze / Intercom:** Trigger the 30-day onboarding sequence directly from product activation events (first login, first key action, first outcome milestone). Set behavioral triggers — if a user misses a milestone by Day 3, fire a nudge sequence automatically before a CSM needs to intervene manually.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build the feedback synthesis database as a shared PMM + PM workspace. Use AI summarization to auto-categorize weekly feedback into: PMF signals, feature requests, messaging insights, proof content. This eliminates the 4-hour manual synthesis session most PMMs suffer through each week.
- **G2 / TrustRadius:** Schedule automated review request emails at measured peak satisfaction moments (e.g., Day 21 after first meaningful outcome milestone, not Day 3 after signup). G2's Campaign Manager allows you to track review completion status and trigger polite follow-ups without manual tracking.
- **Gong / Chorus / Clari:** Use AI call analysis to automatically surface the most quotable moments from weekly feedback calls. Set up a "beta quote bank" smart filter that flags high-sentiment moments and delivers a digest weekly — eliminates manual call review for proof content mining.
- **Slack:** Create a private #beta-founding-team Slack channel as the primary community space for design partners. The social identity of being in an exclusive, named founding group dramatically increases engagement, retention, and willingness to participate in advocacy programs.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Beta seats aren't filling — recruitment emails generating low response rates**
Solution: The most common cause is recruiting for product fit instead of advocacy fit. Revisit your scoring rubric and check whether you're prioritizing use case alignment over advocacy potential — flip the weighting. Also audit your recruitment copy: if it leads with "try our new feature," rewrite it to lead with "we want your expertise." If fill rate is still below 50% after copy revision, add direct AE involvement — have account executives personally invite their top accounts for the first 5 design partner seats. Personal invitations from a known relationship convert 4–6x better than PMM-sent cold recruitment emails.

**Problem: Beta participants are engaging with the product but not converting to paid at target rate**
Solution: Check the timing of the first commercial conversation — most beta programs introduce pricing too late (week 8 of 10), when inertia and budget cycles have already set in. Move the first commercial touchpoint to week 4–5, when product enthusiasm is highest and the "aha moment" is freshest. Also verify the offer structure: if beta-exclusive pricing expires at GA but AEs haven't communicated a hard deadline, participants will default to waiting. Make the expiration date explicit and concrete. If the issue persists, escalate to VP of Sales — a beta program that doesn't convert is a sales process problem, not a marketing problem.

**Problem: Feedback is largely positive but PMF score is below 40% threshold**
Solution: This pattern almost always means either (1) your beta population skews toward polite early adopters who underreport dissatisfaction, or (2) the product has genuine engagement gaps that participants aren't volunteering. Run a structured "friction mapping" interview: "Walk me through the last time you sat down to use [product]. Where did you stop, and why?" Listen for the moments they don't mention, not the ones they do. If PMF is genuinely below threshold after honest diagnosis, delay GA — shipping to market with sub-40% PMF produces negative reviews within 90 days that permanently damage launch momentum and can cost 12–18 months of reputation recovery.
