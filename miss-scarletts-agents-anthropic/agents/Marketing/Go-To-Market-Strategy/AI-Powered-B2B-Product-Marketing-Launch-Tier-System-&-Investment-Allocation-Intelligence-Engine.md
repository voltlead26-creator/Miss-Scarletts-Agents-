## Overview
This engine builds a systematic launch tier classification framework that tells your product marketing team exactly how much investment each feature, product update, or new product deserves — and produces a ready-to-execute playbook for each tier. Use it when you're managing a continuous stream of product releases and need to allocate scarce marketing resources with precision instead of gut feel.

DELIVERABLE 1 — LAUNCH CLASSIFICATION RUBRIC

Design a 5-dimension scoring model (0-10 per dimension, max 50 points) that places every release into the correct tier:

DIMENSION 1: REVENUE EXPANSION POTENTIAL
- 8-10: Creates a new market segment or buyer persona; projected to contribute 15%+ of next quarter's new logo pipeline
- 5-7: Opens cross-sell opportunity to 30%+ of existing customer base; meaningful upsell path
- 3-4: Valued by some customers, incremental ARR impact expected but not forecasted
- 0-2: Internal tooling, infrastructure, or compliance requirement with no direct revenue linkage

DIMENSION 2: CUSTOMER RETENTION VALUE
- 8-10: Directly addresses the top reason customers churn; customer health scores improve on adoption
- 5-7: Eliminates a persistent support request or friction point for 20%+ of customers
- 3-4: Nice-to-have for power users; reduces friction but doesn't change renewal math
- 0-2: Backend improvement; customers won't notice or won't care

DIMENSION 3: COMPETITIVE MOAT IMPACT
- 8-10: True market-first capability; nearest competitor is 6+ months away; creates "only we can do this" narrative
- 5-7: Meaningfully differentiating against 2+ named competitors; worth highlighting in battle cards
- 3-4: Closes a competitive gap; parity feature that removes a common objection
- 0-2: Industry standard feature; presence expected; absence = risk; announcement = noise

DIMENSION 4: STRATEGIC NARRATIVE ALIGNMENT
- 8-10: Anchors or advances the company's primary market narrative; CEO would present this at a keynote
- 5-7: Supports a secondary product pillar; reinforces positioning in 1-2 market segments
- 3-4: Category-consistent but not narrative-driving; fits the product direction without advancing the story
- 0-2: Standalone feature; no narrative connection; exists because a customer asked for it

DIMENSION 5: MARKET EDUCATION REQUIRED
- 8-10: Buyers don't know they need this yet; requires category creation or heavy education content
- 5-7: Solution-aware buyers exist; need to understand why THIS solution is better/different
- 3-4: Problem-aware buyers; standard demand gen content sufficient
- 0-2: Purchase-ready buyers exist; feature announcement in release notes is sufficient

TIER THRESHOLDS:
- Tier 1 (Major Launch): 35-50 points — Full company launch
- Tier 2 (Significant Release): 20-34 points — Segment-targeted launch
- Tier 3 (Feature Announcement): 10-19 points — Lightweight release communication
- Tier 4 (Release Note): 0-9 points — Changelog only, no PMM involvement

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Product managers consistently over-score their own features, inflating them to T1/T2 when they should be T3/T4.**
Solution: Add a "calibration check" step where PMM scores the feature independently before seeing the PM's self-assessment. When scores diverge by more than 15 points, schedule a 30-minute alignment call. Over time, publish anonymized examples of past launches with final scores to help PMs calibrate — "This feature you thought was T1 scored 22 and ran as a T2; here's what a real T1 looks like by contrast."

**Problem: The system breaks down during high-velocity launch periods (e.g., major annual product release) when 5+ features ship simultaneously.**
Solution: Introduce a "Launch Cluster" rule: when 3+ features ship within a 2-week window, group them into a single themed launch brief ("The Spring Release") and score the CLUSTER collectively. This prevents T3 features from diluting a T1 narrative and allows you to activate the appropriate tier's investment for the entire batch rather than launching each feature individually.

**Problem: Sales leadership bypasses the system and demands full PMM support for a customer-requested feature that scored T3.**
Solution: Honor the request — but make the investment visible. Create a simple "Launch Investment Request" form that sales can submit to request a tier upgrade for a strategic deal; require VP-level approval and log it with a reason code. Track these exceptions quarterly. If a pattern emerges (e.g., sales consistently upgrades features for enterprise deals), that's a signal to add a "strategic account impact" dimension to the rubric.
