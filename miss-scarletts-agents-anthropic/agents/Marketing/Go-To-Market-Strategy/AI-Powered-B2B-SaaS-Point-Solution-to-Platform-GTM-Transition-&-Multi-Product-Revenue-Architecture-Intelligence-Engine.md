## Overview
Designs the complete go-to-market transition strategy for B2B SaaS companies evolving from a point solution (single feature, single buyer) to a platform (multiple products, multiple buyers, compounding revenue). Use this when you have $5M+ ARR in a core product and are launching a second product, when your sales team is losing platform deals to competitors who bundle more, or when leadership has committed to a platform narrative but marketing still leads with feature messaging.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales team defaults to selling the core product and ignores platform upsell opportunities.**
Fix: The incentive structure is misaligned. If AEs earn commission at the same rate on a $18K single-product deal as they would on a $48K platform deal, they'll rationally choose the shorter, lower-risk cycle. Immediately implement a platform deal accelerator (e.g., 1.5x commission multiplier on multi-product deals, or a quarterly bonus for platform attach rate). Also audit whether the sales team is actually certified — if reps can't confidently demo the second product, they won't bring it up. Run a "platform pitch challenge" where every AE presents the platform demo in 10 minutes to their peers; failing reps go through targeted coaching before they're allowed to run platform meetings.

**Problem: Existing customers react negatively to the platform announcement — they feel sold to, not served.**
Fix: This happens when the platform launch is positioned as "we have more stuff to sell you" rather than "we built something that makes what you already have more valuable." Reframe the communication: start with what improves for current users (new data connections, new insights available), then introduce the new products as optional upgrades, not mandatory. Send a "platform upgrade preview" to your top 50 customers 30 days before the public launch — make them feel like insiders who influenced the product, not targets for an upsell campaign. Never announce pricing increases at the same time as a platform launch.

**Problem: Analysts are not adopting your platform category name — they're still calling you a point solution.**
Fix: Analyst category adoption takes 12-18 months minimum and requires more than a briefing. You need a three-part strategy: (1) Publish original data that defines the category — a research report that quantifies the problem your platform solves becomes the citation analysts use; (2) Recruit 5+ named customers willing to speak publicly about the category concept — analysts verify category claims through customer interviews; (3) Commission an analyst to write an inaugural report on the category (sponsored research) to accelerate adoption. Until analysts recognize your category, use the phrase "what analysts call [adjacent category], plus [your new capability]" — this creates a bridge rather than asking buyers to learn a new concept from scratch.
