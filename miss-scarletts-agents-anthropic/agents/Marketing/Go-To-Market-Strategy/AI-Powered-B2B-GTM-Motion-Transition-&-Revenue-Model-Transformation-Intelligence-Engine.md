## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce/HubSpot:** Create separate pipelines and lead sources for each GTM motion from Day 1 — this is critical for clean attribution data during transition
- **Mixpanel/Amplitude:** Implement PLG funnel events (signup → activation → expansion → conversion) as new motion launches; map against sales-led funnel for comparison
- **Notion/Confluence:** Build a "Transition War Room" wiki with live metrics dashboard, weekly velocity snapshots, and decision logs for each gate
- **Gong/Chorus:** Tag calls by motion type to build separate call libraries for each motion — prevents sales training contamination between motions
- **ChartMogul/Baremetrics:** Configure separate revenue cohorts by GTM motion from launch date — you'll need clean motion-level ARR data for the board

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales team is sandbagging the new motion — routing enterprise prospects back to their existing sales motion to hit quota**
Solution: Implement motion-specific deal registration with protected commissions. Enterprise deals closed by overlay AEs earn 1.5x accelerator; any deal crossed over to old motion without approval triggers deal desk review. Make protecting the new motion financially rewarding, not punitive.

**Problem: PLG free tier is being used by SMB customers who would have converted to paid under old sales motion, destroying revenue**
Solution: Implement usage-based gates that trigger automated in-product sales sequences. If a team uses >70% of their free tier limits, auto-route to sales-assisted upgrade path (not self-serve). Add "team size" qualification to free signup flow — companies >25 employees go to sales-assisted track.

**Problem: Board and investors interpret the transition as a sign of strategic confusion — ARR growth dips in Q1 and they want to halt the transition**
Solution: Prepare a pre-mortem narrative before board meetings. Show the ceiling math projecting no-transition ARR, demonstrate leading indicators of new motion traction (signups, trials, pipeline), and present a clear 90-day trigger for full commit/retreat. Frame the valley as "planned investment period" with defined success criteria — ambiguity is what kills board confidence, not the valley itself.
