## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Low certification completion rate (below 35%) despite high enrollment
**Solution:** Completion failure almost always traces to module length and perceived job relevance. Audit average drop-off point using LMS analytics — that module is where relevance breaks. Rebuild dropped modules with shorter segments (max 20 minutes), add a "why this matters for [job title]" opener to every module, and implement progress saves so mobile learners can resume. Consider adding a "fast-track exam option" for experienced practitioners who want to test out without sitting through content they already know — this dramatically improves completion among senior ICP personas.

**Problem:** Certifications not generating measurable pipeline — attribution shows low or zero influence
**Solution:** Attribution failure has two root causes. First, verify your CRM integration is actually capturing certification completion and linking it to the correct contact record — test end-to-end with a dummy completion before blaming program performance. Second, if integration is working but influence is low, your certifications are reaching the wrong audience (practitioners outside your TAM). Shift distribution from organic-only to employer partnership and agency certification programs where every certified person works at a company in your addressable market.

**Problem:** Content goes stale as product evolves, creating a mounting maintenance burden
**Solution:** Build certification content review into your product release cycle from Day 1. Assign a "certification DRI" to each product team who flags content impact in sprint planning whenever a feature changes. Set all certifications to expire in 12-18 months and require a "What's New" micro-module for renewal — this forces content freshness through the learner base without requiring a full rebuild. Use AI to diff old module content against current product docs after each release and surface specific sentences that are no longer accurate.
