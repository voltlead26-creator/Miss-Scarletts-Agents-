## Troubleshooting

**Problem: LinkedIn-attributed website traffic appears very low (< 0.5% click-through rate on posts) despite high impressions**

Fix: LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes outbound links in the post body, reducing distribution by 30–50%. Test the "link in first comment" strategy: post without any link in the body, then add the CTA link as the first comment immediately after publishing. Also audit your content mix — if >30% of posts are link-heavy product announcements, your overall page reach will be suppressed. Shift to link-free thought leadership posts for reach, and reserve link posts for high-priority conversion moments (demo launch, webinar registration, new case study).

**Problem: Employee advocacy program launches but employees stop sharing after the first 2 weeks**

Fix: The content bank is the most common failure point. If employees have to write their own captions, participation collapses within 10 days. Ensure you provide 3 ready-to-post caption options per piece of content — one for technical/product roles, one for sales/CS, one neutral executive voice. Sharing must be a 2-tap mobile action (click link, tap share). Additionally, public recognition is more motivating than private acknowledgment: name your top 3 advocates each month in the company all-hands and on the company LinkedIn page itself. Make advocacy visible and celebrated, not a background task.

**Problem: CMO or CFO pushes back on LinkedIn's pipeline contribution because the UTM-attributed numbers look small ($50K–$200K/quarter)**

Fix: Apply and clearly communicate the dark social correction. LinkedIn is heavily consumed via mobile app (where UTM tracking is unreliable), private DMs (content shared without links), and offline conversations triggered by posts ("I saw your LinkedIn post about..."). Research from Dreamdata and Nielsen B2B Institute supports a 3–5× dark social multiplier for enterprise B2B SaaS. Present your attribution with stated assumptions: "Our CRM directly attributes $X to LinkedIn using UTM tracking. We estimate LinkedIn's true pipeline influence is $X–$Y after applying a 4× dark social adjustment, consistent with B2B SaaS industry benchmarks. We'll refine this multiplier as we collect more data." Presenting a range with methodology is more credible than a single number.
