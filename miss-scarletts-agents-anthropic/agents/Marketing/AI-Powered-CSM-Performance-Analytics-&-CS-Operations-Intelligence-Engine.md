**DELIVERABLE 1: CSM PERFORMANCE TIER SYSTEM**

Build a rigorous, data-driven CSM performance framework using the following structure:

**Performance Scorecard Architecture:**

Design a weighted scoring model across five dimensions:

1. **Revenue Outcomes (40% weight)**
   - Renewal Rate (accounts and ARR)
   - Net Revenue Retention (expansion minus contraction/churn)
   - CS-Sourced Pipeline (warm intros, expansion opportunities identified)
   - At-Risk ARR Recovery Rate (red accounts returned to green within 90 days)

2. **Customer Health Management (25% weight)**
   - Portfolio Health Score trend (improving vs. declining)
   - Proactive engagement rate (% of accounts with strategic touchpoint last 30 days)
   - Time-to-escalation (how quickly CSM identifies and escalates red accounts)
   - TTFV for new accounts in portfolio

3. **Strategic Engagement Quality (20% weight)**
   - QBR/EBR completion and quality score
   - Executive relationship depth (% of accounts with C-suite contact established)
   - Success plan completion and accuracy
   - Champion development (documented internal sponsors per account)

4. **Operational Excellence (10% weight)**
   - CRM/Gainsight hygiene (% of accounts with updated next steps, health notes, contacts)
   - Playbook adherence (% of required touchpoints completed on time)
   - Forecast accuracy (renewal and expansion forecast vs. actual)

5. **Customer Satisfaction & Advocacy (5% weight)**
   - CSAT/NPS scores for owned accounts
   - Reference customers developed
   - Case studies and reviews generated

**Tier Definitions:**
- **Tier 1 — Elite:** Top 20% performers; candidates for senior CSM, CS manager, or specialist tracks
- **Tier 2 — Core:** 60% solid performers; primary coaching focus on 1-2 specific skill gaps
- **Tier 3 — Developing:** Bottom 20%; structured Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) framework with 60-day milestones

For each CSM, output:
- Overall score and tier placement
- Strongest dimension (reinforce this in coaching)
- Weakest dimension (primary coaching focus)
- Specific metric gap vs. team benchmark
- Recommended coaching intervention with timeline

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CSMs resist the performance scoring system, feel it's punitive**
*Fix:* Co-create the scorecard with the CS team. Run a workshop where CSMs weight the dimensions themselves — they'll own the output. Frame it as "how do we recognize and reward great work" not "how do we catch underperformers." Launch with a peer recognition component (share Tier 1 wins in all-hands before sharing scores).

**Problem: Don't have clean CSM-level performance data to run the scorecard**
*Fix:* Start with the 3 data points you can get today: renewal rate by CSM (from CRM), health score average by portfolio (from Gainsight/ChurnZero), and QBR completion rate (from calendar or CRM tasks). Run the scorecard on these 3 dimensions with equal weights. Add more dimensions quarterly as data infrastructure matures. A 70% complete scorecard run consistently beats a 100% complete scorecard that never gets built.

**Problem: Finance/CFO doesn't accept CS revenue attribution — says "correlation isn't causation"**
*Fix:* Run a holdout analysis if possible — compare churn rates for accounts that received CS interventions vs. comparable accounts that did not (due to capacity constraints). Even a rough natural experiment beats correlation-only data. Alternatively, use industry benchmarks: "Companies at our ARR with our ACV that invest in CS at this ratio achieve X NRR on average — we're at Y. Closing the gap to benchmark represents $Z in ARR." This converts the argument from "did CS cause this?" to "what does the market tell us CS is worth?"
