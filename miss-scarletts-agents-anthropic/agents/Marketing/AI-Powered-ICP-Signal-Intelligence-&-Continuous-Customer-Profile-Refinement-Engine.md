## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce CRM:** Map refined ICP attributes to custom properties. Use the GTM Routing Rules table to set up lead scoring workflows that auto-assign records to the correct sales queue. Trigger an ICP tier stamp on every new account.
- **Clay / Apollo / ZoomInfo:** Use trigger event criteria (hiring signals, funding events, tech stack changes) to build live ICP-scored prospect lists that auto-update weekly. Connect Clay enrichment flows to push ICP tier scores directly into your CRM.
- **Gong / Chorus / Clari:** Feed the refined champion/buyer titles and pain point language into your call intelligence tagging taxonomy. Use conversation intelligence to validate which ICP attributes appear in won vs. lost calls.
- **Segment / Amplitude:** Map the "behavioral signals predicting conversion" output to product analytics events. Build cohort reports that track whether new signups match Tier 1 behavioral patterns within the first 14 days.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Publish the refined ICP specification to your internal wiki with a quarterly review date. Link to this document from your sales playbook, battlecard, and onboarding materials so the whole GTM team works from one source of truth.
- **Google Sheets + Zapier:** Build a lightweight ICP scoring sheet that auto-populates from CRM exports. Use Zapier to trigger a Slack alert to your RevOps team when a high-score account goes 7 days without a follow-up.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: My win/loss sample is too small (fewer than 20 deals) to produce statistically meaningful patterns.**
*Solution:* Expand the lookback window to 6-12 months. If sample is still small, supplement with qualitative data: run 5-8 customer interviews (use the Quick Copy-Paste version as your pre-interview analysis brief) and ask customers directly "What would have made you not buy?" Weight qualitative signals lower in your confidence scores until you reach n=20+ for each attribute.

**Problem: The refined ICP excludes a major current customer segment that the CEO or board expects us to serve.**
*Solution:* Flag this as a "Strategic Segment" and build a separate ICP specification for it rather than forcing it into the data-driven ICP. Maintain both in parallel, track win rate and NRR separately, and let the data speak over 2-3 quarters. Use the Advanced Version's Tier 3 / Opportunistic section for strategic segments with below-average metrics.

**Problem: ICP attributes keep shifting every quarter, creating confusion in the sales team.**
*Solution:* Distinguish between "core attributes" (change <2x/year, require >80% signal confidence to update) and "dynamic attributes" (trigger events, tech stack signals — update monthly). Lock core attributes for 2 quarters unless a significant market event occurs. Use a changelog in your ICP doc so sales always knows what changed and why.
