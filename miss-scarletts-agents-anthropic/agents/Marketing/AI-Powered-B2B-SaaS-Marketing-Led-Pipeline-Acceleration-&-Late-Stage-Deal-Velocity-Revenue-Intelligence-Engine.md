## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce:** Build pipeline health score as a rollup field on the Opportunity object. Use Process Builder or Flows to trigger marketing automation actions when score drops below 50 or competitor field is populated. Add "Marketing Pipeline Acceleration Active" checkbox to track which deals are in the program.
- **HubSpot:** Use Deal Stage enrollment triggers to start the appropriate content sequence. Build a custom "Pipeline Acceleration" workflow that checks deal health criteria daily and assigns the appropriate sequence. Use Deal-based workflows with CRM sync to Salesforce for bi-directional field updates.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Configure topic trackers for competitor mentions in all sales calls. Auto-trigger competitive counter-play delivery when Gong detects competitor name in a recorded call. Export champion signal phrases to help score champion development.
- **Highspot / Seismic:** Build "Pipeline Acceleration" collections by deal stage and buying persona. Configure Salesforce integration so content engagement (viewed, shared, time spent) writes back to Opportunity activity feed — this data feeds your content effectiveness metrics.
- **Slack:** Create a #pipeline-acceleration Slack channel with Salesforce Chatter integration. AEs get real-time notifications when: deal health score drops 10+ points, content is viewed in the deal room, or stall reactivation sequence completes without response.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Use as the Digital Deal Room template base for companies without Highspot/Seismic. Build a standard deal room template with sections for: mutual success plan, implementation timeline, security documentation, case studies, and next steps. Share a unique URL per deal.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** AEs resist marketing involvement in their deals, claiming it creates "noise" for prospects.
**Solution:** Flip the model — make AEs the hero. All marketing-led pipeline acceleration touches are sent from or attributed to the AE, not marketing. Marketing acts as the production and logistics engine; the AE gets the credit. Show AEs the win rate data for deals with vs. without program support after 30 days to convert skeptics with results.

**Problem:** CRM data quality is too poor to run deal health scoring accurately (missing fields, wrong stage dates, no champion contact).
**Solution:** Start with a manual scoring sprint. Have marketing ops review all Stage 3+ deals in a 2-hour session, hand-score them, and use this as the baseline. Simultaneously, launch a data quality improvement campaign: make champion contact and competitor field mandatory in Salesforce for Stage 3 advancement. Within 60 days, data quality will support automated scoring.

**Problem:** Content assets don't exist for the late-stage personas and use cases needed (e.g., no security documentation, no industry-specific ROI calculator).
**Solution:** Use the pipeline health scoring to identify the top 5 deals at greatest risk of stalling or losing. Build content specifically for those 5 deals first — this creates urgency and shows immediate ROI. Each asset created for a specific deal becomes a reusable template for all similar deals going forward. Prioritize ruthlessly: what's costing you the most deals right now?
