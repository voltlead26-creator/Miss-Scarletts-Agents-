## Troubleshooting
- **Low CLO response rates to outbound despite personalization**: The most common failure is leading with product capability instead of business problem. CLOs have a finely tuned filter for vendor pitches — they receive 30-50 vendor emails per week. Test the "peer-to-peer" reframe: have your own CLO or a CLO customer record a 30-second Loom video as the opening touch ("I'm a CLO, not a sales rep — here's why I wanted to reach out personally"). CLO-to-CLO cold outreach achieves 3-5x the reply rate of SDR-to-CLO outreach. If your company doesn't have a CLO, identify a customer CLO willing to make warm introductions to 3-5 target accounts in exchange for a speaking opportunity or reference partnership benefit.
- **High MQL volume but low SQL conversion from L&D segment**: Usually means content is attracting L&D practitioners (Directors of Training, Instructional Designers) rather than L&D executive buyers (CLO, VP L&D). Audit your gating fields — add "L&D budget responsibility" and "team size" as qualifying fields on your anchor asset form. Shift content angles from "how to build better training programs" (practitioner appeal) to "how to prove L&D's impact on business performance" (executive buyer appeal). Also examine your nurture sequence — if Email 3 and beyond don't escalate to business impact language, mid-funnel CLOs will disengage.
- **Long sales cycles (12+ months) stalling in committee**: CLO deals frequently stall when the economic buyer (CHRO or CFO) hasn't been engaged before the evaluation stage. Implement a "multi-thread early" rule: within the first 2 discovery calls, ask the CLO to introduce you to their HRIS/IT counterpart and their CHRO/CFO sponsor before you complete the full demo. Build a "CHRO/CFO Business Case" one-pager that the CLO can share upward without requiring a vendor call — reduce the ask from "schedule a meeting with your CFO" to "share this 3-slide summary with your CFO this week." Deals where CFO is engaged before RFP stage close 40% faster than deals where CFO enters at contract review.
