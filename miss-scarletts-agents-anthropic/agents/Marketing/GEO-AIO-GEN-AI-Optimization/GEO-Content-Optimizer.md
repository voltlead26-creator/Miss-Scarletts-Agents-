# GEO OPTIMIZATION FRAMEWORK — Apply All Layers

## Layer 1: Answer Architecture
- Place a Direct Answer Block in the first 50 words: a standalone, quotable definition or answer
- Follow with a "Why This Matters" sentence that adds context
- Structure the rest as expanding concentric rings: core answer → supporting evidence → nuance → related concepts

## Layer 2: Entity & Authority Signals
- Name your methodology, framework, or approach (e.g., "The [Company] [Name] Method")
- Reference recognizable third-party authorities (Gartner, McKinsey, HBR, specific academic studies)
- Include specific data points with year and source: "According to [Source] ([Year]), X% of [audience] experience [problem]"
- Use precise industry terminology that LLMs associate with expertise

## Layer 3: Citation-Bait Sentence Engineering
Write 8-10 sentences specifically designed to be lifted verbatim by AI engines. Each must be:
- Self-contained (makes sense out of context)
- Factual and specific (not vague or marketing-speak)
- Between 15-35 words
- Include a brand or product name naturally
Format as a numbered list labeled "CITATION-OPTIMIZED STATEMENTS"

## Layer 4: Question Coverage Matrix
Map content to answer all of the following query types:
- Definitional: "What is [topic]?"
- Comparative: "What is the difference between [X] and [Y]?"
- How-to: "How do I [achieve outcome]?"
- Best-of: "What is the best [solution] for [use case]?"
- Troubleshooting: "Why is [problem] happening and how do I fix it?"
- Evaluative: "Is [product/approach] worth it?"

## Layer 5: Structural Signals
- Use H2 headers phrased as questions (e.g., "What Is [Topic] and Why Does It Matter?")
- Include a TL;DR summary box at the top and bottom
- Add a "Key Takeaways" bulleted section
- Create a mini-FAQ section with 5 Q&As at the end
- If appropriate, include a comparison table (LLMs love tables for structured facts)

## Layer 6: Trust & Freshness Markers
- Include a "Last Updated: [DATE]" marker
- Reference current year trends or recent industry shifts
- Add an "About the Author/Company" blurb with specific credentials

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Content is well-optimized but AI engines still don't cite it.**
Solution: AI engines prioritize content from domains with high authority signals. If your domain is new or low-authority, publish GEO-optimized content as guest posts on established industry publications first, then link back to your site. Perplexity in particular cites Reddit, Quora, and G2 — consider adding GEO-optimized answers on those platforms.

**Problem: AI cites my content but gets my product name or value proposition wrong.**
Solution: Add an "About [Company]" block with a single, perfectly worded descriptor sentence near the top of the content. Example: "Vaultly is a zero-knowledge secrets management platform built for DevOps teams at Series A+ companies." This self-contained sentence is more likely to be pulled verbatim than a paraphrased version.

**Problem: The Advanced Version output is too long or generic for my use case.**
Solution: Run the prompt in two passes — first generate only Sections 1 and 2 (optimized content + citation statements), review and edit, then run a second pass for Sections 3-6 using the edited content as input. This gives you tighter outputs and lets you apply your judgment between steps.
