## Integration Tips

**Wikidata (Direct Edit):**

For company entries, the most impactful properties to complete first are P31 (instance of), P452 (industry), P856 (website), and P159 (headquarters location). Changes appear in AI retrieval systems within 2-4 weeks.

**Google Search Console (Knowledge Panel Claim):**
Once a Google Knowledge Panel appears for your brand (which requires sufficient entity signals to exist), claim it via Google Search Console → Enhancements → Knowledge Panel. This allows you to suggest corrections to factual data. Priority: get a Wikidata entry created first — Knowledge Panel generation correlates strongly with Wikidata presence.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: We created a Wikidata entry two months ago but AI tools still describe us inaccurately.**
Solution: Wikidata changes propagate to AI model training data on different timelines depending on the system. Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems like Perplexity and ChatGPT Browse update within days to weeks. Models with static training cutoffs (like base GPT-4) may not reflect changes for 6-18 months. Focus short-term wins on Perplexity, Gemini, and Copilot — all use live retrieval. For base model accuracy, the strategy is volume: the more high-authority web sources that describe you accurately, the faster the signal propagates into future training runs.

**Problem: Someone keeps reverting our Wikipedia article or it was deleted for "notability."**
Solution: Wikipedia requires independent coverage from reliable sources — press releases, company blogs, and paid content don't count. Build notability first by securing 3+ substantive (not just mention) articles in recognized industry publications (TechCrunch, VentureBeat, SC Magazine, Dark Reading, Forbes, Bloomberg). Funding round announcements in major publications are strong notability signals. Once coverage exists, resubmit the article via Wikipedia:Articles for creation (AFC) with explicit citations to each independent source. Do not resubmit immediately after deletion without new notability evidence — it will be deleted again.

**Problem: Our brand is being confused with a competitor or a similarly-named company in AI responses.**
Solution: Entity disambiguation requires making the distinction between entities unmistakably clear across multiple authoritative sources. Steps: (1) Add a disambiguation note to your Wikidata entry using the "different from" (P1889) property, linking to the other entity. (2) Publish a blog post or FAQ page on your website explicitly addressing the confusion: "Not to be confused with [Other Company]." (3) Ensure your Crunchbase and LinkedIn descriptions include geographic and market scope signals that differentiate you (e.g., "US-based B2B SaaS company" if the lookalike is UK-based or in a different market). (4) Build co-citation volume — the more times authoritative sources accurately describe your brand in context, the clearer the entity signal becomes relative to the confusion entity.
