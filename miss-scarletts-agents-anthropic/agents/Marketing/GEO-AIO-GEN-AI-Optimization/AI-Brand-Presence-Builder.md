## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI engines describe your brand inaccurately (wrong category, wrong customer size, outdated info).**
Solution: The most common cause is that the AI's training data contains old or conflicting descriptions. Deploy the Brand Narrative Consistency Protocol immediately — update Crunchbase, LinkedIn About section, G2 overview, and your own About page to use the identical 2-sentence canonical description. Within 4-8 weeks of consistent indexing, AI answers should begin correcting. For urgent cases with ChatGPT, submit a correction via OpenAI's brand inquiry form; for Google AI Overviews, use Search Console's feedback mechanism.

**Problem: Despite having great reviews and content, AI still recommends competitors instead of you.**
Solution: Volume and authority matter more than quality at the early stage. AI engines apply a "minimum credibility threshold" — typically 25-50 reviews on G2/Capterra, at least 3-5 publications with DA > 50 mentioning your brand, and active community presence. Run a gap analysis against your top competitor's third-party footprint. The single highest-leverage tactic: publish original research data (even a 100-person survey) that gets picked up by 3+ publications — AI engines heavily weight proprietary data citations.

**Problem: Reddit community moderators remove posts that mention your product.**
Solution: Shift to a pure value-contribution strategy — employees answer questions with expertise and never mention the product unless directly asked. Build rep over 30-60 days, then when directly asked "what tool do you use?" the authentic recommendation lands without appearing promotional. Never post from company accounts; only from real employee personal accounts with full disclosure of affiliation.
