## Troubleshooting

**Problem: We published great content but AI citation rate hasn't improved after 60 days.**
Solution: AI models with static training data (like GPT-4 base) won't pick up new content until their next training cycle (6-18 months). Focus optimization on retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) — Perplexity, ChatGPT Browse, and Gemini with live search pull current web content. Prioritize getting your content indexed by Perplexity and appearing in Bing's index (which Copilot uses). Publish to high-DA sources first (industry publications, not just your own domain) since RAG systems trust high-authority sources more.

**Problem: We appear in AI responses but our competitors are always mentioned first.**
Solution: AI models cite brands based on frequency of association with a topic across their training data and live retrieval. Run a "mention velocity" campaign: brief 10-15 industry bloggers, analysts, and podcast hosts to mention your brand in context of the specific query topic. Each organic third-party mention acts as a vote. Simultaneously, ensure your own content uses the exact phrases buyers use in AI search queries — AI tools pattern-match query language to relevant content.

**Problem: AI tools cite us but the description is outdated or inaccurate.**
Solution: This indicates the AI is pulling from an older training data source or an outdated page. First, update your Wikipedia/Wikidata entry (AI models heavily weight Wikipedia). Second, update your Crunchbase and LinkedIn company page descriptions. Third, publish a clear "About [Your Company]" page that defines your product in simple, citable language with structured JSON-LD markup. Request Perplexity to re-index the page. For Google Gemini, update your Google Business Profile and Knowledge Panel via Google Search Console.
