## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Published displacement content but still not appearing in LLM responses after 6 weeks.**
Solution: LLMs retrieve from their training data (for non-retrieval-augmented models like Claude) or from live web indexing (for Perplexity, Bing Copilot). For training-data-dependent models, content must achieve sufficient co-citation across multiple indexed sources — not just your own domain. Priority fix: Get your content mentioned on G2, Capterra, TrustRadius, and 2–3 industry publications within 4 weeks of publishing. Co-citation is the primary citation signal for trained models. For retrieval-augmented models like Perplexity, check that your pages are indexed in Bing (submit sitemap to Bing Webmaster Tools) and that page load speed is under 2 seconds.

**Problem: LLMs are citing the competitor's framing of the comparison (e.g., "Momentum.io is good for small teams, Salesloft is better for enterprise").**
Solution: This means the competitor's content is dominating the definition narrative. Counter this immediately with a direct factual rebuttal structured as a FAQ: "Is Momentum.io only for small sales teams?" with a clear, data-backed answer ("No — 73% of Momentum.io's ARR comes from companies with 500+ employees. Customers include [named enterprise companies]..."). FAQ schema markup ensures LLMs can extract and cite this rebuttal directly. Also seed this corrective framing in G2 review responses and analyst briefings.

**Problem: Comparison page is getting organic traffic but LLMs are not citing it.**
Solution: Organic search ranking and LLM citation are related but distinct. LLMs weight content that: (1) front-loads the answer in the first 150 words, (2) includes explicit comparison language ("vs", "alternative", "compared to"), (3) provides structured data with specific numbers rather than qualitative claims, and (4) is externally referenced. Audit your page against these four criteria. Most common fix: rewrite the introduction to lead with the answer ("Salesloft and Momentum.io are not direct competitors — they serve different buyer problems. Here's the honest breakdown...") and add 3 specific customer metrics in the first 200 words.
