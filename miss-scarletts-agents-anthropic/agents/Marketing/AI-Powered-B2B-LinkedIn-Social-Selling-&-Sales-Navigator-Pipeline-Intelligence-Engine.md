## Integration Tips

- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Use the "Lead List" and "Account List" features to save prospects and activate automated alerts for job changes, news, and content activity. Export weekly alert digests and paste into this prompt for batch outreach drafting.
- **Clay.com:** Enrich your Sales Navigator prospect exports with Clay to pull LinkedIn recent posts, job history, tech stack, and funding events automatically — feed this enriched data directly into the Advanced prompt.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Log LinkedIn touches as Activities with a custom "LinkedIn Touch" type. Set up a LinkedIn Sequence stage in your pipeline to track connection → reply → meeting progression.
- **Taplio / Shield Analytics:** Use Taplio for LinkedIn content scheduling and analytics (SSI impact per post), and Shield for team-level LinkedIn performance tracking across all reps.
- **Zapier / Make.com:** Build a workflow: Sales Navigator alert → Clay enrichment → AI message draft → Slack notification to rep → manual review → send. This achieves 80% automation without crossing LinkedIn's TOS.
- **Notion:** Store approved message templates organized by persona archetype and buying signal type — allows reps to pull the right template in seconds.
- **Gong / Chorus:** After calls booked via LinkedIn, tag them as "LinkedIn-sourced" in your conversation intelligence tool to analyze win rates and talk tracks that close LinkedIn-sourced deals faster.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Connection requests not being accepted (below 20% acceptance rate)**
Solution: The connection note is too generic or too obviously templated. Go back to SECTION 3 Touch 1 and identify a more specific personalization anchor — ideally something they published or publicly commented on within the last 14 days. Avoid referencing company-level news (funding, layoffs) in the first touch as it signals you've been stalking their company rather than genuinely interested in them as a professional.

**Issue: Replies drop off after Touch 2 (no response to Touch 3 or 4)**
Solution: Your soft introduction in Touch 3 is likely leading with your product before building enough curiosity. Rewrite Touch 3 to lead with a provocative pain-point question anchored to a signal, and bury or remove the "one sentence on what we do" — let them ask what you do instead of volunteering it. Alternatively, test leading Touch 3 with a benchmark or data point specific to their industry rather than a question.

**Issue: LinkedIn restricting your account for too many outreach messages**
Solution: You've exceeded LinkedIn's weekly limits (typically 100-150 connection requests/week for standard accounts, 200-300 for Sales Navigator). Reduce volume immediately and increase quality — target only Score 7+ prospects per SECTION 1. Add 48-72 hour delays between touches. Ensure your profile has 500+ connections, a professional photo, and consistent recent activity before scaling outreach. Never use third-party automation bots that send messages without manual review — this violates LinkedIn TOS and risks permanent account suspension.
