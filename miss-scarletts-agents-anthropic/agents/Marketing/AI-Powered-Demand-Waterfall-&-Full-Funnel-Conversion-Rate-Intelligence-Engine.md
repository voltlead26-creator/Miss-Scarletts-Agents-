## Overview
Runs a complete demand waterfall analysis across all funnel stages — from raw inquiries through closed-won — diagnosing where conversion rates are below benchmark, identifying root causes of drop-off at each stage, and generating a prioritized optimization roadmap. Use this when your pipeline is insufficient to hit revenue targets, when conversion rates have degraded quarter-over-quarter, or when marketing and sales disagree on where the "real" problem lies.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot/Marketo**: Pull the raw funnel data from the "Funnel Report" or "Lifecycle Stage Report" before running this prompt. Export to CSV for cleaner data input. After analysis, use the revised MQL definition to update your lifecycle stage automation rules.
- **Salesforce**: Use the "Leads by Source" and "Opportunity Pipeline" reports to populate Stage 3-6 data. The Stage Analysis report in Einstein Analytics provides cohort conversion data across quarters.
- **Looker/Tableau**: If your BI tool has a funnel visualization, screenshot it and describe what you see in the prompt — the AI can interpret narrative descriptions of funnel shapes effectively.
- **Gong/Chorus**: Pull the top 10 loss reasons from call recordings and paste them into the "Primary loss reasons" field — this produces significantly more accurate root cause diagnoses than CRM loss-reason dropdowns (which are often gamed).
- **Clearbit/ZoomInfo**: The most common fix from this analysis is adding firmographic enrichment to the MQL definition. Both tools have HubSpot/Marketo native integrations for real-time enrichment at form fill.
- **Notion/Google Docs**: Use the experiment backlog output as a live testing roadmap in Notion. Assign owners and track results in a table that links back to the original hypothesis.
- **Zapier/Make**: Automate monthly funnel data collection by setting up Zaps that export CRM stage counts to a Google Sheet on the 1st of each month — then use that Sheet as your recurring input for this prompt.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: I don't have accurate stage-by-stage conversion data — my CRM reporting is unreliable.**
Solution: Start with what you know confidently (usually MQL volume from your MAP and closed-won from finance). Estimate the gaps by asking your AE team "roughly what % of leads you get from marketing do you accept and work?" — directional data produces directional insights. Flag the gaps explicitly in your prompt input; the AI will identify which diagnoses are data-confidence-dependent.

**Problem: The AI gives generic recommendations that don't match my business model (e.g., recommending BANT for a PLG motion).**
Solution: Be explicit about your sales motion in the CONTEXT section. For PLG, replace Stage 2-3 with "PQL" (Product Qualified Lead) criteria and describe your activation milestones. The framework works for any funnel shape as long as you define your stages clearly.

**Problem: The revenue recovery math seems too optimistic — the AI is assuming linear improvements.**
Solution: Ask the AI to model conservative, base, and optimistic scenarios (e.g., 50%, 75%, 100% of benchmark improvement). Also ask it to account for lead volume changes if improving MQL quality means stricter scoring that reduces MQL volume — the net revenue impact may still be positive, but the math needs both sides.
