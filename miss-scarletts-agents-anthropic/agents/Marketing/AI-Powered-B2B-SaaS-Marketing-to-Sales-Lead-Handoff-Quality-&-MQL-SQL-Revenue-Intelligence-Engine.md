## Overview
Diagnoses and optimizes the marketing-to-sales lead handoff by analyzing MQL acceptance rates, rejection reasons, follow-up velocity, SLA compliance, and conversion leakage at each funnel stage. Use this when your MQL-to-SQL rate is below benchmark (industry median: 13%), sales is complaining about lead quality, pipeline coverage is missing targets despite sufficient MQL volume, or you need board-ready proof of marketing's revenue contribution beyond top-of-funnel vanity metrics.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce**: Build a "Lead Handoff Health Dashboard" using Salesforce reports: (1) MQL Created date + First Activity date to measure response time; (2) Lead Disposition/Rejection Reason picklist field (create if missing) for BDRs to log rejection reason before recycling; (3) Campaign Influence report to track marketing-sourced vs. marketing-influenced pipeline separately. Schedule weekly automated report delivery to CMO + VP Sales.
- **HubSpot**: Use HubSpot's "Lead Handoff" workflow to stamp the timestamp when a contact reaches MQL status, then track time-to-first-task-created by the assigned BDR. Build a custom property "Days Since MQL" and use workflow enrollment triggers to alert BDR manager if no activity logged within SLA window.
- **Chili Piper / LeanData**: These tools handle instant MQL routing based on scoring thresholds and territory assignment. Chili Piper's "Distro" product auto-assigns MQLs to available BDRs in round-robin and fires Slack/email alerts — reducing response time from hours to minutes without manual BDR queue monitoring.
- **6sense / Bombora / G2 Buyer Intent**: Integrate intent data as a scoring signal in your MAP (Marketo/HubSpot) to weight in-market accounts higher in lead scoring, reducing "no recognizable pain" rejections by surfacing intent signals before the BDR ever makes contact.
- **Slack**: Build a Slack bot (via Zapier or Salesforce Flow + Slack API) that posts a message to #bdr-queue every time a Tier 1 ICP MQL is created, with lead details and a direct CRM link. Include an SLA countdown timer so BDRs can see urgency at a glance without opening Salesforce.
- **Google Sheets / Looker Studio**: Build a weekly "Lead Handoff Scorecard" that auto-refreshes from CRM exports: SAL rate by week, response velocity by BDR, rejection reason breakdown by source, SLA compliance %. Share via email digest every Monday morning to CMO and VP Sales.
- **Clearbit / ZoomInfo Enrich**: Instrument real-time form enrichment to fill in company size, industry, and employee count at form submission. Block or flag leads where enrichment returns company size outside ICP range — prevents non-ICP MQL creation before it ever enters the BDR queue.

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem**: Sales doesn't log rejection reasons consistently — the disposition field is optional and BDRs skip it, making rejection analysis impossible.
  **Solution**: Make the rejection reason picklist field a mandatory CRM field before a lead can be changed to "Disqualified" or "Recycled" status. Use Salesforce validation rules or HubSpot required properties to enforce this at the CRM level, not just as a process ask. Frame it to sales as protecting them — accurate rejection data gives marketing the proof to fix targeting, which reduces wasted BDR time on bad leads.

- **Problem**: Marketing and sales have a longstanding adversarial relationship around lead quality — any analysis is immediately dismissed as "marketing covering themselves."
  **Solution**: Start the engagement by presenting the analysis as a joint RevOps problem, not a marketing problem. Lead with the velocity data (not scoring or targeting) since BDR response time is clearly a sales-side variable — showing that 18% of MQLs are never contacted builds credibility that this analysis is balanced. Present in a joint CMO-CRO meeting, not separately to each team. Include a slide showing the dollar value of the revenue opportunity — executives align around money faster than around process.

- **Problem**: The MQL volume is so low that conversion rates are statistically unstable — you have 80 MQLs/month and can't detect meaningful patterns.
  **Solution**: Extend the analysis window to 6–12 months for statistical stability (minimum 200 MQLs per analysis to detect meaningful signals). Group lead sources into broader buckets (paid vs. organic vs. outbound) rather than channel-specific analysis until volume justifies segmentation. Focus the short-term roadmap on the highest-impact operational fixes (response velocity, CRM data completeness) rather than lead scoring recalibration, which requires larger sample sizes to validate.
