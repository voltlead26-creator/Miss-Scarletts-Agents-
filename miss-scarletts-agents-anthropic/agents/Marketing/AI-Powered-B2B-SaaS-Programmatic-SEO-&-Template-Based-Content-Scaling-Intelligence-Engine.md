## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Google is indexing programmatic pages but not ranking them — they're stuck on page 4-6 with no movement after 60+ days.**
Solution: This usually indicates a thin content or duplicate content signal. Audit the pages that are stuck: (1) ensure each page has ≥ 1,200 words of substantively differentiated content — pages sharing 70%+ of identical text will be suppressed; (2) add unique customer quotes, G2 review excerpts, or use-case-specific sections that cannot be templated; (3) build 2-3 internal links FROM high-authority pages (like your blog or homepage) TO the stuck programmatic pages; (4) run a content uniqueness check — if your template produces near-identical content for different variables, add conditional content blocks that render different sections based on competitor/industry/use-case type.

**Problem: Programmatic pages are generating impressions but click-through rate is below 1%, meaning traffic never materializes despite rankings.**
Solution: The issue is almost always the title tag or meta description formula. (1) Test adding the current year to title tags — e.g., "[Product A] vs [Product B] (2026)" consistently lifts CTR 15-30% for comparison pages; (2) ensure meta descriptions include both brand names and a concrete differentiator within the first 100 characters — many templates bury the value prop; (3) add structured FAQ schema so the SERP result shows FAQ dropdowns, which significantly increases real estate and CTR; (4) A/B test emotional vs. rational meta description variants — "Save 40 hours per month" vs. "Compare features, pricing, and integrations" — for your ICP.

**Problem: The AI content generation prompt produces generic, unconvincing competitor comparison copy that reads like a marketing brochure — not a trustworthy resource.**
Solution: Three fixes: (1) Add explicit instruction to the prompt: "Acknowledge at least 2 genuine strengths of [Competitor] before presenting our advantages — buyers researching this query already know the competitor and will distrust one-sided content"; (2) Ground the prompt in real review data — pull 3-5 specific G2/Capterra quotes (both positive for competitor and negative) and require the prompt to cite them with attribution; (3) Add a "Who should NOT choose us" section — counterintuitively, this transparency dramatically increases conversion rate for buyers who ARE a good fit, because it signals honesty and reduces purchase anxiety.
