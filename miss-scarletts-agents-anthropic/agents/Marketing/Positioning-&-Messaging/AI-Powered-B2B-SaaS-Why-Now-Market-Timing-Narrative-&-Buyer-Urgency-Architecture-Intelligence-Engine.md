## Overview
Constructs the "Why Now" temporal urgency layer of your B2B positioning — the narrative component that answers why buyers must act in THIS quarter, not next year. Use when deals are stalling in late-stage pipeline, your market awareness campaigns aren't converting, or you're entering a new market and need to manufacture urgency without manufactured pressure tactics. Draws on Andy Raskin's strategic narrative framework, the Challenger Sale's "commercial teaching" approach, and Jobs-to-be-Done theory to map external market forces to internal buyer pain — then cascades the urgency architecture into campaigns, outbound sequences, and sales plays.

Step 5 — CAMPAIGN ACTIVATION COPY: Write 3 versions of the urgency narrative for:
- LinkedIn Sponsored Content headline + first line (under 150 characters)

- Sales discovery question that surfaces this urgency organically

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales team doesn't use the urgency narrative consistently on calls.**
Fix: The narrative is probably too complex or too abstract. Run a 30-minute workshop with 5-7 top performers. Ask each rep to say the urgency story in their own words in 60 seconds — if they can't, simplify the language. Create a laminated "Urgency Playbook" with 3 bullet points per lever that fits in a notebook. Urgency narratives fail not from bad content but from poor operationalization.

**Problem: Buyers say "we're aware of that" and it doesn't create urgency.**
Fix: They're aware of the force but haven't connected it to their specific operational gap. You're operating at the macro level; you need to descend to the operational level. Instead of "The SEC requires 4-day disclosure," say "When your biggest vendor has a breach — and statistically, one will in the next 12 months — can your team produce a defensible risk assessment in 96 hours using your current tools? Walk me through how you'd do that today." Force them to confront the execution gap, not just the market force.

**Problem: Urgency narrative feels manipulative to the sales team and they resist using it.**
Fix: This is the most important signal — if your own team finds it manipulative, buyers will too. Review the narrative against this test: "Is this urgency grounded in something that is genuinely true and verifiable in the external market, or are we manufacturing false scarcity?" If it fails the test, the narrative needs revision, not the sales team. Market-based urgency (real regulatory deadlines, real competitive moves) feels completely different from deal-based urgency ("this price expires Friday"). Make sure your team understands the distinction and has permission to use market urgency confidently.
