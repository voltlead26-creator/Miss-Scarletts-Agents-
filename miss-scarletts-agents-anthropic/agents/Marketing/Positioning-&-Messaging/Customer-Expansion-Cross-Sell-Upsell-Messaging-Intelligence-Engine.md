## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Build expansion enrollment trigger in Workflows: contact property "export_limit_hits" ≥ 3 → enroll in expansion sequence
- Use Deal stage "Expansion Identified" to track pipeline separately from new logo
- Custom property: "expansion_signal_type" (seat growth / usage limit / feature trigger) for segmented reporting

**Gainsight / ChurnZero / Totango:**
- Create Health Score rule: customers hitting feature limits are GREEN for expansion, not RED
- Timeline Event: "Expansion Outreach Initiated" — log when CSM sends Touch 1
- Playbook: trigger EBR scheduling when tenure = 90/180/365 days AND health score ≥ 70

**Salesforce:**
- Opportunity Type = "Expansion" — keep separate from "New Business" for NRR modeling
- Custom field: "Expansion Trigger" picklist (in-app limit, CSM-initiated, product usage, EBR)
- Report: Expansion Pipeline by Segment × Trigger Type — run weekly in rev ops stand-up

**Pendo / Amplitude / Mixpanel (in-app triggers):**
- Guide trigger: fire "upgrade modal" when feature_limit_hit event fires 3rd time in 30 days
- Cohort: create "expansion-ready" segment (power users + plan limits hit) for targeted in-app messaging
- A/B test: outcome-led CTA vs. feature-led CTA — measure modal CTR and downstream upgrade rate

**Zapier / Make.com automation:**
- Trigger: usage threshold hit in product DB → create task in Salesforce → enroll contact in HubSpot sequence → notify CSM in Slack
- Route: expansion deal ≥$10K ACV → create deal in Salesforce and assign to AE (not CSM)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Expansion emails feel generic and get low open rates (<20%).**
Fix: The most common cause is missing behavioral personalization. Every expansion email must reference at least one specific customer data point: "Your team exported 5,342 reports last month" beats "you're a power user." Pull actual usage data from your CDP or product database before sending. If you don't have the data piped into your email tool yet, prioritize that integration before scaling the campaign.

**Problem: Customers say "we're not using everything we have" and stall expansion.**
Fix: This objection means your onboarding left feature adoption gaps that are now blocking expansion. Before running expansion messaging, audit feature adoption depth for the target segment — customers below 40% feature adoption need an activation campaign first, not an expansion campaign. Sequence: run a 30-day "unlock your full plan" activation push, then layer in expansion messaging when adoption crosses 60%. The EBR Slide 1 (value delivered) must be credible before Slide 3 (expand now) lands.

**Problem: CSMs aren't using the expansion email templates — they're writing their own.**
Fix: This is a CSM trust issue, not a content issue. Hold a 30-minute enablement session where CSMs review the email sequence with real customer examples, share the A/B test data showing template performance vs. freeform, and let them customize Touches 1-2 with their own voice while keeping the structure. Give them a "personalization guide" — which fields they must customize (customer data, usage stat, CSM name) vs. which to leave as-is (subject line structure, CTA).
