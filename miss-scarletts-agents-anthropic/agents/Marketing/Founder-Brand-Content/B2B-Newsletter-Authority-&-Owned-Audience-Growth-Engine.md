## Integration Tips

- **beehiiv → HubSpot:** Use beehiiv's native HubSpot integration or Zapier to sync new subscribers with `Newsletter Subscriber = true` property and lifecycle stage = `Subscriber` — then build a workflow that promotes to `MQL` when they click 3+ issues or click a demo CTA
- **Substack → Salesforce:** Use Zapier (Substack → Salesforce) to create a Lead record on subscribe, add to `Newsletter` campaign, and trigger a Salesforce flow when they click product-related links
- **ConvertKit / beehiiv → Segment:** Fire a `newsletter_subscribed` event to Segment on subscribe and `newsletter_issue_clicked` on link clicks — use this for product analytics correlation between newsletter engagement and trial/demo conversion
- **Google Sheets Pipeline Tracking:** Create a monthly newsletter attribution sheet: pull subscriber growth, demo requests tagged `source=newsletter` in CRM, and closed-won deals where newsletter was in the buyer's engagement history
- **Notion Editorial Calendar:** Build a Notion database with Issue Number, Publish Date, Title, Core Argument, CTA Type, Open Rate, Replies, Demos Attributed — review weekly and use for quarterly editorial retrospectives
- **Make.com Automation:** Build a flow where a HubSpot contact who clicks a newsletter CTA link AND has `Company Size > 100` AND `Title contains CTO OR VP Eng` gets automatically added to an SDR sequence task within 24 hours

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Open rates drop below 30% after first 5 issues**  
Solution: You have a subject line problem or a list quality problem — not a content problem. A/B test subject lines aggressively (beehiiv and ConvertKit both support this natively). Run a re-engagement campaign: send a one-question email to inactive subscribers asking what they want more of. Remove anyone who hasn't opened in 60 days — a clean list with 45% open rate beats a bloated list with 20% open rate every time. Also audit your from-name: `Devon at Meridian` outperforms `Meridian Ops Newsletter` every time.

**Problem: Growing subscribers but zero demos or pipeline**  
Solution: Your editorial-to-pipeline bridge is broken. Check three things: (1) Are your CTAs visible and specific, or buried in long paragraphs? Move your demo CTA to right after the main insight, not at the bottom. (2) Is your ICP match rate high enough? Enrich your list with Apollo or Clearbit — if 60%+ of subscribers are students, agencies, or non-ICP roles, your growth tactics are pulling the wrong audience. (3) Are you using UTM parameters to track which newsletter links drive demo form submissions? If not, you have a measurement problem, not a pipeline problem.

**Problem: Can't maintain consistent publishing schedule**  
Solution: The most common newsletter failure mode. Fix it with a "minimum viable issue" standard: define a shorter format (500 words, 2 sections instead of 4) that you ship when the full format isn't ready — never miss a send date. Build a content bank: every time you have an insight or draft idea, drop it into a Notion `Issue Bank` database. Aim to always have 3 issue ideas ready to develop. If still struggling, switch from weekly to biweekly — consistency beats frequency every time.
