## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The community launched, 40 members joined, and 90% are lurking — nobody posts**
Solution: Lurking isn't failure — it's the default state of online communities. The fix is making the first post feel low-stakes and identity-affirming. Replace the generic "introduce yourself" prompt with an opinionated question that validates the member's expertise: "What's the most counterintuitive thing you've learned about [community topic] that you wish you'd known 3 years ago?" This format works because it (a) requires zero vulnerability, (b) positions the member as an expert, and (c) creates responses that other members genuinely want to read. Supplement with direct 1:1 DMs to the 10 founding members you most want to see post: "Hey — would love your take on the question I posted in #field-reports this morning. Your perspective from [specific context] would really add to the conversation." Personal asks dramatically outperform open prompts.

**Problem: The community is generating engagement but zero pipeline after 6 months**
Solution: The community is building brand, not pipeline — which means either (a) members are not ICP, or (b) the conversion bridges aren't being used. Audit the member list: what percentage match your actual ICP by job title, company size, and buying authority? If < 60% match ICP exactly, you have an audience problem — tighten gatekeeping and recruit more intentionally. If ICP composition is healthy, the issue is conversion bridge inactivity. Pull a list of all members who have exhibited 2+ pipeline readiness signals in the past 90 days and have not received a Bridge 1 or Bridge 2 outreach. Assign each to a founder or team member for personalized outreach within one week. Community pipeline doesn't happen passively — it requires a human to make the first move when the signal appears.

**Problem: The founder can't sustain 3-4 hours per week on community — it's not scaling**
Solution: This is by design a founder-heavy activity in the first 90 days, but should become team-supported by month 4. The transition playbook: (1) Identify your top 3 super members and formally give them a "Community Curator" role with specific responsibilities (seeding one channel each, co-hosting one event per quarter); (2) Hire or designate a team member (often a content marketer or customer success hire) to own day-to-day moderation and weekly programming; (3) The founder's role shifts from content creator to "celebrity guest" — appearing at monthly live events, doing occasional field reports, and handling all Bridge 2 and Bridge 3 conversion outreach personally. Founder presence remains essential for pipeline conversion even when day-to-day operations are delegated.
