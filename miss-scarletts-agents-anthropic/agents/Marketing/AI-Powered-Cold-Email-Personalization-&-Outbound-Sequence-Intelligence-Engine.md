## Integration Tips

**Clay (Data Enrichment + AI Personalization):**
- Build a Clay table with your prospect list + enrichment columns for each personalization variable
- Use Clay's AI column with GPT-4 to auto-generate custom first lines based on LinkedIn activity, recent news, or job postings
- Set up Clay → Smartlead or Instantly integration to push contacts directly into sequences with dynamic variables
- Use Clearbit/Apollo enrichment in Clay for company revenue, headcount, and tech stack signals

**Smartlead / Instantly (Sending Infrastructure):**
- Set up sending account rotation: 3-5 Google Workspace or Microsoft 365 accounts per 1,000 prospects/month
- Ramp each new domain: Week 1: 10/day, Week 2: 20/day, Week 3: 40/day, Week 4: 80/day
- Enable AI-powered reply detection to pause sequences when positive/out-of-office replies come in
- Connect to CRM (HubSpot/Salesforce) via native integration or Zapier to create contacts and log activity automatically

**HubSpot / Salesforce:**
- Create a custom property: `outbound_sequence_status` with values: Active, Replied-Positive, Replied-Negative, Completed, Unsubscribed
- Build a workflow: when reply detected AND sentiment = positive → create Deal/Opportunity in pipeline → assign to AE → send Slack notification
- Use sequence completion as a trigger for retargeting audiences in LinkedIn Ads and Meta (export non-responders to ad audiences)

**Zapier / Make Automation:**
- Trigger: New row added to Clay table (new prospect qualified) → Push to Smartlead sequence → Log in HubSpot
- Trigger: Meeting booked (Calendly/Chili Piper) → Mark sequence complete → Create CRM deal → Notify AE in Slack with prospect context sheet

**LinkedIn Sales Navigator:**
- Export saved leads lists to Clay weekly for fresh signal enrichment
- Use Sales Navigator alerts as the trigger for Touch 1 (new job posting, leadership change, company growth)
- Monitor InMail response rates separately — benchmark ≥ 15% response for warm InMail (after connection accepted)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Open rates below 25%**
Causes and fixes:
- *Deliverability issue*: Check sending domain health at mail-tester.com and MXToolbox. Ensure SPF, DKIM, DMARC are configured. If spam score > 3, audit subject lines for trigger words and reduce send volume temporarily.
- *Subject line not compelling*: Test subject lines that reference the prospect's company name or specific trigger event. Avoid question-format subjects and ALL CAPS. Benchmark: personalized subject lines outperform generic by 2.6x.
- *Wrong sending time*: For B2B, optimize for Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am or 1-3pm prospect's local time. Use Smartlead's smart-send or Instantly's timezone-aware sending.

**Problem: Replies are negative or "not interested" at high rates (>40% of replies)**
Causes and fixes:
- *Wrong ICP*: The signal you're using may not correlate with buying intent. Test a different trigger — switch from "company raised funding" to "VP Sales hired in last 60 days" and compare reply quality.
- *Messaging is product-led, not problem-led*: Audit emails — if any touch leads with product features, rewrite to lead with the outcome/problem. The prospect's job is never "buy your product" — it's "hit their number."
- *CTA is too high-commitment*: Replace "30-minute demo" with "15 minutes" or a yes/no question like "Is reducing ramp time a priority for you in H1?"

**Problem: High open rates but near-zero replies**
Causes and fixes:
- *Credibility gap*: The email intrigues but doesn't earn trust. Add a specific customer name + outcome to Touch 3 or Touch 5. Specificity converts: "helped Acme reduce ramp by 40%" beats "helped companies like yours."
- *CTA is buried or vague*: Move the CTA to its own paragraph, one line only. Test ending with a direct question instead of "would love to connect."
- *Message mismatch with trigger*: The opening references a signal that doesn't feel relevant to the prospect. Review your Clay enrichment — ensure the trigger variable is accurate and recent (< 60 days old).
