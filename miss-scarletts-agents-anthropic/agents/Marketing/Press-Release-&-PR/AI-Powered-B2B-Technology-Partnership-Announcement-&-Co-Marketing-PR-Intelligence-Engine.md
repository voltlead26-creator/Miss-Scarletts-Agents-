## Overview
Transforms a technology partnership, integration launch, or strategic alliance into a coordinated, dual-brand PR and demand generation program — covering joint press release architecture, co-authored blog strategy, media pitch personalization, social amplification playbooks, and pipeline attribution — so both companies maximize earned media coverage, generate net-new pipeline from the announcement, and build long-term brand authority. Use this when announcing a new integration, reseller agreement, OEM deal, co-sell motion, or strategic technology alliance where both brands need to win.

## Integration Tips
- **HubSpot Campaigns**: Create a dedicated campaign for the partnership announcement to track all generated contacts, email engagement, landing page conversions, and pipeline under one attribution umbrella. Both companies should coordinate UTM parameters to enable cross-company attribution reporting.
- **Salesforce Partner Community**: If running a co-sell motion, tag all announcement-generated leads with partner source in Salesforce. Use Partner Community to share deal registration and mutual customer account lists with appropriate data privacy guardrails.
- **PR Newswire / Business Wire**: Schedule joint press release distribution 7-10 days in advance of target date. Use both companies' accounts for simultaneous distribution to maximize pickup. Optimize the online version with hyperlinks to joint landing page and both company websites.
- **Cision / Meltwater**: Set up media monitoring alerts for both company names + partner name combination to track every earned media pickup in real time. Export a weekly coverage report to share with both PR teams and leadership.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Run matched audience paid amplification against a target account list of joint prospect companies for 2-4 weeks following the announcement to ensure every target account sees the partnership message at least 3 times across both company pages.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Create a tracker for "partnership mention" in sales calls to identify when reps are using the announcement in their outreach and which companies are responding. Use this data to build case studies and refine the joint sales playbook.
- **Zapier Automation**: Build a Zap that fires when someone submits a form on the joint landing page → adds to HubSpot list → triggers a partnership-specific nurture sequence and simultaneously notifies both companies' sales teams of new joint interest.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner company has different announcement timelines and wants to delay — but we need to announce now for a conference deadline**
Solution: Decouple your announcement from a fully joint press release if necessary. Announce the integration from your side as a product update with a quote from the partner's executive that they can approve independently, and the partner can publish their own announcement or supporting content in the following week. Use a "rolling announcement" strategy where your press release goes out first and the partner issues a supporting statement or separate release within 48-72 hours. Make sure embargo and quote approval timelines are written into the partnership agreement before you start the PR process to prevent this scenario in future announcements.

**Problem: No named customer is willing to be quoted or referenced publicly for the announcement**
Solution: This is extremely common with new integrations — the first cohort of customers is still evaluating. Use these alternatives in order of effectiveness: (1) Aggregate anonymized outcome data ("across early access customers, median detection time decreased by X%"); (2) Reference the problem without a named customer ("CISOs at three Fortune 500 financial institutions told us..."); (3) Reference the customer in a bylined blog post with softer attribution ("a healthcare customer using both platforms recently told our team..."); (4) Recruit a design partner with a named reference commitment in exchange for product roadmap input. Set a 90-day goal to convert one early adopter to a full named case study with video testimonial.

**Problem: Media coverage is low despite press release distribution — only picked up by wire syndication, no original stories**
Solution: Wire distribution without journalist relationships rarely generates original stories for partnership announcements. Retroactively run a media strategy: (1) Identify 5-8 specific journalists who have covered either company OR competitor partnerships in the last 6 months — pitch them a follow-on exclusive angle (customer interview, technical deep-dive, market data story) rather than the press release itself; (2) Submit the announcement to relevant category newsletters (e.g., security weekly roundup newsletters, data engineering Substacks) which often generate more qualified traffic than top-tier media; (3) Repitch the story 30 days later with a "one month in" data angle — "X customers activated the integration in the first 30 days and median detection time improved by Y%" is a stronger story than the original announcement.
