## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Create a campaign object for the funding announcement and tag all outbound emails, inbound form fills, and deal touches with a UTM source (`utm_source=funding-announcement&utm_campaign=[round]-[date]`). Build a dashboard that tracks deal velocity for all opportunities touched within 90 days of announcement vs. the prior 90-day baseline. This gives you the pipeline impact number you'll need for your next board deck.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Within 24 hours of announcement, use the LinkedIn Matched Audiences feature to serve your announcement content as a Thought Leader Ad to a custom audience of your ICP. Upload your prospect list + a lookalike audience (2-3x list size). Budget $5,000-$10,000 for the first 30 days to amplify organic momentum with paid reach.
- **Press Release Distribution (PR Newswire / Business Wire / GlobeNewswire):** Use wire distribution for SEO and financial news syndication value, not for generating top-tier reporter interest. Wire coverage gets you Google News indexing and Bloomberg Terminal syndication — it does not replace direct journalist outreach. Allocate 30% of your PR budget to wire and 70% to direct relationship-driven outreach.
- **Notion / Google Docs:** Build a shared Announcement War Room document with your leadership team that includes: embargo lift countdown, reporter tracking grid (who you pitched, their response, deadline), real-time coverage log, and social media amplification checklist. Assign a single person as "coverage quarterback" for the 72-hour announcement window.
- **Lattice / Rippling (HR):** Simultaneously trigger your talent brand campaign in your ATS/HRIS. The 72-hour window after a funding announcement is the peak moment for inbound applications. Make sure open roles are updated, your careers page reflects the new funding and headcount plan, and Glassdoor/LinkedIn company page is current before embargo lifts.
- **Slack automation:** Set up a #funding-announcement Slack channel and use Zapier to auto-post any new media coverage (via Google Alerts RSS → Zapier → Slack). The real-time coverage feed keeps your team energized and makes it easy to reshare each piece of coverage as it lands.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** No Tier 1 reporter is responding to pitches and all coverage is wire syndication rewrites.
**Solution:** Tier 1 reporters receive 200+ funding pitch emails on busy news days. Your press release copy-pasted into an email is deleted immediately. Fix: Personalize every Tier 1 pitch with (a) a specific reference to a recent article they wrote that your story connects to, (b) one data point that isn't in your press release — an exclusive stat or customer anecdote, and (c) a clear story angle that's genuinely different from "startup raises money." If you're Series A or smaller, don't chase TechCrunch for the funding story — go for a profile piece (founder journey, market category creation, or customer outcome story) and mention the funding as supporting context. Funding stories below $20M rarely get standalone Tier 1 coverage unless there is a compelling narrative beyond the dollar amount.

**Problem:** The funding announcement generated a spike in website traffic and social engagement but zero pipeline movement — no new demo requests, no reactivated closed-lost deals.
**Solution:** This means your demand conversion layer was missing. The announcement generated awareness but not action. Fix for next time: (1) Add a CTA to every piece of announcement content ("See what $47M faster means for your team" → demo link), (2) Email your entire prospect database within 24 hours of announcement lift — not with the press release, but with a 3-sentence "here's what this means for you specifically" email that connects your funding to their pain, (3) Have your AEs reach out personally to all Tier 1 deals in pipeline using the funding as a "reason to call" on the day of announcement. Funding announcements have a 72-hour attention window — after that, the demand capture opportunity is largely gone.

**Problem:** Investor quote sounds generic and corporate ("We believe in the team and the market opportunity").
**Solution:** Investor quotes that don't say anything specific are actively damaging because they signal the investor didn't care enough to provide a real perspective. Before embargo lifts, schedule a 20-minute call with your lead investor partner and ask three specific questions: (1) What specific data point in your diligence made you most confident in the investment? (2) What's the market signal you saw that convinced you the timing was right NOW vs. 18 months ago? (3) Who is the specific buyer persona you've seen win with this product, and what outcome did they achieve? Use their answers to write a quote that contains at least one specific metric, one market insight, and one forward-looking statement. Get it approved by their comms team 48 hours before embargo lifts — not the morning of.
