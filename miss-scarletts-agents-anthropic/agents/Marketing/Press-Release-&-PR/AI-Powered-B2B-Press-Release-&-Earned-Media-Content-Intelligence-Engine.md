## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Journalists aren't responding to pitches**
Fix: Check if the subject line leads with your company name — it shouldn't. Rewrite with the story angle or market problem first. Also check pitch length — most ignored pitches are over 200 words. Cut to under 120 and remove all adjectives. If pitch timing coincides with industry conferences or earnings season, delay by 2-3 days as journalist inboxes are flooded.

**Problem: Press release sounds like marketing copy, not news**
Fix: Run the "So what?" test on every paragraph — if the answer is "it's great for us," cut it. Replace superlatives ("industry-leading," "best-in-class") with specifics (numbers, customer names, comparison data). Read the first paragraph aloud: if it doesn't tell you the news in the first sentence, rewrite the lede. Add an external data source (Gartner, IDC, industry report) to the second paragraph to establish market stakes.

**Problem: Got coverage but no pipeline lift**
Fix: Coverage without conversion usually means the CTA in the press release and social posts is too soft. Add a specific, time-limited offer to the social amplification: "We're opening 10 beta spots for [new feature] for qualified mid-market teams — DM us." Also check if Sales has been briefed — if they don't know coverage dropped, they can't reference it in outbound. Send a Sales Slack alert within 1 hour of embargo lift with 3 approved talking points and the journalist's article link.
