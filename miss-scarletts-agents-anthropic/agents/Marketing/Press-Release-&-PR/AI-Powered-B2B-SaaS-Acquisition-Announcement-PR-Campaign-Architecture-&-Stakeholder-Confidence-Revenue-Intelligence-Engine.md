## Troubleshooting

**Problem: A customer called your sales rep threatening to cancel before the official announcement email arrived.**
Root cause: Either there was an internal leak, or they read the press release before your email deployed. This is why the customer email must go out simultaneously with or before the press release goes live — not after.
Fix: Accelerate your customer email deployment to go out 5 minutes before the embargo lifts. Give your CS team a "call log" script they can use immediately for any customer who calls before the email arrives: "Yes, I was about to reach out to you — here's exactly what it means for your account..."

**Problem: Competitor reps are calling your customers within hours of the announcement with specific FUD talking points.**
This will happen. Assume it. Plan for it.
Fix: Pre-brief your top 20 named account CSMs the evening before the announcement (under NDA) so they can make personal calls starting at 7:00 AM on announcement day — before any competitor has a chance to land. The first conversation a customer has about the acquisition should be with you, not your competitor. Time-to-first-contact wins this battle.

**Problem: The press release was picked up but the coverage is negative — focused on uncertainty or integration risk rather than strategic upside.**
Fix: This is usually a failure of the press release headline and lede. If the headline was "Company A Acquires Company B for $X" rather than "Company A and Company B Join Forces to [Customer Benefit]," the coverage will center on the transaction rather than the value. For future releases: reframe. For immediate remediation: engage your top-tier press contacts with a "here's the story you should be writing" follow-up pitch within 48 hours, using the customer-benefit narrative and an offer for a CEO interview.
