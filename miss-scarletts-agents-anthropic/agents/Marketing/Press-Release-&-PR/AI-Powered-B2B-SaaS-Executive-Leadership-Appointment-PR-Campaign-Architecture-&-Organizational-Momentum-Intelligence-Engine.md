## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The press release feels generic and no journalists are responding.**
Solution: Executive hire press releases fail when they lead with the hire rather than the story. Reframe the pitch around the market trend this hire signals — journalists want the "so what for the industry," not the "who joined what company." Try pitching the angle: "Why [category] companies are raiding [Tier-1 Company] for executive talent — and what it signals about where the market is heading."

**Problem: The new executive is uncomfortable with the social content or wants to write their own posts.**
Solution: Use the Advanced Version's social posts as a brief, not copy — share them as "here's the strategic messaging framework and a draft to react to." Most executives will rewrite in their own voice, which is actually better. Your job is to give them the structure, the key messages, and the data points; let them own the words.

**Problem: The announcement is generating PR buzz but no pipeline impact.**
Solution: The announcement alone won't create pipeline — the demand hooks do. Audit whether sales actually used the announcement talking points in the 2 weeks following launch. If not, run a 30-minute sales enablement session with the new executive on the call to give the announcement credibility and make reps feel equipped to reference it.
