## Integration Tips

- **CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot):** Add "Awards Cited" field to opportunity records so you can track which recognitions appear in sales notes and correlate with win rates. Build a report: Win Rate by Awards Mentioned in Opportunity.
- **G2/TrustRadius:** Set up automated review request triggers in your customer success platform (Gainsight, Totango, ChurnZero) — fire review asks at Day 90 post-go-live and post-expansion.
- **Notion/Confluence:** Build a shared Awards Submission Library — store approved company narrative blocks, metrics, and customer quotes so any team member can assemble a submission in 2 hours instead of 20.
- **Slack:** Create `#awards-program` channel with automated reminders 30 days before each submission deadline. Pin the Awards Calendar with owner assignments.
- **Press Release Distribution:** Use Business Wire or PR Newswire for Tier 1 wins; PRWeb or EIN Presswire for Tier 2; direct journalist email for analyst evaluations (never wire these — brief journalists directly under embargo).
- **Website CMS:** Create a `/recognition` page that auto-aggregates award badges. Use schema markup (`Award` structured data) so Google surfaces recognition in brand search results.
- **Sales Enablement (Highspot/Seismic/Guru):** Create an "Awards & Recognition" card that reps can insert into proposals. Auto-update when new awards are won — reps should never be using stale recognition claims.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Submissions consistently lose despite strong metrics.**
Solution: The writing is probably too self-promotional. Award judges are practitioners reading 200+ submissions — they're pattern-matching for specificity and honesty. Replace every adjective ("innovative," "industry-leading") with a number. Replace "we help customers save time" with "customers reduce X process from 14 days to 6 hours." If you've lost twice in the same category, request judge feedback — most organizations provide it on request.

**Problem: Won awards but no pipeline impact — reps aren't using them.**
Solution: Awards die in press releases. The problem is activation, not the award itself. Build a 1-slide "Awards & Recognition" insert for every proposal template. Add the award to the email signature of every AE and SDR. Run a 15-minute SKO session showing reps how to introduce recognition naturally: "Third parties have evaluated us against every alternative — here's what they found." Track in CRM.

**Problem: G2 review count is too low to achieve Leader status.**
Solution: Review velocity is a function of ask volume and timing. Most companies ask once at go-live and never again. Build a 3-touch ask sequence: (1) Day 90 post-go-live in-app prompt, (2) Post-QBR email from CSM, (3) Post-expansion milestone from AE. Incentivize internally — G2 review count is a CS team metric, not just marketing. A company with 200 customers and 20 reviews has a 10% ask rate — target 35%+.
