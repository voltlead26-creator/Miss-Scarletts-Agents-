# AI-Powered B2B Journalist Pitch Engineering & Earned Media Coverage Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** pr, earned-media, media-relations, b2b, automation, content, outreach

## Overview
Generates hyper-personalized journalist pitches, identifies the highest-probability media targets for a specific announcement or story angle, and produces a complete earned media outreach campaign with follow-up sequences — eliminating the hours of manual media research and rewrite cycles that kill most B2B PR programs.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior B2B PR strategist with 15 years of media relations experience. I need you to create a journalist pitch for the following:

Company: [Company Name]
Industry/Category: [e.g., B2B SaaS, cybersecurity, fintech]
Announcement/Story: [What happened — product launch, funding, research, milestone]
Key differentiator: [What makes this genuinely newsworthy vs. self-promotional]
Target outlets: [e.g., TechCrunch, VentureBeat, The Information, trade press like SC Magazine]

Create:
1. A subject line (under 8 words) that would compel a jaded tech journalist to open the email
2. A 150-word pitch email that leads with the news hook (not the company), names a specific journalist angle, includes one compelling data point, and ends with a clear ask
3. A 7-day follow-up sequence (Day 3, Day 5, Day 7) with different angles/assets for each touch
4. A "no response" pivot pitch that repositions the story for a different beat

Format each as copy-paste ready emails. Do not use hollow phrases like "game-changing" or "revolutionary."

JOURNALIST TARGETING FRAMEWORK:
For this announcement, identify and pitch to THREE tiers of media targets:

Tier 1 — National Tech/Business Press (1-2 targets):
Identify the specific beat reporter at each outlet whose recent 5 articles align with this story angle. Do not suggest the outlet generically — name the journalist persona, their known interest area, and the specific angle that fits their current beat coverage.

Tier 2 — Vertical Trade Press (2-3 targets):
Identify the trade publications where this company's buyers actually read. For each, craft a different angle that serves that outlet's specific reader — not the same pitch reskinned.

Tier 3 — Newsletter/Podcast/Creator Press (2-3 targets):
Identify B2B-relevant newsletters, industry podcasts, or LinkedIn creators who cover this category. These generate outsized word-of-mouth in buying communities despite lower domain authority.

DELIVERABLES — produce all of the following:

1. NEWSWORTHINESS AUDIT
Rate this announcement on five criteria (1-10 scale with reasoning):
- Timeliness (is there a news peg — trend, regulation, competitor move?)
- Magnitude (does the data/scale matter to journalists' readers?)
- Conflict (does this challenge a prevailing belief or dominant player?)
- Human interest (is there a customer story or person at the center?)
- Exclusivity potential (can you offer a journalist first-look advantage?)
Overall verdict: Tier 1 story / Tier 2 story / Trade-only story / Not yet ready (with specific fix)

2. STORY ANGLES (3 variants)
Angle A — Trend story: Position the announcement as evidence of a larger market shift
Angle B — Data story: Lead with a proprietary research finding or customer outcome stat
Angle C — Controversy/contrarian: Challenge a widely held assumption in the industry

For each angle, provide:
- One-sentence story frame (what the journalist would write as their lede)
- The headline they might use
- Why an editor would greenlight this story

3. PITCH EMAILS (per outlet tier)
For each target, write:
- Subject line: Under 8 words, no buzzwords, curiosity-driving
- Opening hook: Lead with the news, not the company (first sentence names the insight/data/event)
- The ask: Specific — an interview, a briefing, an exclusive data set, or a contributed piece
- Offer: What you'll give them that no one else gets (exclusive data, first interview, embargoed access)
- Length: 150 words max for Tier 1; 200 words for trade press

4. FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCE (7-day cadence)
Day 3 — "New angle" follow-up: Offer a different story frame, attach a supporting asset
Day 5 — "Social proof" follow-up: Add a customer quote or early reaction from the market
Day 7 — "Last touch" follow-up: Short, direct, offer to take it off their plate entirely (written contribution, exclusive data summary)
No-response pivot (Day 14): Reposition for a different beat or suggest a roundup inclusion

5. CONTRIBUTED ARTICLE BRIEF
If the journalist doesn't bite, you need an owned placement. Create:
- Title and 3 section headers for a byline article that carries the same story
- Target publication for the contributed piece (specific outlet and section)
- 200-word abstract to pitch to their editors

6. MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK
Define success metrics for this campaign:
- Tier 1 coverage hit: [specific outlet and story type]
- Share of voice benchmark: How many pickups constitute a successful campaign?
- Secondary metrics: Referral traffic from coverage, social amplification, sales team usage of press mentions
- Timeline: When to declare the campaign complete and debrief

CONSTRAINTS:
- Never use: "game-changing," "revolutionary," "disruptive," "best-in-class," "cutting-edge," "innovative"
- Every claim must be supportable — flag any assertion that requires a data source
- Pitches must be readable on mobile in under 30 seconds
- Follow-up emails must add new value — no "just checking in" touches
- All content must be ready to paste into Gmail/Outreach/HubSpot with zero editing required

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot CRM:** Create a "Media Contact" object type with journalist name, beat, outlet, last contacted date, and coverage history. Use sequences for the 7-day follow-up cadence
- **Muck Rack / Cision:** Export journalist contact lists tagged by beat keyword; import the personalized pitch emails as sequence templates per outlet tier
- **Notion:** Build a "Press Campaign" database with each journalist as a record — track pitch sent, follow-up status, response, coverage link, and sales team notification
- **Google Sheets + Zapier:** Log coverage pickups automatically via Google Alerts → Zapier → Sheets; trigger a Slack notification to sales leadership when a Tier 1 story lands
- **Apollo.io / Outreach:** Use the sequence builder for the 7-day follow-up cadence; set reply detection to auto-pause the sequence when a journalist responds
- **Loom:** Record a 60-second spokesperson teaser video to embed in the Day 5 follow-up — increases response rates by ~40% vs. text-only for trade press

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Pitches get no response despite compelling data**
Solution: The data is likely interesting but the angle isn't — journalists don't cover research, they cover stories. Reframe: Who is harmed by this finding? Who benefits? What should buyers/investors/operators do differently? Run the pitch through "Angle C" (the contrarian frame) before resending.

**Problem: Only trade press covers it; no Tier 1 interest**
Solution: Tier 1 journalists write for non-specialist general audiences. If your story is too technical or category-specific to explain in one sentence to a non-buyer, it belongs in trade press first. Build social proof with 3-5 trade placements, then re-pitch Tier 1 as a "trend story" with proof of market momentum.

**Problem: Journalist responds but story never publishes**
Solution: Ask directly: "What would need to be true for this to move forward?" Common blockers: (1) editor didn't greenlight it — offer to add a named enterprise customer for credibility; (2) reporter is on deadline elsewhere — offer a 2-week hold; (3) story isn't differentiated enough — offer exclusive data or a competing analyst quote to add controversy.
