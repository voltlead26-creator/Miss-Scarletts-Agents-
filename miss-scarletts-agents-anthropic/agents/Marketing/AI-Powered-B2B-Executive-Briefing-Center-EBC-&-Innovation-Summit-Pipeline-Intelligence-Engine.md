### SECTION 3: EBC AGENDA DESIGN & FACILITATION GUIDE

Provide a full facilitator guide for a 3-hour executive visit:

**Pre-arrival (30 min before):**
- Facility setup and personalization (branded welcome materials with their logo, name cards, printed industry landscape map showing where they sit vs. competitors)
- Team briefing: each internal participant gets a 1-page brief with visitor names, their role, their priority, and the specific conversation prompt they're responsible for during the visit
- Welcome gift setup: [personalized executive leave-behind — e.g., signed copy of relevant business book, custom industry report, quality branded item — never a generic bag of swag]

**Welcome & Alignment (15 min):**
- Facilitator opens: "Before we begin, I want to make sure this 3 hours is genuinely useful for [economic buyer name]. Can you take 60 seconds and share the one thing you're hoping to learn or decide today?" [Capture this verbatim — it tells you where to spend the time]
- Set the agenda and give them permission to redirect: "Here's what we planned. Tell us if you'd rather go deeper on any of these, or skip one entirely."

**Vision & Industry Perspective (20 min):**
- NOT a company pitch. Frame as a peer executive discussing the industry shift that makes the status quo untenable.
- Structure: (1) The macro shift affecting their business category; (2) How leading companies are responding vs. lagging companies; (3) The organizational capability required to win in this environment; (4) How your customers have built that capability.
- Challenger design: Create constructive tension by showing them a data point or insight they haven't seen before that challenges their current approach.

**Customer Outcome Showcase (30 min):**
- Lead with 1–2 customer stories, each under 10 minutes, structured as: company situation (identical to their situation) → specific pain they were in → the decision they made → the measurable outcome in 90 days, 6 months, and 12 months → what it made possible for their business and career.
- Reserve 10 minutes for Q&A — let them drive. The questions reveal their priorities better than any discovery framework.

**Tailored Product Demonstration (30 min):**
- NOT a feature tour. Show the specific workflow that solves their most urgent pain — identified in the pre-briefing intelligence brief.
- Structure: Problem setup (30 seconds showing their current state) → Your solution in action (the 3–5 minute workflow demo) → The downstream impact (what becomes possible with this data/capability) → The integration point with their existing stack.
- Facilitator rule: If they ask "can it do X?" the answer is either "yes, let me show you" or "not yet, here's our roadmap — but here's how customers are solving that today."

**Co-Creation Workshop or Roundtable (45 min):**
- Format A — Co-creation: "Here's a real customer situation similar to yours. Let's work through together how you'd approach this with our platform." Use a whiteboard (physical or virtual) and build the solution with them. This creates ownership before they've purchased.
- Format B — Executive roundtable: Bring in 1–2 additional current customers (virtual or in-person) for a peer conversation on a strategic topic. Your facilitator guides the discussion. Never pitch. Let peer testimony do the work.
- MEDDPICC signals to capture during this session: Metrics they care about (listen for the number they keep coming back to), Decision process questions ("how do you typically evaluate something like this?"), Paper process ("what does procurement look like for a deal this size?"), Identify champion reinforcement moments.

**Wrap-Up & Explicit Next Step (15 min):**
- Review: "We covered [X, Y, Z]. What was most valuable for you? What questions do you still have?"
- Ask for the next step by name: "Based on what you've seen today, what's the right next step for us? I want to suggest [specific next step with date] — does that work?"
- Never leave without a written, calendar-invited next step. If they won't commit to a next step, that's your most important deal signal.

**Executive-to-Executive Close (15 min — CEO/CRO only):**
- One-on-one or small group, informal — coffee after the formal session.
- Topics: their personal vision for the company, the board/investor pressure they're navigating, the career outcome they're building toward.
- Your executive's job: listen 80%, share 20%. Plant one strategic insight they'll remember. Ask "is there anything you'd need to feel completely confident moving forward?"

### SECTION 4: POST-EBC FOLLOW-UP SEQUENCES

Provide 3 tracks with complete copy:

**Track A — Hot (expressed interest or verbal commitment during visit):**

- Hour 4: Economic buyer email from your executive: Subject: "Great to meet you today, [Name]" — 4-sentence personal note referencing one specific thing they said, the peer story that resonated, the agreed next step, and one resource (customer case study or ROI model)
- Day 1: Champion email from deal AE: Subject: "Next steps from today's briefing" — bulleted recap of commitments made, proposed evaluation plan with milestone dates, and request to schedule technical deep dive / business case workshop / procurement kickoff
- Day 3: Value-add from your executive to economic buyer: an article, data point, or industry insight tailored to what they said their priorities are — no ask, pure value
- Day 7: Champion check-in: "Checking in on the evaluation — is there anything you need from us to move the technical review forward? Here's [specific resource they asked for]."

**Track B — Warm (engaged, attended, but no explicit next step committed):**
- Hour 4: Champion email from AE: "Thank you for making the trip today" — reference 2–3 moments from the visit, send the one deliverable they asked for, and softly propose the next step: "Would a 45-minute working session with [technical stakeholder] make sense this week?"
- Day 2: Economic buyer email from your executive: Subject: "[Their company] + [Your company] — the path forward" — 5-sentence note: (1) what you heard their priority is; (2) how your customers with the same priority achieved [specific outcome]; (3) one question that advances their thinking; (4) proposed next step; (5) offer to connect them with a reference customer
- Day 5: Personalized case study or ROI model delivered to champion (not yet to economic buyer) — tailored to their company size, industry, and pain. Ask champion to review and share it internally.
- Day 10: Champion call from AE — goal is to identify what happened internally since the visit, any new stakeholders, any competitor activity, and to re-secure the next step
- Day 21: Executive-to-executive reengagement from your CEO/CRO: Subject: "[Industry trend] and what it means for [their company]" — share a proprietary insight or data point, then ask: "Would it be valuable to get 30 minutes on the calendar to share what we're seeing from the rest of your industry?"

**Track C — Cold (attended but disengaged or unresponsive since visit):**
- Day 3: AE to champion: "Hope the visit was valuable — curious what you took away from it. Anything you'd like us to dig deeper on?"
- Day 7: Automated nurture email (from marketing): industry report or benchmark data relevant to their vertical — no sales pitch
- Day 14: Champion re-engagement from AE: "I know timing may not be right yet — totally fine. What would need to change on your end for this to become a priority?" [This question reveals the real objection]
- Day 21: Economic buyer email from your executive: Short (3 sentences): share one peer outcome, acknowledge that timing is everything, leave the door open: "Whenever you're ready to revisit, I'd love to connect you directly with [reference customer name, same title, same industry]."
- Day 30: Champion breakup email: "I don't want to keep showing up in your inbox if the timing isn't right. I'll follow up in 90 days — but if anything changes before then, here's my direct line." [Breakup emails often reactivate cold deals.]

- Executive sponsor confirmed: yes/no (economic buyer agreed to be the point of contact going forward)
- Champion strength score: 1–5 post-visit (did their internal credibility increase?)
- Competitive status: displaced, still evaluating, chose competitor, no decision

**Program-level KPIs (quarterly):**
- EBC visits completed vs. planned
- Pipeline generated (new) or accelerated (existing deals) — attributed to EBC program
- Win rate for deals with EBC visits vs. without (target: 20–35% lift)
- Average sales cycle for deals with EBC visits vs. without (target: 25–40% compression)
- Revenue closed and sourced by EBC program (quarterly and trailing 12 months)
- Net Promoter Score from visitors (post-visit survey, 2 questions: "How valuable was today?" and "How likely are you to recommend [Your Company] to a peer?")
- Reference customer pipeline: how many EBC visitors became reference customers, case studies, or advisory board members

**Program optimization levers:**
- If win rate is below 40% → Tighten qualification criteria; EBC is being used too early in the deal cycle or on wrong accounts
- If deal velocity isn't improving → Agenda isn't creating urgency; rebuild the co-creation session to be more specific to their pain
- If follow-up response rate is below 50% → Post-visit communications are too generic; rebuild Track A/B follow-up sequences with more personalization
- If executives aren't attending → Invitation approach isn't compelling; upgrade the invitation sender or the framing of what visitors will experience
- If pipeline isn't converting → Close motions after EBC aren't tight; rebuild the "wrap-up and explicit next step" segment

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Economic buyers attend but don't engage or leave early.
**Solution:** Your agenda is too product-focused. Rebuild the vision session to lead with an industry insight they haven't seen — something that challenges their current assumptions and positions your company as a strategic advisor, not a vendor. The rule: if they could learn what you're sharing by reading your website, it's not EBC-worthy.

**Problem:** You're getting EBC visits but not winning deals or compressing cycles.
**Solution:** Your follow-up sequences are too generic or too slow. The first 24 hours after an EBC visit are when trust is highest and deal momentum is greatest. If your first follow-up arrives 2+ days later or reads like a standard sales email, you've wasted the visit. Rebuild your Track A sequence to be 5 sentences, hyper-specific to what the economic buyer said in the room, and contain exactly one clear next step.

**Problem:** Champions are requesting EBC visits but economic buyers aren't attending.
**Solution:** Your invitation approach isn't compelling enough for the economic buyer. The invitation must come from your executive (CEO/CRO) to their executive (CFO/CEO/CRO), reference a specific peer company outcome, and make the visit feel like a strategic conversation — not a vendor demo. Add one line: "I've asked [peer executive name] to join us virtually for 20 minutes to share how they approached this." Peer-to-peer commitment lifts economic buyer attendance by 40%+.
