## Overview
Transforms your raw content analytics data into a comprehensive monthly editorial intelligence brief: which content drives pipeline, which wastes budget, what to create next, and how to report content contribution to the CMO and board. Use this when your content team is producing without a clear feedback loop, when leadership questions content ROI, or when you need a data-driven content roadmap for the next quarter.

- Content efficiency ratio: pipeline dollars influenced per $1,000 of fully-loaded content spend, benchmark $3,000–$8,000 for B2B SaaS
- Editorial velocity: number of publishable pieces produced per content FTE per month, benchmark 4–8 for B2B long-form programs

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Use HubSpot's Content Analytics and Attribution Reports to pull content-sourced and content-influenced pipeline data. Set up custom properties for "First Content Touch" and "Last Content Touch" on contact and deal records. Use the Multi-Touch Revenue Attribution Report (Marketing Hub Enterprise) to populate Module 4 data.
- **Salesforce + Pardot/Marketing Cloud**: Build a Salesforce report that joins Campaign Influence data with Opportunity records. Use the Pardot First-Touch and Last-Touch attribution fields. Export to Google Sheets or Looker for the scorecard module.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Set up GA4 conversion events for each gated content form, newsletter signup, and demo request. Use Explorations → Funnel Exploration to trace content pages visited before conversion. Export to Looker Studio for automated dashboard refresh.
- **Semrush / Ahrefs**: Use Position Tracking and the Content Audit tool to automate the triage module. Export keyword ranking changes and organic traffic estimates for the top/bottom content asset lists.
- **Notion / Airtable**: Use this report as the data source for your editorial calendar. Build an Airtable base with a "Content Asset Value" field (Compound / Stable / Declining / Dead Weight) updated monthly from this prompt's output.
- **Slack integration**: Set up a monthly Slack post to the #marketing-leadership channel with the headline numbers from Module 6. Use Zapier + OpenAI API to auto-generate the executive summary from your GA4 and HubSpot data pulls.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Content attribution data is unreliable — CRM shows almost no content-influenced pipeline.**
Solution: This is usually a UTM tracking gap, not a real attribution gap. Start by auditing the last 20 closed-won deals manually: open each contact record and review the activity timeline for content touches. If you find content touches that aren't being captured in pipeline attribution, your tracking is broken at the UTM or CRM campaign association layer. Fix UTM parameters on all content CTAs first, then rebuild the attribution view. Do not try to build Module 4 reporting before this is fixed.

**Problem: The editorial recommendations feel generic — the AI isn't generating ideas specific enough to drive real pipeline.**
Solution: Add more specificity to the competitive context and ICP sections. Instead of "CFO at SaaS companies," write "CFO at Series B SaaS companies with $8M–$25M ARR who are currently using Excel and QuickBooks and are evaluating moving to a dedicated FP&A tool for the first time." The more specific the ICP description and the more real data you provide (actual top posts, actual competitor URLs), the more precise the editorial recommendations will be.

**Problem: The 90-day roadmap is too ambitious for the content team's actual capacity.**
Solution: After generating the roadmap, run it through a capacity reality check. List all content pieces, estimate hours each requires, divide by your team's available weekly content hours (typically 60–70% of FTE hours after meetings and admin), and cut until the roadmap fits. Prioritize by pipeline ROI score from Module 3, not by what seems most interesting. A smaller roadmap executed well outperforms an ambitious roadmap executed poorly every time.
