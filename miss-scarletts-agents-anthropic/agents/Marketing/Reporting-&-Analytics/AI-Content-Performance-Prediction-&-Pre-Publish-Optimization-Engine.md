## Overview
This engine scores your content across six performance dimensions before it's published — predicting organic traffic potential, ranking probability, conversion likelihood, and pipeline influence using AI analysis against competitive benchmarks and buyer intent signals. Use it when you want to know whether a piece of content is worth distributing or needs optimization before you invest in promotion.

## Integration Tips

- **Semrush / Ahrefs**: Feed the engine the top 10 ranking URLs for your target keyword before submitting content. Export their word count, readability scores, and keyword density to give the AI concrete competitive benchmarks.
- **Clearscope / MarketMuse / SurferSEO**: Use their semantic topic suggestions as your input for Dimension 2 (Topical Depth) — paste their "required terms" list into the prompt to get more precise gap analysis.
- **HubSpot / Marketo**: After content publishes, create a custom property tracking "Pre-Publish Readiness Score" on each content asset. Use this score as a predictor variable when analyzing which content drives pipeline, closing the feedback loop.
- **Notion / Google Sheets**: Build a "Content Scorecard" database where each row is a content piece and each column is a dimension score. Run this engine on every piece at draft stage and track improvement trends across quarters.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate the workflow — when a blog post is moved to "Ready for Review" in your CMS, trigger a Slack notification with a template to submit it through this engine before scheduling distribution.
- **Google Search Console**: After 90 days, compare actual ranking positions and CTR against the engine's predictions. Feed this data back into the prompt as "historical prediction accuracy" to calibrate future forecasts.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The engine gives vague suggestions like "add more depth" without telling me what specifically to write.
**Solution:** In your prompt, paste the H2/H3 outline of the top 3 competing pieces alongside your content. Explicitly instruct: "Compare my outline against competitors and list the specific subtopics and subheadings they cover that mine doesn't." This forces section-level gap identification rather than general advice.

**Problem:** Conversion Architecture scores are consistently low but I don't understand how to fix the CTAs.
**Solution:** Add this to the prompt: "For each CTA fix you recommend, rewrite the actual CTA copy as an example — don't just describe what to change. Follow the formula: [Specific action] + [Specific outcome] + [Specific timeframe or qualifier]." Example output should read: "Download the 12-week enablement program template used by 40+ Series B sales teams" — not "improve your CTA copy."

**Problem:** Organic traffic predictions feel too optimistic or pessimistic for my domain.
**Solution:** Calibrate the engine by providing 3 examples of content you've already published with known outcomes: "This piece published 6 months ago now gets X organic sessions/month and ranks at position Y for Z keyword." Ask the engine to adjust its prediction model based on this historical accuracy data before forecasting your new piece.
