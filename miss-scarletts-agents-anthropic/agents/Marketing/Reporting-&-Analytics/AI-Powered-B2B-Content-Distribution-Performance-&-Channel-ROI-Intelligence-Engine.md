## Overview
This engine analyzes how your content performs across every distribution channel — organic search, email, LinkedIn, paid amplification, content syndication, partner networks, and dark social — and generates a ranked optimization plan to reallocate distribution effort toward the channels driving the most pipeline. Use it when your content library is growing but you don't know which channels are actually moving deals, or when you need to make data-backed decisions on where to invest content amplification budget.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Export contact source attribution data by content piece via Reports > Analytics Tools. Map UTM parameters to channels and pull MQL-to-deal conversion by source.
- **Salesforce**: Use Campaign Influence reports to see which content campaigns influenced opportunities. Set up Primary Campaign Source field for single-touch attribution baseline.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Create custom exploration reports segmenting by content category + traffic source. Use Conversion Paths report to see multi-touch channel sequences.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Download performance data by post type and objective. Map organic + paid performance to pipeline using UTM tracking.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate weekly channel performance reports by pulling data from GA4 + HubSpot + LinkedIn APIs into a Google Sheet, then trigger a weekly AI analysis prompt.
- **Notion**: Build a "Distribution Intelligence Dashboard" page that aggregates weekly channel scores and auto-flags any channel dropping >15% in efficiency.
- **Bombora / TechTarget**: Request SAL-to-opportunity conversion data monthly — syndication vendors often don't surface this unless asked. Build it into your vendor QBR scorecard.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Can't attribute pipeline to LinkedIn organic because UTM parameters break on mobile.**
Solution: Use LinkedIn's native analytics for impressions/clicks as a proxy, then correlate weeks with high LinkedIn organic engagement to pipeline created within 14–21 days. Create a "LinkedIn organic influence" custom field in your CRM for sales reps to mark self-reported source.

**Problem: Email newsletter MQLs look high but sales won't work them.**
Solution: Segment newsletter MQLs by engagement depth (clicked 1 link vs. clicked 3+ links) and company firmographics. Build a secondary MQL scoring model specific to email — high-volume email openers at non-ICP companies inflate numbers without pipeline value. Tighten scoring before passing to sales.

**Problem: Content syndication generates downloads but zero pipeline.**
Solution: Syndication works best for awareness-stage content. If you're syndicating TOFU content and expecting BOFU outcomes, shift your syndication content to comparison guides, ROI calculators, and case studies. Also verify your follow-up nurture sequence activates within 24 hours of a download — most syndication leads go cold within 48 hours.
