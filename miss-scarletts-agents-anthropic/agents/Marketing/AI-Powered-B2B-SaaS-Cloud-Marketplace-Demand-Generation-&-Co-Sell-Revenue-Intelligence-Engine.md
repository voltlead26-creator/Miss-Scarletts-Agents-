## Troubleshooting

**Problem: PDM is unresponsive or disengaged after initial outreach**
Solution: PDMs are measured on co-sell revenue generated for their customers — not on ISV relationships. Lead with customer names and specific accounts in your territory where you have traction, not with your product pitch. Share 1-2 specific customer case studies from their vertical. Request an "account mapping" session (not a vendor demo), and come prepared with a list of 20-30 named accounts you're actively working in their territory. PDMs respond to specificity and proof of revenue potential, not relationship building for its own sake.

**Problem: Marketplace listing gets traffic but very low trial starts (<1%)**
Solution: The most common cause is a mismatch between the keyword that drove the click and the listing content. Audit your top 5 traffic-driving search terms and compare them against your listing headline and first 200 words. If buyers searching "dbt monitoring" land on a generic "data observability" headline, they'll bounce. Run A/B tests on your listing headline and short description using AWS Marketplace's listing analytics. Also audit your screenshots — enterprise buyers need to see familiar UI patterns and integration logos (Snowflake, dbt, Airflow icons) within the first 3 screenshots.

**Problem: Private offers sent but not accepted — deals stalling in procurement**
Solution: Private offers stall for three reasons: (1) No economic buyer engaged before the offer was sent — champion doesn't have authority to accept; (2) Procurement team doesn't know how to process a marketplace transaction and defaults to their standard vendor process; (3) The offer discount wasn't compelling enough relative to the friction of changing procurement process. Fix: Before sending the private offer, confirm you have a named economic buyer and a confirmed EDP/committed spend balance. Include a 1-page "How to Accept Your AWS Marketplace Private Offer" guide in the email. Offer to do a 30-minute call with their IT procurement or cloud finance team to walk through the acceptance process. Frame the marketplace transaction as "the path of least resistance" — no new vendor contract, no legal review, instant subscription from existing budget.
