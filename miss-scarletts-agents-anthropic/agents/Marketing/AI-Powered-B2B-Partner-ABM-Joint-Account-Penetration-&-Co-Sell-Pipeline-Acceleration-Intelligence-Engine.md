# AI-Powered B2B Partner ABM Joint Account Penetration & Co-Sell Pipeline Acceleration Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** partner-marketing, account-based-marketing, co-sell, abm, channel-marketing, joint-pipeline, buying-committee, b2b, enterprise, partner-abx

## Overview
Designs and executes coordinated vendor-partner Account-Based Marketing campaigns against a shared list of named target accounts — combining account overlap intelligence with AI-generated account-specific content, synchronized multi-thread outreach sequences, and clear vendor/partner role assignments — to generate joint pipeline from accounts that neither company could penetrate alone. Use it when you have 5-50 named accounts you share with a channel partner, GSI, or VAR and need to coordinate a 60-90 day joint pipeline sprint.

## Integration Tips

- **Crossbeam / Reveal:** Use these account overlap platforms to identify shared accounts before running this prompt. Export the overlap data and paste directly into the Account Overlap Data section of the Advanced Version. Crossbeam provides Salesforce-native integrations that auto-populate partner attribution fields.
- **Salesforce / HubSpot:** Use the CRM tracking setup output to add custom fields and create a shared campaign record both vendor and partner teams can update. Create a Shared Deal Room using Salesforce's Account Teams feature or HubSpot's collaboration tools.
- **Outreach / Salesloft / Apollo:** Build separate sequences for vendor SDR touches and partner BD touches, linked by shared account tags so both sides can see each other's activity history without duplicating effort.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Use TeamLink to identify mutual connections between vendor team and target account contacts — surface these to the partner before launching the sequence so the partner can make warm introductions before cold vendor outreach begins.
- **Slack Connect:** Create a dedicated Slack Connect channel with the partner for each Tier 1 account sprint. Use it to log real-time account signals, coordinate before calls, and debrief after every touch.
- **Notion / Google Docs:** Build the joint account plan as a shared live document both vendor AE and partner BD can update asynchronously. Include the buying committee map, sequence log, and next best action tracker.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner BDs agree to co-sell but never execute their assigned outreach touches.**
Solution: Front-load the program with actions only the vendor can take — partner touches should come after the vendor has warmed the account. Reduce the ask on partners to 2-3 high-leverage touches (executive intro email, LinkedIn connection, joint call attendance) rather than a full sequence. Tie partner co-sell participation to MDF release: partners must execute their assigned touches within 7 days or MDF for that account is paused.

**Problem: Vendor and partner are both working the same account independently and creating confusion for the prospect.**
Solution: Before launching, run a conflict check: pull all accounts from partner's CRM (with their permission via a shared Crossbeam connection) and cross-reference against vendor's open pipeline. Establish a 48-hour registration rule — first party to register an account owns vendor-partner coordination for that account. Add the conflict resolution clause to the Rules of Engagement document before any outreach begins.

**Problem: The joint value proposition resonates with you and the partner but prospects don't understand why two companies are calling them together.**
Solution: The buying committee sees this as "why are you both here?" rather than "what a great partnership." Fix the opening narrative: instead of leading with the partnership, lead with the outcome. "We're calling together because 11 companies like yours have achieved [specific metric] by using [partner's implementation expertise] to get [vendor's product] live in 30 days instead of 6 months" is a far stronger opening than "we've partnered with [partner] to offer you…" Rewrite all first-touch messages to lead with the account-specific outcome, not the partnership announcement.
