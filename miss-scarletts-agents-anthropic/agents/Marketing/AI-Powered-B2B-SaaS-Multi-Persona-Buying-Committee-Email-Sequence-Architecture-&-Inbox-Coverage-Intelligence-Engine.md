## Overview
Designs a coordinated, multi-persona email sequence system that targets every member of a B2B buying committee simultaneously — without message collisions, contradictory positioning, or persona-blind nurture that kills deals. Use it when your average deal involves 5+ stakeholders, when nurture sequences are treating a 7-person buying committee as a single contact, or when you need marketing to hold multiple inbox conversations in parallel while sales works the deal.

## Success Metrics

**Sequence health indicators:**
- Persona open rate by role: Target 35%+ for economic buyers, 45%+ for champions
- Cross-persona reply rate: 15%+ across sequence (any persona replying)
- Meeting scheduled from sequence (no SDR intervention): 8–12% of sequence entries
- Sequence-to-pipeline contribution: Track in CRM by opportunity source
- "Sequence saved a stalled deal" rate: Measure deals that re-engaged after 14+ day silence

**Sequence quality signals:**
- Unsubscribe rate below 0.5% (too high = wrong personas or wrong tone)
- Email forwarding rate: 3%+ indicates content being shared internally (strong signal)
- No more than 2% "hard bounce" — indicates persona targeting is accurate
- Time-to-meeting reduction: Compare average deal time for sequenced vs. non-sequenced accounts

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Use HubSpot Sequences with Enrollment criteria: Contact Property = Persona Type (custom field)
- Build Workflow branches on "Email open" and "Email click" properties per sequence step
- Use "Associated Company" record to group personas for cross-persona coordination logic
- Integrate with HubSpot Deals to pause sequences when deal stage advances or rep logs a call

**Salesforce + Pardot/Marketing Cloud:**
- Use Engagement Studio (Pardot) to build persona-specific nurture tracks with branching
- Create Salesforce Custom Objects for "Sequence Enrollment" to track which personas are in which track per Opportunity
- Use Salesforce Flow to trigger alerts to reps when high-value engagement (click/reply) is detected
- Use Account-level reports to visualize coverage across all personas for a single deal

**Salesloft / Outreach:**
- Build Cadences by persona type, use Cadence Steps with personalization tokens
- Use Salesloft's "Account View" to see all persona cadence status in a single deal
- Configure "Persona" as a custom field on Person records to drive cadence enrollment rules
- Set up Slack/CRM notifications for "high-intent" steps (pricing page click, case study download)

**Clay + AI Layer:**
- Use Clay to research each persona's recent LinkedIn posts, job tenure, and company news
- Feed research output as personalization variables into sequence templates
- Use Clay's AI column to generate persona-specific "reason for outreach" sentence per email
- Sync enriched data back to CRM to keep persona records current

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Personas at the same account are getting similar emails and some are forwarding to each other, creating confusion.**
Fix: Add a "shared account suppression" rule — when two personas at the same account are both in active sequences, ensure Email 2+ reference different proof points and different CTAs. Add a coordination tag ("Acme-buyingcommittee-Q3") to all emails for that account so you can audit the full conversation view before sending.

**Problem: The sequence is getting strong open rates but no replies or meeting bookings.**
Fix: Your subject lines are working but your CTAs are too high-friction. For complex deals, replace "Book a 30-minute call" with micro-CTAs: "Does this match what you're seeing?" (reply-focused), "Worth 10 minutes to review this?" (lower commitment), or "I can send the specific ROI model for [INDUSTRY] — useful?" (value-forward). Track reply rate vs. meeting-booked rate separately.

**Problem: The IT/security persona is opening emails but forwarding to unknown stakeholders, triggering ghost contacts in the CRM.**
Fix: This is actually a positive signal — they're sharing internally. Set up a CRM rule: when a new contact at a target account opens a tracked email for the first time with no prior enrollment, auto-enroll them in a "new stakeholder welcome" 2-email sequence (softer, context-setting) before adding them to the full persona sequence. Alert the rep immediately with context: "New stakeholder detected at [Account] — IT Director appears to have shared your email with [Title]."
