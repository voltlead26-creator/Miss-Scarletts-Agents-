## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CFO/CRO contacts won't accept meetings from SDRs; only respond to peer referrals**
Solution: Shift sourcing strategy to referral-first. Arm your existing CFO/CRO customers with a structured referral ask ("Would you be willing to introduce us to 2 peers who are facing the same [regulatory challenge]?"). Use roundtable attendees as introduction bridges — post-event, ask each attendee to nominate 1 peer for the next dinner. Build a formal CFO Reference Program with reciprocal value (early access to benchmark data, invitation to exclusive regulatory briefings).

**Problem: Deals stall at Third-Party Risk and Vendor Management — months of back-and-forth on security questionnaires**
Solution: Pre-build a "Regulatory-Ready Vendor Package" — a ZIP file containing: SOC2 Type II report (current year), penetration test executive summary, BCDR plan summary, data residency attestation, pre-filled SIG Lite questionnaire, GDPR/CCPA DPA template, and your standard MSA with financial services-specific addendums. Publish a vendor portal landing page (e.g., trust.clearvault.com) where IT security and Third-Party Risk teams can self-serve all documentation without waiting for sales. Track page visits as a deal acceleration signal.

**Problem: Regulatory content performs well but doesn't convert to pipeline — executives engage but don't respond to follow-up**
Solution: The problem is likely sequence timing and channel mismatch. Financial executives read content at 6–8am before their day starts and on weekends — adjust email send times to Tuesday/Wednesday 7am local time. More importantly, replace SDR email follow-up with a direct LinkedIn InMail from the CEO or Head of Risk Strategy personal account within 48 hours of the content download — reference the specific asset, ask a single genuine question about their situation. Response rate from CEO LinkedIn InMail to CRO/CFO is typically 3–5x higher than SDR email for regulated-industry executives.
