## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Customers agree to create content but never follow through.
**Solution:** The ask is too large. Break it into micro-commitments: first ask for 3 bullet points about their outcome (5-minute ask). You ghostwrite the full post from those bullets and send back for approval. Most executives will approve and post a polished draft because the editing friction is minimal. Add a 48-hour "I'll send you a draft" promise to create accountability on your side.

**Problem:** Advocate posts get high engagement but no attributable pipeline.
**Solution:** Dark social attribution is inherently leaky. Focus on closing the self-reported attribution loop: add "Did a peer or community post influence your decision to book this call?" to your discovery call script, and track it in CRM. Over 90 days, you'll see the self-reported influence rate. Secondarily, run an account overlap analysis: which accounts in your active pipeline had employees who engaged with an advocate post in the 90 days before they became an opportunity?

**Problem:** Compliance, legal, or HR policies prevent customers from posting publicly about vendors.
**Solution:** Pivot to dark social channels that are lower-visibility: private Slack community testimonials, anonymous review submissions to G2/TrustRadius, internal Champion letters they write to their own procurement team that your sales team can reference, and speaking at private invite-only peer roundtables (your company hosts, 10-15 peers, no recording). These dark formats often have higher conversion value than public posts anyway because they feel more authentic.
