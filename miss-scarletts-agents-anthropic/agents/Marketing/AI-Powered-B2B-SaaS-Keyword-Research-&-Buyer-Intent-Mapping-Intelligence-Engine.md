## Overview

This prompt runs a systematic keyword research and buyer intent mapping workflow for B2B SaaS — identifying high-value keyword clusters across every stage of the buying journey, surfacing competitive gaps, and producing a pipeline-weighted content prioritization matrix. Use it when building or refreshing your organic content strategy, entering a new market, or when organic traffic is not converting to pipeline at the rate it should.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior B2B SaaS SEO strategist with 12+ years of experience turning organic search into predictable pipeline. Run a comprehensive keyword research and buyer intent mapping analysis for [Your Company], a [product description] targeting [ICP: job title, company size, industry].

**Context:**
- Product: [What it does in one sentence]
- Primary competitors: [Competitor 1], [Competitor 2], [Competitor 3]
- Target buyer persona: [Title, company size, industry]
- Average deal size: $[X] ACV
- Current organic traffic: [Monthly sessions or "minimal/unknown"]

**Deliverables — produce each section:**

1. **Seed Keyword Universe** — Generate 40–60 seed keywords organized by category: problem-aware (what buyers search when experiencing the pain), solution-aware (searches for product categories and features), competitor-aware (brand + competitor comparisons), and job-to-be-done (outcome-based queries like "how to reduce employee attrition").

2. **Buyer Intent Classification** — For each seed keyword cluster, classify intent as:
   - TOFU (Top of Funnel): Educational, problem-aware, high volume, low commercial intent
   - MOFU (Middle of Funnel): Solution evaluation, category comparison, moderate intent
   - BOFU (Bottom of Funnel): High commercial intent, vendor comparison, demo/pricing queries

3. **Competitive Gap Analysis** — Identify keyword clusters where competitors rank in positions 1–10 but your domain has no presence. Flag clusters where competitors rank poorly (positions 11–30) as displacement opportunities.

4. **Pipeline Value Score** — Assign a 1–10 pipeline value score to each cluster based on: estimated monthly search volume × buyer intent strength × average deal size alignment × competitive feasibility.

5. **Content Prioritization Matrix** — Output a ranked table of the top 20 keyword clusters to target first, with: cluster name, primary keyword, estimated monthly searches, intent stage, pipeline value score, recommended content format (blog post, landing page, comparison page, tool/calculator), and suggested title.

6. **Quick-Win Opportunities** — Identify 5 keywords where ranking in the top 3 positions is achievable within 90 days based on low domain competition and strong buyer intent.

Format output as structured tables with an executive summary at the top: "These 20 clusters represent an estimated [X] monthly qualified visitors when ranked, mapping to approximately [Y] net new pipeline opportunities per month at our conversion rates."

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI generates keyword clusters that are too broad and won't realistically drive B2B pipeline (e.g., "project management" instead of "project management software for engineering teams").**
Solution: Add specificity to your ICP definition in the prompt. Include 3–5 verbatim pain points from customer reviews (pull from G2 or Gong call transcripts). The more specific the buyer persona, the more precise the keyword output. Also explicitly instruct: "Avoid generic head terms with >50,000 monthly searches that attract mixed consumer and enterprise intent."

**Problem: The competitive gap analysis doesn't surface realistic displacement opportunities — everything looks extremely competitive.**
Solution: Check that you've included your actual domain rating/authority score. If your DR is below 30, add "Focus exclusively on keywords with KD (keyword difficulty) below 35 and where competitors in positions 1–10 have DR below 50." This forces the prompt to find realistically winnable clusters for your current domain strength.

**Problem: The pipeline value scores feel arbitrary and not grounded in real business context.**
Solution: Provide actual conversion rate data: "Our organic visitor-to-MQL conversion rate is X%, MQL-to-opportunity rate is Y%, opportunity-to-close rate is Z%." With real funnel math, the AI can calculate genuine pipeline value per monthly search — turning the scoring model from relative prioritization into an actual dollar forecast per cluster.
