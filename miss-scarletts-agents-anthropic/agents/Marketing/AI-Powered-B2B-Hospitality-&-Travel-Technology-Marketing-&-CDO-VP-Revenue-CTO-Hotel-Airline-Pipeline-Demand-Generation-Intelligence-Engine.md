## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Long sales cycles mean pipeline metrics look weak at 30 and 60 days, creating internal pressure to kill campaigns too early.
**Solution:** Establish "pipeline influence" metrics separate from "pipeline sourced." Track account engagement velocity (number of touchpoints per account per week) as a leading indicator. Show that influenced accounts close 2x faster than uninfluenced. Present a 6-month pipeline projection model to leadership in Week 1 so expectations are set before pressure mounts.

**Problem:** Hospitality buyers ghost after initial interest — they're operationally overwhelmed, especially during peak travel seasons (summer, holidays).
**Solution:** Map your outreach calendar to the hospitality demand calendar. Avoid reaching out during peak check-in periods (weekends, US holidays, major events in their market). Best outreach windows: Tuesday-Thursday, 9am-11am local time, in January-February (post-holiday shoulder season) and September-October (pre-Q4 budget planning). Build "pause and resume" triggers in your SDR sequences based on their property's occupancy calendar.

**Problem:** The technology champion (Revenue Manager) loves the product but can't get CDO/Owner sign-off on the investment.
**Solution:** Build a CFO/Owner-specific ROI calculator that translates RevPAR lift into bottom-line NOI impact (which is what owners care about, not RevPAR). Produce a "Letter to Your Owner" template that champions can use to internally sell up. Create a reference customer call matching the exact property type and size — peer validation from a similar independent hotel owner closes this objection faster than any sales conversation.
