### SCRIPT TEMPLATE: Primary Angle — "[Signal-Based Hook]"

**PRE-RECORDING PREPARATION (2 minutes)**

Research checklist (complete before pressing record):
- [ ] Signal identified: [Specific thing you noticed — write it down in one sentence]
- [ ] Proof point matched: [Which customer story is closest to their company stage and pain]
- [ ] CTA decided: [Exact ask — calendar link, yes/no reply, resource request]
- [ ] Thumbnail personalization: [What will appear in the preview image — their name on a website/LinkedIn screengrab, or text overlay on face-cam thumbnail]

**RECORDING GUIDE — BEAT BY BEAT**

**Beat 1 — Opening Hook (0–5 seconds): The "This Is For You" Moment**
Script:
"[First name] — [reference the specific signal in one natural sentence. Sound like you're talking to a colleague, not reading a script. Example: 'I saw you just posted about the challenge of SDR ramp time — made me want to reach out directly.']"

Direction: Look directly into camera. Say their name first. Reference the signal before introducing yourself.

Anti-patterns to avoid:
- "Hi [Name], my name is [X] and I work at [Company]..." (self-intro first = instant tab-close)
- "I hope this video finds you well..." (generic = no trust)
- Referencing something so obvious it signals you used a mass tool ("I saw you work at [Company]...")

---

**Beat 2 — Pain Frame (5–20 seconds): Name the Problem They're Living With**
Script:
"The reason I'm reaching out is [role]-specific: most [buyer title] I talk to are dealing with [specific pain — described in the prospect's language, not your product's language]. And the downstream effect is [the business consequence that keeps them up at night — lost pipeline, missed quota, burned headcount, wasted budget]."

Direction: No product mention yet. You are demonstrating you understand their world, not pitching yours.

Customization note: Adjust the pain framing based on company stage:
- Early-stage/startup (< 50 employees): Focus on speed, resource constraints, founder urgency
- Mid-market (50–500 employees): Focus on scale, consistency, ops efficiency
- Enterprise (500+): Focus on risk, compliance, cross-functional alignment, board metrics

---

**Beat 3 — Proof Moment (20–40 seconds): Make the Outcome Real**
Script:
"I'm reaching out specifically because we just finished working with [peer company description — similar size, similar stage, similar pain — do NOT name the actual company without permission]. They were dealing with exactly this. In [timeframe], they [specific outcome with metric — e.g., 'reduced ramp time from 90 days to 52 days and saw 31% improvement in first-quarter quota attainment']. What made the difference was [one specific thing — tied to your product's core mechanism, but explained as an outcome not a feature]."

Direction: Deliver the metric with a pause before and after. Numbers need a breath around them to land. Lean slightly forward — it signals conviction.

Proof point selection criteria:
- Most similar company type to the prospect's company → highest emotional resonance
- Most dramatic metric → most memorable
- Most recent → most credible
- If no perfect match: use the closest analog and say "similar to your situation" rather than fabricating specificity

---

**Beat 4 — Personalized Screen Share (Optional, 30–45 seconds for Tier 1 technical buyers)**
Instruction: If your product is visual and the buyer is technical/analytical, consider a 15-second screen share showing:
- Their company's public data in your dashboard (if applicable)
- A relevant template/framework your product generates
- A competitor benchmark showing their likely gap (sourced from G2, public data)

Script during screen share:
"I actually pulled [their company name / a company like theirs] into [product] just to show you what this looks like in practice — [narrate what they're seeing in 2 sentences, tie it to the pain from Beat 2]."

---

**Beat 5 — CTA (40–75 seconds): One Clear, Low-Friction Ask**

Select CTA type based on prospect tier and signal strength:

**CTA Option A — Yes/No Reply (highest reply rate, lowest meeting rate)**
"Quick question before I take more of your time — is [specific pain from Beat 2] something [Company name] is actively trying to solve this half? Even a one-word reply helps me figure out if it's worth continuing the conversation."

**CTA Option B — Micro-Commitment (moderate reply rate, higher meeting intent signal)**
"If any of this resonates, I'd love to share the full story of how [proof point company type] got from [before state] to [after state]. I can send a 2-minute written breakdown, or we can talk through it in 15 minutes — whatever works better for you."

**CTA Option C — Direct Calendar Ask (lower reply rate, highest meeting rate per reply)**
"If this is relevant, I'd love 15 minutes to walk through whether what worked for [peer company description] could apply to [Company name]. I'll drop a link below — easiest to grab a time if you want it, and no pressure if the timing's off."

# DELIVERABLE 5: A/B TESTING ROADMAP FOR VIDEO PROSPECTING

**Testing priority (run sequentially, not simultaneously — minimum 100 sends per variant for directional signal, 300 for statistical significance):**

**Test 1 (Weeks 1–2): Video vs. No Video — Baseline**
- Control: Email-only Touch 1 (same angle, same proof point, no video)
- Variant: Email + video Touch 1 (same angle, same proof point, video embedded)
- Metric: Reply rate (all replies within 7 days of Touch 1)
- Expected lift: 15–30% improvement in reply rate with video (if not seeing this, the video itself is the problem — not the channel)

**Test 2 (Weeks 3–4): On-Camera vs. Screen Share Opening**
- Control: Rep on-camera for full video (face-cam only)
- Variant: Rep on-camera for first 10 seconds, then screen share of prospect's website or relevant data for 20 seconds, back to face-cam for CTA
- Metric: Video completion rate AND reply rate (screen share typically increases completion, but does it increase replies?)

**Test 3 (Weeks 5–6): Thumbnail Format**
- Control: Prospect's name overlaid on rep's face-cam thumbnail
- Variant: Prospect's company website screenshot as thumbnail background with name overlaid
- Metric: Email open rate + video click-through rate (thumbnail determines click, so isolate this from reply rate)

**Test 4 (Weeks 7–8): Video Length**
- Control: 60–75 second video (current standard)
- Variant: 30–45 second video (remove proof point, lead directly from pain to CTA)
- Metric: Video completion rate AND reply rate — shorter videos may drive higher completion but lower intent

**Test 5 (Weeks 9–10): Touch Placement**
- Control: Video in Touch 1 (email)
- Variant: Video in Touch 3 (email) — send 2 text-only touches first to establish context, then video
- Metric: Meeting booked rate per sequence enrolled (not just reply rate — does sequence position affect pipeline output?)

# DELIVERABLE 7: COMPLIANCE & VIDEO STORAGE FRAMEWORK

**Video storage and link management:**
- Loom: Links expire after [set to 'never expire' for prospect-sent videos] — ensure settings are configured correctly before sending; expired links in follow-up sequences destroy trust
- Vidyard: Use Vidyard's "Video Hub" to organize by prospect segment — enables team-wide reuse of Tier 2/3 shared recordings
- Sendspark: Workspace organization by campaign — name videos as "[ICP Segment] — [Pain Angle] — [Date Recorded]" for easy retrieval
- Asset retirement protocol: Archive (do not delete) recordings older than 6 months — proof points and market conditions change, and old videos may contain outdated claims

**Legal compliance for video outreach:**
- CAN-SPAM: Video links in prospecting emails are subject to same rules as text emails — include unsubscribe mechanism and physical address in every email wrapper
- GDPR (EU/UK): Do not use screen recordings that capture EU prospect's personal data (their LinkedIn profile, company website with personal data visible) without ensuring legitimate interest basis is documented
- LinkedIn video DMs: LinkedIn's native video DM feature (available on mobile) is fully compliant — third-party LinkedIn video tools must comply with LinkedIn's automation limits; stay below 50 DMs/day per account to avoid restriction
- Recording disclosure: Some US states (CA, FL, IL, WA, MD) and most of Europe require disclosure if a video is recorded. For prospecting videos, you are the one being recorded — no disclosure required. If you screen share a live call, disclose immediately.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Video view rate is below 20% (prospects aren't clicking to watch)**
Solution: The email subject line or thumbnail is the failure point — not the video content. Run a 2-week subject line test: try "Quick video for [First name]" vs. "[Pain topic] — 60-second thought" vs. "[Reference their specific signal in 4 words]." Simultaneously audit your thumbnail: does it clearly show personalization (prospect's name, company name, or website screenshot visible in the preview)? Generic face-cam thumbnails with no text perform 40–60% worse than personalized thumbnails. Do not re-record the video until you've confirmed the delivery mechanism is working.

**Problem: Prospects watch the full video but don't reply**
Solution: The CTA in the video is asking for too much. Audit the last 10 seconds of your script — if the ask is "book a 30-minute call" or "let me know if you want to see a demo," replace it with a yes/no question: "Is [pain] a live priority for [Company name] right now?" Yes/no questions generate 2–3x more replies than calendar asks in video CTAs. Also check if you're sending the follow-up email (Touch 2) within 24 hours of the video being watched — the highest reply rate window for post-view follow-up is within 2 hours of a 75%+ completion event.

**Problem: SDRs are taking 8–10 minutes per video (efficiency problem, not quality problem)**
Solution: The bottleneck is research, not recording. Implement the Clay signal detection workflow (Deliverable 4, Step 1–2) to pre-generate the opening hook before the SDR sees the prospect. If Clay is not available, build a 3-column Google Sheet: Prospect name | Signal (pre-researched by a VA or AI) | Suggested opener. SDRs should pick up a pre-researched prospect, glance at the opener, and press record in under 30 seconds. Recording time should be 60–90 seconds. Total time per Tier 1 video should be ≤ 3 minutes.
