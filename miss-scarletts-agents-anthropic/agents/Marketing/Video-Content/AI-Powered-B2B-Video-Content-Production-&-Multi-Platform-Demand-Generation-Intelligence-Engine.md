## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Videos get under 200 views organically and aren't generating any pipeline.
**Solution:** The issue is almost always one of three things: (1) Title/thumbnail optimization — your title has no search keyword and doesn't create curiosity; test 3 title variants using TubeBuddy A/B before concluding the content is weak. (2) Distribution failure — you're relying on the platform algorithm instead of active seeding; in the first 4 hours post-publish, have 10+ employees engage, share in relevant Slack communities, and send to your email list. (3) Wrong content type — if your ICP isn't searching for this topic, organic discovery won't work; validate demand with Google Search Console or Semrush before investing in production.

**Problem:** Executives agree to appear on video but the footage looks and sounds amateur, damaging brand perception.
**Solution:** Don't attempt to film executives in their home offices without equipment. Invest in a simple $800 travel kit: Elgato Key Light, Rode Wireless GO II mic, iPhone 15 Pro on tripod. Alternatively, hire a local corporate videographer for 4-hour shoot sessions ($800-1,500) and batch-record 4-6 episodes at once. For remote recording, use Riverside.fm which captures uncompressed local recordings regardless of internet connection. Send a pre-shoot prep guide (location, clothing, lighting check) 48 hours before every session.

**Problem:** You produce videos but sales team never uses them or references them in deals.
**Solution:** Sales adoption of video content requires deliberate enablement, not just availability. (1) Build a "Video Sales Kit" in Highspot or Seismic: organize videos by deal stage and buying persona, not by topic. (2) Create a 60-second "video shortcut" for SDRs: a Loom template library with pre-built intros they can send at each deal stage. (3) Track which videos close deals (via CRM attribution) and publish a monthly "top 3 videos that influenced pipeline" to the sales team — social proof drives adoption.
