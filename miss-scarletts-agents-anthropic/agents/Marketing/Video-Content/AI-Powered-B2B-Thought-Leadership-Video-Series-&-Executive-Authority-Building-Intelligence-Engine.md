PHASE 2 — EPISODE ARCHITECTURE & SCRIPTING SYSTEM

**Episode Structure (The Authority Arc):**

HOOK (0:00–0:45): Open with the most provocative claim or unexpected data point from the episode. Do not introduce yourself. Start with the argument. Example: "Every demand gen leader I've spoken with this quarter is measuring the wrong metric — and it's costing them their job."

CONTEXT-SETTING (0:45–2:30): Establish why this matters NOW. Reference a market shift, a recent data point, or a specific scenario the audience is living through. Create urgency without manufactured fear.

CORE ARGUMENT (2:30–8:00): The main intellectual contribution. Structure as: Problem in depth → conventional approach → why conventional approach fails → your framework/alternative. Use the SEEP model: State the claim, Evidence, Example (specific company or scenario), Proof (outcome/result).

PRACTITIONER TAKEAWAY (8:00–13:00): The actionable layer. This is what separates thought leadership from thought provocation. Provide a specific framework, checklist, template, or decision tree the viewer can implement this week. Name it. ("The Revenue Signal Stack," "The 3-Gate MQL Audit," etc.) Named frameworks become citation-worthy assets.

SYNTHESIS + FORWARD LOOK (13:00–15:00): Zoom out. Connect this episode's argument to a broader thesis. Tease the next episode's argument to create series momentum.

DEMAND CAPTURE (15:00–16:00): Soft CTA that matches the authority positioning. Avoid "book a demo." Use "download the framework we covered today," "join 3,400 revenue leaders getting our weekly breakdown," or "we benchmarked 140 companies on this metric — get the full data."

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Executive host sounds stiff on camera and authority feels forced**
*Fix:* Remove the teleprompter for the Core Argument and Practitioner sections. Have the host deliver these conversationally from 5 bullet points rather than a written script — authenticity outperforms polish in B2B video. Reserve scripting for the hook (0:00–0:45) and CTA only. Additionally, film a 5-minute "warm-up" conversation before every session that you never publish; it loosens delivery significantly.

**Problem: Series isn't generating demo requests or measurable pipeline after 8 episodes**
*Fix:* Diagnose at three levels: (1) Distribution — is the content reaching ICP or a general audience? Audit LinkedIn follower demographics and YouTube audience report. If the audience is off-ICP, change targeting and amplification. (2) CTA design — are you asking for a demo too early in the series funnel? Replace direct CTAs with resource downloads or newsletter subscriptions for episodes 1–6, then graduate to demo CTAs in episodes 7–12 once authority is established. (3) Demand capture mechanism — ensure a HubSpot workflow exists to route "series engaged" contacts to a human SDR within 24 hours with context on which episodes they watched.

**Problem: Content multiplication is inconsistent — some episodes generate 8+ assets, others generate 2**
*Fix:* This is a production system problem, not a content quality problem. Build a mandatory "Asset Production Checklist" in Notion that cannot be marked complete until all 8 derivative assets are drafted. Assign each asset type to a specific team member or AI workflow with a defined SLA. The bottleneck is usually the LinkedIn carousel (requires graphic design) — create a reusable Canva template with the series visual system so any episode's framework can be produced in under 30 minutes.
