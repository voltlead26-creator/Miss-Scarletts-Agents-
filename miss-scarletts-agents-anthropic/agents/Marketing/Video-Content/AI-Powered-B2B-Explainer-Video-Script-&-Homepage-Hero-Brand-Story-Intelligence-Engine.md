**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** video, b2b, content, conversion, brand-story, saas, awareness

## Overview
Generates production-ready explainer video scripts — including homepage hero videos, brand narrative films, "how it works" walkthroughs, and awareness ad scripts — calibrated to B2B SaaS audiences. Use this when you need a 60–120 second video that converts skeptical buyers who have never heard of you into pipeline-ready prospects.

Write the script in this format:
- SCENE + VISUAL DIRECTION: What's shown on screen
- VOICEOVER: What the narrator says
- ON-SCREEN TEXT: Key callout text or captions

Structure the script using the Problem-Agitate-Solve-Proof-CTA framework:
1. Open with the pain (5 seconds — pattern interrupt)
2. Agitate the status quo (15 seconds — make it relatable)
3. Introduce the solution (20 seconds — product reveal)
4. Show how it works (30 seconds — 3 key capabilities)
5. Social proof flash (10 seconds — stat or customer quote)
6. CTA (10 seconds — specific next step)

Tone: Confident, direct, human. No corporate jargon. Speak like a smart colleague, not a vendor.
Output word count: 150–200 words of voiceover (narrated at ~130 WPM = 90 seconds).

## SCRIPT WRITING RULES
1. Open with a scene the viewer immediately recognizes from their own life — no "Introducing [Product]" openers
2. Never say "our platform" in the first 20 seconds
3. Use second-person ("you", "your team") for the problem section, first-person plural ("we") for the solution reveal
4. Every capability mentioned must link to a business outcome — no feature dumps
5. The proof section must feel earned, not grafted on
6. CTA must be specific and low-friction — "Watch a 2-minute demo" beats "Learn more"
7. Avoid: "best-in-class", "end-to-end", "seamless", "robust", "synergy"
8. Read voiceover aloud and time it — flag any line over 12 words as a potential pacing risk

## Success Metrics

- **Script quality gate:** Voiceover reads naturally aloud at conversational speed (target: 130–140 WPM)
- **Clarity test:** Show scene 4 (reveal) to someone unfamiliar with the product — can they explain what it does in one sentence?

- **Loom / Wistia / Vidyard:** Upload final video and use the platform's heatmap analytics to see where viewers drop off — use drop-off data to rewrite the scene that loses attention
- **HubSpot / Marketo:** Gate the "How It Works" video behind a form for MQL capture; embed ungated homepage hero in nurture email sequences with video thumbnail CTAs (video emails get 2–3x higher CTR)
- **Notion:** Use the Distribution Copy Package section as a video launch checklist — assign YouTube description, LinkedIn post, and email copy as tasks to your content team

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Script sounds like a product brochure, not a story**
Solution: Replace every "we" or "our" statement in the first 30 seconds with a "you" or "your team" statement. The product should be the solution to the buyer's story, not the subject of the video. Rerun the Advanced prompt and add: "Rewrite scenes 1-3 so the buyer is the protagonist, not the product."

**Problem: Script runs 30-45 seconds too long when read aloud**
Solution: Run this edit pass prompt: "Trim the voiceover text to exactly [TARGET WORD COUNT] words. Remove any sentence that does not (a) advance the emotional arc, (b) explain a capability, or (c) state proof. Kill all transitional filler phrases like 'That's where we come in' or 'And that's exactly why we built this.'"

**Problem: The "How It Works" section is too technical for a general audience video**
Solution: Prompt the AI to re-explain each capability as a "before vs. after" contrast: "Before: [what they did manually / the pain]. After: [what happens now / the outcome]. Remove any reference to underlying technology or architecture."
