## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sprint is generating MQLs but none are converting to SQLs**
*Solution:* The MQL definition is too broad or the wrong audience is converting. Do an immediate audit: pull the 20 most recent MQLs, review their firmographic fit against ICP. If <60% match, tighten LinkedIn targeting by adding company size and seniority filters. Brief SDRs on what a "sprint-quality MQL" looks like — not all MQLs are equal. Consider adding a 1-question form field ("What's your timeline for evaluating this?") to improve qualification at the top.

**Problem: Closed-lost reactivation is getting no response**
*Solution:* The outreach feels generic or the timing trigger isn't compelling. Personalize to the specific reason they didn't buy last time (pull from CRM notes). Use a different channel — if AE emailed last time, try LinkedIn InMail or a phone call. Change the frame from "just checking in" to a specific, external trigger ("I saw [their company] just announced [expansion / new initiative] — our customers in similar situations typically see X outcome"). Try a break-up email as the third touch — paradoxically, these generate the highest response rates.

**Problem: Budget running out faster than pipeline is materializing**
*Solution:* Immediately pause the slowest-producing channel (by pipeline $ influenced, not just MQL volume). Shift freed budget to the fastest speed-to-pipeline channels: outbound SDR (cheapest, fastest) and closed-lost reactivation (zero cost). Do not spread budget thin — it is better to fully fund two channels than underfund four. Review if brand awareness spend is included — cut it entirely during a sprint (awareness doesn't close gaps in 30 days).
