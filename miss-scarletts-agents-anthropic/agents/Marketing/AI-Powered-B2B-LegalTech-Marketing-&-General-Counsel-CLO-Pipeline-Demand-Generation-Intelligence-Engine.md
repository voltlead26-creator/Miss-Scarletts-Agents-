## Overview
This engine generates complete, channel-ready demand generation programs targeting General Counsel, Chief Legal Officers, and Legal Operations leaders at enterprise and mid-market companies. Use it when you sell legal AI, contract lifecycle management, e-discovery, compliance automation, matter management, or any technology where the legal department controls or heavily influences the budget.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low email open rates from GC/CLO contacts (<15%)**
Solution: GCs receive 200+ vendor emails daily. Subject lines must lead with a peer insight or regulatory trigger specific to their industry, not a product claim. Use a personal sender name (VP of Legal at [Company] or your own company's CLO) rather than a marketing alias. Test send times: Tuesday–Thursday, 6:00–8:00 AM in recipient's timezone, before the daily docket begins. A/B test subject lines with benchmark data vs. peer story angles.

**Problem: High demo-to-close drop-off after CISO review**
Solution: Create a "CISO Fast-Track" package: pre-completed security questionnaire (SOC 2 Type II report, penetration test summary, data processing agreement, AI model training data policy — especially critical for legal AI given privilege concerns), and offer a 30-minute dedicated security review call with your CISO/CTO within 5 business days of request. Legal deals die in security review more than any other stage. Getting ahead of this proactively cuts security review time by 40–60%.

**Problem: Losing to "do nothing" / status quo (manual review)**
Solution: The "do nothing" competitor in legal is the most dangerous because GCs are trained to be conservative. Reframe the risk: quantify the cost of the current approach using their own data. Run a free "Contract Risk Assessment" — analyze 50 of their existing contracts for high-risk clauses. This creates a tangible, personalized ROI case using their own documents and makes the status quo cost visible. Reference: legal malpractice insurance premiums are rising partly due to missed contractual obligations; this is a risk GCs understand viscerally.
