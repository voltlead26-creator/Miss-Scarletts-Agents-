**MODULE 2: AI-POWERED LEARNING PATH & SIMULATION LIBRARY**

2.1 PRODUCT KNOWLEDGE AI DRILLS
Ten AI roleplay prompts — rep pastes each into their AI assistant and responds as if in a live sales call. After each response, the AI evaluates using a structured rubric (ICP relevance, value clarity, evidence quality, next-step discipline):

Drill 1 — The Skeptical VP: "We've looked at [Your Category] tools before and never got the ROI we were promised. Why would this be any different?"
Evaluation rubric: Did the rep (a) acknowledge the skepticism without dismissing it? (b) offer a specific, verifiable proof point — not a generic claim? (c) invite a next step that reduces perceived risk?

Drill 2 — The Feature Minimizer: "Our current solution already does that. What does [Your Product] do that we can't build ourselves?"
Evaluation rubric: Build vs. buy framework fluency; total cost of ownership articulation; opportunity cost of engineering time diverted to non-core infrastructure.

Drill 3 — The Budget Objector: "This isn't in our budget right now. Can you come back next quarter?"
Evaluation rubric: Did the rep explore whether the pain is real but the budget isn't confirmed yet (common)? Did they introduce ROI framing that reframes the conversation from cost to investment? Did they agree on a specific re-engagement trigger rather than a vague "follow up in Q3"?

Drill 4 — The Champion Losing Conviction: "My boss wants to go with [Competitor]. She thinks they're the safer bet since they're bigger."
Evaluation rubric: Champion coaching — arming the champion to sell internally. Did the rep provide a specific "talking track" the champion can use with their boss? Did they offer third-party validation (analyst report, peer company reference) the champion can forward?

Drill 5 — The ROI Skeptic: "Show me the ROI. I need a specific number to present to my CFO."
Evaluation rubric: ROI calculator usage; understanding of the CFO's financial language (payback period, NPV, cost avoidance vs. cost savings); ability to co-build the ROI model with the champion rather than presenting a pre-built number.

Drills 6-10: [Customize for your top 5 specific objections or use-case discovery scenarios based on your Gong/call recording analysis — e.g., technical integration questions, security review objections, competitive direct comparisons, renewal/expansion objections if applicable to the role]

2.2 ICP IMMERSION EXERCISES
Five AI-powered exercises to build genuine buyer empathy (not just surface-level persona knowledge):

Exercise 1 — A Day in the Life: "Describe a typical Tuesday for a [ICP job title] at a [company type, size] company that doesn't yet use [your product category]. What are their top 3 decisions they have to make today, what data do they use to make those decisions, and where does that process break down?"
Output: Rep writes a 300-word narrative. Manager scores it on specificity and accuracy compared to customer interview recordings.

Exercise 2 — The Trigger Event Map: "What are the 5 events that cause a [ICP job title] to start evaluating [your product category]? For each trigger, describe: what happened, who notices it first, what they try to do before buying a solution, and why that workaround eventually fails."
Output: Rep maps 5 triggers with full context. This becomes their prospecting intelligence for identifying in-market buyers.

Exercise 3 — The Buying Committee Navigator: "For a [company size] company in [industry], who are all the people who have a say in buying [your product]? For each person: what's their primary concern, what do they fear will go wrong if the purchase is approved, and what proof would convince them this is the right decision?"
Output: Multi-stakeholder map with influence and objection prediction per role.

Exercise 4 — The Competitor's Pitch: "You are a top rep at [Competitor 1]. Pitch me your product to a prospect who is currently evaluating [Your Product]. Make your strongest possible case."
Output: By articulating the competitor's best argument, reps internalize the counter-messaging more deeply than by reading a battle card.

Exercise 5 — The Win/Loss Reverse Engineer: "Here is a summary of a deal we won [or lost] last quarter. Walk me through what you believe happened, why the buyer made their decision, and what you would have done differently at each stage of the deal."
Output: Analytical thinking and deal forensics — the cognitive skill that separates quota carriers from quota crashers.

2.3 COMPETITIVE ROLEPLAY SIMULATIONS

Simulation A — Mid-Evaluation Rebuttal (Inbound Lead):
Scenario: Prospect submitted a demo request 3 days ago. They mentioned in the form that they're "also evaluating [Competitor 1]." This is a 30-minute discovery call.

Opening (first 60 seconds): "Thanks for joining — I saw you're also looking at [Competitor]. I want to make sure this time is genuinely useful for you, so let me start with what's driving your search and then I'll show you exactly where we're different. Sound good?"

Discovery sequence (MEDDIC-structured):
- Metric: "What does success look like in 90 days if you get this right? How would you measure it?"
- Economic Buyer: "Who else at your company needs to feel good about this before you move forward?"
- Decision Criteria: "When you compare us to [Competitor], what are the 2-3 things that will matter most to your decision?"
- Decision Process: "Walk me through how decisions like this typically get made at your company — and what's the timeline you're working with?"
- Identify Pain: "What's the cost of not solving this? If you're still on your current setup in 6 months, what does that mean for the business?"
- Champion: "Who else internally has a stake in this? Is there someone on your team who would use this daily who should be part of our next conversation?"

Objection branches:
- "We're leaning toward [Competitor] because they have more integrations." → Counter: Specific integration comparison + customer migration story
- "Your price is higher." → Counter: ROI reframe + total cost of ownership analysis
- "We need to check with our IT security team." → Counter: Security review acceleration — offer proactive security documentation package and reference call with IT buyer at a mutual customer

Closing sequence: Mutual Action Plan introduction — "Based on what you shared, here's what I'd suggest as next steps to make sure we respect your timeline..." [specific 3-step MAP]

Simulation B — Cold Outbound Interruption:
Scenario: 8:47 AM cold call. Prospect answered. They're clearly busy. They have 90 seconds of patience.

Cold call structure (90-second version):
- Permission opener (3 seconds): "Hey [Name] — caught you at a bad time?"
- If "no": Pattern interrupt: "I'll be quick — I'm [Name] from [Company]. I work with [ICP profile] who are dealing with [specific pain]. Does that sound familiar to your world at all?"
- If yes, pain resonates: "The reason I'm calling is we've been helping companies like [Company Reference] solve exactly that. Worth 15 minutes this week to see if we're seeing the same thing?"
- If no, pain doesn't resonate: "Fair enough — what's the biggest operational challenge your team is fighting right now?" [pivot to discovery, not pitch]

Objection branches:
- "Send me an email." → Response: "Absolutely — before I do, can I ask one quick question so I can make it relevant? [One question — not a pitch]"
- "We're happy with our current solution." → Response: "Totally get it. Mind if I ask what you're using? [pause] Got it. What would have to be true for you to consider switching?"
- "Not interested." → Response: "Completely understand. Just out of curiosity, is it the timing, the category, or the way I described it that didn't land?"

**MODULE 3: RAMP METRICS DASHBOARD & INTERVENTION TRIGGERS**

3.1 EIGHT LEADING INDICATORS OF RAMP SUCCESS (measure weekly from Day 31 forward)

ACTIVITY LAYER — What reps are putting into the machine:
1. Outbound Activity Volume: dials + emails + LinkedIn touches per week
   - SDR benchmark: 150-200 touchpoints/week
   - AE benchmark: 60-80 touchpoints/week (against named accounts)
   - Intervention trigger: <70% of weekly target for 2 consecutive weeks → immediate sequence audit and messaging review

2. Meeting Booking Rate (outbound): meetings booked per 100 outreach touches
   - Industry average: 2-4% (poor), 5-8% (average), 9-12% (strong)
   - Intervention trigger: <3% after 4 weeks of prospecting → messaging or targeting problem, not effort problem

ENGAGEMENT LAYER — How prospects respond to reps:
3. Demo Show Rate: % of booked demos that actually occur
   - Benchmark: 70-80% (below 60% signals weak qualification at booking)
   - Intervention trigger: <65% → qualification criteria being skipped to inflate pipeline; coach on BANT or MEDDIC filtering at booking stage

4. Discovery Completion Rate: % of discovery calls where a second meeting is booked before ending the first
   - Benchmark: 50-65% (top reps hit 70-75%)
   - Intervention trigger: <40% → discovery call quality issue; review Gong recordings for the 5 most recent discoveries; identify if rep is pitching before diagnosing

PIPELINE LAYER — Deals in the machine:
5. Opportunities Created per Week (from Day 31):
   - SMB AE target: 4-6 new opps/month by end of Month 2
   - Mid-Market AE target: 2-3 new opps/month by end of Month 2
   - Enterprise AE target: 1-2 new opps/month by end of Month 3
   - Intervention trigger: 50% below target for 3+ weeks → investigate whether problem is sourcing (not enough meetings) or qualification (meetings not converting to pipeline)

6. Average Deal Size vs. Team Average:
   - Benchmark: within 20% of team median by Month 3 (ramp reps frequently underprice or target too-small accounts)
   - Intervention trigger: consistently below 70% of team median → rep may be avoiding enterprise conversations due to confidence gap; targeted enterprise roleplay required

7. Pipeline Coverage Ratio (pipeline ÷ quota):
   - Required coverage: 3× quota minimum; 4× preferred for rep in ramp
   - Intervention trigger: below 2.5× at end of Month 2 → pipeline generation emergency; immediate outbound blitz + ICP targeting recalibration

QUALITY LAYER — Are reps on a path to win?
8. Stage Conversion Rates (Discovery → Demo → Proposal → Closed):
   - Stage 1→2 (Discovery to Demo): benchmark 55-70% for qualified reps
   - Stage 2→3 (Demo to Proposal): benchmark 35-50%
   - Stage 3→Won (Proposal to Closed): benchmark 25-40%
   - Intervention trigger: any stage significantly below team average for 3+ weeks → stage-specific coaching, not general encouragement

3.2 INTERVENTION PROTOCOL TIERS

Tier 1 — Yellow Flag (one metric off track): Weekly 1:1 agenda focused specifically on that metric. Manager and rep co-diagnose root cause. No formal action required.

Tier 2 — Orange Flag (two or more metrics off track, or one metric >30% below benchmark for 3 weeks): Formal coaching plan activated. Manager joins 2 live calls per week. Specific skill development assignment with measurable 2-week improvement target. First written documentation.

Tier 3 — Red Flag (ramp targets missed by >40% at the end of Month 2): Performance Improvement Plan discussion. Evaluate: Is this a skills problem (coachable), a fit problem (wrong role type), or a motivation problem (personal circumstances or culture mismatch)? Engage HR. Establish clear 30-day turnaround criteria.

**MODULE 4: MANAGER COACHING CADENCE**

4.1 WEEKLY 1:1 FRAMEWORK (45-60 minutes)

First 15 minutes — Rep-led: Rep presents their pipeline board: what moved forward, what stalled, what needs manager input. Manager listens and probes, does not direct.

Next 20 minutes — Deal strategy: Pick 1-2 strategic deals. Use Deal Qualification Scorecard (MEDDIC or SPICED):
- Is the pain real and quantified?
- Is the economic buyer identified and accessible?
- Is there a compelling event driving urgency?
- Is there a champion with power?
- Does the rep have a mutual action plan with the buyer?

Last 15 minutes — Skill development: One specific skill focus per week (not "you need to get better at discovery" — but "in your last 3 discovery calls, you asked about pain but didn't attach a dollar amount to it; here's how to do that in one question").

4.2 CALL REVIEW PROTOCOL (bi-weekly, 30 minutes)
Select calls using the "One good, one gap" principle: one call where the rep performed well (to reinforce) and one where a specific skill broke down (to develop).

Gong/conversation intelligence review checklist:
- Talk ratio: rep speaking >60% is a red flag; target 40-45% rep / 55-60% buyer
- Discovery depth: number of open-ended questions before first product mention (benchmark: 5-8 questions before any solution language)
- Objection handling: did rep acknowledge, explore, and address — or skip straight to counter-pitch?
- Next step quality: was the next step specific (date, time, attendees, agenda) or vague ("I'll follow up")?
- Champion engagement: did rep engage both the champion and any economic buyer signals in the same call?

4.3 THREE CRITICAL COACHING MOMENTS

Coaching Moment 1 — First Live Discovery Call:
What to observe: Does rep make the buyer feel heard before the product gets mentioned? Are questions genuine (following the buyer's thread) or scripted (following a checklist regardless of buyer responses)?

Post-call debrief (10 minutes maximum, same day):
"What did you learn about their business that you didn't know before the call?" (Insight orientation)
"What would you do differently if you could run that call again?" (Self-reflection before manager input)
"Here's one thing I saw that worked, and one question I'd add next time." (Specific coaching, not evaluation)

Coaching Moment 2 — First Multi-Stakeholder Call:
What to observe: Does rep pre-plan who will be in the room (or Zoom)? Do they open with an agenda that addresses each stakeholder's suspected priority? Do they identify unexpected blockers in real-time?

Post-call debrief:
"Who had the most power in that room, and what was their primary concern?" (Stakeholder reading)
"Was there anyone who seemed skeptical or disengaged? What do you think they need?" (Political intelligence)
"What's your plan to multi-thread — who should we bring in from our side to match their buying committee?"

Coaching Moment 3 — First Pricing Conversation:
What to observe: Did rep introduce pricing after establishing clear value — or did they respond to a buyer price probe before value was anchored? Did they use range pricing or point pricing? Did they defend value or discount immediately under pressure?

Post-call debrief:
"At what point in the conversation did pricing come up, and were you in control of when it came up?" (Sequencing awareness)
"When they pushed back on price, what was your instinct and what did you actually do?" (Behavior vs. intention gap)
"What would it take to never discount more than [X%] on a deal this size?" (Value anchoring skill development)

**MODULE 5: AI AUTOMATION & TOOL INTEGRATION**

5.1 AI ASSISTANT-POWERED SELF-COACHING
Configure a standing AI assistant prompt the rep uses after every prospect interaction:

"I just completed a [discovery call / demo / pricing call] with [role] at [company type]. Here's what happened: [brief description]. Score my performance on: (1) discovery depth — did I understand their situation well enough to prescribe a solution? (2) objection handling — did I acknowledge and explore before countering? (3) next step quality — is my next step specific and calendar-confirmed? For each dimension, give me a score of 1-5 and one specific improvement for next time."

This creates a feedback loop that operates between manager coaching sessions, allowing reps to self-correct in real-time without waiting for a weekly 1:1.

5.2 CRM HYGIENE AUTOMATION
Ramp reps notoriously underinvest in CRM quality, which corrupts pipeline data for the entire team. Automate the following:

- After every meeting: Sales engagement platform auto-logs meeting notes template with required fields: Pain identified (Y/N), Economic buyer identified (Y/N), Next step (date + agenda), Stage qualification criteria met (MEDDIC score)
- Daily: AI summary of all open opportunities with no activity in 7 days → Slack DM to rep: "3 deals haven't moved this week. Do you want help with reactivation messaging for [Deal A], [Deal B], [Deal C]?"
- Weekly: Automated pipeline health score per deal (combination of days in stage, stakeholder engagement, next step presence) → flagged to manager before weekly 1:1 so coaching session focuses on the right deals

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Ramp rep is hitting activity metrics but not converting to pipeline — high volume of meetings booked, almost no opportunities created**
Solution: This is a qualification failure, not an effort failure. The rep is booking meetings based on low-quality signals (someone who returned a cold call does not equal someone in active evaluation). Immediate intervention: audit the last 10 discovery calls in Gong and score each for: was the pain real and quantified, or vague and polite? Did the buyer have a compelling event or timeline, or were they "just looking"? Did the rep create an opportunity after a polite 30-minute conversation with no confirmed pain? Solution: tighten the qualification criteria at the booking stage — implement a mandatory "pre-meeting qualifier" sent via email 24 hours before every discovery call that asks the buyer to confirm their top challenge and their role in the evaluation. Meetings that go unresponded should be rescheduled; meetings from unqualified responders should be redirected to a shorter "15-minute introduction call" rather than a full discovery.

**Problem: Manager doesn't have time to co-pilot calls in Phase 2 as the ramp program requires — the coaching framework collapses under competing priorities**
Solution: The root problem is that manager capacity wasn't budgeted when the hiring plan was approved. For every 2 ramp reps, a manager should have 4-6 dedicated coaching hours per week ring-fenced in their calendar — if this isn't protected, ramp failure becomes predictable. Short-term workaround: designate a Senior AE as "Ramp Partner" for each new hire — they attend calls, run call reviews, and deliver coaching under manager oversight. Compensate the Senior AE with a small ramp bonus ($500-$1,500/month while actively partnering) tied to the ramp rep hitting Phase 2 milestones. This simultaneously develops your Senior AEs' coaching skills and frees manager capacity. Long-term fix: every new hire requisition should include a "ramp enablement cost" line in the hiring plan that includes manager time as a budget item.

**Problem: Rep completed the 90-day program but is still at 45-50% of full quota at Month 4 and not accelerating**
Solution: Differentiate between three distinct stall causes before prescribing a solution. (1) Pipeline volume problem: rep doesn't have enough deals to hit quota even with a perfect win rate — return to prospecting intensity and ICP targeting. (2) Pipeline quality problem: rep has coverage ratio but deals aren't advancing past Stage 2 — diagnosis is usually weak discovery or champion development; run intensive Stage 1→2 conversion coaching for 3 weeks. (3) Win rate problem: deals reach proposal stage but aren't closing — competitive or pricing issue; co-pilot the next 3 pricing and negotiation calls personally. Avoid the mistake of prescribing "more activity" for a quality problem or "better messaging" for a volume problem — misdiagnosis extends the ramp failure and delays the difficult conversation about fit.
