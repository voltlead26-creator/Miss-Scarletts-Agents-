## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "Our CISO has a blanket policy requiring a 90-day shadow review for all new AI vendors."**
Fix: Don't fight the policy — route around it commercially while supporting it operationally. Propose a 30-day paid pilot under a reduced-scope MSA that runs concurrently with the 90-day shadow review. Structure the pilot contract to automatically convert to full MSA upon successful review completion. This lets the champion show business value within the quarter while the CISO review completes. Also: provide the CISO with reference calls from two existing enterprise customers who completed equivalent shadow reviews — their testimony carries more weight than your documentation.

**Problem: "The prospect's procurement team wants to negotiate every clause in the DPA and MSA, which is delaying the deal by 6+ weeks."**
Fix: Identify whether Legal is the actual gatekeeper or is being used as a delay tactic (sometimes a champion who doesn't have full buy-in uses "still in legal review" as cover). If genuine: create a "fast-track DPA" — a pre-negotiated version with 3 minor concessions pre-approved internally that you can offer as a "final offer" to reset the negotiation. Escalate to your legal team to schedule a direct call with their General Counsel — legal-to-legal conversations resolve in hours what champion-to-legal conversations take months to untangle.

**Problem: "We're asked to complete a 500-question custom questionnaire and our answers aren't consistent with our previous responses to another prospect."**
Fix: Build an internal "Security Response Library" (in Notion, Confluence, or a tool like Whistic) of pre-approved, vetted answers to every common security question domain. All responses should be reviewed by your CISO and legal team quarterly. Assign one person in Sales Ops or RevOps ownership of questionnaire consistency — their job is to ensure every response aligns to the master library before submission. Inconsistent answers are a leading indicator of deals lost in procurement to a "reliability" concern, not a technical security gap.
