Use this when reps complain they "can't find what they need," when win rates are flat despite a large content library, or when you're building a new enablement program from scratch.

## Integration Tips

- **Highspot / Seismic / Showpad:** Export your content analytics report (most viewed, most shared, win-rate correlation) and paste the top 20 rows into the advanced prompt. The AI will use actual usage data instead of estimates.
- **Salesforce:** Create a custom "Content Used" multi-select field on the Opportunity object. Pipe field values into the Content Effectiveness Measurement system for automatic tracking. Use Salesforce Reports to build the rep rating dashboard.
- **HubSpot:** Use the Documents tool engagement data (views, time-on-page, downloads) as proxy for content effectiveness. Map document opens to deal stage using workflow timestamps.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Run a saved search for "content" or "do you have anything" across all calls in the last 90 days. Paste the transcript snippets into the prompt's "Sales Feedback" section for instant gap analysis grounded in real rep language.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Use the gap roadmap output to create a structured enablement wiki with owner, due date, and status tags. Link each content asset to its gap analysis justification so stakeholders understand why it was built.
- **Google Sheets:** Build the CFO ROI calculator or the rep scoring rubric as shared Google Sheets. Paste the AI-generated framework directly into the first tab as a "setup guide" so reps understand how to use the tool.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI produces generic recommendations that don't reflect my actual deal dynamics.**
Solution: Paste 5-10 verbatim quotes from your Gong/Chorus call library or Slack sales channel into the "Sales Feedback" field of the advanced prompt. Real rep language forces the AI to ground its analysis in your specific context rather than industry templates. Also add your actual win/loss data from Salesforce.

**Problem: The content gap matrix shows everything as "Critical" — too many priorities to action.**
Solution: Add a constraint to the advanced prompt: "Limit your Critical-priority flags to a maximum of 5. Everything else should be High, Medium, or Low. Justify each Critical rating with a specific revenue impact estimate in dollars." This forces triage.

**Problem: The 90-day sprint plan is too aggressive for my team size (1-2 PMMs).**
Solution: Add to the prompt: "My PMM team is [X] people with [Y] hours/week available for content creation. Adjust the sprint plan to be realistic for this capacity. Suggest which assets to delegate to sales reps, CS, or external agencies vs. own in-house."
