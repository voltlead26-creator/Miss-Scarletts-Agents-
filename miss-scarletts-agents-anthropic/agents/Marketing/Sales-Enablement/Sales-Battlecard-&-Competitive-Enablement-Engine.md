## Overview
Generates production-ready competitive battlecards, objection-handling playbooks, and sales talk tracks by ingesting competitor positioning, pricing, win/loss patterns, and customer language — then packaging it into formats reps actually use in live deals. Use when launching against a new competitor, after a spike in lost deals to a specific rival, or when onboarding a new sales cohort that keeps losing on the same objections.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Battlecard feels too long — reps ignore it after the first page.
**Solution:** Enforce the Section 8 "Quick Hit" format as the primary artifact. Give reps the 200-word Slack version first. The full battlecard becomes a reference document they pull when stuck. Consider creating a "one-pager" PDF version from Sections 1, 3, and 5 only.

**Problem:** Objection responses sound scripted and reps won't use them verbatim.
**Solution:** Run a 30-minute roleplay session with your top 2-3 reps to workshop the language until it sounds natural in their voice. Document their edits — the best reps will improve the scripts. Update the battlecard with their phrasing and credit them (reps are more likely to use a script they helped write).

**Problem:** Competitor updates their product or pricing and the battlecard goes stale.
**Solution:** Set a Google Alert for "[Competitor] pricing", "[Competitor] new feature", and "[Competitor] funding". Route alerts to a dedicated Slack channel. Assign a PMM to own a quarterly battlecard refresh cycle — treat it like a sprint with a definition of done. Add a visible "Last Updated" date and confidence rating (High/Medium/Low) to each section.
