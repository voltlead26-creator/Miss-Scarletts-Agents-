## Integration Tips
- **Salesforce**: Create a custom POC object in Salesforce to track success criteria progress, stakeholder engagement scores, and POC health weekly. Use Salesforce Flows to auto-trigger email sequences when POC stage fields update.
- **HubSpot**: Build a "POC" deal pipeline stage with associated playbooks. Use HubSpot Sequences for the 5-email POC cadence with automated personalization tokens pulling from deal properties.
- **Gainsight / ChurnZero**: Sync POC health score into CS platform so CSMs can monitor POC engagement in their existing workflow without switching tools.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Host the shared POC success criteria document in a prospect-accessible workspace. Use Notion's database views to give the champion real-time visibility into criteria tracking.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Train conversation intelligence filters to flag POC-related signals (budget mentions, competitor comparisons, timeline urgency language) in calls and surface them to AE + PMM.
- **Slack**: Create an automated Slack alert in the revenue team channel when POC health score drops below 50 or when a green-flag commercial signal is detected. Use Zapier or native integrations.
- **DocuSign / PandaDoc**: Pre-load contract templates so the moment a prospect asks for contract language, your team can send a pre-approved MSA within minutes — not days.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Prospect champion agrees to success criteria in kickoff but goes silent by Week 2**
Solution: Silent champion = internal blocker you haven't mapped yet. In Week 2 email, explicitly ask "Is there anyone internally who has questions about the POC that I can help you address?" — this surfaces blockers diplomatically. If silence continues past Day 12, escalate to AE and consider executive-to-executive outreach from your VP Sales to their economic buyer with the subject: "Quick check-in on [COMPANY] POC progress."

**Problem: POC results are positive but the prospect keeps asking for "more time" to evaluate**
Solution: "More time" is almost always a proxy for a different concern — usually internal consensus, budget approval process, or a competing priority. Use the Day 21 email's three-path close to make the "not now" path explicit and non-threatening. Then directly ask: "What would need to be true for you to move forward in the next 30 days?" The answer will tell you what's actually blocking the deal.

**Problem: Economic buyer joins the midpoint review and asks questions your champion can't answer**
Solution: This is a gift, not a crisis. Economic buyers who engage mid-POC have high purchase intent. Immediately send a follow-up email directly to the economic buyer (CC: champion) within 2 hours of the meeting: "Great meeting you today, [NAME]. Here are the three questions you asked and the answers..." This positions your team as responsive and builds direct relationship with the decision-maker — which is exactly what you want.
