## MODULE 1: CERTIFICATION FRAMEWORK ARCHITECTURE

Design a three-tier certification system with clear gates between levels:

### TIER 1 — FOUNDATION CERTIFICATION (Required: All reps within 30 days of hire)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Articulate the company story and category narrative in < 90 seconds
- Describe the ICP across 3 dimensions: firmographic, psychographic, technographic
- Execute a qualification call using the MEDDIC/MEDDPICC framework
- Deliver the core value proposition adapted to 3 buyer personas

**Knowledge Assessment Design:**
Create 25 multiple-choice and scenario-based questions covering:
1. Company history, mission, and category positioning (5 questions)
2. ICP identification and negative ICP scenarios (5 questions)
3. Discovery question sequencing and MEDDIC application (7 questions)
4. Value proposition delivery and persona adaptation (5 questions)
5. Pricing model, packaging tiers, and discount authority (3 questions)

Passing threshold: 80%. Two retake attempts before manager review required.

**Roleplay Scenarios (3 required for certification):**
- Scenario A: Cold discovery call with VP Sales who opens with "we already have Salesforce, what do you do that's different?"
- Scenario B: Inbound demo request from a Director (not economic buyer) who wants pricing before seeing the product
- Scenario C: Multi-stakeholder intro call where IT Director challenges security and compliance unprompted

**Scoring Rubric for Roleplay Assessment:**
Build a 10-point rubric evaluating:
- Opening (credibility, relevance, permission to proceed): /2 points
- Discovery depth (MEDDIC coverage, active listening, follow-up): /3 points
- Value articulation (specificity, persona relevance, proof points): /2 points
- Objection handling (acknowledge/bridge/respond vs. defend): /2 points
- Close / next step commitment: /1 point

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Reps complete the certification but revert to old habits within 30 days**
Fix: Training decay is neurological, not motivational — the content is fine, the reinforcement cadence is missing. Implement the spaced repetition layer from Module 5 immediately. Specifically: 3 micro-quizzes in the first 7 days post-certification are non-negotiable. Also audit your Gong data — if you can prove that certified reps close more deals, publish that data weekly in your team Slack channel. Social proof from peers is more powerful than manager coaching for behavioral change.

**Problem: Managers say "my reps are too busy to do training — it's hurting quota attainment"**
Fix: This is the wrong frame, and you need data to reframe it. Pull your Gong/CRM data on the rep cohort that has NOT been certified — if their ramp is 30+ days longer and their win rate is 8+ points lower, you can model the revenue cost of skipping training. Present it as: "30 hours of training now vs. 4.5 months of sub-quota performance." If leadership is still resistant, launch a pilot: certify 5 reps fully, leave 5 on the old path, compare 90-day metrics. The data will make the argument for you.

**Problem: Assessment pass rates are too high (>90% on first attempt) — reps aren't being challenged**
Fix: Your questions are testing recall rather than application. Move from "What does MEDDIC stand for?" to "A champion tells you the CFO approved budget last quarter — what's your next MEDDIC move?" Scenario-based questions with plausible wrong answers require judgment, not memory. Aim for a calibrated 65-75% first-attempt pass rate on Foundation, 55-65% on Practitioner — it signals the certification actually stretches reps.
