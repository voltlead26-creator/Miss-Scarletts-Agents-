## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Sales reps aren't using the content you create**
*Root cause:* Content is too long, too generic, or not in the formats/tools reps actually use during selling.
*Fix:* Do 3 shadow sessions where you join live sales calls and observe what gaps reps actually face in the moment. Cut your one-pager to a single slide. Move everything into the CRM or sales engagement tool they already have open during calls. Run a 30-minute "show me how you'd use this" session with 3 reps before publishing to the team.

**Issue: The discovery questions feel scripted and reps sound robotic**
*Root cause:* Questions are written in marketing language, not how a rep naturally speaks.
*Fix:* Record 3 of your best discovery calls, transcribe the questions the top performer asks, and rewrite the framework using their exact vocabulary. Run role-plays in Gong Engage or Chorus Moments where reps practice until the questions feel natural. Create a "question menu" so reps can choose the phrasing that fits their style.

**Issue: Win rate isn't improving even with the new materials**
*Root cause:* Content quality is fine but the real problem is earlier in the funnel — wrong ICP, unqualified pipeline, or champion without authority.
*Fix:* Run a 30-deal win-loss audit. Tag each deal with: (a) ICP fit score, (b) champion strength score, (c) budget confirmed Y/N, (d) executive sponsor engaged Y/N. You'll likely find that 60%+ of losses had a disqualifying signal visible at discovery that was ignored. Update your qualification criteria and train reps to exit bad deals earlier rather than trying to rescue them with content.
