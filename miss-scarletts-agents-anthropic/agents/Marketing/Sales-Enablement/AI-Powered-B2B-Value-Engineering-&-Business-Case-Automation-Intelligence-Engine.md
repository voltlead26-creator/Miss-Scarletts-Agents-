## Overview
Generates fully automated, CFO-ready business cases and ROI models by mapping your solution's value drivers to a prospect's specific cost structure, strategic priorities, and measurable outcomes — eliminating the week-long manual process and enabling sales and PMM teams to produce board-quality justification documents in minutes.

## Integration Tips

**CRM (Salesforce / HubSpot):**
- Store business case as an attachment on the Opportunity record
- Create a custom field: "Business Case Delivered Date" — triggers automated champion follow-up sequence
- Tag business case version to deal stage (e.g., "v1 at Demo Stage → v2 at Economic Buyer Stage")

**Deal Room (Notion, Highspot, Seismic, Showpad):**
- Create a prospect-specific deal room with the Executive One-Pager as the pinned top document
- Track which sections the economic buyer opened and for how long — feeds rep coaching
- Add a Loom video walkthrough from the AE over the one-pager to humanize the numbers

**Finance / Approval Workflow (DocuSign, Ironclad):**
- Embed the financial model as an approved template that procurement can forward internally for budget approval
- Auto-populate deal-specific variables from Salesforce opportunity data

**Automation (Zapier, Make, Clay):**
- Trigger business case generation automatically when deal moves to "Economic Buyer Engaged" stage in CRM
- Pull prospect firmographic data (Crunchbase, LinkedIn, ZoomInfo) to auto-populate company size, ARR, and headcount assumptions
- Use Clay to enrich prospect's recent press releases and earnings data for "why now" framing

**Analytics:**
- Track business case → closed-won correlation in your BI tool (Tableau, Looker, Metabase)
- A/B test: deals with business case delivered vs. without — report lift to VP Sales monthly

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "Our prospects say the ROI numbers feel too good to be true"**
Fix: Lead with your most conservative scenario (40% realization) and show break-even sensitivity. Frame it as: "Even if we only deliver 25% of projected value, you still get a positive return." Anchor on one high-confidence metric (e.g., time savings) rather than leading with total ROI.

**Problem: "The CFO rejected the business case without meeting with us"**
Fix: Your champion sent the full financial model before the CFO was primed. Instead, have your champion send only the Executive One-Pager with a handwritten (or personalized) note. The goal of the one-pager is to get the meeting — not close the deal. Save the full model for the CFO meeting itself.

**Problem: "Our value drivers are hard to quantify (e.g., 'better decision-making', 'strategic clarity')"**
Fix: Convert soft benefits into hard time/money equivalents. "Better decision-making" = fewer bad hires × $180K/hire cost. "Strategic clarity" = hours saved in executive planning meetings × $250/hr executive time. Use the HARD/SOFT/STRATEGIC framework: always anchor 70% of the business case on HARD savings, use SOFT and STRATEGIC as upside.
