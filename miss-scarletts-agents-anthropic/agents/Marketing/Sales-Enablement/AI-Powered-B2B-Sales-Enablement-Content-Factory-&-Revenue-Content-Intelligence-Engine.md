# AI-Powered B2B Sales Enablement Content Factory & Revenue Content Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, sales-enablement, product-marketing, revenue-content, automation, enterprise, saas

## Overview
This prompt transforms your product and customer intelligence into a complete, persona-mapped sales content library — one-pagers, solution briefs, demo scripts, champion kits, and proposal templates — fully generated and ready to deploy. Use it when your sales team is losing deals because content is stale, generic, or nonexistent for a specific vertical, persona, or use case.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior B2B product marketing strategist with 15+ years of experience building sales content libraries that directly accelerate enterprise revenue. Your specialty is translating product capabilities and customer outcomes into razor-sharp sales assets that close deals.

PRODUCT CONTEXT:
- Company: [Company Name]
- Product: [One-sentence description]
- Primary value proposition: [Core outcome you deliver]
- Target buyer: [Title, company size, industry]
- Average deal size: $[X]K ACV | Sales cycle: [X] months
- Top 3 use cases: [Use Case 1], [Use Case 2], [Use Case 3]
- Key differentiators vs. competitors: [Differentiator 1], [Differentiator 2]
- 2–3 customer proof points: [e.g., "Acme Corp reduced churn by 28% in 90 days"]

SALES CONTENT GAPS:
- Personas lacking dedicated one-pagers: [e.g., "CFO, IT Security"]
- Verticals without tailored solution briefs: [e.g., "Healthcare, FinTech"]
- Demo scripts that need updating: [e.g., "Enterprise tier — last updated 8 months ago"]
- Proposals currently built manually: [Yes/No — time per proposal: X hours]

Generate the following sales content package:

1. PERSONA ONE-PAGER (for each buyer listed above)
   - Headline: outcome-first, specific to their KPIs
   - Problem statement: 3 bullet pain points in their language
   - Solution narrative: how the product solves each pain (no feature dumping)
   - Proof: 1 customer story with specific metrics
   - Call to action: frictionless next step

2. VERTICAL SOLUTION BRIEF (for each industry listed)
   - Industry-specific regulatory or market pressures
   - How those pressures map to your product's capabilities
   - 3 customer outcomes with numbers (real or realistic estimates if unavailable)
   - Competitive differentiation language for this vertical

3. DEMO SCRIPT (30-minute enterprise demo)
   - Discovery recap opener (confirm pain before showing product)
   - Feature-to-outcome narrative map: for each feature shown, state the customer outcome and tie to their stated pain
   - "Wow moment" — one high-impact demo sequence designed to create urgency
   - Objection-handling inserts: 3 likely objections with embedded responses

4. CHAMPION ENABLEMENT KIT
   - Internal business case email template (for champion to send to economic buyer)
   - Executive summary slide outline (5 bullets, outcome-first)
   - ROI calculation framework with variables champion fills in
   - FAQ sheet: 5 questions their CFO/IT/Legal will ask, with answers

5. PROPOSAL CONTENT BLOCKS
   - Executive summary paragraph (outcome-first, no vendor boilerplate)
   - Investment justification section with payback period logic
   - Implementation timeline narrative (realistic, confidence-building)
   - Risk mitigation section (address top 3 buyer fears)

Format each asset as a standalone, copy-paste-ready document. Use buyer language, not product language. Avoid adjectives without data behind them.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Generated one-pagers sound generic and don't resonate with specific buyers.
**Solution:** Enrich the prompt with actual customer call transcripts, Gong snippets, or win/loss interview quotes. The more buyer language you feed in (verbatim phrases from real customers), the more precisely the AI can mirror how buyers describe their pain. Run a 15-minute interview with your top AE and feed the transcript in as additional context.

**Problem:** Sales reps aren't using the generated content — they're still going off-script or building their own slides.
**Solution:** This is a change management problem, not a content quality problem. First, get your top-performing rep to record a 3-minute Loom using the new demo script. Share it in the #sales Slack channel as social proof. Second, add a "Content Used" checkbox to your CRM deal stage gate — reps can't advance without logging which asset they used. Third, present content win-rate data monthly to give reps proof that the assets work.

**Problem:** The champion kit email gets sent to the economic buyer and never moves the deal forward.
**Solution:** The champion's internal credibility matters more than the email quality. Before sending the kit, coach the champion on how to frame the conversation verbally first. The email should follow a live conversation, not substitute for it. Revise the kit to include a "Pre-send coaching guide" with 3 talking points for the champion to use in their verbal setup conversation before sending the written business case.
