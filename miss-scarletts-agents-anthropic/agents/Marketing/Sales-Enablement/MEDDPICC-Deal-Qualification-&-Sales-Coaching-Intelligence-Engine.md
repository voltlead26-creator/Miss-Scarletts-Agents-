- YELLOW (4-7): Impact is directionally understood but not quantified or not tied to EB's goals

**E — ECONOMIC BUYER**
- GREEN (8-10): EB identified, met with, confirmed budget authority, and aware of investment range
- YELLOW (4-7): EB identified but not yet engaged, or engagement was superficial/ceremonial
- RED (1-3): EB unknown, or champion claims to be EB but doesn't have authority

**D — DECISION CRITERIA**
- GREEN (8-10): Formal scorecard or RFP criteria obtained; we have influenced at least 2 criteria to favor our differentiation
- YELLOW (4-7): Verbal criteria shared but not documented; limited influence over criteria
- RED (1-3): Criteria unknown or entirely competitor-shaped

**D — DECISION PROCESS**
- GREEN (8-10): Complete buying process mapped including all committees, approvals, and checkpoints; Mutual Action Plan agreed upon
- YELLOW (4-7): High-level process known but steps after technical evaluation unclear
- RED (1-3): Process opaque; no MAP; rep is following prospect's timeline blindly

**P — PAPER PROCESS**
- GREEN (8-10): Procurement contacted, contract format confirmed, legal/security review timeline known, signatures identified
- YELLOW (4-7): Know procurement is involved but haven't engaged them; no security review scheduled
- RED (1-3): No visibility into paper process; close date likely to slip

**I — IDENTIFY PAIN**
- GREEN (8-10): Explicit, urgent pain tied to a strategic initiative with a known cost of inaction; pain is EB-level
- YELLOW (4-7): Pain identified at champion level but not elevated to EB; urgency unclear
- RED (1-3): Pain is vague ("we want to improve"), reactive, or initiative-driven by vendor not buyer

**C — CHAMPION**
- GREEN (8-10): Champion has organizational credibility, access to EB, is actively selling internally, and has personal upside tied to success
- YELLOW (4-7): Champion is friendly but passive; hasn't demonstrated internal selling behavior; unknown influence level
- RED (1-3): No true champion; "champions" are users without power or access

**C — COMPETITION**
- GREEN (8-10): Full competitive landscape mapped; know competitor's strengths and weaknesses; have placed competitive landmines in criteria; know prospect's current preference
- YELLOW (4-7): Know who competitors are but don't know prospect's lean or have unaddressed FUD

## Troubleshooting

**"The output is too generic — it doesn't feel specific to my deal"**
Feed richer input data. Paste actual call transcripts (even partial), champion email threads, or Gong AI summaries. The more raw signal, the more precise the coaching. If you only have 3 sentences of notes, the AI is working from limited signal and will default to pattern-matching.

**"The deal health score seems too low / doesn't match my gut feel"**
The score is weighted toward economic buyer and champion strength because research shows these two dimensions are the highest predictors of close. If your gut says the deal is stronger, check whether you have EB and champion evidence that wasn't captured in your notes. Also verify: are you confusing a friendly user (champion proxy) with a true champion who is selling internally?

**"The March 31 close date analysis says it'll slip but my manager says to keep it in Commit"**
Run the Paper Process dimension explicitly: How many days does legal review take? How many days for security? Has procurement ever seen a contract of this size? If the answers suggest you need 30+ days, the prompt is right. Use the output to have a data-driven forecast conversation with your manager rather than a gut-feel debate.
