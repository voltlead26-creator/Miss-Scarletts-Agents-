## Overview
Transforms weekly pipeline reviews from gut-feel status updates into AI-driven coaching sessions — generating deal health scores, identifying at-risk opportunities before the call, and producing manager-ready coaching playbooks with specific marketing interventions for each rep's book. Use it to increase forecast accuracy, accelerate late-stage velocity, and give every rep a deal strategy advisor in their pocket.

## Success Metrics

- **Forecast accuracy:** Weekly commit forecast should land within ±10% of actual closed-won by quarter end
- **Coaching conversation quality:** Each 1:1 references a specific deal and specific coaching question — not generic pipeline review
- **Days in stage reduction:** Track average days per stage before and after implementing AI-driven coaching — target 20% reduction in Stage 3 and Stage 4 stall time
- **At-risk deal save rate:** Track what % of RED deals flagged in the review converted to Closed-Won or meaningfully advanced — target 25%+ save rate
- **Marketing play response rate:** Track which marketing interventions (content, exec outreach, ROI tools) generate prospect re-engagement — use to build a ranked playbook over time
- **Pipeline coverage accuracy:** Ensure weighted pipeline math is validated against historical stage win rates — recalibrate stage probabilities each quarter

## Integration Tips

- **CRM (Salesforce / HubSpot):** Export pipeline to CSV weekly → paste into the Advanced prompt → push deal health scores back as a custom field (e.g., "AI Health Score"). In Salesforce, create a custom Pipeline Review Dashboard that surfaces the RED/YELLOW/GREEN flags as a list view. In HubSpot, use the "Notes" field to log AI coaching recommendations after each 1:1.
- **Slack / Teams:** Set up a Monday morning automation that posts the executive pipeline summary and top 3 at-risk deals to your #revenue or #pipeline Slack channel. Reps get their individual deal health score as a DM. Use Zapier or Make to automate the weekly post from a Google Sheet that receives the AI output.
- **Gong / Chorus / Salesloft:** Pull last call transcript snippets for each deal and include them as context in the AI prompt — the model can then identify whether the rep actually uncovered economic buyer, quantified pain, and established next steps during the call. This creates a closed-loop coaching system.
- **Clari / Boostup / Aviso:** If you're running a dedicated forecast intelligence tool, use this prompt to supplement — not replace — those tools. The AI is strongest at generating coaching language, deal strategy, and marketing plays; your RI tool is better at ML-based probability scoring.
- **Google Sheets:** Create a pipeline tracking template with the exact columns the prompt requires. Connect to your CRM via Zapier or a native sync. Run the AI analysis every Monday morning and store outputs in a "Pipeline Review" tab for historical coaching tracking.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Store rep coaching plans in individual Notion pages. After each pipeline review, paste the rep-specific coaching brief into their page. Over 12 weeks, you'll have a coaching history that shows skill development patterns and recurring obstacles.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI's deal health scores don't match my intuition about which deals are healthy.**
Solution: Calibrate the scoring weights to match your historical win rate data. If "last activity recency" is less predictive in your sales motion than "champion confirmation," adjust the weights in the Advanced version accordingly. The scoring model is a starting framework — tune it using your own closed-won and closed-lost data over 2 quarters.

**Problem: The coaching questions feel generic and don't land well in 1:1s.**
Solution: Add more deal-specific context to the prompt. Include the last 2–3 call notes or email snippets for each deal. The more context the AI has about what the rep actually said and what the prospect actually responded to, the more specific and useful the coaching questions become. Also specify the rep's experience level — coaching a 2-year enterprise AE requires different questions than coaching a 6-month SDR moving into an AE role.

**Problem: Reps resist the pipeline review process and see it as surveillance.**
Solution: Frame the output as a "deal strategy assistant" rather than a "performance grading tool." Give reps access to their own deal health scores 24 hours before the manager sees them — this creates self-coaching behavior and reduces the surveillance perception. Reps who use the AI coaching prompts independently before 1:1s tend to have better conversations because they've already thought through the hard questions.
