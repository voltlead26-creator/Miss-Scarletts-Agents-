ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK:

**Module 1: Win Pattern Extraction**
Apply the following lenses to identify what separates closed-won from closed-lost:

A) DISCOVERY EXCELLENCE SCORING
- Evaluate: Did the rep uncover the economic impact of the problem? (Monetize the pain)
- Evaluate: Was success criteria co-created with the champion?
- Evaluate: Did rep map the decision process and committee?
- Evaluate: Was competitive context proactively surfaced (not just reactive)?

B) TALK TRACK EFFECTIVENESS
- Extract verbatim phrases used in won deals that differ from lost deals

C) OBJECTION HANDLING PATTERNS
- Catalog all objections by type: price, timing, competitor, champion, risk
- Score responses: Acknowledge → Explore → Reframe → Validate
- Identify which responses converted to next-step commitment

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Output is too generic, doesn't feel like it reflects real calls
*Fix:* Paste actual verbatim excerpts from transcripts (even 5-10 sentence snippets) rather than paraphrased summaries. The more buyer language in the input, the more specific the output. Include deal outcome alongside each snippet.

**Problem:** Competitive intelligence section produces marketing messaging, not real buyer language
*Fix:* Include the exact words prospects used when mentioning competitors ("they said Salesforce already does this" is more useful input than "prospect compared us to Salesforce"). Add a section: "Competitor objections we heard this quarter verbatim: [list them]"

**Problem:** Rep coaching recommendations feel too cookie-cutter
*Fix:* Add rep-level data to the prompt: "Rep A closed 4/6 deals this quarter. Here are their call stats: talk ratio 58%, avg 12 questions per call, 3 competitive deals." Segment by rep archetype rather than asking for one-size-fits-all coaching.
