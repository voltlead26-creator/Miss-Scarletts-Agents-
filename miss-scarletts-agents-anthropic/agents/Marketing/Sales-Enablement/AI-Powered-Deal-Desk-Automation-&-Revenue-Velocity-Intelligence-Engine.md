## Troubleshooting

**Issue 1: Prompt produces generic discount recommendations not tied to your actual margin model**
*Fix:* Add your actual gross margin targets, COGS breakdown, and approval authority matrix to the Company Context section. The more specific your margin floor and approval tiers, the more actionable the output. If you have a published discount schedule (e.g., 10% self-serve, 20% Deal Desk, 30% VP), paste it verbatim into the prompt.

**Issue 2: Non-standard terms risk analysis lacks legal precision**
*Fix:* This prompt produces sales-facing risk summaries, not legal opinions. For high-stakes terms (unlimited liability, IP indemnification, data processing agreements), always route the flagged terms to legal counsel. Use the output as a first-pass triage tool to prioritize which terms need attorney review, not as a substitute for it.

**Issue 3: Deal acceleration playbook feels too generic for the specific buyer**
*Fix:* Feed more MEDDPICC detail — especially the champion's internal stakeholder map, the paper process steps, and any specific objections raised in calls. The more specific the input about where the deal is actually stalling, the more targeted the stall-risk mitigation tactics. If you have Gong or Chorus call transcripts, paste key buyer quotes to ground the playbook in real buyer language.
