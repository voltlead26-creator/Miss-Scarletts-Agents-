## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a dedicated "Partner" pipeline separate from your customer pipeline. Use custom contact properties: `partner_type`, `ipp_score`, `partner_tier`, `activation_status`, `first_deal_date`. Set workflow triggers: application submitted → scoring workflow → routing to AE or self-serve sequence.
- **Salesforce:** Build a `Partner Account` record type with custom fields mirroring IPP scoring attributes. Connect to PRM (Impartner, Allbound, or Salesforce PRM) for deal registration tracking. Use Salesforce Flows to auto-assign partner AEs based on geography and tier score.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Upload partner prospect lists (company names + emails) as "Matched Audiences" for Account-Based targeting. Create a separate LinkedIn Campaign Manager account or sub-account for partner recruitment to isolate spend from customer campaigns.
- **Zapier/Make:** Trigger: New partner application form submitted → Enrich via Clearbit/Apollo → Score in Google Sheets → Update HubSpot → Notify partner AE in Slack with score summary. Use Make to sync approved partner records to your PRM.
- **Apollo/Clay:** Use Clay to build automated partner prospecting workflows — pull MSP company data from LinkedIn + G2 partner badges + job posting signals → enrich → score → push to HubSpot as partner prospects → trigger cold email sequence in Outreach or Instantly.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High application volume but low-quality partners applying**
*Solution:* Your IPP targeting is too broad, or your paid media audiences are not specific enough. Add negative keywords to Google campaigns (exclude "freelance," "consultant," "solopreneur"). On LinkedIn, narrow to companies 15-500 employees in your target verticals. Add a qualifying question to your application: "What is your company's annual recurring revenue?" — this single question filters out 60%+ of unfit applicants.

**Problem: Qualified partners approve but never activate (submit first deal)**
*Solution:* The gap between approval and activation is a training/motivation failure, not a recruiting failure. Audit your Day 0–30 nurture sequence: Are partners getting live training, not just documentation? Add a mandatory 45-minute "First Deal Fast Start" call with a partner AE within 7 days of approval. Consider a first-deal SPiF (special incentive) — e.g., $500 cash bonus for first deal closed within 60 days — to create urgency.

**Problem: Partner recruitment campaigns are cannibalizing customer-facing ad spend**
*Solution:* LinkedIn audience overlap is the most common cause. Create separate LinkedIn Campaign Manager campaigns with explicit audience exclusions — exclude job titles that are your end-customer buyers (e.g., exclude CISO, VP IT if you're recruiting MSPs, not selling direct). Use distinct UTM parameters for all partner campaigns to separate attribution in HubSpot/GA4.
