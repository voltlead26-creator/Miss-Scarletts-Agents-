## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI models give inconsistent citation results when running the same query on different days**
Fix: AI model responses vary by session, model version, and knowledge cutoff. Use a standardized query template (exact same phrasing), run each query 3 times per model, and record the most common citation pattern rather than single-test results. Track trends over 4-week rolling periods rather than week-over-week to smooth variance.

**Problem: New content is published but no AI citations appear after 60 days**
Fix: Check three citation blockers: (a) Content may not have enough trust signals — add named expert quotes with titles and companies, add 3+ authoritative external citations, add statistical claims with sources. (b) Domain authority may be too low for AI models to surface it — accelerate backlink acquisition through PR and guest posts on high-authority sites. (c) The query may require content cluster authority, not a single page — build 3–5 supporting pages linked to your primary page to establish topical authority depth.

**Problem: Competitors dominate every priority query and displacement seems impossible**
Fix: Reframe the interception strategy. Instead of displacing incumbents on their strongest queries, identify the 15–20 emerging or underserved queries where no vendor has established citation dominance yet — these are your fastest citation wins. Also look for queries specific to your differentiated use cases (e.g., a specific industry, integration, or compliance requirement) where competitors have thin content. Own the niche queries first, then use that citation authority to compete for broader queries.
