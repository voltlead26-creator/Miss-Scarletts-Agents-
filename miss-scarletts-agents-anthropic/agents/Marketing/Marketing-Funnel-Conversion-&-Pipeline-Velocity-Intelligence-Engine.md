# Marketing Funnel Conversion & Pipeline Velocity Intelligence Engine - Diagnose Every Bottleneck From Click to Closed-Won

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, analytics, pipeline, funnel, conversion-rates, velocity, saas, reporting, revenue-operations

## Overview
Analyzes full-funnel conversion rates stage by stage (Visitor → Lead → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Closed-Won), calculates pipeline velocity, identifies bottlenecks costing you revenue, and produces an automated weekly/monthly intelligence report with prioritized fix recommendations. Use this when pipeline coverage is below target, close rates are dropping, or leadership asks "where exactly are we losing deals?"

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "We don't use a formal MQL stage — leads go straight to the sales team"**
Solution: Run the analysis with a simplified 4-stage funnel: Visitor → Lead → Opportunity → Closed-Won. Use the same framework for revenue leakage and velocity. Then use the output's "Fix Roadmap" section to build the business case for implementing an MQL qualification layer — the revenue leakage analysis will quantify exactly what unqualified lead routing is costing you in sales productivity.

**Problem: "My win rate looks terrible (below 20%) but leadership thinks our pipeline is strong"**
Solution: A low win rate is almost always a pipeline quality problem, not a closing problem. Use Section 5 (Segmentation Analysis) to break win rate by ICP segment, deal size, and lead source. You'll typically find that 1–2 segments have acceptable win rates while others drag the average down. Present the "segment win rate table" to show leadership that the total pipeline number is inflated by low-fit opportunities — and recommend tightening SQL criteria rather than adding more pipeline.

**Problem: "Our sales cycle varies wildly — some deals close in 2 weeks, others take 18 months"**
Solution: Segment pipeline velocity by deal size tier (e.g., <$10K ACV, $10–50K, $50K+). Calculate separate velocity figures and fix roadmaps for each tier. Enterprise deals with long cycles need different interventions (executive sponsor access, mutual action plans, procurement navigation) than transactional SMB deals. Report velocity as a blended number for the board but manage it separately by segment internally.
