# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Strategic Account Land & Expand Campaign Architecture & Multi-Year Revenue Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** land-and-expand, strategic accounts, enterprise marketing, campaign architecture, multi-year pipeline, ABM, buying committee, B2B, NRR, expansion revenue

## Overview

This prompt deploys an AI agent to architect a complete 18–24 month strategic account marketing campaign journey—from first enterprise awareness touchpoint through multi-product expansion—mapping phase-by-phase campaign programs, buying committee engagement sequences, milestone-based trigger logic, and cross-functional orchestration. Use it when building the campaign architecture for your top 20–50 named accounts or designing a systematic expansion program for strategic accounts already in your portfolio.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior B2B enterprise marketing strategist specializing in strategic account campaign architecture for SaaS companies with $25K–$500K+ ACV deals. Design a complete 18-month Land & Expand Campaign Architecture for the following strategic account.

Target Account: [Company name, industry, employee count]
Our Product: [What it does, one sentence]
ICP Champion Persona: [e.g., "VP of Operations, 500-5,000 employee manufacturing companies"]
Economic Buyer: [e.g., "CFO or COO"]
Key Pain Point We Solve: [e.g., "Manual approval workflows causing 4-week invoice cycle times"]
Current Account Status: [New prospect / Early-stage opportunity / Customer (existing product) / Stalled deal]
Existing relationship: [e.g., "No contact" / "Champion identified but no exec sponsor" / "3 users on Starter plan"]
ACV target: [e.g., $180,000 initial land; $400,000 at full expansion]
Primary competitors in this account: [e.g., "SAP, internal tools, status quo"]

Deliver a complete 18-Month Strategic Account Campaign Architecture including:

1. ACCOUNT INTELLIGENCE BRIEF: Write a research framework to map the account's org structure, identify all buying committee roles, uncover pain point evidence (earnings calls, job postings, press releases, LinkedIn activity), and score the account's "sales-readiness" on a 1-10 scale with rationale.

2. CAMPAIGN PHASE MAP: Design 4 distinct campaign phases with clear entry and exit criteria:
   - Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Awareness & Champion Activation — getting on the account's radar, identifying and engaging the champion
   - Phase 2 (Months 4-6): Evaluation Acceleration — supporting the champion through internal selling, executive engagement, competitive displacement
   - Phase 3 (Months 7-9): Close & Onboarding — post-signature marketing to ensure adoption and early wins
   - Phase 4 (Months 10-18): Expansion — cross-sell/upsell campaigns, executive relationship deepening, multi-department rollout

3. BUYING COMMITTEE ENGAGEMENT PLAN: For each committee role (Champion, Economic Buyer, Technical Evaluator, Legal/Procurement, End Users), write a specific sequence of marketing touches including content type, channel, message, and timing.

4. 5 CAMPAIGN ASSETS TO PRODUCE: Specify the exact assets needed for this account (e.g., custom ROI calculator, industry benchmark report, competitor comparison, executive briefing deck), who produces each, and when in the campaign timeline they're deployed.

5. EXECUTIVE ENGAGEMENT PROGRAM: Design a 6-touch executive marketing sequence (C-suite to C-suite) to create an executive sponsor relationship parallel to the champion-driven pipeline.

6. EXPANSION TRIGGER MAP: Define the top 5 product adoption signals or business events that indicate expansion readiness, and write the first-touch outreach for each.

7. SUCCESS METRICS: Define 8 KPIs to track monthly across the 18-month campaign (pipeline metrics, engagement metrics, and revenue metrics).

Flag assumptions made and what account intelligence would sharpen the campaign architecture.

SECTION 2: 18-MONTH CAMPAIGN PHASE ARCHITECTURE

Design four distinct campaign phases. For each phase, specify:

PHASE STRUCTURE:
- Phase Goal (single sentence)
- Entry Criteria (what must be true before this phase begins)
- Exit Criteria (what must be achieved to advance to the next phase)
- Key Milestone (the one event that defines success in this phase)
- Marketing-Sales Coordination Points (where marketing hands off to sales and vice versa)
- Budget Allocation ($ or % of total account budget)

PHASE 1 — AWARENESS & CHAMPION IDENTIFICATION (Months 1-3):
Goal: Get on the account's radar, identify and activate the most likely champion, and generate an inbound signal or establish a warm relationship.

Campaign Programs:
A. Digital Surround Strategy:
   - LinkedIn Sponsored Content targeting job titles in the target account (if >500 employees, target by company + title)
   - Retargeting campaign: any account employee who visits our website sees "Financial Services teams at [Account Size] save X hours/month" ads
   - Programmatic ABM display on IP addresses matching account headquarters
   - Ad creative for Phase 1: Problem-aware messaging (not product). Focus on "signs your approval workflows are costing you more than you think"

B. Content-Led Outreach (SDR + Marketing sequenced together):
   Day 1: SDR sends warm email using a specific pain-point data point from their recent earnings call or job posting
   Day 3: Marketing sends LinkedIn connection request from executive sponsor (VP Marketing or CEO) with no ask — just shared context
   Day 8: SDR follows up with a relevant case study or industry report (not a product pitch)
   Day 15: Direct mail piece — a physical "State of Finance Operations 2026" benchmark report or personalized audit summary sent to Champion's address
   Day 22: SDR email referencing the direct mail with a soft CTA to a 15-minute discovery call

C. Thought Leadership Positioning:
   Ensure target account executives see our content on LinkedIn 3-5x per week through:
   - Boosted posts from our executive LinkedIn accounts targeting account's domain
   - Industry publication ads (e.g., CFO.com, Financial Times ads if account is large enterprise)
   - Sponsored content in newsletters they likely read (e.g., industry associations, regional business publications)

D. Intelligence Gathering:
   Define 3 trigger events that indicate Phase 1 is working: (1) account employee visits pricing page, (2) SDR gets a response, (3) champion accepts LinkedIn connection

---

PHASE 2 — EVALUATION ACCELERATION (Months 4-6):
Goal: Support the identified champion through the internal selling process, neutralize objections before they reach the economic buyer, and drive executive-to-executive relationship.

Assumption: Champion has been identified and is internally advocating. A discovery call has happened. Opportunity is in Stage 2-3 in CRM.

Campaign Programs:
A. Champion Enablement Kit (custom-built marketing assets for this account):
   - Custom ROI Model: A tailored spreadsheet calculator showing their specific numbers (based on their disclosed headcount, transaction volume, and cost data) — built by marketing, reviewed by champion, presented to CFO
   - Business Case Template: A pre-filled executive presentation they can use to sell internally — include competitive comparison, security compliance summary, implementation timeline, and reference customer in same industry
   - Objection Neutralization One-Pager: Preemptively addresses the top 3 objections likely to arise internally (IT consolidation concern, build-vs-buy, implementation risk)

B. Executive Engagement Program (parallel track to champion):
   - Our CFO/CEO sends a personal note (not a sales email) to their CFO referencing a shared challenge or industry context: "I saw your Q3 earnings call mention the cost efficiency initiative — we've helped 3 similar FSI companies reduce AP cycle time by 70%. Happy to share what worked." No CTA. Just a warm opening.
   - Invite economic buyer to an intimate executive roundtable dinner (8-12 executives, peer-to-peer discussion, no product demo)
   - Share a private research report or early access to an industry analysis — value first, no ask

C. Technical Evaluator Engagement:
   - Invite IT/security evaluator to a Technical Deep Dive webinar with our VP of Engineering (not a sales call — a genuine technical discussion)
   - Send SOC 2 Type II report, security architecture documentation, and enterprise integration guide proactively
   - Schedule a 45-minute "Integration Architecture Workshop" to map their tech stack to our capabilities

D. Competitive Displacement Content (if a competitor is in the evaluation):
   - Build a custom competitive comparison document showing side-by-side capabilities, implementation timelines, total cost of ownership, and customer references
   - Prepare a "Reference Customer Introduction" — connect champion with a peer at a similar company who can speak to their experience
   - Create a "Why Customers Choose [Us] Over [Competitor]" one-pager specifically for the accounts's evaluation criteria

---

PHASE 3 — CLOSE & LAND ACTIVATION (Months 7-9):
Goal: Support the final decision and purchase, then immediately activate marketing to drive rapid adoption in the initial module so the expansion case builds itself.

Campaign Programs:
A. Pre-Signature Marketing:
   - Proposal validation support: review their RFP response or proposal for competitive positioning
   - Procurement readiness kit: vendor security questionnaire pre-fills, DPA/BAA templates, insurance certificates ready to deliver without delay
   - Implementation anxiety reduction: "What your first 90 days will look like" content — timeline, team requirements, milestone calendar

B. Welcome & Onboarding Campaign (Days 1-90 post-signature):
   Day 1: Welcome email from our CEO to their CFO/champion — personal, not templated
   Day 7: "Your Success Team" introduction — introduce CSM, solutions engineer, and their named AE
   Day 30: First value milestone email — "Your team has processed [X] workflows. Here's the time saved so far."
   Day 60: Benchmark report: "Where you are vs. companies at 60 days — and what the top performers do differently"
   Day 90: Executive ROI Review invitation: "Let's share what the first quarter delivered — and map what the next year could look like"

C. User Activation Campaign:
   - In-app onboarding tips and milestone alerts
   - Weekly "workflow spotlight" email to active users showing a tip or power feature
   - Department-level leaderboard (if applicable): "Your team has automated 340 tasks this month — here's how you compare to peers"
   - Executive digest: Monthly 1-page summary of team productivity impact sent to the economic buyer (keeps them informed, builds expansion case)

---

PHASE 4 — EXPANSION ORCHESTRATION (Months 10-18):
Goal: Grow from the initial module to 2-3 additional product modules or departments. Target 2x ACV expansion by month 18.

Expansion Trigger System — for each trigger, define: Detection Method | First-Touch Message | Owner | Expected Conversion Timeline

Trigger 1 — HIGH ADOPTION IN INITIAL MODULE:
Detection: >80% of licensed users active for 3+ consecutive months; processing volume growing >15% MoM
First-Touch: CSM + AE joint call: "Your team is at 87% adoption — the top companies at this stage typically ask about expanding to [HR/IT module]. Want to see a 15-minute demo of how [Company Similar to Theirs] did it?"

Trigger 2 — NEW EXECUTIVE HIRE IN EXPANSION DEPARTMENT:
Detection: LinkedIn alert for new VP of HR or VP of IT hired at account; Bamboo HR integration signal if available
First-Touch: Marketing sends "New to the role? Here's what your peers in HR Operations are automating in their first 90 days" content via LinkedIn + SDR warm outreach

Trigger 3 — DEPARTMENT EXPANSION (HEADCOUNT GROWTH):
Detection: LinkedIn headcount growth >20% in target expansion department; job posting signals
First-Touch: "Your finance team has grown significantly — accounts payable teams at your scale typically add [Module X] to handle the volume. We have benchmark data on what that costs without automation."

Trigger 4 — CHAMPION CHANGE / NEW INTERNAL CHAMPION:
Detection: Original champion changes roles or departs (LinkedIn alert; CSM awareness)
First-Touch: Executive sponsor on our side reaches out to new contact AND existing champion's manager to maintain continuity: "I wanted to personally introduce [new name from our team] who will be your day-to-day partner as Sarah moves into her new role."

Trigger 5 — BUSINESS EVENT TRIGGER (M&A, GEOGRAPHIC EXPANSION):
Detection: News alert for merger, acquisition, or new office opening
First-Touch: "You've announced [event]. Here's how [similar company] used workflow automation to integrate two organizations in 60 days vs. the industry average of 9 months."

CONSTRAINTS:
- Every campaign touch must have a clear "why now" rationale — never reach out without a specific trigger or hook
- Executive engagement program must never look like a synchronized sales motion — tone is always peer-to-peer, value-first
- Champion enablement assets must be designed for internal distribution — they will be forwarded to people we've never met; quality and brand must hold up without context
- Expansion campaigns must not launch until adoption of initial module hits 70%+ — premature expansion campaigns signal desperation and damage trust
- All timelines assume a 3-6 month enterprise sales cycle for initial land — adjust phase timing if your average cycle is shorter or longer
- Flag any recommendation that requires dedicated engineering or CS resources beyond standard enterprise onboarding

## Success Metrics

**Phase 1-3 KPIs (Track Monthly in CRM):**
- Buying Committee Coverage: % of identified committee members with at least 1 documented marketing or sales engagement — target: 80%+ by end of Phase 2
- Champion Engagement Score: Number of content pieces consumed + meetings attended + internal champion actions taken (business case shared, exec meeting set) — composite score updated weekly
- Executive Reach Rate: % of economic buyers engaged (meeting held or relationship established) — target: 100% by end of Phase 2
- Pipeline Velocity: Number of days from Phase 1 start to Stage 3 opportunity — benchmark: 90-120 days for $150K+ ACV deals
- Asset Deployment Rate: % of planned campaign assets delivered on schedule — target: 90%+

**Phase 4 KPIs (Expansion, Track Monthly):**
- Adoption Depth: % of licensed users active in initial module — expansion campaigns should only launch when >70%
- Expansion Pipeline Coverage: Number of expansion opportunities opened relative to installed base — target: 60% of strategic accounts have active expansion opportunity by Month 12
- Expansion ACV Growth: Expansion MRR from strategic accounts as % of original land ACV — target: 100% expansion (2x ACV) within 24 months
- Net Revenue Retention for Strategic Account Cohort: Target 140%+ NRR on named account program

**Leading Indicators (Weekly):**
- New buying committee member engaged this week (yes/no)

## Integration Tips

**Salesforce:**
- Create a "Strategic Account Campaign" object in Salesforce to track each phase, milestone, and buying committee engagement score separately from the standard Opportunity record — enables cohort analysis on what campaign patterns drive fastest close times
- Custom field on Account: "Expansion Readiness Score" (calculated from CSM input + product usage data) — refreshes monthly; triggers expansion campaign enrollment when score crosses 7/10
- Salesforce Activity capture: log all marketing touches (direct mail, executive emails, event attendance) as Activities on the Contact record — ensures full attribution across the 18-month campaign

**HubSpot:**
- Use HubSpot's "Company-level" workflows to enroll all contacts at a strategic account into the campaign — ensures every new champion or evaluator discovered mid-campaign is automatically enrolled in the right phase sequence
- Build a "Buying Committee Engagement Dashboard" in HubSpot: by strategic account, show total touches per role, content consumed, meetings held, and estimated influence score
- Document tracking: use HubSpot Documents (or tools like Aligned, Seismic) to detect when your champion shares assets internally — a forwarded business case is one of the highest-signal expansion and close indicators

**Salesforce + LinkedIn Sales Navigator (Integration):**
- Set up LinkedIn Sales Navigator alerts for all buying committee members: new posts, role changes, company news — route alerts into Salesforce as Tasks for the AE to action
- Use Navigator's "Relationship Map" to visualize and close buying committee gaps — share the map in weekly deal review with marketing and sales to coordinate coverage

**Gong / Chorus (Conversation Intelligence):**
- Create a Gong tracker for strategic account keywords ("cost reduction initiative," "approval workflow," "digital transformation") to surface relevant mentions in sales calls — marketing uses these to sharpen asset messaging in real time
- Configure Gong's "Deal Boards" to flag strategic accounts that haven't had a meeting in 14+ days — triggers a marketing "re-engagement" touch to prevent momentum loss

**6sense / Demandbase / Bombora (ABM & Intent Data):**

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "The champion is engaged and loves our product, but we can't get access to the economic buyer — the deal is stalling at the champion level."**
Solution: This is the most common failure mode in enterprise campaigns. Execute the "Executive Parallel Track" immediately: your executive sponsor sends a personal, value-first note to the economic buyer independent of the sales cycle — not referencing the deal, just referencing a relevant business challenge. Simultaneously, invite the economic buyer to a peer-only executive event (no product discussion, no sales reps in the room) where they can hear from similar companies. If the champion is willing, ask them directly: "What would it take to get Sarah on a 20-minute call with our CFO? We want to make sure we're solving the right problem at the right level." Champions who can't get executive access are often not senior enough — reassess whether you've found the real champion or just a friendly contact.

**Problem: "We completed a successful land, but 9 months later expansion is stalled — the champion is satisfied but not advocating for expansion."**
Solution: A satisfied champion who isn't advocating usually means: (a) they don't have visibility into the ROI they've generated, or (b) they don't have a compelling "why expand now" narrative to bring to leadership. Fix (a) by deploying the Executive ROI Review — a 1-page monthly report showing the business impact in CFO-friendly language, automatically sent to the economic buyer and champion. Fix (b) by bringing the "peer expansion story" — a reference customer video or written case study from a similar company that expanded from the same initial module. Champions advocate when they see peers doing it and when they have the ammunition to make the business case. Marketing's job is to build that ammunition and put it in the champion's hands.

**Problem: "Our executive engagement program feels transactional — executives are declining invitations and the CFO-to-CFO emails aren't getting responses."**
Solution: Executive outreach fails when it's clearly synchronized with a sales cycle. Review the messaging: if the executive email references your product, your company in a promotional way, or asks for a meeting within the first touch, it reads as a cold sales email in an executive's clothing. Effective executive engagement is genuine: your CFO shares a non-promotional industry insight, invites them to a peer dinner where our company is not the center of discussion, or makes a warm introduction to another non-competing executive who can solve a separate problem they're facing. The relationship must precede the relevance. If you've only sent product-adjacent content, reset with a 90-day "give-only" phase before making any ask.
