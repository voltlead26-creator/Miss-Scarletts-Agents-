## Overview
Companies revealing their priorities through open job postings are broadcasting buying intent before they ever fill out a form. This engine monitors hiring patterns across your total addressable market, classifies roles as product-specific buying signals, and automatically triggers multi-channel demand generation campaigns — turning LinkedIn job feeds and ATS data into a real-time pipeline engine.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "We detect signals but our outreach feels generic and response rates are low (<5%)"**
Solution: The personalization framework is the difference between signal-based outreach that works and spam. Use Framework A (Growth-Stage Empathy) as your foundation — acknowledge the organizational stage the hiring implies, NOT the specific job posting. A/B test subject lines that reference the pain point vs. subject lines that reference the growth stage. Also audit your timing: emails sent 3-7 days after a job posting appears perform 2x better than same-day emails (the hiring manager is more focused, less overwhelmed by applicants).

**Problem: "Our Hot account list is full of companies that aren't actually good fits"**
Solution: Your signal taxonomy needs calibration. Add ICP filters BEFORE scoring: only run the model against accounts that match your baseline ICP (employee count, funding stage, industry). Then audit 20 recently closed-won deals: what job postings were they running 30-90 days before signing? Build your signal taxonomy backward from those patterns, not forward from intuition.

**Problem: "Job posting data goes stale quickly — a role fills and we're still targeting them"**
Solution: Build "signal decay" into your model. If a Tier 1 posting is more than 45 days old with no new signals, drop the account to the next tier. More importantly: role removals are ALSO signals — if a platform engineering role disappears in under 30 days, they either filled it fast (they're moving fast and need tools NOW) or pulled it (budget freeze — deprioritize). Monitor both additions AND removals.
