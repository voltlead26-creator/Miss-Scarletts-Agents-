## Overview
Designs a complete demand generation program where customer proof assets (case studies, testimonials, review awards, reference customers, ROI documentation) serve as the primary pipeline driver rather than supporting content. Maps your proof library to buyer personas, stages, and segments, then builds full-funnel campaigns that use peer validation as the conversion engine. Use this when you have customer wins but aren't systematically turning them into demand, when your brand-first campaigns underperform, or when buyers in your category rely heavily on peer reviews to make purchase decisions.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Use contact/company properties (industry, segment, deal stage) to trigger proof-matched email workflows automatically. Create a custom property "Proof Asset Deployed" to track which assets touched each contact.
- **Salesforce + 6sense**: Map 6sense intent scores to proof deployment triggers. When an account hits Intent Stage 3+, auto-assign the matched case study to the owning AE and insert proof into their outreach queue.
- **G2 Buyer Intent**: Pipe G2 buyer intent data (accounts viewing your G2 profile) into a dedicated proof-led nurture sequence that shows G2 review highlights and badge image immediately.
- **Gong/Chorus**: Monitor call recordings for proof mentions. Tag calls where reps used proof, track win rate correlation. Feed insights back to PMM for proof gap identification.
- **Notion/Confluence**: Build a centralized "Proof Library" with metadata tags (industry, company size, persona, proof type, metric, recency) to enable instant proof retrieval by SDRs and AEs.
- **Canva/Figma**: Create standardized proof asset templates (quote cards, logo strips, metric callouts) that marketing can update without design resources when new proof arrives.
- **Zapier**: Trigger proof collection workflows (NPS > 8 → auto-send review invitation → auto-add new review to proof library → notify SDR team of new proof available).

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Customers won't give permission to use their name or logo publicly.**
Solution: Create an "anonymous proof" tier — use the company's industry, size, and result without naming them (e.g., "A Series C SaaS company in HR tech achieved 34% improvement in..."). For internal sales use, build a restricted reference database where customers agree to peer calls but not public attribution. Prioritize getting logo permission for 5-10 marquee names as your highest-impact short-term initiative.

**Problem: Case studies are outdated (18+ months old) and buyers dismiss them as irrelevant.**
Solution: Add a "What's Changed Since This Case Study" section to every existing case study — update with current product version, new features used, expanded outcomes. Launch a quarterly "Customer Win Flash" format — a 300-word, 48-hour-to-produce update on a recent win that keeps your proof library fresh without full case study production cycles.

**Problem: Review scores are lower than competitors and you can't use them in campaigns.**
Solution: Shift focus from aggregate rating to review volume velocity and recency. "87 reviews in 6 months" signals momentum even if the rating is 4.3 vs. a competitor's 4.7 with 20 old reviews. Focus campaigns on specific review themes where you win ("ease of implementation," "support quality") rather than overall rating. Run a targeted 30-day review remediation campaign with unhappy customers to resolve issues and invite them to update their reviews.
