## Integration Tips

- **CRM (Salesforce/HubSpot):** Set up a custom object or tag "Champion Move" with fields for old company, new company, tier classification, and sequence status. Trigger this prompt workflow via a Process Builder/Workflow rule when the contact record's company changes.
- **Job Change Data Providers:** Integrate with Warmly, UserGems, or Champify to receive real-time alerts when contacts change jobs. Pipe the structured alert data directly into this prompt as input variables.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Use the "Connections of" and "Past Company" filters to find new hires at target accounts who previously worked at your customer base. Run this prompt for each qualified result.
- **Outreach / Salesloft / Apollo:** Build the 5-touch sequence as a cadence template. Use custom variables for personalization tokens (previous win, outcome, new company signal) pulled from CRM enrichment.
- **Slack:** Create a #champion-moves Slack channel. Automate the AE alert (Deliverable 3A) to post there via Zapier or Make whenever a Tier 1 contact is identified.
- **Sendoso / Reachdesk:** Connect the gifting recommendation to your direct mail platform. Trigger gift sends automatically at Day 7 for all Tier 1 contacts with a verified mailing address.
- **Google Sheets / Notion:** Maintain a Champion Movement Tracker with columns: Contact, Previous Company, New Company, Tier, Sequence Status, Pipeline Created, Close Date. Update weekly.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The contact's sentiment at their previous company was poor (NPS detractor or churned customer).
**Solution:** Do not run this sequence for detractors. Route to a separate "relationship repair" track — acknowledge the past experience first, ask what went wrong, and offer to show what's changed before any pipeline motion. Forcing a standard champion sequence onto a detractor will damage the relationship permanently.

**Problem:** The new company is not in your ICP (wrong size, industry, or no budget authority at new title).
**Solution:** Set a Tier 3 classification and move to a long-term nurture track — one email per quarter with genuinely useful industry content, no ask. Champion relationships are long-term assets. The contact may move again or refer you to colleagues.

**Problem:** The AE who owned the relationship has left your company and there's no one with context on this contact.
**Solution:** Run the account intelligence brief first, then route to the AE owning the new company's territory. Brief them on the relationship history pulled from CRM notes. The outreach should come from the new AE, not a generic marketing alias — relationship capital requires a human sender.
