# AI-Powered B2B Integrated Campaign Brief & Full-Funnel Creative Asset Production Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** demand-gen, b2b, campaign-planning, creative-brief, multi-channel, content-production, pipeline

## Overview
Transform a campaign goal and ICP definition into a board-ready campaign brief plus every creative asset needed to launch across LinkedIn, Google, email, and landing pages — in one AI session. Use this when a campaign must go from zero to fully built in days, not weeks, without waiting on agency roundtrips or lengthy creative reviews.

Campaign theme idea (optional): [e.g., "The hidden cost of manual reporting"]

Produce the following in order:

1. CAMPAIGN BRIEF (1 page): Campaign name, core insight (the "why now"), single messaging theme, three messaging pillars, primary CTA, target audience segment, budget split by channel (%), 90-day success metrics.

3. LINKEDIN ADS: Write 3 sponsored content ads (Headline 70 chars / Intro copy 150 chars / CTA label) and 1 conversation ad opening message (150 words). Each ad targets a different persona from the messaging matrix.

4. GOOGLE SEARCH ADS: Write 2 responsive search ads — 15 headlines (30 chars each) and 4 descriptions (90 chars each) — targeting high-intent competitor and problem-aware keywords. Include 5 keyword suggestions with match type.

7. CAMPAIGN LAUNCH CHECKLIST: 15-item pre-launch QA list an AI agent or coordinator can execute without human judgment calls.

Output all sections clearly labeled. Use specific, concrete language — no marketing fluff.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Use the campaign brief output to create a Campaign in HubSpot. Map each asset (email sequence, landing page, ads) to the campaign for unified attribution. Use Smart Content to deliver persona-specific landing page variations.
- **Salesforce / Pardot:** Import the email sequence into Engagement Studio. Map the "Objection to Pre-empt" field from the messaging matrix to Lead Source and Campaign Influence for accurate multi-touch attribution.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Use the Insight Tag retargeting parameters from the ad package to build matched audiences. Upload the ICP list as a Matched Audience for outreach paired with the conversation ad.
- **Google Ads:** Import RSA headlines into a shared library to reuse top performers across campaigns. Connect Google Ads to your CRM via enhanced conversions to optimize toward SQL rather than form fill.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate lead routing — when a landing page form submits, trigger: (1) CRM record creation, (2) Slack alert to SDR with lead score, (3) enrollment in Day 4 email follow-up, (4) LinkedIn matched audience update.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Store the campaign brief as the single source of truth for the campaign. Link all Figma designs, copy docs, and performance dashboards back to the brief page.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The messaging matrix outputs feel generic — every persona sounds the same.
**Solution:** The AI needs sharper differentiation inputs. Re-run Module 2 with specific customer interview quotes for each persona, a concrete list of objections your sales team hears by role, and 2-3 actual deal loss reasons by persona. Generic inputs produce generic outputs — garbage in, garbage out.

**Problem:** LinkedIn ad CTRs are below 0.3% after the first week.
**Solution:** Isolate the issue: low CTR is almost always a hook problem, not a targeting problem. Run the Module 3 Step 1.1 Hook variants through a fresh AI session using A/B test framing: "Write 10 LinkedIn ad hooks for [persona] using these 5 frameworks: data shock, counterintuitive claim, status threat, cost of inaction, social proof inversion." Test the top 3 hooks against your control.

**Problem:** The email sequence generates opens but no replies.
**Solution:** The body copy is likely too long, too product-focused, or the CTA ask is too big for a cold email. Cold outbound works when it feels like a 1:1 message from a human, not a nurture email from marketing. Re-run Module 5 with this constraint added: "Each email must read like it was written specifically for this one person, must be under 100 words, and must ask for a micro-commitment (read a 2-paragraph story, answer a yes/no question) rather than a meeting."
