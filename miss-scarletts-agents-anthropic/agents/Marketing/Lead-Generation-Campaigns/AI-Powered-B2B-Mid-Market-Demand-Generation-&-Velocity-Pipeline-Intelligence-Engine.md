## Troubleshooting

**Problem: M1 accounts aren't converting to meetings despite engagement signals**
*Solution:* Audit the signal quality — "engagement" may be inflated by competitor research or job seekers visiting your site. Add a negative filter: exclude visits from IP ranges associated with known competitors, staffing agencies, or companies outside your ICP size range. Also audit your SDR messaging — generic "I saw you visited our site" messaging typically produces <2% reply rates. Require SDRs to reference a specific content piece + connect it to a business outcome the company just announced.

**Problem: MQL volume is high but SQL conversion is below 15%**
*Solution:* Your MQL threshold is too loose. Raise the minimum account engagement score to qualify, add a firmographic gate (must meet 3/5 ICP criteria), and require at least one "high-intent" signal (pricing page visit, calculator completion, or demo request) rather than just content consumption. Also add a human review step for borderline accounts before SDR assignment — automated scoring misses context that a 30-second human review catches.

**Problem: Channel spend is high but pipeline attribution to marketing is below 30%**
*Solution:* This is almost always an attribution capture problem, not a marketing performance problem. Audit UTM governance — ensure every ad, email, and event uses consistent UTM parameters. Install attribution tools (HockeyStack, Dreamdata, or Bizible) to capture multi-touch influence, not just first-touch. Educate sales team to log "how did you hear about us?" on every discovery call as a CRM field. Run a 90-day cohort analysis comparing opportunities with vs. without marketing touches in the previous 60 days to calculate true marketing influence.
