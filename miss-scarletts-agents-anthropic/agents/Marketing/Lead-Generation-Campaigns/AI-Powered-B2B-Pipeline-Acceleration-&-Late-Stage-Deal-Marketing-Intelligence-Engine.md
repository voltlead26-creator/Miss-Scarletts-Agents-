## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales team isn't activating marketing plays on their deals**
Solution: Remove friction — build plays directly into CRM so they trigger automatically without AE action. For manual plays (like executive dinner invitations), give AEs a 1-click "Nominate for Acceleration Program" button in Salesforce. Show AEs the win rate data for deals that had marketing plays vs. those that didn't — let the numbers sell the program. Run a weekly 15-minute "Pipeline Marketing Review" with sales managers to review Tier 1 deals together.

**Problem: Personalization is too generic — every email looks templated**
Solution: Build a data enrichment workflow before any email sends. Use Clearbit/Clay/Apollo to append account-specific data: recent news (PR Newswire trigger), tech stack changes (6sense/Bombora), hiring signals (LinkedIn), and financial signals (10-K filings for public companies). Insert 1 hyper-specific fact per email — "I saw Hartwell hired 3 data engineers last month — that signals..." — that removes the template feel entirely.

**Problem: Executive engagement events have low attendance from stalled deal contacts**
Solution: Event positioning is the problem, not the audience. "Executive dinner" reads as "sales trap." Re-position as: "[Your CEO] is hosting a private peer discussion with 12 financial services CISOs on AI data risk — attendance is by referral only." Use scarcity (limited seats), exclusivity (referral-only), and peer-to-peer framing (not vendor-hosted). Have a neutral third party (analyst firm, advisory board member) co-host to remove vendor perception. Follow up by phone, not email, for Tier 1 deals.
