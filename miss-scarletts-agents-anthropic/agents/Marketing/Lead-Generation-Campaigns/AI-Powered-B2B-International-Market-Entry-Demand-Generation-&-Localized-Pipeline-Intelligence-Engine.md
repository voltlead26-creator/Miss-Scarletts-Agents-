# AI-Powered B2B International Market Entry Demand Generation & Localized Pipeline Intelligence Engine - Build Pipeline from Zero in New Geographic Markets

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** international expansion, demand gen, localization, b2b, pipeline, go-to-market, multi-country, market entry, globalization

## Overview
Designs and executes a complete demand generation program for entering a new geographic market — from local channel selection and ICP localization to pipeline targets, first-90-day campaign sequence, and attribution model. Use this when expanding from your home market to a new country or region where you have zero brand presence and zero pipeline.

**MODULE 3: 90-DAY LAUNCH PLAYBOOK**

PHASE 1 — FOUNDATION (Days 1-30): Build Before You Burn
Goal: Set up infrastructure so Day 31 campaigns are not wasted spend.

- Week 1: ICP validation calls. Have local AE conduct 10 discovery calls with prospective customers in the market — NOT to sell, but to validate ICP assumptions, understand local terminology, and identify which pain points resonate. Document verbatim quotes.
- Week 1-2: Local asset audit. Identify which home-market assets can be reused, which need localization, and which need to be created from scratch. Minimum viable asset set: localized homepage, 1 local or globally recognized case study, 1 localized demo, SDR email templates in local tone/voice.
- Week 2-3: Tech stack setup. CRM geo-routing rules for local leads. LinkedIn Campaign Manager setup with local audience targeting. Google Ads with geo-targeting active. UTM taxonomy updated to track local market attribution separately.
- Week 3-4: Outbound list building. Build a 500-1,000 account target list using local data sources (Companies House in UK, Bundesanzeiger in Germany, local LinkedIn Sales Navigator). Enrich with email/phone. Have SDR begin personalized outreach by Day 28.

KPIs for Phase 1: 500+ outbound accounts identified and enriched | All tech stack configured | 10 ICP discovery calls completed | Local asset minimum viable set ready | LinkedIn audience validated (50K+ reachable ICP)

Phase 1 Go/No-Go Criteria: If ICP discovery calls reveal fundamentally different buying criteria or budget unavailability, PAUSE paid activation and return to leadership with updated market assumptions before spending.

PHASE 2 — ACTIVATION (Days 31-60): Generate First Signals
Goal: Launch all core channels and generate first MQLs and first pipeline.

- LinkedIn Ads live: Thought Leader Ads featuring local AE + Sponsored Content using adapted home-market creative + Conversation Ads for first local event invitation. Budget: $[X]/month. Daily monitoring required for first 2 weeks.
- Google Search Ads live: Brand terms + top 3 category/intent terms + competitor terms. Budget: $[X]/month.
- SDR outbound running: 30 personalized outreach sequences per day. Cadence: Email Day 1 → LinkedIn connection Day 3 → Phone Day 5 → Email Day 8 → LinkedIn message Day 12 → Breakup email Day 21. Target: 5 meetings booked per week by Day 60.
- First local event: Attend (or sponsor) one industry event. Pre-book 8-10 meetings via LinkedIn outreach to registered attendees. Capture ALL conversations in CRM within 24 hours.
- Analyst briefing: Brief local Gartner/Forrester analyst. Not to pitch — to educate them on the product category and get feedback on local market positioning.

KPIs for Phase 2: 15+ MQLs | 8+ SQLs | $[X] pipeline created | 10+ SDR meetings booked | 1 analyst briefed | First LinkedIn CPL benchmark established

Phase 2 Go/No-Go Criteria: If CPL is more than 3x home-market CPL AND meeting-to-opportunity rate is below 30%, reassess channel mix before increasing budget.

PHASE 3 — OPTIMIZATION (Days 61-90): Double Down on What Works
Goal: Identify top 2-3 performing channels and shift budget toward them.

- Weekly channel performance review: Track MQL volume, SQL conversion rate, CPL, and pipeline contribution by channel. Cut channels below threshold; increase budget on outperformers.
- First pipeline review: Review all open opportunities with CRO/CSO. Identify the top 3 accounts by close probability and build account-specific pursuit plans.
- Local reference customer identification: Among first 10 customers/prospects engaged, identify 1-2 who might become reference customers for this market. Begin relationship investment now.
- Content gap analysis: Based on actual sales conversations, identify 2-3 content assets that would accelerate deals (local competitor comparison, local regulation/compliance guide, local ROI study). Commission these for production in month 4.

KPIs for Phase 3: $[X]M pipeline created total (cumulative 90 days) | First closed-won deal or advanced negotiation | Top 2 performing channels identified | Local reference customer relationship initiated

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Create a custom "Market" property on Contact and Company records (values: US / UK / DACH / APAC etc.) to track pipeline by geography from Day 1
- Build a UK-specific lead routing workflow: if IP country = UK and lead score ≥ 45, route to UK AE queue immediately
- Create separate email marketing lists by market to prevent cross-market messaging contamination
- Set up campaign attribution by appending market code to UTM parameters (e.g., utm_campaign=uk-legal-q2-linkedin)

**Salesforce:**
- Create a "Region" field on Lead, Contact, and Opportunity objects; build reports filtered by region from Day 1

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CPL in new market is 3-4x home market and campaigns are not converting.**
Solution: This is normal in first 60 days — brand recognition is zero and audiences need multiple touches before engaging. Switch 30% of LinkedIn budget from lead gen forms to awareness content (Thought Leader Ads, video content) to build recognition before conversion campaigns. Run 90-day patience test — CPL should drop 30-50% as your retargeting pool builds and your landing page messaging is optimized for local terminology. If CPL is still >2.5x after 90 days, run creative A/B tests with locally-adapted copy vs. direct home-market creative to identify message-market fit issues.

**Problem: SDR is booking meetings but meeting-to-opportunity conversion rate is below 20% (vs. 40%+ home market).**
Solution: This signals that either (a) the ICP title mapping is off and meetings are with non-buyers, (b) the discovery call script is using home-market terminology that doesn't resonate locally, or (c) the pain points being used in outreach are not acute in this market. Fix: Have local AE shadow 10 SDR calls and diagnose. Rebuild discovery call script with local terminology. Conduct 5 re-qualification calls with warm prospects to test new messaging. If conversion remains low after 30 days of iteration, revisit ICP assumptions with a second round of market research interviews.

**Problem: Local events are generating meetings but not progressing to pipeline.**
Solution: Event-met prospects are often in "learning mode" not "buying mode." Build a post-event nurture track with a 90-day sequence that re-engages them with relevant content 3-4 weeks after the event (when the buzz fades), invites them to a more intimate follow-up event (smaller roundtable = higher engagement), and connects them with a local customer reference who can speak to peer experience. Create a "local market insights" report to send 30 days post-event as a high-value re-engagement asset.
