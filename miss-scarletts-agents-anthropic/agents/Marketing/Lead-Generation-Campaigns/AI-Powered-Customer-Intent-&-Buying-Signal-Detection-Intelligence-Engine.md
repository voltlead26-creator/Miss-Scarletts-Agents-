## Overview

Detects and scores high-intent buying signals across multiple data sources (firmographic changes, behavioral engagement, technographic shifts, market triggers, and conversation intelligence) to identify accounts and prospects ready to buy in the next 30-90 days — enabling real-time sales and marketing activation at critical revenue moments. Use this when you need to accelerate pipeline by targeting buyers at peak intent, reduce sales cycle length by qualifying accounts showing clear buying behaviors, or optimize marketing spend by concentrating resources on accounts with measurable purchase readiness signals.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot:** Create a custom field for Intent Score (0-100 number) and automatic workflow rules that trigger at score 70+ (alert AE), 60+ (add to high-intent nurture), 50+ (flag for marketing). Set these fields to auto-calculate based on rule engine (last site visit, email engagement, recent call, technographic changes).

- **Marketing Automation Platform (Marketo / Pardot):** Build an engagement program that automatically increases email cadence and message relevance when an account hits intent score 65+. Use dynamic content to call out specific signals detected ("We noticed you recently hired a VP of [Role]...").

- **CDPs (Segment, mParticle, Tealium):** Send intent score signals to your data warehouse. Build real-time audiences for accounts 80+, 70+, 60+ and connect these to paid media platforms for audience suppression (don't waste spend on in-conversation accounts) or granular retargeting (different message for each intent tier).

- **Conversation Intelligence (Gong, Chorus, Dialpad):** Automatically surface win theme language in sales calls. Flag when a prospect uses language identical to closed deals in the same vertical. Recommend sales plays based on what worked with similar accounts.

- **Google Analytics 4 / Mixpanel:** Track high-intent accounts separately (segment by account ID). Measure time-to-conversion from first signal to demo request vs. from demo request to closed deal. Use cohort analysis to identify which signal combinations correlate with fastest closes.

- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator / Apollo:** Build intent-based lists: accounts with 70+ score + your target persona + no current connection. Use these for targeted sequence outreach. Reference the specific intent signals in personalization.

- **Outreach / Salesloft:** Create cadences that automatically adapt based on intent score. 80+ accounts get 5-email aggressive sequence over 10 days. 50-60 accounts get longer nurture. Under 50 accounts get minimal outreach (they'll come to you).

- **Business Intelligence (Looker, Tableau):** Build an intent detection dashboard: accounts by intent score quartile, trending signals by vertical, false positive analysis (high-score non-deals), signal performance over time. Share weekly with sales leadership.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Intent scores are too high — too many false positives (accounts scoring 80+ but never closing)**

Solution: Recalibrate your signal weights based on historical win data. Run an analysis: of all accounts that closed in the past 12 months, what was their average intent score at the time they scheduled a demo? You've likely over-weighted a signal (like website visits) that looks like intent but predicts nothing. Reduce the weight of shallow engagement signals (single site visit) and increase weight of explicit intent (demo scheduled, proposal sent, buying committee identified).

**Problem: Sales team ignores the intent scores because they don't trust them**

Solution: Score the last 20 closed deals retroactively. Show what their intent score was 30, 60, and 90 days before close. Publish this as a proof point: "These 20 deals averaged a score of 72 sixty days before they closed. Here are 15 active accounts with scores of 70+." Build credibility first, then ask for adoption. Make the score easy to see in their daily workflow (email summary, CRM widget, Slack notification).

**Problem: Your company doesn't have conversation intelligence or technographic data — signals are thin**

Solution: Start with behavioral and firmographic signals only. Score based on: Website engagement (site visits, pages visited, time spent) + Email engagement (open rate, click rate) + Organizational signals (funding, new hires, news mentions) + Sales conversation stage. This 4-factor model is predictive even without the advanced data sources. As you get richer data sources, fold them in. You don't need perfect data to beat random outreach.
