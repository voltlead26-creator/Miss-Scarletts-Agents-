## Troubleshooting

**Common Issues:**

- **Problem:** AI voice agent is triggering "Is this a robot?" objections at a rate above 30% of conversations, causing early hang-ups before qualification begins.
  **Solution:** Audit the opening 8 seconds for unnatural pacing, robotic intonation patterns, or overly formal language. Rewrite the opening hook to use contractions, shorter sentences, and colloquial phrasing ("Hey {FIRST_NAME}" instead of "Hello {FIRST_NAME}, this is..."). Configure the voice AI platform's prosody settings to add micro-pauses (0.3–0.5 seconds) after every third word cluster — this mimics human thinking rhythm. Add the AI disclosure earlier (within the first 5 seconds) using confident, matter-of-fact language: "I should let you know upfront — I'm an AI assistant for Mosaic. But I'm calling because..." — transparency paradoxically increases trust and conversation length.

- **Problem:** Meeting show rates are below 55%, meaning the AI is booking calls that prospects don't treat as real commitments.
  **Solution:** The booking close is too passive — the AI is accepting calendar acceptance without extracting a verbal commitment. Revise the meeting close script to require the prospect to repeat back the time: "So we're set for Thursday at 2pm your time — does that work?" Then add a pre-meeting confirmation sequence: (1) immediate SMS with calendar invite, (2) day-before email with a personalized "here's what we'll cover" agenda, (3) 1-hour-before SMS reminder with AE name and a preview insight. Each touchpoint should reference something specific from the call to signal that a human reviewed the conversation.

- **Problem:** MEDDIC qualification completion rate is below 2 dimensions per call, meaning AEs are receiving low-quality meetings with no context.
  **Solution:** The qualification questions are too complex or are being asked too early before rapport is established. Move MEDDIC discovery to after the value articulation sequence — let the AI plant a pain mirror first ("A lot of CFOs we talk to say their close is still 3+ weeks even after their ERP implementation...") and wait for the prospect to respond with affirmation or frustration. Qualification questions land better when the prospect has already emotionally engaged with the pain. Also reduce each MEDDIC question to a single, conversational ask — "What's the biggest drag on your close process right now?" is more likely to yield a MEDDIC-valid response than "Can you describe the financial metrics you're trying to improve and the associated cost of not addressing them?"
