## Overview
Automates the entire RFP (Request for Proposal), RFI (Request for Information), and RFQ (Request for Quotation) response process — from scoring and triage to final submission-ready documents — using an AI agent that analyzes evaluation criteria, matches company capabilities, writes scored-section responses, and assembles a compliant, win-themed proposal. Use this when your team spends 20-80+ hours per RFP and needs to respond to 5 or more RFPs per quarter without sacrificing response quality.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI response sounds generic and could apply to any vendor, not specifically to our company.**
Solution: You haven't provided specific enough company context. Go back to the Advanced Version and fill in the COMPANY PROFILE section with exact metrics (not ranges), real customer names (redact to initials if needed), and specific feature names from your product — not marketing category language. The more specific the input, the more differentiated the output. Run the prompt again with at least 3 quantified customer outcomes.

**Problem: The response doesn't address the prospect's specific industry language or terminology.**
Solution: Add the prospect's job titles, KPIs, and industry jargon explicitly to the PROSPECT INTELLIGENCE section. For medical devices: mention FDA, MDR, CAPA, 510(k). For financial services: mention AML, KYC, Basel III. Paste 2-3 paragraphs from the RFP preamble verbatim — the AI will mirror their language back to them. If you have access to the prospect's annual report or 10-K, paste their "Business Challenges" section.

**Problem: We have genuine gaps in meeting 2-3 requirements and the AI tries to paper over them.**
Solution: This is intentional behavior you should override by adding "CONSTRAINT: Flag all gaps honestly; do not paper over weaknesses" to the prompt. For real gaps, the AI should recommend one of four strategies: (1) a compliant workaround using your ecosystem, (2) a partner who covers the gap, (3) a roadmap commitment with a timeline, or (4) a frank no-bid recommendation. Submitting a dishonest response damages trust even when you win.
