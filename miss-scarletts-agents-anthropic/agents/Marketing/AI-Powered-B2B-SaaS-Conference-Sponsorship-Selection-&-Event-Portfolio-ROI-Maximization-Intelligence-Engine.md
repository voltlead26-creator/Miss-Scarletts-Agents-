## Overview

Use this before committing event budget at the start of each fiscal year or quarter.

**PART 4: AT-EVENT PIPELINE CAPTURE PLAYBOOK**

**4.1 — Pre-Event Account Intelligence Briefing**

Two weeks before each Anchor/Core event, run an AI-powered account intelligence briefing covering:
- List of registered attendees matched against your CRM (existing contacts, open opportunities, target accounts)
- 6sense or intent data overlay: which registered accounts are showing buying signals in the last 30 days

- Priority meeting hitlist: top 30 accounts ranked by: (ICP fit score × intent score × deal stage × relationship warmth)
- Briefing format: one-page account brief per top-30 account, delivered to AEs/BDRs attending the event

**4.2 — Booth Experience Design**

For Anchor events, the booth should not be a product demo station. It should be a curated problem-diagnosis experience:

- Entry hook: A visual provocation (e.g., "82% of enterprise contract disputes begin with a clause your team never flagged — what's yours?") — provokes curiosity, not product interest
- Engagement mechanic: 90-second AI-powered assessment (on iPad/kiosk): "How mature is your contract intelligence operation?" — generates a personalized score and benchmark report
- Result: Attendee receives immediate value (their score + peer benchmarks), you capture full contact info + self-declared pain points
- Follow-up hook: "Your full benchmark report with recommendations will be in your inbox within 2 hours" — creates anticipation and open rates of 70%+
- Meeting conversion: Booth conversation ends with "Based on what you told me, I think our team could show you something specific to [their stated pain] in 20 minutes — are you free Thursday 2pm?"

**4.3 — Executive Meeting Program (Anchor Events Only)**

Run a parallel executive meeting track with your CEO/VP Sales/senior AEs:

- Target: C-level and VP-level contacts at top 20–30 accounts
- Format: Private "strategic conversation" (not a demo) — 30-minute peer discussion framed as insight-sharing
- Location: Quiet booth meeting room or nearby hotel suite (book early; suites at major conferences sell out 3 months in advance)
- Agenda: Open with a research insight or trend observation, ask 3 discovery questions, share 1 relevant customer story, identify next step
- Goal: Uncover a new champion, advance a stuck deal, or disqualify accounts wasting sales time

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce + Splash**: Set up event campaign hierarchy before the event — one parent campaign (event name) with child campaigns (booth scan, hosted dinner, speaking session) for granular attribution. Every lead must have campaign source populated at point of capture.
- **6sense or Bombora**: Pull intent data report 3 weeks before each event — filter for accounts showing "cloud security" or "CSPM" topic spikes who are also registered attendees; route to AEs as "hot" pre-event targets.
- **Chili Piper**: Embed a meeting booking link in all pre-event email outreach ("Book a 20-minute meeting at our booth — choose a time that works for you"). Converts 3–5× better than "reply to schedule."
- **Gong**: Tag all post-event discovery calls with event source; enables correlation analysis between event touchpoints and deal velocity.
- **Google Sheets / Notion Event Budget Tracker**: Maintain a running all-in cost tracker that includes not just sponsorship fees but booth shipping, AV, travel, hotel, per diem, swag, and activation costs — the true cost of events is 2.2–2.8× the sponsorship fee.
- **HubSpot Marketing Events**: Use the native HubSpot Events object to track attendance, engagement, and pipeline influence without custom workarounds if not using Splash.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Event produces high lead volume but low pipeline conversion (many badge scans, few qualified meetings, almost no sourced deals).

**Solution**: The event has an audience quality problem — your ICP is not actually concentrated there. Apply the ICP match scoring rubric retroactively: what % of your scanned leads matched your exact ICP? If <35%, the event is a branding expense, not a pipeline engine. Either (a) negotiate a downgrade to Satellite tier with a hosted dinner format targeting the 20–30 actual ICP attendees, or (b) drop the event entirely. Do not optimize your booth or follow-up sequence until the audience quality problem is solved.

---

**Problem**: Sponsorship packages keep selling out before you commit, forcing you into undersized packages that limit pipeline outcomes.

**Solution**: Build a 12-month event commitment calendar in Q4 of the prior year. Rank your top 3 Anchor events and commit by October/November for the following calendar year. Most Tier 1 conferences sell Platinum/Gold packages 9–12 months out. Create a "first look" relationship with 2–3 event organizers by agreeing to a multi-year deal — this gives you early access to inventory before it's available to the market. The cost premium of committing early is typically 0%; the cost of being shut out of the right tier is $200K+ in lost pipeline.

---

**Problem**: Your CFO refuses to approve the event budget because "events are hard to measure."

**Solution**: Present the event ROI model before the fiscal year begins, not after. Show the CFO: (1) last year's events by tier, with cost-per-meeting and cost-per-pipeline-dollar for each; (2) proposed this-year portfolio with projected ROI based on prior performance; (3) the specific "kill criteria" — if Event X doesn't hit $Y pipeline by date Z, it gets dropped. Pre-agreeing on kill criteria converts event investment from a faith-based decision to a performance contract. CFOs approve performance contracts.
