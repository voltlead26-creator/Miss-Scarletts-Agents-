## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The pipeline architecture model shows we can't hit the ARR target even with perfect execution.**
Solution: This is the most valuable output — it means your target is set unrealistically given current conversion rates and capacity. Bring this math to the CRO before committing to the target, not after missing it. Use the model to negotiate either a lower target, additional headcount/budget, or an extended timeline. The prompt just saved you a quarter of wasted effort.

**Problem: The AI-generated Sales-Marketing Alignment Contract creates commitments Marketing can't actually deliver.**
Solution: Run the advanced version with your actual capacity data in the "Marketing Team Capacity" section completed in full. If the model generates $9M pipeline commitments but you have $250K budget and 3 marketers, the output will be calibrated to reality. If you skipped that section, go back and fill it in — the contract is only valid if grounded in actual resource constraints.

**Problem: Sales leadership refuses to sign the SLA commitments in the alignment contract.**
Solution: Ask the AI to produce a "Sales SLA Impact Analysis" — paste in your MQL data from last quarter alongside the current average follow-up time, and ask it to calculate the pipeline revenue leak caused by slow MQL follow-up. Quantifying the revenue impact of SLA gaps (e.g., "48-hour follow-up time is costing us ~$400K in pipeline per quarter") is typically more persuasive than asking for a process change on principle.
