## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The executive summary sounds generic and could apply to any vendor.
**Solution:** The Challenger reframe requires a specific commercial insight — an industry trend, a regulatory change, or a benchmark the buyer hasn't seen. Add to your prompt: "Include a specific statistic from [industry] in 2025 that reframes why the status quo is more expensive than the prospect realizes." If you don't have one, run a web search for "[industry] AR/[relevant metric] benchmark 2025" and paste the result into context.

**Problem:** The proposal is too long and the buyer won't read it.
**Solution:** Add the constraint: "This prospect's evaluation committee has 8 members; most will spend under 7 minutes on the proposal. Generate a 1-page 'Proposal Brief' version first, then the full document. The brief should cover: outcome summary, 3 proof points, pricing headline, and next step only." Use the brief as the email body; attach the full proposal.

**Problem:** We have a genuine gap in the RFP requirements (a feature we don't have).
**Solution:** Do not omit the gap — evaluators penalize non-responses more than honest gaps. Add to the prompt: "We do not currently support [requirement]. Generate a response that: (a) acknowledges the gap honestly, (b) proposes a partner integration or manual workaround that achieves 80% of the outcome, (c) includes a roadmap commitment with a realistic date, and (d) reframes why our approach to this requirement is actually lower risk than a native feature that's under-tested."
