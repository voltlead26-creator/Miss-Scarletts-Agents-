## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High enrollment but low active member rate (members enroll and never engage again)**
This is the most common loyalty program failure mode — points are earned but never redeemed, and no emotional connection forms. Diagnosis: (1) Is your minimum redemption threshold too high? If customers need 500+ points and your AOV earns them 78 points per order, they need 7 purchases before redeeming — they'll forget the program exists. Lower threshold to require 2-3 purchases to reach. (2) Are your non-purchase earn actions working? If members can only earn via purchase, engagement between orders disappears. Add review and social earn actions and actively promote them in your post-purchase sequence. (3) Are you sending enough loyalty-specific communications? Most brands only email about the program at enrollment and never again. Members need at least 1 loyalty-focused touchpoint per month (points summary, tier progress, double-point event).

**Problem: Loyalty program is costing too much (redemption rate eroding margin)**
If your redemption rate is >50%, you have a structural problem — either your earn rate is too high, your point value is too generous, or you're attracting deal-seekers not loyal customers. Fix: (1) Audit which customer segments are redeeming most — are they otherwise high-LTV customers or one-time buyers who joined for the discount? Segment and analyze. (2) Shift from discount-only redemptions to experience-based rewards (early access, exclusive products, free shipping) — these have lower financial cost but equal or higher perceived value. (3) Introduce a minimum purchase requirement to redeem (e.g., must spend $40+ to apply points) to ensure redemptions correlate with new revenue.

**Problem: Top-tier VIP customers feel the benefits aren't worth maintaining status**
VIP programs fail when the benefits are just "more of the same" — more points, bigger discounts. Fix: (1) Interview your top 20 VIP customers and ask directly: "What would make you choose us over any competitor, regardless of price?" Their answers will reveal non-discount benefits they actually value. (2) Add genuine access and recognition benefits — founder calls, product co-creation, event invitations, personal "account manager" email from a named team member. (3) Make VIP status visible externally — branded packaging for VIP orders, exclusive colorways, early access that's 48-72 hours before standard early access (not just 24 hours earlier than public).
