SECTION 3: PROGRAM DESIGN DELIVERABLES

Deliver a complete B2B Google Search Ads demand capture architecture:

A. INTENT-MAPPED KEYWORD ARCHITECTURE

Stage 1 — Problem-Aware Keywords (Highest Funnel):
- Definition: Searcher knows they have a pain but doesn't know the solution category yet
- Examples: "how to reduce invoice processing time," "manual accounts payable problems," "why are our vendor payments late"
- Bidding posture: Lower bids, content-led landing pages, micro-conversion optimization (content download, newsletter)
- Estimated conversion rate: 0.5–2% (these are research queries, not buying queries)
- Role in pipeline: Captures future demand; builds remarketing pools for RLSA
- Match type: Broad Match with smart bidding + aggressive negative keyword list to prevent bleed

Stage 2 — Solution-Aware Keywords (Mid-Funnel):
- Definition: Searcher knows the solution category, is evaluating options
- Examples: "accounts payable automation software," "best AP automation tools," "AP automation for manufacturing"
- Bidding posture: Core budget concentration; demo request as primary conversion goal
- Estimated conversion rate: 2–6% for demo requests with strong landing pages
- Role in pipeline: Primary demand capture layer — highest ROI non-brand keywords
- Match type: Phrase Match + Broad Match (with smart bidding Target CPA or Maximize Conversions)

Stage 3 — Vendor-Aware Keywords (Bottom Funnel):
- Definition: Searcher is actively evaluating specific vendors including you and your competitors
- Examples: "[Your Brand] pricing," "[Your Brand] demo," "[Your Brand] vs [Competitor]," "[Competitor] alternative"
- Bidding posture: Maximum aggression; this is the most valuable search traffic in existence
- Sub-category: Brand terms (must win at >90% impression share), Competitor terms (strategic bidding)
- Estimated conversion rate: 8–25% for brand terms, 3–8% for competitor terms
- Match type: Exact Match for brand terms to prevent cost inflation; Broad/Phrase for competitor conquest

B. CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE

CAMPAIGN 1: Brand Defense
- Goal: Own 90%+ of branded search impression share at the lowest possible CPC
- Campaign type: Search
- Keywords: [Brand name], [Brand name] + product modifiers (pricing, demo, login, review, features, alternatives, vs.)
- Match types: Exact + Phrase (never Broad Match on brand — you'll pay for your own name on irrelevant queries)
- Bid strategy: Maximize Conversions or Target Impression Share (95%+) — never let competitors own your brand SERP
- Negative keywords: Add all competitor brand names as negatives to keep this campaign focused
- Landing page: Homepage or brand-specific landing page with demo CTA
- Budget: 15–20% of total SEM budget (brand CPCs are cheap; protect impression share fully)
- KPI: Impression share (target 90%+), brand CPC (should be $3–$15 for most B2B SaaS), brand CTR (35–60%)

CAMPAIGN 2: Non-Brand Demand Capture (Solution-Aware)
- Goal: Intercept in-market buyers researching your solution category before they pick a vendor
- Campaign type: Search
- Keywords: [Solution category] + software/platform/tool/automation + industry modifiers + size modifiers
- Match types: Phrase Match (core terms) + Broad Match with smart bidding (expansion terms)
- Bid strategy: Target CPA set to 150% of current blended CPA (data gathering phase); transition to Target ROAS once 50+ conversions/month are logged
- Landing page: Solution-specific landing page with demo request form, social proof, ROI calculator link
- Budget: 40–50% of total SEM budget
- KPI: Cost-per-demo-request, demo-to-MQL rate, impression share vs. competitors

CAMPAIGN 3: Competitor Conquesting
- Goal: Intercept buyers actively researching your direct competitors; position as the credible alternative
- Campaign type: Search
- Keywords: [Competitor brand], [Competitor] pricing, [Competitor] alternatives, [Competitor] vs, [Competitor] reviews
- Ad copy rules: Never mention competitor by name in ad copy (Google policy + legal risk); use positioning language ("Purpose-built for [ICP]," "No [key competitor weakness]," "Switch in 30 days")
- Landing page: Dedicated competitor comparison page (/vs/[competitor]) with a specific "switching offer" CTA
- Bid strategy: Manual CPC or Enhanced CPC — do NOT use smart bidding on competitor terms until you have 30+ conversions/month from these campaigns (insufficient data for algorithms)
- Budget: 15–25% of total SEM budget
- CPCs: Expect 2–4x higher CPCs on competitor terms than your own non-brand terms — this is normal
- KPI: Cost-per-demo from competitor campaigns (expect 30–50% higher than non-brand campaigns — the intent is still worth it)

CAMPAIGN 4: RLSA — Remarketing Search Lists
- Goal: Bid up or bid differently on previous website visitors who are back searching your category
- Audience segments to create:
  * All website visitors (last 30 days)
  * Pricing page visitors (last 14 days) — highest intent; bid +100–200% above base
  * Demo page visitors who did NOT convert (last 7 days) — add tailored ad copy with urgency/offer
  * Blog/content readers (last 60 days) — lower intent; moderate bid increase (+30–50%)
  * Converted visitors (demo booked) — EXCLUDE from all campaigns to stop wasting spend
- Layering strategy: Add RLSA audiences as "Observation" to all campaigns first (no bid change) to gather data; after 30 days, apply bid adjustments based on observed conversion rate differences
- Bid adjustment logic: If remarketing audience converts at 3x the cold audience rate, apply +200% bid adjustment
- KPI: Conversion rate lift from RLSA vs. non-RLSA traffic; incremental demo volume; ROAS improvement

CAMPAIGN 5: Performance Max (Supplemental Only)
- Role clarification: Performance Max should SUPPLEMENT — not replace — Search campaigns for B2B
- Use case: Remarketing to warm audiences across Google's network (Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps) while Search campaigns handle intent capture
- Asset groups: Create separate asset groups for each audience type (existing customers excluded, competitor prospects, blog readers)
- Audience signals: Upload CRM contact lists as audience signals; let PMax learn from your best converters
- Exclusion lists: Exclude branded search terms from PMax (protect your Brand campaign's impression share)
- Budget: 10–15% of total SEM budget maximum until Search campaigns are fully optimized
- Monitoring: Check "Insights" tab weekly for PMax search term categories — intervene if PMax is cannibalizing brand or wasting spend on irrelevant categories

D. SMART BIDDING CALIBRATION FOR B2B LONG-CYCLE DEALS

The Core Problem with Default Smart Bidding for B2B:
Google's smart bidding algorithms are trained on conversion speed — they optimize toward conversions that happen within 30 days of a click. B2B SaaS deals that close in 90–180 days get "devalued" by the algorithm because it can't see the downstream revenue. This creates a systematic bias: smart bidding will favor cheap, quick-converting clicks (e.g., early-stage content downloads) over expensive, slow-converting demo requests that actually drive pipeline.

SOLUTION — Three-Layer Conversion Architecture:

Layer 1 — Micro-Conversions (Training signal only — do NOT include in primary bidding):
- Content downloads, newsletter signups, pricing page visits
- Role: Provides behavioral signal to Google — which audience segments engage
- Setup in Google Ads: Track as conversions but set "Include in conversions" = OFF so they don't dilute smart bidding targets
- Value: $0 (no direct revenue signal)

Layer 2 — Macro-Conversions (Primary smart bidding signal):
- Demo requests, contact form submissions, free trial signups
- Role: Primary conversion event that smart bidding optimizes toward
- Setup: "Include in conversions" = ON
- Value: Set conversion value equal to [ACV × demo-to-close rate] — e.g., if ACV = $48K and close rate from demo = 20%, set conversion value = $9,600
- Bid strategy recommendation: Maximize Conversion Value (once 30+ conversions/month); Target ROAS (once 50+ conversions/month and baseline ROAS established)

Layer 3 — Offline Conversion Import (Smart bidding's revenue north star):
- Push Salesforce opportunity stage data back to Google Ads via the Google Ads offline conversion import API
- Events to import: Opportunity Created, Opportunity Qualified (MEDDIC Stage 3+), Opportunity Won
- Lag: Import with 7-day delay to match click → opportunity creation timeline
- Value: Assign dollar values matching actual opportunity size (not ACV — actual opportunity amount from Salesforce)
- Impact: Retrains smart bidding algorithms on actual revenue signals, not just form submissions; shifts budget to keywords and audiences that generate real revenue, not just demo requests

Smart Bidding Ramp Protocol:
- Month 1: Maximize Conversions (no target) — let Google gather learning data without constraint
- Month 2: Set Target CPA at 130% of observed Month 1 CPA — give the algorithm room while guiding it
- Month 3: Tighten Target CPA to 110% of Month 2 CPA — continue constraining toward efficiency
- Month 4+: Transition to Target ROAS once offline conversion import is live and providing revenue signal (requires 50+ conversions/month)
- Rule: NEVER change smart bidding targets more than 15% in a single adjustment — large swings throw the algorithm into a re-learning period (status: "Limited — Learning") and performance degrades for 1–2 weeks

E. PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION FRAMEWORK

UTM Taxonomy for Google Search:
- utm_source=google
- utm_medium=cpc
- utm_campaign=[campaign-type]-[campaign-name] (e.g., "search-brand-brand-defense" or "search-nonbrand-ap-automation-us")
- utm_content=[ad-group-name] (e.g., "ap-automation-manufacturing" or "competitor-tipalti")
- utm_term={keyword} — use dynamic keyword insertion to pass exact match search term to UTM

CRM Integration for Pipeline Attribution:
- Capture utm_source, utm_medium, utm_campaign, utm_term in hidden form fields on all landing pages
- Write these values to Salesforce Lead fields: "Lead Source," "Lead Source Detail," "Paid Keyword," "Paid Campaign"
- When lead converts to Opportunity, carry forward all UTM fields to the Opportunity record
- Create a Salesforce report: "Google Ads Sourced Opportunities" filtered by Lead Source = "Google Ads" with Campaign/Keyword breakdown
- Pipeline calculation: Sum of Opportunity Amount for all opps where Lead Source = Google Ads AND Close Date is within reporting period

Offline Conversion Import Setup (Google Ads → Salesforce → Google):
- Step 1: Capture Google Click ID (GCLID) in a hidden form field on demo request page
- Step 2: Store GCLID on Lead/Contact record in Salesforce
- Step 3: When Salesforce opportunity reaches Stage 3+ (SQL/Qualified), export GCLID + timestamp + opportunity value to CSV
- Step 4: Upload CSV to Google Ads (Tools → Conversions → Uploads) on weekly cadence
- Step 5: Google Ads uses this data to retrain smart bidding algorithms on actual revenue events, not just form submissions

Weekly B2B SEM Dashboard KPIs:
| Metric | Definition | Target/Alert Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Impression Share (Non-Brand) | % of available impressions captured | >40%; alert if <25% (budget or bid issue) |
| Brand Impression Share | % of branded search impressions owned | >85%; alert if <75% (competitor bidding on brand) |
| CTR by Campaign | Click-through rate per campaign | Brand: >35%; Non-Brand: >4%; Competitor: >3% |
| Avg. CPC by Campaign | Average cost per click | Monitor trend; alert on >20% week-over-week spike |
| Cost-Per-Demo (Macro Conversion) | Total spend / demo requests | Set target based on deal economics; alert on >150% of target |
| Demo Volume by Campaign | Demos attributed per campaign | Track weekly; alert if drops >20% week-over-week |
| RLSA Conversion Rate Lift | Remarketing vs. cold audience CVR | Target: RLSA converts at 2–4x cold; if <1.5x, audiences need refinement |
| Search Term Quality Score | % of spend on relevant intent queries | Target: <10% of spend on negative keyword candidates; weekly search term audit |

F. 30/60/90-DAY OPTIMIZATION ROADMAP

Month 1 — Foundational Cleanup:
Week 1: Full search term audit — export 90 days of search query data; categorize every term as "Keep," "Exclude," or "New Ad Group"
Week 2: Negative keyword build — create a master negative keyword list covering: jobs/careers terms, student/educational intent, DIY/free intent, competitor names in wrong campaigns, irrelevant industry modifiers
Week 3: Match type audit — ensure brand terms are Exact Match only; convert excessive Broad Match to Phrase Match for non-brand core terms
Week 4: Landing page alignment — each campaign should have a dedicated landing page with message match; eliminate sending all clicks to homepage
KPI target: Reduce irrelevant query spend by 30%; improve CTR by 15% through tighter ad group/keyword/ad message match

Month 2 — Smart Bidding Transition:
Week 5–6: Consolidate ad groups (Google's smart bidding works better with more conversion data per ad group — fewer, larger ad groups outperform many small ones)
Week 7: Activate RLSA audiences in Observation mode across all campaigns
Week 8: Begin smart bidding transition — switch from Enhanced CPC to Maximize Conversions (with no target) on highest-volume non-brand campaigns first
KPI target: Maintain demo volume within 15% of Manual CPC baseline while laying foundation for optimization phase

Month 3 — Scaling & Attribution:
Week 9–10: Activate RLSA bid adjustments based on 30 days of observed Observation data
Week 11: Launch offline conversion import (GCLID → Salesforce → Google Ads upload)
Week 12: Expand into Competitor campaign (if not already active) with dedicated comparison landing pages
KPI target: Cost-per-demo trending down 15–25% vs. Month 1 baseline; offline conversion import active and providing >20 imported conversions/month

## Success Metrics

- **Non-brand impression share:** Should reach 35–50% within 60 days of optimization; below 25% signals either budget constraint or Quality Score issues requiring ad/landing page relevance fixes
- **Cost-per-demo trend:** Should improve 20–30% from Month 1 to Month 3 as smart bidding learns from conversion data; if flat or worsening after Month 2, audit search term quality and landing page conversion rate first
- **Demo-to-MQL rate from Google Search:** B2B Search leads should convert to MQL at 45–65% (higher than LinkedIn or display, lower than direct traffic); if below 35%, search intent is off — too many informational queries converting
- **Brand impression share:** Must stay above 85% — any drop signals a competitor has begun aggressively bidding on your brand and requires immediate bid response
- **RLSA conversion rate lift:** Remarketing search audiences should convert at 2–4x cold audience rates; if the lift is less than 1.5x, audiences are too broad (all-site visitors vs. high-intent page visitors)
- **Offline conversion import coverage:** Within 90 days of implementation, Google Ads should be receiving at least 30 offline conversion events/month — below this threshold, smart bidding doesn't have sufficient signal to optimize toward revenue
- **Pipeline ROI:** Google Search-sourced pipeline should deliver 8–15x return on ad spend when measured at the opportunity level in CRM; if ROAS (pipeline) is below 5x, review search term quality and demo-to-opportunity conversion process

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce:** Use the GCLID capture → hidden form field → Salesforce Lead field approach to connect Google clicks to CRM opportunities; build a Salesforce report "Google Ads Attribution" with filters for Lead Source = "Google / CPC" segmented by campaign name (mapped from utm_campaign hidden field); schedule this report to your paid search manager weekly
- **HubSpot:** Enable Google Ads integration in HubSpot (Marketing → Ads → Google Ads); HubSpot natively captures UTM parameters on contact records and allows you to create contact lists by campaign for offline conversion audiences; use HubSpot's "Ads Attribution" report to see multi-touch pipeline influence across Google campaigns
- **Marketo:** Use Marketo's Google Ads integration to sync offline conversions automatically — when a Marketo lead reaches "MQL" status, Marketo can push the conversion back to Google Ads without manual CSV uploads; dramatically simplifies offline conversion import workflow for teams already using Marketo
- **Google Looker Studio:** Build a B2B SEM pipeline dashboard connecting Google Ads data (via Google Ads connector) with Salesforce data (via Supermetrics or Salesforce connector); key metrics: Google Ads spend by campaign → demos generated → opportunities created → pipeline value → revenue closed; enables true ROI reporting from click to close
- **Unbounce / Webflow:** Use landing page tools with native Google Ads integration for dynamic keyword insertion (DKI) in landing page headlines — if searcher types "AP automation for manufacturing," the landing page headline dynamically displays "AP Automation for Manufacturing Teams" — increases Quality Score and conversion rate simultaneously
- **6sense / Demandbase:** Layer intent data on top of RLSA — when accounts from your target account list are showing intent signals in 6sense, increase Google Ads bids for those accounts via Customer Match (upload the account's email domain contacts to Google Customer Match) for a coordinated search + ABM surround effect

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Smart bidding enters "Learning — Limited" status and performance drops for weeks**
Solution: The algorithm needs a minimum of 30–50 macro-conversions per month to exit learning mode reliably. If you have fewer conversions, either (a) add micro-conversions (pricing page visit, scroll depth) to supplement the signal — set them as "Include in Conversions" temporarily to boost conversion volume — or (b) consolidate ad groups so conversion volume concentrates rather than spreading across many small ad groups. Never make large bid target changes (>20%) or significant keyword changes during the learning period — they restart the learning clock.

**Problem: High CTR but poor demo quality — sales team complains leads aren't ICP**
Solution: The search terms converting are likely too broad or too early-funnel. Run a 30-day search term audit filtered to "converted queries only" — if you see informational intent terms (how-to, what is, best practices) converting at high rates, your landing page is generating false positives. Fix by tightening the landing page CTA (make the demo offer more specific and higher-friction, e.g., require industry and company size selection), or by adding informational-intent terms to a separate campaign with a content offer rather than a demo CTA. Also check your RLSA audiences — sometimes remarketing audiences are too broad (all-site visitors instead of high-intent page visitors).

**Problem: Competitor campaigns have very high CPCs and poor conversion rates**
Solution: Competitor keyword CPCs are inherently inflated because the competitor bids on their own brand, and Google's Quality Score algorithm penalizes ads with low relevance (your ad for "[Competitor]" has a lower Quality Score than their own brand ad). Accept that competitor CPCs will be 2–4x non-brand CPCs — the economics only work if your landing page converts at a high enough rate. If cost-per-demo from competitor campaigns exceeds 2x your non-brand CPD, invest in the comparison landing page first — a weak "/vs/[competitor]" page with no strong differentiation will never make competitor campaigns profitable, regardless of bidding strategy.
