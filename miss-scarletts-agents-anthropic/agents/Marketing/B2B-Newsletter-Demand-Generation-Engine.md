## Integration Tips

**Beehiiv / ConvertKit / Mailchimp:**
- Use custom fields to tag subscribers by acquisition source (LinkedIn, paid, referral, organic search) — enables attribution analysis in HubSpot/Salesforce
- Set up automation rules: if subscriber clicks product CTA 2+ times → sync to CRM with tag "newsletter-warm-lead"
- Enable referral program (Beehiiv Boosts or SparkLoop) to create subscriber-refers-subscriber loops

**HubSpot:**
- Create a custom "Newsletter Source" contact property with values: organic, LinkedIn, paid, co-promotion, word-of-mouth
- Build a Newsletter Influence report: filter deals where associated contact has newsletter source property set AND created date after first issue send
- Set lifecycle stage to "Marketing Qualified Lead" when contact property "newsletter_clicks_last_30_days" ≥ 3

**Salesforce:**
- Create Campaign object for each newsletter issue; log email engagement as Campaign Member activity
- Use Campaign Influence model to attribute pipeline to newsletter campaigns
- Trigger a Salesforce task for SDR outreach when lead score crosses threshold from newsletter engagement

**Zapier / Make:**
- Zapier: Beehiiv new subscriber → HubSpot contact create → Slack #marketing notification
- Zapier: HubSpot contact "newsletter warm lead" tag added → create Salesforce lead → assign to SDR sequence
- Make: Weekly: Pull Beehiiv analytics API → populate Google Sheets dashboard → send summary email to team

**Notion:**
- Use Notion as issue planning database: properties for issue number, publish date, content pillar, primary CTA, A/B test running, UTM parameters
- Link each issue to its performance metrics via a Notion rollup formula

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Open rates below 25% after 60 days**
Fix: Audit your subscriber acquisition channels — if most subscribers came from paid ads or gated content, they have low intent. Prioritize organic LinkedIn and referral acquisition. Run a re-engagement sequence ("Should we keep sending you Build Secure?") with a hard unsubscribe for non-responders. Shrink list, improve rates. Also: test subject line formulas — specificity and numbers outperform clever wordplay for B2B audiences.

**Problem: High open rates but zero demo conversions from newsletter**
Fix: Your offer ladder is broken. Check: (1) Is your primary CTA visible above the fold on mobile? (2) Is the CTA a direct demo/trial link or a vague "learn more"? (3) Are you sending a trigger email within 24 hours when a subscriber clicks a product CTA? Most newsletters bury the conversion ask. Add a "This week's sponsor is us" section explicitly pitching the product once per month, framed as a reader benefit, not a sales pitch.

**Problem: List growth stalled at under 500 subscribers after 90 days**
Fix: Your subscriber acquisition system is the constraint, not your content. Implement: (1) LinkedIn post that pitches the newsletter directly 2x/week (not content excerpts — direct subscribe CTAs), (2) A subscribe CTA in every piece of content you publish anywhere, (3) One newsletter partnership/swap with a complementary newsletter targeting the same ICP (DM 10 newsletter operators this week). Content quality doesn't grow lists — distribution does.
