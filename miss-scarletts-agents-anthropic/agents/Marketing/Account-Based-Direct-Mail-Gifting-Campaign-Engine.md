**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** abm, direct-mail, gifting, outbound, pipeline-acceleration, b2b, personalization, sendoso, alyce, enterprise-sales

## Overview
Designs end-to-end account-based direct mail and gifting campaigns — from gift selection and personalized copy to delivery timing, follow-up sequencing, and ROI measurement. Use it when digital outreach has saturated your target accounts and you need a high-response physical touch to break through, accelerate stalled pipeline, or re-engage ghost accounts.

## Success Metrics

- **Response rate within 14 days of delivery**: Track replies across email, LinkedIn, and phone — Tier 1 campaigns should deliver 20-35% response rates versus a 2-5% cold email baseline
- **Meeting booked rate**: The primary conversion goal — target 15-25% of gifted accounts booking a first call within 30 days
- **Pipeline influenced rate**: Percentage of gifted accounts that enter or advance in your CRM pipeline within 90 days — target 30-40% for Tier 1
- **Cost per meeting booked**: Total campaign cost (gifts + fulfillment + platform fees + AE time) divided by meetings booked — should be significantly below your standard CPM from paid channels
- **Delivery confirmation rate**: 90%+ using platform tracking; flag undelivered packages immediately for re-routing or digital pivot
- **Follow-up email open rates**: Delivery-day follow-up emails should achieve 60-80% open rates versus the 25-35% cold email average — the package creates anticipation and recognition
- **Sentiment in replies**: Track reply sentiment qualitatively — positive or curious replies indicate gift and messaging resonance; negative or irritated replies signal a calibration issue with gift selection or timing

## Integration Tips

**Sendoso**: Use Sendoso's eGift and physical gift flows to automate delivery tracking. Set up a Sendoso "touch" that fires when a Salesforce opportunity is tagged with the campaign — triggers shipping, tracks delivery status, and logs the touch in CRM automatically. Use Sendoso's Address Confirmation feature for home delivery without needing to ask for the address directly.

**Alyce**: Use Alyce's AI gift selection to let the recipient choose their own gift within a curated category — ideal for Tier 2 and Tier 3 accounts where the "right gift" is harder to predict. Alyce's choice model also removes compliance risk because the recipient selects what's appropriate for them.

**HubSpot / Salesforce**: Create a custom campaign property called "Direct Mail Touch" with a timestamp. Build a smart list or report showing all contacts who received a gift and track their pipeline progression at 30/60/90-day intervals. Set up automated task creation in CRM the day after expected delivery to prompt the AE to execute the follow-up sequence.

**Zapier / Make**: Build an automation: when a CRM opportunity is tagged "Tier 1 — Gift Campaign," trigger a Sendoso gift order via API, log the send in CRM, and enroll the contact in the follow-up email sequence in HubSpot or Outreach. This removes manual steps and ensures consistent execution across the sales team.

**Outreach.io / Salesloft**: Build a dedicated "Post-Gift" sequence with the 5-touch email cadence from this prompt. Trigger the sequence enrollment via Zapier when Sendoso confirms package delivery. Use the sequence's "manual task" feature to prompt AEs for LinkedIn touches and phone calls at the specified days rather than embedding them in the automated sequence.

**Clay**: Enrich the target account list with home office indicators (LinkedIn remote status), verified office addresses, and persona-level interest data before sending gifts. A gift sent to an empty office or a wrong address wastes budget and damages credibility.

**Google Sheets + Loom**: After the campaign, have each AE record a 2-minute Loom video summarizing gift delivery outcomes, reply sentiment, and what they'd change. Consolidate in Sheets as a campaign retro. Use the data to update gift selection and note templates for the next cohort.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low delivery confirmation rates — packages being returned or lost.**
Fix: Do not ship to office addresses without confirming the recipient works on-site. Use the Sendoso Address Confirmation workflow, which sends the recipient a low-friction link to enter their preferred shipping address as a "gift is on its way" notification — this dramatically increases successful delivery and actually builds anticipation before the package arrives. For international accounts, confirm customs restrictions before selecting physical goods; default to Sendoso eGift options for non-US recipients.

**Problem: Gifts are being received but generating no replies — the campaign is landing in silence.**
Fix: Audit the note copy first. If the note contains any product pitch, feature reference, or CTA with a deadline, it will be perceived as a sales tactic and will be ignored. The note must read like a human gesture from a peer, not a marketing asset. Second, check gift relevance — a branded backpack sent to a C-suite executive will be perceived as generic swag and discarded. Run the gift-persona matching through the Advanced Version to verify alignment. Third, audit timing — gifts sent during earnings seasons, company-wide off-sites, or holiday travel windows often get delayed or opened in contexts where follow-up is impossible.

**Problem: A recipient responded negatively — they found the gift intrusive or inappropriate.**
Fix: Immediately have the AE send a genuine apology and remove the contact from all future gifting campaigns permanently. Do not attempt to recover the relationship with another gift. Flag the account in your gifting platform's suppression list. Review your gift selection and note copy to identify what triggered the response — most commonly it is either too-high gift value (feels like a bribe), a gift that implies surveillance ("we noticed you're interested in..."), or a tone-deaf selection for the recipient's culture or industry. Brief the full sales team on the finding to prevent recurrence.
