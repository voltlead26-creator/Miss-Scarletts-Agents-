## Troubleshooting

**Champion blocks access to other stakeholders ("I'll handle the internal process"):**
This is the most common multi-threading failure. It means your champion either lacks confidence in their internal selling ability, fears losing control of the deal, or doesn't trust you enough yet. Resolution: Reframe your ask — "I don't want to go around you, I want to make you look like a hero internally. Can we do a 10-minute prep call so you're completely armed for the exec conversation?" Then send them the internal business case draft and ask for their edits. Once they feel ownership, they'll open doors.

**Economic buyer agrees to a meeting but doesn't show up or sends a proxy:**
This indicates low urgency or low perceived value at the exec level. The champion hasn't successfully sold internally. Resolution: Before the meeting, send a one-page pre-read framed as "what your peer CFOs are asking about [category]" — not a product sheet. On reschedule, shorten the ask: "15 minutes, no slides, just your 3 biggest questions about [business outcome]." If they send a proxy, treat the proxy as the new champion and start the process again — they clearly have influence.

**Multi-threading attempts are flagged by champion as "going around them":**
This happens when outreach to other stakeholders is too product-forward or looks like a sales move. Resolution: Pivot every outreach to be a content-led, value-first touch, not a meeting request. Send the CFO a "thought leadership" resource relevant to their industry, not a demo invite. This builds ambient awareness within the account without triggering political friction. Update champion proactively: "I sent [CFO Name] that fintech benchmark report we discussed — keeping him informed."
