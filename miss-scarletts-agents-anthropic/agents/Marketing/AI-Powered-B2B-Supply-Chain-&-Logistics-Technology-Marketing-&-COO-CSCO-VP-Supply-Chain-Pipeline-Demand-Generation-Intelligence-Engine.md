## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** COO and CSCO personas aren't responding to outbound sequences even with trigger-event personalization.
**Solution:** Supply chain executives — especially at manufacturers — rely heavily on peer referrals and analyst/consultant relationships, not vendor cold outreach. Rebalance strategy to: (1) identify the internal supply chain planning champion (Director of S&OP, VP Planning) as the entry point rather than going directly to COO — they have the pain acuity and will pull the COO in once convinced; (2) invest in Gartner and CSCMP event presence where COOs are actively seeking peer benchmarking; (3) use executive roundtable invitations (peer learning, not vendor pitches) as the primary COO engagement hook, as these outperform cold demo requests 8:1 for this persona. COOs at $100M-$500M manufacturers often don't have dedicated supply chain staff and rely on their ERP vendor — create content that specifically addresses "what SAP/Oracle doesn't solve for demand planning" to intercept during ERP renewal conversations.

**Problem:** Generated messaging sounds too similar to existing SCM platform competitors (Blue Yonder, Kinaxis, o9).
**Solution:** The market differentiation gaps that resonate with mid-market COOs vs. enterprise SCM platforms are: implementation speed (18-month enterprise SCM implementations vs. 6-8 week mid-market deployments), total cost of ownership (enterprise license + SI fees vs. SaaS self-serve), and ERP-native integration depth (mid-market companies can't afford a 9-month integration project). Feed specific competitive displacement scenarios into the Advanced prompt — "We're competing against companies continuing to use SAP IBP + Excel" performs better than positioning against Blue Yonder at mid-market. Also input 3-5 actual Gong call transcripts from won deals to extract the exact language prospects used when they decided to buy.

**Problem:** The 90-day plan is too broad — there are too many supply chain verticals (manufacturing, distribution, retail, CPG) to execute effectively.
**Solution:** Re-run Deliverable 3 with a single vertical constraint: "We have 2 marketers and $30K/month. Focus exclusively on discrete manufacturers $100M-$500M revenue running SAP ECC or S/4HANA. Remove all other segments." The engine will produce a tightly focused plan — typically: one trigger-event outbound sequence (SAP migration), one vertical-specific benchmark report, LinkedIn ads targeting manufacturing COOs, and a single executive roundtable hosted at a regional manufacturing cluster. Vertical concentration consistently outperforms broad supply chain messaging by 2-3x on pipeline conversion for resource-constrained teams.
