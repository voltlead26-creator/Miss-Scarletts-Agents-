Deconstructs your ARR waterfall into its component movements — new business, expansion, contraction, and churn — then attributes each dollar to its originating go-to-market motion, including marketing-sourced, sales-sourced, partner-sourced, and product-led. Use this quarterly (or monthly for high-velocity businesses) to produce a board-ready marketing contribution narrative, identify which ARR levers marketing controls, and build the defensible financial case that earns CMOs a seat at the revenue table.

- **Attribution Coverage Rate:** >80% of closed ARR has documented marketing touchpoint data in CRM; less than 80% signals a data infrastructure gap that systematically undercounts marketing's contribution
- **Marketing Revenue Multiplier:** ARR contribution ÷ marketing spend should be $3–8x for B2B SaaS (varies by ACV and segment: enterprise = lower multiplier, SMB/PLG = higher); track QoQ trajectory, not just absolute value

- **Gainsight:** Pull Health Score at time of churn for each account. Join to ARR data to build the "churn by health score + marketing engagement cohort" analysis. Export the Health Score timeline + marketing engagement overlay to reveal leading indicators.
- **ChartMogul / Baremetrics / Stripe:** For PLG companies, use ChartMogul's MRR movements API to pull the waterfall components automatically. Map plan upgrades (expansion ARR) to marketing campaign records by matching upgrade timestamp to email click or in-app campaign conversion timestamp (±7 days).
- **Google Sheets / Notion:** Build a quarterly ARR Bridge template in Google Sheets with pre-built formulas for marketing multiplier, dark churn %, and attribution coverage rate. Update it monthly with data from CRM exports. Embed the Notion-linked sheet in the CMO's board prep document.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CRM data shows inconsistent GTM source — "Marketing" source is massively undercounted because sales reps delete or overwrite the original lead source when they work the deal.**
Solution: Implement a CRM governance policy where Original Lead Source is a locked, non-editable field set at the moment the lead record is created (use a workflow/validation rule in Salesforce or HubSpot to prevent overwrite). If the field is already corrupted historically, run a data remediation project: join closed-won opportunities to Campaign Member records to reconstruct marketing touchpoints retroactively. For a quick fix, add a "Marketing Influenced" boolean field that marketing ops controls independently of the source field.

**Problem: Finance doesn't trust marketing's ARR contribution number because the methodology is opaque.**
Solution: Document the attribution methodology in a one-page "Attribution Methodology Card" — define exactly what counts as "marketing-sourced" (e.g., "Any closed deal where the originating lead record was created by a marketing program before the first SDR outreach attempt"), what counts as "marketing-influenced" (e.g., "Any closed deal where the contact had 3+ tracked marketing interactions within 180 days of close date"), and what's explicitly excluded. Have the RevOps leader co-sign the methodology. Present it to Finance before the board meeting — surprise attribution claims in a board deck destroy credibility.

**Problem: Expansion ARR attribution to customer marketing is difficult to prove because CS and Product both claim they drove the expansion.**
Solution: Use event sequencing — don't claim expansion attribution if there was no marketing touchpoint. Instead, analyze the 60-day pre-expansion window and document which touchpoints occurred. Build a shared attribution model with CS: marketing gets credit for touchpoints it generated (email engagement, event attendance, content consumption); CS gets credit for QBRs and relationship management; product gets credit for in-app triggers. Use a shared 100-point allocation model (e.g., Marketing: 30pts | CS: 50pts | Product: 20pts) that's agreed upon cross-functionally before the board review.
