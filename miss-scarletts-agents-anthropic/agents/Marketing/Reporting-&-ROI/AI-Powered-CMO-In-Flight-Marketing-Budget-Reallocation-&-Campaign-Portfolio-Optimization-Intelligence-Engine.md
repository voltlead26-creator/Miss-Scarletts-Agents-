## Overview

Builds a real-time marketing budget reallocation system that analyzes in-flight campaign performance across all channels, identifies diminishing returns and efficiency gaps, and produces a defensible reallocation recommendation — including how much to shift, where to move it, and the projected pipeline impact of each decision. Use when quarterly performance data shows significant variance across channels and you need to make mid-flight budget decisions before the quarter ends, when pipeline coverage is under target and you need to optimize remaining spend for maximum pipeline generation, or when the CFO demands a data-driven rationale for why you're moving budget out of an underperforming channel.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** "My channel CPL data is inconsistent — Google Ads reports one CPL, Salesforce shows a different number, and HubSpot shows a third."
**Solution:** This is a lead source attribution conflict, not a CPL problem. Before running any reallocation analysis, align on ONE definition: CPL = marketing spend attributed to that channel ÷ MQLs that have that channel as the primary Lead Source in Salesforce. Lock the Lead Source field as the single source of truth for channel attribution. Rebuild channel spend reports in Salesforce using Opportunity Source (not Lead Source) for pipeline attribution — they will differ, and you need both.

**Problem:** "The 'recommended' channels (email, content) don't have the capacity to absorb more budget — email list is largely exhausted and content production takes 6 weeks."
**Solution:** Distinguish between channels that are investment-constrained (email nurture capacity, content production runway) vs. channels that are pure-auction channels where budget immediately converts to spend (Google Ads, LinkedIn Ads). For investment-constrained channels, shift budget to the most efficient remaining auction channel (typically Google Search — brand terms, which have near-zero saturation risk). For content, consider investing in content distribution and promotion rather than net-new production — spend $20K amplifying your top-performing existing content via paid social and paid newsletter sponsorships rather than waiting 6 weeks for new content.

**Problem:** "Events are the biggest budget line and we've already committed to them — the cost is sunk. The reallocation analysis says events are underperforming but we can't get the money back."
**Solution:** Separate committed-and-sunk costs from committed-but-not-yet-spent costs. For sunk events, run the analysis only on uncommitted and remaining-spend-eligible budget. For the upcoming event in week 9: calculate the "pipeline conversion window" — if the event is in week 9 of 13 and your average MQL-to-pipeline time is 52 days, no deal generated at that event will close this quarter. Therefore, the event's value should be measured against Q4 pipeline contribution, not Q3 coverage. If Q4 pipeline is already adequately covered, the upcoming event spend may legitimately be an opportunity cost that justifies reallocation to faster-converting channels.
