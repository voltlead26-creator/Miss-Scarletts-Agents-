# Marketing ROI Reporting Automation - Board-Ready Revenue Attribution Reports

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** roi, reporting, attribution, b2b, strategy, analytics, board-reporting

## Overview
Transforms raw marketing data into board-ready ROI reports that directly connect marketing spend to pipeline and revenue. Use this when you need to justify budget, prove marketing's revenue contribution, or build a recurring reporting cadence for executive stakeholders.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version
You are a Chief Marketing Officer presenting to the board of directors. Using the data below, generate a complete marketing ROI report that:

1. Calculates blended CAC, LTV:CAC ratio, and payback period by channel
2. Shows pipeline contribution (marketing-sourced vs. marketing-influenced)
3. Identifies the top 3 highest-ROI channels and explains why they outperform
4. Flags the 2 worst-performing channels with a recommended action (cut, test, or restructure)
5. Projects next quarter's revenue contribution if current budget is maintained vs. 10% increase vs. 10% cut

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Pull channel-level deal data via Reports > Revenue Attribution; export as CSV and paste directly into the Advanced Version's input fields
- **Salesforce**: Use Salesforce Reports to pull "Opportunity Source" filtered by close date; match to marketing campaign spend in your marketing automation platform
- **Google Sheets**: Build a running input template with formulas that auto-calculate ROMI, CAC, and LTV:CAC as you paste in new data each quarter; then feed the populated sheet to this prompt
- **Looker / Metabase**: Schedule this prompt as a monthly automation by connecting your BI tool's exported data via a Zapier or Make scenario
- **Notion**: Store board report outputs in a Notion database tagged by quarter; link each report to the strategy doc it informed to build an institutional memory trail
- **Slack**: Use the Executive Summary bullets as a weekly #marketing-metrics post to keep cross-functional stakeholders aligned between board meetings

## Troubleshooting

- **Problem:** Attribution data is incomplete — some deals have no source recorded.
  **Solution:** Add this instruction to the prompt: "Flag deals with missing attribution as 'Unknown Source' and note what percentage of total revenue this represents. Treat this as a data quality risk and include a one-line fix recommendation." This prevents the model from misattributing revenue and surfaces a real operational problem.

- **Problem:** The revenue figures in the output don't match what's in the CRM.
  **Solution:** Specify your attribution model explicitly in the prompt input. First-touch vs. last-touch vs. multi-touch produce dramatically different channel revenue numbers. Ask the model to note which model was used and show the delta if the model were switched.

- **Problem:** The board pushes back that "marketing can't take credit for revenue that sales closed."
  **Solution:** Add a "Marketing-Sourced vs. Marketing-Influenced" split and have the model define each category clearly. Frame marketing-influenced as pipeline acceleration — deals that moved faster because of marketing touchpoints — rather than ownership. This framing typically lands well with sales-oriented boards.
