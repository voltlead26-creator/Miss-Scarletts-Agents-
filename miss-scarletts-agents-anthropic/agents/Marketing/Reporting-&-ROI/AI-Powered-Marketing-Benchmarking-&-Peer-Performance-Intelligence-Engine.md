## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce CRM**: Pull pipeline coverage, MQL volume, MQL-to-SQL conversion, and CAC data directly from CRM dashboards. Build a saved report called "Marketing Benchmark Inputs" that exports monthly with all required metrics pre-formatted
- **Google Analytics 4 / Looker Studio**: Build a GA4 custom report with website visitor volume, lead conversion rate, and channel attribution data. Schedule monthly export to a Google Sheet used as benchmark engine input
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Export monthly CPL and CTR data by campaign, aggregate by audience type, and calculate blended LinkedIn CPL for the engine input
- **Gong / Chorus (Conversation Intelligence)**: Pull marketing-influenced pipeline by querying deals where marketing touchpoints appear in the deal history — often 15-25% higher than CRM attribution alone
- **Notion/Confluence**: Build a "Marketing Benchmark Tracker" page with a recurring monthly table. Paste benchmark scorecard output each month to track percentile trend over time
- **Board Deck (Google Slides / PowerPoint)**: The benchmark scorecard table, executive summary bullets, and board narrative sections are designed to copy directly into board deck appendix slides — no reformatting needed
- **OpenView SaaS Benchmarks, Keybanc Private SaaS Survey**: Cross-reference AI output with these annual reports (published Q1-Q2 each year) to validate benchmark ranges used by the engine

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The engine generates benchmark ranges that seem too wide or don't match my industry
**Solution:** Add maximum specificity to your company context — especially sub-vertical (not just "SaaS" but "B2B SaaS security for financial services"), exact ACV, and GTM motion. Benchmarks for a $5K ACV PLG company differ massively from a $120K ACV enterprise company. If ranges still seem off, specify: "Use only benchmarks from companies with ACV between $X and $Y targeting [segment]."

**Problem:** My data is incomplete — I don't have all the metrics requested
**Solution:** Fill in what you have and add "UNKNOWN — need to instrument" for gaps. The engine will still produce a useful partial scorecard AND generate a measurement gap analysis showing which missing metrics matter most for the board and exactly how to start tracking them within 30 days.

**Problem:** The board narrative sounds too generic or doesn't fit my situation
**Solution:** After the initial run, paste the narrative back and add: "Revise this narrative given that [specific circumstance — e.g., 'we just lost our top two enterprise reps' or 'we over-indexed on paid media last quarter and underperformed']. The board already knows about X. Reframe to acknowledge it while emphasizing forward momentum."
