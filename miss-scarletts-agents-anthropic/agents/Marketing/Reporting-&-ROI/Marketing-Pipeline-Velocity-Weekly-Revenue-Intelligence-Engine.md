# Marketing Pipeline Velocity & Weekly Revenue Intelligence Engine - Automated CMO Operating Rhythm

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 15 min | **Tags:** b2b, strategy, reporting, pipeline, revenue-intelligence, automation

## Overview
Automate the weekly pipeline velocity brief every revenue-focused CMO sends to their executive team — turning raw CRM and marketing data into an AI-generated operating rhythm report with anomaly flags, week-over-week channel shifts, and a 3-scenario revenue forecast. Use this every Monday morning before the revenue leadership standup.

1. **Executive Pulse** (3 bullets max): What's the headline story this week — pipeline health, acceleration, or concern?
2. **Pipeline Velocity Score**: Calculate velocity = (# of deals × avg deal size × win rate) ÷ avg sales cycle days. Flag if velocity is up, down, or flat vs. last week and explain why.
3. **Channel Intelligence**: Which channels over/underperformed vs. 4-week average? What's the 1 channel shift that deserves attention?
4. **Anomaly Flags** (red/yellow/green): Flag the 2-3 metrics furthest from trend with root cause hypothesis.

6. **Revenue Forecast — 3 Scenarios**: Based on current pipeline and velocity trends, forecast end-of-quarter closed pipeline in Base / Upside / Risk scenarios with key assumptions.

Tone: Direct, numbers-first, no jargon. Assume the CEO will read this in 4 minutes while drinking coffee.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The 3-scenario forecast feels too wide — executives don't trust ranges that span $3M+.**
Solution: Narrow the scenario spread by adding more specific assumptions. Include: "Assume win rate stays within ±3% of historical average" and "Exclude any deal >$500K from Base Case — model these separately as binary outcomes." The more specific your inputs on stalled deal stages and close probability, the tighter the model becomes. Provide stage-specific historical close rates rather than blended win rate.

**Problem: Anomaly flags are triggering every week because the 4-week average is too noisy.**
Solution: Extend the baseline to an 8-week rolling average for more stable anomaly detection. Also add seasonality context: "Week [X] historically runs [X]% above/below average due to [fiscal quarter end / trade show season / summer slowdown]." Prompt the AI to apply seasonal adjustments before flagging anomalies.

**Problem: Action items are too generic — they come out as "review email performance" instead of specific tactics.**
Solution: Add this instruction to the prompt: "Each action item must name: the specific tactic (not category), the exact audience or asset being changed, the measurement method, and the person responsible by job title. If you cannot be specific without more data, flag what data is needed to specify the action." The more granular your channel inputs, the more specific the action items.
