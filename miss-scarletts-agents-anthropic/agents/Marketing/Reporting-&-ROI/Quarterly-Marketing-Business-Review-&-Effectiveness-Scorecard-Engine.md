# Quarterly Marketing Business Review & Effectiveness Scorecard Engine - AI-Powered CMO QBR Intelligence System

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** b2b, strategy, reporting, qbr, effectiveness, scorecard, cmo, saas, automation, finance

## Overview
Transform a quarter's worth of marketing data into a comprehensive, board-ready Quarterly Business Review in under 20 minutes. This engine produces the full CMO QBR package: performance vs. targets, channel efficiency rankings, program ROI analysis, team capacity review, and a data-backed Q+1 strategic plan — eliminating the 8–12 hours most CMOs spend manually assembling this document each quarter.

### SECTION 3: QUARTER NARRATIVE

**What Went Well:** Brand momentum accelerated significantly in Q4 — branded search volume grew 11% QoQ with zero incremental investment, indicating that the "Revenue Intelligence Maturity Model" content campaign from Q3 is compounding. Partner referrals hit a 4-quarter high at $1.55M in influenced pipeline with a $2,742 cost-per-SQL — our most efficient channel for the second consecutive quarter. The PLG self-serve trial pilot converted 14 paid accounts from trial in its first full quarter, contributing $186K ARR at a $3,100 CAC versus the company's $91K blended CAC — early signal that PLG could meaningfully reduce acquisition costs at scale.

**What Didn't Work:** The $440K events investment — primarily Dreamforce sponsorship ($210K) and two regional field events ($230K) — generated only $1.9M in influenced pipeline and 41 SQLs at a $10,732 cost-per-SQL, more than 3x our most efficient channels. Root causes are threefold: (1) the Dreamforce booth was in a low-traffic hall location negotiated late, reducing ICP traffic by approximately 60% vs. model; (2) only 34% of field event leads were followed up by SDRs within 48 hours due to the October ramp gap; (3) we lacked a direct mail/gifting follow-up sequence to re-engage event contacts, leading to a 68% lead decay rate. The outbound SDR team also underdelivered in October (-40% of monthly target) as two reps ramped simultaneously without adequate support coverage.

**Single Biggest Learning:** Partner referrals and content compounding are our most capital-efficient growth levers by a factor of 2.5x, yet they represent only 14% of our program budget. We have consistently under-invested in these channels relative to events and paid media. Q1 2026 will test a deliberate rebalancing: -$150K from events, +$80K to partner co-marketing, +$70K to content production, with a direct target of improving blended Marketing Efficiency Ratio from 6.4x to 8.0x by Q2.

## Success Metrics

- QBR document completed and distributed to executive team within 48 hours of quarter close
- Scorecard accurately reflects all tracked KPIs with RAG status that aligns with what leadership already intuitively knows (sanity check)
- At least 1 program is killed or pivoted based on the bottom programs analysis — not just acknowledged
- Budget reallocation recommendation is specific enough that Finance can action it without follow-up questions
- Channel efficiency ranking produces at least 1 non-obvious insight the CMO hadn't previously articulated
- Q+1 priorities have explicit success metrics — not "improve" or "grow" but "increase MQL→SQL from 28% to 31% by Week 8"
- CEO or board references a specific insight from the QBR document in post-meeting follow-ups

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Build a "QBR Data Collection" dashboard with date-filtered custom reports for: pipeline created by original source (quarterly), MQL/SQL funnel by source, contact to customer conversion rates, and deal velocity by lead source. Schedule this to auto-export as a PDF or CSV every last Friday of the quarter. Paste directly into the Advanced prompt's Section B inputs.
- **Salesforce**: Create a "Marketing QBR" report folder with 6 saved reports: (1) Pipeline created by campaign source (quarter filter), (2) Opportunities created by lead source, (3) Closed-won by campaign first touch, (4) Deal stage velocity averages, (5) MQL-to-SQL funnel report, (6) Channel cost data from Campaign Spend tracking. Use Salesforce's "Export to Excel" and consolidate into a single paste for the Advanced prompt.
- **Google Analytics 4 / Looker Studio**: Build a "QBR Web Performance" dashboard with: quarterly organic sessions, branded vs. non-branded search split (via Search Console integration), demo/trial page conversion rates by traffic source, and pricing page visit trends. Share as a live Looker Studio link with the CMO and marketing ops team.
- **6sense / Bombora / Demandbase**: Export the quarterly "In-Market Accounts" trend report and "Intent Spike Accounts" list. Add these counts to Section B as leading indicator context — rising intent volume 6–8 weeks before pipeline growth is a leading indicator the QBR can use to contextualize demand patterns.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Pull the "Win/Loss by Marketing Source" report to validate whether marketing-sourced deals have different competitive win rates, objection patterns, or sales cycle lengths. This data is gold for Section 3's narrative on what deals marketing sent were actually closeable.
- **Google Sheets / Notion**: Create a quarterly "QBR Template" with pre-labeled data input cells for every field in the Advanced prompt. Share with the marketing ops analyst who fills it in during the final week of the quarter. The CMO then pastes the completed template into the prompt and receives the full document within minutes of quarter close.
- **Slack / Teams**: Automate distribution — once the QBR document is generated and approved, use a Zapier flow to post a summary "QBR Highlights" message to #executive-team and #marketing-all channels with the 3 key takeaways and a link to the full Notion page. Schedule for Monday morning of the first week of the new quarter.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The channel efficiency ranking produces results that conflict with what the CMO "knows" about channel performance — the table doesn't match intuition.**
Solution: Verify that attribution logic is consistent. The most common source of conflict: cost figures include different cost bases (some channels include staff time, others only media spend). Standardize all channel costs to include: media spend + agency fees + pro-rated staff time + content/creative production costs allocated to that channel. Also confirm the pipeline attribution model — first-touch vs. last-touch vs. multi-touch will produce radically different pipeline-per-dollar figures by channel. Specify the attribution model explicitly in the prompt inputs and add: "Note that all pipeline figures use [first-touch / multi-touch / position-based] attribution."

**Problem: The Q+1 priorities come out as vague strategic themes rather than specific, actionable initiatives.**
Solution: Add the following constraint to the prompt: "Each Q+1 priority must be specific enough that a new marketing hire could understand exactly what to do in their first week without asking for clarification. Every priority must include: (a) the specific programs we're running, not just the category; (b) the exact budget line being shifted; (c) the numerical target with a measurement date; (d) the name of the team member responsible." The more granular the Q[X] data inputs, the more specific the AI can make the Q+1 plan.

**Problem: The "What Didn't Work" section is too generic — it doesn't identify real root causes.**
Solution: Provide more specific failure data. For each underperforming program, include: (a) the original hypothesis for why it would work, (b) the specific metrics that showed it wasn't working (and when you first saw the signal), (c) any mid-quarter adjustments you tried and their results. The richer the failure data, the more specific the root cause analysis. Also add this instruction: "For each underperforming program, generate 3 possible root causes ranked by likelihood, with evidence for each from the data provided. Do not accept 'market conditions' as a root cause without data supporting it."
