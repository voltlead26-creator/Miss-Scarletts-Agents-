# AI-Powered CMO Weekly Strategic Intelligence Brief & Revenue Signal Synthesis Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 15 min | **Tags:** b2b, enterprise, strategy, reporting, executive-communication, automation, revenue-intelligence

## Overview
Synthesize signals from across your entire marketing ecosystem—pipeline health, competitive moves, campaign anomalies, market trends, and team performance—into a concise, decision-ready weekly intelligence brief for the CMO. Use this when you need to stay ahead of revenue risks, competitive threats, and strategic opportunities without drowning in dashboards.

**GREEN LIGHTS — DOUBLE DOWN**
1-2 bullets. What's working better than expected and deserves more budget, attention, or amplification this week.

**COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE SUMMARY**
2-3 bullets. Competitive moves and what they mean for our positioning, pipeline, or messaging. Include a recommended response for each.

1-2 bullets. Intent spikes, search trends, or market events that represent a short-lived demand generation opportunity to act on now.

2-3 bullets. Upcoming milestones, risks on the horizon, and pipeline events the CMO should have in their calendar.

Tone: Direct, honest, no spin. Assume the CMO has 5 minutes and needs to make decisions, not read a report.

OBJECTIVE: Generate a structured CMO Weekly Intelligence Brief with the following sections:

---

SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE PULSE — THE WEEK IN 30 SECONDS
Three sentences maximum. State: (1) where we are vs. pipeline target and what that means, (2) the single biggest risk or opportunity this week, (3) the CMO's most important decision this week. No data — pure synthesis.

SECTION 2: PIPELINE HEALTH SCORECARD
Present as a table:
| Metric | This Week | 4-Week Avg | vs. Target | Status |
For metrics: New pipeline generated | Pipeline QTD vs. target | Deal slip risk | MQL volume | SQL conversion rate | Lead response time | Pipeline velocity ($/day)
Status = 🟢 On Track / 🟡 At Risk / 🔴 Off Track
After table: 2-3 sentences interpreting the pattern, not just the numbers. What story does the funnel tell this week?

SECTION 3: CAMPAIGN PERFORMANCE ANOMALIES — ACT NOW
For each anomaly detected (threshold: >15% deviation from 4-week average):
- Channel/Campaign: [name]
- Signal: [what changed and by how much]
- Hypothesis: [most likely cause in 1 sentence]
- Action: [specific recommendation — who does what by when]
- Revenue at risk or opportunity: [$X] pipeline impact if not addressed / if capitalized

SECTION 4: COMPETITIVE INTELLIGENCE BRIEF
Format each signal as: SIGNAL → IMPLICATION → RESPONSE
Prioritize by: (1) immediate pipeline threat, (2) messaging/positioning impact, (3) medium-term strategic concern
End with: "This week's competitive posture recommendation" — one sentence on whether to attack, defend, or hold in messaging.

SECTION 5: DEMAND OPPORTUNITY WINDOW
Identify 1-2 time-sensitive demand opportunities based on intent data, search trends, or market news.
For each: What's the signal? What's the window (how long before it closes)? What campaign or tactic can capitalize on it? Who needs to act and when?
These are opportunities that will not exist next week — flag urgency clearly.

SECTION 6: OPERATIONAL RED FLAGS
Only include items that will affect pipeline or revenue if not resolved within 5 business days.
For each: Issue → Pipeline/Revenue impact → Owner → Resolution deadline

SECTION 7: THE CMO DECISION LIST — THIS WEEK
3-5 decisions ranked by urgency and revenue impact.
Format for each:
- DECISION: [Specific choice to be made]
- CONTEXT: [2 sentences of supporting data]
- OPTIONS: [Option A — Option B (or Option A — do nothing)]
- RECOMMENDATION: [What the data suggests]
- DEADLINE: [When this decision must be made to avoid pipeline impact]
- STAKE: [$X pipeline or revenue impact]

SECTION 8: NEXT 2 WEEKS — FORWARD VISIBILITY
Timeline view of: upcoming campaign launches, key events, budget decision points, competitive announcements expected, forecast calls, and board/exec reviews requiring marketing input.
Highlight: anything that requires the CMO's attention in advance (approvals, budget decisions, stakeholder alignment).

---

TONE REQUIREMENTS:
- No marketing jargon — write for a revenue-focused executive
- Lead with implications, not descriptions: "This means X" not "X happened"
- Every data point earns its place — if it doesn't drive a decision, cut it
- Be honest about risks — a CMO who sees problems early can fix them
- Calibrate urgency accurately — not everything is a "red flag"

OUTPUT: Plain text formatted for Slack/Notion/email distribution. Use headers, bullet points, and tables. Total brief should be readable in under 7 minutes.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Build a weekly "CMO Intelligence Report" dashboard in your CRM with saved reports for: new pipeline by source (week), deal slip risk (opportunities with no activity >7 days), MQL volume, and SQL conversion rate. Schedule a Friday 5pm automated export. Paste the output directly into the Advanced Prompt's pipeline section.
- **Google Analytics 4 + SEMrush / Ahrefs**: Set up weekly automated email reports for organic traffic anomalies and keyword rank changes >5 positions. Import into the "Organic SEO" anomaly section. For intent-driven keyword surges, configure Google Trends alerts for your 5 highest-intent category keywords.
- **Bombora / 6sense / G2 Buyer Intent**: Configure weekly intent surge alerts for your ICP segment. Export the top 50 surging accounts to a Google Sheet. This feeds directly into Section 5 (Demand Opportunity Window) and should be the first data input you collect each Monday morning.
- **LinkedIn Ads / Google Ads Manager**: Build a custom weekly performance report showing CPL, CTR, and conversion rate with % change vs. 4-week rolling average. Any metric moving >15% in either direction gets flagged in the Anomaly section. Use Google Ads Automated Rules to get email alerts when CPA exceeds threshold — these become same-day inputs, not weekly inputs.
- **Slack automation**: Use Zapier or Make to create a "CMO Intelligence Inputs" Slack channel. Each Monday morning, post automated pulls from HubSpot (pipeline generated last week), Google Ads (CPL change), and Bombora (intent surge count). Your ops team fills in the qualitative signals (competitive news, campaign delays). You paste the compiled inputs into this prompt by noon.
- **Notion + Claude**: Create a Notion page with the weekly input template pre-formatted. Use Notion's API to auto-populate quantitative fields from connected data sources. Run the compiled brief through Claude each Monday and publish the output to a shared CMO workspace for the leadership team.
- **Competitive intelligence tools (Crayon, Klue, Kompyte)**: Configure weekly digests from your CI platform filtered to: competitor ad activity changes, new competitor content published, and review site rating shifts. Add these as the primary feed for Section 4.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The brief feels like a data dump — too many signals, no clear priorities.**
Solution: Add this instruction before running the prompt: "Apply ruthless triage. Include only signals where the CMO's decision or action would meaningfully change a pipeline or revenue outcome. Discard signals that are informational but not actionable. If a signal doesn't change a decision, it belongs in an appendix, not the brief." Also reduce the number of channels and data sources you're feeding in — start with 5 key inputs, not 15.

**Problem: The "Decision List" recommendations are too vague (e.g., "consider reviewing ad creative").**
Solution: Add the constraint: "Every decision must name a specific person, a specific action, a specific dollar or pipeline impact, and a specific deadline. Eliminate any recommendation that uses the words 'consider,' 'explore,' or 'review' without specifying exactly what the CMO is deciding." Also provide more quantified data inputs — vague inputs produce vague outputs.

**Problem: The competitive intelligence section is generic and doesn't connect to our specific pipeline.**
Solution: Add this context before the competitive section: "We have [X] active opportunities where [Competitor A] is in the deal. The buyer concern we hear most in Stage 3 is [specific objection, e.g., 'Clari has more integrations']. Use this context to frame competitive intelligence as deal-specific implications, not general market commentary." The more deal-stage context you provide, the more actionable the competitive response recommendations become.
