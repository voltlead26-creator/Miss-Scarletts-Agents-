**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** b2b, saas, cmo, reporting, revenue-attribution, budget-pacing, pipeline-intelligence, monthly-review, executive-communication

## Overview
Transform raw monthly marketing data into a board-ready business review that proves revenue contribution, surfaces execution gaps, drives budget reallocation decisions, and aligns marketing with sales and finance for the next 30 days. Use this at the end of every calendar month to run your MBR and enter the new month with a clear plan.

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot / Salesforce CRM:**
Export monthly pipeline data using these standard reports: "Pipeline Created This Month by Source," "MQL-SQL Conversion by Lead Source," "Closed Won by Marketing Campaign." Map these directly to the pipeline and funnel sections of the prompt.

**Looker / Tableau / Domo:**
Pre-build a "Monthly MBR Data Export" dashboard that auto-populates the key metrics table. Paste the CSV export directly into the prompt — Claude will format and interpret it.

**Google Analytics 4 / Amplitude:**
Pull channel-by-channel traffic and goal conversion data. Use the UTM parameter report to attribute pipeline to specific campaigns for the Channel ROI League Table section.

**Gong / Chorus (Conversation Intelligence):**
Pull the monthly "Win/Loss by Lead Source" and "Top Objections by Segment" reports. Paste these into the competitive context and sales-marketing alignment sections for data-grounded feedback.

**Slack Automation:**
Use a Slack workflow to collect sales feedback on MQL quality at month-end. Send a structured 5-question survey to AEs/SDRs; paste aggregate responses into the prompt's marketing-sales alignment section.

**Zapier / Make (Formerly Integromat):**
Automate data collection: trigger a monthly Zap that pulls Salesforce pipeline data, HubSpot campaign performance, and ad platform spend on the last business day of each month, consolidates into a Google Sheet, and sends you a pre-filled prompt ready for Claude.

**Notion / Confluence:**
Store completed MBRs in a "Marketing Intelligence Archive" database. Tag each review with key metrics so you can trend-analyze performance over 6-12 months and identify seasonal patterns.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The output is too descriptive and doesn't drive decisions.**
Solution: Add this constraint to the prompt: "Every section must end with a specific, accountable action item — who does what by when. No section is complete without an action." If the CMO scorecard section is missing recommendations, explicitly ask the model to "add a one-line recommendation for each metric that is ⚠️ or ❌."

**Problem: Attribution data is incomplete — you don't know which channels drove which pipeline.**
Solution: Use the multi-touch influence model instead of first-touch attribution. In the prompt, replace "pipeline attributed (first-touch)" with "pipeline influenced (any marketing touch within 90 days of opportunity creation)." This typically provides 40-60% more data coverage and is more defensible to skeptical sales leaders anyway. Also flag the attribution gap explicitly in the MBR as a data infrastructure priority.

**Problem: Sales leaders dispute the MQL quality data and the MBR becomes a blame session.**
Solution: Before the MBR, pull actual rejection data from Salesforce (MQL rejection reasons field) and pre-populate the "rejected MQL reasons" section with real data, not assumptions. Present the MQL-SQL conversion by lead source rather than blended — this typically shows that the dispute is channel-specific, not a systemic quality issue, and moves the conversation from blame to surgical fix.
