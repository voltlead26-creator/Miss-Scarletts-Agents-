# GTM Efficiency & Magic Number Intelligence Engine - Calculate, Benchmark, and Defend Your Go-To-Market Efficiency to Investors and the Board

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, saas, analytics, cmo, reporting, board, investors, gtm-efficiency, magic-number, burn-multiple, unit-economics, revenue-operations

## Overview
Calculates your SaaS Magic Number, Burn Multiple, Marketing Efficiency Ratio, and GTM spend-to-ARR ratios — then benchmarks them against investor expectations, identifies which GTM motions are over- or under-invested, and produces a CFO/board-ready efficiency narrative. Use this when preparing board decks, responding to investor diligence questions about GTM efficiency, or building the business case for a headcount or budget change.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "We use multi-touch attribution, so we can't cleanly separate marketing-sourced vs. sales-sourced ARR — what do I put in for Marketing MER?"**
Solution: Use first-touch attribution for Marketing MER (any deal where the first touchpoint was a marketing channel: organic search, paid ad, content download, webinar). This overstates marketing's role in late-stage deals but is the cleanest single-source input for efficiency modeling. Separately, note in your board narrative that you use multi-touch attribution for internal optimization decisions, but first-touch for the MER benchmark to maintain comparability with industry data. If you have multi-touch data available, calculate both and show the range (e.g., "MER is 1.2x first-touch / 2.1x multi-touch weighted by influence").

**Problem: "Our Magic Number looks artificially low because we hired a large SDR team in Q3 and their pipeline won't close until Q1/Q2 next year — how do I explain the lag?"**
Solution: Present a "Lagged Magic Number" alongside the standard metric. Standard Magic Number uses prior quarter S&M to measure current quarter ARR — but for pure outbound with 90+ day sales cycles, this mismatch is real. Build a version where you compare current quarter closed-won ARR against S&M spend from 2 quarters prior (matching the actual sales cycle). Show both in the board deck with an explanation: "Standard Magic Number: 0.72. Cycle-adjusted Magic Number (matching our 67-day median sales cycle): 1.08." Investors appreciate the transparency and it demonstrates you understand your own GTM mechanics.

**Problem: "Our gross margin is lower than typical SaaS (e.g., 55% due to significant services revenue) — does this change the benchmarks?"**
Solution: Yes, materially. All the benchmarks in this prompt assume 70–80% gross margins typical of pure software SaaS. If your gross margin is below 65%, adjust the LTV:CAC minimum threshold upward (need >4:1 instead of >3:1 to generate the same dollar LTV) and be transparent with investors that margin improvement is a roadmap item. Also check whether your services revenue is strategic (accelerates land-and-expand) or accidental (compensating for product gaps). If strategic, frame low margin as deliberate investment with a path to 70%+ as the product matures and services revenue percentage declines.
