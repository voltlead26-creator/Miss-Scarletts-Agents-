## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce:** Enable the Campaign Influence model (Enterprise+) and set it as the default influence tracking method. Build two reports simultaneously — "Primary Campaign Source" for first-touch attribution and "Campaign Influence Related List" on opportunities for any-touch tracking. Export both quarterly. Automate these reports to run on the 1st of each month so you always have current data 5 days before board prep begins.
- **HubSpot:** Use the Revenue Attribution Report (Marketing Hub Professional+). Pull first-touch, last-touch, and linear attribution simultaneously from HubSpot's reporting tab. Export all three to Google Sheets — paste each column into this prompt's data inputs. The contrast between first-touch and linear views is often your strongest evidence that marketing contributes throughout the funnel, not just at awareness.
- **Gong / Chorus (conversation intelligence):** Search call recordings for your specific marketing asset names (e.g., "[Your Case Study Title]", "[Your Webinar Name]", "[Your Report Title]"). Every mention in a winning sales call is buyer-voice evidence that is independent of your attribution model and therefore harder for finance to dismiss. Build a quarterly habit of pulling the top 20 closed-won call recordings and searching for marketing asset mentions.
- **Google Search Console:** Pull 16 months of branded query volume data. Create a correlation chart in Google Sheets: branded search volume by month on one axis, inbound demo requests from Salesforce by month (45-day lag) on the other. This is verifiable, cross-system evidence of brand investment converting to pipeline — and it does not require your CRM attribution model to be working correctly.
- **Zapier / Make.com:** Build a monthly automation: on the 1st of each month, pull (1) closed-won ARR by campaign source from Salesforce, (2) marketing spend by category from your budget tracker spreadsheet, (3) organic traffic and branded search data from Search Console. Aggregate in Google Sheets. Trigger a Claude API call with the Quick Copy-Paste prompt. Post the output to Slack #finance-weekly so CFO and CRO see the monthly attribution snapshot before any ad-hoc questions arise.
- **Notion / Confluence Evidence Library:** After each quarter's board meeting, paste the AI-generated evidence portfolio and stakeholder narratives into a shared "Marketing ROI Archive" document. Capture the attribution methodology used that quarter. Over 4 quarters, this creates a continuously improving record that shows (1) attribution methodology maturing, (2) the consistency of marketing's contribution story, and (3) the quarter-over-quarter trend data that boards find most convincing.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "My CRM shows zero marketing-sourced pipeline because our AEs create all opportunities and don't log campaign sources. Finance only sees what's in Salesforce and Salesforce shows nothing."**
Solution: This is a data hygiene and process problem, not an attribution failure — but it requires a two-track response. Immediate: present attribution evidence from the marketing system side. HubSpot or Marketo shows which accounts engaged with your content, emails, and ads before becoming opportunities in Salesforce — export that account list and cross-reference with closed-won accounts. Present as "marketing activity correlated with pipeline" rather than "marketing-sourced pipeline." Medium-term: work with RevOps to implement a rule where the CRM opportunity creation screen requires a campaign source field — pre-populate it with the most recent marketing campaign touchpoint from your MAP. This is a 30-day fix that changes the measurement basis for every subsequent quarter.

**Problem: "Our CFO says all our attribution is self-serving because marketing owns the attribution model and gets to decide what counts."**
Solution: The CFO is making a valid governance point, not just a tactical objection. Resolve it by proposing a joint attribution council — CMO + CRO + CFO agree on attribution rules before the quarter begins, not after it ends. Commit to using only first-touch (the most conservative method) as the primary headline metric for one quarter, with influence view as supplementary. This demonstrates you're willing to use the most skeptic-friendly methodology. Simultaneously propose bringing in a third-party attribution vendor (Dreamdata, Attribution.io, or Triple Whale for B2C) whose methodology is independent of your team — this removes the "marking your own homework" perception permanently.

**Problem: "We have a 9-month average sales cycle, so the marketing investment that drove this quarter's revenue happened 3 quarters ago — we can't show a clean current-quarter ROI calculation."**
Solution: Don't fight the attribution lag — make it your argument. Present a "cycle-adjusted attribution" framework alongside the standard one. Standard: "Marketing's program spend in Q1 2026 will close as revenue in Q4 2026." Cycle-adjusted: "The $3.2M ARR we closed in Q1 2026 was seeded by marketing investments from Q2 2025 — here's what those investments were and what they cost." Both views are true; presenting both shows you understand your own revenue motion better than finance does. For the board, shift to leading indicators as the primary marketing metrics: pipeline created, content-engaged accounts in ICP, and inbound lead velocity — all of which are 90-day predictors of future ARR at your sales cycle length.
