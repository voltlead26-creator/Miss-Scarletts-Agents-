DELIVERABLES:

**SECTION 1: H1 PERFORMANCE DIAGNOSIS**

Provide a structured, unvarnished H1 audit:

**A. Pipeline Gap Root Cause Analysis**

If H1 pipeline attainment is below 85%:
- Decompose the gap: How much is from demand generation volume (not enough MQLs)? How much is from conversion rate deterioration (MQL-to-SQL, SQL-to-Opp)? How much is from pipeline velocity (longer sales cycles diluting quarterly attribution)?
- For each root cause, diagnose the driver: Was the target unrealistic? Was channel performance worse than historical baseline? Did product-market fit signals shift? Did sales capacity constrain conversion? Did ICP targeting drift?
- Classify each root cause as: (a) execution problem you can fix in H2, (b) structural problem requiring a strategy shift, or (c) market problem outside marketing's control
- Be explicit about which problems the CMO owns and which require sales or product involvement to solve

**B. Channel Efficiency Heat Map**

For each channel in the H1 data, calculate and assess:
- **Efficiency ratio**: Pipeline generated per dollar invested. Flag any channel below 2x as a "cost center" requiring scrutiny; 3-5x as "on benchmark"; above 5x as "potential to scale."
- **Trend direction**: Was the channel improving, stable, or declining through H1 (if you have Q1 vs. Q2 data)? Declining efficiency early is an early warning sign; don't average it away.
- **Concentration risk**: Flag if any single channel accounts for more than 35% of pipeline — concentration above this level creates vulnerability to algorithm changes, platform price increases, or market saturation.
- **Headroom assessment**: Is this channel approaching saturation (audience overlap rising, CPMs increasing, diminishing returns visible) or does it have clear room to scale?

**C. Marketing-Sales Alignment Diagnosis**

Assess the health of the marketing-sales handoff:
- If MQL-to-SQL acceptance rate is below 50%, this is a signal quality problem, not a volume problem — diagnose whether the root cause is targeting (wrong accounts in ICP), timing (too early in buying journey), qualification criteria (too loose), or sales team behavior (cherry-picking or slow follow-up).
- If marketing-sourced pipeline as % of total pipeline has declined vs. plan, diagnose whether this reflects a shift toward outbound/partner sourcing (acceptable) or a genuine demand generation shortfall (concerning).
- Provide a specific action to improve one underperforming marketing-sales metric in H2 — make it concrete (e.g., "implement 4-hour response SLA for content download MQLs from target accounts, monitored weekly").

**D. H1 Budget Autopsy**

For underspend: Identify whether this reflects strategic caution (good), execution failure (bad — late hires, delayed campaigns), or budget sequencing (neutral — events-heavy H1 back half). Underspend is not inherently positive; if it came from execution failures, the same failure modes will persist in H2.

For overspend: Identify the cause and whether the overage generated proportional pipeline. Overspend that produced proportional pipeline is investment; overspend without proportional pipeline is waste.

**SECTION 2: H2 CHANNEL STRATEGY & BUDGET REALLOCATION**

**A. Channel Recommendations with Rationale**

For each H1 channel, provide a specific H2 recommendation:

- **Scale (increase budget by X%)**: Only if: efficiency ratio is above benchmark AND the channel shows headroom to scale without diminishing returns AND H2 buyer behavior supports continued investment. Show the math for expected H2 pipeline from the increased investment.

- **Maintain (same budget)**: If the channel is performing at benchmark and market conditions support continuation. Confirm that "maintain" is a deliberate choice, not inertia.

- **Optimize (same budget, change approach)**: If the channel has potential but execution was suboptimal — specify exactly what changes (different audience, creative approach, offer type, landing page, sales handoff process). "Optimize" without a specific hypothesis is not a strategy.

- **Cut (reduce budget or eliminate)**: If efficiency ratio is below 2x after at least 60 days of fair testing, or if the channel's structural characteristics don't match your ICP (e.g., B2B SaaS buyer who isn't active on a given platform). Provide the exit plan and where the freed budget goes.

**C. High-Confidence vs. Experimental Split**

Structure the H2 budget as:
- **Core allocation** (70-80%): Channels with proven H1 performance and clear H2 scaling path
- **Optimization allocation** (10-15%): H1 channels that underperformed but have a credible fix hypothesis

## Success Metrics

Your H2 recalibration is high quality if:

- **Specificity test**: Every budget recommendation has a dollar amount, an expected pipeline output, and a rationale. "Optimize LinkedIn" with no allocation or hypothesis is not a strategy.
- **Math closes**: H2 budget allocations sum to total H2 budget. Pipeline targets per channel sum to total H2 pipeline target. Required MQL volume is mathematically consistent with pipeline target and win rates.
- **Root causes are real**: H1 diagnosis names specific, testable root causes (ICP drift, conversion rate problem, sales cycle extension) rather than generic explanations ("market was tough," "budget was tight").
- **Risks are disclosed**: Board narrative names at least 2 scenarios that would invalidate the H2 commitment — this signals analytical rigor, not pessimism.
- **Actions are time-bound**: H2 program calendar has specific launch dates, owners, and kill switch criteria. A plan without dates is a wish.
- **Sales alignment is addressed**: The plan explicitly addresses at least one marketing-sales friction point identified in H1.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't have clean channel-level pipeline attribution — everything goes into 'direct' or 'unknown source.'"**
Fix: Run the audit anyway, but be explicit about your attribution limitations. Use proxy metrics — MQL volume by channel, opportunity source field in CRM, cost-per-MQL by channel. Acknowledge the limitation in the board narrative. Then make fixing attribution a H2 MOps priority so you're not in this position at the next review. A rough attribution model you believe is worth more than false precision from a broken model.

**Problem: "Finance won't tell me my H2 budget until August."**
Fix: Build two scenarios — (a) H2 budget equals H1 budget, (b) H2 budget is cut 15%. Run the pipeline math for both. When finance releases the number, you can immediately communicate the pipeline impact of each scenario. This positions you as proactive and analytical, not reactive.

**Problem: "My H2 pipeline math shows a gap I can't close without more budget, and I'm not going to get it."**
Fix: Document the gap explicitly and present it to the CRO and CEO as a joint problem, not a marketing problem. Show the math: "At current budget and efficiency, H2 marketing-sourced pipeline will be $X.XM. The revenue target requires $X.XM. Closing this gap requires either: (a) $Y additional marketing budget, (b) X additional SDR capacity to compensate with outbound volume, or (c) a revised new-logo target that accounts for the pipeline shortfall." This is not throwing the problem over the wall — it's forcing a company-level decision with clear options and consequences.
