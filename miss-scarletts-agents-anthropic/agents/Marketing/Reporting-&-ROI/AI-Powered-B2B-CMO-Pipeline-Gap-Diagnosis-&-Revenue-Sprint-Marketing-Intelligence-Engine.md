## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The pipeline math produces unrealistically optimistic numbers (e.g., $5M expected from $20K budget).**
Solution: Check your conversion rate assumptions — most first-touch demand sprints convert at 15-25% MQL-to-Opportunity, not 40%+. Apply a 0.7x confidence discount to all "medium" estimates and 0.5x to "low." If you're still above target, ask the AI to stress-test assumptions against your historical channel benchmarks.

**Problem: The recommended sprints require skills or tools my team doesn't have.**
Solution: Add a constraint to the prompt: "My team cannot produce video content, run programmatic advertising, or access LinkedIn Sales Navigator this quarter." The engine will re-design sprints around your actual capacity. You can also ask it to suggest a freelancer/agency mix for specific execution gaps.

**Problem: The sprint timeline assumes 6 weeks but we only have 2 weeks left.**
Solution: Re-run the prompt with "Weeks remaining: 2" — this dramatically changes the sprint menu. With 2 weeks, the engine should automatically shift to velocity plays on existing pipeline (executive outreach, urgency campaigns, deal desk acceleration) rather than new pipeline generation, since new MQLs rarely close within 14 days in enterprise B2B.
