## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce**: Enable Campaign Influence (Customizable Attribution) for multi-touch model; use Salesforce Reports to build the Channel Attribution Proof table; Revenue Cloud for closed-loop revenue matching
- **HubSpot**: Enable Revenue Attribution Reports in Marketing Hub Enterprise; configure multi-touch attribution settings; use custom contact properties for self-reported attribution capture (add "How did you first hear about us?" to demo request forms)
- **Bizible / Marketo Measure**: Configure custom channels for dark social, community, and AI search; use the Boomerang feature for re-engagement attribution; export the Position-Based model data directly into the CFO report template
- **Dreamdata / Metadata / Northbeam**: Connect all paid channels + CRM for automated multi-touch B2B attribution; use the "Revenue by Channel" report as the base for Section 3.4
- **Gong / Chorus**: Mine closed-won call transcripts for mentions of marketing content, events, and channels that influenced the deal — this becomes your anecdotal evidence layer for CFO conversations

- **Zapier / Make**: Automate monthly attribution report generation by triggering a data pull from CRM → this prompt template → formatted output to Notion or Google Slides on the 1st of each month

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: CFO dismisses the attribution model as "designed to make marketing look good"**
Solution: Present 3 attribution model options — conservative (last-touch), moderate (position-based 40/20/40), and aggressive (first-touch) — and ask Finance to choose. Show the revenue number for each. A model Finance helped select carries their implicit endorsement. Document the choice in the Attribution Agreement. The act of offering Finance a choice shifts the conversation from adversarial to collaborative.

**Issue: CRM data is too incomplete to run a credible attribution audit**
Solution: Don't attempt to audit all historical data. Instead, run a "clean cohort" analysis: pull only the last 90 days of closed-won deals where UTM data and CRM fields are complete (typically 40–60% of deals). Use this clean cohort to build the attribution model, then extrapolate to the full pipeline with stated confidence intervals. Simultaneously, implement the three 30-day quick wins from Section 2.3 so the next quarter's data is auditable. Present the clean-cohort finding alongside a 60-day data improvement plan.

**Issue: Sales leadership disputes the attribution numbers and undermines the CFO conversation**
Solution: Before the CFO meeting, run a joint attribution workshop with the VP of Sales. Present 5 anonymized closed-won deals from the last quarter, walk through the full touchpoint timeline for each (content consumption, ad impressions, event attendance, SDR touches, demos), and ask the VP to assign attribution weights. You will find alignment on individual deals even when there's disagreement in aggregate. Use these 5 deals as concrete "marketing origin stories" in the CFO conversation — specific deal examples are far more persuasive than aggregate model output.
