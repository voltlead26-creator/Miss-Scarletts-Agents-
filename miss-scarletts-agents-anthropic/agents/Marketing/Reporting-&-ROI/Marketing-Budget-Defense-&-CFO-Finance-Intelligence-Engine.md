## Overview
Transform raw marketing data into an airtight financial business case that speaks the CFO's language — payback periods, NPV, EBITDA impact, and risk-adjusted ROI. Use this when budget season arrives, when finance requests cuts, or when you need to defend marketing as a revenue-generating P&L driver rather than a cost center.

You are the CMO of a B2B SaaS company preparing a rigorous financial defense of your marketing budget for the CFO and board. - Marketing-influenced revenue: [e.g., $11.3M] (LTM)
- Blended CAC (marketing portion): [e.g., $4,200]
- Average ACV: [e.g., $24,000]
- Average customer LTV: [e.g., $72,000] (3-year)
- Gross margin: [e.g., 74%]
- Average sales cycle: [e.g., 67 days]
- MQL-to-closed-won rate: [e.g., 8.4%]
- Budget breakdown: Paid media [e.g., $1.4M], Content/SEO [e.g., $600K], Events [e.g., $700K], Headcount [e.g., $1.1M], Tools [e.g., $400K]

Produce the following sections:

1. **The Business Case (Executive Summary)**: 3 bullet points a CFO will read in 90 seconds — marketing ROI, payback period, and the cost of cutting budget by 25%.

2. **Financial ROI Model**:
   - Marketing ROI = (Marketing-sourced revenue − Marketing spend) ÷ Marketing spend
   - LTV:CAC ratio for marketing-sourced customers
   - Payback period in months
   - Gross-margin-adjusted marketing ROI
   - Compare these to SaaS industry benchmarks (LTV:CAC >3x = healthy; payback <18 months = efficient)

3. **Three Investment Scenarios** (table format):
   - Scenario A — Invest (budget +20%): projected pipeline, revenue, and new customers
   - Scenario B — Maintain (flat budget): projected pipeline, revenue, and new customers
   - Scenario C — Cut (budget −25%): projected pipeline shortfall, revenue at risk, and recovery timeline

4. **Cost of Inaction Analysis**: If marketing budget is cut 25%, model the pipeline gap, projected revenue loss over 12 months, and the cost to rebuild lost momentum (with a 6-month pipeline lag assumption).

5. **Channel ROI Breakdown**: For each budget line, show spend, pipeline generated, revenue attributed, and ROI. Flag the 2 highest-ROI channels to protect and the 1 lowest-ROI channel where reallocation is justified.

6. **Benchmark Comparison**: Compare our marketing efficiency ratios against [industry] SaaS benchmarks — marketing as % of ARR, LTV:CAC, CAC payback, marketing-sourced revenue %.

7. **CFO Risk Register**: Identify 3 risks if the budget is cut (e.g., pipeline coverage falling below 3x, CAC increasing due to brand equity erosion, competitive displacement) with probability and financial impact estimates.

8. **The Ask**: A clear, one-paragraph budget recommendation with the investment amount, expected return, and 90-day proof points to validate the investment is tracking.

Tone: Quantitative, precise, no marketing buzzwords. Use financial terms (ROIC, NPV, payback period). Format as a CFO-ready memo, not a marketing presentation.

**Google Sheets / Excel:**
Build a "Marketing Finance Model" spreadsheet with three tabs (Actuals, Scenarios, Benchmarks). Paste the prompt's scenario table output into the Scenarios tab. Use Sheets formulas to auto-calculate sensitivity ranges as you adjust win rate or ACV assumptions.

**Notion / Confluence:**
Create a standing "Budget Defense" page in your CMO operating system. Run this prompt quarterly and paste the output into a versioned Notion database. Use Notion AI to diff outputs quarter-over-quarter and auto-generate a "what changed" summary for the CFO.

**Looker / Tableau:**

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI produces vague ROI numbers without showing the math.**
Fix: In the Quick Version, add this instruction after the data block: "Show every calculation step explicitly. For each financial metric, write the formula, then the numbers plugged in, then the result." The model will then expose its math, which you can audit and correct.

**Problem: The scenario analysis assumes linear scaling (cutting 25% budget = 25% less pipeline), which is unrealistic.**
Fix: Add this context to the prompt: "Apply a non-linear scaling model: the first 15% of budget cuts typically reduce pipeline by 20–30% due to fixed costs (brand continuity, content compounding, agency minimums) and the loss of high-leverage marginal activities. Adjust scenario projections accordingly." This forces the model to model diminishing returns properly.

**Problem: The CFO objection rebuttals feel generic.**
Fix: Before running the prompt, list the exact verbatim objections your CFO has raised in the past 2 budget cycles. Add them as: "Known CFO objections to address: 1) '[exact quote]' 2) '[exact quote]' 3) '[exact quote]'" The model will tailor rebuttals to your specific CFO's mental model rather than generating generic responses.
