## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a "YouTube Organic" source in contact properties; use UTM parameters `?utm_source=youtube&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=[video-slug]` on all lead magnet landing page links. Set up a workflow to auto-enroll YouTube leads into a video-specific nurture sequence.
- **Salesforce:** Add "YouTube Channel" as a Lead Source picklist value. Build a campaign for each high-intent video series to track influenced pipeline and closed-won attribution.
- **Zapier / Make:** Connect YouTube Studio → Google Sheets → Slack to auto-notify the team when a video hits a view velocity milestone (e.g., 500 views in 48 hours) for rapid comment engagement.
- **Vidyard / Loom:** Repurpose top-performing YouTube videos as gated sales assets; embed in outbound sequences for "video-qualified" prospect warm-up.
- **Google Analytics 4:** Set up a YouTube referral segment to track post-click behavior — pages visited, time on site, demo request rate — to validate video-to-pipeline conversion quality.
- **Notion / Airtable:** Build a video production database with columns: Keyword, Target CPC (proxy for buyer intent), Script Status, Publish Date, 30-day Views, Lead Gen Count, SQL Count — run a quarterly ROI review.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Videos get high impressions but low CTR (under 3%)
**Solution:** The thumbnail or title isn't communicating a specific, tangible outcome. A/B test two thumbnail variants using YouTube Studio's "Test & Compare" feature. Titles should answer "what will I learn/get" in under 8 words. Avoid abstract titles like "B2B Sales Strategy" — use specific outcomes: "Cut Sales Cycle 40% With This 3-Step Process."

**Problem:** Views plateau after day 3 — no long-tail search traffic building
**Solution:** Your keyword targeting may be too competitive or misaligned with search intent. Re-audit your description for keyword density (primary keyword should appear in the first sentence, title, and 3-5 times in the description naturally). Add your keyword to the video's spoken dialogue within the first 60 seconds — YouTube's auto-captions help index spoken keywords. Also check your tags: remove overly broad tags (e.g., "marketing") and replace with exact-match long-tail phrases.

**Problem:** Getting views but no lead magnet conversions
**Solution:** The lead magnet offer is likely disconnected from the video topic. Each video needs its own dedicated lead magnet — a "Sales Forecast Accuracy" video should not promote a generic "B2B Marketing Playbook." Redesign the in-video CTA to name-drop the specific deliverable ("grab the free Forecast Accuracy Diagnostic I mentioned") and place an in-video card at the exact moment you reference it. Ensure the landing page headline mirrors the CTA language word-for-word.
