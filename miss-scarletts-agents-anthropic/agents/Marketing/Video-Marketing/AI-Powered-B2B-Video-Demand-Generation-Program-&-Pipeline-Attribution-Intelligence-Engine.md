## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Videos getting views but no pipeline influence**
*Solution:* Your CTAs are too weak or mismatched to buying stage. Stage 1-2 videos should NOT CTA to "book a demo" — that creates friction. Map CTAs: Stage 1 → download benchmark report; Stage 2 → take assessment; Stage 3 → watch comparison webinar; Stage 4 → schedule demo. Run CTA A/B tests on end screens and video description links.

**Problem: Attribution model showing no video influence even though videos have high view counts**
*Solution:* You likely have a tracking gap — views are happening but not being linked to known contacts in CRM. Audit: (1) Is Wistia/Vidyard HubSpot integration installed and firing? (2) Are YouTube viewers anonymous? If >80% of your traffic is anonymous, invest in deanonymization tools (6sense, Clearbit Reveal, or HubSpot website visitor identification) to match company-level data even without form fills.

**Problem: Content team burning out trying to produce one video per week**
*Solution:* Shift to a "batch production" model — film 4-6 videos in one half-day session with your founder or SME. Use AI scripting (Claude, ChatGPT) to pre-write scripts 2 weeks ahead. Repurpose aggressively: one 20-minute interview = 1 YouTube long-form + 4 LinkedIn clips + 8 email GIFs + 1 podcast episode. Your per-video output doubles without adding production days.
