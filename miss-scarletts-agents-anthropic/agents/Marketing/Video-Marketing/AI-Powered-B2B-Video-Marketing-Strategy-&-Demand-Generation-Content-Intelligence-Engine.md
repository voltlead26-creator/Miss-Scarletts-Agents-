## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot + Wistia/Vidyard**: Embed all website videos through Wistia or Vidyard to fire HubSpot contact activity events at 25%, 50%, 75%, and 100% watch milestones. Use these events to trigger email nurture sequences, update lead scores, and notify assigned sales reps when target account contacts watch high-intent BOFU videos.
- **Salesforce**: Create custom fields on Lead and Opportunity objects: "Video Engaged" (boolean), "Videos Watched Count" (integer), and "Last Video Watched" (text). Build reports comparing average close rate and deal velocity between video-engaged and non-engaged segments.
- **YouTube Studio + Google Search Console**: Connect YouTube Studio to GSC via Google Analytics 4 to identify which video titles drive the most high-intent website sessions. Use this data to prioritize your next quarter's scripting topics.
- **Zapier / Make.com**: Automate the distribution workflow — new video published in Wistia → post to YouTube → schedule LinkedIn clips via Buffer → add to newsletter queue in Beehiiv/HubSpot → notify sales team in Slack with shareable link and suggested use case.
- **Opus Clip**: Connect your YouTube channel to Opus Clip for automatic short-form clip generation. Set parameters to extract the 3 best clips per video based on AI-scored "virality" factors. Review and approve in batch weekly rather than editing each clip manually.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Build Custom Audiences from video viewers in LinkedIn (anyone who viewed 25%+ of your LinkedIn native video) and from YouTube viewers via Google Ads remarketing tags. Use these high-intent audiences for Account-Based retargeting campaigns with a lower CPL than cold prospecting.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Video content gets views but generates no pipeline impact**
Root cause is almost always misaligned CTAs or content that's too educational without creating urgency to act. Solution: Audit every video's CTA against buyer stage — TOFU videos should drive newsletter subscription or downloadable resource, not demo requests. MOFU videos should drive a free trial or product tour. BOFU videos should drive a sales conversation. Also check timing — place CTAs after the 50% watch mark when engagement peaks, not at the end when most viewers have already left. A/B test adding a mid-video CTA (at ~40% through) against end-only CTAs.

**Problem: Production velocity is too slow to sustain consistent cadence**
Implement the "1-to-8 Content Atomization" model — produce one long-form anchor video (12-15 min) per week and systematically extract 8 derivative assets: 3 LinkedIn short clips (Opus Clip), 1 email newsletter section (Descript transcript), 1 SEO blog post (transcript-to-article via Claude), 2 Twitter/LinkedIn text posts (key quotes), and 1 YouTube Short (first 60 seconds as standalone hook). This multiplies distribution from a single production session and reduces per-asset cost to under $200 for most AI-assisted setups.

**Problem: Difficulty getting executive buy-in for video budget**
Don't ask for full program budget upfront. Run a "60-Day Video Pipeline Pilot" — produce 3 highly specific BOFU videos (a competitive comparison, a customer ROI story, and a live product demo for your top use case). Track every CTA click, demo request, and deal influenced in your CRM. Present the pilot results as "cost per influenced opportunity" and benchmark it against your existing paid channels. Most B2B video programs show $6-15 in pipeline per $1 of production cost at 90 days when content is correctly targeted and distributed. A $15K pilot that influences $90K-$150K in pipeline is a straightforward budget justification.
