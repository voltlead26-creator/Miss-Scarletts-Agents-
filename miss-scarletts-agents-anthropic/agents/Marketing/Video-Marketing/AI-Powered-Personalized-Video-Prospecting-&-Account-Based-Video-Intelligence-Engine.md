## Integration Tips

- **Vidyard + HubSpot:** Embed Vidyard links in HubSpot email sequences; use HubSpot workflows triggered by Vidyard "video watched > X%" events to move contacts between lifecycle stages, alert SDRs via Slack, and auto-enroll in accelerated sequences
- **Loom + Salesloft/Outreach:** Use Loom's native Salesloft integration to embed video thumbnails directly in Salesloft cadence steps; Loom tracks views and syncs back to Salesforce activity history
- **HeyGen + Clay:** Use Clay to enrich a target account list with personalization signals (LinkedIn posts, news, tech stack), export as CSV, then use HeyGen's bulk video generation with CSV variable injection to produce 500 personalized AI avatar videos in under 2 hours
- **Synthesia + Salesforce:** Build Synthesia video templates with [FIRSTNAME] and [COMPANY] variables; trigger video generation via Salesforce workflow when a new opportunity is created above a deal size threshold; auto-attach video link to opportunity and send via email automation

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI avatar looks robotic and prospects are mentioning it feels impersonal**
Solution: Use HeyGen's "Expressive" avatar mode and include specific verbal tics and pauses in the script (written as "[brief pause]" or "..."). Add at least one unscripted-sounding line like "and honestly, this is the part that surprises most people." Test all AI avatar videos with 3 internal reviewers before sending. If the avatar still feels stiff, switch to screen-share + voiceover format which feels more authentic at scale.

**Problem: High open rates but low thumbnail click-through (< 20%)**
Solution: Your email subject line is working but the thumbnail isn't compelling. Test three changes: (1) Change thumbnail text to a direct question ("Altura Analytics + SOC 2 in 90 days?"), (2) Use a GIF preview showing the first 3 seconds of the video instead of a static thumbnail, (3) Add a hand-drawn arrow or circle annotation on the thumbnail pointing to their company name or logo.

**Problem: Video watch rates drop off sharply at the 15-second mark**
Solution: Your hook earned the click but the transition to your pitch is too abrupt. Listeners need a "bridge" that earns continued attention. After your personalized hook, add one curiosity gap sentence before transitioning to your message: "The reason I'm reaching out — and this is specific to where [Company] is right now — is..." Then pause one beat before continuing. This signals "what I'm about to say is specifically for you" and watch-through rates typically improve 20-30%.
