## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Signal volume is high but meeting-booked rate is below 5% across all trigger types.**
Solution: The issue is almost always signal-to-relevance mismatch — the trigger is real but the outreach copy isn't making the explicit connection between the event and the prospect's pain. Audit Touch 1 across your lowest-performing trigger types. The opening line must name the specific trigger event ("Saw {Company} just announced the {funding_amount} Series {round}") and connect it directly to a problem your product solves within two sentences. Vague references to "growth" or "exciting times" without naming the event perform as poorly as evergreen cold email. Also verify signal freshness — if average days-from-signal-to-first-touch is >10 days, the buying moment has often passed.

**Problem: Signal monitoring infrastructure detects too many false positives (low-fit companies or irrelevant events triggering enrollment).**
Solution: Add a firmographic fit pre-filter before any signal enrollment. The scoring model should require a minimum firmographic fit score (≥20/30) before a signal triggers rep action. Common false positive sources: LinkedIn "Changed Jobs" alerts catching internal promotions rather than new external hires; Crunchbase alerts firing for venture debt rounds rather than equity raises; hiring surge alerts triggering on seasonal contractor volume rather than full-time headcount growth. Tighten alert criteria: require "Series B+" flag on funding alerts, require "new company" flag (not just title change) on leadership hire alerts, and require "full-time roles only" filter on hiring surge counts.

**Problem: SDR team is not respecting signal SLA — Tier 1 signals going stale before first touch.**
Solution: SLA compliance requires workflow automation, not willpower. Automate Tier 1 enrollment — when a composite score ≥75 hits the system, Apollo/Outreach auto-enrollment should fire without rep intervention. Reserve human judgment for personalization, not enrollment decisions. Additionally, add a daily Slack digest that shows each rep their open Tier 1 signals with a freshness countdown ("3 signals >5 days old — act now before window closes"). In weekly pipeline reviews, track signal-to-first-touch time as a team KPI with visibility to leadership. SDR response time to trigger signals is directly correlated with meeting-booked rate — every day of delay reduces meeting conversion by an estimated 8–12%.
