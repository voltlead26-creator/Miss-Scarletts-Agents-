### DELIVERABLE 1: EXECUTIVE BRIEFING MEMO (pre-read)
A 1-page document sent to the executive sponsor 48 hours before the QBR. Include:
- Meeting objective and agenda preview
- Top 3 wins since last review (with quantified impact)
- 1 strategic discussion topic requiring executive input
- Renewal/partnership message (confident, not salesy)

### DELIVERABLE 2: QBR SLIDE NARRATIVE (30-minute meeting)
Generate speaker notes for each slide section:

**Slide 1 — Partnership Highlights**
Open with the customer's language, not yours. Reference their strategic goals back to them. Set the tone: this is a strategic partnership review, not a vendor check-in.

**Slide 2 — Value Delivered: ROI Summary**
Use the IMPACT framework:
- I: Inputs (what they deployed / how many users)
- M: Metrics moved (quantified before vs. after)
- P: Process improvements (workflows changed)
- A: Adoption milestones (features unlocked)
- C: Cost/time savings (calculate in dollars or hours)
- T: Time to realize value (how fast did outcomes arrive)

**Slide 3 — Goals Review: Previous Quarter Commitments**
For each commitment made last QBR: [Complete ✓ / In Progress ⚡ / Blocked ✗]
For blocked items: explain root cause and revised plan. Take accountability for vendor-side delays.

**Slide 4 — Health Scorecard**
Use a Red/Yellow/Green scoring matrix across 5 dimensions:
1. Product Adoption (usage vs. license capacity)
2. Business Outcomes (KPI progress vs. target)
3. Relationship Depth (stakeholder coverage)
4. Support Experience (ticket volume and resolution quality)
5. Strategic Alignment (their goals vs. your roadmap fit)
For each Yellow or Red dimension: name the specific risk and proposed intervention.

**Slide 5 — Roadmap & Innovation Preview**
Map 3 upcoming product capabilities directly to their stated strategic objectives. Use their language ("You mentioned wanting to reduce manual reporting — here's how [Feature X] eliminates that"). Include beta access or early access offer if applicable.

**Slide 6 — Success Plan: Next 90 Days**
Three SMART objectives with: Goal → Owner (CSM + Customer) → Success metric → Target date → Check-in cadence.

**Slide 7 — Strategic Opportunity**
Present the expansion proposal using the SPIN framework:
- Situation: where they are today
- Problem: the gap creating friction or missed opportunity
- Implication: cost of inaction (quantified)
- Need-payoff: what solving it unlocks (quantified ROI projection)
Present this as a recommendation, not a pitch. Frame it as your professional obligation to flag it.

**Slide 8 — Renewal Confidence Statement**
A 2-sentence value affirmation followed by a clear, assumption-free renewal ask. Example: "Based on [specific outcome], you've realized [ROI]. We'd like to confirm renewal and build the next phase together — are you aligned?"

---

### DELIVERABLE 3: POST-QBR FOLLOW-UP EMAIL
Send within 2 hours of the meeting. Include:
- 3-sentence meeting recap (what was discussed, decided, actioned)
- Action item table: Item | Owner | Due Date
- Expansion proposal one-pager attachment summary
- Next meeting placeholder with agenda preview
- One personalized detail from the conversation to reinforce human connection

---

### DELIVERABLE 4: INTERNAL ACCOUNT STRATEGY NOTE
For the CSM's manager and AE. Include:
- Renewal confidence rating: [1-5] with rationale
- Expansion deal stage: [Qualified / Discovery / Proposal / Verbal]
- Risk flags requiring leadership intervention
- Recommended next actions for AE (expansion) and CSM (health)
- Forecast: renewal probability % and expansion close probability %

---

**FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS:**
- Use the customer's name and specific data points throughout — never generic placeholders in final output
- Quantify everything: avoid "improved significantly" — write "reduced time-to-report by 3.2 hours/week"
- Executive language: confident, direct, outcome-focused — no jargon, no passive voice
- Flag any data gaps that would strengthen the narrative (prompt the CSM to gather them)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The output is generic — it doesn't feel specific to my customer.**
Fix: The more specific your input data, the sharper the output. Replace every bracketed placeholder with actual numbers and names. The prompt is calibrated for specificity — vague inputs produce vague QBRs. If you're missing data (e.g., exact baseline metric), run `Ask your champion: "What was your [metric] before we started working together?"` and add the answer before generating.

**Problem: The expansion proposal feels like a sales pitch, not a CSM recommendation.**
Fix: Instruct the AI: "Rewrite the expansion proposal as a professional recommendation, not a pitch. The CSM is flagging an opportunity they believe serves the customer's stated goal of [X]. Use 'we recommend' not 'we'd love to offer you'." The SPIN framework in the prompt keeps it consultative, but tone calibration via follow-up prompt refines it further.

**Problem: The executive memo is too long / my sponsor won't read it.**
Fix: Add this constraint to the prompt: "Rewrite the executive briefing memo for a CEO who reads on mobile. Max 150 words. Use 3 bullet points only. Remove all pleasantries. Lead with the most important win." Executives respond to brevity and specificity — the prompt supports this trim but defaults to a fuller version for thoroughness.
