Phase 2 — Technical and Compliance Validation (Weeks 4–8):

  - Regulatory coverage mapping: Produce written documentation of how your platform addresses each relevant compliance mandate (SEC, MiFID II, ERISA, ESG) in the prospect's environment
  - Security and vendor due diligence: Pre-complete CAIQ (Cloud Security Alliance) questionnaire, SOC 2 Type II report, Business Continuity Plan, and Vendor Due Diligence Questionnaire — deliver complete package before they ask

Phase 3 — Proof-of-Value (Weeks 9–14):
  - Live environment proof: Process client's actual data (one month or one quarter) through your platform in parallel with current process
  - Exception reporting: Auto-generate "Reconciliation Exception Report" showing differences between current system output and your platform output — demonstrate accuracy
  - Performance: Generate client performance attribution report in your platform alongside current process — compare output accuracy and production time
  - Operational efficiency measurement: Document actual hours spent by their team producing deliverables in your platform vs. current state — real operational improvement evidence
  - Compliance report generation: Produce one regulatory report (e.g., Form ADV performance exhibits or MiFID II cost disclosure) in your platform — demonstrate compliance accuracy

Phase 4 — Business Case and Procurement (Weeks 15–16):

  - Investment committee brief: Generate executive summary of evaluation findings + ROI quantification + peer reference summary — formatted for CIO or Managing Partner to present to investment committee
  - Procurement package: Draft MSA, data processing agreement, security addendum, implementation timeline — deliver complete package simultaneously with commercial proposal
  - Implementation certainty: Provide firm implementation timeline with named implementation lead assigned — institutional buyers are highly risk-averse about implementation risk

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Outreach to CIOs and Managing Partners is going unanswered — institutional buyers are completely ignoring outbound**
Solution: Institutional investment management professionals have extremely high email and phone screening — cold outreach from unknown vendors is almost universally ignored. Immediately pivot to three alternative first-touch strategies: (1) Warm introduction through custodian or fund administrator relationship manager — call your Schwab Advisor Services or Fidelity Institutional relationship contact and ask for a personal introduction to their client; a single warm introduction from a trusted custodian RM has conversion rates 15–20× higher than cold outreach to the same prospect; (2) Conference or event-based first meeting — attend the next SIFMA, IMN Family Office Forum, or local CFA society event where your ICP is present; meet prospects as a peer, share your operational research, and make no product pitch; the business card exchange at a peer event is the standard first-touch for institutional sales; (3) Publish original research that gets distributed through industry association channels — contribute your benchmark data to NICSA, FPA, or CFA Institute publications; institutional buyers read association research with high credibility; a CIO who reads your data in a CFA publication will take your outreach call when they see your firm name.

**Problem: Evaluations are progressing with operations contacts but stalling at investment committee review with no CIO engagement**
Solution: This is the most common pipeline failure mode in WealthTech — the COO champion is genuinely enthusiastic but lacks organizational authority to close without CIO or Managing Partner investment committee approval. Deploy a parallel executive engagement track immediately: (1) Create an "Investment Committee Brief" asset specifically for the COO champion to present upward — one-page executive summary of evaluation findings, operational impact metrics from proof-of-value phase, peer reference quotes from firms the CIO will recognize, and ROI quantification; provide this to the champion at Week 8, not Week 16; (2) Request a "CIO Architecture Alignment" call — positioned as an investment strategy alignment meeting ("We want to ensure our platform roadmap aligns with your investment strategy evolution") rather than a sales call; CIOs respond to strategic alignment framing; (3) Build an executive briefing sequence specifically triggered when the opportunity advances to "Investment Committee Review" stage — 3-touch sequence: benchmark comparison for the CIO's AUM band, reference introduction from a CIO at a peer firm the prospect will recognize, and a one-page regulatory risk assessment showing compliance exposure of continuing with current technology; tracking CIO email engagement separately in CRM as a required stage gate ensures this becomes systematic.

**Problem: Competitors are winning deals by offering lower prices and bundled platform deals through custodian relationships**
Solution: Price competition from custodian-bundled platforms (e.g., Schwab's Tamarac, Fidelity's WealthSite integration) is a real structural challenge in the WealthTech market. Do not match on price — it is not sustainable and signals commoditization. Instead, own the "independence and data integrity" positioning: (1) Publish independent benchmark data showing that bundled custodian platforms have higher reconciliation error rates and more limited multi-custodian support — give CIOs factual evidence to evaluate the trade-off of custodian lock-in; (2) Build a strong second-custodian story — most $1B+ family offices use 2–4 custodians; the bundled custodian platform advantage disappears entirely when the client has a non-custodian relationship; develop specific content showing multi-custodian data accuracy vs. single-custodian-optimized platforms; (3) Deepen custodian partnerships to match — simultaneously pursue co-marketing agreements with the same custodians offering bundled competitors; your platform's open architecture and superior reconciliation accuracy for multi-custodian clients is a genuine value proposition for custodian relationship managers who want to keep clients happy and growing AUM; a custodian RM whose client is unhappy with the bundled platform's reconciliation accuracy will refer them to you; (4) Target the custodian's "loss leader" clients — firms evaluating a move to a second custodian for diversification, or firms with growing alternatives allocations that the custodian's platform handles poorly, are highest-probability competitive displacement opportunities.
