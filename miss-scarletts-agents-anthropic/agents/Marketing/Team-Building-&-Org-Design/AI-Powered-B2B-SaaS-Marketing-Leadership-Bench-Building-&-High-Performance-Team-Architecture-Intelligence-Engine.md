## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Direct reports feel threatened by the transparency around potential ratings and succession planning  
**Solution:** Frame the conversation around *investment* not evaluation — share that you're identifying people to receive more development resources, not building a "B team" list. Run the succession matrix at the CMO level; share individual development plans 1:1, not in group settings.

**Problem:** The AI generates development plans that feel generic rather than personalized to specific people  
**Solution:** Feed in more behavioral specifics about each person — recent wins, failures, feedback they've received, what they've said they want. The more specific your input about actual observed behavior, the more actionable the output. Include verbatim quotes from 1:1 conversations if available.

**Problem:** High-potential marketers leave before the development investment pays off  
**Solution:** Make the 12-month development commitment visible and documented in a mutual "development agreement" that both you and the individual sign. This creates psychological commitment on both sides and signals that you're serious. Review it in every 1:1, not just quarterly reviews.
