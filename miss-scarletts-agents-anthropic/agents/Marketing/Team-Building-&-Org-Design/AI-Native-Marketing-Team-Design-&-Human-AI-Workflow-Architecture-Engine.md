## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI content quality is inconsistent and requires too much human editing, defeating the productivity gains.**
Solution: The issue is almost always prompt engineering, not the AI tool. Invest 2 weeks in building a "Prompt Library" for each content type — standardized prompts that encode your brand voice, target audience, and quality standards. Also implement a "Brief First" workflow: AI generates a structured brief, human approves the brief, then AI produces the full piece. This cuts editing time by 60%.

**Problem: The team is resistant to AI tools and productivity gains aren't materializing because people aren't using them.**
Solution: Don't mandate tools — show wins. Identify 1-2 early adopters on the team (usually the most ambitious junior marketer) and have them demo real time savings in a team meeting. Then offer "AI Office Hours" — weekly 30-minute sessions where anyone can bring a task and see how AI handles it live. Resistance drops dramatically when people see their own job becoming easier, not threatened.

**Problem: The business case showed $200K in savings but the CFO is skeptical because they're worried about quality degradation affecting pipeline.**
Solution: Run a 30-day controlled pilot before the full transition. Keep the existing team while running AI workflows in parallel, then compare outputs: traffic, MQL volume, conversion rates, and pipeline generated. Quantify that AI-produced content performs at 85-95% of human-only baseline, while producing 2-3x the volume. Present the pilot data — concrete evidence of maintained quality is more persuasive than projections.
