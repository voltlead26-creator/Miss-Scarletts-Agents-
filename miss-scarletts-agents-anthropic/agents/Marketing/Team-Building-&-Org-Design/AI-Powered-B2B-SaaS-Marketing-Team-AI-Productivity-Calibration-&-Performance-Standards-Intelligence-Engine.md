## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Team members feel surveilled and micromanaged by the adoption scorecard.**
Fix: Co-create the scorecard with the team, not for them. Run a 1-hour workshop where the team defines what "great AI use" looks like for each role. Team-authored standards are self-enforcing.

**Problem: Output volume increases but brand voice quality degrades — stakeholders complain content "sounds AI-generated."**
Fix: Add a mandatory human-voice editing pass as a quality gate before publication. Create a "brand voice red list" of phrases and patterns that immediately signal AI authorship without human refinement. Make quality the manager's accountability metric, not the individual contributor's volume metric.

**Problem: One high performer refuses to use AI tools, citing ethical or quality concerns, and is consistently missing the new volume targets.**
Fix: Separate the performance conversation from the adoption conversation. If output quality and business impact remain high via a non-AI workflow, recalibrate their targets to reflect their working style while having an honest conversation about the long-term sustainability of that approach. Forcing AI adoption on strong performers often costs you the employee.
