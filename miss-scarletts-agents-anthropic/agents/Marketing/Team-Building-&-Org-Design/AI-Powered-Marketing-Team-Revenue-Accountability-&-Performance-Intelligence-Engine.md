## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce:** Create a custom "Marketing Team Attribution" report type that joins Campaign Influence data with Opportunity Close data. Set up a Salesforce Dashboard per marketer using record sharing rules so each person sees their own attribution data in real time.
- **HubSpot:** Use the "Revenue Attribution" report in HubSpot Marketing Hub Enterprise (requires Enterprise tier). Create a contact property "Primary Marketing Owner" that maps to the team member responsible for that lead's nurture sequence.
- **Marketo:** Build Smart Lists per marketer that surface their program influence statistics. Use the Program Performance Report with multi-touch attribution enabled and export quarterly to feed into scorecards.
- **Looker / Tableau:** Build a "Marketing Accountability" dashboard with one tile per role, pulling pipeline contribution, efficiency ratios, and quarter-over-quarter trends. Share view-only access with each team member so there are no data surprises in reviews.
- **Lattice / 15Five / Workday:** Map the 5-section scorecard structure to your HRIS's custom review template. Use the AI-generated narrative from Section D as the first-draft "manager narrative" field — have managers edit rather than write from scratch.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Store each team member's quarterly scorecard in a private Notion page with consistent structure. Use Notion AI to generate the performance narrative using a prompt template that pulls in the KPI data.
- **Zapier:** Automate a quarterly Zap that (1) exports Salesforce attribution data to a Google Sheet, (2) triggers a Notion page creation from a template, and (3) sends a Slack message to each team member with a link to their draft scorecard.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High-performing creatives (designers, brand managers) feel the system is unfair because their work is hard to attribute to pipeline.**
Solution: Give the qualitative score (Section B + Section C) a higher weight for roles that are 2+ steps removed from pipeline generation — use 30% qualitative weight for brand/design vs. 10% for demand gen. Additionally, use brand health metrics (aided awareness, branded search volume growth, share of voice) as their primary KPIs rather than forcing direct pipeline attribution. Frame brand investment as the "rising tide" that improves every other channel's efficiency.

**Problem: Sales team disputes marketing's pipeline attribution and the conflict undermines the system.**
Solution: Create a joint Marketing-Sales Attribution Calibration meeting (monthly, 30 min) where Sales and Marketing review 10 randomly selected closed-won deals and agree on attribution credit for each. Use these calibration sessions to tune the attribution model over time. Document all agreed edge-case rules in a shared "Attribution Rulebook" in Notion — when disputes arise, the rulebook is the referee, not a manager's opinion.

**Problem: Team members game the system by focusing on metrics that are easiest to hit rather than highest-value activities.**
Solution: Include at least one "quality gate" per role that cannot be gamed by volume — for example, Demand Gen Managers must maintain MQL-to-SQL conversion rate above a floor (not just hit MQL volume), Content Managers must maintain a content engagement score (time on page + backlinks + rankings) not just publish velocity. Additionally, run a quarterly "gut check" with Sales leadership: do the top performers on the scorecard feel like the most valuable marketers from Sales' perspective? Mismatches signal a metric design problem.
