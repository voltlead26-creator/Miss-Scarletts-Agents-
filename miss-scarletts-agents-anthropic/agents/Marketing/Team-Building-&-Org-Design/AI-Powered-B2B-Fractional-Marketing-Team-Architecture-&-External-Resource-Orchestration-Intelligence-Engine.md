## Success Metrics

- **Cost per pipeline-dollar:** Marketing team total cost ÷ pipeline influenced — target: <15% of pipeline created
- **Output per dollar:** Number of campaigns, content pieces, and experiments per $10K of team cost — should increase QoQ
- **Ramp time:** External resources producing first deliverable within 14 days; reaching full output within 45 days
- **Retention:** Fractional/freelance relationship duration — high churn (< 6 months) signals brief quality or scope problems, not resource quality
- **Knowledge retention score:** % of key playbooks and assets documented internally after any resource offboards — target: 100%
- **Pipeline attribution coverage:** % of pipeline with a tracked marketing touchpoint — target: 80%+ within 6 months

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Use this framework to define who owns which HubSpot workflows, properties, and reports. External resources should have role-based access only — never admin rights. Document in a HubSpot access log updated quarterly.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Create a "Marketing Team Wiki" where all external resources are required to document their work. Structure it with: Campaigns → SOPs → Briefs → Results. This is your insurance policy against resource churn.
- **Google Sheets / Airtable:** Build a simple "Resource Roster" tracking each person/agency, their contract type, monthly cost, primary KPIs, and next review date. Review it in the monthly marketing leadership meeting.
- **Slack:** Create dedicated channels for each external resource or agency (e.g., #agency-seo, #freelancer-design). This creates a searchable record of decisions and reduces email chaos.
- **Loom:** Use async video briefing for external resources. A 5-minute Loom is clearer than a 500-word brief and takes half the time to create.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Fractional executive isn't driving results — just having meetings.**
*Solution:* The problem is almost always scope clarity, not talent. Go back to basics: define 3 specific monthly deliverables with measurable outcomes. If they're in 6+ meetings per week, they're being used as a senior employee, not a fractional executive. Fractional = strategy + oversight, not execution. Reduce meeting load by 50% and require async updates instead.

**Problem: Agency keeps missing KPIs but their contract auto-renews.**
*Solution:* Never sign auto-renewing agency contracts. All agency agreements should be 3-month terms with explicit renewal based on hitting KPIs defined in the contract. If KPIs aren't in your current contract, renegotiate at the next renewal. Add a 90-day "probationary period" to all new agency contracts where either party can exit with 30 days notice.

**Problem: Freelancer quits mid-project and takes all the knowledge with them.**
*Solution:* This is a documentation failure, not a freelancer problem. Institute a rule: no deliverable is "done" until it's documented in your team wiki. For writers: the brief + outline is as important as the article. For designers: the brand guide and file organization matter as much as the final asset. For ad specialists: the campaign structure and naming conventions must be documented before they touch your ad account.
