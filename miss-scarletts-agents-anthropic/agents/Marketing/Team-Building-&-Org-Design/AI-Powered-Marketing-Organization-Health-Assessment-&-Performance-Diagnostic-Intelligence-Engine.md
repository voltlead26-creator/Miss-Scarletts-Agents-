# AI-Powered Marketing Organization Health Assessment & Performance Diagnostic Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** cmo, leadership, org-design, team-health, marketing-maturity, diagnostics, b2b, saas, performance-management

## Overview
Generates a comprehensive, scored health assessment of an existing marketing organization — covering people, process, technology, strategy, and culture — and produces a prioritized improvement roadmap. Use it when a CMO inherits a team, prepares for a board review, makes a case for budget/headcount, or needs a quarterly reset after missing pipeline goals.

FRAMEWORK: MARKETING ORGANIZATION HEALTH INDEX (MOHI)

Score each dimension 1–10 using the benchmark levels:
  1–3 = Critical risk: Active revenue drag, needs immediate intervention
  4–5 = Below benchmark: Functional but underperforming vs. stage peers
  6–7 = At benchmark: Solid foundation with clear growth levers
  8–9 = Above benchmark: Competitive advantage in this dimension
  10  = Best-in-class: Top 5% for stage; a model others would benchmark against

DIMENSION 1: PEOPLE & SKILLS HEALTH
Assess:
- ICP for the marketing team itself: Do you have the right roles for your GTM motion?
- Skills gap analysis: What capabilities are missing that limit revenue impact?
- AI fluency distribution: What % of the team uses AI tools daily vs. occasionally vs. never?
- Talent risk: Who are the 1-2 people whose departure would cause immediate damage?
- Manager-to-IC ratio and span of control
- Culture proxy: What does attrition rate and tenure distribution tell you?

Framework: Use the "T-Shaped Marketer Composition Model" — every team needs depth specialists (demand gen, PMM, ops) and breadth generalists. Identify where the team is over-indexed vs. under-indexed.

DIMENSION 2: STRATEGY & POSITIONING HEALTH
Assess:
- ICP sharpness: Can every marketer articulate the top 3 pains of the primary buyer in 30 seconds?
- Messaging differentiation: Is the positioning meaningfully different from the top 3 competitors?
- Category strategy: Are you competing in an existing category or building a new one? Is the strategy explicit?
- Channel-market fit: Are the channels being used actually where the ICP spends attention?
- Account intelligence feedback loop: Is market research continuously updating strategy?

Framework: Use the "Category POV Clarity Test" — if you removed the company name from the homepage and replaced it with a competitor's name, would it still make sense? Score based on how much distinctiveness would be lost.

DIMENSION 3: DEMAND GENERATION HEALTH
Assess:
- Pipeline coverage ratio vs. stage benchmark (Series B: 3.5x; Series C: 4x; Pre-IPO: 4.5x)
- Channel diversification: Is pipeline dangerously concentrated (>60%) in one channel?
- Full-funnel conversion benchmarks: MQL-to-SAL, SAL-to-SQL, SQL-to-Close vs. industry peers
- Inbound-to-outbound balance for your GTM motion
- Dark funnel capture: Are you measuring influenced pipeline from organic, dark social, and brand?
- Lead quality scores: What does sales say about lead quality? NPS ≥ +30 is the target.

Framework: Use the "Demand Waterfall Leak Audit" — map every stage conversion and identify where the biggest revenue leak is occurring. A 5% improvement at the MQL-to-SAL stage is worth more than doubling top-of-funnel volume in most B2B orgs.

DIMENSION 4: CONTENT & BRAND HEALTH
Assess:
- Content quality signal: Do blog posts rank in top-5 organic positions for target keywords?
- Buyer journey coverage: Is there strong content for awareness, consideration, and decision stages?
- AI search readiness (GEO): Is the brand being cited in ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity responses for key topics?
- Brand distinctiveness: Does the brand have a recognizable voice, visual identity, and point of view?
- Content-to-revenue attribution: Can any content piece be directly traced to pipeline influence?
- Content production efficiency: Time from brief to published; % of content that hits target metrics

Framework: Use the "Content Coverage Matrix" — map every ICP pain point × buyer stage × format. White space = revenue opportunity. Bloated areas = production waste.

DIMENSION 5: MARKETING TECHNOLOGY HEALTH
Assess:
- Stack rationalization: How many tools are redundant, underused (<30% feature adoption), or creating data silos?
- CRM data quality: What % of contacts have complete, accurate, non-duplicate data?
- Attribution model maturity: Can you credibly report marketing's revenue contribution to a CFO?
- Automation coverage: What % of lead routing, nurture, and scoring is automated vs. manual?
- AI tool integration: Are AI tools used in isolated tasks or embedded in repeatable workflows?
- MarTech debt: When was the last full stack audit? Is technical debt causing campaign velocity drag?

Framework: Use the "MarTech Efficiency Ratio" — divide measurable pipeline influenced by marketing technology by total MarTech investment. Benchmark: $8–$15 pipeline per $1 of MarTech spend for Series B/C B2B SaaS.

DIMENSION 6: ALIGNMENT & OPERATIONS HEALTH
Assess:
- Sales-Marketing SLA: Is there a written, signed SLA covering lead definition, response time, and feedback loops?
- OKR alignment: Are marketing OKRs traceable to revenue outcomes vs. activity metrics?
- Meeting cadence: Is there a weekly marketing-sales pipeline sync, monthly QBR prep, and quarterly strategy review?
- Reporting quality: Does the CMO produce a weekly 1-page revenue signal brief? Is it acted on?
- Cross-functional relationships: How are CS, Product, and Finance relationships with Marketing rated?
- Decision velocity: How long does it take to launch a new campaign from concept to live? (Benchmark: <10 days for tactical; <30 days for integrated)

Framework: Use the "Revenue Accountability Index" — measure what % of marketing OKRs are revenue-linked vs. activity-linked. Top-performing marketing orgs have ≥70% revenue-linked OKRs.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The output gives generic recommendations ("improve content quality") instead of specific actions.
**Solution:** The Advanced version requires specific, named problems in the "Known Issues" field. Replace vague descriptions like "content underperforms" with specific evidence: "Blog traffic is flat for 8 months; top 5 posts average 320 visits/month with 0 conversions."

**Problem:** The MOHI scores feel arbitrary without real benchmark data.
**Solution:** Add your actual metrics to the Performance Data section of the Advanced version. Even partial data (e.g., pipeline coverage ratio and MQL-to-SAL rate) dramatically improves scoring accuracy. Reference your company stage benchmark in the prompt explicitly: "Series C benchmark is 4x pipeline coverage."

**Problem:** Sales leadership dismisses the assessment as "marketing grading its own homework."
**Solution:** Use the output as a starting framework, then run a 30-minute working session with the VP Sales or CRO to validate the Alignment & Operations score specifically. Their input on the Sales-Marketing relationship dimension turns the assessment from a self-evaluation into a joint accountability document.
