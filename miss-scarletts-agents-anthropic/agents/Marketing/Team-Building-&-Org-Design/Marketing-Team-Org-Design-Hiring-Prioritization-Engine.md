# Marketing Team Org Design & Hiring Prioritization Engine - AI-Powered Headcount Planning for CMOs

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** b2b, leadership, team-building, org-design, hiring, strategy, headcount-planning

## Overview
Analyzes your company's growth stage, current team, revenue targets, and marketing gaps to generate a prioritized hiring roadmap, optimal org structure, and role-by-role justification — all in one pass. Use it when you're building a marketing function from scratch, inheriting a team, preparing a headcount request for the board, or scaling from seed to Series B and beyond.

**HIRE #1 — PRODUCT MARKETING MANAGER (Critical)**

**Why this hire first:** Crestline's 8 AEs are walking into enterprise deals without competitive battlecards, a consistent pitch narrative, or objection-handling frameworks. At $42K ACV with legal buyers who compare multiple vendors rigorously, this is costing 20-30% of winnable deals. A PMM will pay for themselves within 90 days by lifting win rate — before a single inbound lead arrives.

**What they own:**
- Competitive intelligence and battlecards (updated quarterly)
- Messaging hierarchy and positioning for General Counsel vs VP Legal personas
- Sales enablement content: pitch decks, one-pagers, ROI calculators, case studies
- Product launch go-to-market (feature releases, new use cases)
- Customer and win/loss interviews (minimum 4/quarter)
- Pricing narrative and packaging language

**Must-have skills:**
- 3-5 years B2B SaaS PMM experience; enterprise or legal/compliance tech preferred
- Proven ability to write and own a full messaging framework
- Has interviewed customers and translated insights into sales materials
- Comfortable presenting to AEs and running enablement sessions

**Red flags:**
- Can only talk about awareness metrics (impressions, traffic) — not revenue outcomes
- Has never owned a battlecard update in a competitive deal cycle
- No experience with enterprise buyers; only PLG/self-serve background

**Compensation benchmark:** $110-$135K base, 0.1-0.2% equity (Series A)

**Time-to-impact:** First battlecard and pitch deck update: 45 days. Measurable win rate improvement: 90 days.

**The one interview question:**
"Walk me through the last time you changed a sales rep's behavior based on something you learned from a customer interview. What did you learn, what did you change, and how did you know it worked?"

Great answer: Describes a specific customer insight (a fear, a misconception, a job-to-be-done) that directly contradicted how reps were pitching. Changed a specific section of the deck or talk track. Followed up by reviewing call recordings to confirm adoption. Tied it to a conversion metric.

Mediocre answer: Talks about building a new case study or updating the website. No direct connection to rep behavior or revenue.

**HIRE #2 — DEMAND GENERATION MANAGER**

**Why this hire second:** With PMM locking the message, Demand Gen activates the channels. Legal buyers research heavily on LinkedIn and Google before engaging sales — a DG Manager with SEO and paid search experience will build the inbound pipeline engine that turns the content investment into leads. This hire unlocks MQL volume within 60-90 days of starting.

**What they own:**
- SEO strategy and execution (keyword mapping, on-page optimization, content briefs)
- Paid search and LinkedIn Ads campaigns (targeting GC/VP Legal personas)
- Webinar program (quarterly legal industry topics to build email database)
- MQL and pipeline reporting: weekly dashboard, monthly analysis
- Website conversion optimization (landing pages, CTAs, lead capture)
- Lead scoring configuration in HubSpot in partnership with Ops

**Must-have skills:**
- 3-5 years B2B demand gen, ideally in SaaS selling to enterprise buyers
- Hands-on HubSpot or Marketo experience (can build campaigns, not just manage)
- Has run paid LinkedIn campaigns targeting director-level and above buyers
- Understands full-funnel attribution — not just lead volume

**Red flags:**
- Measures success in traffic and MQL volume only, never pipeline or revenue
- Has only worked in high-volume PLG environments (wrong muscle memory for enterprise)
- Cannot articulate their cost-per-MQL or cost-per-closed-won from previous roles

**Compensation benchmark:** $100-$125K base, 0.08-0.15% equity (Series A)

**Time-to-impact:** First campaigns live: 30 days. First inbound MQLs: 45-60 days. Measurable pipeline contribution: 90 days.

**The one interview question:**
"Tell me about a time you had to build inbound pipeline from near-zero. What were your first 30 days? What did you prioritize first, and why?"

Great answer: Starts with ICP research and channel validation before touching any tool. Describes hypothesis-driven prioritization with rationale. Can quote specific numbers from the build.

Mediocre answer: Immediately jumps to tactics ("I set up HubSpot and started running ads"). No mention of research, prioritization rationale, or early measurement.

**HIRE #3 — MARKETING OPERATIONS ANALYST**

**Why this hire third:** By month 4-5, you will have two active channels generating leads, a PMM producing content, and an events program running. Without ops, nothing is measured correctly, HubSpot is a mess, and the CMO is spending 30% of their time in spreadsheets. This hire frees up the CMO for strategy and gives the team a reliable data layer.

**What they own:**
- HubSpot administration (workflows, lifecycle stages, lead scoring, database hygiene)
- Marketing attribution model (first-touch, last-touch, and multi-touch in parallel)
- Weekly and monthly performance reporting (dashboard maintenance, not just exports)
- Campaign tracking infrastructure (UTM governance, channel tagging)
- Tech stack evaluation: recommend additions, deprecate unused tools, manage renewals

**Must-have skills:**
- 2-4 years marketing ops in a B2B SaaS environment
- HubSpot certified (Operations Hub or Marketing Hub professional)
- Can build a multi-touch attribution model without a BI team
- SQL or Google Sheets power-user level — can pull and manipulate data independently

**Red flags:**
- Treats ops as a ticket queue, not a strategic function
- Cannot explain the difference between lead source and attribution model
- Has never owned a database quality initiative

**Compensation benchmark:** $85-$105K base, 0.05-0.1% equity (Series A)

**Time-to-impact:** Attribution model running: 45 days. First clean pipeline report: 60 days.

**WHAT NOT TO HIRE FOR YET**

1. **Brand/Creative Director** — Crestline's brand problem is a messaging problem, not a visual identity problem. A PMM solves this for $40K less with 10x more revenue impact. A brand director at $3.2M ARR optimizes aesthetics when the strategic foundation is not yet built.

## Success Metrics
- Win rate change: Track deal win rate 90 days before vs 90 days after PMM hire; target 5-15% lift
- Sales cycle length: Time from opportunity creation to close; PMM impact visible within 1 quarter
- Inbound pipeline contribution: % of pipeline sourced by marketing; target 20-30% by month 9 at Series A
- MQL-to-SQL conversion: If below 20%, operations or targeting is broken; if above 40%, volume is the constraint
- Headcount efficiency: Pipeline generated per marketing FTE; benchmark against fundraising stage peer group
- Time-to-productivity per hire: Days from start date to first measurable output; target under 60 days for IC roles

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI recommends roles that don't fit your budget or headcount approval**
Solution: Rerun the prompt with a stricter constraint — "You have exactly 1 hire and $130K salary budget. Given everything above, what is the single highest-ROI role?" Force a hard trade-off rather than an ideal-world org. Then use the "make vs buy vs agency" output to cover remaining gaps with fractional or contractor resources at a fraction of FTE cost.

**Problem: The org design is right but you cannot get CEO or board buy-in on headcount**
Solution: Run the output through the [CMO Board Presentation Builder](../Reporting-&-ROI/CMO-Board-Presentation-Builder.md), specifically the "headcount investment justification" section. Frame each hire as an investment with a projected return, not a cost. Use the "90-day activation plan" to show the board exactly what each new hire will own and deliver — specificity reduces objection far more than financial arguments alone.

**Problem: You hired based on this output, but the person is not performing as expected**
Solution: The prompt designs for the role archetype, not a specific candidate's actual capabilities. Rerun the Advanced Version with your actual hire's background listed under "current team," then regenerate the next hiring priority with that constraint factored in. Also revisit whether the red flags section surfaced anything during interviews that was rationalized away — it usually did.
