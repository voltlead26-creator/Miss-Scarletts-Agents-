## Success Metrics

**Transformation health indicators to track weekly:**
- AI tool active usage rate: % of team logging in to AI tools daily (target: 80%+ by Day 60)
- Output velocity: content pieces, campaigns, reports produced per FTE per week (target: +40% by Day 90)
- Quality score: internal QA pass rate on AI-assisted work (target: maintain >90% quality bar)
- Team engagement: pulse survey eNPS (target: hold or improve vs. pre-transformation baseline)
- Voluntary attrition: 0 unplanned departures of "stay" roles during 90-day window
- Time-to-proficiency: Days for redesigned roles to reach pre-transformation output quality (target: <30 days)
- Cost per pipeline dollar: marketing cost as % of pipeline generated (target: -15% in 6 months post-transformation)

**Green/yellow/red thresholds:**
- Green: All metrics on track, team adoption strong, no surprise departures
- Yellow: 1-2 metrics lagging; trigger manager 1:1 check-ins and coaching sprint
- Red: >2 metrics off-track, or a key retention risk materializes; escalate to CEO and pause expansion of AI scope until stabilized

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The team is "complying but not committing" — they're using AI tools but only when required, and output quality has actually declined.**
Solution: You've hit Kotter's Step 5 failure mode — removing obstacles but not empowering action. Run a "prompt quality sprint" where the team spends 2 hours together comparing AI output vs. their manual work. Create a peer-learning Slack thread where wins and discoveries are shared daily. People adopt when they see colleagues succeed, not when they're mandated. Also audit whether the AI tools being used match the actual tasks — poor tool-task fit kills adoption.

**Problem: Your best performer is threatening to leave because they feel their expertise is being devalued by AI.**
Solution: This is an ADKAR "Desire" failure — they haven't connected their professional identity to the AI-augmented future. Schedule a 1:1 using these exact questions: "What parts of your work are you proudest of — the parts no AI could replicate?" Then explicitly redesign their role to concentrate on those areas. Give them ownership of the AI prompt library or quality standards for their function. People need to feel like the architect of AI, not replaced by it.

**Problem: The CMO/CEO is questioning the transformation midway through because early metrics show output dip before improvement.**
Solution: This is the J-curve of any transformation — output dips before it climbs. Prepare a "transformation lag" narrative in advance: "In weeks 1-6, we're rebuilding workflows and training the team. Pipeline impact will show in weeks 8-16." Use the success metrics dashboard to show leading indicators (adoption rates, prompt quality) even when lagging indicators (pipeline contribution) haven't moved yet. Frame it as investing in a force multiplier, not experiencing a performance problem.
