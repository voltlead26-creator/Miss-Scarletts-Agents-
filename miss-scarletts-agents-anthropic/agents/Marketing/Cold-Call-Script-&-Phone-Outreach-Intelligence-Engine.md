Tone: [Consultative / Challenger / Peer-to-peer]

1. GATEKEEPER SCRIPT — 3 variations to get past an EA or front desk without being deceptive (20 words max each)
2. OPENING HOOK — A 10-second permission-based opener that acknowledges you're interrupting and earns 30 more seconds. Zero "How are you today?" or "Did I catch you at a bad time?"
3. BRIDGE STATEMENT — 2 sentences connecting the trigger signal to a pain they feel right now
4. DISCOVERY QUESTIONS — 4 questions in SPIN order (Situation → Problem → Implication → Need-Payoff) specific to this persona
5. MINI PITCH — 30-second value proposition using the "We help [X] achieve [Y] by [Z], and here's how we did it for [similar company]" formula
6. OBJECTION HANDLING — Word-for-word rebuttals for: "Send me an email," "We're not interested," "We already have a solution," "Call me next quarter"
7. VOICEMAIL SCRIPT — 20-second max, includes one specific hook tied to the trigger, ends with a reason to call back (not just "call me back")
8. CLOSE — Two close variations: a direct calendar ask and a lower-commitment "next step" close for resistant prospects
9. POST-CALL FOLLOW-UP EMAIL — Subject line + 5-sentence email to send within 5 minutes of hanging up, references something specific from the call

Output each section with a header and ready-to-read-aloud script. No filler, no fluff.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Reps are getting hung up on within 5 seconds of the opener.**
Solution: The opening hook is almost always the culprit. Audit the first 10 words of your opener — if it contains "I'm calling because," "I wanted to reach out," or leads with your company name before establishing relevance, rewrite using the permission-based format in Section 3. Gong data consistently shows that openers leading with the prospect's trigger signal (not the rep's product) increase talk time by 40% before the 30-second mark.

**Problem: Discovery is turning into a one-sided pitch — the prospect stops talking after the first question.**
Solution: Your discovery questions are too complex or compound (two questions in one). Simplify each question to a single sentence under 12 words. After asking, add a silent pause of 3-5 seconds even if it feels uncomfortable — Gong research shows the best reps wait an average of 2.8 seconds after asking a question, creating space for the prospect to give a longer, more revealing answer. Also check: are you asking Situation questions before Problem questions? Reversing the SPIN order collapses conversational trust.

**Problem: Voicemail callback rate is under 1%, so reps are skipping voicemails entirely.**
Solution: If voicemails aren't generating callbacks, the hook is generic. The trigger signal must be hyper-specific — "you posted 4 AE jobs" outperforms "you're growing your team" by 3-5x in callback rate. Run A/B tests across two voicemail scripts for the same account segment over a 30-day period and measure callbacks in your CRM. Also ensure reps leave the voicemail simultaneously with the companion email — the multi-channel moment (voicemail + matching subject line) is what drives opens and callbacks, not either channel alone.
