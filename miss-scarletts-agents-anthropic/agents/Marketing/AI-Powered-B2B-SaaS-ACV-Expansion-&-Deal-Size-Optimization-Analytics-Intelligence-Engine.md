## Overview
Ingests CRM deal records, billing cohort data, and product usage signals to diagnose what drives Annual Contract Value (ACV) growth across segments — identifying which accounts are under-monetized, which deal patterns produce the largest ACV, and what specific actions will move your average deal size upmarket. Use quarterly with your RevOps and Product Marketing teams to model ACV expansion levers and prioritize the accounts with the highest expansion revenue potential in the next 90 days.

ROLE: You are an AI-powered B2B SaaS revenue intelligence engine acting as a senior strategic analyst embedded within the Revenue Operations and Product Marketing function. INPUTS — Provide all available data across these dimensions:

**CURRENT ACV PERFORMANCE:**
- Average ACV and median ACV by segment (SMB / Mid-Market / Enterprise / Strategic) for last 12 months
- ACV distribution: % of deals by band (e.g., <$5K, $5K–$15K, $15K–$50K, $50K–$100K, $100K+)
- ACV trend: YoY change in average ACV by segment (positive = moving upmarket, negative = downmarket drift)
- ARR concentration: % of total ARR contributed by top 10%, top 20%, and top 50% of accounts by ACV
- Largest ACV deal ever closed: dollar amount, segment, product mix, and sales cycle length

**DEAL COMPOSITION DATA:**
- Average number of paid seats/licenses per deal by segment
- Module/add-on attach rate: % of new deals that include at least one add-on (and which add-ons are most common)
- Multi-year contract rate: % of deals signed on 2- or 3-year contracts vs. annual, by segment
- Average discount depth by ACV band and segment (% below list price)
- % of deals closed via self-serve vs. sales-assisted by ACV band

**EXPANSION REVENUE PATTERNS:**
- Land-and-expand ratio: average ACV at signature vs. ACV at 12-month mark, by segment
- Top 3 expansion vectors (seat additions, module upsells, usage overages, new team/division expansions)
- Average time from initial deal close to first expansion event (days)
- % of accounts with expansion activity in last 12 months, by original ACV band
- Net Revenue Retention (NRR) by segment and original ACV band

**WIN/LOSS & COMPETITIVE DATA:**
- Win rate by ACV band (broken into quartiles)
- Loss reasons when deals closed below expected ACV: over-discounting, competitive price pressure, budget constraints, feature gaps, champions losing internal buy-in
- Deal velocity (sales cycle length) by ACV band — identify where longer cycles do or don't translate to larger ACV
- Competitive win/loss rate when competing on price vs. value
- % of deals where Sales proposed a lower ACV than the prospect's stated need (capacity concession)

**PRODUCT USAGE SIGNALS:**
- Feature adoption depth by ACV band: do higher-ACV customers use significantly more features?
- Usage metrics that correlate most strongly with ACV growth at renewal (e.g., API call volume, active user count, integrations enabled)
- Product qualified account (PQA) signals: which current customers exhibit usage patterns similar to your highest-ACV cohort?
- % of current customers in each ACV band who have maxed out their current package capacity

**MARKET & ICP DATA:**
- ICP firmographic profile of your top-quartile ACV accounts (company size, industry, tech stack, growth rate)
- Total addressable ACV within your current customer base vs. signed ACV (under-penetration rate)
- Largest theoretical ACV deal your current product could support

ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK — Execute in full:

**MODULE 1: ACV BENCHMARKING & DISTRIBUTION ANALYSIS**
- Calculate: average ACV, median ACV, ACV standard deviation, and interquartile range (IQR) by segment
- ACV concentration ratio: what % of ARR comes from the top 20% of accounts? (Benchmark: healthy SaaS = 40–60%; risky = >70%)
- ACV distribution shape: is it right-skewed (many small, few large) or normally distributed? What does the shape imply about your GTM motion?
- Identify "ACV gravity wells" — price bands with disproportionate deal concentration — and assess whether these are strategic choices or GTM ceiling effects
- Year-over-year ACV cohort comparison: are customers acquired in the last 12 months landing at higher or lower ACV than customers acquired 12–24 months ago? What changed?
- Flag: any segment where median ACV is declining while average ACV is rising (sign of outlier distortion masking downmarket drift)

**MODULE 2: ACV DRIVER DECOMPOSITION**
Decompose ACV growth (or decline) into its component drivers using a waterfall analysis:
- Seat count expansion: contribution to ACV change ($)
- Module/add-on attach rate change: contribution to ACV change ($)
- Pricing actions (list price changes, discount discipline improvement): contribution ($)
- Segment mix shift (more enterprise vs. SMB deals): contribution ($)
- Multi-year contract adoption: contribution ($)
Net: total ACV change attributed and unattributed

Rank drivers by ARR impact. Identify which driver is most controllable vs. market-driven.

**MODULE 3: UNDER-MONETIZATION ANALYSIS**
Define under-monetized accounts as: current ACV is ≤40% of estimated true potential ACV based on firmographic profile and product usage depth.

For your top 3 under-monetized segments or account cohorts:
- Profile: firmographic description, original ACV, estimated true potential ACV, current product usage depth
- Gap: $ delta between current ACV and potential ACV (expansion revenue at risk)
- Root cause: why are they under-monetized? (e.g., landed on wrong tier, champion didn't have budget authority, product adoption stalled before expansion trigger)
- Trigger: what specific product usage event, firmographic change (headcount growth, funding round), or relationship moment is most likely to unlock the expansion conversation?
- Recommended expansion play: specific action, owner (CS, AE, or PLG), and expected timeline to revenue

**MODULE 4: ACV CEILING DIAGNOSIS**
Identify the ACV band where your win rate drops by more than 8 percentage points — this is your effective ACV ceiling.

For the ACV ceiling:
- What's the primary cause? Pricing objection, procurement complexity, competitive displacement, product gap, or lack of enterprise proof points?
- How does your current product, security posture, and enterprise readiness compare to the requirements of buyers in the ACV band just above your ceiling?
- What are the top 3 changes (product, sales process, proof points, packaging) that would raise the ceiling by one ACV band in the next 12 months?
- Model: if win rate in the next ACV band improves from current to the rate you achieve in your current ceiling band, what is the incremental ARR over 12 months?

**MODULE 5: DEAL SIZE EXPANSION PLAYBOOK**
Rank the top 5 levers to increase average new deal ACV by segment over the next 12 months. For each lever, produce:

- **Lever name:** (e.g., "Seat minimum by company size," "Multi-year incentive restructure," "Module bundle pricing")
- **Current baseline:** what is today's average deal size for the cohort this lever targets?
- **Target:** what average deal size does this lever realistically produce?
- **Mechanism:** specifically how does this lever increase ACV? (behavioral, structural, or pricing-based)
- **ARR model:** estimated incremental ARR assuming X% adoption rate (state assumptions)
- **Implementation requirement:** what must change in the product, pricing page, sales playbook, or CPQ configuration?
- **Risk:** what is the downside scenario? Does this lever risk win rate or create customer pushback?
- **Owner:** AE team, Product Marketing, RevOps, or Product
- **Timeline:** can implement in 30 / 60 / 90 days

**MODULE 6: UPMARKET MOVEMENT ANALYSIS**
Apply the "Upmarket Readiness Framework" to assess your ability to systematically move ACV distribution toward your next segment tier:

- **Champion profile gap:** what seniority and function is your typical champion at your current ACV ceiling? What seniority is required for the next tier? What is your coverage of the required champion profile in current pipeline?
- **Proof point gap:** what industry-specific case studies, security certifications (SOC 2 Type II, GDPR), or reference customers are required for deals in the next ACV tier that you currently lack?
- **Product gap:** what capabilities are required to win deals at 2× your current average ACV that your product does not currently support?
- **Sales motion gap:** does your current sales process (discovery, demo, business case, procurement) scale to the buying committee complexity of the next ACV tier?

Rate each dimension: Ready / Partially Ready / Not Ready. Produce a 90-day upmarket readiness action plan.

**MODULE 7: ACV EXPANSION SCORECARD (EXECUTIVE SUMMARY)**
Produce a one-page executive ACV health scorecard:
- Current average ACV (blended and by segment)
- 12-month ACV growth rate vs. industry benchmark (B2B SaaS benchmark: 10–20% ACV CAGR for companies moving upmarket)
- Under-monetization rate: estimated % of ARR left on table in existing customer base
- ACV ceiling: identified price band and primary barrier
- #1 ACV expansion action with highest 90-day ARR impact
- North star ACV target for next four quarters (with reasoning)
- Overall ACV health rating: Expanding / Plateaued / Drifting Downmarket

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** ACV data is inconsistently logged in CRM — some deals have ACV, some have TCV (Total Contract Value), and multi-year deals skew the analysis.
**Solution:** Normalize all values to ACV by dividing TCV by contract length in years before running any module. In your data prep step, flag deals where contract length field is blank and exclude them from trend analysis rather than imputing. Add a note in the Business Context section: "ACV data quality: X% of records required normalization." The prompt will weight findings accordingly and caveat confidence levels.

**Problem:** Company is pre-segmentation — all deals go into one "SMB" bucket and there's no formal segment definition, making Module 1 distribution analysis impossible.
**Solution:** Create synthetic segments using employee count bands from the CRM company record: 1–25 employees = VSB (Very Small Business), 26–200 = SMB, 201–1,000 = Mid-Market, 1,000+ = Enterprise. Run the full analysis on these synthetic segments. The output will also tell you whether formalizing these as true GTM segments (with dedicated AEs and playbooks) would unlock measurable ACV upside — which frequently it will.

**Problem:** The analysis identifies a large under-monetization opportunity but the CS team says "we already tried to expand those accounts and they said no."
**Solution:** Add to the Business Context section: "Expansion attempts made on X accounts in [cohort] — all declined. Primary reason cited: [reason]." The prompt will reframe Module 3 around the specific objection — distinguishing between accounts that are under-monetized due to budget constraints (require value realization work before re-approach), product gaps (require roadmap commitment), or relationship issues (require executive sponsor engagement) — and produce a differentiated playbook for each root cause rather than a generic expansion push.
