## Overview
Transforms thought leadership programs from "trust us, it works" anecdotes into a rigorous, executive-ready pipeline influence measurement system — proving precisely how TL content, executive speaking, and intellectual authority move deals, shorten sales cycles, and generate pipeline that traditional attribution models miss. Use this when your board is questioning TL investment, when you need to defend the content budget, or when you want to build the infrastructure to continuously optimize where you invest in intellectual authority.

3a. DEFINING TL-INFLUENCED PIPELINE — EXACT CRITERIA
A closed-won deal qualifies as "TL-Influenced" if ANY of the following criteria are met before the opportunity reaches Stage 3 (Technical Evaluation / Security Review):
- At least one buying group member has a TL Engagement Score ≥ 25 in CRM
- A TL asset was shared by the champion to additional internal stakeholders (documented by AE in Salesforce TL Assets in Deal field)
- The prospect cited TL content as a reason for responding to outbound or submitting an inbound request (captured in intake form or first call notes)
- Gong analysis detected ≥1 unprompted TL content mention in any recorded call during the deal
- The account was in a TL-triggered intent-based outbound sequence that generated the first meeting

WEIGHTING MODEL (for TL Influence % on each deal):
Assign influence weights based on when TL engagement occurred and its depth:
- TL consumed pre-opportunity creation (demand creation): 35% influence weight
- TL consumed at discovery or qualification stage: 25% influence weight
- TL consumed at evaluation stage: 20% influence weight
- TL consumed at negotiation or late-stage closing: 10% influence weight
- TL-triggered inbound (prospect raised hand via TL channel): 100% sourced pipeline (separate from influenced)

Use the highest applicable weight per deal — do not stack weights. A deal is never more than 100% influenced.

QUARTERLY CALCULATION SPREADSHEET (Google Sheets structure):
Column A: Opportunity Name | Column B: Closed-Won ARR | Column C: TL-Influenced (Y/N) | Column D: TL Influence Weight % | Column E: TL-Influenced ARR (B × D) | Column F: TL-Sourced (Y/N) | Column G: TL Channel That Sourced | Column H: TL Assets in Deal | Column I: Gong TL Mentions

Summary row calculations:
- Total TL-Influenced ARR: =SUMIF(C:C,"Yes",E:E)
- TL Influence Rate: Total TL-Influenced ARR ÷ Total Closed-Won ARR
- TL-Sourced Pipeline: =SUMIF(F:F,"Yes",B:B)
- TL Investment-to-Influence Ratio: Total TL-Influenced ARR ÷ Quarterly TL Investment

Report this comparison in the CMO dashboard as a bar chart with percentage deltas labeled. A statistically meaningful TL velocity lift is ≥15% faster close time for Cohort A.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The CFO rejects the TL influence methodology, saying "that's correlation, not causation — you can't prove TL caused those deals."
**Solution:** Acknowledge the objection directly and propose an incrementality test rather than arguing attribution philosophy. Design a 90-day holdout: run the TL program as normal for 50% of target accounts (randomly selected) and pause all TL touchpoints for the other 50%. Compare pipeline generation rate, deal velocity, and win rate between cohorts at 90 days. This is the only method that satisfies a causal standard and will resolve the CFO's objection with data rather than argument. In the meantime, present TL data with explicit confidence labels: "High-confidence TL contribution" (inbound from TL channel + documented by prospect) and "Directional TL influence" (TL engagement in buying group with no self-report) — never present both as the same certainty level.

**Problem:** The tagging protocol breaks down within 60 days because AEs are not logging TL mentions in Salesforce and UTMs are inconsistent across the content team.
**Solution:** Remove human dependency from every measurement step where automation is possible. (1) Deploy a shared UTM Builder tool — a Google Sheet or Notion database where content team members input asset details and the UTM populates automatically; no manual UTM creation ever. (2) Replace AE manual logging with Gong automation — Gong detects TL mentions and writes the count to the Salesforce TL Mention Count field via the native Gong-Salesforce sync; AEs only confirm, they do not initiate. (3) Reduce the manual Opportunity TL field to one free-text field ("TL Assets Referenced in Deal") and make it optional — enforce only the auto-calculated fields. Have marketing ops run a monthly 15-minute data audit covering the 10 most recently closed deals: manually verify whether their buying group contacts engaged with TL content that was or was not captured. The audit catches systematic tagging failures before they corrupt a full quarter of data.

**Problem:** The TL Influence Rate is below 10% after 90 days of active measurement, making the program appear ineffective to leadership.
**Solution:** A low early influence rate is almost always a data capture failure, not a program effectiveness failure. Run this 4-step diagnostic: (1) Manually audit the 15 most recent closed-won deals — did any buying group contacts engage with TL content that is not reflected in their HubSpot TL Engagement Score? If yes, the HubSpot-Salesforce data sync is broken. Fix the integration before re-running the calculation. (2) Test all TL asset URLs: are UTM parameters stripping on your CMS or landing page redirects? A UTM strip on report download pages is the single most common cause of missing TL engagement data. (3) Review the TL Engagement Score threshold — if it is set at 25 but most contacts who engage with TL assets score 12-18 (indicating they read one article but did not download the flagship report), lower the threshold to 15 and recalculate. (4) If data capture is confirmed working and influence rate is still below 10% after 120 days, the program has a distribution problem: TL content is not reaching known CRM contacts during active buying cycles. Implement the HubSpot automation from the Integration Tips section — when a contact in an open deal has zero TL engagement, trigger an AE notification to directly share one relevant TL piece as part of their next touchpoint.
