## Overview

This prompt architects a systematic, AI-orchestrated expert interview series program that transforms practitioner conversations into a high-authority content engine — producing multi-format pipeline-generating assets from each interview while building category authority over time. Use it when launching a new thought leadership content program, when existing content lacks original expert perspectives, or when your team needs to differentiate in a market flooded with AI-generated generic content.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Guests commit to an episode but cancel or ghost before recording.**
Solution: Implement a "pre-commitment" protocol — send the top 5 interview questions immediately after booking to increase their psychological investment in the session. Require a calendar confirmation (Google Calendar or Calendly with a 48-hour reminder). If a Tier 1 guest cancels once, offer to reschedule once; if they cancel again, deprioritize and replace with a warm Tier 2 guest rather than waiting.

**Problem: Episodes are being recorded but content assets aren't getting made — production bottlenecks.**
Solution: Separate "creation" from "editing." Use the AI prompt templates in Section 4 immediately after the transcript is ready — generate all 10 assets in one AI session before your editor touches them. Your editor's only job is quality QA and voice refinement, not creation. Budget 3 hours per episode maximum for the entire post-production workflow.

**Problem: The series is getting listens/reads but isn't generating pipeline — content consumers aren't converting.**
Solution: Audit the CTA architecture. Most B2B series fail at this point because they use "read our blog" or "follow us" as CTAs instead of genuinely useful mid-funnel conversion points. Replace all content CTAs with: (1) "Get the full season transcript as a PDF guide" (email capture with high intent signal), (2) "Join 800 RevOps leaders in our bi-weekly office hours" (community + pipeline motion), or (3) "See how [Company] automates what this episode describes" (product-aware bridge). Test each CTA format for 4 episodes before drawing conclusions.
