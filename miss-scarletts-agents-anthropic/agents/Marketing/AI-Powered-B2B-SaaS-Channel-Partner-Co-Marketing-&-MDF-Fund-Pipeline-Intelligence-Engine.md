## Overview
Channel partner co-marketing is the systematic practice of deploying Marketing Development Funds (MDF), joint campaign assets, and demand generation programs through resellers, value-added resellers (VARs), distributors, and system integrators (SIs) to generate partner-sourced pipeline at scale. This engine designs your full partner co-marketing program — from tiered fund allocation and campaign playbooks to joint campaign execution workflows, partner enablement content, and closed-loop revenue attribution. Use it when you have an established reseller/SI network generating less than 20% of your pipeline, when MDF spend lacks trackable ROI, or when you're launching a structured channel program for the first time.

## Integration Tips

**Salesforce:**
- Create a custom "Partner" lookup field on Opportunity object linked to an Account record type = "Partner"
- Build a "Channel Marketing Dashboard" report type: Partner as row, MDF Spent + Pipeline Created + Pipeline Multiple as columns
- Use Campaign Hierarchy: Parent = "Channel Co-Marketing Q[X] [Year]" → Child = "[Partner Name] — [Campaign Type] — [Date]"
- Enable "Campaign Influence" (Customizable Attribution) to track partner-influenced deals separate from partner-sourced

**HubSpot:**
- Use "Original Source Drill-Down 1" = "Channel Partner" and "Drill-Down 2" = [Partner Name]
- Create a Partner company association on every partner-sourced contact record
- Build a custom "MDF Program" property on the Contact and Deal objects
- Use Workflows to auto-assign partner-sourced leads to the correct partner in your PRM

**Impartner / Allbound / Salesforce PRM:**
- Create a campaign module where partners submit MDF requests, upload campaign assets, and submit proof of performance — all integrated with your main CRM
- Enable "market development fund" module with automatic tier-based fund visibility
- Auto-send weekly pipeline-from-MDF report to each partner's channel manager

**Google Sheets (for teams without PRM):**
- Tab 1: Partner MDF Tracker (partner name, tier, total allocation, claimed, remaining, pipeline generated)
- Tab 2: Campaign Log (campaign name, partner, start date, MDF amount, lead count, SQL count, pipeline $)
- Tab 3: ROI Dashboard (auto-calculate MDF-to-pipeline multiple by partner and campaign type)
- Share with partners so they see their own pipeline contribution in real-time (creates healthy competition)

**Zapier / Make:**
- Auto-create a HubSpot/Salesforce campaign record when a partner submits an MDF request via Typeform
- Trigger Slack alert to channel marketing manager when MDF proof-of-performance is submitted
- Auto-send "campaign launch" email to partner when MDF is approved with asset download links

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partners request MDF but never execute campaigns**
Solution: Implement a "use it or lose it" policy — MDF funds expire 90 days after approval if no campaign launch is confirmed. Add a required "Campaign Kick-Off Call" within 14 days of approval where your channel marketing manager reviews the playbook step-by-step with the partner contact. Track MDF utilization rate by partner and deprioritize partners below 50% utilization in next-quarter tier reviews.

**Problem: Partners run campaigns but leads don't show up in CRM or aren't trackable**
Solution: Make UTM parameter setup and CRM tagging a prerequisite for MDF approval — not an afterthought. Provide partners a "UTM Builder" Google Sheet pre-loaded with their partner ID, approved campaign codes, and CRM field values to paste. Require a 15-minute "attribution setup call" before any campaign goes live. Add a 10-day post-campaign check-in to confirm leads were uploaded to CRM with correct source tagging.

**Problem: MDF programs keep generating MQLs but SQLs and pipeline are low**
Solution: The list quality problem — partners are promoting to their entire customer base, not filtering for your ICP. Add an ICP Filter Requirement to all MDF approvals: partners must provide a list of target attendees/recipients filtered to companies matching your size, industry, and title criteria. For webinars, require pre-registration survey with company size and job title to screen post-event; only promote to qualified registrants in follow-up. Review partner list sources — if they're using broad industry association lists instead of their own client/prospect contacts, the campaign will underperform.
