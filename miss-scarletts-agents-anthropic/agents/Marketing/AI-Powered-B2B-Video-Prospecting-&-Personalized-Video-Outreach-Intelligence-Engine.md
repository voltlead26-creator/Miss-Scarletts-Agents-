## Integration Tips

- **Vidyard:** Use Vidyard's HubSpot/Salesforce integration to auto-log video views as contact activity. Set up playback notifications to trigger same-day follow-up from SDR when prospect views >50% of video.
- **Outreach/Salesloft:** Build the 6-touch sequence as a template with variable fields populated from Salesforce custom fields (trigger event, company name, trigger source). Tag all video steps so you can A/B report by step type.
- **Apollo.io:** Use Apollo's intent signals and job change alerts as trigger inputs — pipe directly into the personalization variables table above.
- **Loom:** For Loom users, enable the "Loom in Gmail" extension so video thumbnails are embedded natively in Gmail compose without requiring an external landing page click.
- **Zapier/Make:** Build a trigger: when a contact is tagged "video-outreach-ready" in HubSpot CRM → auto-create a Loom recording task in Notion with pre-populated script fields from the HubSpot contact record.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Use "Save leads" alerts on target accounts to get notified of trigger events (new hire, LinkedIn activity) and route directly into this video prospecting workflow within 24 hours of the signal.
- **Gong/Chorus:** Mine Gong calls from closed-won deals to identify the exact language, pain framing, and objection patterns that resonated with each ICP — feed these into the INSIGHT section of the script.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: SDRs say recording videos feels awkward and they avoid doing it.**
Solution: Reduce the activation energy. Record all videos in one sitting per vertical segment — batch 20 videos in 90 minutes using the personalization variables table. The first 3 videos feel weird; by video 8, it's natural. Create a "swipe file" of your top 5 performing video scripts so reps can model their delivery.

**Problem: Video view rates are below 20% — prospects aren't clicking play.**
Solution: The thumbnail and subject line are the conversion bottleneck, not the video itself. Test: (1) replace static thumbnail with an animated GIF of the first 3 seconds, (2) add the prospect's company URL or name overlaid on the thumbnail to signal personalization before they click, (3) A/B test "Video inside" vs a plain-text subject line — sometimes de-signaling the video format increases curiosity opens.

**Problem: High view rate but low reply rate — prospects watch but don't respond.**
Solution: Your offer is too vague or your ask is too high-friction. Audit the last 10 seconds of your script: Are you asking for 30 minutes or 15? Are you offering two specific times or an open-ended "let me know when you're free"? Replace any open-ended CTA with two specific calendar slots. Also check: is the value-add in the PS line compelling enough to prompt a reply even from someone not ready to book?
