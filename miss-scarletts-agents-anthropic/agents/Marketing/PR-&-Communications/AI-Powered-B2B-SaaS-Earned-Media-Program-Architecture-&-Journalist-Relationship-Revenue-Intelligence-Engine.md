## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Pitches are being ignored by Tier 1 journalists**
*Fix:* The most common cause is pitching a product feature as news. Journalists cover market shifts, human stories, and data surprises — not your product. Audit your last 5 pitches: does each one have a data hook, a contrarian angle, and a "so what for readers" answer in the first sentence? If not, reframe the angle before re-pitching. Also check your pitch length — anything over 200 words gets skimmed. And never pitch on Monday morning or Friday afternoon.

**Problem: Getting coverage but seeing no pipeline impact**
*Fix:* Coverage without conversion infrastructure is a brand exercise, not a revenue activity. Verify three things: (1) your UTM parameters are correctly implemented in your press release and linked content, (2) your website has a dedicated "As Seen In" trust bar prominently featuring logos, and (3) your retargeting audiences include people who visited from earned media referral domains. If all three are in place and still no pipeline, the coverage is reaching the wrong audience — audit which publications are driving traffic and whether those readers match your ICP.

**Problem: Journalists covering competitors but not us**
*Fix:* Pull the last 10 articles covering your top competitor and identify the common thread — is it a specific journalist who has become their de facto reporter? Is it a particular angle (funding, customer, research)? Is the competitor offering exclusives while you're doing broad distribution? The fastest fix is to offer the journalist who covers competitors most frequently an exclusive briefing — not a pitch, a briefing — on a story they haven't seen yet. Lead with their interests, not yours.
