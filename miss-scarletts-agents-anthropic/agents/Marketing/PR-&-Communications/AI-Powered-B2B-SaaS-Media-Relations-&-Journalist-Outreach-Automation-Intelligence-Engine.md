## PHASE 4: COVERAGE QUALITY SCORING ENGINE

For every piece of coverage earned, score across four dimensions to calculate a Coverage Quality Score (CQS):

**Dimension 1 — Reach Score (1-5)**
5 = Tier 1 publication, 1M+ monthly readers (TechCrunch, Forbes, WSJ)
4 = Strong vertical trade pub, 100K–1M readers
3 = Niche vertical publication, 20K–100K readers
2 = Newsletter or podcast, 5K–20K subscribers
1 = Blog, fringe outlet, or syndicated repost only

**Dimension 2 — Message Accuracy Score (1-5)**
5 = Headline + lede match your ideal story exactly; 3+ key messages pulled through verbatim
4 = Core message accurate; 1-2 key messages included accurately
3 = Story directionally correct but misses primary differentiation
2 = Story is neutral or vaguely accurate; no key messages
1 = Story is inaccurate, misleading, or negative in framing

**Dimension 3 — SEO & Link Value Score (1-5)**
5 = Followed backlink from DA 80+ domain with keyword-relevant anchor text
4 = Followed backlink, DA 60–80
3 = No-follow backlink or DA 40–60 with link
2 = Brand mention without link on DA 60+ domain
1 = No link, no brand mention, or mention on DA <40 domain

**Dimension 4 — Pipeline Influence Score (1-5)**
5 = Direct attribution: UTM-tracked traffic spike + form fills referencing coverage + sales-confirmed deal influence
4 = Two of the three pipeline signals above
3 = UTM traffic spike only OR anecdotal "saw you in [outlet]" inbound
2 = No measurable attribution but brand plausibility in target accounts
1 = No measurable pipeline influence

**Coverage Quality Score (CQS) Formula:**
CQS = (Reach + Message Accuracy + SEO + Pipeline Influence) / 20 × 100

Target benchmarks:
- Tier 1 placement: CQS > 75
- Tier 2 trade placement: CQS > 55
- Tier 3 newsletter/podcast: CQS > 45
- Any placement below 35: analyze what went wrong and brief journalist proactively

## PHASE 5: JOURNALIST RELATIONSHIP CRM ARCHITECTURE

Track journalist relationships as a revenue-generating asset using a five-stage pipeline model:

**Relationship Stages:**
- Cold: On media list; never interacted; researched but not contacted
- Aware: Opened your pitch OR replied once (even a decline)
- Warm: Has covered you OR participated in a background briefing
- Active: Recurring coverage (2+ stories) OR on editorial advisory / briefing list
- Champion: Proactively includes you in roundups without being pitched; introduces you to colleagues

**Activation Playbook by Stage:**
Cold → Aware: Hyper-personalized pitch with exclusive data or access hook
Aware → Warm: Offer a no-pitch data briefing — pure value, no announcement required
Warm → Active: Quarterly executive availability for background briefings; early embargo access to all major releases
Active → Champion: Co-create publishable content (proprietary data they can own), warm intro to flagship customers for their own separate story, offer them a speaking slot at your user conference

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Pitches are getting opened (confirmed by read receipts or Muck Rack tracking) but no responses — journalists are ghosting after opening.
**Solution:** Your pitch is passing the subject line test but failing the WIIFR (What's In It For the Reader) test. Rewrite your opening sentence: it should state the benefit to the journalist's audience, not describe your company. Apply this rule — if you can't explain why the journalist's readers will act differently after reading this story, don't send the pitch yet. Also audit your pitch length: anything over 175 words loses a journalist in the first read. Cut ruthlessly.

**Problem:** Getting coverage but the stories consistently misrepresent your positioning or miss your key differentiator.
**Solution:** Your pre-pitch briefing materials are too sparse. Create a one-page "Story Cheat Sheet" — your ideal headline, 3 key messages in exact quote-ready language (pre-approved by legal), 2 customer quotes with permission to publish, and one data table they can reference for context. Send this alongside every pitch for Tier 1 targets. Offer post-draft factual review (not editorial review — only fact-checking). The more structured context you provide, the less room for misinterpretation when journalists write under deadline pressure.

**Problem:** Leadership keeps asking for pipeline attribution from PR, but you can't connect coverage to revenue.
**Solution:** Implement three tracking layers simultaneously: (1) Negotiate UTM-tagged URLs in every placement — most digital outlets will add your UTM if you provide the URL in your pitch assets; (2) Add "How did you hear about us?" to all demo request and contact forms with "[Outlet Name]" as a structured option, not just free text; (3) Brief SDRs to ask every new inbound lead during the first call "did you happen to see our recent coverage in [publication]?" This captures dark social and word-of-mouth credit that UTMs miss. Aggregate these three signals into a monthly earned media influence report for leadership.
