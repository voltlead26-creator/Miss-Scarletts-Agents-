## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Pitches are getting ignored or rejected by Tier 1 publications.
**Solution:** The most common reason Tier 1 contributor editors pass: (1) article reads like a press release or promotes the company's product, (2) the thesis is already covered — search for your working title before pitching, (3) the pitch is too long. Cut your pitch to 100 words and your article outline to 3 sentences. Editors make decisions in 90 seconds. If rejection continues, move to Tier 2 publications, build a track record of 3–4 published pieces, then re-approach Tier 1 with the published clips as social proof.

**Problem:** The executive's voice sounds like PR-polished corporate speak and editors say it doesn't feel authentic.
**Solution:** Interview the executive for 20 minutes using this framework: "Tell me about a time a customer told you something that changed how you think about [topic]. What do you believe about [topic] that most of your peers disagree with? What would you tell your 5-years-ago self that you know now?" Use their answers verbatim as the article's backbone. The authenticity comes from specific stories and held beliefs — not polished thesis statements. Run the draft through the "would they say this at a dinner party?" test: if the sentence would feel too formal to say out loud, rewrite it.

**Problem:** Articles are placed but generate no measurable pipeline — UTM traffic is minimal.
**Solution:** Byline programs build pipeline through compounding brand authority, not direct response — treat them as a 6–12 month investment. In the short term: (1) ensure the article includes a natural in-text link to a relevant resource (a report, a tool, a framework) — not to a product page, (2) amplify each placed article through executive's LinkedIn, employee advocacy, and email newsletter on day-of-publication, (3) add the placed article URL to executive email signature and LinkedIn profile — multiplies impressions 3–5x over first-week organic reach.
