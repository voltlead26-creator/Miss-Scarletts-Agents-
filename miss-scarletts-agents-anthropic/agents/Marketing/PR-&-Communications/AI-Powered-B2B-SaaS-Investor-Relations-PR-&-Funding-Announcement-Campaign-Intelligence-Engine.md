## Troubleshooting

**Problem: No Tier 1 outlet wants the exclusive despite a compelling offer.**
Solution: Your story angle may be too product-focused rather than market-trend-focused. Reframe the pitch as a story about a macro trend (CFO authority over AI spend, sales team productivity crisis, etc.) where your funding is proof-of-market rather than the story itself. Offer the journalist a proprietary data asset — original survey data or aggregated platform insights — as the exclusive hook rather than the announcement itself.

**Problem: A competitor announces a funding round the same week and steals your news cycle.**
Solution: Shift 40% of your pitch budget to the "second story" immediately. Brief 2-3 journalists on a feature angle that is uniquely yours: a deep product demo, access to a notable customer willing to go on record, or an op-ed from your CEO about why competitive funding validates the category. Comparative coverage that says "both companies raised, but Meridian does X differently" is still meaningful coverage.

**Problem: Your CEO is uncomfortable or inconsistent in media interviews.**
Solution: Conduct 2-3 simulated interviews (30 minutes each) in the week before the announcement, recording all sessions. Focus the first session on delivering the 3-message triangle without prompting. Focus the second on bridge techniques for hostile questions. Review recordings together and identify 1-2 specific verbal habits to eliminate. Consider limiting CEO availability to trusted journalists first and using COO or CPO for supplemental interviews.
