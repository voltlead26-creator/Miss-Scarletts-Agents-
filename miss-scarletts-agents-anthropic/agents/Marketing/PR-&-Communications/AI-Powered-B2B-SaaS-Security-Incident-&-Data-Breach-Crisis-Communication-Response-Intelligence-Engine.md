## Overview

Generates a complete, legally-structured crisis communication cascade for B2B SaaS security incidents and data breaches — customer notification emails by segment, regulatory notifications (GDPR 72-hour, CCPA, HIPAA breach notice), internal communication hierarchy, press holding statement, FAQ, and social media response — all produced simultaneously from a single incident intake. Use immediately when a security incident is confirmed or when a 72-hour regulatory clock is running.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Legal says we can't send customer notifications yet, but the 72-hour GDPR clock is running.**
Solution: Generate the regulatory notification first (Section 6) as a standalone deliverable — regulatory notifications to supervisory authorities operate on a separate obligation and timeline from customer notifications. File the DPA notice on time, and include language that "customer notifications are forthcoming pending legal review." This satisfies GDPR Article 33 independently of Article 34 customer notification requirements.

**Problem: The AI-generated emails sound too corporate and legal despite "transparent" instructions.**
Solution: Add this to your prompt: "Rewrite every sentence written in passive voice into active voice. Replace every corporate euphemism with plain language. Test each sentence against this standard: 'Would a customer read this and feel we're being honest, or would they feel we're minimizing?' If the latter, rewrite it." Then provide 2-3 sentences from your CEO's prior writing to calibrate voice.

**Problem: We don't know the full scope of the breach yet but the regulatory clock is running.**
Solution: GDPR Article 33(4) explicitly permits filing an initial notification with incomplete information, with a commitment to provide additional details in phases. Use the prompt output for Section 6 but populate all uncertain fields with "Under active investigation — update to follow by [date]" rather than leaving them blank. Supervisory authorities expect phased notifications for complex incidents.
