## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Tier 1 journalist declines exclusive or doesn't respond within 48 hours of exclusive offer**
Solution: Don't wait — immediately elevate your strongest Tier 2 journalist to Tier 1 exclusive status. A 14-day pre-launch window is long enough to pivot. The worst outcome is no exclusive, not the wrong exclusive. Send a second exclusive offer to the next journalist within 24 hours of the first decline.

**Problem: A journalist publishes before the embargo date (embargo break)**
Solution: Immediately notify all other embargoed journalists that the embargo has been lifted due to a break; this gives them permission to publish immediately and prevents them from feeling blindsided. Issue the wire release immediately (do not wait for original launch time). Alert your exclusive journalist first if they haven't yet published — offer to move their publish time up. Document the breach and address with the outlet's editorial team afterward, but launch momentum is the priority on the day.

**Problem: Coverage is thin in the first 72 hours — only trade press, no tier-1 pickup**
Solution: Activate the "customer story angle" follow-up immediately (Email 2 in Deliverable 7). Identify whether any Tier 2 journalists you briefed are still undecided — offer them the next exclusive with a unique data angle (first 500 sign-ups, first Fortune 500 customer, first integration partner outcome). Coverage builds on coverage; get 5 trade placements live and use those as social proof in follow-up journalist outreach. Remember: most B2B SaaS launches that generate 30+ placements do so over 2-3 weeks, not 72 hours.
