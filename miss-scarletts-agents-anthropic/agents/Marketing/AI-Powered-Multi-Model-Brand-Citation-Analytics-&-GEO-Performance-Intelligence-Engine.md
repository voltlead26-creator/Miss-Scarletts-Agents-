## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI models give inconsistent responses to the same query**
Solution: Run each query 3 times per model per week and use the median result, not a single data point. LLMs have inherent variability; averaging 3 runs reduces noise by ~60%. Document the response date/time to control for model updates.

**Problem: Can't attribute revenue to AI search — no UTM data from AI-cited clicks**
Solution: Use a multi-signal approach instead of UTM-only: (1) add "AI search" to your "how did you hear about us?" survey, (2) correlate AI citation rate increases with brand search lift in Google Search Console, (3) use Clearbit/6sense to identify accounts visiting after citation spikes. Triangulate across all three signals for a defensible attribution estimate.

**Problem: Competitors are cited significantly more than you despite comparable content**
Solution: Run a "citation source investigation" — for each query where they win, ask the AI model directly: "What sources informed your answer about [competitor]?" Common citation drivers: high-DA press coverage (TechCrunch, Forbes), G2/Capterra review pages, Wikipedia-style reference pages, and original data/research reports. Identify which format they're winning with and build a direct equivalent.
