## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** AI search traffic is too small to draw statistically valid conversion conclusions — only 15-20 AI-attributed sessions per week, making rate comparisons noisy.
**Solution:** Expand your attribution window to 90-day rolling instead of weekly; pool confirmed + probable AI influence using the blended model from Module 1 to increase sample size. With <50 confirmed sessions/week, focus on qualitative pipeline analysis — manually review every AI-sourced opportunity in your CRM for deal characteristics rather than relying on aggregate rates. As GEO visibility improves, sample sizes will grow — build the measurement infrastructure now so you capture the lift accurately when it arrives.

**Problem:** CRM doesn't reliably capture AI attribution — sales team isn't asking or logging the "how did you find us?" question consistently.
**Solution:** Add a mandatory single-select field "Initial Discovery Source" to the opportunity object (not the lead — that's the wrong stage) with AI model options. Train AEs in a 60-second discovery call script: "Before we dive in, quick question — what made you reach out today specifically?" Then: "Had you heard of us before, or did something point you our way recently?" This question reliably surfaces AI attribution in 15-20% of inbound deals. Pair with Gong keyword tracking to catch mentions the AE forgets to log.

**Problem:** The GEO ROI model shows positive AI pipeline numbers, but leadership is skeptical because it overlaps with SEO attribution and direct traffic — feels like double-counting.
**Solution:** Use the incrementality framework: identify a cohort of AI-attributed deals where the buyer had NO prior website visit, NO email engagement, and NO known SDR touch before the AI-referred session — this is provably incremental, not assisted. For most B2B companies, 40-60% of AI-attributed pipeline meets this "cold AI introduction" test. Present this conservative incremental subset as the iron-clad GEO attribution number; present the broader blended number as the ceiling estimate. The conservative number typically still justifies the investment.
