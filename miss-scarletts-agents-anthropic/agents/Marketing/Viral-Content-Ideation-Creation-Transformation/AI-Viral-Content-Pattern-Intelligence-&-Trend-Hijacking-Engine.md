## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The pattern analysis produces generic insights ("use storytelling," "be authentic") that don't translate into distinct formulas.
**Solution:** Feed in more specific corpus examples—include the actual text of high-performing posts, not just descriptions. The more literal the input, the more structural the output. Aim for verbatim text of at least 8 examples, all from the same channel and time period.

**Problem:** Generated content feels off-brand or reads as generic "thought leadership" noise.
**Solution:** Add 3-5 examples of content you've published that felt most "on-brand"—even if they didn't go viral—to Task 2. This anchors the brand voice guardrails. Also specify in the Advanced Version: "Do NOT use [specific phrases/styles you hate]."

**Problem:** Trend hijacking posts feel forced or opportunistic rather than authentic.
**Solution:** Use the Decision Criteria filter in the Hijacking Playbook strictly. A trend is worth hijacking only if your brand has a genuinely surprising or data-backed angle—not just a "congrats to X" post. If you can't find a contrarian or insider angle within 15 minutes of trend detection, skip it.
