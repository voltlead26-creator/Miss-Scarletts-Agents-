## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The campaign launches, gets 200 LinkedIn impressions in the first 24 hours, and then flatlines**
Solution: This is a seeding failure, not an asset failure. The LinkedIn algorithm requires an engagement velocity signal in the first 60–90 minutes of posting to decide whether to show the post to wider audiences. Fix: coordinate 8–12 colleagues to comment (not just like) within the first hour of the launch post going live. Comments must be substantive (2+ sentences, genuine reactions) — generic "great post!" comments are algorithmically deprioritized. If the post doesn't reach >50 engagements in the first 2 hours, delete and repost at a higher-traffic time (Tuesday–Thursday 8–10 AM in your target audience's timezone) with a different opening hook.

**Problem: High download numbers but almost no social sharing**
Solution: Sharing friction is too high or the share trigger is buried. Run this diagnostic: (1) Is there a clear, prominent share CTA inside the asset itself — not just at the end? Research shows 73% of shares happen while consuming content, not after finishing it. Add "Share this finding" CTAs next to the 3 most surprising stats. (2) Does sharing feel like it adds value for the sharer's audience, or does it feel like promoting your brand? Reframe share CTAs from "Share the report" to "Share this benchmark with your team." (3) Is the share mechanism frictionless? Test LinkedIn native share (pre-populated post with stat + link) vs. generic share buttons — native shares convert 4x better than blank-canvas sharing.

**Problem: Great engagement in online communities but no conversion to email subscribers or pipeline**
Solution: The community seeding is working but the landing page experience is breaking the funnel. Diagnose: (1) Is the landing page offer-matched to what was promised in the community post? If you teased "the benchmark data" in a Slack group and the landing page leads with "Download our State of RevOps Report," conversion will suffer — match the headline to the specific angle that drove the click. (2) Is the email gate form optimized for professional email addresses only? Consumer email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo) in a B2B list indicate you're reaching wrong-fit audiences from certain communities — refocus seeding effort on communities with higher professional email density. (3) Add a "No gate" version with a 3-minute teaser preview that converts skeptical community members before asking for the email address.
