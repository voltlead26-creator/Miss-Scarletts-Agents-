# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Mutual Action Plan (MAP) Architecture & Shared Deal Execution Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** mutual-action-plan, MAP, deal-execution, b2b-sales, enterprise-sales, deal-velocity, shared-success-plan, champion-enablement, pipeline-management, close-plan

## Overview
Automates the full architecture of a Mutual Action Plan (MAP) — the shared deal roadmap between seller and buyer that aligns both sides on steps, owners, timelines, and success criteria required to reach a signed contract and realized value. Use it when an opportunity has cleared discovery and entered active evaluation, to replace vague "next steps" with a structured, buyer-co-authored execution plan that dramatically compresses sales cycles, surfaces stalled deals early, and gives champions the internal ammunition to drive urgency.

   - Red flags that signal the MAP is losing traction: missed milestones, stakeholder withdrawal, competitor progress, budget freeze
   - Recovery plays for each stall type — email scripts, re-engagement calls, executive escalation paths
   - Decision: when to compress the MAP vs. when to walk away from the deal

4. NEXT STEPS EMAIL
   - Email to champion introducing the MAP with context, a link placeholder, and a request to co-edit and confirm the first 3 milestones within 48 hours

## Success Metrics

## Integration Tips

**Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:**
- Create a custom "Mutual Action Plan" object or linked task list in your CRM where each MAP milestone maps to a task with due date, owner, and status — update deal stage automatically when milestone % complete crosses 60% and 100% thresholds
- Use opportunity custom fields to track: MAP co-sign date, total milestones, completed milestones, milestone slip count — surface these in your pipeline review dashboard

**Notion / Google Docs:**
- Paste the buyer-facing MAP into a shared Notion page or Google Doc and send the champion a comment-enabled link — never send a PDF, because co-editing signals that this is a collaborative document, not a seller artifact
- Use Notion's database view to maintain a MAP tracker across all active deals with milestone status columns and automated Slack alerts on overdue items

**Slack + Zapier:**
- Set up a Zapier workflow: when a CRM task milestone is marked overdue → post a Slack notification to the deal owner with the at-risk milestone name, days past due, and a link to the recovery playbook
- Create a #map-standup Slack channel for AEs to post daily MAP status updates during active POC periods

**Google Sheets:**
- For teams not using a CRM MAP object: create a Google Sheet template with conditional formatting — milestones due in <3 days turn yellow, overdue milestones turn red
- Share the Sheet with the champion as the "live version" of the MAP and embed it in the deal Slack channel

**DocuSign / PandaDoc:**
- After the champion verbally commits to the MAP, send a formal signature request via DocuSign or PandaDoc — a co-signed MAP is a buying intent signal stronger than any verbal commitment and can be used in internal forecasting with high confidence

**Gong / Chorus:**
- After each milestone-related call, tag the recording with the MAP milestone number so your CS and AE team can review the exact moment each milestone was committed or missed
- Use Gong Alerts to flag calls where the MAP, timeline, or close date was challenged — trigger an automatic AE coaching note

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion refuses to co-sign or share the MAP with their team**
*Likely cause:* The MAP is perceived as a seller pressure tool, not a buyer protection document. The champion may also have low internal credibility or political risk in championing a new vendor.
*Fix:* Rewrite the MAP header and executive summary from the buyer's perspective. Replace any language like "seller commits to…" with "Pipecast guarantees…" and add a "Buyer Protection Clause" section that explicitly states the seller's obligations, cancellation terms if milestones are missed by the seller, and the buyer's right to pause the evaluation at any milestone. Then coach the champion to introduce it as "the framework I'm using to protect us from vendor delays" — not as a sales document.

**Problem: Milestones are slipping repeatedly and the champion stops responding**
*Likely cause:* One of two root causes — either the champion has lost internal budget/political support, or a competitor has advanced and the champion is avoiding a difficult conversation. This is not a MAP problem; it's a deal risk problem.
*Fix:* Trigger the "stall escalation" play — have your executive sponsor send a personal, brief email to the economic buyer (not the champion) acknowledging the milestone delays and requesting a 20-minute alignment call. This move surfaces the real blocker faster than waiting. If the EB also goes dark, score the deal as at-risk and initiate a formal qualification-out process within 5 business days.

**Problem: The MAP becomes outdated and the buyer stops checking it**
*Likely cause:* The MAP was built once and never updated — it became a static document instead of a live deal execution tool.
*Fix:* Establish a weekly MAP review as a standing agenda item in your champion call. Keep the document live in a shared Google Doc or Notion page and update milestone statuses in real time during the call — not after. Introduce a visual "deal health indicator" (green/yellow/red) at the top of the MAP that the champion can reference when presenting to their EB. A MAP that shows "8 of 10 milestones complete — on track for Sept 30" gives the champion a confidence-building artifact for their internal deal advocacy.
