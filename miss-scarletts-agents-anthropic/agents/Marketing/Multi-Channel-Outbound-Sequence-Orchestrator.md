# Multi-Channel Outbound Sequence Orchestrator - AI-Powered Cadence Design Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** outbound, cadence, sequencing, email, linkedin, b2b, sales-automation, sdr, prospecting, multi-channel

## Overview
Designs complete, personalized multi-touch outbound sequences across email, LinkedIn, phone, and video — including messaging, timing logic, follow-up branching, and personalization hooks per touchpoint. Use it when you need to take an ICP segment or individual account tier and produce a production-ready cadence that SDRs or AI agents can execute end-to-end in tools like Apollo, Outreach, Salesloft, or Instantly.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior B2B outbound strategist. Design a complete multi-channel outbound sequence for the following scenario.

Company: [Your Company] — we help [ICP, e.g., "VP Sales at Series B SaaS companies with 50-200 AEs"] achieve [outcome, e.g., "20% higher quota attainment"] by [solution, e.g., "giving reps real-time deal intelligence from CRM signals"].

Target persona: [Job title, seniority, company size, industry]
Trigger / reason to reach out now: [e.g., "they just raised Series B", "posted 3 AE job listings", "visiting our pricing page", "competitor just raised prices"]
Sequence goal: Book a 20-minute discovery call.
Tone: [Consultative / Direct / Challenger / Peer-to-peer]

Design a 10-touch sequence over 21 days using this channel mix: Email (5 touches), LinkedIn (3 touches — connection request, message, comment/engage), Phone (1 touch), Video (1 touch). For each touch include:
1. Day number and channel
2. Subject line or opening hook (for email/LinkedIn)
3. Full message body (under 75 words for email, under 300 characters for LinkedIn DM)
4. CTA (one specific ask — never "let me know if interested")
5. What to do if no reply before next touch (brief behavioral note)

End with: one breakup email for day 22 if no response across all touches.

Make every message feel like it came from a human who did 10 minutes of research on this specific person — not a template blast.

## Success Metrics

- Reply rate: Target 8-15% for a well-personalized sequence (industry average for generic outbound: 1-3%)
- Positive reply rate: Target 3-6% — responses that lead to a booked meeting or expressed interest
- Meeting show rate: >70% — sequences with strong context-setting pre-meeting perform significantly better
- Sequence completion rate: Track what % of prospects reach touch #10+ before going dark — low completion signals deliverability or relevance issues
- Channel attribution: Record which touch number and channel drove the reply — use this data to refine future sequences
- Breakup email response rate: A well-written breakup should pull 5-10% response even from fully cold prospects

## Integration Tips

**Apollo.io:**
- Copy each touch into Apollo's Sequences builder as a new step; set step type (Email / LinkedIn Task / Call Task / Manual Task for Loom)
- Use Apollo merge fields `{{firstName}}`, `{{companyName}}`, `{{customField.triggerSignal}}` to inject personalization at scale
- Set "auto-advance" for email touches; set "manual task" for LinkedIn and phone to preserve human review before sending

**Outreach / Salesloft:**
- Import touches as sequence steps with day offsets matching the cadence output
- Create a custom field for "Trigger Signal" and map it to the personalization hook library from Part 5
- Use Salesloft's Rhythm or Outreach's Kaia to attach call scripts directly to phone steps

**HubSpot Sequences:**
- Build as an Enrollment Sequence; use personalization tokens for the trigger variable
- Set LinkedIn steps as "To-do" tasks so reps are prompted at the right cadence without leaving HubSpot

**Zapier / Make + Clay.com:**
- Trigger: New row added to Google Sheet (account list with trigger signal column) → Action: Enroll contact in Apollo/Outreach sequence and populate the personalization field
- Layer Clay.com for automated signal enrichment before enrollment — pipe the trigger signal directly into the prompt's TRIGGER field to generate sequences at scale

**Loom for Sales:**
- Record using the Part 2 script; enable the Loom CTA button pointing to your calendar link
- Embed a Loom thumbnail as a fake video play button in email (animated GIF with play icon) to dramatically increase click-through rate on video touches

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Open rates are good (40%+) but reply rates are under 2%**
Solution: Your subject lines are working but the body is not earning a reply. The CTA is likely too vague or the value prop is buried. Move the specific outcome-oriented hook to line 1 of the email body before any context-setting. Replace the calendar link as a first ask with a yes/no micro-commitment question — it is far easier to respond to and warms the prospect for the next touch.

**Problem: LinkedIn DMs are getting ignored even after the connection is accepted**
Solution: You are messaging too quickly after connecting. Wait 24-48 hours and engage with one of their recent posts with a thoughtful comment before sending the DM. This warms the relationship and your message arrives to someone who already has you on their radar. Never send a sales pitch in the connection request note itself.

**Problem: The sequence feels generic even after filling in all the variables**
Solution: The "Trigger / Buying Signal" field is the most important personalization lever — if it is vague (e.g., "they visited our website"), the whole sequence loses specificity. Re-run the prompt with a sharper trigger: include the exact job title they posted, the exact funding amount, or the exact technology change detected. Specificity is trust.
