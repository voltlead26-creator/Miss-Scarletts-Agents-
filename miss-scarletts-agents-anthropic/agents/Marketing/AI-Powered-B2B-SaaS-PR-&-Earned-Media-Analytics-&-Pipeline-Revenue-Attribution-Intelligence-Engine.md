### 2. PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION ANALYSIS
Use the overlap method combining three attribution signals:

**Direct Attribution (highest confidence)**
- Deals with buyer-cited PR touchpoint × average ACV = directly attributed pipeline
- Confidence level: High

**Influenced Pipeline (medium confidence)**
- Accounts currently in pipeline that had a documented PR touchpoint (website visit from press referral or analyst report download before opportunity creation)
- Confidence level: Medium

**Correlated Pipeline (lower confidence, signal-based)**
- New opportunities created within 30 days of Tier 1 coverage, in the same segment targeted by that outlet's audience
- Methodology: compare opportunity creation rate in the 30 days post-coverage vs. 30-day baseline, multiply delta by ACV
- Confidence level: Low-Medium

**Dark Funnel Estimation**
- Compare demo conversion rate from PR-referred traffic vs. site average
- Apply the lift percentage to total post-coverage traffic volume to estimate untracked conversions
- Confidence level: Estimated

- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Create a custom "PR Touchpoint" property on contacts and deals. Train SDRs to log answers to "How did you first hear about us?" as a required discovery call field. Build a CRM report showing win rate for deals with vs. without a logged PR touchpoint — this becomes your standing proof of PR ROI.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Tag all outbound links in press releases with UTM parameters (utm_source=press, utm_medium=earned, utm_campaign=[outlet-slug]). - **Meltwater / Cision / Mention / Brand24**: Export weekly coverage reports with outlet DA, estimated reach, and sentiment scoring. Use their API to push data into a Google Sheet or Looker Studio dashboard that auto-calculates QAR and SOV on a rolling basis.
- **G2 / Capterra / Peer Review Platforms**: Export quarterly review volume and score trends. Cross-reference review velocity with win rate changes in deals where buyers conducted peer review research. Use G2 Buyer Intent data to identify in-market accounts researching your category — cross-reference with earned media timing to measure PR's role in triggering research.
- **Gong / Chorus / Salesloft**: Search call transcripts weekly for keywords: "read about you," "saw the article," "analyst recommended," "Gartner said," "G2 review," "Forbes article." This surfaces dark funnel PR attribution without requiring SDRs to manually log everything. Export matches to CRM automatically.
- **6sense / Demandbase / Bombora**: Use account-level intent data to identify which accounts spiked in brand research activity within 30 days of a major press placement. These are your highest-confidence dark funnel pipeline signals — route to ABM sequences immediately.
- **Looker Studio / Tableau**: Build a live PR ROI command center pulling from GA4 + CRM + coverage tool APIs. Include widgets: coverage volume by week, pipeline created by week (overlaid), SOV trend, demo conversion rate from PR-referred sessions, and a running PR ROI ratio. Present this to the board quarterly.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Buyers rarely self-report PR as a touchpoint — "How did you hear about us?" captures under 20% of deals**
Solution: Make the question structured and mandatory at the discovery call stage. Provide SDRs a drop-down in CRM with specific options: Press article, Analyst report (specify which), Peer recommendation, LinkedIn, Google search, Podcast, Other. The specificity increases completion rates by 40–60% vs. open text. Additionally, run dark funnel analysis via 6sense or Demandbase to see which accounts were actively researching your brand in the 30-day window before entering the funnel — this replaces the self-report dependency for most of your high-ACV deals.

**Problem: Correlation between coverage and pipeline spike exists, but the board wants causal proof, not correlation**
Solution: Run a controlled experiment over two quarters. Identify 100 similar accounts (same firmographic profile, same stage of awareness) and split them: 50 accounts receive a targeted PR-seeding campaign (executive outreach referencing the press, ABM sequences that reference the coverage), and 50 receive standard outreach with no PR references. Compare pipeline creation rates, sales cycle length, and win rates between groups. After two quarters, you'll have causality evidence. In parallel, use incrementality testing methodology: compare pipeline velocity in weeks with Tier 1 coverage vs. equivalent weeks without — control for seasonality using prior-year data.

**Problem: PR ROI calculation shows high pipeline influence, but when legal/finance review the methodology they push back on the assumptions**
Solution: Present a range of scenarios — conservative (direct attribution only: self-reported deals), base case (direct + correlated), and optimistic (direct + correlated + dark funnel). Label every assumption clearly. Use your company's own conversion rates and ACV rather than industry benchmarks — this makes the model defensible with internal data. Then propose a 90-day data collection sprint to tighten the methodology: mandatory "how did you hear about us?" logging, GA4 PR attribution setup, and Gong transcript mining. Frame the current report as "baseline with known methodology gaps" and commit to a more rigorous model next quarter.
