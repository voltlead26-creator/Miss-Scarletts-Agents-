## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Scripts feel too long when delivered on camera — videos run 15-20% over target duration**
Solution: Short-form scripts always read faster in your head than on camera. Apply the "word budget" rule: 30-second video = maximum 75 spoken words, 60-second video = maximum 150 words. Re-run each script through the word count and cut the bottom 20% — the CTA and one supporting point are always cuttable without losing the core message.

**Issue: Hook scores are high but completion rates are low — viewers click away after the first 5 seconds**
Solution: The hook sold a promise the body didn't deliver fast enough. Apply the "payoff by 15 seconds" rule — the most valuable insight or reveal must come within the first 15 seconds regardless of total video length. Restructure: Hook → Instant mini-payoff → Expand → Full CTA. Viewers who get value early stay to see if there's more.

**Issue: Scripts work well on TikTok but performance drops on LinkedIn Video**
Solution: LinkedIn's audience scrolls slower and rewards professional framing. Add a credibility signal in the first 5 seconds ("As a [role] who's worked with 300+ [ICP]..."), reduce slang and trend-native language, and replace entertainment-oriented CTAs with value-exchange CTAs ("Download our full analysis — link in bio" vs. "Follow for more"). LinkedIn also favors sound-off: make on-screen text carry 80% of the message without relying on audio.
