# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Podcast Episode Production & Audio Content Creation Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Intermediate | **Time:** 15 min | **Tags:** podcast, audio-content, b2b, thought-leadership, content-production, automation

## Overview
Generate a complete B2B podcast episode production kit — pre-interview research, episode outline, interview questions, intro/outro scripts, SEO show notes, social clip briefs, and promotional copy — from a single topic brief. Use this whenever you're producing a new interview episode, solo episode, or panel discussion and need all production assets ready before recording day.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a B2B SaaS podcast producer and content strategist with 10+ years creating top-ranked industry shows. Produce a complete episode production kit for the following:

SHOW: [Your Podcast Name] — a B2B SaaS podcast for [target audience, e.g., "revenue leaders at mid-market SaaS companies"]
EPISODE TOPIC: [Topic, e.g., "How AI is reducing forecasting variance for RevOps teams"]
GUEST: [Guest Name], [Title] at [Company] — [1–2 sentence professional bio]
EPISODE FORMAT: [Interview/Solo/Panel] — [30/45/60] minutes
PRIMARY LISTENER GOAL: After this episode, listeners should be able to [specific outcome]

Produce the following assets:

1. PRE-INTERVIEW RESEARCH BRIEF
   - Guest's 3 most notable professional achievements (relevant to this topic)
   - Company's recent news, funding, or product milestones
   - Guest's published content, talks, or contrarian positions you can reference
   - 2-3 areas to probe for genuine non-obvious insight
   - 1 "hot take" or assumption you can respectfully challenge on-air

2. EPISODE OUTLINE & ARC (with approximate timestamps)
   - Cold open hook — the first 60 seconds must make the listener lean in; start with a provocative stat, bold claim, or urgent problem — never start with "Welcome back"
   - Episode tension arc: problem → complication → insight → resolution
   - 5–7 segment blocks with time estimates
   - The single most memorable "aha moment" to engineer — name the exact topic and approximate placement (aim for minutes 20–30)

3. INTERVIEW QUESTIONS (12–15 total)
   - 3 warm-up questions to establish rapport and surface credibility naturally
   - 5 insight questions that probe frameworks, counterintuitive opinions, and real lessons from failure
   - 2 tactical questions delivering concrete, actionable takeaways listeners can use this week
   - 2 "edge" questions that respectfully challenge the guest's position or assumptions to create memorable tension
   - 1 closing question (legacy/advice format, e.g., "What would you tell yourself 5 years ago?")
   - For each question: note the INTENDED ANSWER TYPE in brackets: [Story] [Framework] [Data] [Opinion] [Tactical]

4. INTRO SCRIPT (verbatim, 90–120 seconds)
   - Open with the problem/tension — never with the guest's title and company
   - Bridge: why this topic matters right now for the listener
   - Guest intro: 3-sentence narrative bio (from-to career arc, one specific achievement, why this person specifically for this topic)
   - Transition line into the first question

5. OUTRO SCRIPT (verbatim, 60–90 seconds)
   - 3 punchy episode takeaways framed as listener actions
   - Guest CTA: where to connect with them + any resources mentioned
   - Show CTA: subscribe/review/share ask + one-line tease of next episode

6. SEO-OPTIMIZED SHOW NOTES (400–600 words)
   - H1: episode title including primary keyword phrase
   - Episode summary paragraph (120–150 words, naturally including 2–3 relevant long-tail keywords)
   - Timestamped chapters with descriptive topic labels
   - Resources, tools, and links mentioned during the episode (list 5–8 items)
   - Guest bio with LinkedIn URL placeholder
   - Pull quote excerpt (the single best 2–3 sentence quote from the episode)

7. SOCIAL MEDIA CLIP BRIEF (5 clips for the editor)
   - For each clip: estimated timestamp range, verbatim quote excerpt (the hook line), recommended platform (LinkedIn/Instagram Reels/TikTok/X), caption hook text (first line that stops the scroll), and content warning if any topic is sensitive

8. GUEST SHARE TEMPLATE (LinkedIn post, guest's voice)
   - 150–200 words
   - Format: bold hook line → 3 key insights from the conversation → episode link CTA
   - Written in first person from the guest's perspective, not the show's

9. EMAIL NEWSLETTER BLURB (100 words)
   - For your existing subscriber list
   - Lead with the listener benefit, not "new episode"
   - Include 1 compelling stat or quote from the episode
   - CTA: listen now link

Format all output as a structured production document with clear section headers. Make every word production-ready — copy-paste directly into a Notion doc, Google Doc, or podcast CMS.

## Integration Tips

- **Notion**: Create a Podcast Production Database with properties for Episode #, Guest, Status, Record Date. Paste each production kit into an episode page using the section headers as H1/H2 blocks. Use Notion AI to refine scripts after generation.
- **Riverside.fm / Squadcast**: Paste the pre-interview research brief and question set into the "Preparation" section of your session notes so the host has them visible on-screen during recording without the guest seeing.
- **Buzzsprout / Transistor / Podbean**: Copy the SEO show notes directly into the episode description field. Add the pull quote as the episode summary. Use H2 tags for timestamps — most modern podcast players render basic HTML.
- **HubSpot**: Create a podcast episode template in the CMS using the show notes structure. Tag contacts who engage with podcast content as `content-type:podcast` for nurture segmentation. The newsletter blurb maps directly to a HubSpot email template.
- **Buffer / Hootsuite**: Schedule the social clip briefs as a 7-day drip: Day 1 (launch day) — LinkedIn hook clip; Day 3 — tactical takeaway clip; Day 7 — best quote clip. This triples the lifespan of each episode on social.
- **Zapier workflow**: Connect your podcast RSS feed → Zapier → trigger a Slack notification with the episode URL when published → auto-post the internal announcement template to #marketing channel.
- **Google Sheets tracker**: Log each episode's performance data (downloads, clip engagement, guest share Y/N, subscriber delta) in a single sheet. After 20 episodes, feed this data back into the Advanced prompt to identify which question types and guest profiles produce the most shareable clips.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI produces generic questions that sound like every other podcast**
*Solution:* Add specificity to the "Guest's Most Relevant Content to Reference" field in the Advanced version. Include a direct quote or specific framework the guest has published and instruct the AI to either build on it or respectfully challenge it. Generic inputs produce generic questions — the more specific the guest context, the more differentiated the output. Also add: "Avoid any question that begins with 'Can you tell us about your journey' or 'What advice would you give.' These are banned phrases."

**Problem: Show notes are too keyword-stuffed and sound unnatural**
*Solution:* After generating the show notes, run a second pass with: "Review the show notes you just wrote. Remove any phrase that appears more than twice. Rewrite any sentence where the keyword feels forced. The goal is that a human reader would have no idea this was SEO-optimized — every sentence should sound like a thoughtful human wrote it." Then manually add 1–2 internal links to related episodes before publishing.

**Problem: The cold open doesn't match the actual episode content after recording**
*Solution:* Generate the production kit before recording (as designed) but regenerate only Section D (scripts) after recording using a 200-word transcript excerpt of the best moment from the actual conversation. Use this prompt add-on: "Here is the single best exchange from the recorded interview: [paste transcript excerpt]. Now rewrite the cold open to tease this specific moment, and update the 3 listener takeaways in the outro to reflect what was actually said."
