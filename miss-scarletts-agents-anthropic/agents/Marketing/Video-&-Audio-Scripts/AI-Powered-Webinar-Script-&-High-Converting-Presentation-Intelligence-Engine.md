## Overview
Generates a complete, production-ready webinar script — including slide-by-slide narrative, speaker notes, audience engagement moments, and a structured soft-close offer sequence — engineered to maximize registration-to-attendance rate, live engagement, and post-webinar pipeline conversion. Use it when building a new webinar from scratch, converting a sales deck into a value-driven educational format, or systematizing a high-performing webinar into a repeatable evergreen asset.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: The webinar script feels too scripted when delivered — presenter sounds like they're reading**
Solution: The script is a safety net, not a cage. Re-record each section as a voice memo using natural language, then use those voice memos (not the written script) as your actual delivery reference. Key lines to memorize verbatim: the hard open hook, the big promise statement, the offer bridge, and the exact CTA. Everything in between can be conversational — the written script just ensures you hit the key beats in the right order.

**Issue: Offer conversion rate is below 10% despite strong attendance and engagement**
Solution: The problem is almost always the bridge, not the offer. Audit the transition from teaching to CTA — if attendees feel whiplash between "valuable content" and "buy something," the offer feels manipulative rather than helpful. Rewrite the bridge to acknowledge the implementation challenge explicitly: "Now that you have this framework, here's where teams like yours typically get stuck..." — then position the offer as the solution to THAT specific sticking point, not as a product pitch.

**Issue: Drop-off spike at the 30-minute mark regardless of content quality**
Solution: The 25–35 minute window is when webinar fatigue peaks, especially for working professionals attending during business hours. Insert a pattern interrupt here: a live poll, a "quick exercise — grab a pen," or a case study with a surprising result. This resets attention. Also audit whether your engagement moments are concentrated in the first half — distribute them evenly across the presentation at roughly 10-minute intervals.
