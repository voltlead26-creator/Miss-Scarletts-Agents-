## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Store video URL in contact/deal record; trigger email sequence embedding video when deal reaches "Proposal Sent" stage; track link clicks as engagement signal
- **Vidyard / Loom / Wistia:** Upload final cuts; use heat map and viewer analytics to identify which 30-second segments drive the most re-watches — use those as paid social clips
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Use 30-second cuts as Sponsored Content targeting lookalike audiences of existing customers; match customer's industry as audience filter
- **Salesloft / Outreach SDR sequencing:** Embed 90-second cut in Step 3 of outbound sequences targeting same vertical as the customer featured; personalize intro with "I thought you'd relate to [Customer Company] since you're also in [Industry]"
- **Notion / Google Drive:** Build a "Proof Library" organized by industry, company size, use case, and objection addressed — link each video to its sales enablement package for instant rep access
- **Zapier automation:** When a deal is marked "Closed-Won" in CRM → auto-trigger customer success team Slack notification with testimonial request template → route to this prompt for script production
- **Google Ads / YouTube:** Run 30-second pre-roll cuts targeting in-market audiences for your product category; use customer's industry as audience refinement signal

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Customer approves the interview but freezes on camera and delivers stilted, scripted-sounding answers.**
Solution: Send the Pre-Interview Coaching Guide 48 hours before the shoot. During the shoot, never show the customer their "scripted" answers — instead ask the questions conversationally and coach them to "forget the camera and just talk to me like you're telling a colleague." Run 2-3 warm-up questions before recording. The interviewer question script is designed to elicit natural answers, not for the customer to memorize.

**Problem: The customer won't approve specific metrics (legal/compliance concern in regulated industries).**
Solution: Replace hard numbers with relative comparisons ("significantly faster," "measurably fewer errors") or time-anchored directional statements ("within 90 days, we saw improvement across every key metric"). For financial services, healthcare, or legal customers, always route the script through their comms/legal team before the shoot. Include a disclaimer slide or voiceover as needed: "Results may vary based on company size and implementation."

**Problem: The video gets great views but isn't driving pipeline or influencing deals.**
Solution: The distribution strategy is broken, not the video. Audit: Is the video being shown to in-market buyers or broad audiences? Is the CTA specific and low-friction (demo booking link, not "learn more")? Are SDRs using it in sequences targeting the same vertical as the featured customer? Run the 90-second cut as LinkedIn retargeting to website visitors who viewed your pricing page — this is the highest-intent audience for testimonial content.
