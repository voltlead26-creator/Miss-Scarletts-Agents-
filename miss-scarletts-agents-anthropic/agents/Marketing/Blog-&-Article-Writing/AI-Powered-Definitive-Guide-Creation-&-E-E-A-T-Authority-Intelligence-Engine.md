## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The guide produces excellent content but fails to rank because the target keyword is dominated by high-DA publications (Forbes, HubSpot, Salesforce) with hundreds of referring domains.
**Solution:** Shift the target to a more specific long-tail variant where the primary query expresses a more specific intent (e.g., instead of "SaaS financial model," target "SaaS financial model for Series B CFOs" or "how to build a SaaS financial model in 2025"). Use Module 1's SERP deconstruction to identify where competitor DA is lower. Alternatively, use this guide as a linked resource and instead target multiple lower-competition cluster articles that funnel authority to the definitive guide.

**Problem:** The E-E-A-T section feels generic because the author has no verifiable credentials or real-world experience data.
**Solution:** Conduct a structured customer interview specifically for this guide — 30-minute call with 3 customers using the exact questions this guide addresses. Use their specific (anonymized) numbers and decisions as the "experience" signals. This creates real, original practitioner data without requiring the author to have personal experience. Alternatively, commission a micro-survey of 100+ practitioners using Pollfish or SurveyMonkey Audience and lead with the original data.

**Problem:** Six months after publishing, the guide has organic traffic but zero measurable pipeline contribution.
**Solution:** The conversion architecture is misaligned with the search intent. Audit the CTA placement and offer: if the guide attracts early-stage researchers, a "book a demo" CTA will generate near-zero conversions. Replace with a high-value, low-commitment offer (template, calculator, checklist) that captures email addresses and triggers a longer nurture sequence. Then track how many email subscribers from the guide eventually become pipeline within 90 days.
