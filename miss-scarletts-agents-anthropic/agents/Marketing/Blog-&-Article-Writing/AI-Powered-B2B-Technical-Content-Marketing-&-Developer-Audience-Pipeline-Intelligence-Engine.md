## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Technical articles get good traffic but almost no trial signups (conversion rate under 0.5%)
**Solution:** The CTA is probably too sales-oriented for a developer audience. Replace "Book a Demo" with "Run this in your environment free" or "Get the benchmark tool." Developers want to self-serve; a 14-day free trial with full API access and no credit card converts 3-4x better than a demo request form for this audience.

**Problem:** Engineers on your team resist writing content because it feels like "marketing"
**Solution:** Reframe the ask: have them write about a technical problem they genuinely solved internally — not about your product. The product mention becomes a natural conclusion ("we built X to solve this, here's how you can use it"). This format also performs better on Hacker News because it feels authentic.

**Problem:** Articles are technically accurate but rank poorly on Google and get ignored by developers
**Solution:** Two issues — SEO and distribution. For SEO: the article title needs to match the exact search query a developer types (test with Google's autocomplete and "People Also Ask"). For distribution: you must actively seed the article in 3-5 developer communities within 48 hours of publish, or it will never gain momentum. Organic search alone takes 3-6 months to kick in.
