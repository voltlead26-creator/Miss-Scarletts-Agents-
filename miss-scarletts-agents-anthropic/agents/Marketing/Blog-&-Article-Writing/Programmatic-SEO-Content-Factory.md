## Overview
Turns a structured data set (competitors, use cases, integrations, templates, or locations) into a production-ready library of high-intent SEO pages that rank, convert, and compound over time. Use it when you need to capture thousands of long-tail searches without writing each page from scratch — the same playbook that drove Zapier, HubSpot, Canva, and G2 to millions of monthly organic visitors.

## Integration Tips
- **Google Sheets + Make/Zapier**: Maintain your programmatic data set (competitor list, use case list, etc.) in a Google Sheet. Build a Make scenario that reads each row, passes it through this prompt via an AI action, and writes the output to a staging sheet for review. A 100-page program can be drafted in under 4 hours of automated runtime.
- **Webflow CMS / Contentful**: Map the structured output from this prompt (title, meta, H1, sections, FAQ, CTA) to CMS field types. Each page becomes a CMS item — content editors can review and publish without touching code. Use Webflow's CMS API or Contentful's Content Management API to push approved pages directly from your staging sheet.
- **Ahrefs / Semrush**: Before running a large batch, export keyword volume data for your target variable list (all competitors, all integrations, etc.). Filter to include only rows with search volume above your minimum threshold (suggested: 100+ monthly searches for niche B2B, 500+ for broader markets) to avoid generating pages with zero ranking potential.
- **Google Search Console**: After publishing, use the Coverage report to monitor indexing status per programmatic page. Set up a custom Search Console segment for your programmatic URL pattern (e.g., all URLs containing /vs- or /for-) to track aggregate performance separately from your editorial content.
- **Screaming Frog**: Run a monthly crawl of your programmatic page set to catch thin content flags (word count below 600), missing meta descriptions, duplicate H1s across pages, and broken internal links. Schedule alongside your content refresh cycle.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: For competitor comparison pages that convert, tag the lead source as "programmatic-seo-competitor" in your CRM. Track downstream win rates for these leads separately — competitor-aware leads that self-select through a comparison page often close at higher rates and require fewer touches than generic inbound leads.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Programmatic pages are getting indexed but not ranking — stuck on page 5-10 for target keywords.**
The most common cause is thin content differentiation: the pages look programmatic because they are. Run the SERP for your target keyword and read the top 3 results — identify the one specific angle, data point, or depth they all lack, then add it explicitly to your template. A second common cause is domain authority gap: programmatic SEO works best on domains DA 30+ that already have ranking content in this topical area. If your domain is new, invest first in 5-10 high-authority editorial pieces in the same topic cluster before launching the programmatic program.

**Problem: Google is indexing some pages but not others — 40%+ of the program is in "Discovered, not indexed" state.**
This signals a crawl budget or quality threshold issue. Google's classifier may be flagging too many similar pages as low-value. Audit the non-indexed pages for content similarity: if the same paragraphs appear across 10+ pages with only the variable name swapped, you've built a thin template. Fix by requiring the "Content Differentiation Signal" section to include a unique benchmark, workflow step, or insight per variable — not just a name swap. Also add internal links from high-authority pages to the programmatic set to improve crawl priority.

**Problem: Pages convert traffic to signups but sales reports these leads are low quality.**
Programmatic pages attract users at varying stages of intent. For competitor comparison pages, add a qualification question to your CTA form ("What tool are you replacing?") and route confirmed competitive switchers to a high-touch sequence. For use-case pages attracting the wrong ICP, tighten the hero section language — add company size, industry, or specific workflow language above the fold to pre-qualify before the click-to-conversion event.
