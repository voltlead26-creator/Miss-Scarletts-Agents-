## Step 2: Content Production

### A. LinkedIn Thought Leadership Article (1000-1500 words)
Structure:
- **Hook** (2-3 sentences): Open with the most counterintuitive insight. No "I'm excited to share" or "As a [title], I've seen." Lead with the insight itself.
- **The Problem Reframe**: What does the SME believe the industry gets wrong?
- **The Framework** (with subheadings): Break the core insight into 3-4 actionable components
- **Real Evidence**: Specific example from the SME's experience (anonymized if needed)
- **Implication for the Reader**: What should they do differently starting Monday?
- **CTA**: One clear next step — not "DM me," but something concrete like "Here's the 3-question audit we use with every new client: [link]"

Tone: [Technical but accessible / Practitioner-to-practitioner / Strategic executive]
Voice cues from transcript: [Extract specific phrases, sentence rhythms, vocabulary the SME uses]

### B. SEO Blog Post (1500-2500 words, full draft)
- Primary keyword: [e.g., "enterprise data mesh architecture" or "MLOps at scale"]
- Search intent: [Informational / Commercial / Navigational]
- E-E-A-T signals to include:
  - First-person experience statements from the SME
  - Specific tools, technologies, or methodologies by name
  - Results from named (or anonymized) client/company examples
  - Contrarian expert opinion that challenges conventional advice
- Schema-ready FAQ section (4-6 questions with concise answers)
- Internal linking suggestions: [3-5 topics to link to within the company blog]
- Featured snippet optimization: Write a 50-60 word direct answer to the primary question

### C. LinkedIn Post Series (5 standalone posts)
Each post should feel like a different content type:
1. **Story Post**: Opens with "I made a $[X] mistake when..." or "Three years ago, we almost [failed at X]..."
2. **Framework Post**: "The [Name] Framework for [Outcome]: [List format]"
3. **Contrarian Take**: "Unpopular opinion: [provocative statement]. Here's why [SME name] is right..."
4. **Data/Benchmark Post**: Lead with a specific number or finding, explain what it means
5. **Question Post**: A question the SME asked themselves early in their career that changed how they think

Each post: 150-300 words, 3-5 relevant hashtags, ends with a question to drive comments.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The output doesn't sound like the SME — it sounds generic.**
Solution: Feed the prompt 3-5 verbatim quotes from the transcript first and say "Maintain this exact sentence rhythm and vocabulary throughout." If the SME uses jargon, include a "Voice Guide" section listing their preferred terminology. The more raw transcript text you provide, the better voice matching becomes.

**Problem: The SME is too technical and the content alienates non-technical buyers.**
Solution: Add to the prompt: "Translate every technical concept using an analogy that a VP of Marketing at a Fortune 500 would understand. For every technical term used, include a one-sentence plain-English explanation in parentheses." Then run the output past one non-technical reader before publishing.

**Problem: The SME won't approve the content because they're worried about revealing proprietary frameworks.**
Solution: Add a section to your prompt: "Flag any statement that reveals a client-specific methodology, proprietary system, or competitive advantage. For each flagged item, offer an alternative phrasing that conveys the insight without revealing the specific IP." Then review flagged sections with the SME in a 15-minute approval call.
