## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Create a dedicated "Series Subscriber" list in HubSpot using a form with series-specific hidden field values; trigger episode-specific nurture sequences based on which episodes contacts open
- **Salesforce**: Add a custom field "Content Series Engaged" to the Lead/Contact object; update via HubSpot sync; sales reps filter their activity views by this field to prioritize high-intent prospects
- **Notion**: Build an episode production database with status, assignee, draft URL, publish date, and promotion checklist; link each record to the episode brief template
- **Zapier**: When a new episode is published (RSS trigger from CMS), automatically push the intro paragraph + link to the team's Slack #marketing channel and create a LinkedIn post draft in Hootsuite or Buffer
- **Google Search Console**: Create a URL group for all `/series/[name]/` paths; monitor impressions and CTR separately from blog traffic to isolate series-driven SEO performance

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The series has strong email open rates but zero pipeline attribution — sales isn't mentioning it.
**Solution:** Create a one-page "Series-to-Sales Bridge" doc for every episode that translates the content angle into a discovery question reps can ask ("I know some of our customers just read our breakdown of X — is that something your team is wrestling with?"). Add this to the sales enablement Slack channel within 24 hours of each publish.

**Problem:** Episode quality degrades by episode 4-5 as the team runs out of fresh angles.
**Solution:** Before publishing episode 1, complete briefs through episode 8. Map the evidence arc (episodes 4-7) to specific customer interviews, partner data, or proprietary product usage insights you can access. The series dies when writers improvise topics; pre-planning prevents the blank-page problem mid-run.

**Problem:** Organic traffic isn't growing despite on-page SEO optimization.
**Solution:** The series lacks external authority signals. Add a deliberate link-building motion: reach out to the 3-5 newsletters or podcasters your ICP already consumes and offer to be a guest contributor citing the series data. One feature in a relevant newsletter (even 2,000 subscribers) often generates more qualified backlinks than 10 outreach emails to generic bloggers.
