# Content Audit & SEO Refresh Intelligence Engine - Systematic Content Decay Recovery & Organic Traffic Restoration

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** seo, content-audit, content-refresh, organic-traffic, content-strategy, b2b, content-ops

## Overview
Systematically audit your entire blog/content library to identify decaying content, triage each asset by update priority, and generate specific refresh strategies that recover lost organic rankings and traffic. Use this engine when you have 50+ published pages and want to stop content rot before it erodes your search visibility — or when organic traffic has plateaued or declined.

## Integration Tips
- **Google Search Console**: Export URL Performance report (12 months, all pages) as CSV — paste directly into the Quick version prompt as the data input. Filter to pages with >500 impressions to focus on pages with real demand signal.
- **Ahrefs / Semrush**: Export organic keywords by URL to feed into Phase 2 decay analysis. The "Content Gap" report is gold for identifying what competitors rank for that your refreshed content should cover.
- **Google Analytics 4**: Export landing page report with engagement time and conversion rate to identify high-traffic/low-conversion pages that need CTA refreshes in addition to SEO updates.
- **Notion / Airtable**: Build your Phase 1 triage table directly in a database — add fields for "Owner", "Status", "Refresh Date", and "Post-Refresh Traffic" to track the program end-to-end.
- **Google Sheets**: Use the Phase 6 tracking framework as a live dashboard — paste monthly GSC exports and auto-calculate % change vs. baseline with formulas.
- **HubSpot CMS / WordPress**: After each refresh, update the "Last Modified" date but preserve the original publish date in schema markup — this signals freshness to Google without losing the age authority signal.
- **Screaming Frog**: Before starting the audit, crawl your site to identify technical issues (broken internal links, missing meta descriptions, orphaned pages) that can suppress refresh results if left unfixed.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Content was refreshed but rankings haven't moved after 45 days.
**Solution:** Google's re-crawl lag can extend to 6-8 weeks for lower-authority domains. Manually submit the refreshed URL in Google Search Console → URL Inspection → Request Indexing. Also verify the page is not accidentally set to noindex, has no canonical pointing elsewhere, and that internal links pointing to it use the exact canonical URL (no trailing slash inconsistencies).

**Problem:** Merged content (post-301-redirect) is losing rankings instead of consolidating them.
**Solution:** This typically means the destination URL has a thin content problem or the merge wasn't executed properly — the best content from the sunset URL wasn't fully integrated. Wait 30 days before assessing; if still declining, temporarily reverse the redirect, add the missing sections to the destination URL first, then re-implement the redirect.

**Problem:** The audit is producing too many "Refresh & Expand" classifications — feels overwhelming.
**Solution:** Apply a stricter filter: only classify a URL as Refresh & Expand if it has BOTH >200 clicks/month AND >5,000 impressions/month AND has lost >25% of traffic year-over-year. Everything else goes to Quick Win or a lower-priority queue. Limit your active refresh pipeline to 10-15 URLs maximum at any time to maintain execution quality.
