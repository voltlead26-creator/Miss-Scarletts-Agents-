**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** seo, content-strategy, pain-point-seo, buyer-intent, b2b, demand-gen, organic, blog, keyword-research, conversion

## Overview
Maps your buyers' specific pain points to high-intent search queries, then generates a complete content strategy and first drafts for articles designed to intercept buyers at the exact moment they're actively searching for solutions. Use it when you want organic content that drives pipeline — not just traffic — or when your blog is generating visits but no leads.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a custom property "First Organic Pain-Point Keyword" to capture the search query that drove first-touch. Use HubSpot's campaign tool to group content by buyer journey stage and report on MQL-to-pipeline attribution per stage.
- **Salesforce:** Add organic content touchpoints to the campaign influence model. Set up a Pardot (or Marketing Cloud) tracking URL structure that passes UTM source/medium/content to lead records for lifecycle reporting.
- **Google Search Console:** Connect GSC to GA4 and HubSpot to pull keyword-level data into your CRM. Export top landing pages monthly and cross-reference with pipeline reports to identify highest-converting organic entry points.
- **Clearscope / Surfer SEO:** Use the content brief output from this prompt as the strategic layer, then run the target keyword through Clearscope or Surfer to add semantic completeness scoring before publishing.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build a "Pain-Point SEO Master Brief" database using the Module 4 output. Assign each article to a writer with the brief attached — reduces briefing time from 2 hours to 15 minutes.
- **Zapier / Make:** Automate a workflow: new lead with "organic" first-touch → tag with buyer journey stage → enroll in persona-specific email nurture sequence → notify SDR if score exceeds threshold.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The prompt produces generic keyword ideas that already have massive competition (HubSpot, Salesforce, Gartner ranking on page 1 for everything).
**Solution:** In your prompt input, specify your domain authority range and explicitly request "low-competition, high-intent, long-tail variants only." Add a constraint: "Prioritize keywords with fewer than 10 well-optimized results on page 1, or where the top-ranking content is more than 2 years old and hasn't been updated."

**Problem:** The content brief output doesn't differentiate enough from what competitors have already published.
**Solution:** Feed the actual top-3 ranking articles into the prompt (paste their H2 outlines or key takeaways) and add the instruction: "For each section of my outline, specify exactly what these competitor articles are missing and how my version will provide a 10x more useful answer." Original proprietary data is the most powerful differentiator — identify what internal data (customer results, product usage, survey data) you can incorporate.

**Problem:** Organic content is getting traffic but not converting to leads or demos.
**Solution:** The conversion architecture may be misaligned with the traffic you're attracting. Run the Module 5 conversion prompts separately, focusing on understanding the specific stage of the searcher. Awareness-stage traffic requires a nurture-first offer (template, guide, checklist), not a "book a demo" CTA. Audit your top-traffic articles: are CTAs staged to buyer journey, or defaulting to a demo request that's too big an ask for early-stage visitors?
