## Troubleshooting
- **Problem:** Article ranks well on Google but gets zero citations in Perplexity and ChatGPT.
  **Solution:** The article likely lacks "citation-ready" passages — self-contained, 50-80 word factual summaries that AI engines can extract without context. Audit each H2 section: does the opening sentence provide a complete, declarative answer to an implicit question? If not, add a "Key Takeaway" box after each section with 3 bulleted facts in active voice. Also check whether your article is indexed and accessible to web-crawling AI engines — block robots.txt rules can prevent discovery.

- **Problem:** High organic traffic but near-zero lead magnet downloads (conversion rate below 0.5%).
  **Solution:** The lead magnet is not topically aligned to the article's reader intent. A buyer reading "what is a good churn rate" wants a benchmark report, not a generic "SaaS growth guide." Audit every CTA: the offer should feel like the logical next step for a reader who just finished that specific section. Replace generic CTAs with section-specific offers. Use HubSpot smart CTAs to show different offers based on whether the visitor is a known contact.

- **Problem:** AI engines cite competitors instead of your article even after publishing a well-structured post.
  **Solution:** Competitor articles likely have more authoritative backlink profiles or longer publication history. Accelerate authority signals: (1) submit the article to 3–5 industry newsletters for republication with canonical link, (2) reach out to the 2–3 analysts or influencers quoted in the article and ask them to share it, (3) build 2 satellite articles that link to this one using the target keyword in anchor text. AI engines also favor content that receives engagement signals — promote the article across LinkedIn and email to drive immediate traffic and social shares that reinforce its authority.
