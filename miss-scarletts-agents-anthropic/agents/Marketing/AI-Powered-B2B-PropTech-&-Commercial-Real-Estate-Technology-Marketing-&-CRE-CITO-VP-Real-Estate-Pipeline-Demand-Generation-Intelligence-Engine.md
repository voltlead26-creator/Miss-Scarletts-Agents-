## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CRE executives engage with content but defer to IT for vendor evaluation — pipeline stalls**
*Fix:* Restructure your champion development strategy. The CRE executive must feel ownership of the technology decision, not just endorsement. Provide them with a "CRE Technology Business Case Toolkit" that lets them drive the evaluation process, set the criteria, and present findings to the asset committee — with IT as implementation partner, not decision-maker. Reframe your messaging: "This is a real estate strategy decision with a technology component, not an IT procurement." Run separate executive briefings for CRE leadership and IT simultaneously — never let IT control the evaluation without a CRE executive co-sponsor.

**Problem: Deals enter pilot and then go dark — no decision after pilot completes**
*Fix:* The pilot likely produced insights without producing a decision-ready business case. Institute a "Week 4 Business Case Session" as a required milestone in your pilot agreement — a structured meeting where you and the champion build the ROI model together using their actual data. Agree on the decision timeline and asset committee presentation date before the pilot begins. If that meeting doesn't happen, the pilot wasn't scoped correctly. Add "pilot success criteria" to the Statement of Work: what does a successful pilot look like, and what happens after it succeeds?

**Problem: Competing with "do nothing / keep using Excel" more than actual competitors**
*Fix:* The status quo wins when the cost of change feels higher than the cost of staying. Reframe with risk quantification: audit the ICP's known lease exposure using publicly available data (SEC filings often list significant leases) and build a "Portfolio Risk Exposure Calculator" that translates manual lease management into specific dollar risk — missed options, CAM errors, compliance gaps. When a VP Real Estate sees "$2.1M in identifiable lease risk in their portfolio," the status quo stops feeling safe. Testimonials from peers who "used to be Excel-only" are more persuasive than any feature comparison.
