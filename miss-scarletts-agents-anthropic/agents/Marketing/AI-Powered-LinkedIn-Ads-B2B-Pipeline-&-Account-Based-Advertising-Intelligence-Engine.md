## Success Metrics

- **Lead quality rate:** MQL conversion from LinkedIn leads should reach 35–55% within 60 days (vs. industry avg of 15–25%); if below 25%, audience is too broad
- **Cost-per-opportunity:** LinkedIn CPO should be 3–5x ACV; if CPO > 15% of ACV, restructure targeting or offer
- **Frequency:** Keep average frequency at 2–4 for cold audiences, 6–10 for ABM accounts; above 12 = creative fatigue
- **Thought Leader Ads CTR:** Should outperform brand page ads by 2–3x; if not, test different executives or content topics
- **BOFU-to-meeting rate:** Demo request leads from LinkedIn should convert to a held meeting at 25–40%; below 20% means lead quality is off or follow-up speed is the issue
- **Pipeline influence:** 30–50% of open pipeline should show LinkedIn touch within 90-day window in CRM (multi-touch attribution)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High lead volume but low MQL conversion rate (below 20%)**
Solution: Audience is too broad or offer is too early-stage. Audit who is actually submitting forms — if titles don't match ICP, tighten targeting with seniority filters (Director minimum) and add job title exclusions (e.g., exclude "Coordinator," "Analyst," "Assistant"). Also test switching from broad TOFU offers (guides, reports) to BOFU offers (demo, audit, assessment) in your lead gen campaigns.

**Problem: CPMs are extremely high ($100+) with small audience size**
Solution: LinkedIn CPMs spike when audiences are too narrow (under 50,000 members). Remove one targeting dimension — typically the skills or interests overlay — or expand geography. The job title + company targeting combination is usually sufficient; layering skills creates excessive restriction. Also check if "LinkedIn Audience Expansion" is disabled — turn it off for ABM campaigns but enable it for cold prospecting to improve delivery.

**Problem: Thought Leader Ads underperforming vs. brand page ads**
Solution: The executive featured likely lacks a strong LinkedIn presence. Thought Leader Ads only amplify content that's already resonating — promote posts that achieved at least 3–5% engagement rate organically. If executives don't post regularly, ghostwrite 2–3 posts per month before spending ad budget. Alternatively, test your most prolific content creators (even if not C-suite) — individual contribution with high engagement beats executive title without content quality.
