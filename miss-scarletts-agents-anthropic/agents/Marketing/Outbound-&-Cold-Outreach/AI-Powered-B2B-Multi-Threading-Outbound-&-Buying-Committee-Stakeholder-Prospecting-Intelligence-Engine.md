## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion goes silent after initial engagement and won't forward internal selling assets**
Solution: The champion may lack political capital or fear internal scrutiny. Send them a "low-risk" asset instead — something educational they can share without it looking like a vendor recommendation (e.g., "Here's an industry benchmark on this problem — thought this might be useful for your team planning"). Re-open the conversation with value before asking them to advocate. Also audit: did you introduce the Economic Buyer too early before the champion felt confident enough to sponsor internally?

**Problem: Economic Buyer replies negatively that "too many people on their team are being contacted"**
Solution: This means your timing coordination failed — two outreach touches hit too close together. Immediately pause all other sequences at that account. Reply to the Economic Buyer personally (not via automation): acknowledge it, offer to consolidate all communication through one contact, and ask who their preferred point of contact is. This converts a negative into a meeting-booking opportunity. Then rebuild your sequence with 48+ hour gaps between touches to different stakeholders.

**Problem: Technical Evaluator is the blocker and keeps raising new security/compliance objections**
Solution: Technical Evaluators who generate endless objections are often either (a) genuinely blocked by a real policy or (b) playing interference for someone above them who isn't bought in. Run the "objection tree" — ask: "If we satisfied [specific objection], would you be ready to move forward, or is there something else holding things up?" This isolates whether it's real or political. If political, activate champion to align with the technical evaluator separately before your next formal conversation. Provide a security/compliance package proactively (SOC 2 report, pen test summary, architecture diagram) before they ask.
