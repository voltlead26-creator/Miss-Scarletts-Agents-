## 2. DISCOVERY CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK

### Permission Architecture
Write a bridge from opener to discovery that earns the next 90 seconds without asking "Do you have a few minutes?"
- **Transition statement** (1 sentence): Acknowledges their time, delivers immediate value signal
- **Insight statement** (1–2 sentences): Something true, specific, and slightly uncomfortable about their current situation
- **Permission question** (1 sentence): Reframes the call as valuable to them, not an interruption

### SPIN Discovery Questions (Tailored to Persona)
*Situation Questions (2):* Confirm factual context you don't have from research
*Problem Questions (2):* Surface pain they're already aware of — validate it exists
*Implication Questions (2):* Expand the cost of the problem — make it feel bigger and more urgent
*Need-Payoff Questions (1):* Get them to articulate the value of solving it in their own words

### Value Statement (Post-Discovery)
Write a 3-sentence value statement that:
- Connects their stated pain (from implication questions) to your specific capability
- Delivers a customer proof point with a named outcome and real metric
- Ends with a micro-commitment question that moves toward a meeting

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Openers are getting polite "not interested" hang-ups before SDRs finish the second sentence.**
Solution: The opener is likely too generic or too long. Test the "Unconsidered Problem" opener first — it creates cognitive dissonance that's harder to dismiss in 3 seconds. Also audit the first word: if your SDRs are opening with their own name or company name, most prospects hang up before they get past it. Move name/company to after the hook.

**Problem: SDRs get through the opener but stall during objection handling — calls end too quickly.**
Solution: Your team is likely skipping the "Redirect Question" step and moving straight to the reframe. Train the Acknowledge → Redirect → Reframe sequence as a reflex. The redirect question is the linchpin: it keeps the prospect talking and reveals the real objection underneath the surface objection. Run 10-minute objection drills twice weekly using call recordings as source material.

**Problem: Meeting show rates are below 60% even when calls convert.**
Solution: Frame all meetings as "discovery" or "strategy conversation," never "demo" or "product walkthrough." Add a pre-meeting micro-prep email (sent within 30 minutes of booking) that includes: (1) a confirmation of the specific problem discussed on the call, (2) two questions you'll explore together, and (3) the specific outcome the meeting will help them achieve. This reduces no-shows by 20–35% by anchoring the prospect's identity to the meeting goal before it happens.
