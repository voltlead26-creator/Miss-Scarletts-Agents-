**Leading vs. Lagging Indicator Framework**
- Leading (controllable, measure daily): Prospects enrolled, emails personalized, calls attempted, LinkedIn touches, video messages sent

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AEs are rejecting meetings as unqualified (35%+ rejection rate)**
Solution: Add a mandatory 3-question pre-qualification gate in your CRM before meetings are logged as qualified (pain confirmed? economic buyer reachable? timeline exists?). Brief AEs on what "qualified" means before changing the bar for SDRs — misaligned definitions cause most quality disputes. Review the last 20 rejected meetings with both the SDR and AE in the same room to calibrate together.

**Problem: Reply rates collapse after first 2 weeks of new sequences**
Solution: Your sequences are getting pattern-matched as templates by email providers and recipients. Fix: (1) Increase AI-personalized first-line variation — no two emails to the same company should share the same opening angle; (2) Manually inspect the last 50 sent emails — if >30% have the same first-line structure, your AI prompts are too narrow; (3) Rotate message angles every 30 days; (4) Check sending domain health using MXToolbox and Google Postmaster Tools — a reputation issue will destroy open rates overnight.

**Problem: SDRs spend 60%+ of their day on admin and data entry instead of outreach**
Solution: This is a workflow architecture failure, not a rep performance failure. Audit every CRM field your SDRs manually fill in — most can be auto-populated from Clay, Apollo, or sequence tool sync. The target: SDRs should spend <10% of their day on admin. Anything they're manually entering that a tool could capture is pipeline you're leaving on the table.
