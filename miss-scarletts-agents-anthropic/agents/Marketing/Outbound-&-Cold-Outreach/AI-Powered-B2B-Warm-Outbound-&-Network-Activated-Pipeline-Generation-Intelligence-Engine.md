DELIVERABLE 2 — WARM INTRODUCTION REQUEST PLAYBOOK

Build a complete ask framework for each relationship archetype:

**Type A — Customer Champion Intro Request**
Situation: Happy customer who knows a contact at a target account.
Channel: Direct message or email.
Timing: Ask after a value-realization milestone (first QBR, successful onboarding, strong NPS response).

Message framework (write 3 variations — low ask / medium ask / high ask):
- Low ask: "Would you be comfortable making a LinkedIn introduction?"
- Medium ask: "Would you be willing to send a quick note saying I should connect with [Name]?"
- High ask: "Would you be open to a 3-way email introduction and briefly sharing what you've gotten from [Product]?"

For each: provide the exact copy template, recommended channel, timing trigger, and follow-up cadence if no response.

**Type B — Board Member / Advisor Intro Request**
Situation: Board member or advisor has a personal or professional relationship with a VP or C-suite decision-maker at a target account.
Channel: CEO or VP Sales to board member via email or Slack.
Preparation requirements: Pre-brief deck (1 slide) explaining the opportunity, why this account, and exactly what you're asking for.

Provide:

- Ask email template: sent by CEO/VP Sales to board member, specific enough that the board member can forward it directly
- Board member tracking log: how many asks per quarter is reasonable per board member before relationship fatigue sets in (guideline: 2-3 per quarter per board member)

**Type C — Investor Network Activation**
Situation: VC/PE investor has portfolio company relationships, LP relationships, or direct connections at target accounts.
Channel: Investor update email + direct Slack/email to specific investors.
Timing: Quarterly investor update with an embedded "targeted ask" section.

**Type D — LinkedIn Social Proximity Outreach (Self-Serve Warm)**
Situation: Target contact has 5+ mutual connections with the AE/SDR, no personal relationship but strong social proximity.

5-touch warm proximity sequence:
- Touch 1 (Day 1): LinkedIn connection request with a note referencing 2+ mutual connections by name: "We have [Name A] and [Name B] in common — I'd love to connect."

- Touch 3 (Day 8): Share a relevant piece of content (customer case study or insight) with a 1-sentence tie to their business; still no ask
- Touch 4 (Day 14): Soft ask — reference a mutual connection who is a current customer: "[Name] at [Company] is using us to solve [X] — thought it might be relevant given your role"
- Touch 5 (Day 21): Direct meeting ask with a specific value hook and explicit social proof from mutual connection

For each touch: provide the exact copy, character limits for LinkedIn, and A/B variants.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Team is uncomfortable asking customers for introductions — program stalls at the ask**
Solution: The discomfort is almost always caused by a too-vague or too-large ask. Reduce friction with extreme specificity: never ask "do you know anyone?" — always ask for one named person at one named company. Pre-write the introduction email so the advocate can forward it in 30 seconds. The ask should be: "Would you be willing to forward this email to Daniel at Apex Auto Parts? I've already written it — you'd just need to hit send." Pair the ask with a value delivery moment (just shared a benchmark report, just helped solve a problem) to make the exchange feel natural rather than transactional.

**Problem: Board and advisor network asks go unanswered or introductions are low quality**
Solution: Vague asks to busy executives get deprioritized. The quarterly board ask format works only if it is specific: a named target, a named contact at the target, the deal size context, and a pre-written email they can forward in under 2 minutes. If asks go unanswered, move the ask format from board update to a 1:1 Slack message to the specific board member who has the relevant connection. Include: "I know you know Richard Park — would you be open to a 30-second email intro? I've written the copy." Response rates for specific, low-friction asks are 3-4x higher than generic network asks.

**Problem: Warm intro pipeline is not being tracked separately and ROI is invisible to leadership**
Solution: Warm intro ROI is dramatically better than cold but remains invisible without deliberate attribution. Mandate that every AE or SDR who benefits from an introduction updates the CRM "Introduction Source" field before the discovery call. Run a monthly report comparing cold vs. warm pipeline velocity, win rate, and ACV — share this report in the weekly revenue meeting. Once leadership sees 2x win rates on warm pipeline, the program gets resourced appropriately. If tracking compliance is low, add "Introduction Source = Warm" as a required field for any opportunity meeting booked within 14 days of a logged intro request.
