## Troubleshooting

**Problem: All leads are from IT/OT Directors, not COOs or Plant Managers**
Solution: Audit your content — if the primary CTAs are about "platform architecture," "API integration," or "data security," you're attracting IT buyers. Restructure your above-the-fold content to lead with production outcomes (OEE %, downtime hours, warranty costs). On LinkedIn, shift targeting from "IT Director" and "OT Engineer" job titles to "VP Operations," "Plant Manager," and "Director of Manufacturing" — these may have lower click rates initially but will generate 3× higher pipeline velocity once they convert.

**Problem: Site Assessment program isn't filling — COOs won't commit to a 2-day on-site visit**
Solution: The barrier is time commitment, not interest. Offer a 90-minute "Virtual Plant Intelligence Session" as an entry point — a structured Zoom call where prospects share their OEE and downtime data and receive a preliminary ROI model. Convert 40–50% of virtual sessions to in-person site assessments. Also audit your qualification criteria — if you're requiring "COO champion confirmed" before offering an assessment, you're blocking your own pipeline. Offer assessments to Plant Managers and let the COO discovery happen during the assessment readout.

**Problem: Deals stall at CapEx committee review — 4–6 month approval delays**
Solution: This is a champion enablement problem, not a demand gen problem. Build a "CapEx Committee Presentation Kit" — a ready-to-use PowerPoint template with your ROI model, competitive comparison, implementation risk mitigation, and reference case studies pre-formatted for CFO/Board presentation. Your champion shouldn't have to build their own business case — you should build it for them. Also offer a "CapEx Committee Readout" call where your CFO or VP Finance co-presents the financial model alongside the champion, lending external credibility to the numbers.
