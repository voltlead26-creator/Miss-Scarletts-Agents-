## Integration Tips

**Canva / Adobe Express (design execution):**
- Build a carousel template with your brand colors, font system, and logo placement — save as a Canva Brand Kit so every carousel takes 20 minutes to design, not 2 hours
- Use the LinkedIn Document size spec: 1080 x 1080px (square) or 1080 x 1350px (portrait) per slide

- Create a UTM-tracked link for any CTA that directs to your website (even if you're using "link in bio" strategy)
- Tag LinkedIn Document as a content type in your CRM — track which carousels generate profile views → demo request sequences

- Taplio provides carousel-specific analytics not available in native LinkedIn: per-slide dwell time, drop-off points, and follower attribution per post
- Shield Analytics tracks trending content and identifies your highest-performing post types over time — use it to validate which content pillars drive most follower growth

- Set up a Zap: LinkedIn post → Google Sheet row (log every carousel with date, topic, impressions, saves, comments) for manual tracking

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Carousel gets strong cover click but high drop-off by slide 3-4**
Solution: Your Promise Slide (slide 2) is either missing or weak — readers need immediate confirmation that swiping is worth their time. Add a "Here's what you'll learn" slide at position 2 with 3-4 specific, outcome-focused bullets. Also audit slides 3-4 for density — if you're cramming too many ideas onto one slide, split them. Each slide should have one idea, communicated in under 10 seconds.

**Problem: Post gets impressions but no saves or comments — engagement rate under 1%**
Solution: The content is too generic for your audience. "5 tips for better email marketing" will always underperform against "The exact email sequence that generated 47 demos in 3 weeks for a 150-person SaaS company." Specificity — real numbers, real companies (anonymized if needed), real timeframes — is the only cure for low engagement on LinkedIn. Rewrite your carousel with at least 3 specific, real-world examples before reposting.

**Problem: Strong saves but follower growth is flat — carousel isn't converting viewers to followers**
Solution: Your CTA slide isn't giving a strong enough reason to follow. "Follow me for more content like this" competes with 10 million other posts saying the same thing. Replace it with a specific publishing promise: "I post one RevOps framework every Tuesday. Next week: the exact attribution model we use to credit marketing for 60% of pipeline." A specific promise about future value is 3-5x more likely to convert a casual reader into a follower than a generic follow request.
