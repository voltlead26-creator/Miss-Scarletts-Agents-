## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Videos get clicks but viewers drop off within the first 60 seconds.**
Solution: Run the "Hook Audit" — your first 30 seconds need to: (1) state the payoff of watching ("By the end of this video you'll know exactly..."), (2) create a pattern interrupt (surprising stat, bold claim, or visual), and (3) eliminate any logo animations or music intros longer than 3 seconds. Recut the first 60 seconds of your top 5 videos using this formula and republish.

**Problem: Videos rank for keywords but generate zero leads.**
Solution: Your CTA architecture is likely mismatched to video intent. Awareness videos should offer a low-friction lead magnet (checklist, calculator, template), not a demo request. Audit your end-screens and description CTAs against the Conversion Architecture (Module 6). If 80%+ of your content is awareness-stage, you need at least 2–3 mid-funnel "comparison" or "case study" videos that carry harder CTAs.

**Problem: Channel growth stalls after first 30 videos despite consistent publishing.**
Solution: This is almost always a thumbnail/title CTR problem, not a content quality problem. Pull your 10 lowest CTR videos and rewrite their titles and redesign their thumbnails using the frameworks in Module 4. YouTube will re-serve updated thumbnails to new audiences. Simultaneously, launch a Shorts series repurposing your best-performing long-form clips — Shorts that get traction algorithmically push viewers to your channel page, spiking subscriber conversions.
