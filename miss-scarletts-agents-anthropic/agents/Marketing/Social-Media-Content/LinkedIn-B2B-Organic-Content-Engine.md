## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts get under 1% engagement despite good copy**
Solution: The first 60-90 minutes after posting are critical for LinkedIn's algorithm. Immediately after posting, ask 5-10 colleagues to leave substantive comments (not just "great post"). The algorithm amplifies posts that get early engagement velocity. Also audit your post time — experiment with Tuesday 8am vs. Wednesday noon for your audience's timezone concentration.

**Problem: Lots of vanity engagement (likes from random people) but no ICP response**
Solution: Your content is too broad. Run the prompt again with a stricter ICP filter — ask it to write content that only someone who is a [EXACT TITLE] at a [EXACT COMPANY TYPE] would care about. Niche content gets less total engagement but more qualified engagement. Also audit your hashtags — broad hashtags (#Marketing, #Leadership) attract broad audiences; niche hashtags (#RevOps, #SalesForecasting) attract buyers.

**Problem: Engagement drops off after week 2**
Solution: You've likely shifted into a single content mode (all educational, all proof, etc.). LinkedIn audiences need variety. Check your content mix against the 40/25/20/15 framework and rebalance. Also introduce a "hot take" or contrarian post — these reliably spike reach because they generate comments from people who disagree, which the algorithm loves. Run the engagement pillar prompt section to generate fresh debate-style posts.
