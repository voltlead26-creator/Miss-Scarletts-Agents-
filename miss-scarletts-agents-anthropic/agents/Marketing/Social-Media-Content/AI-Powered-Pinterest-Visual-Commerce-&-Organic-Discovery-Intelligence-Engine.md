## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Pins are getting impressions but zero outbound clicks**
Solution: The issue is almost always the landing page mismatch, not the Pin. The Pin promises something (e.g., "5 dining table ideas") but the landing page is a generic homepage. Create dedicated landing pages that match Pin content exactly. Add UTM tracking to confirm which Pins drive traffic vs. clicks that bounce.

**Problem: Content is getting saved but saves aren't converting to website traffic or sales**
Solution: Saves without clicks indicate you're creating inspirational content but missing purchase intent signals. Audit your top-saved Pins — if none are product Pins, you're over-indexed on aspiration and under-indexed on conversion. Add 1-2 Shopping Pins per 5 lifestyle Pins, and make the path from Pin to product page frictionless (direct product URL in Pin, not homepage).

**Problem: Account impressions plateau after initial growth**
Solution: Pinterest rewards fresh content — if you're recycling the same Pins, growth stalls. Implement the "Top 10 Refresh" system: every 90 days, identify your top 10 Pins by saves, rewrite their titles and descriptions with updated keywords, and re-pin them to 3 new relevant boards. This signals freshness to the algorithm without requiring entirely new creative.
