## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts are getting consistent reach (8K-15K impressions) but zero demo requests or website traffic.**
Solution: This is an awareness-to-conversion gap — high reach content is serving the right purpose (mental availability), but you're missing the bottom-of-funnel content layer. Check your content mix: if more than 70% of posts are problem-aware or solution-aware content, you have no content that drives people to act. Add 1-2 posts per week that are explicitly bridge content — benchmark reports, ROI calculators, or case study previews with the URL in the first comment. Also check that your LinkedIn profile and company page have a clear demo CTA in the "About" section and a pinned post with a link — organic reach means nothing if the profile doesn't convert visitors.

**Problem: Hook writing isn't working — posts top out at 200-400 impressions regardless of quality.**
Solution: This is an early-signal problem. LinkedIn's algorithm amplifies or suppresses posts within the first 60 minutes based on engagement signals from your immediate network. If your first-degree connections are not in your ICP (e.g., your connections are mostly colleagues and recruiters, not finance VPs), the algorithm never serves the post to the relevant ICP audience. Fix in two ways: (a) Have the post author connect with 50-100 ICP-relevant people in the week before a major post so the initial audience is seeded correctly; (b) Coordinate with 3-5 team members to genuinely comment (not just like) within the first 15 minutes of publishing — comment velocity in the first hour is the single strongest amplification signal.

**Problem: Executive accounts post inconsistently — the CEO goes 3 weeks without posting, then posts 5 times in one week.**
Solution: Inconsistency destroys follower-growth compounding. The LinkedIn algorithm deprioritizes accounts that post in bursts — it interprets this as low-commitment and reduces distribution. Build a 45-day content buffer for each executive account by running a monthly 90-minute "content extraction" session: ask the executive 10 questions from your internal interview protocol, record the conversation in Notion, then have a content writer turn it into 15-20 posts with executive approval in a Notion review queue. Schedule all posts using Taplio or AuthoredUp at the optimal time (Tue-Thu, 7-9am local time for B2B audiences) at a consistent cadence. The executive reviews and approves in advance but doesn't need to be "in the moment" of posting.
