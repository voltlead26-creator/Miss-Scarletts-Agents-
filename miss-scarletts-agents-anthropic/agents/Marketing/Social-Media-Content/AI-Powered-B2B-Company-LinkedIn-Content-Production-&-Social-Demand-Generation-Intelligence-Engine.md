DELIVERABLE 1 — CONTENT PILLAR ARCHITECTURE

Design 5 content pillars based on ICP pain journey and LinkedIn algorithm signals:

**PILLAR 1 — PROBLEM DEFINITION (Awareness / TOFU)**
- Audience state: Buyer knows they have pain but hasn't framed it as a technology problem yet
- Post objective: Make the implicit explicit. Name the enemy. Crystallize the cost of the status quo.
- LinkedIn format: Text-heavy hook post or data stat post
- Algorithm play: High-friction opinion posts generate more comments than agreement posts — take a contrarian stance
- Example post structure: "Most [ICP title]s are losing [X] per year to [specific inefficiency]. They just can't see it in their data yet. Here's why..." → explain the invisible cost → end with a question that invites ICP to self-identify
- Content sources to mine for pillar ideas: G2 review language, Gong call transcripts, sales discovery call recordings, customer onboarding interviews
- Cadence: 2x per week

**PILLAR 2 — CATEGORY EDUCATION (Awareness-Consideration bridge / TOFU-MOFU)**
- Audience state: Buyer is researching the category but evaluating multiple approaches (build vs. buy, point solution vs. platform)
- Post objective: Establish company as the most credible educational voice in the category
- LinkedIn format: Carousel posts (5-8 slides), LinkedIn Articles, data posts with chart images
- Algorithm play: Carousels get 3-5x more saves than text posts; saves are the highest-quality LinkedIn engagement signal for B2B
- Example post structure: "5 things [ICP title]s get wrong about [category]" — frame each misconception as a real buyer belief, correct it with data or a real scenario, never name competitors
- Content sources: Original research reports, analyst data, customer advisory board insights
- Cadence: 1x per week

**PILLAR 3 — PROOF & SOCIAL VALIDATION (Consideration / MOFU)**
- Audience state: Buyer is actively evaluating solutions and looking for proof that this approach works for companies like theirs
- Post objective: Demonstrate concrete, specific outcomes — not vague "improved efficiency" stories
- LinkedIn format: Customer story snippets, stat-based outcome posts, "before/after" structure
- Algorithm play: Tag the customer company (with permission) to extend reach to their network; use pattern-interrupt numbers in the first line ("$2.3M saved. 90-day implementation. No IT project.")
- Example post structure: "[Customer type] was processing [volume] RFQs manually. [Timeframe] after switching: [3 specific metrics]. What changed: [the mechanism — the why, not just the what]"
- Content sources: Customer success team call notes, QBR decks, renewal conversations, G2 reviews, NPS comments
- Cadence: 1x per week

**PILLAR 4 — THOUGHT LEADERSHIP & INDUSTRY PERSPECTIVE (Trust / MOFU-BOFU)**
- Audience state: Buyer is shortlisting vendors and looking for evidence that this team truly understands their world
- Post objective: Demonstrate earned expertise, not just product features
- LinkedIn format: Long-form text posts (1,200-1,800 characters), executive point-of-view posts written in CEO/VP voice
- Algorithm play: First-person narrative + contrarian POV + specific numbers = highest share rate for B2B thought leadership
- Example post structure: "[Observation from market/customer conversations]." → "Here's what that tells me about where [industry] is heading..." → "The companies winning [metric] by [timeframe] are doing [3 specific things]" → "What's your read on this?"
- Content sources: CEO/VP conversations, board meetings, customer advisory board sessions, conference learnings, competitive intelligence
- Cadence: 1x per week

**PILLAR 5 — PRODUCT & COMPANY MOMENTUM (Credibility signal / BOFU)**
- Audience state: In-market buyers doing final due diligence and vendor research
- Post objective: Signal momentum, innovation, and stability — give late-stage buyers confidence
- LinkedIn format: Short announcement posts, team posts, milestone posts — keep them human and specific, never press-release tone
- Algorithm play: Employee reaction within the first 60 minutes of posting dramatically amplifies reach; pre-schedule employee notification via Slack/Teams before posting
- Example post structure: "Quick update: [specific milestone — not vague]. What this means for [ICP role]: [1-2 specific implications]. Here's the full story: [link or DM CTA]"
- Content sources: Product team launch notes, sales team wins, customer success milestones, hiring announcements
- Cadence: 2x per month (don't overdo product posts — they're the lowest-engagement pillar)

DELIVERABLE 2 — LINKEDIN ALGORITHM OPTIMIZATION SYSTEM

**The 90-Second Rule:**
LinkedIn's algorithm decides within 90 minutes of posting whether content gets wide distribution or dies. During that window, it measures: comment velocity, reshare velocity, saves, and dwell time (how long people stop scrolling on your post). Engineer for all four:
- Dwell time: Use line breaks every 1-2 sentences. Force the algorithm to read a "pause" as a reader engaging with the post.
- Comment velocity: End every post with a genuine question that ICP buyers will feel compelled to answer — not "What do you think?" but "[Specific scenario] — have you seen this at your company?"
- Save rate: Include something worth bookmarking — a framework, a list, a stat, a cheat sheet
- Reshare: Posts that make people look smart when they share them get reshared; "teach me something I can tell my team" beats "validate my existing belief"

**Hashtag Protocol:**
- Use 3-5 hashtags maximum (LinkedIn punishes hashtag stuffing)
- Mix 1 high-volume (100K+ followers: #procurement, #supplychain), 2 mid-volume (10K-100K: #procurementtech, #operationsmanagement), 1 niche/owned (under 5K: create your own category hashtag)
- Always place hashtags at the END of the post — mid-post hashtags interrupt reading flow and reduce dwell time
- Never use irrelevant trending hashtags — LinkedIn's algorithm detects topic mismatch

**First Comment Strategy:**
Post the first comment on your own post within 2-3 minutes of publishing. Use it to:
1. Add a related stat or data point that extends the argument
2. Drop the CTA or link (keeping links out of the main post body avoids LinkedIn's link penalty)
3. Tag 1-2 internal subject matter experts with a question to seed the comment thread

**Optimal Posting Windows (B2B Enterprise ICP):**
- Best: Tuesday 8:00-9:00 AM (local time of your largest ICP geography)
- Strong: Wednesday 10:00-11:00 AM, Thursday 8:00-9:00 AM
- Avoid: Friday afternoon, Saturday, Sunday morning (lower B2B professional LinkedIn activity)
- Never post two company posts within the same 18-hour window — they cannibalize each other's distribution

DELIVERABLE 4 — EMPLOYEE ADVOCACY SYSTEM

**Advocacy Architecture — Role-Based Activation**

The goal is NOT to get employees to copy-paste company posts. It is to get employees to add their authentic perspective to company content — which dramatically extends reach to their personal networks (which your company page cannot reach).

**Tier 1 — High-Activation Employees (activate on every post)**
These roles have the highest-quality LinkedIn networks for your ICP:

*Account Executives / Enterprise AEs*
- Why they matter: Their networks are procurement and ops professionals at target accounts
- Reshare script template: "My team just put this out and I think about this every week in conversations with [role type]. The thing that hits closest for me: [specific data point or insight from the post]. What I hear from customers is [add 1-2 sentence genuine perspective]. Worth a read."
- Activation: Slack DM 2 hours before post goes live with pre-written reshare copy. Make it 1-click to modify and share.

*Customer Success Managers*
- Why they matter: Their networks are in-contract customers who influence expansion and referrals, plus their professional peers at prospect accounts
- Reshare script template: "This came up in a customer conversation last week and I wanted to share our team's take. [Reference specific line from post]. In my experience working with [type of team, not specific customer], the [insight] is even more pronounced when [add customer-pattern observation]. Tag someone who should see this."
- Activation: Same Slack DM process; CS lead coordinates their team

*Subject Matter Experts / Solutions Engineers*
- Why they matter: Deep technical credibility — their engagement signals "the experts agree"
- Reshare script template: "Adding my perspective to this one — [add genuine technical observation that supports the post's point]. The data point I'd add: [1 specific technical insight]. If you want to geek out on the methodology behind this, happy to chat."
- Activation: Tag in Slack with specific section of post to react to — don't ask for a generic reshare, ask for a specific addition

**Tier 2 — Periodic Activation (2-4 posts per month)**

*Product / Engineering Leaders*
- Activate on: Product-pillar posts and category education posts
- Script template: "From a product perspective, [add insight]. We built [feature] specifically because of this pattern."

*Executives (CEO, VP Sales, VP Customer Success)*
- Activate on: Thought leadership posts where exec is named as perspective source
- Script template: Pre-written in the CEO/exec voice — they review and post with one edit

**Advocacy Cadence System:**
- Create a dedicated Slack channel: #linkedin-amplify
- Post to channel 2 hours before each company post with: (1) the post preview, (2) pre-written reshare copy for each role type, (3) a clear ask ("AEs and CSMs — please amplify this one by noon today")
- Track: Which employees amplify consistently, whose reshares generate the most engagement (proxy for network quality)
- Recognize: Monthly shoutout for top advocates; connect advocacy to company goals visibility

DELIVERABLE 5 — PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION FRAMEWORK

**UTM Taxonomy for LinkedIn:**
All links shared on LinkedIn (in first comment, bio, or DM follow-ups) use this structure:
- utm_source=linkedin
- utm_medium=organic-social (vs. utm_medium=paid-social for ads)
- utm_campaign=[content-pillar] (e.g., problem-definition, proof, category-education)
- utm_content=[post-format]-[week] (e.g., carousel-w3, text-hook-w1)

**Dark Social Attribution — The 3-Signal Method:**
LinkedIn organic drives significant "dark" pipeline — prospects who engage on LinkedIn but navigate directly to your website without clicking tracked links. Capture this through:

1. *Conversion survey question*: Add "How did you first hear about us?" to your demo request form with LinkedIn as an option. Weight responses — LinkedIn will be underreported even here (people forget), so multiply by 1.4x.

2. *CRM source tracking*: When an inbound lead self-reports LinkedIn, tag them with "LinkedIn-organic" source in CRM. Analyze conversion rates and ACV for this cohort vs. all other sources quarterly.

3. *Follower-to-ICP mapping*: Monthly, export your new LinkedIn page followers and cross-reference against your ICP firmographic criteria and open pipeline. Companies that follow your page within 30 days of a sales touch are LinkedIn-influenced opportunities — tag in CRM accordingly.

**3 Leading Indicators LinkedIn Is Working (Before Demo Requests Appear):**
1. *ICP follower growth rate*: Are net new followers matching your ICP company size, title, and industry? (Export follower analytics monthly and segment manually or via LinkedIn analytics)
2. *Post saves and shares rate*: Saves indicate bookmark behavior — prospect is saving for later reference. Track saves-per-post; above 0.5% for an established page signals strong intent-matching content
3. *Inbound DM volume*: Track DMs from ICP-matching prospects — even "great post" DMs are pipeline signals. Log in CRM when a DM matches an ICP contact.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts are getting impressions but zero engagement or comments.**
The hook isn't resonating with your ICP. Audit your last 10 posts: what % start with a number, a specific scenario your ICP lives in, or a contrarian statement? Generic hooks ("Here's why procurement matters in 2025") generate passive scrolling. Specific hooks ("We analyzed 340 enterprise procurement cycles. The companies with the best cycle times were not the most efficient. Here's what they were hiding.") force a pause. Rewrite your weakest 3 posts using the hook structure from Deliverable 3 and A/B test.

**Problem: Employee advocacy isn't happening consistently — people agree in Slack but don't reshare.**
Reduce friction to near-zero. Don't ask employees to "reshare and add their thoughts." Pre-write the reshare copy for each role type, put it in the Slack message, and ask for 1 edit before posting. Also: leadership needs to model this behavior — when executives reshare consistently, it signals that advocacy is a priority, not an optional extra.

**Problem: We're growing followers but not seeing inbound leads or pipeline attribution.**
Check your content mix: if more than 50% of your posts are Pillar 5 (product/company momentum) or generic industry news, you're building an audience that follows you for updates, not because you solve their problem. Shift the calendar to 80% Pillars 1-4 (problem, education, proof, thought leadership). Also audit your CTAs — every post should have at least one implicit or explicit way for a prospect to raise their hand (DM prompt, link in first comment, demo CTA on proof posts).
