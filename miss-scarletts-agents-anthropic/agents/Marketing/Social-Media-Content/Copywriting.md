### Content Mix Architecture

Produce the following post types as specified in the content brief:

**THOUGHT LEADERSHIP (40%):**
Original perspective, contrarian take, or industry insight that establishes expertise. Format: assertion → evidence → implication → invitation to discuss.
Example angle: "Everyone is adding AI to their product. Nobody is thinking about what happens when your entire feature set becomes a commodity overnight."

**SOCIAL PROOF (30%):**
Customer results, case studies, testimonials, or usage stats formatted as story or data hook. Format: result first → context → credibility → how they got there → your role.
Example: "One of our customers went from 14-day deployment cycles to same-day. Here's exactly what changed (and it wasn't the tech)."

**PRODUCT EDUCATION (20%):**
Feature-benefit posts, how-to content, or product walkthroughs that demonstrate value without feeling like ads. Format: problem → solution → specific outcome → soft CTA.
Example: "Most teams don't know our auto-sync feature exists. Here's what it does and why your ops lead will love you for turning it on."

**ENGAGEMENT BAIT (10%):**
Polls, questions, "hot take" posts, or prompts designed to drive high comment volume and algorithmic boost. Format: opinion or scenario → ask for their take → respond to replies within 60 minutes.
Example: "Hot take: The daily standup is a solved problem. Fight me in the comments."

## Success Metrics

**Engagement Quality:**
- LinkedIn: Comment rate >1.5% (comments ÷ impressions) indicates resonance with ICP
- X: Retweet-to-like ratio >15% signals virality potential
- Instagram: Save rate >3% shows evergreen value (people bookmarking to return)
- TikTok: Watch-through rate >50% on videos under 60 seconds

**Conversion Signals:**
- DM rate: Posts with keyword DM triggers should generate 5–25+ inbound DMs if the audience is right
- Profile visit rate: High-performing thought leadership posts drive 3–8% of viewers to your profile
- Connection/follow requests: 10+ new relevant connections per viral post is a strong signal

**Business Outcomes:**
- Attributed pipeline: Track UTMs from any links; track DM conversations through to demo booked
- Follower quality: ICP follower growth rate (not vanity followers) week over week
- Engagement velocity: Speed of first 30 minutes of engagement predicts algorithmic amplification

**Copy Quality Indicators:**
- Does the hook work as a standalone sentence (no context needed)?
- Would you personally share this post? If not, rewrite it.
- Does it pass the "so what?" test — is the insight genuinely useful to the reader?
- Does it use brand voice without sounding like it was written by a committee?

## Integration Tips

**Buffer / Hootsuite / Sprout Social:**
- Copy finalized post text directly into the scheduler. Include hashtags in the first comment (LinkedIn) or caption (Instagram) depending on your brand strategy.
- Use UTM parameters on any links: `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=thought-leadership-q2`
- Schedule LinkedIn posts Tuesday–Thursday, 8–10 AM or 12–2 PM in your audience's primary timezone for maximum organic reach.

**HubSpot / Salesforce:**
- When a post generates DM conversations, log them as activities against the contact record immediately.
- Create a "Social DM" source in your CRM to track pipeline sourced from organic social copy.
- Build a HubSpot list of contacts who engage repeatedly with your social content — these are warm accounts for ABM sequencing.

**Notion / Google Docs Content Calendar:**
- Organize posts by content pillar column (Thought Leadership / Social Proof / Product Education / Engagement).
- Track engagement metrics 7 days post-publish in a "Performance" column.
- Archive top performers in a "Swipe File" database to recycle winning frameworks (not content) quarterly.

**Clay / Apollo / AI Enrichment:**
- Use winning copy frameworks to personalize outbound messages — if a specific hook angle generates 50+ LinkedIn comments, the language resonates with your ICP. Repurpose the insight as cold email subject lines or LinkedIn connection request messages.
- Export DM leads from social into Clay to enrich with firmographic data before routing to sales.

**Canva / Figma:**
- For carousel posts, copy each slide's text directly from the Advanced Version output into your design template.
- Maintain a brand kit in Canva that locks typography and colors so copy can be applied instantly.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts get impressions but zero engagement — nobody comments or shares.**
Solution: The hook may be too broad or the topic too generic. Run the prompt again with a more specific, counterintuitive angle. Test "The Hot Take" hook — deliberately state a position 30% of your audience disagrees with. Controversy and specificity drive comments faster than anything else.

**Problem: The copy sounds "AI-generated" and lacks authentic voice.**
Solution: Before finalizing, paste the draft into the prompt again and add: "Rewrite this in a more conversational, human tone. Remove all clichés. Imagine a specific person — [name a real colleague or customer] — texted this to a friend. How would they say it?" Feed back a sample of your own best-performing posts as style examples.

**Problem: High engagement but no business outcomes — lots of likes, zero demos.**
Solution: The content is resonating but the CTA is either missing, too soft, or not attracting buyers. Add a "qualifier" DM trigger (e.g., "DM me the word DEMO if you have 20+ engineers and want me to calculate your meeting tax"). This filters for intent and company size simultaneously. Also verify that engaged followers match your ICP — if they don't, adjust the topic pillar to attract buyers, not cheerleaders.
