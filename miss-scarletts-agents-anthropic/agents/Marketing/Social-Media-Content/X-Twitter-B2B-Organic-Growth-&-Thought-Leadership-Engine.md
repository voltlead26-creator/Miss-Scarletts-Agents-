## Integration Tips

- **Buffer / Hypefury / Typefully:** Export the 30-day calendar directly into scheduling tools. Typefully is especially strong for threads — use the thread composer for all multi-tweet content.
- **Notion:** Create a content database with columns: Date, Pillar, Format, Status, Impressions, Engagements, Replies, DMs Generated. Link to your broader content ops system.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** When high-value prospects engage (like, reply, follow), trigger a CRM task to route to SDR or AE. Use tools like Trigify or PhantomBuster to automate X engagement → CRM enrichment.
- **Zapier / Make:** Build a flow: New follower matching ICP criteria (filtered by bio/title keywords) → create contact in HubSpot → assign to SDR sequence.
- **Taplio / Shield Analytics:** Connect for advanced X analytics beyond native analytics — track follower growth segmentation, content performance by format, and audience quality scoring.
- **Newsletter integration:** Every high-performing thread (>5K impressions, >50 engagements) should be repurposed into a newsletter section or LinkedIn article within 48 hours while momentum is fresh.
- **Competitor monitoring:** Set up X Lists with key competitors, customers, and ICP accounts. Use TweetDeck or native Lists to monitor daily — this feeds your content calendar with real-time conversation topics.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts get impressions but almost no replies or engagement.**
Solution: The content is informative but not conversational. Add a direct question at the end of every post ("What's your experience with this?" / "Am I missing something here?"). Contrarian or incomplete-feeling posts generate more replies than comprehensive ones — leave something to debate. Also ensure you're engaging with others' posts BEFORE you post your own content each day (30-minute engagement window primes the algorithm).

**Problem: Growing followers but no pipeline or DMs from target buyers.**
Solution: Audit who is actually following you — if it's peers and marketers rather than buyers, your content pillars are positioned wrong. Shift the ratio: spend 50% more content on buyer pain agitation (the problems they feel daily) and reduce insider/practitioner content. Add clear, non-salesy CTAs 1x/week: "If you're dealing with [specific problem], DM me — happy to share the framework we use." Qualify that you only respond if they match specific criteria.

**Problem: Strong performance on educational content but can't convert to thought leadership / authority status.**
Solution: Educational content builds impressions, but authority comes from having a POINT OF VIEW. Identify 3-5 beliefs that are genuinely contrarian to mainstream industry opinion. Write threads that argue a position and defend it with data and lived experience. Pick fights with bad advice in your category — respectfully disagree with popular frameworks and explain why. Thought leaders are recognizable for what they stand FOR, not just what they know.
