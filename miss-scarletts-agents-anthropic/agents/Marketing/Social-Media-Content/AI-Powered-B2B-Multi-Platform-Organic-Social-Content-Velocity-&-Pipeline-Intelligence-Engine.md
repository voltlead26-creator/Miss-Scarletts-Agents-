## Integration Tips

- **Buffer / Hootsuite / Sprout Social:** Export the 30-day calendar as a CSV with post text, platform, publish date/time, and media asset references. Use bulk scheduling import to queue all posts for the month in one session.
- **HubSpot:** Create a custom property called "First Social Touch Platform" on the Contact object. Use a workflow triggered by UTM source = "organic-social" on first website visit to populate this field automatically.
- **Salesforce:** Add a Campaign Influence record for every organic social UTM touch that appears in the lead/contact activity timeline within 30 days before an opportunity creates.
- **Notion / Airtable:** Build a Content Intelligence Hub that stores: (1) post performance data, (2) best-performing hooks by pillar, (3) comments that became pipeline conversations — feeding this data back into future AI prompt inputs creates a compounding performance loop.
- **Make (Integromat) / Zapier:** Set up a workflow where every new blog post triggers an AI prompt that generates LinkedIn, X, and Threads variants, drops them into a Notion review queue, and auto-schedules upon approval — target 15-minute time-to-draft from publish.
- **Slack / Teams:** Create a #social-amplify channel. Use a Zapier trigger that posts to this channel every time a new social post goes live with the link and pre-written comment templates that team members can copy-paste in 30 seconds.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI-generated content sounds generic and loses brand voice after 10–15 posts**
Solution: Build a "voice library" — a Notion doc with 20–30 examples of your best-performing posts tagged by format and pillar. Include this library in every AI prompt as reference material. Regenerate voice calibration prompts monthly by adding new top performers and removing older examples. The fresher the reference library, the sharper the output.

**Problem: High-volume posting triggers engagement drop (algorithm penalizes frequency)**
Solution: Platform algorithms reward consistency but penalize low-engagement posts more than infrequency. If engagement rate drops below 1.5% on LinkedIn or 0.8% on X/Twitter, pull back to 3–4 posts/week and focus entirely on hook quality. Run a 2-week "hook lab": post only contrarian and data-driven hooks, measure which outperforms by 2x, then use that format as the template for the next batch. Volume is second to engagement rate — always fix the latter first.

**Problem: Employee advocacy program gets ignored after week 2 despite initial enthusiasm**
Solution: Friction is the enemy. Most advocacy programs fail because they ask employees to create content. Remove creation entirely — provide ready-made posts with 3 caption variants (short/medium/long) and one image. Use a tool like Bambu, Dynamic Signal, or a simple Slack bot that delivers "your post for today" with a direct link to share on LinkedIn. Tie advocacy to a visible leaderboard that resets monthly with a small prize. Measure and publicly celebrate employees whose shares generated profile visits or DMs.
