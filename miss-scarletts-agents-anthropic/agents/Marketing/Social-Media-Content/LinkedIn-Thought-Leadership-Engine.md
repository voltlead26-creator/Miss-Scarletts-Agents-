# LinkedIn Thought Leadership Engine - Authority-Building Content System

**Difficulty:** Intermediate | **Time:** 15 min | **Tags:** linkedin, b2b, thought-leadership, social, content, awareness

## Overview
Generates a complete LinkedIn thought leadership post from a raw idea, experience, or data point — applying proven narrative structures (contrast hook, counterintuitive insight, story arc) to build personal or brand authority with a B2B audience. Use it weekly to maintain a consistent executive or brand presence without ghostwriting overhead.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a LinkedIn content strategist who has helped B2B executives grow from 2,000 to 50,000+ followers. Your posts combine personal credibility, counterintuitive insights, and a clear takeaway.

Write a LinkedIn thought leadership post based on this idea: [Paste your raw idea, observation, or lesson learned in 1-3 sentences]

Requirements:
- Hook (first line): Must stop the scroll. Use contrast, a surprising number, or a bold claim. NO "I'm excited to share..." openings.
- Body: 3-5 short paragraphs (2-3 lines each). Use white space liberally. Tell a story or present a framework.
- Insight: Include one counterintuitive or non-obvious observation that makes readers think "I've never heard it put that way."
- CTA: End with a question that invites comments, or a challenge that prompts shares. Not "What do you think?" — be specific.
- Length: 900-1,200 characters (the ideal LinkedIn range before "see more").
- Format: Plain text. No bullet points. No hashtag spam (max 3 relevant hashtags at end).

Tone: Direct, confident, self-aware. Avoid corporate jargon, buzzwords, and humblebrags.

## Integration Tips

- **Notion:** Create a "LinkedIn Content OS" database with fields for raw idea, post structure type, draft, publish date, and performance metrics. Run this prompt from a Notion AI block or paste output directly into your content calendar.
- **HubSpot:** Tag contacts who engage with LinkedIn posts using HubSpot's social monitoring feature. Create a workflow that adds a "LinkedIn Engaged" property to leads who click through to your site from LinkedIn — these leads close 2-3x faster in most B2B orgs.
- **Buffer / Hootsuite / Taplio:** Schedule posts for Tuesday-Thursday between 7-9am or 12-1pm in your audience's primary timezone. Use Taplio's analytics to track post-level performance and identify which structure types (story, contrarian, framework) perform best for your specific audience.
- **Zapier:** Build a zap: When a LinkedIn post exceeds X impressions (via Taplio or Shield API) → create a Notion task to repurpose it into a blog post, email newsletter section, or sales talk track.
- **CRM enrichment:** When inbound leads mention they found you via LinkedIn, tag them in Salesforce/HubSpot with the specific post topic. After 10+ leads, you'll have a map of which thought leadership themes drive pipeline.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The hook feels too bold or self-promotional.**
Solution: Shift from outcome-first to failure-first framing. Instead of "We 10x'd pipeline with LinkedIn," try "I almost canceled our LinkedIn program after 6 months of zero results. Here's what changed." Vulnerability disarms skepticism and makes the eventual win more credible.

**Problem: Posts get views but no comments or DMs.**
Solution: Your CTA is too open-ended. "What do you think?" generates shrugs. Instead, ask a question with stakes: "Has your team ever killed a high-performing channel because it didn't look 'professional' enough?" or "Which of these three approaches have you tried — and did it work?" Specific questions get specific answers.

**Problem: The post reads well but sounds like it could be about any company.**
Solution: Add one layer of specificity that only your company/role could know: a named internal metric, a specific customer quote (anonymized), a real date range, or a tool your team actually uses. Generality is the enemy of memorability — one concrete detail does more for credibility than five polished paragraphs.
