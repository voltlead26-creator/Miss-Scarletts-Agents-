## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts feel generic and could apply to any SaaS company**
Solution: The most common cause is an under-specified brand voice and insufficient competitor context. Add 3–5 examples of copy you would NEVER write (e.g., "excited to share," "proud to announce," "our team worked hard") and 2–3 example posts from competitors you want to differentiate from. Re-run the prompt with the instruction: "Make this sound nothing like [Competitor X]'s LinkedIn page."

**Problem: Engagement is high on thought leadership but zero pipeline from social**
Solution: Your MOFU and BOFU posts are likely missing or too subtle. Audit your content pillar ratio — most B2B brands over-index on TOFU thought leadership (70%+) and under-invest in MOFU posts (customer outcomes, ROI data, product capability with specifics). Run the advanced version with an explicit instruction to produce 3 BOFU-stage posts per week with direct demo/trial CTAs in the first comment.

**Problem: The AI-generated posts use corporate jargon the prompt told it to avoid**
Solution: Add a "Brand Voice Red List" section to the advanced prompt with 10–15 specific phrases to ban (e.g., "synergies," "best-in-class," "leverage," "circle back," "move the needle"). Also add 5 example posts from brands with voices you admire — the AI uses contrast learning effectively when shown both what to avoid and what to emulate.
