## Troubleshooting

**Problem: TikTok Shop product tags aren't driving clicks despite high video views.**
Solution: The issue is almost always hook-to-product alignment. If the hook attracts viewers who aren't buyers (e.g., trending audio attracts teens but product is for 35+ women), product tag taps will be negligible. Rerun the content brief prompt specifying "buyer-intent hook only — no trend-bait." Also check that the product tag appears at a natural moment (after proof, not during the hook) and that price is visible on the product card before requiring a tap.

**Problem: Live shopping shows generate engagement but low checkout completion.**
Solution: Three root causes: (1) Offer isn't exclusive enough — if the discount is available on your website, viewers have no urgency. Add a code that expires at show end. (2) Checkout friction — test having a moderator post the direct shop link every 90 seconds in comments. (3) Trust gap — open the show with 60 seconds of social proof (reviews, press mentions, before/afters) before showing any product. Checkout requires more trust than a standard purchase because it happens in real-time.

**Problem: Creator content generates views but no GMV from affiliate links.**
Solution: The creator isn't driving a "shop now" moment with enough conviction. Brief them using the exact scarcity/urgency scripts from Section 4 and require that the shop link appears on-screen for a minimum of 8 seconds, not just in bio. Also audit whether the creator's audience skews toward your buyer persona — a creator with 80K followers where 60% are outside your target geography will generate noise, not sales. Add audience demographic requirements to your Tier 1/2 vetting checklist.
