## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low engagement despite posting consistently for 30 days**
- Most likely cause: Content sounds like marketing copy in a community that has zero tolerance for it. Bluesky's technical and media-heavy audience immediately identifies and ignores corporate voice.
- Solution: Audit your last 20 posts. Count the number that share a genuine failure, an honest opinion, original data, or a contrarian take vs. posts that announce, promote, or describe your product. If the ratio is less than 4:1 non-promotional to promotional, you've broken the community's trust. Reset by posting 2 weeks of purely educational, opinionated, or research-backed content with zero product mentions. Then reintroduce product content at a 10:1 ratio.
- Quick fix: Find your most engaged engineers, customer success managers, or founders and have them post authentically in their own voice. A human with a job title performs 5-8x better than a brand account on Bluesky.

**Problem: Can't get accepted into starter packs or relevant custom feeds**
- Most likely cause: Your account is either too new (under 30 days, under 100 followers) or your content doesn't clearly signal your category.
- Solution for starter packs: Engage meaningfully with the starter pack curator's posts for 2-3 weeks before requesting inclusion. Curators notice who's been in their replies adding value. A DM saying "I've been following your work on [X topic] — I think [Company/Account] would be a good addition to your [Pack Name] starter pack because [specific reason tied to their curation criteria]" works significantly better than cold outreach.
- Solution for custom feeds: Research which keywords the feed algorithm filters on (many feed builders share their criteria publicly). Then deliberately include those keywords in 3-5 posts over a 2-week period. If the feed is manually curated, reach out directly to the feed builder with a link to your best-performing posts as evidence of content quality.

**Problem: Bluesky followers don't convert to pipeline — all vanity metrics**
- Most likely cause: No UTM tracking, no CTA strategy in posts, and no pathway from Bluesky to your owned channels (email, demo, free trial).
- Solution: Every 5th post should include a link with a UTM-tagged destination — not to your homepage, but to a high-value piece of content (original research, free tool, benchmark report) behind a light-touch email capture. The sequence is: Bluesky post with link → Resource landing page (UTM-tagged) → Email capture → Welcome sequence that qualifies intent → SDR follow-up if ICP criteria met. Track this in HubSpot filtered by utm_source=bluesky. If no one is clicking links, the content isn't generating enough curiosity — increase the gap between what you share in the post and what's available at the link.
