Designs end-to-end UGC campaigns — from identifying seeding opportunities and writing customer outreach sequences to building rights management workflows, amplification briefs, and performance frameworks. ## Integration Tips

- **TINT / Stackla / Bazaarvoice:** Connect your brand hashtag and @mentions to a UGC aggregation platform that auto-imports content, flags it for review, and manages rights requests with one-click approval workflows — eliminates manual DM outreach at scale
- **Meta Business Suite (Spark Ads) + TikTok Creator Marketplace:** Once UGC creators authorize Spark Ads permissions, their organic posts can be boosted as paid ads directly from their profiles — higher trust signals than traditional dark posts, typically 15-25% lower CPMs
- **HubSpot / Klaviyo:** Tag customers who submit UGC in a dedicated "UGC Creator" list; trigger automated post-submission thank-you flows; segment creators for early product access, VIP offers, and future campaign invitations
- **Figma / Canva Brand Kit:** Create a "UGC-ready" template library so designers can quickly add minimal brand overlays (logo, CTA bar) to UGC assets without over-polishing the authentic feel
- **Notion / Airtable UGC Tracker:** Build a content pipeline database with fields for: creator handle, platform, format, submission date, quality score, rights status, paid approval status, and performance metrics — this becomes your ongoing creative library
- **Google Sheets + Zapier:** Automate the rights clearance pipeline — when a creator clicks the authorization link, Zapier writes a row in your UGC tracker, triggers a Slack notification to the brand manager, and sends the creator their reward confirmation — zero manual steps
- **Yotpo / Okendo (for DTC):** Embed UGC directly into product review widgets on your PDP — combine star ratings with creator photos and videos for a conversion lift that typically beats static reviews by 2-3x

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Outreach emails get opened but very few customers actually create and submit content.
**Fix:** The incentive or friction is off. Run this diagnostic: (1) Is the incentive meaningful relative to the effort? A $15 discount code for a 30-second video is undersized for most brands — test a $40+ credit or a free product replenishment. (2) Is the brief intimidating? If customers open it and see a long PDF, they'll abandon. Rebuild the brief as a 6-line text message with one clear "here's what to do" sentence and a single example video link. (3) Add a peer example immediately — show one actual customer video in the first email so they can visualize the bar (it should look attainable, not perfect).

**Problem:** Most UGC coming in is beautiful flat-lay photography, but you need authentic video for paid social.
**Fix:** Your content prompts are too generic or visually-oriented. Replace all photo-centric prompts with explicit "film yourself" language. Add a line to the brief: "We specifically need video — even 15 seconds of you talking is more valuable to us than the most perfect photo." Consider a "video-only" incentive tier (e.g., $30 for photo, $60 for video) to shift the mix. Seed the hashtag with 5-10 video examples from team members or existing advocates before launching the campaign.

**Problem:** You're getting UGC but can't clear rights fast enough — assets sit idle for weeks before reaching paid teams.
**Fix:** Your rights workflow has too many manual steps. Switch from custom DMs to a standardized rights request tool (TINT, Pixlee, or a simple Typeform link embedded in a DM template). The goal: a creator can go from "I agree" to "rights cleared" in under 60 seconds. Also, pre-authorize your top 20 repeat creators for blanket usage rights in exchange for a monthly product credit — this creates a standing UGC library that needs zero per-asset clearance.
