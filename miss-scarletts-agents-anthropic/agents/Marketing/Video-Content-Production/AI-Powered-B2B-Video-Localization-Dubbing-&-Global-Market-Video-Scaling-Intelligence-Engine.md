## Overview
Uses AI dubbing, voice cloning, and cultural adaptation frameworks to transform a single English B2B marketing video into market-ready, localized versions for 10–50+ international markets — at 1/10th the cost and 20x the speed of traditional localization. Use this when entering new geographies, running multi-region demand generation campaigns, or building a scalable global video content library that drives pipeline with non-English speaking buyers.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: AI dubbing sounds robotic or uncanny — hurts brand credibility in formal enterprise markets (Germany, Japan)**

Solution: Don't use AI lip-sync dubbing for these markets. Switch to either: (a) a clean AI voiceover layered over the original video with subtle audio mix (original voice at 5% volume, dubbed at 95% — creates natural atmosphere), or (b) a Synthesia AI avatar presenter recording a fully native-language version with the same script. Japan-specific: budget for a Japanese-speaking presenter (even 1 video/quarter) to record anchor thought leadership content in native Japanese — this dramatically outperforms AI dubbing for senior Japanese B2B buyers.

**Problem: Translated value proposition isn't landing — localized video has low completion rate despite technical quality**

Solution: The translation is likely semantically accurate but culturally flat. Run the translated script through this diagnostic: (1) Does it lead with a claim or a problem? German/French buyers respond better to problem-first framing, not American confidence-first hooks. (2) Does the CTA match local business etiquette — is "Book a Demo" too aggressive for this market? (3) Are the statistics referenced relevant to the target market, or are they U.S.-centric numbers that don't resonate? Run Claude with this prompt: "You are a native [Language] B2B marketing director. Review this translated marketing script and flag every sentence where a local buyer would feel this is clearly an American company that translated its content rather than a vendor that understands their market."

**Problem: Video localization ROI is hard to prove — international pipeline attribution is murky in CRM**

Solution: Establish a localization control group before scaling. For your first localized market: (a) split your target ICP contact list 50/50; (b) one half receives English-only video in outreach sequence; (c) other half receives localized video in same sequence. Track MQL rate, demo request rate, and opportunity creation rate at 90 days. This gives you clean data to calculate localization lift and justify continued investment. If running too small a sample for statistical significance, use engagement metrics (completion rate, CTA click rate) as leading indicators — localized video typically shows 30–60% higher completion in non-English markets even before pipeline data matures.
