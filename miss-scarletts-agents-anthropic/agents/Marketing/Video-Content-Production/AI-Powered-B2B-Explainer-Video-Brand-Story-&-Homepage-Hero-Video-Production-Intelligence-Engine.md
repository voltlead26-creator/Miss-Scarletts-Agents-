## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The script feels like a product features list rather than a story**
Solution: Return to the Narrative Strategy Brief. Every scene must map to a narrative function — if "Mechanism" takes more than 15 seconds, you're pitching features, not selling transformation. Cut to the single most differentiated thing you do and prove it with an outcome, not a capability.

**Problem: The VO sounds too corporate / generic**
Solution: Read the script out loud at normal speaking pace. Anything that sounds like a press release fails. Replace noun-heavy corporate language ("enables organizations to leverage") with action-verb buyer language ("you stop guessing, your team stops flying blind"). The buyer should hear their own internal monologue reflected back.

**Problem: The A/B test hooks all feel too similar**
Solution: Each hook must be genuinely different in emotional strategy. Pain-Led creates relief ("finally someone gets it"). Provocation creates cognitive dissonance ("wait, is that true about me?"). Outcome-Led creates desire ("I want that"). If all three hooks are opening with the same emotion, you haven't pushed far enough on the provocative and outcome variants.
