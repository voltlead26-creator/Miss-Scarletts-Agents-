## 1. PRE-PRODUCTION INTERVIEW STRATEGY

### A. Customer Interview Preparation Brief (for the interviewer)
- **Warm-Up Questions (before camera):** 3 questions to loosen the customer and get them in the emotional headspace of the "before" state
- **Core Interview Questions (on camera):** 12 questions, sequenced to draw out: Before State → Discovery Moment → Evaluation → Decision → Implementation Experience → Business Outcome → Personal/Career Impact → Peer Recommendation
- **Metric Prompts:** Specific probes to surface hard numbers when the customer gives vague answers (e.g., "When you say 'significantly faster' — can you give me the before and after time?" / "What's the dollar impact of that time savings?")
- **What NOT to Ask:** List of 5 question patterns that produce corporate-speak answers ("What do you like best about working with us?" = boring) and their replacements
- **The "Mirror" Question:** One single question designed to make the customer articulate the exact emotional state of the target viewer — so prospects hear their own fear/frustration in someone else's words
- **Story Capture Prompts:** 3 techniques to elicit a spontaneous story (not a prepared statement) — these clips are gold

### B. Pre-Interview Client Prep Sheet (for the customer)
A one-page brief to send the customer the day before:
- What we'll cover and why it matters
- 3 topics to think about in advance (WITHOUT scripting their answers)
- What to wear (or not wear) on camera
- Technical setup if recording remotely (lighting, audio, background)
- What to expect: "This is a conversation, not a presentation"

## 2. STORY ARC ARCHITECTURE

Develop the complete narrative spine using this framework:

### Before State (The World Before)
- **Functional Pain:** What process was broken, slow, or error-prone?
- **Emotional Pain:** How did the broken process make the customer FEEL? (stressed, exposed, embarrassed in front of leadership, wasting team's talent)
- **Business Cost of Inaction:** What was the quantified or estimated cost of staying in the "before" state? (e.g., revenue at risk, hours wasted, competitive disadvantage)
- **Failed Alternatives:** What did they try before finding you? Why did alternatives fail or feel inadequate?
- **The Trigger Event:** What happened that forced them to finally act? (new hire, missed quarter, board pressure, competitive threat)

### Turning Point (Why You)
- **Discovery:** How did they first encounter your solution?
- **Aha Moment:** The specific feature, demo moment, or conversation that made them believe this was different
- **Internal Champion Dynamics:** Who championed this internally? What resistance did they overcome?
- **Decision Moment:** The single sentence that captures WHY they chose you over doing nothing or choosing a competitor

### After State (The New World)
- **Immediate Win:** What improved within the first 30–90 days?
- **Full Transformation:** What is fundamentally different about how their team works now?
- **Unexpected Benefit:** What positive outcome surprised them that they didn't expect?
- **Career/Personal Impact:** How has this affected the customer personally — their credibility, confidence, or standing with leadership?

### Proof Architecture
- **Hard Metrics (Tier 1):** Specific, time-bound, attributable numbers
- **Soft Outcomes (Tier 2):** Team adoption rate, executive visibility, process change
- **Future Commitment Signal (Tier 3):** Are they expanding usage, renewing early, becoming a reference?

### Peer Recommendation
- **The "Tell a Colleague" Moment:** What would they say to someone in the same situation who is evaluating your solution?
- **The Specific Audience:** "If you're a [title] at a [company type] who is struggling with [pain], you need to..."

Scene labeling guide — every scene MUST have one of these narrative functions:
- **Hook:** Pattern-interrupt opening — starts in the customer's "before" emotional state
- **Problem:** Amplifies the pain and the cost of inaction
- **Failed Alternative:** Validates that the problem is real and alternatives failed
- **Discovery/Turning Point:** How they found you and what convinced them
- **Implementation Reality:** Honest, non-promotional — includes any friction overcome
- **Proof:** Hard metric with attribution context (before/after)
- **Unexpected Win:** The bonus outcome that builds enthusiasm
- **Peer Recommendation:** Direct address to the viewer — the "you should try this if..." moment
- **CTA:** Single clear next action

### Social Cut (60-sec version)
- Condensed arc: Hook (0–8s) → Pain (8–18s) → Proof (18–40s) → Recommendation (40–52s) → CTA (52–60s)

- Add captions approach (word-by-word kinetic or sentence-by-sentence banner)
- Silent-optimization notes (all meaning must survive with audio off)

### Paid Social Ad Cut (30-sec version)
- Open with the single most arresting metric or quote (0–5s)
- Problem statement (5–15s)
- Outcome proof (15–25s)
- CTA with specific offer (25–30s)
- Notes on copy for thumbnail and first-frame still

## 5. BUYER JOURNEY DISTRIBUTION SEQUENCE

Map each asset to the buyer journey stage where it does maximum work:

### Awareness Stage
- Where: LinkedIn organic, paid social prospecting, YouTube pre-roll
- Format: 30-sec paid cut, quote cards
- Goal: Create recognition of the problem — not yet asking for a meeting
- Trigger: Deploy when ICP prospect matches account list but has not engaged yet

### Consideration Stage
- Where: Mid-funnel email sequence (position 3–5 of 8), retargeting ads, gated resource
- Format: 60-sec social cut, blog post with embedded hero film
- Goal: Make the buyer believe transformation is possible for THEM specifically
- Trigger: Prospect downloads content, visits pricing page, or opens email 2x

### Decision Stage
- Where: Sales follow-up after demo, deal room, late-stage email sequence
- Format: Hero film, sales leave-behind PDF
- Goal: Overcome final objection, build internal champion's business case
- Trigger: Opportunity moves to Stage 3+ in CRM, or champion shares internally

### Post-Close (Customer Expansion)
- Where: Onboarding sequence, QBR materials, customer community
- Format: Quote cards, blog post
- Goal: Reinforce purchase decision, inspire reference conversations
- Trigger: Day 30 and Day 90 post-purchase

# CONSTRAINTS
- Every customer quote must sound like a human speaking, not a press release — no "leveraging synergies," no "holistic solutions"
- The customer is the hero. Your company's name appears as few times as possible in the first 60 seconds
- Every metric must be attributed with context: "within 90 days," "compared to our previous tool," "across the enterprise team of 80 reps" — not just the raw number

- Interview questions must be open-ended — no yes/no questions, no leading questions that telegraph the desired answer
- CTA must match the viewer's stage: decision-stage video uses "Book a demo"; awareness video uses "See how [Company] did it"

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The customer sounds like they're reading from a script — polished but lifeless**
Solution: Stop the interview and ask an unexpected tangential question ("What was your team's initial reaction when you first showed them this?"). The goal is to disrupt the prepared-statement mode. The best clips always come 3–4 minutes after a question is asked, once the customer has processed and relaxed. If the interview was fully polished, schedule a 10-minute "casual follow-up" call and open with "Tell me about a specific moment in the first week when things clicked" — and let it roll.

**Problem: The customer refuses to share specific metrics on camera**
Solution: Offer three alternatives: (1) Use rounded/directional numbers ("roughly 40–50% improvement"), (2) Frame it as a comparison ratio instead of absolute numbers ("cut our reporting time in half"), (3) Focus on time saved rather than revenue impact, which is easier to approve legally. If all metrics are off the table, pivot the story to emotional/organizational transformation — the "I used to dread that Monday morning call; now I look forward to it" arc is more authentic than a hollow placeholder metric anyway.

**Problem: The video performs well on LinkedIn but sales team won't use it in their sequences**
Solution: The sales adoption problem is almost always a relevance mismatch. Survey 5 AEs: "What's the most common objection you hear after the demo that this story doesn't answer?" Then produce a 90-second "objection-specific" cut that opens on that exact objection and has the customer address it directly. Sales teams adopt videos when they feel like precise tools, not marketing content.
