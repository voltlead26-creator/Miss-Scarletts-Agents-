## Overview
Builds a complete B2B video content engine — producing channel-specific scripts, production briefs, distribution playbooks, and a 90-day video calendar across YouTube, LinkedIn, short-form, webinars, and product demos. Use this when you want to systematize video production at scale, replace fragmented one-off video requests with a repeatable AI-automatable workflow, and connect every video asset to measurable pipeline outcomes.

## Integration Tips

- **Wistia + HubSpot:** Embed all sales and website videos in Wistia to get per-contact watch data synced to HubSpot contact timelines. Use "watched 75% of video" as a marketing qualification trigger for sales follow-up sequences.
- **YouTube Analytics + Salesforce:** Use UTM parameters on every YouTube CTA link. Create a custom Salesforce field "Video Touch" and build a report showing opportunities where a video-source UTM appears in campaign history.
- **Notion video production hub:** Build a Notion database with one row per video: Title, Channel, Script (embedded), Production Brief, Status (Scripted/In-Production/Editing/Scheduled/Published), Distribution checklist. Connect to your content calendar view.
- **Loom for sales enablement:** For internal sales training videos and deal-specific demos, use Loom. Integrate Loom with Salesloft/Outreach to track when prospects open and watch sales videos sent via email.
- **Descript for AI-powered editing:** Paste your script into Descript, record once, and use AI overdub/correction to fix delivery errors without re-recording. Cuts editing time by 60–70% for talking-head videos.
- **Zapier automation:** When a YouTube video is published (trigger: new YouTube video via RSS), automatically post to LinkedIn company page, send internal Slack notification, and create a HubSpot email draft for newsletter announcement.
- **Riverside.fm for remote recording:** Use Riverside for remote executive interviews, customer case studies, and multi-speaker panel videos — outputs high-quality local recordings from distributed teams.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Executives agree to be on camera but keep canceling or delivering wooden performances**
Solution: Reduce their time commitment by switching to "interview format" — you ask 5 questions, they answer conversationally, you edit into a polished video. Record in 20-minute batches. Send them the first 3 questions in advance but not all 5 — it keeps answers natural. Build a "greatest hits" library of authentic moments from these sessions to reuse across multiple videos.

**Problem: Videos get views but no demo requests or pipeline contribution**
Solution: The CTA is likely too soft ("let us know in the comments") or too early (before the viewer trusts you). Audit your top 10 videos: (1) Does the CTA appear in the final 15% of the video? (2) Is there exactly ONE action requested? (3) Is there a friction-reducing reason ("no pitch, just a working session")? Also check UTM tracking — pipeline may already exist but attribution is broken. Run a "video influenced pipeline" CRM report for the last 90 days.

**Problem: Can't produce 3 videos/week — team is bottlenecked**
Solution: Shift the production model. 80% of your output should be Tier 1 (talking-head, zero B-roll, recorded on a clean background with a decent USB mic — producible in 2 hours from script to publish). Reserve Tier 2–3 production for your 4 "pillar" videos per quarter. A single $250/month investment in a freelance video editor on Contra or Toptal can handle Tier 1 batch editing in 2 hours/week.
