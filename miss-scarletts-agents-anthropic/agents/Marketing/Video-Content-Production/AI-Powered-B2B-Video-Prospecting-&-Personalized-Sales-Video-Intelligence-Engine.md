## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High thumbnail click-through rate but low video completion — prospects click but abandon within 10 seconds**
Solution: The thumbnail promised something (personalization, relevance) that the opening 10 seconds didn't deliver. Audit whether your opening line is genuinely specific — "I saw your Series B announcement" is too generic. "I saw your post about how your Series B puts you on a path to 200 headcount by Q3 — and that's exactly the inflection point where [your problem] becomes a board-level issue" is specific. If you're using AI-generated videos, check whether the personalization tokens are actually dynamic or whether the video feels like a template with a name inserted.

**Problem: Video reply rates are strong but meetings aren't converting — prospects reply but deflect or go dark before booking**
Solution: Your video CTA is creating reply behavior but not meeting behavior. Two fixes: (1) Replace open-ended CTAs ("would love to chat if you're interested") with calendar-specific asks ("I have Thursday 2pm or Friday 10am EST — which works?"). Specificity forces a binary decision instead of a vague "sure, let's connect." (2) Ensure your video gives enough value that the prospect wants more — if the video is all setup and no payoff, they've extracted what they came for and have no incentive to book. End the video with a partially resolved insight: "Here's what we typically find... I'll save the rest for a 15-minute call."

**Problem: AI-generated videos (HeyGen/Synthesia) are getting called out as fake by prospects, damaging brand trust**
Solution: Disclosure and authenticity signals matter. Consider adding a brief "I used AI video tools to reach you at scale, but the research and message are genuinely mine" note in the email — many prospects respect the transparency and it differentiates you from spam. Alternatively, switch high-value accounts (Tier 1) back to authentic recorded video and reserve AI generation for Tier 3 only. Ensure AI avatar lip-sync quality meets a "passes a 5-second test" standard before sending — low-quality AI video does more damage than no video at all.
