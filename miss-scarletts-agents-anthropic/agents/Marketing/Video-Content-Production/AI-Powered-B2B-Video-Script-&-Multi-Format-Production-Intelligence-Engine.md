## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The script sounds like marketing copy, not natural speech**
Solution: After generating the script, read every [SPEAKER/VO] line aloud at normal speaking pace. Any sentence you have to re-read or that takes > 15 words to make its point needs to be broken into two sentences. Run the script through a "reading grade level" checker — aim for Grade 8–10 for maximum clarity.

**Problem: The proof section feels vague or unbelievable**
Solution: Replace any outcome statement that doesn't include (a) a specific metric, (b) a timeframe, and (c) an implicit comparison to the "before" state. If you don't have real customer data, use the format: "A [company size]-person [industry] company" — this is specific enough to be credible without naming the customer. Avoid "up to X%" framing; use directional results ("from 31% to 74%") instead.

**Problem: The CTA isn't converting viewers to action**
Solution: Test two CTA variants: (1) outcome-led ("See what your pipeline looks like 90 days from now"), and (2) low-friction ("Takes 20 minutes, no slides, no sales pitch"). Measure click-through from video end-card or description link. The CTA in the script should match the CTA on the landing page exactly — any mismatch between what the viewer heard and what they see kills conversion.
