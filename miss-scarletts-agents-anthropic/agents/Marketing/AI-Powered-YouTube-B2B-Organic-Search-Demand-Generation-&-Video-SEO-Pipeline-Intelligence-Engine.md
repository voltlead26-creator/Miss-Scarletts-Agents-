## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Use hidden form fields to capture utm_campaign (video slug) on every YouTube-linked form. Build a "YouTube Sourced" Smart List and a Revenue Attribution report broken down by individual video. Set up a dedicated YouTube nurture sequence (5 emails) for contacts who download video resources, designed to move them from educational interest to demo request.
- **Salesforce:** Create a YouTube Campaign for each video. Use Campaign Member records to track multi-touch influence. Build a "YouTube Influenced Pipeline" report on the Opportunities object filtered by accounts where any associated contact has a YouTube Campaign Member record.
- **Notion:** Build a Video Production Hub in Notion with a database tracking every video's title, target keyword, publish date, lead magnet, UTM link, and performance metrics (imported weekly from YouTube Studio and CRM).
- **Zapier:** When a YouTube-sourced contact books a demo (Calendly webhook), automatically create a Salesforce lead, tag with video source, and notify the assigned SDR in Slack with the video they watched and resource they downloaded.
- **VidIQ or TubeBuddy:** Install the Chrome extension for real-time YouTube keyword research, competitor channel analysis, and bulk tag management. Run a weekly keyword audit to identify new ranking opportunities as competitor videos age.
- **Google Search Console:** Connect your website's Search Console to track YouTube videos that appear in Google's video carousels — these generate website traffic from the video without the viewer ever visiting YouTube.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Videos are getting views but generating zero form fills or leads**
Solution: Your CTA is buried or your lead magnet isn't compelling enough. Audit the last 5 videos: Is the lead magnet mentioned verbally in the video (not just in the description)? Is the landing page linked in the first line of the description? Is the lead magnet directly relevant to the video topic (not a generic company brochure)? Fix the three in that order. Also add a pinned comment on every video with a direct offer — many viewers never scroll to read the description.

**Problem: Videos aren't ranking for target keywords after 60 days**
Solution: The keywords you're targeting may be too competitive for a new channel, or your video's watch time signals are weak (YouTube deprioritizes videos with high drop-off). Run this diagnosis: (1) Check your Average View Duration — if under 35%, the content isn't matching search intent, rewrite the hook; (2) Check if competitor videos ranking above yours have 10x more views — if yes, target less competitive long-tail variations first and build domain authority; (3) Verify your title contains the exact keyword phrase in the first 4 words — YouTube's algorithm weights title placement heavily.

**Problem: High subscriber growth but low pipeline contribution**
Solution: Your content is attracting practitioners at lower seniority than your ICP. Audit your video topic mix — if most content is tactical how-to (e.g., "How to write cold emails"), you're attracting SDRs and junior marketers, not decision-makers. Shift 40% of your calendar to strategic content targeting VP+ buyers (e.g., "How to structure your outbound motion for $50M ARR growth"), and add explicit ICP signals in your titles ("for SaaS companies" / "for VP Sales" / "at Series B"). Decision-maker buyers search for strategic content, not tactical tutorials.
