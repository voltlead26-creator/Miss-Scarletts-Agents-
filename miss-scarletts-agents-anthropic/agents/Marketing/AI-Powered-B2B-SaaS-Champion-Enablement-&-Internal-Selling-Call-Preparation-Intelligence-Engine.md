## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion is enthusiastic but keeps saying "the timing isn't right" and internal meetings keep getting delayed or canceled.**
Solution: This is a priority misalignment problem, not a champion relationship problem. The champion believes in your product but cannot make it a priority above competing internal projects. Run the Champion Situation Assessment scoring — if their alignment with current company strategic priorities scores below 3, you may have the wrong champion, a timing problem that is real, or a "the company isn't ready to buy" situation that no amount of champion enablement will fix. Specific intervention: Ask your champion directly: "What is the #1 priority consuming your leadership team's attention right now?" If the answer is unrelated to the problem you solve, propose delaying the internal push until that priority resolves (or pivot to position your solution as ENABLING that priority). If the answer IS related to your problem, the issue is that your champion hasn't connected the dots between their crisis and your solution — rebuild the business case narrative around their current burning fire.

**Problem: The business case numbers don't resonate with the economic buyer — they say "I don't believe the ROI figures."**
Solution: This is a methodology credibility problem. Generic ROI calculators fail with sophisticated economic buyers because they use industry averages that don't feel real. Instead, rebuild the business case from their own data: ask the champion to pull their actual CRM data (deal cycle length, win rates, AE ramp time) and replace your template estimates with their real numbers. Even if the resulting ROI is lower than your benchmark, numbers the buyer recognizes from their own system carry 10× more credibility than numbers from a vendor's calculator. Then present two scenarios — conservative (using the buyer's own data with zero assumptions) and upside (adding industry uplift assumptions explicitly labeled as estimates). The CFO will trust the conservative scenario enough to approve.

**Problem: Security or IT review has stalled for 4+ weeks with no clear path to resolution.**
Solution: Long security reviews kill deals. The fix is not patience — it's escalation with a structured process. Have your champion request a "Security Fast Lane" call: position it as "I'd like to bring our CISO and your CSO/VP Security together for a focused 45-minute technical review to accelerate the security portion of our evaluation." Provide your security team a pre-filled security questionnaire answering the top 20 financial services/enterprise security questions BEFORE the call — so the call is a confirmation conversation, not a discovery conversation. In parallel, offer a reference call between their IT security team and the security team at a comparable customer. If after both interventions the review remains stalled, escalate: have your executive (VP Sales or CRO) call Marcus directly to discuss the timeline and whether a time-boxed security pilot (controlled environment, limited data scope) would accelerate approval.
