## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:** Create a custom "Partner Source" field on Contacts and Deals. Build a workflow that auto-tags any contact with a co-marketing UTM source into the partner attribution pipeline. Set up a partner-specific lifecycle stage between MQL and SQL to track partner-sourced lead quality separately.

**Salesforce:** Create a Campaign hierarchy where the parent campaign is "Co-Marketing Partners" and child campaigns are individual partner programs. Use Campaign Member Status to track: Responded (downloaded joint asset), Attended (webinar), Trial Activated, Opportunity Created. Build a Partner Influenced dashboard using Campaign Influence models.

**Notion / Airtable:** Build a Partnership CRM with fields: Partner Name, Tier, Status (Prospecting → Signed → Active → Review), Primary Contact, MOU Expiry, Active Campaigns, MQL Delivered YTD, Pipeline Influenced YTD, QBR Date, Renewal Decision. Share a filtered view with your Tier 1 partners so they can see their performance metrics in real time.

**Zapier:** Automate partner lead routing — when a new contact is created with utm_source = [partner-name], auto-create a Salesforce task for your partner manager to send a personalized welcome email within 24 hours.

**Google Sheets (Shared Reporting):** Build a partner-facing dashboard tab showing: joint campaign MQL count, webinar registration/attendance, content engagement, and pipeline contribution. Keep company-confidential metrics (revenue closed, win rate) in a separate tab visible only to your team.

**Canva for Teams / Figma:** Create a co-branding template library with pre-approved logo lockups, co-branded webinar slide decks, email header templates, and social card formats for each active partner. Reduce asset production time from 3 days to 4 hours.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner agrees to co-market but consistently deprioritizes the campaign when execution begins.**
Fix: This is the most common co-marketing failure mode. Prevent it at the agreement stage — include specific promotional commitments in the MOU (exact send dates, list minimums, social post counts). Build a shared project tracker in Notion or Asana with due dates for each partner's deliverables. Schedule a bi-weekly sync with their PMM counterpart — the personal relationship is what makes promotions happen. If they miss two consecutive commitments, invoke the performance clause in the MOU and downgrade to Tier 3.

**Problem: Attribution shows co-marketing MQLs have much lower conversion rates than inbound.**
Fix: This typically signals audience mismatch — the partner's list is less qualified than your ICP even if their company profile matches. Audit the job titles and company sizes of co-marketing leads vs. your typical MQL profile. If there's a mismatch, tighten the segmentation for the next campaign (request that the partner only email their [specific segment] sublist rather than their full database). Alternatively, add a 2-question qualifying survey on the joint asset download gate to filter out non-ICP leads before they enter your nurture.

**Problem: Sales team doesn't trust or prioritize partner-sourced leads.**
Fix: Sales distrust of partnership leads is common when the program is new and pipeline attribution is opaque. Solve it by: (1) briefing sales on every active partnership — what the partner does, why their buyers are ideal, what specific campaign generated the lead; (2) building a "partner context" field in Salesforce that auto-populates when a partner-sourced lead comes in, explaining the campaign and partner relationship; (3) tracking partner-sourced win rates separately for 90 days and sharing the data with sales leadership. When sales sees partner-sourced ACV is 20% higher than average, their enthusiasm for these leads changes immediately.
