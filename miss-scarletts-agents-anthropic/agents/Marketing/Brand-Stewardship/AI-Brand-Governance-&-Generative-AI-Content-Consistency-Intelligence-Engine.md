## Overview
In 2026, every function in your company — sales, customer success, product, engineering, demand gen, executives — is generating content with AI. Without a codified, AI-embeddable brand governance system, your brand fractures silently: 14 slightly different value propositions, inconsistent tone across channels, rogue claims that create legal exposure, and a brand that feels generic because everyone's prompting from scratch. This engine builds a "Brand Constitution" — a structured, prompt-ready brand governance framework that any team member can paste into Claude, ChatGPT, or any AI agent to generate on-brand output every single time. Use it to codify your brand for the AI era, audit existing content for drift, and build the governance workflow that scales brand consistency without a compliance bottleneck.

**Output Example (Component 1 excerpt — Brand Constitution):**

You are writing content for Dashlink, a revenue forecasting tool built for VP Sales and RevOps leaders at B2B SaaS companies who are tired of forecast calls that feel like amateur astrology.

VOICE: Direct, data-grounded, slightly irreverent. We write the way a sharp analyst talks to a trusted colleague — no hedging, no corporate filler, no enthusiasm without evidence. We are warm but we never pander. We are confident but we prove it with specifics, not superlatives.

TONE RULES:
- Write in active voice. Always. "Your forecast is broken" not "Forecasting challenges are commonly experienced."
- Lead with the problem or the insight, never the feature. "See why 73% of Q3 deals slip before week 6" not "Dashlink's deal health monitoring dashboard."
- Use numbers when you have them. Approximate when you don't. Never say "significantly" — say "3.2x" or "47%" or "in the bottom quartile."
- Contractions are fine. Exclamation points are not.
- Second person ("you," "your reps," "your pipeline") always outperforms third person generalities.

FORBIDDEN PHRASES (never generate these):
- "AI-powered" → say what the AI actually does: "predicts slip probability 14 days before the close date"
- "actionable insights" → say the action: "shows your reps exactly which deals to call this week"
- "end-to-end solution" → we solve one thing extremely well, not everything
- "leverage" as a verb → use "use," "apply," or "deploy"
- "scalable growth" → growth is the outcome; describe the mechanism instead
- "unlock" → our customers aren't locked out; they're making better decisions

OUTPUT STANDARD: Before publishing, every piece of Dashlink content should pass this test — could a VP Sales at a 150-person SaaS company forward this to their CEO and say "read this, it's sharp"? If the answer is no, revise.

## Integration Tips

- **Notion AI / Notion custom prompts:** Paste the Brand Constitution into Notion's AI custom instructions field — all Notion AI-generated content in your workspace will inherit the brand system automatically
- **Custom GPT (ChatGPT):** Create a private "Brand Agent" GPT with the Brand Constitution as the system prompt; share the link with every team — zero training required
- **Claude Projects:** In Claude, create a Project for each team (Sales Brand Agent, Content Brand Agent) and paste the Brand Constitution + the team's channel overlay as the project instructions
- **HubSpot AI Assistant:** Add the Brand Constitution to HubSpot's custom prompt settings; email and CRM-generated content auto-applies brand rules
- **Outreach / Salesloft:** Embed the Brand Constitution in sequence AI generation settings so every SDR-generated email applies brand rules at the point of creation
- **Slack AI (if deployed):** Add the Brand Constitution to the Slack AI custom system prompt configuration so internal AI-drafted Slack messages (especially to customers) stay on-brand
- **Zapier / Make automation:** When AI-generated content enters your review queue via Zap, attach the Brand Constitution + Rubric as a checklist item before the "approve for publish" step

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The Brand Constitution is too long and teams skip it.**
Solution: Create a 150-word "Pocket Constitution" — the top 5 voice rules, top 5 forbidden phrases, and the 1-sentence brand test. Run the full 400-word version only in Custom GPTs and Claude Projects where it's embedded once and applied automatically. The Pocket Constitution is for humans; the full Constitution is for machines.

**Problem: The AI ignores parts of the Brand Constitution and defaults to generic language.**
Solution: The most common failure point is the brand instructions competing with the task instructions — the AI prioritizes "write a cold email" over "sound like us." Fix: restructure the prompt so the brand persona comes BEFORE the task, and add an explicit instruction at the end: "Before finalizing this output, check it against the forbidden phrases list. If you find any, rewrite without changing the meaning." Specificity beats generality — replace "be direct" with "use active voice, lead with the outcome, avoid words ending in '-ize' or '-ation'."

**Problem: Different teams' Brand Constitutions are drifting out of sync as the brand evolves.**
Solution: Designate one "Brand Constitution owner" in your marketing ops team. Create a Notion page that is the single source of truth for the current Brand Constitution version. Each team's Custom GPT, Claude Project, or HubSpot prompt links back to this version. Add a quarterly calendar reminder to run the "Brand Constitution Maintenance Protocol" from Component 7, compare the current Constitution against your 3 most recent best-performing content pieces, and issue updates if drift is detected.
