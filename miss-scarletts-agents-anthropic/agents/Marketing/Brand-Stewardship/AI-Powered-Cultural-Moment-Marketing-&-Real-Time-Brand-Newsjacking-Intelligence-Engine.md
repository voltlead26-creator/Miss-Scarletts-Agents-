## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:** Create a "Moment Marketing" campaign tag in HubSpot. All content published under a reactive moment gets tagged at campaign level so you can measure influenced pipeline from moment marketing separately from evergreen content.

**Brandwatch / Talkwalker:** Set up Boolean search queries for your brand's core topic clusters. Configure velocity alerts — when a topic goes from 100 to 1,000 mentions/hour, trigger a Slack notification to your brand team's #moment-marketing channel.

**Slack Workflow:** Build a Slack workflow where any team member can submit a "moment flag" with a trending URL, the team votes Go/No-Go with emoji reactions in 15 minutes, and the brand lead makes the final call. Documented decisions = learning over time.

**Notion / Confluence:** Maintain a "Moment Marketing Playbook" that captures every attempt — go and no-go — with outcomes. This becomes your brand's institutional memory for future AI prompting context.

**Google Alerts + Zapier:** For lower-cost monitoring, set Google Alerts for your top 20 keywords at "as it happens" frequency → pipe results via RSS into Zapier → post to a dedicated Slack channel for human triage.

**Salesforce:** Add a custom field to leads: "First Touch: Moment Marketing." When a prospect's first interaction with your brand was a viral reactive post, that's a distinct acquisition segment with different conversion patterns — measure it separately.

**Buffer / Sprout Social:** Pre-build approved post templates with your voice guidelines embedded as scheduling notes. When a moment hits, your social manager can adapt a pre-approved template rather than starting from scratch, cutting approval time in half.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Content feels forced or inauthentic — the connection between the brand and the moment is a stretch.
**Solution:** Go back to the Brand-Moment Relevance Fit score. If you scored below 6/10 on topical connection, don't publish. The worst moment marketing is when a brand clearly has no business being in the conversation. Instead, use this as intel: what WOULD a natural fit look like for your brand? Write that in your playbook for the next opportunity.

**Problem:** The approval chain is too slow — by the time legal clears it, the moment has passed.
**Solution:** Create a tiered approval system. Pre-approve a library of "moment marketing templates" — formats, voice patterns, risk thresholds — that can publish with only brand manager approval. Reserve legal/CMO sign-off for content touching sensitive topics. The goal: ≤45-minute end-to-end cycle for standard moments.

**Problem:** The reactive content generated strong engagement but zero pipeline impact — followers liked it but prospects didn't convert.
**Solution:** Moment marketing's primary ROI is brand equity and top-of-funnel awareness, not direct conversion. However, add conversion layers: every viral moment post should include a soft CTA (newsletter subscribe, resource download) and be supported by a retargeting audience of engagers. Track 30-day pipeline influence — people who engaged with the moment post and later visited your pricing page, requested a demo, or matched ICP criteria.
