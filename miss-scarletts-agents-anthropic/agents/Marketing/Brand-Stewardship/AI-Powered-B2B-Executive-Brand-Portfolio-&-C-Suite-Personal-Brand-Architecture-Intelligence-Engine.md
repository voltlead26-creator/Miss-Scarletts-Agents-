- Add UTM parameters to all executive bio links and content CTAs: `?utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=executive-brand&utm_campaign=[exec-name]`
- Create a "Touched Executive Content" field in Salesforce/HubSpot to track deal overlap
- Build a weekly Slack notification (via Zapier) when a tracked account follows an executive or engages with their content

**Content Production Workflow:**
- Use Notion or Airtable to manage the shared editorial calendar across all executives
- Set up a shared AI workspace (Claude, ChatGPT) pre-loaded with each executive's voice profile for ghostwriting acceleration
- Use Loom for executive "brain dump" recordings — 10 minutes of unfiltered thinking becomes 3–4 LinkedIn posts after AI processing

**Analytics & Reporting:**
- Brandwatch or Mention.com for share-of-voice tracking across executive name + topic cluster
- Shield Analytics or Taplio for LinkedIn-specific performance data (unavailable in native LinkedIn)
- Build a Looker Studio or Tableau dashboard combining LinkedIn analytics, UTM-tagged pipeline data, and share-of-voice metrics

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Executives agree to the plan but then go silent — posts stop after week 2.**
*Solution:* The root cause is almost always friction in the production process, not lack of willingness. Implement a "10-minute brain dump" protocol: each executive records a Loom (voice or video) answering 2–3 prepared questions weekly. Marketing converts these into polished posts for exec review and one-click approval. Target: exec time investment under 30 minutes/week.

**Problem: The CEO's content is performing well but is generating controversy — off-brand statements that create sales friction.**
*Solution:* Establish a pre-publish review gate with a 24-hour window for CMO or communications review on any post before it goes live. Implement a "Brand Guardrail Card" — a one-page document each executive keeps accessible with their 5 "never say" statements and 3 "always say" alternatives for sensitive topics. Define in advance what triggers escalation to CMO review (competitor mention, regulatory topics, personnel statements, customer naming).

**Problem: Attribution is unclear — it's hard to prove executive brand content drives pipeline.**
*Solution:* Use three measurement proxies in parallel: (1) Milestone-attributed pipeline — track accounts that follow ≥2 executives AND show buying intent signals within 30 days; (2) Sales-self-reported attribution — add a "How did you first hear about us?" field to discovery calls and track "saw [exec name]'s post" responses; (3) CRM first-touch tracking — any account where the first CRM activity is a LinkedIn click from executive bio counts as executive-brand sourced.
