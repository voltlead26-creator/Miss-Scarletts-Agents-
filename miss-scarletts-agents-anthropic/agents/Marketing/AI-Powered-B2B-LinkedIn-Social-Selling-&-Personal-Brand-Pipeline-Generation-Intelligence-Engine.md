**4-STEP WARM DM SEQUENCE:**

DM 1 — The Genuine Opener (send within 24-48 hours of warm trigger):

Length: 60-80 words max. No links. No CTA to book a call.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts get strong impressions but no ICP-fit engagement — only peers and recruiters engage**
*Cause:* Content is written for the seller's peer group (other sellers/marketers), not for the buyer
*Fix:* Audit your last 10 posts — if the topics are about sales tactics, LinkedIn tips, or your own career, you've built an audience of sellers, not buyers. Shift 100% of content to topics your ICP cares about (their job problems, their industry trends, their business metrics). The easiest reset: pick your ICP's most painful daily challenge and write 5 posts explaining why that challenge exists and how to solve it. Sellers will disengage; buyers will start following.

**Problem: DM open rates are high but no one replies after DM 1**
*Cause:* The DM reads like a soft pitch despite good intentions — or the engagement trigger wasn't warm enough
*Fix:* (1) Check DM 1 for any phrase that sounds like setup for a pitch: "I help companies like yours," "I noticed you might be dealing with," "We work with companies in your space." These phrases trigger sales resistance instantly. Remove them. (2) Verify the engagement trigger was genuinely warm — if you only liked their post without commenting, it's not warm enough for a DM. Add a substantive comment first, wait 24-48 hours for them to reply or like your comment, then DM. (3) Make your DM question so specific to what they actually said that they feel compelled to answer — generic curiosity signals low effort.

**Problem: Warm DM sequences are progressing but meetings aren't converting to SQLs**
*Cause:* Social selling is attracting wrong-stage or wrong-ICP contacts — people who are curious but not actively evaluating
*Fix:* Add a soft qualification question earlier in the sequence (DM 2 instead of DM 3): "Is [their stated problem] something your team is actively trying to fix this half, or is it more on the backburner?" — this surfaces urgency before investing further in the relationship. Also review your content pillars: if your posts attract people interested in the topic but not in buying a solution, adjust to include more content about the cost of inaction, urgency signals, and evaluation criteria — which self-selects for active buyers over passive readers.
