## Troubleshooting

**Problem: We published GEO-optimized content 60 days ago but still aren't appearing in AI answers.**
Solution: AI models have different crawl/index latency than Google. Check that: (1) Perplexity has indexed your content (search "site:yourdomain.com" in Perplexity), (2) Your content includes direct, scannable answers in the first 100 words — AI engines prefer content that immediately answers the question, (3) Your domain authority is sufficient — for competitive queries, you may need 15–25 referring domains pointing to that specific page before AI engines weight it highly, (4) Test with slightly different query wordings — AI citations are query-specific.

**Problem: We appear in AI answers but competitors are always mentioned before us (or instead of us).**
Solution: This is a share-of-voice problem, not a visibility problem. Build the "consensus signal" — AI models favor brands that appear across multiple independent sources. Execute Tier B and Tier C authority building (trade publications, community presence, G2 reviews) to increase the number of independent sources that mention your brand in context of these queries. Also audit whether competitor content has more specific data (statistics, benchmarks) than yours — AI models preferentially cite quantitative claims.

**Problem: Our CEO doesn't see GEO as a real demand channel and won't fund it.**
Solution: Run a 30-day pilot measuring only Perplexity click-throughs (easiest to track via referrer data in GA4). Perplexity users click through to sources at 2–4× the rate of Google users, and it's trackable. If you can show even 20–50 monthly sessions from Perplexity that convert to form fills at your existing site conversion rate, the pipeline math becomes compelling. Present the pilot as "inbound SEO insurance" — GEO authority protects your existing organic traffic as Google incorporates more AI answers and reduces traditional click-through rates.
