## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Field PMs engage with content but COOs never respond — pipeline stalls at field champion level**
Solution: Your content is too tactical — field PMs read "how to manage RFIs better" but COOs read P&L risk. Reframe every content piece in financial terms: not "faster RFI resolution" but "reducing the $1.2M average cost of a 30-day RFI delay on a $15M project." Create a COO-specific content track with executive roundtable dinners as the primary engagement mechanism — COOs respond to peer conversations, not white papers. Also build an internal champion kit: give your field PM champion a 10-slide deck, a CFO business case template, and a peer reference contact to hand to their COO directly.

**Problem: "We just bought Procore 2 years ago — why change?" objection kills 40%+ of deals**
Solution: This is a sunk cost / vendor consolidation objection, not a features objection. Build a dedicated "Procore + [Your Platform]" integration narrative — position as a financial intelligence layer on top of Procore, not a replacement. Create content specifically targeting "Procore users who still have cost control problems" — this is a high-intent search cluster. Develop a "Procore Gap Analysis" one-pager that quantifies what Procore doesn't deliver on job cost forecasting and financial visibility. Price your solution against the cost of overruns ($95K platform vs. $340K+ annual overrun reduction) — reframe the buying decision from "replace Procore" to "close the profitability gap Procore left open."

**Problem: Long sales cycle (9–12 months) is exhausting marketing budget before deals close**
Solution: Introduce a "90-Day Paid Pilot" program at $15,000–$25,000 for one active project — this converts the longest-cycle deals into short-term revenue while the full platform deal progresses. The pilot also functions as a proof-of-concept that eliminates the COO's technology risk objection. Track pilot-to-full-platform conversion rate (target: 75%+) — this becomes your most powerful sales asset. Also deploy quarterly executive roundtable dinners to maintain COO relationship during the long evaluation cycle without requiring sales rep time on every touchpoint.
