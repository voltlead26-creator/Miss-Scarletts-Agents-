## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sales leadership won't commit to the bi-directional SLAs — they view it as marketing "grading" them.**
Solution: Reframe the SLA conversation as a revenue partnership, not accountability theater. Start with Marketing's SLAs only — make Sales the "customer" for 60 days. Once Marketing proves it can hit its SLAs consistently, propose the bi-directional model as a way to "protect the pipeline together." The first SLA you ask of Sales should be the easiest possible one (e.g., disposition leads within 5 days, not 3). Build trust before adding accountability.

**Problem: The Revenue Council becomes another status meeting — no real decisions get made.**
Solution: Add a mandatory "Decision Log" to every meeting. The last 5 minutes are reserved for recording decisions made and decisions deferred (with owner and deadline). Any meeting with zero decisions logged is flagged by RevOps as a "zombie meeting" and either gets restructured or killed. Appoint a rotating "Decision Enforcer" role each quarter — this person's job is to call out when the group is discussion-looping instead of deciding.

**Problem: Product doesn't want to give Marketing 6 months advance notice — they say the roadmap changes too much.**
Solution: Implement a "Confidence Tier" system. Month 1-3 roadmap items are "High Confidence" (PMM plans for them). Month 4-6 items are "Planning Signal" (PMM does lightweight preparation only). Month 7+ are "Exploratory" (no marketing preparation needed). This removes the risk for Product of over-committing while giving Marketing enough signal to avoid surprise launches. Start by asking for 6-week notice only — that's a low bar Product can commit to immediately.
