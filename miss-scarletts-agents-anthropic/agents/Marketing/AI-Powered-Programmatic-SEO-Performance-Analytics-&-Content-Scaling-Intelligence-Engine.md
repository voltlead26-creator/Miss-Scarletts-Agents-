# AI-Powered Programmatic SEO Performance Analytics & Content Scaling Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25-30 min | **Tags:** seo, programmatic-seo, content-scaling, organic-analytics, b2b, saas, traffic-intelligence

## Overview
This engine analyzes your programmatic SEO operation across hundreds to thousands of auto-generated pages — diagnosing template performance, keyword cannibalization, index bloat, content decay, and pipeline attribution — to maximize organic traffic and revenue from scaled content production. Use it when running a programmatic SEO program with 200+ auto-generated pages and need to optimize the system, not individual pieces.

## Integration Tips

- **Google Search Console API + Google Sheets:** Automate weekly export of programmatic page performance data segmented by URL pattern using GSC's URL prefix filter. Build a dashboard that auto-calculates clicks-per-indexed-page by template type. Zapier can trigger a Claude analysis run whenever a new weekly dataset is loaded.
- **Ahrefs / Semrush Site Audit:** Schedule weekly crawls specifically scoped to your programmatic page URL patterns. Export "thin content," "duplicate content," and "non-indexable" flags and pipe into this prompt for action planning.
- **Screaming Frog + Python:** For large programs (5,000+ pages), use Screaming Frog in scheduled mode to crawl your XML sitemap weekly and flag any pages returning 4xx/5xx status codes or triggering near-duplicate content warnings.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Tag inbound form submissions and demo requests with the referral URL to identify which programmatic templates drive highest-quality pipeline (not just traffic). Build a Salesforce report or HubSpot contact property for "first-touch programmatic template."
- **Notion / Airtable content database:** If your programmatic pages are generated from a database (as most are), maintain a field for index status, last GSC impressions, and conversion data per record. Use this prompt's output to flag records for human editorial review or automated refresh.
- **Google Looker Studio:** Build a programmatic SEO health dashboard with template-level performance cards, indexation rate gauge charts, and a conversion attribution waterfall — refresh daily from GSC API.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Google is declining to index a large % of your programmatic pages ("Crawled - currently not indexed")**
Solution: This is typically a content quality signal, not a crawl budget issue. Pull a sample of 20 non-indexed pages and run a content quality audit: Are they meaningfully different from each other? Does each page have at least 400 words of unique content? Does the page answer a real question or primarily contain a list of links? Improving content quality on 50–100 non-indexed pages first will signal to Google that your templates produce helpful content, and indexation rates typically improve within 4–8 weeks. Avoid the temptation to submit these pages via the GSC URL inspection tool repeatedly — it doesn't override quality signals.

**Problem: Traffic is heavily concentrated (top 5% of pages drive 80%+ of traffic)**
Solution: This is normal for programmatic SEO programs under 12 months old, but signals that most templates haven't achieved ranking velocity. Run the template performance matrix to identify which templates have strong underlying search demand but weak on-page signals. The fastest fixes are: (1) improve title tags to match exact-match keyword intent, (2) add FAQ schema markup to capture People Also Ask slots, and (3) improve internal linking from high-authority pages to your best programmatic templates. Traffic concentration typically normalizes to 70/30 distribution (top 20% driving 70% of traffic) within 6–9 months for healthy programs.

**Problem: High programmatic traffic, near-zero conversions**
Solution: Programmatic pages typically attract informational-stage traffic. If conversion rates are below 0.3%, the issue is usually: (1) no visible above-the-fold CTA relevant to the page's search intent — comparison pages need a "See how we compare" CTA, use-case pages need a "See [product] for [use case]" demo CTA, (2) CTA copy is generic ("Request Demo") rather than intent-specific ("Compare pricing with [Competitor]"), or (3) the page serves a non-buyer intent (researcher, student, competitor employee). Segment programmatic traffic by keyword intent category and apply conversion benchmarks per intent tier: commercial intent pages should convert at 0.8–1.5%, informational at 0.1–0.3%.
