## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Customer gave vague answers ("it improved things a lot") without specific metrics**
Solution: Use the Advanced prompt's Phase 1 to extract Jobs-to-be-Done and emotional outcomes instead of leading with data. Reframe: "What would you tell a peer who asked if it was worth it?" and use that language. Credible specificity beats manufactured metrics — "our Monday morning pipeline meetings went from 2 hours of arguing to 20 minutes of decisions" is more believable than a vague percentage.

**Problem: Legal/PR won't approve customer naming or specific metrics for publication**
Solution: Generate two versions from the start: a named version (submitted for approval) and an anonymized version ("A Fortune 500 fintech company") with the metrics intact. The anonymized version can go live immediately while approval is in progress. Use the industry and company size as identifiers: "A 450-person fintech company processing $2B+ annually." Run the named version through your standard legal approval workflow using the [NEEDS APPROVAL] flags in the output.

**Problem: The interview covered 6 different use cases and there's no clear single story**
Solution: Segment the outputs. Run the Advanced prompt once per distinct use case/outcome, treating each as a separate "story module." Then create a master case study that frames it as "Three Ways Meridian Transformed Revenue Operations," using each module as a section. This gives you both atomic proof points (for specific objections) and a comprehensive enterprise narrative (for executive buyers evaluating breadth of impact).
