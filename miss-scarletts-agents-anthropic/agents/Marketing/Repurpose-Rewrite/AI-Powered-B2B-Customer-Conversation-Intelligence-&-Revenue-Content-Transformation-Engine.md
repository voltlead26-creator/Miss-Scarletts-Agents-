## Integration Tips
- **Gong**: Create a "Content Flag" tracker topic to automatically tag calls containing content-worthy moments (strong metaphors, competitive mentions, quotable pain statements). Export tagged highlights weekly to the content team as a structured digest.
- **Chorus/Salesloft**: Use smart playlists to build a "Content Mine" collection of best discovery calls; review in a weekly 15-minute content sprint. Chorus's AI summary feature generates call debrief data you can paste directly into the Quick Copy-Paste version above.
- **HubSpot**: Tag all content created from conversation intelligence with a "VOC-sourced" custom property. Build a report comparing engagement metrics (clicks, conversions, pipeline influenced) for VOC-sourced vs. non-VOC content to quantify the method's ROI.
- **Notion**: Build a "Conversation Intelligence Content Library" database where each entry contains: source call date and deal stage, extracted buyer quotes, content assets created, publish date, and 30/60/90-day performance metrics. Use Notion AI to surface themes across entries monthly.
- **Slack**: Create a #content-intelligence channel and ask every AE to share one memorable quote or insight per week after their calls. Content team turns the best submissions into LinkedIn posts within 48 hours. Public wins reinforce the behavior.
- **Make (Integromat) or Zapier**: Automate the pipeline — Gong highlight clip created → webhook fires → pushes transcript to Notion content library → creates Asana content brief ticket → notifies content team in Slack. Full zero-touch handoff from call to content queue.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Content based on one call doesn't resonate with the broader ICP**
Solution: Run this prompt with a batch of 5-10 calls rather than a single conversation. Explicitly instruct the AI: "Identify which pain themes appear in 3 or more calls, and which are idiosyncratic to a single account. Only build content around themes with 40%+ call frequency." Content derived from repeated, cross-account patterns will land with the whole market, not just one buyer.

**Issue: Sales team won't share call recordings due to customer privacy concerns**
Solution: Use structured call debrief templates instead of raw recordings. Create 5 CRM fields your reps fill in after every call: (1) most memorable customer quote, (2) new objection heard for the first time, (3) competitor name mentioned, (4) outcome language the customer used for success, (5) one content idea generated by the call. Even partial completion across your team creates a rich, privacy-safe content intelligence stream within 30 days.

**Issue: Output still sounds like marketing copy even when using customer language**
Solution: Add this constraint to any prompt variant: "Under no circumstances should this content mention our product by name, make a product claim, or include a CTA in the body of the piece. The goal is to make readers feel understood — not sold to. Write as if you are a practitioner who experienced this problem personally and is sharing what they learned." This one instruction eliminates 90% of the 'salesy' tone problem.
