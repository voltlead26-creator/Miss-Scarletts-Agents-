## MISSION: PHASE 1 — VOICE-OF-CUSTOMER INTELLIGENCE EXTRACTION

### 1A. Buyer Language Codebook

Analyze every review and extract a language codebook organized by category:

**Pain Language (Before State):**
Extract every phrase customers use to describe the problem before your product. Group by theme. Mark frequency (how many reviews mention each theme). Flag the most emotionally charged language — these are gold for ad copy and hero sections.

Format:
| Pain Theme | Exact Customer Language | Frequency | Emotional Intensity (1-5) | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**Outcome Language (After State):**
Extract every phrase describing results, improvements, and transformation. Group by: time savings, revenue impact, team efficiency, customer satisfaction, risk reduction. Mark which outcomes are specific (have numbers or percentages) vs. qualitative.

Format:
| Outcome Theme | Exact Customer Language | Specific/Qualitative | Reviewer Title | Best Use Case |
|---|---|---|---|---|

**"Aha Moment" Language:**
Find reviews that describe the moment of realization — when the customer first understood the product's value. These are often the most powerful for mid-funnel content and demo follow-up sequences.

**Differentiation Language:**
Extract every mention of how your product compares to alternatives, including:
- Direct competitor comparisons (with specific differences named)
- "We tried X before and switched because..." narratives
- Feature advantages mentioned organically (not prompted by review questions)
- Switching decision language ("we chose [Product] because...")

**Persona Language:**
For each reviewer role (VP CS, Implementation Manager, CTO, etc.), extract:
- The specific problems they care most about (role-specific pain)
- The outcomes they measure success by (role-specific wins)
- The language patterns specific to their role (ops vs. strategy vs. technical)

### 1B. Sentiment Pattern Analysis

**Positive Theme Clustering:**
Group all positive mentions into themes. Rank by frequency. For each theme:
- Is this theme featured prominently in your current marketing? (Yes/No/Partially)
- Is this theme featured in your top competitor's marketing? (Flag if you have a differentiated positive that competitors can't claim)
- What content format would amplify this theme best? (Case study / video testimonial / landing page section / email / LinkedIn post)

**Negative Theme Clustering:**
Group all critical mentions (3-star and below + cons sections of 5-star reviews). For each negative theme:
- Frequency and severity rating
- Is this a product issue, onboarding issue, or expectation-setting issue?
- Can this be addressed with content (FAQ, help doc) or does it require product/pricing changes?
- What is the best preemptive marketing response?

**Hidden Gem Identification:**
Find reviews that mention product capabilities or outcomes that are underrepresented in your current marketing. These are your highest-leverage content opportunities — customers love them but you're not marketing them.

## CONSTRAINTS AND QUALITY STANDARDS

- Never fabricate or paraphrase customer language — all quotes must come directly from the input reviews. Flag any claim that cannot be attributed to specific review data.
- Do not use review data in ways that would violate reviewer privacy (use first name + last initial, never full names without explicit permission).
- Every content asset must pass this test: "Would a skeptical buyer be more convinced after reading this?" If not, it's not strong enough.
- Competitive claims from reviews must be clearly framed as "what customers say" not "objective fact" to stay compliant with advertising standards.
- All generated copy should sound like a real person wrote it, not like AI-generated marketing. Read each output aloud — if it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.
- Flag any review that appears fake, incentivized, or potentially violating G2/Capterra terms of service.

Output format: structured sections with clear headers. Where tables add clarity, use them. All copy assets should be ready to use with minimal editing.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Review input is too thin — only 5-10 reviews, making pattern analysis unreliable.**
Solution: Do not generate content yet. First, run a review generation sprint: email your 50 most successful customers this week with a personal note from the CSM asking for a G2 review. Target 20 reviews minimum before running this prompt. In the interim, supplement with customer interview transcripts, NPS open-text responses, and win/loss call notes to build a richer voice-of-customer dataset.

**Problem: All the reviews are generic praise without specific language ("Great product! Love the team!") — no useful quotes to extract.**
Solution: These reviews are typically a product of generic review request emails. Implement the Review Quality Improvement section of Phase 3 immediately — specifically the coached review prompts ("Tell us about the biggest time you saved"). Contact your top 10 reviewer-customers and ask them to update their reviews with more specific details, offering a small token of appreciation (gift card, charity donation in their name). Also check if review prompts on your G2 page are too open-ended.

**Problem: The objection themes in reviews don't match the objections my sales team hears most.**
Solution: This is a meaningful signal — either (a) dissatisfied prospects never become customers and therefore never leave reviews (your review base is selection-biased toward happy customers), or (b) your sales team is not surfacing the real objections in deal reviews. Supplement with: win/loss interview transcripts from deals you lost, CRM "reason lost" field data, and Gong call recordings tagged "objection." Build your objection-handling guide from this complete picture, not reviews alone.
