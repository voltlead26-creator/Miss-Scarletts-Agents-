## Integration Tips
- **Notion**: Create an "Innovation Lab" database with one row per channel pilot; link to pilot brief, Day 30/60 scorecards, and go/no-go decisions. Use Notion AI to auto-summarize monthly channel performance reports.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce**: Add "Original Source Detail" or custom "Self-Reported Source" field to capture dark-funnel channel attribution from demo/trial request forms.
- **Slack**: Set up a `#channel-innovation` channel where AI agents post weekly citation monitoring reports, anomaly alerts from experimental channel tracking, and go/no-go recommendation summaries.
- **Google Looker Studio / Tableau**: Build an "Innovation Portfolio" dashboard that shows all active pilots in one view with traffic, engagement, pipeline signal, and budget pacing — link to the main marketing performance dashboard so CMO has one view.
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate the weekly channel monitoring workflow — AI agent queries Perplexity/ChatGPT for brand citations, scrapes performance metrics from relevant platforms, and appends results to the Notion innovation tracker automatically.
- **Linear / Jira**: Create a "Channel Innovation" project with standardized issue templates for pilot setup, Day 30 checkpoint review, and Day 60 decision — ensures nothing falls through the cracks when the team is busy with core channels.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The pilot produces data but it's impossible to attribute pipeline to the experimental channel.**
Solution: Implement self-reported attribution (add "How did you first hear about us?" to every demo/trial form with explicit platform options). For B2B, also run a 1-question Typeform survey to newly created opportunities asking about pre-pipeline research behavior. Accept that 20-30% of AI search-influenced pipeline will show as direct — use trend analysis (does "direct" traffic from ICP-matching companies increase during pilot?) as a proxy signal.

**Problem: The CFO or CEO kills the innovation budget because "we haven't proven ROI on existing channels yet."**
Solution: Reframe the narrative using the Insurance Model: "This $18K/quarter isn't optional experimentation — it's competitive intelligence spend. If [major competitor] launches a new channel and finds a $120 CPL before we do, they'll have 12 months of head start. We're buying early information, not lottery tickets." Present the structured go/no-go framework to demonstrate that every dollar is accountable, and share the channel graduation model showing how last year's experimental channel ([example]) now contributes [X]% of monthly pipeline.

**Problem: The team launches a pilot but no one owns the go/no-go checkpoints, and the channel just runs indefinitely without a decision.**
Solution: Create a standing "Channel Portfolio Review" on the CMO's calendar for the last Friday of each month. Before the meeting, the AI agent auto-generates a Channel Status Report for all active pilots. Each pilot is explicitly in one of three states: IN-PILOT (gathering data), DECISION PENDING (checkpoint hit, awaiting CMO go/no-go), or GRADUATED/KILLED. Make channel decisions a formal record in the innovation tracker — the absence of a decision is never acceptable.
