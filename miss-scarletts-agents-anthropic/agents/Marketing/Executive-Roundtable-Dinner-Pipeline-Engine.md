## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot:** Create a custom "Executive Event" campaign type. Tag every attendee contact with the event name, date, and post-event qualification tier (Hot/Warm/Cold) as a contact property. Build a pipeline report filtered to event-sourced and event-influenced opportunities to track 90-day ROI.
- **Outreach / Salesloft:** Build the 3-touch invitation sequence as a cadence with smart steps (email → LinkedIn task → call task). Use field merge for personalization variables. Set a "no-reply" branch to auto-advance through touches; set a "replied" branch to notify the AE for personal follow-up.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Build the target guest list using Lead Lists filtered by job title, company size, and seniority. Use "Changed jobs in past 90 days" and "Posted on LinkedIn in past 30 days" as activity filters to prioritize the warmest executives for your anchor outreach.
- **Notion / Google Docs:** Build the facilitator guide as a shared doc that the AE accesses on their phone during cocktail hour for quick reference on each guest's pre-briefed background and pain signals.
- **Zapier / Make:** When an executive responds to the invitation (via form or email tag), auto-create a task in your CRM, add them to the confirmed guest list, and trigger the Day-before reminder email at T-24 hours.
- **Calendly / Chili Piper:** Embed a "Book a follow-up call" link in Day 1 post-event emails for hot leads. Use round-robin routing to the correct AE based on account ownership in CRM.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low RSVP rate (below 25% of invites sent)**
*Solution:* Audit three things in order: (1) List quality — are these actually your ICP, or are you spraying broadly? (2) Invitation framing — if it sounds like a vendor event, executives opt out instantly. Test removing your company name from the subject line and leading with the peer problem instead. (3) Anchor credibility — executives respond to "your peer from [Company X] is attending," not "industry experts will join." Secure your anchor guests before sending the first wave and use their names explicitly with permission.

**Problem: High RSVP rate but low show rate (below 55%)**
*Solution:* Executive no-shows spike when the event feels low-stakes to cancel. Fix: (1) Send a personal phone call or LinkedIn message from a senior executive on your team (not an SDR) 48 hours before the event — humanizes the commitment. (2) Brief anchor guests to personally text 1–2 of their confirmed peers the day before. Peer-to-peer accountability raises show rates dramatically. (3) Send a day-before teaser with one compelling stat or discussion topic that creates genuine FOMO.

**Problem: Attendees engaged during dinner but follow-up sequences go cold**
*Solution:* The gap between event excitement and post-event action is the most common failure mode. Fix: (1) Capture an explicit micro-commitment during the event itself — "Would it be helpful if I sent you the peer benchmarks we discussed?" is a low-commitment yes that creates a natural follow-up hook. (2) Personalize Day 1 emails with a direct quote or observation from the dinner — generic "thanks for attending" emails get ignored; "You mentioned your Q3 board deadline" gets opened. (3) For Warm leads, share a post-event insight summary within 24 hours — it demonstrates the event delivered value and keeps you top-of-mind while the conversation is fresh.
