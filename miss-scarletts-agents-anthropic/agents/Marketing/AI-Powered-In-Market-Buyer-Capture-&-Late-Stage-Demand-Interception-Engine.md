# AI-Powered In-Market Buyer Capture & Late-Stage Demand Interception Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25-30 min | **Tags:** demand-gen, inbound, intent-data, review-sites, b2b, competitive-displacement, late-stage, abm, conversion

## Overview
Identifies and intercepts buyers who are actively evaluating solutions in your category but have not yet engaged with your brand — then deploys a coordinated system across review sites, comparison SEO, intent-based retargeting, and programmatic ABM to capture them at peak buying readiness. Use this when you have strong product-market fit but are losing deals to competitors you're never even invited to evaluate, or when intent data shows accounts spiking on category keywords without converting to pipeline.

- **G2 Buyer Intent + HubSpot/Salesforce:** Use G2's native CRM integration to push Buyer Intent signals as contact/company property updates in real time; build a workflow that creates a high-priority SDR task when a target account views your G2 profile or a competitor's profile. - **6sense / Demandbase:** Map 6sense account stage (Awareness / Consideration / Decision / Purchase) to specific email nurture tracks, ad audiences, and SDR priority tiers. Use 6sense's account segment builder to create a "Hot Intent" list that auto-syncs to LinkedIn Campaign Manager as a Matched Audience for same-day retargeting activation.
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Build a "Competitor Comparison" campaign specifically targeting job titles at companies showing intent signals. Upload your CRM list of intent-spiking accounts as a Matched Audience and layer on job title targeting. Use LinkedIn's "Engagement Retargeting" to build an audience of people who interacted with your organic content — combine with intent to create a "warm + in-market" super-segment.
- **Google Ads:** Create separate campaigns for each high-intent keyword cluster (comparison, alternative, review). Use Customer Match to suppress existing customers and current opportunities from comparison campaigns (don't waste budget on accounts already in pipeline). Set up "target ROAS" bidding on comparison campaigns once they have 30+ conversions — Google's algorithm will find in-market buyers similar to those who have converted.
- **Zapier / Make automation:** Build a Zap that fires when a contact visits the pricing page twice in 7 days → creates a high-priority HubSpot task for their assigned SDR + sends a Slack alert to the account AE → automatically enrolls the contact in a "high-intent pricing page" retargeting audience via the HubSpot-LinkedIn integration.
- **Drift / Intercom (website chat):** Deploy a targeted chatbot on comparison pages and the pricing page that activates only for visitors from companies matching your ICP (use Clearbit Reveal or 6sense for firmographic identification). Chatbot opening message should reference the comparison context: "Evaluating [category] solutions? Here's what [Company Type] teams typically ask us first" — not a generic "Can I help you?"

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Comparison pages aren't ranking despite being published for 3+ months**
Solution: Comparison pages fail to rank for one of three reasons: (1) Insufficient backlink authority — your domain authority is too low to outrank a competitor who has 10× more backlinks. Fix: Run a targeted digital PR campaign to earn 5–10 backlinks specifically to the comparison page from relevant SaaS review publications, industry blogs, and community forums. A single link from a high-DA review site (G2's blog, Capterra's editorial) can move a comparison page 15+ positions. (2) Thin content — 800-word pages won't outrank 3,000-word comprehensive comparisons. Audit your top competitor's comparison pages: word count, schema markup, and UX elements. Match depth, then add original data (review scores, feature tables, customer quotes) they don't have. (3) Keyword mismatch — you're targeting "Raven vs CrowdStrike" but buyers search "CrowdStrike alternative" or "CrowdStrike competitor." Use Google Search Console to see what queries are impressioning for your comparison pages and optimize for actual search patterns, not assumed ones.

**Problem: G2 Buyer Intent signals are generating SDR outreach but few positive responses**
Solution: The failure mode here is SDRs calling out the intent signal explicitly ("I saw you were looking at our G2 profile...") or leading with a generic pitch despite knowing the account is evaluating. Fix the outreach angle: intent data tells you *when* to reach out, not *what to say*. Structure the outreach around the comparison context without revealing the signal — e.g., "IT teams evaluating EDR solutions often have the same question about implementation timeline, so I wanted to share how [Similar Company] went live in 72 hours." This is relevant to where they are (evaluation) without creeping them out. Also check: are your SDRs reaching the right person? G2 Buyer Intent shows company-level signals; the person researching may be an analyst, not the economic buyer. Have SDRs identify the actual IT Director or CISO at the account and contact them directly.

**Problem: Retargeting campaigns have strong impression delivery but near-zero demo conversions**
Solution: High impressions with low conversions on retargeting almost always means audience-offer mismatch — you're showing the wrong message to a technically correct audience. Diagnose by segment: (1) Pricing page retargeting converting poorly → the offer is too soft (you're showing a blog post to someone who just evaluated your pricing). They need a direct-response offer: "Compare total cost with [Competitor] in 20 minutes" with a one-click booking link. (2) Blog readers retargeting converting poorly → this is expected. Blog retargeting rarely converts at demo rate — the right conversion event is an email capture for a high-value asset (ROI calculator, buyer's guide). Don't measure blog retargeting on demo conversion. (3) General site retargeting performing well but pricing page retargeting flat → your pricing page experience is the problem, not the retargeting. The ad is working but the page is creating friction. Test: direct pricing page retargeting traffic to a dedicated landing page with a clear offer, not back to the pricing page itself.
