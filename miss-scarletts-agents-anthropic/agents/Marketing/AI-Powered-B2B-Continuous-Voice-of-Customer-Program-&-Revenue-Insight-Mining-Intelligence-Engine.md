## Overview
Designs and runs a fully automated, always-on B2B Voice of Customer (VoC) intelligence program that continuously mines customer signals across every touchpoint — turning raw feedback, NPS surveys, support tickets, review sites, win/loss interviews, and sales call transcripts into prioritized revenue actions. Use it when you need to replace ad-hoc customer research with a systematic, AI-agent-driven insight loop that directly informs product roadmap, messaging, and GTM decisions.

## Integration Tips

- **Gong / Chorus:** Use Topic Trackers to auto-flag calls containing competitor names, pricing language, or churn risk signals. Export weekly summary to Slack channel via webhook.
- **Gainsight / Totango:** Map VoC insight categories to customer health score attributes. When a theme (e.g., "data coverage complaint") reaches threshold frequency for an account, trigger an automated CS task.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Use the weekly Flash Report template as a recurring Notion database entry. Tag insights by theme, segment, and source for searchable institutional memory.
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Create custom properties for "VoC-driven churn risk" and "VoC-flagged expansion signal" — update via Zapier when Zendesk tickets or NPS responses meet threshold criteria.
- **Slack:** Build a #voc-intelligence Slack channel. Use Zapier or Make.com to auto-post: (a) new G2 reviews within 1 hour, (b) NPS Detractor submissions immediately, (c) weekly Flash Report every Monday 8am.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Response rates on NPS and CSAT surveys are below 15%, making the data statistically unreliable for segment-level analysis.**
Solution: Shift from batch surveys to transactional triggers — send NPS 30 days after onboarding completion, 7 days after a support ticket close, and 14 days before renewal date. This typically 3-4x response rates because the timing is relevant. Also reduce survey to 1 question + 1 open text field — every additional question cuts completion rate by ~12%.

**Problem: The AI extraction prompts produce generic themes ("customers want better support") rather than specific, actionable insights.**
Solution: Force specificity by restructuring your extraction prompt with three constraints: (1) require exact verbatim quotes (no paraphrasing), (2) require segment context (company size + role + tenure for every insight), and (3) require a revenue connection hypothesis (what business metric is affected). Generic themes are a symptom of inputs without context — always include customer metadata alongside the feedback text.

**Problem: Stakeholders ignore the VoC reports because they feel like "research outputs" rather than business decisions.**
Solution: Restructure every deliverable to lead with the action, not the finding. Instead of "27% of customers report X," write "We need to [specific action] because 27% of customers in [segment] told us [exact quote] — this is costing us an estimated $[X] in churn risk." Revenue leaders act on revenue framing, not research framing. Run a 15-minute monthly "VoC Review" in the leadership team meeting where you present exactly 3 insights with proposed owners and ask for a decision, not just awareness.
