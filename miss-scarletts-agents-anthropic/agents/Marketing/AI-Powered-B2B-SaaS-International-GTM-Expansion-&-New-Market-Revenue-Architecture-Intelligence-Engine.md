## SECTION 6: International Expansion Risk Register

For each risk: **Description | Probability (H/M/L) | Impact (H/M/L) | Early Warning Signals | Mitigation | Contingency**

**Risk 1: Regulatory/Compliance Surprise**
Unforeseen data residency, privacy, or industry-specific compliance requirement blocks contract signing or requires costly infrastructure investment before first close. Early signal: Legal redlines concentrate on data clauses in first 5 deals. Mitigation: Engage local privacy counsel before first customer conversation; build GDPR/local data processing addendum into standard contract from Day 1.

**Risk 2: ICP Mismatch**
US ICP assumptions materially wrong — local buyer title, deal size, or sales cycle differ by >30%. Early signal: Discovery call conversion to SAO below 15% in first 60 days. Mitigation: Treat AI-generated ICP as hypothesis; run mandatory 20-call calibration sprint with first local hire at Day 45 and adjust TAL accordingly.

**Risk 3: Talent Acquisition Failure**
Unable to hire quality first AE/SDR within 60-day target window, delaying pipeline by 2+ months. Early signal: Offer rejected by top 2 candidates; recruiter reports thin local candidate pool. Mitigation: Pre-engage 2 specialist local sales recruiters before budget approval; prepare EOR contractor option as 90-day bridge.

**Risk 4: Competitive Preemption**
Incumbent local competitor or US rival accelerates expansion in direct response to [COMPANY_NAME]'s market entry announcement. Early signal: Competitor-sourced FUD showing up in discovery calls; deal losses citing competitor's new local team. Mitigation: Avoid premature public announcement of expansion; brief top analyst before competitor does; build battle card specific to local competitor dynamics.

**Risk 5: Channel Partner Underperformance**
Selected channel partners fail to generate expected pipeline; partner-led entry produces <50% of projected pipeline contribution. Early signal: No partner-sourced SAOs by Month 4. Mitigation: Require mutual business plan with signed partner before granting MDF; maintain 2 backup partner candidates per market; be willing to pivot to direct model if channel lags by Month 5.

**Go/No-Go Trigger**: If cumulative ARR at Month 6 is below 40% of Base projection AND pipeline coverage is below 2x, trigger a structured market review: (a) invest more to accelerate, (b) extend runway with lower burn rate, or (c) pause market entry and redirect resources to stronger market.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Market scores are too close to confidently rank**
Solution: Add a tiebreaker criterion — "fastest path to first reference customer." Which market has the most pre-existing relationships (existing US customer with local office, warm partner intro, or analyst with local relationships)? Run a "regret minimization" frame: which market would you most regret ceding to a competitor if they established leadership there first?

**Issue: Revenue ramp projections feel too optimistic for board**
Solution: Apply a conservative "international discount factor" — use 1.5x your domestic sales cycle assumption for months 1-6, and 1.25x for months 7-12. Present the ramp as three scenarios (Bear/Base/Bull) with a narrative for what would need to be true for each. Boards trust ranges more than point estimates, and it demonstrates analytical rigor rather than wishful thinking.

**Issue: First local hire rejects the AI-generated ICP after 30 days of discovery calls**
Solution: Treat the AI-generated ICP as a falsifiable hypothesis, not a mandate. Build a structured "ICP calibration sprint" at Day 45 — local hire presents findings from 20 discovery calls against each ICP assumption. Update the TAL and messaging accordingly. The AE's pattern recognition from local calls is a higher-quality signal than AI-derived assumptions from non-local data.
