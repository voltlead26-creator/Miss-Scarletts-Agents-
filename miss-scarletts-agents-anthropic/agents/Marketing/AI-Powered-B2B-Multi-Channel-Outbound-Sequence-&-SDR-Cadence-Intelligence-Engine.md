## Integration Tips

- **Outreach / Salesloft:** Map all personalization tokens (funding date, trigger event, persona-specific hook) to custom prospect fields before importing. Use "step-level A/B" to test subject line variants directly inside sequences. Set OOO detection to auto-pause and auto-resume at touch following the OOO window.
- **Apollo.io:** Use Apollo's sequence auto-enrollment to trigger cadence entry when a prospect matches the saved ICP search filter (e.g., "VP Legal Ops + Series C in last 90 days"). Sync reply dispositions to HubSpot via native integration.
- **HubSpot Sequences:** Use workflow-based enrollment to auto-enroll contacts who hit a lead score threshold or who visited the pricing page. Use HubSpot's "If/then" branches to route positive replies directly to AE-owned sequences.
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Build a saved search matching your Tier 1 trigger criteria. Export to CSV weekly, enrich with Clay or Apollo, and auto-enroll into cadence. Use TeamLink to identify warm paths before cold outreach.
- **Clay.com:** Use Clay to build a real-time personalization waterfall: Pull LinkedIn headline → Pull recent company news → Pull funding data from Crunchbase → Pull tech stack from Clearbit → Generate AI-personalized opening line per row. Push enriched data to Outreach/Apollo in one click.
- **Gong / Chorus:** After meetings are booked from the cadence, tag the source sequence in Gong. Over time, analyze which opening lines, subject lines, and channels correlate with deals won — feed that signal back into cadence optimization.
- **Salesforce:** Log cadence step completions as Activities. Create a "Cadence Source" field to track which cadence variant generated each opportunity. Build a dashboard showing meeting-booked rate and sourced pipeline per cadence version.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Email reply rate below 3% despite strong list quality.**
Solution: Audit the opening line of touch 1 — it's almost always the culprit. The first sentence must reference something specific, researchable, and relevant to the prospect's current situation. Replace generic value prop openers ("We help companies like yours...") with trigger-specific hooks ("Saw {Company} just announced {event}..."). Also verify subject line is under 7 words and avoids spam-trigger words (free, guaranteed, limited time, solution, ROI).

**Problem: LinkedIn connection requests being ignored (acceptance rate below 20%).**
Solution: The connection note is likely too pitchy or too long. Strip the message down to a single genuine observation or shared context (mutual connection, their recent post, shared industry event) — no pitch, no ask, no URL. Also ensure the rep's LinkedIn profile has a professional photo, complete About section, and recent activity before outbound volume increases. Prospects vet the profile before accepting.

**Problem: Cadence completion rate is low — prospects falling out before touch 5.**
Solution: Check bounce rates on email (if >5%, the list needs additional email validation via NeverBounce or ZeroBounce before import). Check if reply-handling automation is pausing sequences correctly — if positive replies aren't auto-pausing, reps are sending follow-ups to booked meetings, damaging credibility. Also confirm that phone voicemails are being logged as completed steps, not skipped, to avoid artificial gaps in the sequence.
