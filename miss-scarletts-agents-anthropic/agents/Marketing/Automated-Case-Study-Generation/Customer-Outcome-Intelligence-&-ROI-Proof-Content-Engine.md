# Customer Outcome Intelligence & ROI Proof Content Engine - AI-Powered Revenue Impact Story Mining & Business Case Generation

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** b2b, case-study, roi, proof-of-value, enterprise, sales-enablement, content, automation

## Overview
Mines raw customer data — CRM notes, support tickets, QBR summaries, usage metrics, NPS verbatims, and renewal conversations — to surface quantified ROI stories, then auto-generates compelling proof assets (case studies, one-pagers, business case frameworks, and champion decks) calibrated to specific buyer personas, deal stages, and verticals. Use this when your pipeline is stalling because you lack proof that speaks to a prospect's specific industry and pain, or when your PMM team needs a scalable system to turn every closed deal into revenue-generating proof content within days.

- Size: [Employee count / ARR]
- Product used: [Product/plan]
- Time as customer: [X months]
- Key stakeholders: [Champion name/title, Economic buyer title]

Do the following:

1. OUTCOME MINING — Scan all data for any explicit or implicit metrics: time saved, revenue impacted, cost reduced, headcount avoided, churn prevented, deals closed faster, productivity gains. List every quantifiable outcome you find, even partial ones (e.g., "saved 2 hrs/week per rep").

2. STORY ARCHITECTURE — Build the narrative using the Problem-Amplification-Solution-Outcome (PASO) framework:
   - Before state: What was broken before? Be specific about pain.
   - Amplification: What was the cost/risk of that problem? Hard numbers.
   - Solution: How did our product solve it specifically? Not features — actions.
   - Outcome: The 3 headline metrics. Format: [X% improvement in Y] or [Saved $Z in Q].

3. PROOF ASSET OUTPUT — Produce these three ready-to-use assets:
   A. 150-word case study snippet (for website/ads)
   B. Sales one-pager talking points (bullet format, champion-ready for internal selling)
   C. Subject line + opening line for an email nurture using this proof (for prospects in same industry)

4. CONFIDENCE RATING — For each metric you used, rate it: Verified (from hard data), Inferred (calculated from partial data), or Stated (customer said it but unverified). Flag any that need follow-up.

Tone: Specific, credible, no marketing fluff. Every claim must connect to a named outcome.

## Integration Tips

- **CRM (Salesforce / HubSpot)**: Build a custom object for "Customer Proof Points" with fields mapped to each metric category. Trigger the prompt automatically when a deal moves to "Closed Won" via workflow + AI integration (e.g., Salesforce Einstein + Claude API).
- **Gong / Chorus**: Pull call transcripts from QBRs and renewal calls directly into the prompt input. Gong's Call Library + API can auto-feed this engine with tagged "success story" calls.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Output proof assets directly into a shared Customer Evidence Library with tagging by vertical, use case, deal size, and persona. Use Notion AI to surface relevant stories to AEs searching for competitive proof.
- **Highspot / Seismic / Showpad**: Auto-publish completed one-pagers and snippets into your sales enablement platform with searchable metadata (industry, competitor displaced, metric type).

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Customer data is too thin — only 1-2 vague metrics, no specifics.
**Solution:** Use the Evidence Gap Analysis output (Step 6) to generate a structured 15-minute customer discovery call. Ask: "What would you tell your peer at a similar company about the impact?" and "If you had to justify renewal to your CFO in one number, what would it be?" Often the best metrics are never formally recorded — they exist in informal conversations. Also use the Inferred calculation method: take partial data (e.g., "saved 2 hrs/week") and extrapolate across user count + loaded hourly cost to produce a credible estimated figure, clearly labeled as such.

**Problem:** The ROI story sounds generic — "saved time, improved efficiency" — indistinguishable from competitors.
**Solution:** Force specificity with the PASTE method: **P**erson (who specifically), **A**ction (what exact workflow changed), **S**ituation (before state with a number), **T**ransformation (what's different now with a number), **E**vidence (what verifies this). Generic = "we saved time on reporting." Specific = "Priya's 6-person ops team eliminated 3-day board prep cycles by automating data consolidation from 4 source systems, recovering 288 hours/quarter."

**Problem:** Champion loves the story but can't get the CFO to care — economic buyer isn't responding.
**Solution:** Translate the operational metric into a financial one the CFO owns. Use the ROI Amplification Method: time savings → loaded cost calculation → P&L line item. Pair with the "Objection Handler" asset (Section D), specifically the "we don't have budget" response. The CFO needs to see the cost of the status quo, not just the benefit of buying — frame the proof as: "This is what staying on your current system costs you every quarter."
