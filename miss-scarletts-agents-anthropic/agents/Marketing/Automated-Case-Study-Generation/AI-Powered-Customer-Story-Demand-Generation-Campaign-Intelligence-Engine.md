Use this when you have a compelling customer outcome and want to extract maximum pipeline impact from it without manual campaign planning.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a campaign tag (`[Customer]-story-campaign`) and attach all assets. Use the email copy in HubSpot Sequences for the outbound 3-touch. Set up a Contact List of lookalike prospects for enrollment. Use the campaign UTM parameters to track pipeline influence in HubSpot Attribution Reports.
- **Salesforce:** Add a Campaign object for this story campaign. Log the case study as a campaign member touchpoint. Use the battle card addition in the Sales Enablement tab of opportunity records. Build a report: "Opportunities touched by [Story Campaign] in last 90 days."
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Use the ad copy directly in Sponsored Content. Create a Matched Audience from your target account list. Set up a retargeting audience for anyone who clicks the case study link and serve them the ABM one-pager ad next.
- **Outreach / Salesloft:** Load the 3-touch cold email sequence as a cadence. Map to the correct persona sequence based on title. Enable A/B testing on Subject Line variants from Section 2.3.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Store the full campaign brief in your content operations wiki. Link the story to your Content Proof Library so sales can self-serve.
- **Zapier:** Automate: when a new deal is tagged "Closed Won" + matches customer profile → trigger a task to your content team to collect case study inputs → route completed inputs to this prompt workflow.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The assets feel generic even though you provided specific data.
**Fix:** The inputs are the quality ceiling. If your "results" section says "improved efficiency," the prompt cannot manufacture specificity. Go back to the customer and get the actual numbers: revenue saved, time reduced, headcount avoided, percentage changes with timeframes. The more granular the input, the more specific and credible the output.

**Problem:** Your legal team won't approve naming the customer publicly.
**Fix:** In the CONSTRAINTS field, add: "Cannot name customer — use [INDUSTRY] descriptor instead (e.g., 'a mid-market SaaS company in financial services')." Specify this in every asset instruction. The story still works with anonymization; just ensure the industry descriptor is specific enough to be credible.

**Problem:** The campaign calendar feels overwhelming — you can't execute all 8 asset types simultaneously.
**Fix:** Add this instruction: "Produce a minimum viable campaign version with only [LinkedIn Organic × 2] + [Cold Outbound Email × 3] + [Sales Battle Card]. I will expand to full channel activation in Phase 2." This lets you launch in days, not weeks, and add channels as capacity allows.
