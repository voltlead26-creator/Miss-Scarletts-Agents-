## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Customer gives vague, safe answers during interview ("It's been great, really positive experience")**
*Fix:* Pause and reframe: "I love hearing that. Can you help me make that real for someone watching this? If you had to pick ONE specific moment when you thought 'okay, this was the right call' — what comes to mind?" Always probe for a specific moment, not a general sentiment. If they struggle, offer a prompt: "Was there a meeting, a report, a conversation where you noticed something was different?"

**Problem: Legal/procurement blocks video release for 6+ months**
*Fix:* Get written case study and audio-only testimonial approved first (faster review cycle). Use the interview transcript to generate a written case study immediately. Once internal approvals are done, film the video. For highly regulated industries (healthcare, finance, government), prepare a "sanitized" version brief that replaces specific numbers with ranges ("reduced costs by more than 30%") and removes named individuals while keeping company logo.

**Problem: The video looks amateur and customers are embarrassed by the production quality**
*Fix:* This engine includes a Remote Recording Technical Checklist for a reason — use it. For high-value customers ($100K+ ARR), budget for a local Fiverr/Thumbtack videographer to film on-site. The $500-$1,500 spend on professional filming pays back immediately in close rate improvement. Alternatively, use a service like Testimonial.to for self-recorded videos with guided prompts — quality is lower but conversion still beats no video at all.
