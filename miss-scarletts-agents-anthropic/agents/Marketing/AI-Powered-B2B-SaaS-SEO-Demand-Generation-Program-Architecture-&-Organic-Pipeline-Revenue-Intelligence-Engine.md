## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Content is ranking but not converting to MQLs**
Solution: This is a conversion architecture problem, not a traffic problem. Audit your top 10 organic landing pages using Microsoft Clarity or FullStory — identify where users exit without converting. Add inline CTAs at scroll depth 40% and 70%, test a bottom-of-post demo booking CTA, and implement exit-intent overlays with a content upgrade specific to that page's topic. Common fix: the CTA offer doesn't match the visitor's intent stage — decision-stage pages need demo CTAs, not newsletter signup.

**Problem: Organic traffic growing but organic pipeline attribution looks flat**
Solution: Usually a UTM and attribution setup problem. Verify: (1) organic sessions in GA4 are properly tagged and not misattributed to direct; (2) UTM parameters are passing through to your CRM form submissions; (3) your attribution window covers the full sales cycle length (if sales cycle is 90 days, set attribution lookback to 120 days minimum). Also check whether your pipeline attribution model credits organic in multi-touch sequences — many CRMs default to last-touch paid, which buries organic contribution.

**Problem: Competitors dominate decision-stage keywords and rankings aren't improving**
Solution: Competitor comparison and alternative pages lose to competitors who use real data and social proof. Upgrade your comparison pages with: (1) side-by-side feature tables with linked proof points; (2) G2/Capterra review quotes specific to the pain the competitor has; (3) customer migration case studies showing measurable ROI lift after switching; (4) a dynamic "last updated" date in page metadata and schema to signal freshness. Also build backlinks specifically to these pages through digital PR and partner placements — comparison pages need domain authority concentrated on them, not just distributed site-wide.
