## Integration Tips

**Customer.io / Intercom / HubSpot Sequences:**
- Load all 8 email templates as a single workflow with behavior-based suppression rules
- Suppress any email in the sequence the moment a user hits the Aha Moment event (don't keep nurturing someone who already converted)
- Tag all contacts with `onboarding_stage` property so sales has real-time context during upgrade conversations

**Mixpanel / Amplitude / PostHog:**
- Create a dedicated "Onboarding Funnel" report tracking all 6 stages defined above
- Set up a cohort comparison: "Users who hit Aha Moment within 7 days" vs. "Users who didn't" — compare 30-day conversion rate and ACV
- Build a "Stuck User" alert: fire a Slack notification to growth/CS when an ICP-fit account has been in signup state for 48+ hours without completing Step 1

**Pendo / Appcues / Userflow (DAP):**
- Build the Critical Path checklist as a persistent side-panel widget (not a modal that blocks work)
- Trigger celebration confetti/animation exactly at Aha Moment completion — this is not vanity, it anchors the memory of the value moment
- A/B test in-app tooltip copy natively without engineering using your DAP's experimentation module

**Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:**
- Sync `aha_moment_reached` and `trial_days_remaining` as CRM properties
- Set up a PQL alert workflow: when `aha_moment_reached = true` AND `company_size >= 100`, create a CRM task for an SDR to send a personalized upgrade email within 4 business hours
- Build a "Self-Serve Onboarding" pipeline stage in CRM to track conversion velocity separate from outbound pipeline

**Zapier / Make.com:**
- Connect Mixpanel event `aha_moment_completed` → HubSpot contact property update → SDR Slack notification
- Connect `trial_expiry_approaching` (T-48h) → trigger personalized video message from Loom template via Vidyard or Sendspark

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Aha Moment definition is fuzzy — the team can't agree on what it is.**
Fix: Run a cohort correlation analysis in your product analytics tool. Identify the single in-product event that, when completed in the first 7 days, most strongly predicts 90-day retention and paid conversion. Don't define the Aha Moment by intuition — let the data define it. If you don't have enough conversion data yet (<200 paying customers), use qualitative customer interviews: ask your best customers "when did you first realize this was worth paying for?"

**Problem: Email open rates are low (<20%) despite good subject lines.**
Fix: The problem is almost certainly sender reputation or send timing, not copy. Check: (1) Are you sending from a product subdomain (app.yourcompany.com) vs. your main domain? Use your main domain. (2) Are you sending at 9am in the user's timezone or at the server's timezone? Always localize. (3) Is your list warming configured correctly for new trial signups? Ramp volume slowly if you're sending >500/day from a new domain.

**Problem: Users complete Step 1 but still don't reach the Aha Moment.**
Fix: The gap is between "data connected" and "report generated" — this is almost always a UX issue, not a marketing issue. The user doesn't know what to do next after connecting their data. Audit the post-connection experience: is there a clear next step CTA? An auto-generated sample report? A "now do this" tooltip? If the product experience between Step 1 completion and Aha Moment requires more than 3 clicks, that's an engineering fix, not a copy fix. Escalate to product with data showing the drop-off point.
