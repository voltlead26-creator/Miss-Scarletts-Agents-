# Cold Email Sequence Builder - Multi-Touch Outbound Cadence Generator

**Difficulty:** Intermediate | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** b2b, email, outbound, prospecting, conversion

## Overview
Generates a complete, personalized multi-touch cold email sequence (5–7 touches) tailored to a specific ICP, pain point, and offer — with subject lines, body copy, and follow-up logic baked in. Use it when building outbound cadences for a new campaign, entering a new market segment, or re-activating cold accounts.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are an expert B2B sales copywriter specializing in cold outbound email. Write a 5-touch cold email sequence targeting [Job Title] at [Company Type, e.g., Series B SaaS companies with 50–200 employees].

My company: [Company Name] — we help [ICP] achieve [specific outcome] without [common pain/cost].
Primary pain point we solve: [Pain Point]
Our proof point: [Stat or case study, e.g., "Customers cut onboarding time by 40%"]

Sequence rules:
- Email 1: Pattern interrupt + insight-led opening. No pitch. End with a soft question.
- Email 2 (Day 3): Add social proof or relevant case study. One clear CTA.
- Email 3 (Day 7): Reframe the problem from a different angle. Use a "have you considered" approach.
- Email 4 (Day 12): Short. Conversational. Reference their likely objection directly.
- Email 5 (Day 18): The breakup email. Low pressure, leave the door open.

For each email provide:
1. Subject line (and one A/B variant)
2. Preview text (under 90 characters)
3. Body copy (under 150 words)
4. P.S. line (optional but powerful)

Tone: direct, peer-to-peer, no buzzwords, no "I hope this finds you well."

SEQUENCE STRUCTURE:

Touch 1 — Day 0 | Insight Hook
- Framework: Lead with a data point, industry trend, or counterintuitive observation relevant to their role
- Goal: Earn a reply or click — NOT to pitch
- CTA: Single soft question ("Is this something your team is navigating?")

Touch 2 — Day 3 | Social Proof
- Framework: Peer-company story — "A [similar company] was dealing with [same pain] and here's what changed"
- Goal: Build credibility and relevance
- CTA: "Would a 15-minute call be worth it to see if we can do the same for [Company]?"

Touch 3 — Day 7 | Reframe
- Framework: Challenger Sale — show them something they haven't considered about their current approach
- Goal: Create constructive tension
- CTA: Ask if they'd like to see the analysis/framework

Touch 4 — Day 12 | Objection Pre-empt
- Framework: Address the most likely objection head-on ("I know [common objection] — here's why this is different")
- Keep it under 80 words
- CTA: Restate the single offer clearly

Touch 5 — Day 18 | Breakup
- Framework: Give them an easy out, but make the benefit of replying clear
- Tone: Lighter, even slightly self-aware
- CTA: "Just let me know either way — I won't follow up after this."

[Optional Touch 6 — Day 25 | Trigger-Based Re-entry]
- Only send if a trigger event occurs (job change, funding round, hiring surge, product launch)
- Reference the trigger directly in line 1

[Optional Touch 7 — Day 35 | Long-game Nurture]
- Send a piece of content (report, case study, tool) with zero ask
- Sets up future pipeline without pressure

## Success Metrics
- Reply rate: Target 3–8% for a well-targeted sequence (industry benchmark: 1–2%)
- Positive reply rate: 40%+ of replies should be "interested" or "not now" (not "remove me")
- Open rate: 40–55% for touch 1 (strong subject line indicator)
- Meeting booked rate: 1.5–4% of total sequence recipients
- Unsubscribe/spam rate: Below 0.5% — if higher, revisit targeting or tone

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Low open rates (under 25%) on Touch 1**
Solution: Your subject line is the bottleneck — not the body. Test 3 variants: (1) a specific data point, (2) a question, (3) a counterintuitive statement. Avoid all-caps, emoji, and words like "free," "guarantee," or "demo" in subject lines — they trigger spam filters.

**Problem: Replies are mostly "not interested" or "remove me"**
Solution: Your targeting is off, not your copy. Revisit your ICP criteria — job title, company size, industry, and tech stack must all align. A well-targeted sequence should generate "not now" replies, not hard rejections. Tighten the list before scaling.

**Problem: Touch 1 gets replies but subsequent touches go cold**
Solution: Touch 2 is likely too pitchy after a strong opener. If someone didn't reply to Touch 1, they haven't earned a full case study yet — soften Touch 2 to a lighter "following up on a thought" style. Reserve the full proof point for Touch 3.
