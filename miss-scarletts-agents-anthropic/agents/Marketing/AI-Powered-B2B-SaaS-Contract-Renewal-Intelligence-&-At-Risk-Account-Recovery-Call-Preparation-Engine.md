## Overview
Transforms raw account health signals, usage data, and engagement history into a complete renewal call intelligence package that maximizes contract retention and expansion — even for accounts flagged as churn risk. Use this before every renewal conversation (60–90 days out) to give your CS team, AE, or renewal manager a data-backed playbook that turns at-risk accounts into expansion wins.

## Success Metrics

- **Renewal closed on-time**: Signed contract before original end date (no lapse)
- **NRR impact**: Renewal ACV ≥ prior year (ideally expansion attached)
- **Churn risk reclassification**: Account moves from Red to Yellow/Green post-call engagement
- **Champion re-engagement**: Named new internal champion within 2 weeks of call
- **Expansion planted**: Expansion either closed or in active evaluation within 30 days post-renewal
- **Time-to-close**: Signed agreement within 14 days of renewal call
- **Competitive displacement prevented**: No active evaluation by next QBR

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The AI-generated talk track feels generic despite detailed input.**
Solution: The quality of "voice of customer quotes" and "open issues" fields drives specificity. If you have Gong access, pull 2-3 verbatim customer quotes from call transcripts and paste them directly. The model uses customer language to mirror back their framing — without real quotes, it defaults to generic phrasing.

**Problem: The churn risk score seems too low for an account I know is in trouble.**
Solution: Add a "renewal blocker" field to your input with explicit signals you know (e.g., "Budget cut in Q1 memo leaked by champion," "New CFO said on all-hands he's cutting SaaS spend 20%"). Implicit signals like low NPS don't always outweigh positive usage metrics without context. The more qualitative intelligence you add, the more accurate the risk calibration.

**Problem: The expansion play feels forced given the account's health score.**
Solution: For Red-rated accounts (risk ≥7), use the NEGOTIATION PARAMETERS section and omit the expansion play from the call entirely. Insert the expansion only in the follow-up email (not the live call) as a post-renewal bonus. Leading with expansion when an account is at-risk signals misaligned priorities and accelerates churn.
