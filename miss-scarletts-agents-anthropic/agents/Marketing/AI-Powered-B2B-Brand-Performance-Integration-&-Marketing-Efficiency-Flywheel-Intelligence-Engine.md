## Overview

Use this when rising CPLs suggest performance saturation, when the CFO challenges brand spend, or when you need to architect a marketing mix that balances short-term pipeline with long-term brand equity.

## Troubleshooting
- **Problem:** CFO rejects brand budget because "there's no direct revenue attribution in 90 days"
  **Solution:** Reframe brand investment as "CAC insurance" — calculate the 3-year CAC inflation cost at current trajectory without brand, and show that number exceeds the brand budget request by 3-5x. Present performance-only as a financial risk, not a marketing preference. Propose a 6-month experiment with pre-agreed success metrics and a kill clause if indicators don't respond.

- **Problem:** Performance team resists brand budget reallocation because it reduces their MQL targets
  **Solution:** Restructure OKRs to include "brand halo metrics" (branded search growth, direct traffic lift) alongside MQL volume. Then design a 60-day incrementality test: pause brand campaigns to establish a baseline, reactivate, and measure the lift in performance CPLs from brand-warmed audiences. Most companies see 15-30% CPL improvement on performance campaigns running simultaneously with active brand campaigns — make that data the argument.

- **Problem:** No competitive SOV data available to build the business case
  **Solution:** Build a DIY SOV proxy using free tools: (1) Use SparkToro to estimate what share of your ICP's digital attention goes to you vs. competitors; (2) Use SEMrush/Ahrefs "Share of Voice" report for your top 20 category keywords; (3) Count LinkedIn followers and monthly post engagement for you vs. top 3 competitors; (4) Count G2 review velocity (new reviews per month) as a proxy for brand momentum. Combine into a composite "Brand Momentum Index" you can track quarterly.
