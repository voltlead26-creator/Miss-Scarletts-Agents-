## Integration Tips

- **Highspot / Seismic:** Upload every SKO asset (battlecards, win stories, sequence templates) immediately after the event. Tag by competitor, vertical, and deal stage so reps find them during live deals.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Configure scorecards to detect the new messaging keywords introduced at SKO. Run weekly coaching reports for 6 weeks post-event to measure adoption decay.
- **Salesforce:** Create a custom SKO field on Opportunity to track whether reps are applying the new plays — useful for pipeline inspection by managers.
- **HubSpot Sequences:** Pre-load the 3 Q1 pipeline plays as named sequences reps activate in one click. This removes friction and drives adoption of the outbound playbook.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build a living SKO Hub page with links to all recordings, battlecards, win stories, and the pipeline play library. Keep it updated through the year so it remains the single source of truth.
- **Slack:** Create a `#sko-wins` channel where reps post when they use SKO messaging to close a deal or progress an opportunity. Social proof drives peer adoption faster than manager mandates.
- **Spekit / Allego:** Push microlearning reinforcement of the 3 breakout session key concepts as 3-5 minute video modules, delivered in weeks 2, 4, and 8 post-SKO.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The prompt generates generic messaging that doesn't feel specific to our company or competitive situation.
**Solution:** Ensure Module 4 competitive context includes the exact phrases competitors are using in customer conversations — pull from Gong call recordings and G2 reviews before running the prompt. The more specific the competitive "landmines" you provide, the sharper the output.

**Problem:** Breakout session designs are too theoretical and not executable in a 45-minute slot.
**Solution:** Add this constraint to your prompt: "Each session must include one 15-minute role-play with a specific scenario card the facilitator reads aloud. Remove any slide-heavy instructional content longer than 10 minutes. Prioritize practice over presentation." This forces the engine to design for adult learning, not information transfer.

**Problem:** Win stories produced feel anonymous and lack emotional impact.
**Solution:** Provide a real deal from your CRM — company size, industry, the rep's name (optional), the specific objection that was overcome, and the exact ROI number. Instruct the prompt to use the Present-Situation-Complication-Resolution narrative structure and write the verbal version as if the rep is telling it over the phone, not presenting to a boardroom.
