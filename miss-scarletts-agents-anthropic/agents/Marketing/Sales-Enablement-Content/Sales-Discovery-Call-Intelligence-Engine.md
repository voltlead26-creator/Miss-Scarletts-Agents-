OUTPUT FORMAT (produce all 8 sections):

**[SECTION 1] PRE-CALL INTEL BRIEF**
Structure as a 2-minute read:
- Company snapshot: business model, growth phase, key priorities inferred from signals
- Contact profile: likely motivations, communication style, career risk/ambition drivers
- Talk triggers: 2-3 specific conversation starters tied to real signals (funding, hiring, product launch, reviews)
- Red flags: anything that could mean wrong fit, wrong timing, or wrong buyer
- Hypothesis: your best guess at their #1 pain and desired outcome before the call starts

**[SECTION 2] DISCOVERY QUESTION BANK — SPIN FRAMEWORK**
Generate 16 questions total. Label each with its SPIN type and the MEDDIC field it helps uncover.

Situation Questions (4): Establish current state without interrogating
- Focus on: existing process, tools, team structure, current metrics
- Avoid: questions answerable via LinkedIn or their website

Problem Questions (4): Surface explicit pain and dissatisfaction
- Focus on: where current process breaks down, what keeps them up at night
- Use contrast framing: "What does [current state] cost you when it goes wrong?"

Implication Questions (4): Expand the pain to organizational impact
- Connect individual pain to: revenue impact, churn, team morale, executive pressure, competitive disadvantage
- Example frame: "If [problem] continues through next quarter, what happens to [metric they care about]?"

Need-Payoff Questions (4): Get them to articulate the value of solving it
- Prompt them to sell themselves: "If you could eliminate [problem], what would that unlock for your team?"
- Surface their internal ROI language for use in the proposal

**[SECTION 3] MEDDIC / MEDDPICC QUALIFICATION SCORECARD**
For each letter, define:
- What "confirmed" looks like (specific answer or evidence)
- The discovery question that unlocks it
- What to do if you can't confirm it by end of call

M — Metrics: Quantified impact of the problem + success definition
E — Economic Buyer: Who controls budget, their priorities, how to reach them
D — Decision Criteria: Formal evaluation criteria, scoring rubric if they have one
D — Decision Process: Steps from interest to PO, typical timeline, legal/procurement involvement
I — Identify Pain: Explicit pain statement in their words, business impact confirmed
C — Champion: Is this contact a champion? Can they sell internally without you?
[if MEDDPICC add:]
P — Paper Process: Contract, security review, MSA, legal cycle
C — Competition: Who else is in the deal, where you stand, their bias

**[SECTION 4] CHALLENGER COMMERCIAL TEACHING INSIGHT**
Craft a 90-second "Reframe" the rep can deliver mid-call to shift the prospect's thinking:
- Lead with an insight they haven't considered (not a product pitch)
- Connect it to a financial or competitive implication
- Tie it back to your unique capability as the only logical solution
- Use the structure: "Most [role]s we talk to believe [conventional wisdom]. What we've found is [disruptive insight]. That means [implication]. Companies that act on this are [outcome]."

**[SECTION 5] OBJECTION QUICK CARDS**
Identify the 4 most likely objections for this specific persona/stage. For each:
- Objection (in their words)
- Root cause (what fear or priority is driving it)
- Disarm response (acknowledge + redirect in 2 sentences)
- Trap-setting question (a follow-on question that advances your position)

**[SECTION 6] CALL CLOSE SCRIPTS — 3 OUTCOME PATHS**

Path A — Strong Advance (champion confirmed, pain confirmed, timeline clear):
Exact script to propose a specific next step (exec call, technical eval, POC kickoff) with a date

Path B — Soft Advance (interest confirmed, champion unclear, timeline vague):
Exact script to create a follow-up commitment with mutual action plan (MAP) framing

Path C — Disqualification (budget missing, wrong persona, no pain):
Exact script to gracefully disqualify while leaving door open and getting a referral if possible

**[SECTION 7] POST-CALL CRM NOTE TEMPLATE**
Formatted for HubSpot or Salesforce activity log. Include:
- MEDDIC fields as structured fields with [PLACEHOLDER] for rep to fill
- Deal stage recommendation based on what was confirmed
- Recommended next step with owner and due date
- Internal notes section (not visible to prospect) with coaching flags

**[SECTION 8] FOLLOW-UP EMAIL**
Send within 2 hours of call. Include:
- Subject line (use a specific insight or phrase from their actual words)
- Opening: 1-sentence mirror of what they said their #1 priority is
- Body: 3-bullet recap of what was discussed (problems, implications, desired outcome)
- Value bridge: 1 sentence connecting their stated outcome to your solution's mechanism
- Clear CTA: specific ask with a deadline (e.g., "Are you free Thursday at 2pm ET for the 30-min technical walkthrough?")
- P.S.: a relevant case study, stat, or resource specific to their industry

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The discovery questions feel too similar to what's already in battle cards or objection handling frameworks.
**Solution:** Discovery questions are forward-looking and exploratory — they surface unknown pain. Battle cards are defensive and reactive. If overlap occurs, it means your battle card questions are being used too early in the process. Keep discovery questions anchored to SPIN (understanding their world) and reserve battle card language for when a competitor is explicitly named.

**Problem:** The Challenger Reframe sounds like a product pitch and prospects disengage.
**Solution:** The reframe must lead with an insight about their industry or role, not your product. If the insight starts with "Our platform..." it's wrong. Reframe to start with "Most [VP Ops / RevOps / etc.] we work with find that..." and withhold the product tie-in until after they react to the insight.

**Problem:** Post-call email open rates are low even with personalization.
**Solution:** The subject line is the primary lever. Use a direct mirror of a phrase the prospect actually said on the call (e.g., "About the 'intake chaos' you mentioned") — this creates a pattern interrupt and signals you were actually listening. Avoid subject lines that sound like a sales follow-up ("Next steps from our call," "Great chatting today").
