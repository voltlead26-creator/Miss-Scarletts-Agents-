## 3. Discovery Question Architecture
Use layered SPIN + Challenger methodology:

**Situation Questions (context-setting, 3-4 max):**
- One question about current state of [relevant process]
- One question about team structure/headcount
- One question about current tools/stack
- One question about how success is measured today

**Problem Questions (surface pain, 4-5 questions):**
- Questions that expose the hidden cost of the status quo
- Designed to make the prospect articulate the problem in their own words
- Include one "ballpark" ROI question to prime financial thinking

**Implication Questions (deepen pain, 3-4 questions):**
- Questions about second-order effects of the problem
- Stakeholder impact questions (who else is affected by this?)
- Future-risk questions (what happens if this isn't solved in 12 months?)

**Need-Payoff Questions (co-create the vision, 2-3 questions):**
- Questions that help the prospect describe what "solved" looks like
- Questions that connect the solution to strategic business outcomes
- "If you could..." questions that surface ideal outcomes

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The generated questions sound too formal or scripted when spoken aloud.**
Solution: Add this instruction to the prompt: "Write every question the way an experienced rep would say it naturally in conversation — use contractions, informal language, and shorter sentences. Avoid overly formal constructions."

**Problem: The MEDDPICC scorecard is too generic and not specific to our sales motion.**
Solution: Customize the prompt with your specific qualification criteria. Add a section: "Our qualification standard: [describe what a qualified deal looks like for your company — deal size threshold, timeline requirement, must-have use case, etc.]"

**Problem: Discovery questions are too "salesy" — they telegraph the solution instead of exploring the problem.**
Solution: Add this constraint: "Each discovery question must be genuinely neutral — it should not hint at our solution. The question should work equally well in a call for any vendor in this space. Avoid questions that sound like setup for our product pitch."
