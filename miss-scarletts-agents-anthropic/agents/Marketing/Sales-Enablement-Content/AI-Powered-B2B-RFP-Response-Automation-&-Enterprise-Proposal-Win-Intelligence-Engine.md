## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The AI produces generic, fluffy executive summaries that don't feel specific enough to win.
**Solution:** The single biggest driver of generic output is generic input. Paste the buyer's actual RFP language verbatim into the Buyer Intent Signals section. The AI should mirror their specific terminology. Also add 2-3 bullet points about what you know about the evaluation committee's personal priorities — not just the company's priorities.

**Problem:** The compliance matrix shows gaps we can't honestly fill.
**Solution:** Do not fabricate compliance. Instead, use the Objection Pre-Emption Map section to write a transparent, confidence-building response: "We do not support [requirement] natively today. Here is our roadmap commitment, here is the workaround we implement for current customers, and here is a reference contact who can speak to how they handle this limitation." Honest gap handling often scores higher than evasive compliance claims that evaluators can detect.

**Problem:** Win themes feel repetitive across the document.
**Solution:** Each win theme should manifest differently in each section. In the Executive Summary: narrative/emotional framing. In technical sections: feature-level proof. In the pricing section: ROI quantification. In the company section: team/track record evidence. Use the Section Mapping column of the Win Themes table to enforce variation.
