You are a senior management consultant and CFO advisor with 15+ years building executive business cases for enterprise software investments. You've helped procurement teams at Fortune 1000 companies justify millions in technology spend to skeptical finance committees.

Build a complete Executive Business Case & Investment Justification document for the following purchase:

**Purchase Context:**

- Champion: [e.g., VP of Sales Operations, Sarah Chen]

- Current Situation: [e.g., Sales team of 45 reps, 22% win rate, 90-day average sales cycle, reps using manual CRM updates and no call intelligence]
- Primary Problem Being Solved: [e.g., Low win rates, poor forecast accuracy, long sales cycle, new rep ramp time 9 months]

**Build a complete Executive Business Case including:**

1. **Executive Summary** (1 page, CFO-ready) — The business problem, the solution, the financial impact in Year 1 and 3-year NPV, and the recommendation with urgency rationale. Write for a CFO who has 90 seconds to decide if this goes forward.

2. **Current State Problem Quantification** — Translate the operational problem into dollar impact. Calculate the fully-loaded cost of the status quo: lost revenue from low win rates, cost of slow ramp time, opportunity cost of poor forecast accuracy, manager time wasted in manual pipeline reviews.

3. **Future State Financial Model** — 3-year ROI projection with conservative, base, and optimistic scenarios. Calculate: revenue uplift from win rate improvement, cycle time reduction value, rep productivity gains, manager time recovered. Show payback period and IRR.

4. **Cost-of-Inaction Analysis** — What happens if we delay 6 months? 12 months? Make the cost of waiting concrete and quantified. Include competitive risk: what are competitors gaining while we delay?

5. **Implementation & Risk Mitigation Plan** — Timeline, resource requirements, integration complexity, change management approach. For each risk: likelihood, impact, and specific mitigation. This is the section that kills deals — pre-answer every CFO objection.

6. **Vendor Selection Rationale** — Why this vendor vs. build vs. alternatives. Total cost of ownership comparison. Reference customer proof points with specific metrics from companies of similar size and stage.

7. **Decision Recommendation & Next Steps** — Clear ask with decision date, approval path, contract structure options (including phased approach if budget is a concern), and the one metric that will prove success in 90 days.

Output: A complete, formatted business case document ready to present to the CFO and executive committee, written in the champion's voice, using professional financial language with specific numbers throughout.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The financial model feels too aggressive and the CFO won't trust the numbers.
**Solution:** Explicitly label every assumption as "conservative" or "vendor benchmark" and show your math. Include a sensitivity table that shows what win rate improvement is needed just to break even. CFOs trust models they can stress-test. Add a line: "This investment breaks even if we capture just [X] additional deals per quarter — [X]% of our current pipeline."

**Problem:** The champion doesn't have enough internal data to fill in the baseline metrics accurately.
**Solution:** Use industry benchmarks from Gartner, Forrester, or Sales Benchmark Index as proxies, clearly labeled. For win rate: use the CRM's closed-won/closed-lost data. For ramp time: ask HR for average time-to-full-quota. Most champions have access to this data within 24 hours — build the data collection into the champion enablement conversation.

**Problem:** The CFO wants a pilot before committing to a 3-year contract.
**Solution:** This is a good sign — they're engaged, not saying no. The Advanced prompt includes a phased commitment option (Section 7.3, Option C). Pre-build the pilot success criteria into Section 6.3 so that the pilot is designed to prove the ROI thesis, not just test the product. Frame the pilot as "a 60-day business case validation" rather than a free trial.
