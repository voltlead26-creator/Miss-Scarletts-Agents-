## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Prospect's InfoSec team won't engage with our champion — they only want to communicate with our security team directly.**
Solution: Treat this as a feature, not a bug. Have your VP of Security or CISO send a direct email to their CISO peer offering a 30-minute "Security Leader to Security Leader" call. Frame it as a professional courtesy, not a sales call. This is the fastest path through a technical buyer who has tuned out AE communication.

**Problem: Prospect demands on-premise deployment or private cloud — we only offer multi-tenant SaaS.**
Solution: Reframe the architecture conversation. "Multi-tenant" is not the issue — the issue is "can they trust that their data is isolated?" Build a "Logical Isolation Architecture Explainer" (1-page diagram) showing exactly how data is segregated at the application, database, and network layers. Then offer compensating controls: dedicated encryption keys (BYOK), private VPC connectivity, IP allowlisting, and audit log streaming to their SIEM. Most "on-prem required" objections dissolve when logical isolation is clearly evidenced.

**Problem: Security review is legitimately being used to delay a decision (not a real security concern).**
Solution: Surface this reality to your champion and reframe with the Mutual Security Action Plan (MSAP). The MSAP converts "security review" from an open-ended blocker into a time-boxed, owner-assigned project with a clear completion date. If the prospect won't sign the MSAP, that's a signal the delay is political — escalate with your champion to their economic buyer to align security review completion with the business decision timeline.
