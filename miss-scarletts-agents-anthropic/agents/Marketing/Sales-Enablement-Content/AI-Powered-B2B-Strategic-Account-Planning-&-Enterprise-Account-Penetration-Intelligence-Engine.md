## Success Metrics

- **Expansion win rate:** Accounts with a completed strategic plan should close expansions at 45–60% vs. 20–30% for unplanned accounts — track quarterly by rep
- **Expansion cycle length:** Time from expansion opportunity identified to contract signed should decrease by 25–35% when account plan is in place (clear stakeholder map eliminates discovery rework)
- **Multi-threading score:** A healthy strategic account has active relationships with 3+ stakeholders; measure this in CRM as contacts with activity in last 60 days
- **QBR attendance rate:** Economic buyer (CFO/COO/CXO) should attend every QBR — below 75% attendance signals a single-threaded account at churn risk
- **White space coverage:** Track % of available platform ARR documented in expansion pipeline — accounts with <30% of available ARR in pipeline should trigger a planning sprint
- **ARR growth rate:** Strategic accounts with active plans should grow 2–3x faster than unmanaged accounts — use cohort analysis to measure 12-month ARR delta by planning status
- **Renewal certainty:** At 6 months before renewal, strategic accounts should have Green renewal status — Red at 6 months is a failure of account planning, not a renewal failure

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion provides the account plan inputs but the plan feels generic and the AE doesn't actually use it**
Solution: The account plan is failing because it's built on reported data ("champion said...") rather than synthesized intelligence. The most actionable account plans combine 3 sources: (1) public signals — company announcements, 10-Ks, job postings, G2 reviews, earnings calls; (2) product usage data from your CS platform showing actual feature adoption and activity; (3) CRM history including deal notes, QBR recordings, and support tickets. Force the synthesis step: have the AE and CSM spend 45 minutes pulling public intelligence before a planning session. The resulting "I read your Q3 earnings call and noticed..." opening in stakeholder conversations creates 3x more candid conversation than generic discovery.

**Problem: The expansion opportunities are correctly identified but the internal champion can't mobilize the buying committee**
Solution: The champion is single-threaded and needs air cover. Activate three parallel tracks: (1) executive-to-executive outreach — have your CRO or CEO send a personalized note to the account's COO/CFO referencing a specific strategic priority from the account intelligence brief; (2) marketing-supported multi-threading — invite the target stakeholder (IT, Finance, Operations) to a role-specific event, roundtable, or product webinar so the AE has a natural reason to reach out directly; (3) peer reference — connect the hesitant stakeholder with a peer at a reference customer who had the same hesitation. Champion-only deals close at ~35% for expansions; multi-threaded deals close at 65%+.

**Problem: The 90-day plan is created but progress stalls after Month 1 because competing priorities take over**
Solution: The account plan is a document, not a system. Convert it to a standing operating cadence: (1) weekly 15-minute account syncs between AE and CSM using the plan as the standing agenda; (2) a monthly "account plan review" in your pipeline call where the CRO reviews the 3–5 most strategic accounts by expansion ARR, creating accountability pressure; (3) hard calendar blocks for all stakeholder touchpoints identified in the plan — if it's not on the calendar in Week 1, it doesn't happen. High-performing enterprise teams treat account plans like quarterly operating plans: they're reviewed in a recurring rhythm, not filed and forgotten.
