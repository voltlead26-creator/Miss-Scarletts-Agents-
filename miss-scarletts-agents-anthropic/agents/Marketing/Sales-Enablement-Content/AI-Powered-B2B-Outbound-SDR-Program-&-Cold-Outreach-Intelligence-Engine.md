## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Reply rate under 2% after 200+ sends**
Solution: The sequence likely has a deliverability problem before a content problem. Check: (1) domain warm-up — new sending domains need 4–6 weeks of ramp-up; (2) spam trigger words — run templates through mail-tester.com and remove words like "guarantee," "free," "limited time," "just checking in"; (3) sending volume — stay under 100 emails/day per mailbox until sender reputation is established. Once deliverability is confirmed (open rate ≥ 40%), test a single-sentence subject line ("Quick question") vs. current subject lines to isolate content issues.

**Problem: High open rates (40%+) but low replies (under 3%)**
Solution: The subject line is working but the email body is losing them. Most common cause: email is too long (over 150 words) or ends with a vague CTA ("Let me know if you'd like to chat"). Fix: (1) Cut every email to under 100 words; (2) Replace vague CTAs with a single specific question that takes 10 seconds to answer ("Is this on your radar for Q3?"); (3) Test the 3-line punchy email format (Template 4) against current templates — it typically outperforms longer emails by 40–60% in reply rate.

**Problem: Meetings booked but opportunity conversion is low (under 40%)**
Solution: The sequence is attracting the wrong people or setting wrong expectations. Audit: (1) Are SDRs qualifying ICP fit during the sequence (company size, tech stack, pain confirmation) or just booking anyone who replies? Add a qualifying question before the final meeting CTA in the sequence; (2) Are the meeting invites setting context for what the call covers? A "no-agenda" meeting invitation creates buyer skepticism — include a 2-sentence agenda in the calendar invite; (3) Review which sequence variant (Pain-Led, Trigger-Led, Peer Proof) has the highest opportunity conversion and reallocate enrollment accordingly.
