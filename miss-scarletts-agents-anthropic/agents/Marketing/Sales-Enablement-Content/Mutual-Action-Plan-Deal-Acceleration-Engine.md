## DELIVERABLES

### 1. EXECUTIVE ALIGNMENT BRIEF
Write a 3-sentence opening statement that frames this MAP as a joint success initiative. Lead with the buyer's business outcome, not your product. Use the buyer's language.

### 2. MUTUAL SUCCESS CRITERIA
Define 4-6 specific, measurable success metrics the buyer agrees they want to achieve within 6 and 12 months of implementation. Format as: "We will consider this partnership successful when [METRIC] improves from [BASELINE] to [TARGET] by [DATE]."

### 3. REVERSE-ENGINEERED MILESTONE PLAN
Working backward from the target close date, create a milestone table with:
- Milestone name
- Description of what "done" looks like
- Due date
- Owner (Buyer Role / Seller Role)
- Dependencies
- Status (Pending / In Progress / Complete)

Include milestones for: technical validation, security review, legal/procurement, business case approval, executive sign-off, contract execution, and kickoff scheduling.

### 4. STAKEHOLDER ENGAGEMENT PLAYBOOK
For each key stakeholder, provide:
- Their top priority this quarter (inferred from role and context)
- The single insight or data point most likely to move them
- Recommended touchpoint (email, call, executive briefing, peer reference)
- Message framing (lead with their outcome, not product features)
- Risk if disengaged (what happens if we lose this stakeholder)

### 5. RISK REGISTER & MITIGATION
List the top 5 deal risks with:
- Risk description
- Probability (High/Medium/Low)
- Impact if realized
- Mitigation action
- Owner and due date

### 6. BUSINESS CASE SKELETON FOR CHAMPION
Write a 5-bullet business case the champion can bring to their CFO. Include:
- Problem quantified in dollars or hours
- Status quo cost (cost of doing nothing)
- Solution ROI with conservative, base, and optimistic scenarios
- Strategic alignment (how this supports a board-level initiative)
- Risk of delay (what a 90-day slip costs the business)

### 7. CHAMPION ENABLEMENT SCRIPT
Draft a 90-second internal pitch the champion can deliver in their leadership team meeting. Include the hook, the business case headline, the ask, and the objection response for "can this wait until next quarter?"

### 8. NEXT 3 ACTIONS
List the three most important next actions — with specific language for the AE to use when asking the buyer to commit to each one. Sequence by urgency and impact.

### 9. DEAL HEALTH SCORECARD

- Metrics

- Decision Criteria

- Paper Process

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM**: Paste the Milestone Plan into the opportunity's "Next Steps" field or create a custom object for MAP tracking. Attach the full MAP document to the opportunity record for deal review accuracy.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Create a shared MAP workspace page with the buyer's team. Embed the milestone table as a live database; update status weekly to keep both parties accountable.
- **Google Docs / Slides**: Export the MAP as a shared Google Doc with comment access for the buyer champion. Use suggesting mode so the buyer can edit their commitments, increasing ownership.
- **Clari / Gong**: Tag MAP-enabled deals in Clari for forecast categorization. Review Gong call recordings against MAP milestones to identify slippage before it impacts the close date.
- **DocuSign / PandaDoc**: Embed the MAP as Exhibit A in the proposal package — converting a sales document into a contractually referenced deployment timeline raises commitment level.
- **Zapier / Make.com**: Automate MAP milestone reminders via Slack or email when due dates are 5 days out; trigger CS handoff workflow when the final milestone (contract execution) is completed.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: Buyer champion won't share the MAP internally or engage the economic buyer.
**Solution**: The champion is likely not empowered or fears political risk. Reframe the MAP as a "joint project plan" they can present as their own initiative — not as a vendor document. Offer to draft the internal email they'd use to distribute it. If they still resist, the deal lacks a true champion; escalate or disqualify.

**Problem**: MAP milestones keep slipping — procurement started late, legal review dragging.
**Solution**: The Paper Process MEDDPICC dimension is always the silent deal killer. At Stage 3 or earlier, ask: "Who manages vendor onboarding at your company? Can we get them on a 15-minute call this week to understand your process?" Starting legal/procurement 30 days earlier than feels necessary is the single highest-ROI action for reducing cycle time.

**Problem**: The AI generates milestones that don't match your actual sales process.
**Solution**: Add a "Sales Process Context" section to the Advanced prompt: "Our sales stages are [list stages]. Our typical sales cycle is [X] weeks. Our standard paper process requires [security review / legal / procurement / finance sign-off / board approval]." The more deal-specific context you provide, the more accurate the timeline.
