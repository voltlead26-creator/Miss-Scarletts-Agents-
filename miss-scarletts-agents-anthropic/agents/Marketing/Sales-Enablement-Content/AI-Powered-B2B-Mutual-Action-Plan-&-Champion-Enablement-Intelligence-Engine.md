## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion won't share the MAP or internal pitch with the EB**
Fix: The MAP is too complex or feels like a vendor tool. Simplify to 5–7 milestones maximum. Rename it "Our Success Roadmap" or "[Account] Implementation Plan." Rewrite every milestone in the buyer's language. Ask the champion: "What would need to be different about this for you to feel comfortable sharing it with David?" Then rebuild based on their answer. The goal is a document the champion is proud to put their name on, not a sales tracking tool.

**Problem: The EB meeting gets booked but then the champion cancels or reschedules repeatedly**
Fix: The EB has not been pre-sold enough to prioritize this meeting. Diagnose: Has the champion had a hallway conversation with the EB about the ROI? Does the EB know the dollar amount at stake? Use the "cost per month of delay" narrative and have the champion casually drop the number in the next interaction before re-requesting the meeting. If the EB keeps canceling after 3 attempts, escalate via the vendor's executive — offer an "executive peer call" between your CEO/CRO and the EB. This is the "top gun" play.

**Problem: The blocker has escalated to the EB and introduced new technical requirements after decision criteria were locked**
Fix: This is a MEDDPICC "Implicate the Pain" failure — the EB doesn't feel enough pain to push back on the blocker. Do not fight the technical requirement directly. Instead, ask the champion to go back to the EB with one question: "Is [blocker's new requirement] more important than [the financial metric the EB cares about]? Because it adds [X weeks and $Y cost] to the timeline." Force the EB to choose between the blocker's political agenda and their own financial goal. In parallel, have your SE meet with the blocker 1:1 to address the technical concern directly — often the blocker just wants to feel heard, not to actually kill the deal.
