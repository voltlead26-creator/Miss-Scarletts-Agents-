## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM**: Upload finalized assets to the CRM content library tagged by deal stage, persona, and competitor — reps can surface the right one-pager from the sidebar during active deals without leaving the CRM
- **Highspot / Seismic / Guru**: Use the Deal Stage Content Map as your taxonomy for the sales enablement platform — configure analytics to track which assets correlate with closed-won vs. stalled deals
- **Gong / Chorus**: After deploying objection scripts, use conversation intelligence to measure adoption — track how often reps deploy the recommended response structure and correlate with win rate by objection type
- **Notion / Confluence**: Host the MEDDIC discovery framework and talk track as a linked wiki page directly from your Salesforce opportunity page layout so reps access it mid-call
- **Zapier / Make**: Automate stage-based content delivery — when a deal advances to Evaluation in Salesforce, automatically email the AE the evaluation-stage content pack and champion kit with a prompt to customize and send
- **Canva / Google Slides**: Use the pitch deck outline as a creative brief for your design team — the slide-by-slide talk track notes tell designers what emotional response each slide needs to trigger, not just what information to display

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Assets feel generic and reps ignore them after the first week
**Solution:** Pull 5–10 Gong call transcripts from your top-performing reps before generating — feed verbatim buyer language and rep phrasing into the CONTEXT section. The best enablement content sounds like your best rep in the best deal they ever closed, not a product brief written by marketing.

**Problem:** The one-pager works for the VP of Sales but procurement and legal reject the vendor package
**Solution:** Run the Advanced Version separately for each secondary stakeholder persona. The CFO's objections, vocabulary, and success criteria are fundamentally different from the primary buyer's — a single document rarely crosses stakeholder lines. Generate a separate asset for every person who can kill the deal.

**Problem:** Reps say the discovery framework feels scripted and unnatural
**Solution:** Reframe training: these are not scripts to read, they are questions to internalize. Run a 30-minute rep workshop where each AE writes 3 of the MEDDIC questions in their own voice. The framework provides the intent; the rep provides the delivery. Coaching to the outcome of each question (what you need to learn) rather than the exact phrasing reduces rigidity significantly.
