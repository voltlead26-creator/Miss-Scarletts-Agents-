## Integration Tips

- **Gong:** Use Gong's keyword tracker to auto-tag calls mentioning competitor names; feed weekly exports into the Competitive Signal Extraction Prompt to surface new objection patterns without manual review
- **Highspot/Seismic:** Map every asset to at least 3 tags (Competitor, Deal Stage, Persona) so Salesforce Smart Pages surface the right asset at the moment of need — not after the conversation is over
- **Salesforce:** Create a "Competitive Threat" field on Opportunity records; use this to build a win rate dashboard segmented by competitor, enabling you to measure battle card ROI directly from CRM data
- **Slack:** Use a #sales-competitive-intel channel with a weekly auto-post from your Competitive Flash Template; pin the current quarter's top 3 battle cards so new reps can find them instantly
- **Notion/Confluence:** Build a "Living Battle Card Index" with last-updated timestamps and a rep rating system (thumbs up/down + free text) so PMM knows which cards are landing in the field
- **Klue/Crayon:** If you invest in a competitive intelligence platform, use these AI prompts to structure your analysis and output — the prompts serve as the editorial intelligence layer that turns raw CI data into deployable sales content

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Reps don't use the battle cards even after launch — they revert to ad-hoc responses
**Solution:** The root cause is almost always that the cards weren't co-created with reps or weren't validated against real deal conversations. Run a 60-minute "battle card stress test" with your top 3 AEs before publishing: have them role-play each objection using the card and give real-time feedback on what sounds natural vs. scripted. Update the language based on their feedback. Reps use content they helped create.

**Problem:** Battle cards become outdated faster than PMM can update them
**Solution:** Implement the Staleness Scoring Matrix and assign a rotating "Competitive DRI" role among your senior AEs — one rep per competitor per quarter who owns flagging changes and co-authoring updates with PMM. This creates a distributed intelligence network that scales beyond what PMM can monitor alone.

**Problem:** It's hard to prove enablement content drove pipeline — leadership questions the ROI
**Solution:** Add a "Resources Used" multi-select field to Salesforce Opportunities and make it a required field at Stage 3 exit. After 60 days, you'll have enough data to run a simple SQL query or Salesforce report comparing average deal size, cycle length, and win rate for deals where battle cards were used vs. not. Even a 5% win rate lift at your average ACV will generate a compelling ROI story for your enablement investment.
