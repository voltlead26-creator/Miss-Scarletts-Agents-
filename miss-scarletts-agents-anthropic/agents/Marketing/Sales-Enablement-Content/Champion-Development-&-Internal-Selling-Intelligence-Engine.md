## Integration Tips
- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Log champion engagement score as a custom field; trigger champion kit delivery automatically when deal reaches "Business Case" stage; track champion communication cadence in activity timeline
- **Clari / Gong / Chorus:** Feed deal call transcripts into the objection module to auto-update the objection arsenal based on real conversations; use deal intelligence signals to trigger champion re-engagement alerts
- **Highspot / Seismic / Showpad:** Publish champion kits as trackable, personalized microsites; get notifications when champions share content internally; see which stakeholders are consuming which sections
- **Notion / Confluence:** Use Module 6 (Internal Selling Playbook) as a deal-room template; share with champion as a collaborative workspace where they can track their internal actions
- **Slack / Teams Integration:** Configure deal-room Slack channel; use champion kit milestones as automated deal stage triggers for sales manager alerts
- **DocuSign / PandaDoc:** Connect Module 4 (Business Case) output to proposal template to auto-populate financial assumptions when business case is accepted
- **Outreach / Salesloft:** Set champion engagement sequences as automated cadence steps triggered by deal stage transitions

## Troubleshooting
- **Problem:** Champion is enthusiastic but too junior — their internal credibility is too low to drive the deal.
  **Solution:** Re-run Module 3 (Executive Sponsor Acquisition Playbook) focused on identifying and recruiting an executive co-champion. Use the champion as intelligence source and internal guide, not the primary dealmaker. The kit should coach them on how to connect your team directly to the economic buyer.

- **Problem:** The business case numbers don't land with the CFO — they reject the ROI assumptions.
  **Solution:** Re-run Module 4 using the CFO's own language and stated assumptions. Ask the champion to get the CFO's specific objection to each line item, then rebuild the model using the CFO's conservative assumptions. A CFO who edits your model has become your co-author — much harder to reject.

- **Problem:** Champion goes dark after receiving the kit — stops responding to vendor.
  **Solution:** This often signals internal political trouble (blocker emerged, champion lost support, or deal deprioritized). Run Module 7 (Champion Self-Protection Guide) retrospectively: reach out through a different channel (LinkedIn vs. email), ask a peer-level question ("How's the internal conversation going?"), and offer to remove pressure by connecting directly with the executive sponsor without the champion in the middle.
