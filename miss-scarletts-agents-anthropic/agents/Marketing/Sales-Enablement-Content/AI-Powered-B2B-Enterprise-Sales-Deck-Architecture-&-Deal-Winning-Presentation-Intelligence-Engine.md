METHODOLOGY REQUIREMENTS:
Apply these frameworks across the deck:

1. CHALLENGER SALE STRUCTURE
   - Lead with a provocative commercial insight (Teach), not discovery questions
   - Tailor the narrative to this specific buyer's role and priorities (Tailor)
   - Guide them to the decision — don't wait for them to lead (Take Control)
   - The insight must make them say "I didn't know that" or "I hadn't thought of it that way"

2. MEDDPICC ALIGNMENT
   Map every slide to at least one MEDDPICC component:
   - Metrics: Every claim includes a specific, quantified business outcome
   - Economic Buyer: Slides 1-3 speak exclusively to their financial and strategic priorities
   - Decision Criteria: Slide 6 addresses their stated evaluation criteria explicitly
   - Decision Process: Slide 12 maps your ask to their internal approval workflow
   - Identify Pain: Slides 2-3 articulate their pain better than they can themselves
   - Champion: Slides 9-10 give the champion ammunition to sell internally
   - Competition: Address status quo and named competitors on slide 6 without naming them directly

3. LOSS AVERSION PSYCHOLOGY
   - Frame the problem in terms of what they're LOSING every month they don't act
   - Use the "future cost of inaction" calculation: Monthly cost × 12 = annual bleeding
   - Anchor the investment against the cost of the problem (never the cost of the product)
   - The risk of doing nothing must feel greater than the risk of changing

4. ECONOMIC BUYER PERSONA CALIBRATION
   IF CFO/Finance buyer:
   - Lead with payback period, IRR, and 3-year NPV
   - Use loaded cost of headcount (1.4x salary) for any headcount-reduction story
   - Address budget cycle timing explicitly
   - Use "investment" not "cost"

   IF CTO/CISO/Technical buyer:
   - Lead with architecture security and scalability
   - Include implementation risk mitigation
   - Reference technical due diligence findings
   - Address integration complexity and data portability

   IF COO/Operations buyer:
   - Lead with process efficiency and cycle time reduction
   - Use before/after workflow comparison
   - Include change management and adoption plan
   - Reference similar operational transformation stories

   IF CHRO/People buyer:
   - Lead with employee experience and retention impact
   - Frame costs in terms of turnover cost (1.5x-2x annual salary to replace)
   - Include implementation support and training
   - Reference benchmark data on employee outcomes

   IF CEO/Board buyer:
   - Lead with competitive moat and market position
   - Connect to company strategic priorities (growth, profitability, M&A readiness)
   - Minimize operational detail; maximize strategic narrative
   - Use revenue impact, not cost savings, as primary value driver

## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: The AI generates generic content that doesn't feel specific to the prospect.
**Solution**: The prompt quality directly determines output quality. The minimum inputs needed are: 3 specific pain points the prospect actually said (not what you assume), 2 verified customer outcomes with real numbers, and the economic buyer's known priorities (not job title — their stated 2026 goals). Run a discovery call intelligence engine first, then populate this prompt with verbatim quotes from the call.

**Problem**: The deck feels like it's missing the emotional/urgency component — it reads like a brochure.
**Solution**: Explicitly add to the prompt: "Use loss aversion framing throughout. Every problem statement must include the monthly revenue cost of continuing the status quo. The economic buyer must feel that NOT deciding has a greater cost than deciding." Also add: "Include one genuinely surprising insight the prospect has likely never heard — not a product fact, an industry or market insight."

**Problem**: The champion says the deck is too long or too detailed for the CFO.
**Solution**: Ask the AI to generate a "3-slide executive summary version": (1) The problem + cost of inaction in one slide, (2) Proof in one slide with three customer outcomes, (3) Recommended action in one slide with the ROI summary. Less is more for time-pressured economic buyers — the full deck is for the champion to read, not present.
