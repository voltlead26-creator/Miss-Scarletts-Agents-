## Overview

Autonomously generates a complete, deal-specific competitive counter-content kit the moment a competitor enters or escalates in an active deal — producing customized objection scripts, email templates, executive briefing narratives, proof point packages, and FUD-neutralization talk tracks. Use this when a competitor cuts price mid-deal, releases a new feature your prospect cares about, or wins a reference account in your target vertical — and your reps need response content within hours, not days.

## Integration Tips

- **Gong / Chorus:** Set keyword alerts for competitor names in recorded calls. When triggered, automatically pull deal context (stage, ACV, stakeholders) and pipe it into this prompt via Zapier or Make to generate a counter-content kit within minutes of the call ending.
- **Salesforce / HubSpot:** Add a "Competitive Threat" picklist field to Opportunity. When populated, trigger an automation that runs this prompt with deal-field data pre-filled and drops the output into a Chatter/Activity note visible to the rep.
- **Slack / Teams:** Create a `#competitive-deals` channel. Set up a bot that detects "Coupa", "Salesforce", "HubSpot" (your key competitors) in deal discussion threads and auto-posts the relevant FUD neutralization module from this prompt.
- **Highspot / Seismic / Showpad:** Store generated counter-content kits in your sales content management system tagged by competitor, deal stage, and buyer persona so reps can pull pre-generated kits for common competitive scenarios.
- **Clay:** Enrich deal records with real-time competitive intelligence (G2 reviews, LinkedIn competitor posts, Bombora intent signals) and use enriched data as prompt inputs for more precise counter-content generation.
- **Notion / Confluence:** Build a living Competitive Win Room wiki by storing AI-generated counter-content kits organized by competitor. Each time a new kit is generated, append it to the competitor's page with deal outcome tracked.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Generated emails feel generic and don't reference the specific competitive move convincingly.
**Solution:** Be extremely specific in the "Competitive move" field. Instead of "Coupa released an AI feature," write "Coupa announced 'Navi' at their Coupa Inspire conference on April 28, 2026 — it's AI embedded in their sourcing module, currently in limited beta, GA expected Q4 2026." The more specific your input, the more credible and tailored the output.

**Problem:** The FUD neutralization scripts sound defensive rather than confident.
**Solution:** Add the "hidden vulnerability of competitor" field with a specific, factual weakness (support SLA data from G2, recent leadership departures from LinkedIn, customer churn from public reviews). Confident counters come from evidence, not reassurance. Also prompt the AI to reframe each FUD counter as "the data says..." rather than "don't worry about..."

**Problem:** Deal acceleration play doesn't match your company's capacity (e.g., prompt generates an Executive Briefing Center visit but you're a 30-person startup).
**Solution:** Add a constraint to the prompt: "We are a [Series A / Series B / enterprise] company with [X] person sales team. Do not recommend plays that require dedicated EBC facilities, custom hardware environments, or more than [Y] internal stakeholders." The AI will automatically calibrate to plays appropriate to your GTM maturity.
