## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CFO won't engage with the business case — "our team already evaluated this."**
Solution: The executive summary was likely too vendor-centric. Rewrite the opening to lead with a third-party benchmark or publicly reported industry stat ("Healthcare organizations like yours lose an average of 3-5% of net revenue to manual reconciliation errors — [Source: HFMA 2025 Annual Report]") before introducing your company. Send the ROI model as a standalone spreadsheet with a message: "I'd value your feedback on whether the assumptions reflect NorthStar's situation — happy to adjust the model before we meet."

**Problem: Champion loves it but can't get economic buyer in the room.**
Solution: The MAP is not creating urgency. Add a "cost of delay" calculation: "Every 30-day delay in implementation costs NorthStar approximately $[monthly run rate of current state cost]. On a $185K investment, a 90-day delay represents $[3× monthly cost] in additional lost value." Give the champion a business case for why THIS QUARTER matters, not just why you're the right vendor.

**Problem: Competitor undercuts your price in final round and CFO asks for matching.**
Solution: Return to the TCO comparison (Section 5). Don't match on price — reframe on 3-year value. "Matching [Competitor A]'s Year 1 price would require us to reduce our implementation support, which is what prevents the 30% failure rate we see in self-service deployments in your sector. What I can do is [alternative concession: extended pilot, additional users at no charge, success-milestone payment structure] — which addresses your risk without compromising what makes our implementation succeed."
