## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Audience too small — Meta showing "Audience Too Small" error for custom audiences**
Solution: Custom Audiences require a minimum of 100 matched users. If your CRM list is under 1,000 contacts, Meta may match fewer than 100. Fix: (1) Upload all historical contacts including churned leads and event registrants to reach the threshold; (2) Switch from custom audience to interest targeting + geographic restriction as a fallback; (3) Wait until your retargeting pool (website visitors) reaches 1,000+ before activating pixel-based audiences. Lookalike audiences require minimum 1,000 seed size — combine multiple customer segments to reach this threshold.

**Problem: Ad sets stuck in "Learning Limited" status — delivery unstable and CPL volatile**
Solution: "Learning Limited" means Meta's algorithm can't gather enough conversion data to optimize (needs 50 events per ad set per week). Fix: (1) Consolidate ad sets — combine audiences into fewer, higher-budget ad sets; (2) Switch to a higher-volume optimization event (e.g., optimize for "Lead" instead of "CompleteRegistration" if demo requests are low-volume); (3) Increase budget — the minimum to exit learning for most B2B conversion campaigns is $3,000-5,000/month per ad set; (4) Broaden audience — if audience is under 500K, expand lookalike percentage or add interests.

**Problem: Meta CPLs are high but pipeline quality is low — MQL rate below 10%**
Solution: Low MQL rate signals audience mismatch (reaching consumers or SMB, not ICP). Fix: (1) Add a qualification question to Lead Ad forms ("Company size" or "How many contracts does your team manage?") and suppress unqualified answers from MQL qualification; (2) Shift budget from interest targeting to CRM-backed lookalike audiences which have higher ICP signal; (3) Add company domain field to lead forms and filter out personal email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo, Hotmail) in CRM workflow — these indicate consumer or small business respondents; (4) Review Meta's audience breakdown report to see if delivery skews toward certain demographics that don't match ICP.
