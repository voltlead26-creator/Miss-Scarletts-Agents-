## Overview
Designs a complete enterprise Proof-of-Value (POV) program — from success criteria agreement through champion enablement to close — that transforms 30–90 day evaluation periods into signed contracts. Use when your sales cycle includes a formal evaluation phase and you need marketing to systematically reduce no-decision risk, accelerate champion consensus, and produce board-ready ROI evidence that closes six-figure deals.

## Integration Tips

- **Salesforce**: Create a "POV" opportunity stage with custom fields for Success Criteria Status (% complete), POV Health Score, Champion Engagement Rate, and Economic Buyer Last Touch Date. Use Process Builder or Flow to trigger the automated communication sequence when opportunity enters POV stage.
- **HubSpot**: Build a dedicated POV pipeline with 5 stages: POV Kickoff → Criteria Agreed → Midpoint Review → Results Delivered → Close. Use Workflows to send champion sequence emails; use Sequences for AE-to-economic-buyer touchpoints.
- **Outreach / Salesloft**: Build a "POV Champion" sequence and a separate "POV Economic Buyer" sequence. Configure step timing to match the communication calendar above. Tag all POV contacts with a POV end-date field to auto-trigger close-package delivery.
- **Slack/Teams**: Automate a POV health score notification to the AE's Slack channel every Monday morning. Include score, delta, top at-risk criterion, and recommended action.
- **Notion / Confluence**: Build a POV resource center with pinned links to onboarding checklist, MSCA template, and success criteria tracker. Share with champion during kickoff.
- **Google Sheets / Looker Studio**: Auto-populate the POV results scorecard using product usage API or CRM data exports. Schedule a weekly data refresh so the champion always has current numbers.
- **Gong / Chorus**: Review POV kickoff call recordings to identify recurring objections or success criteria gaps. Feed learnings back into the MSCA template quarterly.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Champion goes silent after a strong kickoff call**
Solution: Trigger the "POV health alert" workflow at Day 7 if champion email opens drop below 1. Have the AE send a 2-sentence text message or WhatsApp: "Hey [Name] — quick check-in on the evaluation. Is there anything blocking you this week?" Simultaneously, have the AE's manager send a brief note to the economic buyer re-confirming the POV timeline and strategic importance. Silence usually means internal reprioritization — not lost interest.

**Problem: Economic buyer never engaged beyond Day 1 touchpoint**
Solution: If the Day 21 economic buyer check-in goes unopened for 48 hours, do not re-send the same email. Instead, have the AE call the economic buyer with one specific data point: "[Champion name]'s team already reduced forecast prep time from 8 hours to 45 minutes — I thought you'd want to see that before next month's QBR." Use a metric the economic buyer owns to create urgency, then ask for a 15-minute results preview.

**Problem: Competitor enters the evaluation during the POV**
Solution: Reframe the evaluation, don't abandon it. Within 24 hours of learning about the competitor, schedule an "evaluation strategy" call with the champion. Offer to run a structured bake-off using the existing MSCA success criteria — the same criteria now apply to both vendors. Accelerate delivery of the midpoint ROI summary. If you're ahead on success criteria, make the numbers the story. Send the champion the "objection handling cheat sheet" with language specifically addressing why the competitor's approach is architecturally different.
