## Integration Tips

**Salesforce:**
- Create a custom Campaign Member field for "Event Engagement Score" and "Audience Type" (In-Person / Virtual / No-Show)
- Map ON24 engagement data to Salesforce contact records via the ON24 Salesforce connector within 4 hours of event close
- Build a post-event pipeline dashboard: filter opportunities with "Event Influenced = True" and campaign date within 90 days

**Marketo / HubSpot:**
- Create a Smart List for each lead tier (Hot / Warm / Nurture) triggered by campaign member score
- Set up program triggers: if score crosses 80 within 24 hours of event end → alert assigned SDR in Slack via Zapier
- Clone your standard nurture program and customize for each of the 5 post-event segments (in-person high, in-person low, virtual high, virtual low, no-show)

**ON24:**
- Configure ON24 Engagement Score export to run automatically 1 hour after each session ends
- Enable the ON24-to-Marketo integration to push engagement data as custom fields, not just registration data
- Use ON24 Simulive for time-zone-friendly replays: pre-record select sessions and deliver them with live moderation for EMEA/APAC audiences

**Cvent / Splash (In-Person):**
- Configure badge scan data export to Salesforce via the native connector, mapped to Campaign Member records
- Set up Cvent Lead Capture app for sponsor booths with custom qualifying questions synced to Salesforce Campaign Member fields
- Use Cvent OnArrival for real-time session attendance tracking; configure auto-alerts for VIP target accounts entering the room

**Zapier / Make (Automation):**
- Trigger: ON24 or Cvent engagement score crosses Hot threshold → Action: Slack alert to assigned SDR with engagement summary + CRM link
- Trigger: Virtual attendee downloads resource → Action: add to Marketo "high-intent virtual" smart list + notify AE if existing opportunity in Salesforce

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Virtual attendees feel like second-class citizens — engagement drops after the first session**
*Fix:* Design virtual-native moments that in-person attendees cannot participate in: exclusive virtual-only AMAs with speakers after sessions, virtual networking tables with AI-matched attendees, virtual-only early-access content drops. Announce these in pre-event communications to set expectations. Assign a dedicated virtual emcee who talks directly to the camera audience throughout the event — not just as an afterthought between in-person segments.

**Problem: Lead scoring data isn't flowing from virtual platform to CRM within your 4-hour SLA**
*Fix:* Never rely on the default nightly sync. Pre-configure manual export triggers: set an ON24 or Bizzabo scheduled report to run 30 minutes after each session and email a CSV to your MOps team. Build a Zapier workflow that reads the CSV from Google Drive and pushes engagement scores to Salesforce Campaign Member records. Test the full data flow 2 weeks before the event with a dummy attendee record.

**Problem: Sponsors are unhappy because they feel virtual attendee leads are lower quality than in-person badge scans**
*Fix:* Educate sponsors pre-event with data: virtual attendees who watch 80%+ of a session and download a resource convert to pipeline at rates comparable to in-person badge scans. Build sponsor reporting that shows engagement depth, not just count — a virtual attendee who spent 47 minutes in a sponsored session and downloaded two assets is more valuable than an in-person attendee who walked through a booth for 10 seconds. Provide this as a standard post-event sponsor report within 5 business days.
