## Overview

This engine designs and executes AI-orchestrated win-back campaigns that systematically recover churned B2B SaaS customers — segmenting churned accounts by churn reason, contract value, and reactivation timing, then deploying personalized multi-channel sequences that turn lost revenue into recovered ARR. Use it when you have a churned customer base worth targeting, a product that has materially improved since churn, or competitive win opportunities where former customers are re-entering the market.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot:** Create a "Win-Back" deal pipeline stage separate from your new logo pipeline. Use Workflows to auto-enroll churned contacts based on churn date + intent signal triggers. Use Sequences for AE-driven personal outreach on Tier 1 accounts. Track win-back pipeline in a separate view to isolate ROI.
- **Salesforce:** Build a "Churned Account" list view filtered by Churn Reason + ACV + Last Activity Date. Use Campaign object to track win-back program attribution. Assign win-back opportunities a unique "Lead Source = Win-Back Campaign" for clean attribution.
- **Gainsight / ChurnZero:** Export churned accounts with health score history, churn reason tags, and NPS data into your CRM to enrich win-back segmentation. Use historical engagement data to identify which churned accounts had product usage above the median before churning (often the most winnable).
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator:** Build a saved list of churned account contacts. Use "Job Change" alerts to identify when your champion is now at a new company (warm intro opportunity) or when a new decision-maker has joined the churned account (fresh relationship, no baggage).
- **Bombora / G2 Buyer Intent:** Set up intent topic tracking for churned accounts on your product category keywords. An intent score spike from a churned account 9+ months post-churn is one of the highest-signal win-back triggers available.
- **Zapier/Make:** Connect G2 review alerts → Slack notification to AE when a churned customer posts a negative review about the competitor they switched to. This is a golden win-back trigger that most teams miss entirely.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Churned accounts have opted out of marketing emails, blocking sequence enrollment.**
Solution: Suppress win-back email sequences for opted-out contacts. Route these accounts to AE-driven personal outreach only (not marketing automation). A one-to-one email from the original AE or CSM is legally distinct from marketing automation and often gets higher response rates anyway for high-ACV accounts. Use LinkedIn as the primary channel for opted-out contacts.

**Problem: CRM churn reason data is incomplete — 40%+ of churned accounts have no documented reason.**
Solution: Before building segment-specific sequences, run a brief "account intelligence sprint": have CS ops tag the top 50 highest-ACV undocumented churned accounts by reviewing support ticket history, final renewal call notes, and any exit survey data. For truly unknown accounts, use the generic "What's Changed" sequence that leads with your top 3 most impactful product improvements rather than a reason-specific message. Also consider a brief 2-question re-engagement survey as Touch 1 to gather churn reason data before pitching.

**Problem: Win-back outreach response rates are below 3%, campaigns feel tone-deaf.**
Solution: The most common cause is leading with product features rather than acknowledging the customer's experience. Audit your Touch 1 emails: if the word "we" appears more than the word "you" in the first paragraph, rewrite. Win-back messaging must open with acknowledgment ("a lot has changed since we worked together") not promotion. Also check timing — accounts churned less than 6 months ago are rarely ready to re-evaluate. Delay outreach to the 9–12 month window unless a strong external trigger (competitor price increase, new exec hire) justifies earlier contact.
