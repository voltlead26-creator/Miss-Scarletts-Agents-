## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Data engineers engage with technical content and community programs but refuse demo requests and don't convert to pipeline**

Fix: This is almost always a positioning problem, not a demand problem. Data engineers are highly allergic to feeling like they are being sold to after genuine community interactions. Audit your SDR follow-up sequence — if it sounds like a sales cadence rather than a practitioner conversation, engineers will disengage and warn their network. Redesign your conversion flow: Community engagement → product trial (no SDR contact for 14 days) → trial activation → automated in-product "Talk to a data engineer on our team" offer (peer-to-peer, not SDR) → solution engineer call framed as architecture review. Move the first human touchpoint to a technical conversation, not a discovery call.

**Problem: GitHub stars and OSS downloads are high but OSS-to-paid conversion is below 2% and self-hosted users resist cloud migration**

Fix: The OSS-to-cloud migration objection is almost always about control, cost uncertainty, and switching cost — not product quality. Build a "Total cost of self-hosting" calculator: Engineer hours for maintenance × loaded salary + infrastructure costs + on-call burden + security patching time = true TCO of DIY. Most companies underestimate self-hosting TCO by 4–8x. Publish this calculator as a tool (not gated), drive traffic via GitHub README and community posts, and let the math do the conversion work. Simultaneously, build an automated "self-hosting health check" that detects when an OSS user's deployment is consuming above-average maintenance cycles and triggers a personalized cloud migration offer.

**Problem: Losing deals to a better-funded competitor who outspends on paid media and has stronger analyst relations coverage**

Fix: Don't compete on their terms. Better-funded competitors win on share of voice and analyst placement — match that spend and you lose on efficiency. Win on practitioner trust depth: (1) Launch a "Why we lost to [Competitor]" blog post series with honest technical comparison — data engineers share content that acknowledges trade-offs; (2) Build a "community verified" review campaign — ask your 50 most engaged community members to post honest G2 reviews (positive AND constructive) with technical specificity that generic competitor reviews can't match; (3) Invest in your own annual conference (virtual or in-person) where you control the content agenda and your competitor cannot buy a booth — Coalesce is dbt Labs' most powerful demand gen asset, not their paid ads.
