## Integration Tips

**Google Ads + Salesforce (Closed-Loop Attribution):**
- Implement Google Ads Offline Conversion Tracking (OCT) to import Salesforce opportunity stage events back into Google Ads
- Use Zapier or native Salesforce connector: Trigger → Opportunity Created in Salesforce → Action → Upload conversion to Google Ads with click ID (GCLID)
- This enables YouTube Ads to optimize toward actual pipeline, not just form fills

**HubSpot Integration:**
- Install Google Ads integration in HubSpot to automatically sync UTM data to contact records
- Build a HubSpot list: "YouTube Ads Influenced" = any contact with utm_source = youtube AND lifecycle stage = MQL or later
- Use this list as a Customer Match upload back into Google Ads monthly to create lookalike audiences from your highest-converting YouTube prospects

**Google Analytics 4 Setup:**
- Create GA4 conversion events for each micro and macro conversion
- Build a GA4 Exploration report: "YouTube Ads Path to Pipeline" using path exploration with youtube/paid as starting segment
- Export GA4 audience segments (e.g., "Watched 50%+ of demo video") directly to Google Ads for retargeting

**Notion/Airtable Campaign Tracker:**
- Use prompt output to populate a weekly YouTube Ads optimization log
- Track: campaign name | week | impressions | view rate | CPV | conversions | CPL | pipeline influenced
- Set automated alerts when any metric deviates >25% from weekly average

**Creative Production Workflow:**
- Feed creative briefs from this prompt into a video production brief tool (Frame.io, Loom for async review)
- Use Claude or other LLM to A/B test 5 different hook scripts before production — test as text ads first, then produce winning hooks as video

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Campaigns stuck in "limited" status or not spending budget**
- Solution: YouTube Ads require audience sizes of at least 1,000 users for custom audiences to activate. If targeting is too narrow (e.g., small remarketing lists), broaden to all website visitors first. For new accounts, start with in-market and affinity audiences before custom intent while remarketing lists build. Check brand safety settings — overly restrictive content exclusions can throttle YouTube inventory significantly.

**Problem: High view rate but zero conversions — video is watched but no pipeline**
- Solution: This is a classic "entertaining but not converting" failure. Audit the first 5 seconds: the hook may be engaging but not filtering for ICP intent. Add a qualifying statement early ("If you're a VP of Sales at a SaaS company…"). Audit the CTA: a generic "learn more" drives 3-5x fewer conversions than a specific offer with a deadline. Check landing page alignment — if video promises a demo but LP leads to a generic homepage, conversion will collapse. Implement dedicated YouTube Ads landing pages with mirrored messaging.

**Problem: CPO is 2-3x target after 60 days — YouTube Ads not generating pipeline at acceptable cost**
- Solution: First, verify attribution — are you measuring only last-click conversions? YouTube rarely wins last-click in a long B2B sales cycle. Rebuild your attribution model to include view-through attribution (7-day window) and multi-touch. Second, check audience quality: use the "Audience Manager" report in Google Ads to see conversion rate by audience segment — often 20% of audiences drive 80% of conversions. Pause low-converting audiences and concentrate budget. Third, if creative is the issue, analyze view-through rate at the 5-second mark — under 50% means the hook is failing, under 30% means creative needs a complete rebuild.
