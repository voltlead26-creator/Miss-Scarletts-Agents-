# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Product Launch Content Production Engine - Full-Funnel Launch Asset Generator

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** b2b-saas, product-launch, content-production, multi-channel, automation, pmm

## Overview

Takes a single product or feature launch brief as input and generates a complete, coordinated suite of launch content assets — emails, blog post, LinkedIn posts, sales enablement, ad copy, and landing page — all anchored to a unified messaging spine. Use this when you need the full content package for a B2B SaaS launch in one pass, without seven separate prompts and a week of alignment meetings.

## Quick Copy-Paste Version

You are a senior B2B SaaS product marketing manager. Using the launch brief below, produce a complete launch content package that uses consistent messaging across every asset.

LAUNCH BRIEF:
- Product/Feature Name: [Name]
- Problem it solves: [1-2 sentences]
- Primary persona (job title and seniority): [e.g., VP of Sales, mid-market SaaS]
- Key benefit (what changes for them): [1 sentence with specifics]
- Proof point or early data: [Stat or verbatim customer quote]
- Differentiator vs. primary competitor: [Competitor name + what we do that they don't]
- Launch tier: [Major product / Significant feature / Enhancement]
- Go-live date: [Date]

PRODUCE ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:

1. MESSAGING SPINE (master narrative anchoring all assets):
   - Hero statement: "[Persona] finally [outcome] without [previous obstacle]"
   - Before state: 2 sentences describing the painful status quo
   - After state: 2 sentences describing life with the solution
   - Competitive wedge: 1 sentence on what we do that the competitor can't credibly claim

2. EMAIL SEQUENCE (3 emails):
   - Email 1 (teaser, 3 days before): Subject + 150-word body — hint at pain, do not reveal feature name
   - Email 2 (launch day): Subject + 200-word body — PAS structure (Problem → Agitation → Solution) + primary CTA
   - Email 3 (follow-up, 5 days after, non-openers): Subject + 150-word body — lead with strongest proof point

3. LAUNCH BLOG POST (600 words):
   - SEO-optimized headline
   - Scene-opening intro (describe a painful moment in the persona's day)
   - Problem → Solution → Proof → CTA structure
   - 1 customer use case with before/after numbers

4. LINKEDIN POST SERIES (3 posts, one per launch week day):
   - Post 1 (Day -2): Hook on the painful problem — no feature mention yet
   - Post 2 (Day 0): Launch announcement — outcome first, feature name second
   - Post 3 (Day +4): Mini case study — "X was doing Y. Now they do Z."

5. SALES ENABLEMENT ONE-PAGER:
   - Discovery question to open the conversation
   - 3 customer outcomes with proof
   - Competitive objection + response vs. [Competitor]
   - Status quo objection + cost-of-inaction response
   - Resources list (demo link, blog, customer story)

6. LANDING PAGE HERO COPY:
   - Headline (10 words max, outcome not feature name)
   - Subheadline (25 words max)
   - 3 benefit bullets
   - Primary CTA button text
   - Social proof bar (format: "[X] customers | "[Short quote]" — Name, Title, Company")

7. PAID AD COPY:
   - Google Search ad: 3 headlines (30 chars each) + 2 descriptions (90 chars each)
   - LinkedIn Sponsored: Headline (70 chars) + intro text (150 chars) + CTA label

Rules: No buzzwords ("powerful," "innovative," "seamless"). Every claim must be specific. Customer outcome leads every asset. Maintain the same hero statement across all copy.

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Create a "Product Launch — [Feature Name]" campaign and tag all assets to it for unified pipeline attribution. Build the 3-email sequence as a workflow triggered by a "Product Launch Interest" enrollment property. Set the blog post as gated with a progressive profiling form to capture firmographic data from organic readers.
- **Salesforce + Highspot/Seismic**: Upload the sales one-pager to your sales enablement platform linked to the relevant product category. Track usage data by rep and correlate with win rate in Salesforce — if reps using the one-pager win at higher rates, it validates the messaging and creates social proof for adoption.
- **Notion**: Build a Launch Command Center page with the Messaging Spine pinned as a persistent reference block. Every team member creating launch content references it before publishing. Use a Notion database to track which asset is in which status (draft → review → approved → live).
- **Buffer / Hootsuite / Sprout Social**: Paste the three LinkedIn posts directly into your scheduling tool. Space them 48 hours apart. Schedule Post 1 for 8 AM on Day -2, Post 2 for 9 AM on launch day (give sales 1 hour to see it before it goes live externally), and Post 3 for midday on Day +4 when engagement tends to spike.
- **Google Ads / LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Import both Google ad variations simultaneously and let them run for 200-300 impressions each before pausing the underperformer. For LinkedIn, run the two headline variants as A/B test within one campaign — LinkedIn's built-in testing requires minimum 2 active ads per campaign.
- **Gong / Chorus**: After launch, pull call recordings from the first 2 weeks. Search for mentions of the feature name, competitor names, and pricing. Feed actual customer language from those calls back into the next prompt iteration to sharpen the messaging spine — real buyer language always outperforms our marketing language.
- **Zapier**: Build a Zap that fires when a Salesforce opportunity is tagged with the new feature as primary driver — auto-enroll the account in a post-launch ABM nurture sequence in HubSpot and notify the CSM in Slack.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Output feels generic — copy reads like every other B2B SaaS launch, not our specific product.**
Solution: Add three hard constraints to the prompt: (1) "Do not use the words 'powerful,' 'innovative,' 'seamless,' or 'game-changing' anywhere." (2) "Every paragraph in every asset must contain at least one of: a specific number, a job title, a named competitor, or a concrete scenario from the persona's day." (3) "Write the launch day email as if the CMO is sending it personally to a specific VP of Sales they met at SaaStr — not to a list." Also paste in 2-3 sentences of actual customer language from win interviews or Gong recordings — the AI will mirror the vocabulary.

**Problem: Sales team ignores the enablement one-pager and falls back to generic pitch.**
Solution: Identify the 5 most recent competitive deals lost to the primary competitor. Pull the Gong/Chorus snippets showing the exact objections raised. Paste those verbatim into the prompt under "Competitive Context" and add the instruction: "Write the competitive handling section using the exact language from these real customer objections — not paraphrased." When reps see their actual objections handled word-for-word, adoption increases dramatically. Alternatively, run a 15-minute Slack AMA with the top-performing rep about the feature — then feed that conversation into the prompt for authenticity.

**Problem: Blog post isn't ranking for the target keyword despite strong content.**
Solution: Re-run the blog post section with three additions: (1) "Structure the first 100 words as a direct answer to the search query '[target keyword]' — assume Google will use this as a featured snippet." (2) "Add a comparison table (5 rows) showing [Feature] vs. [Primary Competitor] vs. Status Quo on the metrics that matter most to the persona." (3) "Include a FAQ section at the bottom answering the 5 most common questions people ask about [target keyword] — base them on real search intent." Then submit the updated URL to Google Search Console for priority reindexing.
