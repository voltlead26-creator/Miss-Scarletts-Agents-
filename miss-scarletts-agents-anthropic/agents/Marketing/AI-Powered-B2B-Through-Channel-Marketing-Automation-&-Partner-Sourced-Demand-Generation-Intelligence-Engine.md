## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
- Use HubSpot's Partner Portal (Sales Hub) to distribute campaign assets and track partner deal registration
- Create a custom "Partner Source" property on Contact and Deal objects with standardized picklist values for each partner
- Build a Partner Demand Gen dashboard in HubSpot Reports using filtered views by partner source field
- Use HubSpot's campaign UTM builder and associate all partner campaigns to a parent campaign for roll-up reporting
- Set up a Workflow that auto-assigns partner-sourced leads to the correct Channel Account Manager based on partner company association

**Salesforce:**
- Use Salesforce PRM (partner community) or a connected tool like Impartner/Allbound to manage asset distribution and campaign tracking
- Create a custom Campaign Member Type = "Partner Sourced" to distinguish partner-generated responses from direct marketing
- Build a Partner Pipeline report with Opportunity Source = "Partner" segmented by partner account, campaign, and stage
- Use Salesforce Flow to auto-stamp "Partner Influenced" on any opportunity where a partner contact appears as a Contact Role within 90 days of opportunity creation

**Zapier / Make Automation:**
- Zap: New partner MQL in HubSpot → Slack notification to Channel Account Manager with lead details and partner name
- Zap: MDF request form submission (Typeform/Jotform) → Google Sheets logging → Email approval notification to channel marketing manager
- Zap: Partner campaign UTM click in Google Analytics → tag contact record in CRM with campaign source

**Notion / Google Drive:**
- Maintain a Partner Content Library in Notion or Google Drive with folder structure: /[Partner Tier]/[Campaign Play]/[Asset Type]

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partners enroll in the program but never launch a campaign.**
Solution: The most common cause is asset complexity — partners don't know where to start. Fix it by (1) reducing campaign choice to ONE recommended play for new partners, (2) building a "campaign-in-a-box" that requires zero customization for the first launch, and (3) assigning a Channel Marketing Manager to run a 30-minute "campaign launch" screen-share with each new partner in their first week. Remove friction before removing excuses.

**Problem: Partners execute campaigns but the pipeline quality is poor — MQLs don't convert.**
Solution: Poor MQL quality usually means partners are targeting the wrong accounts or using a lead magnet that attracts non-buyers. Audit the top 20 partner MQLs that didn't convert: what company size, title, and behavior triggered the MQL? Rebuild the ICP criteria and lead qualification threshold. Add a 2-question form field to the landing page ("What is your current [relevant tool] stack?" + "What is your timeline for a solution?") to add behavioral qualification beyond form fill.

**Problem: Attribution disputes — partners claim deals, sales says the deal was already in pipeline.**
Solution: Implement a clear "sourced vs. influenced" definition with a hard rule: partner-sourced = partner was the FIRST to create contact record AND opportunity in CRM; partner-influenced = partner touched the deal at any point but did not create the opportunity. Lock this definition in your partner agreement and CRM workflow. For gray-area disputes, default to "partner-influenced" credit — the cost of misattributing influence credit is far lower than destroying partner trust with a sourced dispute.
