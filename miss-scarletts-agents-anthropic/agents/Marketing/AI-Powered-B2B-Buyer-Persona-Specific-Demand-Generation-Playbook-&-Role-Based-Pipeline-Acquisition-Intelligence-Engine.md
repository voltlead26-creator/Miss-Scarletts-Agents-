## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Create Persona = CISO as a contact property, build workflow branches by this field to route into persona-specific email sequences, ad audiences, and SDR queues automatically
- **Salesforce + Pardot**: Use Engagement Studio program paths filtered by Buyer Role field; sync lead score thresholds to Salesforce assignment rules for automatic SDR routing by persona
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager**: Use the "Matched Audiences" feature to upload your CRM list of target CISO contacts and serve them Thought Leader Ads from your most credible practitioner voice
- **Clay**: Build a persona-enrichment waterfall that auto-classifies every inbound lead by buyer role using job title normalization, then routes to the appropriate nurture track in your MAP
- **6sense or Bombora**: Layer intent data to surface which CISOs at target accounts are actively researching your category — use this to prioritize outbound sequences and increase ad budget toward in-market buyers
- **Gong / Chorus**: Mine your call recordings filtered by CISO contacts to extract exact language patterns, objections, and buying signals — feed these back into your SDR sequences and ad copy
- **Notion + Zapier**: Build a persona playbook wiki in Notion, trigger a Zap to post weekly performance metrics from HubSpot to a Slack channel, keeping the demand gen team aligned without manual reporting

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: High MQL volume but low SDR meeting conversion for this persona**
Solution: The persona-to-product fit is right but the SDR handoff message is wrong. Pull Gong recordings of successful CXO calls and identify the exact phrase that made this persona agree to a meeting — rewrite the SDR opener to mirror that language. Also audit lead source: if most MQLs are from content syndication, they may be research-stage only, not decision-stage — add a behavioral engagement gate before SDR assignment.

**Problem: LinkedIn ads getting impressions but zero CISO clicks**
Solution: CISOs have high ad fatigue and strong professional boundaries on LinkedIn. Switch from company-page ads to Thought Leader Ads run through a practitioner CISO or security leader's personal account — peer-to-peer content dramatically outperforms brand voice for this role. Also test Document Ads with a tangible benchmark or tool — CISOs will click for a useful artifact, not a demo offer.

**Problem: Lead magnet is downloaded but no nurture engagement beyond touch 1**
Solution: The nurture sequence is likely too promotional too fast. CISOs need 3-4 value-only touches before any soft offer. Audit touch 2 and 3 — if they contain product language, replace with peer insight content (what other CISOs are doing, third-party data, analyst quotes). Add a channel shift to LinkedIn retargeting at day 6 so the brand shows up in their feed without being email-intrusive.
