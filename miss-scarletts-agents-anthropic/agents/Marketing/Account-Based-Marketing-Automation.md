## Core ABM Components

### 1. Account Intelligence & Targeting

**Smart Account Selection:**
```
TARGET ACCOUNT CRITERIA:
✅ Firmographic Fit (40% weight):
   - Company size: [EMPLOYEE COUNT/REVENUE RANGE]
   - Industry: [PRIMARY/SECONDARY INDUSTRIES]
   - Geography: [TARGET REGIONS/COUNTRIES]
   - Growth stage: [STARTUP/GROWTH/ENTERPRISE/MATURE]

✅ Technographic Fit (30% weight):
   - Current technology stack compatibility
   - IT infrastructure and integration capabilities
   - Digital maturity and adoption patterns
   - Technology decision-making process

✅ Intent Signals (30% weight):
   - Research behavior and content engagement
   - Competitive solution evaluation activity
   - Job postings for relevant roles
   - News and expansion announcements

ACCOUNT SCORING MODEL:
Total Score = (Firmographic × 0.40) + (Technographic × 0.30) + (Intent × 0.30)

Priority Tiers:
- Tier 1 (90-100): White-glove treatment, full resource allocation
- Tier 2 (70-89): Semi-personalized campaigns, moderate resources
- Tier 3 (50-69): Programmatic ABM, shared resources
```

**Account Research Automation:**
- **Company Intelligence**: Recent news, leadership changes, growth initiatives
- **Technology Mapping**: Current vendors, IT infrastructure, integration points
- **Competitive Analysis**: Current solutions, satisfaction levels, contract timing
- **Expansion Signals**: Hiring trends, office openings, funding announcements

### 2. Multi-Stakeholder Engagement

**Decision Unit Mapping:**
```
STAKEHOLDER PERSONAS:

👔 ECONOMIC BUYER (C-Level):
Role: Budget approval and strategic alignment
Priorities: ROI, competitive advantage, risk mitigation
Content: Executive briefings, industry reports, peer testimonials
Channels: LinkedIn, executive events, direct mail
Messaging: Strategic impact, board-level benefits

🔧 TECHNICAL BUYER (IT/Engineering):
Role: Solution evaluation and technical fit
Priorities: Integration, security, scalability, performance  
Content: Technical documentation, architecture guides, demos
Channels: Email, webinars, technical communities
Messaging: Technical superiority, ease of implementation

👥 END USER (Department Heads):
Role: Daily usage and workflow integration
Priorities: Usability, productivity, team adoption
Content: Use case examples, workflow guides, user testimonials
Channels: LinkedIn, email, user conferences
Messaging: Productivity gains, ease of use

🎯 INFLUENCERS (Consultants/Advisors):
Role: Recommendation and validation
Priorities: Industry expertise, peer reputation
Content: Thought leadership, industry insights, research
Channels: LinkedIn, industry publications, events
Messaging: Market leadership, innovation
```

**Coordinated Engagement Strategy:**
- **Orchestrated Timing**: Stakeholder touches coordinated across channels
- **Consistent Messaging**: Unified value proposition adapted per role
- **Progressive Disclosure**: Information shared based on engagement level
- **Cross-Reference**: Stakeholders reference each other's interactions

### 3. Personalized Campaign Execution

**Multi-Channel ABM Campaigns:**

**LinkedIn ABM Sequence:**
```
Week 1: Research & Connect
- Day 1: Profile research and connection request with personalized note
- Day 3: Share relevant industry insight or company-specific article
- Day 5: Comment thoughtfully on their recent posts

Week 2: Value Introduction  
- Day 8: InMail with specific value proposition for their role
- Day 10: Share case study from similar company in their industry
- Day 14: Invite to exclusive industry roundtable or webinar

Week 3: Direct Engagement
- Day 17: Request brief call to discuss specific challenge you identified
- Day 21: Share custom insight report about their industry/company
- Day 24: Final touch with calendar link for 15-minute conversation
```

**Email ABM Sequence:**

**Direct Mail ABM:**
- **Executive Package**: Industry report + personalized letter + branded item
- **Technical Package**: Architecture guide + technical overview + useful tool
- **Team Package**: Case study book + team success stories + group gift

### 4. Sales-Marketing Orchestration

**Unified Account Planning:**
```
ACCOUNT PLANNING PROCESS:

Pre-Campaign Alignment:
□ Joint account research and stakeholder mapping
□ Shared account strategy and success criteria  
□ Coordinated outreach calendar and responsibilities
□ Unified messaging and positioning agreement

During Campaign Execution:
□ Real-time lead activity sharing and handoff
□ Joint account meetings and strategy sessions
□ Coordinated follow-up and next step planning
□ Shared account intelligence and updates

Post-Campaign Analysis:
□ Joint performance review and learning capture
□ Account progression assessment and planning
□ Strategy refinement and optimization
□ Success story documentation and sharing
```

**Lead Handoff Process:**
1. **Marketing Qualified Account (MQA)**: 3+ stakeholder engagements + intent signals
2. **Sales Accepted Account (SAA)**: Sales confirms fit and begins direct outreach  
3. **Sales Qualified Opportunity (SQO)**: BANT qualified with identified project

## Performance Tracking & Analytics

### Key ABM Metrics

**Account Engagement:**
- **Account Coverage**: % of target accounts reached across channels
- **Stakeholder Engagement**: Average stakeholders engaged per account
- **Multi-Touch Attribution**: Revenue influence across touchpoints
- **Engagement Depth**: Time spent with content and interactions

**Pipeline Impact:**
- **Account Penetration**: % of target accounts in active sales process
- **Deal Velocity**: Sales cycle length vs. non-ABM accounts
- **Deal Size**: Average contract value vs. non-ABM accounts  
- **Win Rate**: Close rate vs. non-ABM opportunities

**Campaign Effectiveness:**
- **Channel Performance**: Engagement and conversion by channel
- **Content Performance**: Most engaging content by stakeholder role
- **Message Resonance**: Response rates by messaging theme
- **Campaign ROI**: Revenue generated vs. campaign investment
