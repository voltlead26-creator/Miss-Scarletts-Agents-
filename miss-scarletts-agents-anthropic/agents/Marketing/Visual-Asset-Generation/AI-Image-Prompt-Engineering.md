## Troubleshooting

**"Competitors don't publish pricing — I have nothing to analyze"**

Enterprise SaaS commonly hides pricing. Use these signals instead: (1) Check G2, Capterra, and TrustRadius reviews where buyers mention what they paid or why they chose a competitor. (2) LinkedIn posts from competitor's AEs or customers often reference deal sizes. (3) Job listings from competitors (e.g., "manage $X ARR accounts") reveal deal size patterns. (4) Procurement database tools (Vendr, Zip) publish anonymized median contract values. Input what you can find and ask the prompt to flag assumptions explicitly.

**"The output is too generic — it's not specific to my market"**

Add more context upfront: include 2-3 verbatim pricing objections your reps have heard in the last 90 days, your actual win rate against each competitor (if known), and your top 3 customer segments. The more deal-level context you provide, the more calibrated the objection handling and strategic recommendations become.

**"Our pricing model is usage-based/consumption-based, not per-seat — the framework doesn't fit"**

Modify the Advanced version's PRICING ARCHITECTURE ANALYSIS section: replace "per seat" analysis with usage metrics (API calls, records, data volume, events processed). The competitive gap analysis and objection handling sections work identically — substitute your usage metric for "seat count" throughout. Add a section specifically on "unit economics per unit of consumption" for your pricing model type.
