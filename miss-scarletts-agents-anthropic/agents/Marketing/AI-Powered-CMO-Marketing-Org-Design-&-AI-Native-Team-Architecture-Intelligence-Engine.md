## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The AI Role Impact Matrix recommends eliminating roles that are actually critical for regulatory compliance, customer relationships, or institutional knowledge.
**Solution:** Add a "Protected Functions" parameter to the Advanced version context before running. List specific roles that cannot be automated regardless of AI capability (e.g., "Customer reference program coordinator owns 40 customer relationships that have contributed to $8M in expansion revenue — flag as Elevate only"). The model will adjust its recommendations accordingly.

**Problem:** The financial model produces unrealistically aggressive savings projections that leadership will question.
**Solution:** Add a "Conservative Scenario" modifier: "Apply a 40% haircut to all time-savings estimates and a 6-month delay to all productivity ramp timelines. Show the base case and conservative case side by side." This produces a credible range rather than a single optimistic number, which boards trust more.

**Problem:** The reskilling roadmap assumes team members are willing to learn AI tools, but you have significant change resistance in your team.
**Solution:** Run the prompt again with the specific friction added: "Assume 30% of retained team members are actively resistant to AI adoption. Design the reskilling plan with a resistance management track: 1) identify resistance early warning signals, 2) create peer champion roles, 3) design opt-in AI experiments with visible wins, 4) set clear consequences for non-adoption at 90 days." This generates a more realistic change management approach.
