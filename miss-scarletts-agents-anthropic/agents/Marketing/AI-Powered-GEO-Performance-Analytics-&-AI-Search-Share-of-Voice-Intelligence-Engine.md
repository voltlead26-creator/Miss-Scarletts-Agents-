## Integration Tips

- **Airtable/Notion GEO Tracker:** Build a live database with columns for each query, each AI engine, brand mention (Y/N), position, sentiment, and date — run weekly spot checks and log results to track trends over time
- **Zapier/Make.com Automation:** Trigger weekly GEO audit runs via scheduled webhook; pipe results into Slack channel alerting marketing team to visibility changes >5 points
- **HubSpot/Salesforce Source Tracking:** Add "AI search recommendation" as a Lead Source option in your CRM; train SDRs and AEs to ask "How did you first hear about us?" during discovery and log AI mentions — feeds real attribution data back into GEO ROI model
- **Google Sheets Dashboard:** Create a rolling 13-week GEO visibility score tracker per engine; chart trend lines per competitor — present in monthly marketing reviews alongside traditional SEO metrics
- **Perplexity API / OpenAI API:** Partially automate query testing by building a simple script that sends 20-50 standardized queries to GPT-4o and Claude APIs, captures responses, and uses a secondary prompt to classify brand mentions — reduces weekly manual testing to 15 minutes
- **Competitor Alert Integration:** Feed GEO audit results into your competitive intelligence dashboard (Crayon, Klue, Kompyte) to correlate changes in AI mentions with competitor content or PR activity

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** AI model responses vary widely across sessions — same query produces different mentions on different runs, making benchmarking unreliable.
**Solution:** Run each query 3× and use the majority result (2 of 3) as the canonical data point. Use the same model and version consistently (e.g., always GPT-4o, not the default auto-select). Document your testing protocol. Week-over-week trends are more reliable than single-point snapshots — focus on 4-week rolling averages.

**Problem:** The GEO visibility score looks low even though anecdotally customers say "I found you through ChatGPT" — the audit doesn't match self-reported attribution.
**Solution:** The query universe may not reflect how real buyers actually search. Pull your last 20 "how did you find us?" responses from discovery calls where AI was mentioned, identify the exact language customers used, and add those verbatim queries to your test set. Customer-reported query language almost always outperforms hypothetically constructed queries.

**Problem:** Hard to get leadership buy-in for GEO investment because there's no clear revenue number attached to AI search visibility.
**Solution:** Use the directional attribution model in Module 6 with conservative assumptions: estimate 5-10% of current inbound "direct" traffic is AI-influenced (industry benchmark for B2B SaaS in 2026), multiply by your average MQL-to-close rate and ACV. Even with conservative assumptions, most B2B companies find $500K-$2M in AI-influenced pipeline once they look. Present this as the "floor" of AI search revenue, not the ceiling.
