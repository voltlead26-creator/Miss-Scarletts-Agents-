## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Executive sponsor is unresponsive to kickoff and milestone communications — emails read but not replied to.
**Solution:** Escalate the conversation to the Champion and ask them to resend with a personal 1-line intro. Compress the content to a single metric the exec cares about most (usually: revenue impact or cost savings in dollars). If no response after 3 CSM attempts, flag as "Executive Sponsor Risk" in your CS platform, request a warm re-introduction via the Champion, and temporarily shift all communication to the Champion until the executive is re-engaged. Do not skip the executive — low executive engagement at Day 30 is the #1 predictor of renewal risk at Day 270.

**Problem:** End users resist adoption because they perceive the tool as surveillance (most common with call recording, email tracking, or activity monitoring products).
**Solution:** Lead all end-user communication with the "help not evaluation" framing — activate the Executive Sponsor's internal launch announcement before any CSM-direct communication reaches end users. The peer trust of an internal executive announcement consistently outperforms vendor-sourced messaging by 3–5x in adoption lift. Include a "What is recorded and what stays private" transparency one-pager in the Quick Start Guide. Have the manager (not the CSM) deliver the activation message in person or on a team call.

**Problem:** The 90-day value report reveals below-target adoption or unrealized KPIs — the numbers don't support the renewal narrative.
**Solution:** Never obscure the gap — transparency at 90 days builds the trust that drives renewal at Day 270. Use the Red milestone template language: acknowledge the shortfall factually, quantify what WAS achieved (there is almost always measurable progress), deliver a specific recovery plan with named owner, concrete action, and revised timeline, and explicitly reframe the 90-day report as "the foundation" rather than the verdict. Accounts where CSMs proactively address gaps at Day 90 renew at 15–20% higher rates than accounts where gaps are minimized and surface at renewal negotiation.
