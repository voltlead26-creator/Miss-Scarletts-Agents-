## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Content completion rates are below 50% despite launching a full curriculum**
*Solution:* Audit your learning path length — most enterprise B2B learners have a 15-minute attention ceiling per session. Break modules > 8 minutes into micro-lessons of 3-5 minutes. Add a "Fast Track" 7-day path that covers only the 20% of features delivering 80% of value. Survey dropouts with one question: "What stopped you from completing?" — the answers are usually: "Too long," "Couldn't apply it right away," or "Not relevant to my role." Address the top 2 reasons in your next content sprint.

**Problem: Support ticket volume didn't decrease after launching training content**
*Solution:* The content exists but isn't being found at the right moment. Implement contextual education triggers: (1) Embed relevant module links directly in your product UI at the exact feature causing confusion (use Pendo, WalkMe, or Appcues to surface tooltips with links), (2) Configure your support chatbot to auto-suggest the relevant tutorial before routing to a human agent, (3) Analyze which ticket categories are highest volume and map each to a specific module — if the module doesn't exist, it's a production gap, not a distribution problem.

**Problem: Customers complete training but still aren't adopting key features**
*Solution:* Your content is teaching what to do, not why it matters to their specific job. Apply the Jobs-to-be-Done lens: rewrite every module objective to start with the business outcome, not the product action. Instead of "Learn how to build a report," use "See which deals are at risk of slipping before your Monday forecast call." Also check if your exercises use a sandbox account vs. their real data — learners who practice on their own pipeline data apply skills at 3x the rate of those using sample data. Add a "setup your real environment" prerequisite to all Tier 2+ modules.
