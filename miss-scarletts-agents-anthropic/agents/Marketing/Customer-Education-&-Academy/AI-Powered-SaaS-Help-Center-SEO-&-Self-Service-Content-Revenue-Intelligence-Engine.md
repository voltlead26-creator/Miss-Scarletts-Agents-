## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Output article recommendations don't match our product's complexity — topics feel too generic.**
Solution: Add your actual top 10 most-asked support questions verbatim to the prompt. Paste real ticket titles from Zendesk or Intercom exports. The more specific your ticket data, the more targeted the deflection recommendations. Also specify your product category at the feature level (e.g., "automated approval workflows with conditional logic and multi-party signing") not just the product name.

**Problem: SEO keyword suggestions have very low search volume for our niche B2B product.**
Solution: This is normal for deep technical B2B SaaS documentation. Add this instruction to the prompt: "Our help center SEO strategy prioritizes long-tail, high-intent keywords even with <100 monthly searches. Focus on keywords buyers use when evaluating [product category] tools, troubleshooting during trial, and comparing to [Competitor A] and [Competitor B]. Include competitor comparison keywords." Also ask explicitly for "bottom-of-funnel buyer research keywords" not just product how-to terms.

**Problem: Behavioral expansion signals in CRM are generating too many false positives — CS team is getting overwhelmed.**
Solution: Add qualification thresholds to the signal rules. Modify the prompt to specify: "For each expansion signal, define minimum engagement thresholds (e.g., 2+ article views in 7 days, not just 1 pageview) and combine with product usage signals (e.g., expansion-signal-sso only fires if user has also created >10 contracts in last 30 days)." This narrows the signal to genuinely sales-ready behavior.
