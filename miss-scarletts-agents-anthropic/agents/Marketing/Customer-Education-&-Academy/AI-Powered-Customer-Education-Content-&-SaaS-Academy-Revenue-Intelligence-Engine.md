## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Module completion rates are below 30% and users drop off after Module 1**
Fix: Audit the length and format of Module 1 — it's almost always too long (8+ min) or too conceptual (theory before product action). Apply the "hands-on by minute 3" rule: Users should complete an in-product action within the first 3 minutes of any module. Also check your enrollment trigger — are you sending users to the academy before they've logged into the product for the first time? Sequence matters: product login → first aha moment → academy enrollment.

**Problem: Certification pass rates are below 50%, creating frustration and support tickets**
Fix: This signals a content-assessment alignment gap. Map every exam question back to a specific module section. If 60%+ of failures cluster around the same question set, that module has a comprehension problem — not a learner problem. Add an ungraded "practice quiz" at the end of each module. Reduce question count and increase scenario-based questions (situational > factual recall). Consider an "exam prep" micro-module that explicitly reviews the assessment framework.

**Problem: The academy has content but CSMs never reference it and customers don't know it exists**
Fix: This is an internal adoption failure, not a content failure. Run a "CSM Certification Sprint" — certify your entire CS team in your own academy within 30 days of launch. Build academy links directly into your CRM (Gainsight, Salesforce) so they appear on every account page. Add a "recommended learning" section to your monthly customer newsletter. Brief Sales to mention the academy in late-stage deals as a differentiator. The academy won't market itself — build it into every customer touchpoint.
