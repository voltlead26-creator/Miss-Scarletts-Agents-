## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Champions complete certification but advocacy doesn't follow — expansion conversations never start despite strong community engagement.
**Solution:** The missing piece is almost always the internal pitch infrastructure. Champions want to advocate but lack the tools to justify budget internally. Close this gap by providing: a pre-filled ROI calculator populated with their actual usage data (not generic numbers), a customizable slide deck formatted for executive presentations, and a genuine offer from the CSM to join the internal meeting as a subject matter expert rather than as a salesperson. When Champions have a credible internal story to tell and a credible person to tell it with, conversion rates from advocacy intent to closed expansion increase by 3x.

**Problem:** Community engagement shows high passive activity (reactions, views) but very low substantive posting and discussion.
**Solution:** This pattern — high lurking, low contribution — indicates members do not yet feel safe sharing real challenges or admitting gaps in their knowledge. Break the pattern with three interventions: (1) Community managers and CSMs share "failure stories" first — what we tried, what didn't work, what we learned — which normalizes authentic sharing; (2) Run a "What's your most annoying [product] limitation?" thread monthly, making it safe and even cool to criticize; (3) In the weekly community digest, feature substantive posts and their authors prominently, demonstrating that contribution earns community status. Authentic community culture takes 60–90 days to establish — be consistent and patient.

**Problem:** CSMs resist using community signals for expansion outreach because they worry it seems like surveillance and will damage the relationship with the Champion.
**Solution:** Reframe the signal as a service trigger, not a monitoring action. Train CSMs to open every community-triggered outreach by referencing the specific value the Champion created: "I saw your post about rolling Runflow out to the Platform team — that kind of initiative is exactly what our top customers do before they expand. I wanted to proactively share what worked for [similar customer] so you don't have to figure it out from scratch." This positions the outreach as helpfulness triggered by the Champion's own success, not as surveillance. In practice, Champions respond positively more than 70% of the time when outreach is framed this way.
