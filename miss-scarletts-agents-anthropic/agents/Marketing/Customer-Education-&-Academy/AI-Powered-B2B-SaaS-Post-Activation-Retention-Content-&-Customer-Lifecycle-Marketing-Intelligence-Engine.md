## Integration Tips

- **Gainsight:** Map each lifecycle stage to a Gainsight Success Plan milestone. Use Journey Orchestrator to trigger email stages based on health score changes. Sync content templates to the Email/CTA library so CS can deploy directly from Gainsight timeline.
- **HubSpot:** Build each lifecycle stage as a separate workflow with enrollment triggers tied to custom properties (feature_activation_score, days_since_last_login, renewal_date). Use HubSpot sequences for at-risk emails (Stage 4) and marketing emails for Stage 1-3 to preserve deliverability separation.
- **Salesforce + Marketing Cloud:** Use Opportunity record fields (renewal date, health score) to trigger Journey Builder paths. Map each stage to a distinct Data Extension for segmentation and attribution reporting.
- **Totango / ChurnZero:** Export customer segments by health score tier and lifecycle stage, then load into your email platform as audience lists. Refresh segments weekly to ensure accuracy as customers move between stages.
- **Intercom:** Use Intercom's "Series" feature for Stage 1-2 in-app messages. Trigger product digest notifications (Stage 1C, 2D) based on last-seen events and feature usage flags. Layer with email for customers who ignore in-app.
- **Notion / Airtable:** Build a Customer Lifecycle Content Tracker with columns: Stage, Asset Type, Status, Trigger, Owner, Last Updated. Link to production files so CS and marketing share a single source of truth.
- **Zapier:** Connect your health scoring tool → Slack (alert CS when customer enters at-risk Stage 4 trigger) and CRM renewal date field → email platform enrollment (auto-enroll Stage 3 content 60 days before renewal).
- **Google Sheets:** Use a lifecycle content performance dashboard with tabs per stage. Track: sends, opens, clicks, feature activation delta, health score movement, and renewal outcome. Refresh weekly from email platform export.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Customers ignore all re-engagement emails (Stage 4) and the sequence completes with no response.**
Solution: The failure is almost always segmentation, not copy. Before deploying Stage 4, verify: (1) the email address is current (check CRM for job change signals via LinkedIn or ZoomInfo), (2) the champion is still at the company, (3) you're not competing with a flood of CS outreach simultaneously. If all checks pass, switch from marketing email to a plain-text direct email sent from the AE or CSM — no images, no footer, personal signature. Response rates for plain-text from a named human are 3-5x higher than branded emails for disengaged accounts.

**Problem: Stage 1 feature adoption emails generate clicks but no actual feature activation.**
Solution: The CTA is sending customers to a page they can't immediately act on. Audit the click destination: if it's a help article, swap to an in-app deep link that drops the user directly into the feature. If deep links aren't available, create a 60-second Loom video showing the exact setup steps, embed the thumbnail in the email, and link to a landing page with one action button ("Set it up now"). Reduce the setup friction to under 3 clicks.

**Problem: Stage 3 ROI emails feel generic because you don't have clean usage data to populate the metrics.**
Solution: Build a tiered ROI calculation approach: (1) Best case — pull actual usage data from your analytics tool (logins, reports generated, deals updated) and do the math; (2) Peer benchmark fallback — use industry median benchmarks ("Teams your size typically save X hours/week") with a clear disclosure that these are benchmarks, not their specific data; (3) Ask approach — send a 3-question email before the ROI summary asking "Help us capture your ROI — what's changed most since using [Product]?" Then use their answers to write a custom ROI summary email. Option 3 has the highest engagement and doubles as voice-of-customer research.
