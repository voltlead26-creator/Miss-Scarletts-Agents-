## Integration Tips

- **Semrush / Ahrefs:** Export keyword rankings for you and each competitor monthly. Feed the CSV into this prompt to get a data-driven organic SOV gap analysis rather than estimated benchmarks.
- **G2 Buyer Intent (G2 Market Intelligence):** Once you qualify for this product, pipe intent data into HubSpot or Salesforce to trigger sales sequences when buyers on your target account list view competitor profiles.
- **Brandwatch / Mention.com:** Set up share of voice tracking dashboards for earned media and social mentions. Schedule monthly automated exports and paste the data into this prompt's Phase 1E input for precise competitive benchmarking.
- **SpyFu / SimilarWeb:** Pull competitor paid search impression share estimates and traffic data. Use as structured input for the paid search SOV section to replace estimates with real intelligence.
- **Google Search Console:** Export your top 1,000 branded and non-branded queries and filter for category keywords where you appear on page 2. These are your highest-leverage quick-win SEO opportunities.
- **Notion / Google Sheets SOV Dashboard:** Build a monthly SOV tracking spreadsheet with one row per channel, one column per competitor. Link this prompt output to auto-populate the dashboard cells each quarter.
- **Zapier → Slack:** Set up a Zapier workflow that pings a #competitive-intel Slack channel any time a tracked competitor gains a new G2 review mentioning your brand name, so you can respond strategically in real time.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Competitor data is too thin to make specific organic SOV estimates.**
Solution: Run the prompt with explicit data you do have — paste in 10–15 keywords from your Semrush/Ahrefs account along with your current rankings and competitor rankings. The prompt performs best as a synthesis engine when given real data, not pure estimation. Supplement with free tools: Google SERP manual checks for your top 20 keywords, SpyFu free tier for competitor keyword volume estimates.

**Problem: The output prioritizes channels that don't match where my ICP actually researches vendors.**
Solution: Add a line to the Business Context section: "My ICP's primary research channels in order of influence: [e.g., peer referrals > G2 > Google search > LinkedIn > analyst reports]." This forces the prompt to weight its recommendations toward the channels that actually drive shortlist decisions for your specific buyer, rather than applying generic B2B benchmarks.

**Problem: The 90-day roadmap feels too ambitious given our team size.**
Solution: Add a resource constraint to the prompt: "Our PMM team is 1 person. Limit recommendations to initiatives one person can execute without agency support in 5 hours/week." The prompt will reprioritize toward highest-leverage, lowest-effort plays — typically review acceleration, AI search entity optimization, and 1–2 organic quick-win pages.
