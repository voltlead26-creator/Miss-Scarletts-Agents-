## Troubleshooting

**Problem: CSMs are resistant — "Marketing is stepping on my accounts."**
Fix: Before launching, run a 30-minute alignment session with CS leadership. Frame marketing as a warm-up layer, not a replacement for CS relationship. Give CSMs a "suppress from campaign" button for any account they're actively working. Share weekly campaign reports with CSMs so they see what marketing is saying. Start with 3 pilot accounts per CSM and co-design the sequence together.

**Problem: Usage trigger data isn't flowing reliably from product to marketing systems.**
Fix: Before building campaigns, audit the data pipeline: (1) confirm product usage events fire correctly in your CS platform, (2) test the Zapier/API connection to your MAP with a dummy account, (3) set a manual weekly export process as a fallback while automation is being fixed. Don't launch trigger-based campaigns until you've verified data reliability with 10 real accounts over 2 weeks.

**Problem: Campaign emails get low open rates (<15%) and customers complain about over-emailing.**
Fix: First, check send frequency — if customers are enrolled in multiple sequences simultaneously (onboarding, product updates, expansion), consolidate to a single weekly touchpoint. Second, audit the subject lines — expansion emails must lead with value, not product. A/B test subject lines: "You're outgrowing your plan" vs. "[Company]: your Q2 usage report" vs. a blank curiosity-style subject. Third, check segmentation — are you sending upsell emails to accounts that just went Red health score? Filter at-risk accounts out of expansion campaigns and route them to churn prevention sequences instead.
