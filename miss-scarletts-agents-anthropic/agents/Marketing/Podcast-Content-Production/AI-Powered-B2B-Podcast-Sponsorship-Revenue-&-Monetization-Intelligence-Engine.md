## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Create a "Podcast Sponsor" deal stage in your CRM pipeline. Track outreach, proposal sent, negotiation, and closed-won. Set automated follow-up reminders at each stage.
- **Notion**: Build a Sponsor Intelligence database with properties for: company name, contact, package tier, contract dates, deliverables checklist, monthly report status, renewal date.
- **Google Sheets**: Use the KPI dashboard template as a Google Sheet with conditional formatting (red/yellow/green). Share a view-only link with sponsors each month.
- **Zapier/Make**: Auto-generate sponsor monthly reports by pulling download data from Chartable or Spotify for Podcasters API → formatting via AI → sending via email on the 5th of each month.
- **Chartable / Podtrac**: Use these platforms to generate credible, third-party verified download counts for your media kit — sponsors trust independent measurement over self-reported numbers.
- **Transistor.fm / Buzzsprout**: Dynamic ad insertion (DAI) platforms let you swap sponsor spots across your back catalog — sell "back catalog" packages to sponsors for passive revenue from legacy episodes.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sponsors say our download count is too low to justify budget**
Solution: Reframe the conversation around audience quality, not quantity. A 4,000-download podcast reaching 100% RevOps directors is more valuable than a 40,000-download general business podcast where 2% are RevOps. Present your ICP % calculation and show CPL (cost per qualified listener) vs. LinkedIn Ads CPL ($80-150/click). Podcast reach at $25-60 CPM typically beats LinkedIn on a per-impression basis.

**Problem: Sponsor wants guaranteed lead counts or pipeline attribution**
Solution: Be transparent that podcasts build brand familiarity, not direct response conversion — and that's what they're buying. Offer a hybrid model: guaranteed download count (contractual minimum) + promo code tracking + quarterly listener survey to measure brand lift. Never guarantee specific pipeline numbers you can't control.

**Problem: No response to outreach after full 4-touch sequence**
Solution: Check your targeting — if you're reaching the wrong persona, no amount of follow-up will convert. Try reaching the same company through a different contact (e.g., if Head of Content doesn't respond, try Demand Gen Manager). Also, time outreach to company events: new funding announcements, product launches, or conference season are when marketing teams have activated budgets and urgent distribution needs.
