## Overview

Designs a launch-ready B2B podcast strategy from competitive positioning to episode 12 — including audio landscape analysis, show format architecture, editorial calendar, platform optimization, and a 90-day launch playbook — so your company enters the podcast market as a category authority, not a commodity voice.

OBJECTIVES: Produce a complete podcast launch strategy that positions this show as the definitive voice in its category, builds a qualified audience that maps to the company's ICP, and creates a sustainable content operation that generates pipeline within 6 months.

ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK — produce all seven modules:

MODULE 1: COMPETITIVE AUDIO LANDSCAPE ANALYSIS
Before designing the show, map the territory:
- Identify the 5 most-listened-to podcasts in this category (use Apple Podcasts category rankings + Spotify charts as proxy)
- For each competitor show, analyze: format, typical episode length, guest quality tier, estimated monthly downloads, listener demographic fit, primary content angle
- Identify OVERCROWDED angles: what topics every show in this space already covers
- Identify WHITE SPACE: topic areas, audience segments, or formats completely underserved by existing shows
- Name the #1 show in the category and diagnose exactly why it works (format, host authority, guest access, topic consistency, community)
- Define the positioning wedge: the ONE dimension on which this new show will be definitively superior

SHOW CONCEPT DEVELOPMENT (using "Only Show That" framework):
Complete: "This is the ONLY B2B podcast in [category] that [unique, specific, defensible promise]"

Present 3 distinct show concepts:
- Concept A (Safe/Adjacent): Builds on existing proven formats in this category — lower risk, slower differentiation
- Concept B (Differentiated): Different format OR different audience angle than existing shows — recommended for most B2B companies
- Concept C (Contrarian): Directly challenges the assumptions of the dominant show — highest upside, highest execution risk

For each concept, provide:
- Show name + tagline (should be searchable AND memorable)
- Apple Podcasts description (250 words max — this is real discoverability copy, not a placeholder)
- Ideal listener persona: specific title, company profile, the one most painful problem this show solves for them
- The "anti-audience" — who this show is explicitly NOT for (positioning clarity requires exclusion)
- What shows it replaces or complements in the listener's weekly rotation
- Business alignment score (1-10): how directly does this concept support the stated primary goal?

Recommendation: Name the winning concept with a 3-sentence rationale referencing the white space analysis.

MODULE 2: FORMAT, STRUCTURE & PRODUCTION STANDARDS
Design the show's core architecture:

EPISODE FORMAT RECOMMENDATION with competitive rationale:
- Interview (access to external expertise, guest network effects, built-in promotion amplification)
- Solo/opinion (fastest to produce, builds host authority fastest, best for AI search citation due to declarative sentence density)
- Panel (rich dialogue, hard to produce without crosstalk, best for community-building shows)
- Narrative/reported (highest production cost, highest differentiation, best for thought leadership brands)
- Hybrid (recommended for most B2B shows: alternate solo deep-dives with interviews)

EPISODE LENGTH BENCHMARKS BY AUDIENCE:
- C-suite / board-level: 20–30 min (respects time scarcity, forces editorial discipline)
- VP/Director practitioners: 35–50 min (allows depth, fits commute)
- Manager/IC practitioners: 45–65 min (can go deep on tactics)
- Choose based on ICP title — do NOT choose length based on how much content you have

EPISODE ARCHITECTURE:
- Hook (first 60–90 seconds): Must deliver the episode's most counterintuitive insight immediately. Never start with "Welcome back to [show name]" — that rewards loyal listeners but punishes first-timers. The first sentence must give a new listener a reason to stay.
- Segment 1 (Context): Frame the problem or topic (5–8 min)
- Segment 2 (Expertise): Core content / interview deep-dive (15–25 min)
- Segment 3 (Application): "So what does this mean for you?" tactical translation (5–8 min)
- CTA (final 90 seconds): Episodically relevant offer — tied to the specific topic just discussed, not a generic demo request
- Close: Consistent show sign-off that builds recall and teases next episode

PRODUCTION STANDARDS BY BUDGET TIER:
- DIY: Blue Yeti or Samson Q2U microphone ($100–150); Descript for editing ($24/mo); Buzzsprout hosting ($12/mo); Canva Pro for graphics
- Startup: Same mic; outsource editing to Fiverr editor ($30–50/episode); Transistor hosting ($19/mo); Riverside.fm for remote recording ($15/mo)
- Professional: Shure SM7B or equivalent ($400); dedicated audio editor (freelance, $75–100/episode); Megaphone or Captivate hosting; video-first recording with Riverside Pro
- Premium: Full production studio or dedicated podcast production agency; video editor for YouTube companion content; PR and chart-performance campaigns

CADENCE DECISION MATRIX:
- Weekly: Maximum growth ceiling (algorithm favors frequency), maximum production burden — only sustainable with dedicated production support
- Biweekly: Recommended for most B2B teams in first 6–12 months; sustainable quality; still algorithmic favorability
- Monthly: Easiest to maintain quality; audience growth 40–60% slower than weekly; only recommend if episodes are genuinely long-form (60+ min) or narrative format

MODULE 3: FIRST 12-EPISODE EDITORIAL CALENDAR
Design a launch season that establishes authority immediately:

BATCH LAUNCH STRATEGY (release Episodes 1–3 simultaneously):
Apple's New & Noteworthy algorithm rewards shows that generate download spikes. Releasing 3 episodes on Day 1 means listeners immediately consume multiple episodes, inflating per-listener download counts and signaling show quality to the algorithm.

EPISODE FRAMEWORK:

Foundation Series (Episodes 1–3 — must be your BEST content):
- Episode 1: "The [Industry] Manifesto" — Host solo episode: 3–5 strongly-held beliefs about the industry that are defensible but not universally held. Must stake a position. Must be shareable by people who agree AND people who disagree.
  * Why: Establishes independence, authority, and a clear POV in the first 25 minutes. Listeners decide if the show is "for them" immediately.
- Episode 2: "The Problem Nobody Talks About" — Interview with a candid practitioner who will speak honestly about a failure, near-miss, or industry dysfunction
  * Why: Vulnerability and case-study formats earn trust faster than credentials. Real stories get shared.
- Episode 3: "The Expert Take" — Your single most impressive available guest (most recognizable name, most senior title, or most cited author in your space)
  * Why: Opens with social proof that attracts listeners who won't risk their time on an unknown show

Proof Series (Episodes 4–8 — demonstrate range and depth):
- Cover 3–4 distinct subtopics within your category (ensures keyword/topic diversity for SEO)
- Guest mix per 5 episodes: 2 practitioners (tactical, in-the-trenches), 1 senior executive (strategic), 1 researcher or analyst (data-backed credibility), 1 adjacent-category expert (unexpected angle)
- Include one data-driven episode: either original survey data OR reanalysis of publicly available industry report with your own interpretation and POV

Signature Series (Episodes 9–12 — begin establishing recurring formats):
- Episode 9: Introduce a recurring framework (e.g., "The [Show Name] Assessment" or "3 Questions I Always Ask") — listeners remember shows that have a repeatable structure
- Episode 10: Contrarian/hot-take episode — pick one widely-held belief in your industry and argue against it with evidence
- Episodes 11–12: Begin sourcing guests from audience nominations (shows you're listening to your listeners; generates community engagement)

GUEST SOURCING CRITERIA for launch batch:
- Minimum: 1,000+ LinkedIn followers OR published industry authority (published article, keynote speaker credit)
- Preferred: Will share episode to their audience of 3,000+ relevant followers within 72 hours of release
- Ideal: Has a story they've never told publicly (exclusive angle increases shareability)
- Avoid: PR-coached executives who won't give real opinions; anyone whose answer to every question circles back to their company's solution; guests who have been on 5+ podcasts in the last 3 months (oversaturated, lower shareability)

MODULE 4: PLATFORM STRATEGY & DISCOVERY OPTIMIZATION

AUDIO PLATFORM PRIORITY:
- Apple Podcasts: Primary for chart performance and "New & Noteworthy"; optimize show description with 3–5 target search phrases; submit RSS 7 days before launch for review
- Spotify: Highest total listenership in B2B tech demographics (25–44); Spotify for Podcasters provides demographic data no other platform offers; optimize show name for Spotify search
- Amazon Music / Audible: Fastest-growing podcast platform; auto-submit via hosting RSS; zero incremental effort
- Google Podcasts/YouTube Music: Auto-publish via RSS; focus effort on YouTube companion content instead

YOUTUBE COMPANION STRATEGY (highest ROI for B2B):
- Video-first recommendation: For any ICP under 45, YouTube is now the #1 podcast discovery platform (source: Edison Research 2024)
- Recording setup: Record all episodes on video via Riverside.fm; audio-only listeners lose nothing, video-first listeners get bonus value
- YouTube episode title format: "[Guest Name] on [Keyword Phrase] — [Show Name] Ep. [#]" (enables YouTube search discovery)
- YouTube Shorts repurposing: Extract 3 best 30–90 second clips per episode; upload as Shorts with text overlay; Shorts drive full-episode discovery
- YouTube SEO: Full transcript as video description (first 200 characters must contain primary keyword); chapter markers with keyword-rich labels

SHOW NOTES & SEO ARCHITECTURE:
Every show note page should be built as a standalone SEO asset, not just an episode summary:
- H1: Episode title with primary keyword phrase
- Opening paragraph (150 words): Episode summary containing 3 semantically related keyword phrases
- Key Timestamps: 5–8 chapters with descriptive labels (these appear in Google search results)
- 5 Bullet Takeaways: Formatted as complete, declarative sentences (optimized for AI citation)
- Featured Quote: Pull quote in blockquote format (highest-shareability element on the page)
- Full Transcript: 1,500+ words minimum; improves accessibility, feeds AI search engines, and drives 3–5x more organic search traffic per episode
- Internal links: 2–3 links to related blog posts or resources (reduces bounce rate, improves SEO authority flow)

AI SEARCH OPTIMIZATION (GEO for Podcasts):
- Schema markup: Add PodcastSeries and PodcastEpisode schema.org markup to all episode pages
- Quotable insight density: Every transcript should contain 5+ standalone, self-contained insights formatted as complete statements of fact ("Companies that launch 3 episodes simultaneously see 40% higher Week 1 download rates than those releasing single episodes")
- Citation anchor strategy: Consistently reference the show name within episode summaries so AI models learn to associate the show name with the topic category
- FAQ content: Add a 3–5 question FAQ section to episode show notes targeting "People Also Ask" entries in Google and AI chat responses

MODULE 5: 90-DAY LAUNCH PLAYBOOK

PRE-LAUNCH (Weeks 1–4):

Week 1 — Tech Foundation:
- [ ] Select and set up podcast hosting (Buzzsprout for beginners, Transistor for growth-focused, Megaphone for enterprise)
- [ ] Submit RSS to Apple Podcasts and Spotify (both require 3–7 business days for approval — do not skip)
- [ ] Set up recording environment: test with 3 throwaway recordings before Episode 1
- [ ] Create show landing page: hero image, 150-word show description, subscribe links, episode teaser, email capture form
- [ ] Set up UTM tracking for all show note links (critical for revenue attribution)

Week 2–3 — Batch Production:
- [ ] Record Episodes 1, 2, and 3 before publishing any (batch launch strategy)
- [ ] Record 60-second trailer: hook + show premise + target listener + subscribe CTA
- [ ] Edit all 3 launch episodes and trailer; quality-check on 3 different listening devices (phone speaker, earbuds, car)
- [ ] Design show cover art at 3000×3000px (most visible branding element on mobile podcast apps — do not use generic fonts)
- [ ] Create guest kit template: pre-written LinkedIn post for guest to share, custom audiogram (Canva), recording link, recording tips

Week 4 — Pre-Launch Momentum:
- [ ] Drop trailer-only to email list and LinkedIn audience (no public Apple/Spotify submission yet)
- [ ] Run "founding subscriber" campaign: direct personal outreach to 50 most relevant contacts asking them to subscribe on Day 1
- [ ] Submit to Apple Podcasts 7 days before launch date (leave buffer for review delays)
- [ ] Coordinate with all 3 launch guests on their simultaneous share timing (lock in a specific time)

LAUNCH WEEK — Hit Apple New & Noteworthy:
- Day 1, 4am UTC (Tuesday): Release Episodes 1, 2, and 3 simultaneously
  * This is the single most important tactical decision. 3 episodes in the feed signals content depth; listeners binge all 3; download counts spike; algorithm registers the show as high-quality
- Launch day email: Personal note from host (not marketing email format) — tell the story of WHY you started the show
- Host LinkedIn post: Personal narrative, not company announcement — the human story of why this podcast exists
- Coordinate all 3 launch guests to share within 2 hours of launch (pre-write their posts for them)
- Target metrics: 300+ downloads in first 72 hours + 150+ Apple Podcasts ratings (threshold for most niche B2B categories to appear in "New & Noteworthy" or category top charts)
- PR element: Submit "just launched" notice to 3 industry newsletters (most will run a brief mention for free)

POST-LAUNCH GROWTH MACHINE (Weeks 5–12):

Weekly content cadence:
- Monday: LinkedIn teaser post (1 key quote from upcoming episode, published by host personal account)
- Tuesday 4am UTC: Episode publishes (Tuesday morning US East = highest podcast consumption time based on Edison Research)
- Tuesday: Full LinkedIn post tagging guest (include a direct question to stimulate comments)
- Wednesday: LinkedIn audiogram clip (60-second video with captions — repurposed from Riverside or Descript)
- Thursday: LinkedIn carousel or text-only insight post repurposing a key framework from the episode
- Friday: Email newsletter recap (150–200 words, 3 key takeaways, link to show notes, CTA)

Growth amplification plays (months 2–3):
- Guest amplification upgrade: Audit which guests shared. If <50% shared, improve the guest kit. Include 3 pre-written LinkedIn post options (short, medium, long-form) + custom branded audiogram sized for their feed. Make sharing frictionless.
- Cross-promotion swap: Identify 3 non-competing shows serving adjacent audiences (e.g., if your show targets CISOs, partner with a DevOps show or a CFO-focused show). Trade 60-second promo read on each other's next episode.
- Community seeding: Share episode insights (not links) in 3–5 relevant Slack/Discord communities where ICP is active. Lead with the insight, mention the show organically. Never drop links without value first.
- Review ask: Script an in-episode review request for Episode 5 — "If this show has saved you time or given you one idea you've already acted on, 30 seconds to leave a review is the most valuable thing you can do to help me keep making it." Follow up with direct email to first 200 subscribers.

MODULE 6: AUDIENCE-TO-PIPELINE CONVERSION ARCHITECTURE
Connect listenership to measurable business outcomes:

LISTENER CONVERSION FUNNEL:
- Awareness → Subscribe (Episode): Show landing page with friction-free email capture; offer: "Get full transcripts and show notes delivered to your inbox"
- Subscribe → Engage (Email): 3-email welcome sequence:
  * Email 1 (immediate): Welcome + your 3 must-listen episodes + what to expect
  * Email 2 (Day 3): The show's "manifesto" — your 3 core beliefs about the industry
  * Email 3 (Day 7): Soft product/service mention — frame as "here's how we help people dealing with [show topic]"
- Engage → Convert (Lead): Episode CTAs drive to a specific, episodically relevant offer (not a generic "book a demo")
  * Template: "If today's conversation about [topic] made you think about [specific pain], we offer [specific relevant resource/offer]. Link in show notes."
- Convert → Advocate (Customer): Feature customer success stories as episodes; create a "listener community" Slack or LinkedIn group; ask loyal listeners for referrals

CTA PLACEMENT SCIENCE:
- Position CTA in the final 90 seconds (listeners who reach the end are 3× more likely to act than mid-episode)
- Make CTA episodically relevant (tied to that specific episode's topic — a generic demo ask converts at <0.3%; a topically relevant offer converts at 1.5–3%)
- Use a single vanity URL per episode (easy to remember, easy to attribute: e.g., yourcompany.com/podcast/security-assessment)

ATTRIBUTION METHODOLOGY:
- UTM parameters on every show note link: utm_source=podcast&utm_medium=show_notes&utm_campaign=[episode-slug]
- Dedicated podcast promo codes for audio CTA offers (tracks radio-style conversion)
- CRM field: "Podcast subscriber" tag on all contacts who came through podcast landing page
- Demo form question: "How did you hear about us?" — track "podcast" mentions quarter-over-quarter
- Expected pipeline math at scale: 1,000 monthly downloads × 8% email conversion = 80 subscribers/month × 4% demo conversion = 3–4 leads/month; improve each conversion rate by 2× and you get 10–12 leads/month by month 6

MODULE 7: PRODUCTION OPERATIONS & SUSTAINABILITY SYSTEM

SUSTAINABLE PRODUCTION WORKFLOW (90 minutes per episode target):
1. Pre-production (10 min): Guest prep email with 5 confirmed talking points; draft show intro; review guest's recent LinkedIn posts for timely hooks
2. Recording (45–60 min): Live recording via Riverside.fm or Squadcast; host keeps a "moment log" during recording (timestamps of key quotes for editing)
3. Editing (20–30 min with Descript): Remove filler words, dead air, and technical issues; Descript AI handles 80% of cleanup automatically
4. Show notes creation (20 min): Paste transcript into Claude/ChatGPT with prompt "Create SEO-optimized show notes with H2 structure, 5 key takeaways as complete sentences, a pull quote, and 150-word summary targeting [keyword]"
5. Distribution setup (15 min): Upload to hosting platform, schedule publication, queue social posts in Buffer or Hootsuite, send episode to newsletter

TEAM REQUIREMENTS BY BUDGET TIER:
- DIY: Host does everything; Descript for editing; Canva Pro for graphics; Buffer for social scheduling; 4–5 hours per episode total
- Startup: Host + 1 part-time VA (10 hrs/month) handles editing, show notes, and social scheduling; host focuses on recording and CTA/email writing only
- Professional: Host + Freelance producer ($75–150/episode) handles all post-production; PMM creates show notes and manages distribution; design resource creates clip graphics
- Premium: Full production agency ($2K–5K/month); dedicated video editor for YouTube; podcast PR firm for chart performance campaigns

CONSTRAINTS:
- Never recommend "be consistent and the audience will come" as a growth strategy — prescribe specific amplification tactics with expected output ranges
- All platform recommendations must reflect 2025–2026 algorithm realities (video-first on YouTube, Spotify demographic data availability, Apple's chart dynamics)
- Show concept must differentiate on exactly ONE primary dimension — shows that claim to be "more tactical AND more strategic AND better guests AND more entertaining" differentiate on nothing
- Production quality recommendations must be budget-appropriate — a DIY podcaster does not need a $1,200 microphone chain
- Revenue attribution must be built in from Day 1 — retrofitting analytics 6 months later means 6 months of blind spend

## Success Metrics

**Audience Quality (not vanity downloads):**
- ICP match rate: Use Spotify for Podcasters demographics — target >55% of listeners in "Manager" or above job function; >40% from companies 200+ employees
- Episode completion rate: >65% average completion signals content relevance; below 50% signals format/hook failure in the first 5 minutes
- Email subscriber conversion: >8% of cumulative downloads convert to email list (industry median is 5–6%; above 10% indicates exceptional show-to-offer fit)

**Growth Health (3-month benchmarks):**
- Episode 1 downloads in first 72 hours: >300 (threshold for most B2B categories to appear in Apple Podcasts charts)
- Guest amplification rate: >55% of guests share their episode within 72 hours of release
- Apple Podcasts rating: 4.6+ stars with >50 reviews within 90 days of launch
- Month-over-month download growth: >15% MoM in months 1–6

**Business Impact (6-month benchmarks):**
- Pipeline attributed to podcast: Track via UTM on show note CTAs + "how did you hear about us" in demo forms; target 5–10% of new pipeline mentioning podcast by Month 6
- Podcast-sourced email subscribers in CRM: Segment and track demo conversion rate vs. all-contact average (podcast subscribers typically convert at 1.5–2× the all-contact rate in B2B SaaS)
- Sales team mentions: Count how often prospects reference the podcast in initial sales calls (qualitative signal of brand building)

**Content Quality Gate:**
- Run a 10% listener survey at Month 3 with one question: "Would you recommend this podcast to a colleague?" — target >75% "definitely yes"
- If <65% recommend: Audit the first-5-minutes hook across last 4 episodes; most quality problems are front-loaded

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Downloads aren't growing past 60–80 per episode after 3 months of consistent publishing**
*Cause:* At this stage, stagnant growth almost always signals a distribution problem, not a content problem. The show is likely not being shared beyond the host's existing audience.
*Fix:* Audit your last 8 episodes — what percentage of guests shared their episode within 72 hours? If below 40%, your guest kit is creating friction. Pre-write 3 LinkedIn post variations for every guest (short/medium/long), send custom Canva audiogram graphics pre-sized for their feed, and send a personal follow-up 24 hours after publish: "Your episode went live — here's a pre-written post if you'd like to share it. Takes 30 seconds." Guest amplification is the highest-leverage growth tactic for shows under 500 downloads/episode; 1 guest with 5,000 LinkedIn followers who shares can add 100–200 new subscribers in a single day.

**Problem: High download numbers but zero pipeline or business impact after 6 months**
*Cause:* Show is attracting the wrong audience (enthusiasts, not buyers) OR CTAs are not converting OR attribution isn't set up to capture impact that exists.
*Fix:* Three-step diagnosis: (1) Pull Spotify for Podcasters demographics — if listener job titles don't match your ICP, content is too broad or positioned wrong. (2) Audit CTA placement — CTAs placed mid-episode or in the first 10 minutes convert at <0.3%; CTAs in the final 90 seconds to the most engaged listeners convert at 1.5–3%. (3) Add "how did you hear about us?" to every demo request form and audit last 90 days of responses — podcast impact is often present but invisible to attribution tools because listeners convert via direct search weeks after first listen.

**Problem: Host sounds unconfident, over-scripted, or generic in early episodes**
*Cause:* Normal for episodes 1–5. Hosts who read from scripts sound like they're reading from scripts. Hosts who have no framework at all ramble. The fix is structured spontaneity.
*Fix:* Before recording any launch episodes, have the host produce a 1-page "Conviction Document": their 5 strongest beliefs about the industry that are specific enough to disagree with. ("B2B content should be shorter" is too vague. "B2B companies that publish less than 2,000-word blog posts are wasting their time because Google's EEAT framework rewards depth, not frequency" is a conviction.) Record 2 throwaway practice episodes — never publish them, just use them to let the host hear their own patterns and eliminate verbal habits. Consider delaying the public launch by 2–3 weeks and recording Episodes 4–6 before publishing Episodes 1–3, so the host's natural cadence improves before the "permanent record" episodes go live.
