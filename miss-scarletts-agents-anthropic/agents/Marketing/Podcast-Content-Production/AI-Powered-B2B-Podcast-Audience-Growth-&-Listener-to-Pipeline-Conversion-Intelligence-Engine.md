## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Downloads are flat despite publishing consistently and promoting on LinkedIn**
Solution: Flat downloads with consistent posting typically indicate a podcast SEO problem, not a content quality problem. First, run a full title audit: open Spotify and Apple Podcasts, search for 5-10 keywords your ICP would use to find your show — does your podcast appear? If not, your show title lacks the keyword your ICP searches for. Second, check your episode title formula: A/B test two title structures for the next 4 episodes (counterintuitive outcome vs. tactical framework) and compare first-week downloads. Third, activate your guest network more aggressively — review the last 10 episodes and count how many guests actually shared the episode on LinkedIn. If fewer than 60%, your guest promotion package needs to be more specific and lower-friction (pre-written posts they can copy-paste in 30 seconds).

**Problem: Can't get podcast email subscribers even with in-episode CTAs**
Solution: Three common failure modes: (1) CTA placement too late — if you're only CTAing in the last 5 minutes, you've lost 30-40% of listeners before the ask; move the primary CTA to minute 3-5. (2) Lead magnet mismatch — "join our newsletter" is not compelling enough for a podcast listener who already gets content from you; offer something specific that delivers value they can't get from just listening (the show notes PDF, a resource mentioned in the episode, a bonus segment). (3) Vanity URL not promoted in show notes — a listener who hears the URL in audio and then goes to show notes should find a clickable link immediately. Audit your last 10 episode show notes: is the email subscribe link in the first 100 words?

**Problem: Podcast has listeners but no measurable pipeline impact after 6+ months**
Solution: This is almost always an attribution gap, not a pipeline gap — the impact exists but isn't being captured. Run a three-part audit: (1) Form audit — add "How did you hear about us?" to every demo request, contact, and trial signup form; add "Podcast" as an explicit option (not "Other"). (2) Sales discovery call audit — pull a random sample of 20 closed-won deals from the last 6 months and have AEs ask customers in QBRs if they listened to the podcast. Anecdotally, 20-40% of customers are listeners who never self-identified. (3) CRM enrichment — run your podcast email subscriber list against your HubSpot/Salesforce contacts; any overlap (same email) should be tagged "Podcast Listener" retroactively. This typically reveals 2-3× more pipeline influence than the UTM-only attribution would suggest.
