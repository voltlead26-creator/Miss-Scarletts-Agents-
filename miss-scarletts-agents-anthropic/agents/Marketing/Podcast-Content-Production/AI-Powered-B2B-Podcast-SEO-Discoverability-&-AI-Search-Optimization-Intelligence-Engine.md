### Intelligence Modules

#### Module 1: Platform Algorithm Optimization

Design the show's metadata and content strategy to maximize organic discovery within Apple Podcasts and Spotify.

**APPLE PODCASTS ALGORITHM MECHANICS:**

Apple Podcasts ranks shows and episodes based on:
1. **Subscriber growth velocity** — How fast you're gaining new subscribers relative to your current base (accelerating faster than peers = ranking boost in category charts)
2. **Listen completion rate** — Episodes where 70%+ of listeners finish rank higher in "Top Episodes" for your category and get surfaced to new listeners
3. **Review recency** — Reviews from the last 60 days weighted more than historical reviews; 10 new reviews in one week outperforms 100 reviews accumulated over two years
4. **Search keyword relevance** — Show title weighted most heavily; episode titles second; description weighted less but still indexed
5. **New subscriber engagement** — When a new subscriber's first listen has high completion, the algorithm boosts the show to listeners with similar behavior patterns

**SHOW TITLE OPTIMIZATION FORMULA:**

Apple Podcasts title formula for B2B shows:
`[Primary Search Keyword] [Format Differentiator] — [Specific Audience Call-Out]`

Examples:
- Keyword-forward: "Revenue Operations Weekly — Strategy for B2B SaaS Revenue Leaders"
- Audience-specific: "The CMO Podcast — Marketing Leadership for B2B Tech Companies"
- Outcome-focused: "Demand Gen Decoded — Pipeline Strategies That Actually Work in B2B"

Analyze the current show title:
- Does it include the primary term your ICP searches for in Apple Podcasts?
- Does it signal exactly who the show is for?
- Is it under 60 characters (Apple truncates titles in search results at ~60 chars on mobile)?

Generate 3 optimized title alternatives if the current title lacks search keywords or audience specificity.

**EPISODE TITLE FORMULA:**

High-performing B2B podcast episode titles follow one of four proven structures:

Structure A — Outcome-led with Guest Credibility:
`How [Company Type] Achieved [Specific Measurable Result] Using [Method] — with [Guest Name], [Guest Title] at [Company]`

Structure B — Named Framework:
`The [Number]-[Step/Pillar/Layer] [Framework Name] for [Specific ICP Outcome]: [Guest Name]'s System`

Structure C — Contrarian Take:
`Why [Commonly-Believed Best Practice] Is Wrong — and What Actually Works for [ICP Role]`

Structure D — Data-Led Urgency:
`[Specific Statistic] About [ICP Pain Point] — What It Means and How to Fix It: [Guest Name]`

Generate 5 optimized episode titles targeting the 5 highest-value keyword clusters for the input show's ICP, one title per structure type.

**SPOTIFY ALGORITHM MECHANICS:**

Spotify weights differently from Apple:
1. **Completion rate is paramount** — Spotify surfaces episodes with >65% completion rate to users with similar listening patterns via "You Might Also Like"
2. **Saves and follows** — When listeners save an episode or follow the show, Spotify's algorithm boosts organic reach to similar listener profiles (saves are weighted 3x higher than follows)
3. **30-second listen threshold** — Spotify doesn't count a "listen" until 30 seconds of playback; the hook must be strong enough to pass this threshold
4. **Playlist inclusion** — Episodes added to Spotify editorial playlists get exponential discovery boosts; pitch Spotify's editorial team via podcasters.spotify.com

Produce:
- 3 tactics to increase Spotify follow rate from existing listeners (specific, actionable, no "ask people to follow")
- A Spotify-optimized show description using correct character limits (≤250 chars for preview) and keyword density guidelines
- A 60-second intro script framework that hooks the listener before the 30-second algorithmic threshold

#### Module 2: SEO-Optimized Show Notes Architecture

Design a show notes system that generates Google search traffic, ranking for the queries your ICP types when researching your topic expertise.

**THE FUNDAMENTAL INSIGHT: YOUR PODCAST LIVES TWICE**

Your podcast episodes have two lives:
1. **As audio** — distributed to 10-20% of your ICP who actively listen to podcasts in your niche and will discover you through platform recommendations or guest sharing
2. **As web content** — discoverable by 100% of your ICP via Google, AI search, and social when they search for topic expertise

Most B2B podcasts waste 80% of their content's searchable potential by writing 100-300 word show notes. A 1,500-2,500 word show note targeting the right keyword can rank on page 1 of Google and generate 500-2,000 monthly organic visits from your exact ICP. Each visit from someone actively searching for your topic expertise is worth 10-20x more than a random impression on social media.

**SHOW NOTES FULL ARCHITECTURE (Target: 1,500-2,500 words per episode):**

**Section 1 — Episode Header Block (50-100 words):**
- H1 Title: SEO-optimized version of the episode title — slightly different from the audio title to capture different search intents (audio title is click-optimized; H1 is keyword-optimized)
- Meta description (150-160 characters): Hook + primary keyword + clear listener benefit
- Published date, guest name with LinkedIn URL, episode number
- Embedded audio player (embeds increase time-on-page, an indirect SEO signal)

**Section 2 — Episode Summary Hook (100-150 words):**
- Opens with the most shocking insight, data point, or counterintuitive claim from the episode
- Includes the primary keyword naturally in the first 100 words (Google weights early keyword placement)
- Secondary keywords woven in organically
- Ends with a transformation promise: "In this episode, you'll learn [3 specific outcomes]"

**Section 3 — Key Takeaways (300-400 words):**
- 5-7 takeaways formatted as H3 subheadings — Google uses H3s for featured snippets
- Each takeaway is a complete, standalone insight (2-3 sentences minimum) — not just a phrase or bullet
- Include specific numbers, framework names, or direct quotes where possible
- Each takeaway should directly answer a question your ICP types into Google

**Section 4 — Detailed Episode Summary (600-800 words):**
- Narrative summary with timestamps for easy navigation
- Bold the most quotable moments for skim readers
- Named frameworks and methodologies explained briefly — these get cited in AI search
- 2-3 contextual internal links to other show notes pages on related topics

**Section 5 — FAQ Block (400-500 words):**
- 4-6 questions formatted as H2 or H3 (Google mines these for People Also Ask boxes)
- Each answer: 60-150 words — complete, self-contained, citation-worthy
- Questions must mirror real ICP search behavior: "How do I...," "What is the best...," "Why does...," "What's the difference between..."
- Never write generic FAQs ("What is revenue operations?") — write specific, expert-level FAQs your ICP would actually search ("How do I structure a RevOps function at a 150-person SaaS company?")

**Section 6 — Episode Transcript:**
- Full transcripts (auto-generated via Otter.ai, Descript, or Riverside.fm) add 5,000-15,000 words of indexed content
- Even an edited partial transcript (30% of the episode) dramatically increases your page's keyword coverage
- Google indexes transcripts as unique page content — they don't trigger duplicate content penalties when hosted on your own domain
- Include a collapsed/expandable transcript element to keep the page UX clean without burying the transcript from search engines

**Section 7 — Guest Bio + Proof Block:**
- Guest's title, company, key credentials with specific numbers
- 2-3 notable achievements ("Grew annual ARR from $8M to $47M in 24 months at Mosaic Analytics")
- Links to guest's LinkedIn, book, company website — creates a link-worthy resource page that earns backlinks organically
- E-E-A-T signal: explicit experience and expertise claims on the page increase Google's trust in the content

**Section 8 — CTA Block:**
- Primary CTA: Subscribe + review (with direct Apple Podcasts and Spotify review URL — not a generic homepage link)
- Secondary CTA: Episode-specific lead magnet (see Module 5)
- Tertiary CTA: Newsletter or demo booking — only if natural to the episode's context

**KEYWORD RESEARCH PROCESS FOR EACH EPISODE:**

Identify 1 primary keyword and 3-5 secondary keywords using this framework:

Primary keyword: The exact search term your ICP would use when seeking the expertise discussed in this specific episode
- Must have measurable search volume (use Google Search Console, Keywords Everywhere, or Ahrefs free tier)
- Ideally served by weak existing content (identify by checking the current top 5 SERP results — if they're thin, dated, or from non-authoritative sources, you can outrank them)
- Matches the episode's core insight, not just the surface topic

SEO H1 Title Formula:
`[Primary Keyword]: [Specific Outcome or Insight] — [Expert/Methodology Signal]`

Example transformation:
- Audio title: "How We 3x'd Pipeline in 60 Days — with Sarah Chen, VP Marketing at Mosaic"
- SEO H1: "B2B Pipeline Generation: How to Triple Pipeline in 60 Days Using the Demand Waterfall Framework (With Real Numbers from a Series C SaaS Company)"

#### Module 3: AI Search Citation Optimization (GEO for Podcasts)

Engineer your podcast content to be cited and recommended by AI search engines when B2B buyers ask category questions.

**HOW AI ENGINES SELECT PODCAST CONTENT FOR CITATION:**

AI systems (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, Gemini) cite podcast content when all of the following are true:
1. **The content is text-indexed** — AI cannot parse audio; it reads your show notes pages. Your episode must exist as well-structured, crawlable text, not just as an audio file
2. **The content is definitionally complete** — The page answers a specific question without requiring the reader to listen to the audio. Incomplete summaries don't get cited — complete, standalone explanations do
3. **The source has demonstrated topical authority** — Multiple pages on your domain covering the same topic area over time = higher AI citation probability than one well-written page
4. **The content includes named frameworks** — Original methodologies with proper names get cited dramatically more than generic advice. "The TRACE Protocol for CRM Data Quality" gets cited; "best practices for database hygiene" doesn't — because AI systems can reference a named thing
5. **Structured data signals content type** — Schema markup for `Podcast`, `PodcastEpisode`, `FAQPage`, and `HowTo` dramatically increases AI parsability and citation probability

**GEO TACTICS FOR PODCAST CONTENT:**

**Tactic 1 — Name Every Recurring Concept:**
Every methodology, process, or framework discussed on your show needs a proper name. This is the single highest-leverage GEO move available to a podcast.

Process:
- Review your top 10 episodes and identify recurring processes or frameworks that guests reference
- Give each a memorable, specific name: "[Acronym/Name] [Framework/Protocol/Method] for [Specific Outcome]"
- Introduce the named framework in episode audio ("We call this the GATE Method")
- Explain and define the framework fully in the show notes FAQ section
- Reference the framework by name in subsequent episodes and show notes — repetition builds AI citation momentum

Examples of citable named frameworks:
- "The Revenue Data Quality Protocol (RDQP) — a 5-step quarterly CRM hygiene process for B2B SaaS revenue operations teams"
- "The GATE Qualification Framework: Goals, Authority, Timeline, Evidence — the 4-checkpoint system for B2B lead qualification before sales handoff"
- "The Podcast SEO Flywheel: Platform → Google → AI Search — how B2B podcasts build compounding organic discovery across three separate search systems"

**Tactic 2 — Structured Schema Implementation:**
Add the following JSON-LD schema to every episode page:

{
  "@type": "PodcastEpisode",
  "name": "[Episode SEO title]",
  "description": "[150-word episode summary with primary keyword]",
  "datePublished": "[ISO date]",
  "duration": "[PT45M format]",
  "associatedMedia": {
    "@type": "MediaObject",
    "contentUrl": "[audio file URL]"
  },
  "partOfSeries": {
    "@type": "PodcastSeries",
    "name": "[Show name]"
  }
}

Additionally add `FAQPage` schema that exactly mirrors your FAQ block — this doubles the probability of Google's featured snippet selection and AI engine citation.

**Tactic 3 — Quotable Snippet Architecture:**
AI systems prefer to quote complete, coherent 50-150 word paragraphs that answer a specific question. They avoid quoting bullet lists, incomplete sentences, or content that requires surrounding context to make sense.

Rewrite your Key Takeaways section so that each bullet is actually a standalone paragraph:

Instead of:
- "Use intent data to prioritize accounts"

Write:
"Intent data should be the primary filter for account prioritization in ABM programs. When a target account shows a spike in research activity around your category keywords — tracked via tools like Bombora, G2, or 6sense — they are statistically 3-5x more likely to enter a buying cycle within the next 90 days. According to Maya Patel in Episode 47, teams that act on intent signals within 48 hours of spike detection see a 40% higher win rate versus teams that wait for MQL routing."

That paragraph is citable. The bullet point is not.

**Tactic 4 — Topic Cluster Architecture for AI Authority:**
AI systems weight topical depth. Five interconnected, cross-linked pages on revenue operations are cited more reliably than one comprehensive page.

Build a topic cluster for each of your 3-4 core themes:
- Pillar page: One comprehensive show notes page (2,500+ words) on the core topic (e.g., "The Complete Guide to B2B Revenue Operations in 2026")
- Cluster pages: 8-12 episode show notes that each cover a subtopic and link to the pillar page
- Internal linking: Every cluster page links to the pillar; the pillar links back to top 3-5 clusters

**Tactic 5 — Perplexity-Specific Optimization:**
Perplexity's algorithm weights:
- Recency — update or republish top pages every 90 days (even minor additions count as fresh updates)
- Citation density — pages that themselves cite primary research, studies, and credible data sources get cited more by Perplexity (cite your sources in show notes and Perplexity sees your content as credible)
- Clean answer formatting — Perplexity prefers content that starts with a direct answer to the implied question, then provides supporting evidence — structure every FAQ answer this way

#### Module 4: Guest-Leveraged Audience Growth Engine

Design a systematic approach to growing your B2B podcast audience through guest relationships — turning every episode into a co-marketing event that expands your reach into precisely the right ICP networks.

**STRATEGIC GUEST RECRUITMENT CRITERIA:**

Beyond topic expertise, prioritize guests who score high on these growth-relevant dimensions:

1. **LinkedIn content creator (weight: high)** — Actively posts 3-5x per week with strong engagement from your target ICP. When they share the episode, your reach expands into a qualified, pre-warmed audience
2. **Follower base overlap (weight: high)** — Use LinkedIn Sales Navigator or Shield to assess what percentage of their followers match your target ICP. A guest with 3,000 followers who are all VP-level B2B tech buyers is worth more than a guest with 30,000 general business followers
3. **Podcast-sharing track record (weight: medium)** — Look at their LinkedIn feed for episodes from other shows they've appeared on. Did they share? How many reactions did those posts get? This predicts whether they'll amplify your episode
4. **Reciprocal audience access (weight: medium)** — Can this guest introduce you to others worth interviewing? The best podcast growth happens through guest-to-guest network effects

**PRE-EPISODE GUEST PREPARATION PACKAGE:**

Send every guest a preparation package 5-7 days before recording. This package has two goals: (1) prepare them for a great episode, and (2) make social sharing as frictionless as possible.

Package contents:
- **Episode brief** (1 page): The 3-4 angles you'll explore, the specific frameworks or insights you hope they'll share, what your audience cares most about
- **Social sharing kit**: 3 pre-written LinkedIn post variants (one for 24 hours before recording goes live, one for release day, one for 7 days after), an audiogram clip recommendation (which part of the episode made the best 60-second clip), and a quote graphic template
- **Episode title input request**: "Before we finalize the title, I'd love your input — what 2-3 angles do you think will resonate most with your audience?" (They often suggest title language that performs better with their own followers)
- **Review request**: "If you enjoy how the episode turned out, leaving a review on Apple Podcasts takes about 60 seconds and is the #1 thing that helps us reach more people like you and your network."

**POST-EPISODE AMPLIFICATION SEQUENCE:**

**Release Day:**
- Email guest directly (not via LinkedIn DM) with: episode link, custom thank-you referencing one specific insight they shared, the social sharing kit with direct links ready to copy-paste, and a personal ask: "Would love your take in the comments if you share it — your perspective always drives the best discussions"
- Publish your own LinkedIn post tagging the guest (your post + their reshare creates two separate discovery events for their audience)
- Share to 2-3 LinkedIn groups where your ICP is most active (groups, not your own feed — different distribution)

**Day 2-3:**
- Reply to every comment on both your post and the guest's reshare — each reply extends algorithmic reach by surfacing the post to commenters' networks
- If the guest hasn't shared yet, send a 2-sentence check-in: "Just checking you got the sharing kit — happy to adjust anything if the copy doesn't feel like your voice."

**Day 7-14:**
- Repurpose the episode's best 60-90 second clip as a native LinkedIn video post (LinkedIn native video gets 3-5x more reach than link posts to external content)
- Send the episode to your email newsletter with 3 specific takeaways pulled verbatim from the transcript

**Day 30:**
- Reference the guest's insight in a future episode or LinkedIn post: "As [Guest Name] said in Episode [X]..." — this creates a content web and typically prompts the guest to reshare the callback

**CROSS-PODCAST PARTNERSHIP STRATEGY:**

Identify 3-5 non-competing podcasts that reach your exact ICP. Contact the host directly (not via their show's general email) with a specific, mutual-value guest swap proposal:

Proposal structure:
- Why their audience would value your expertise (be specific — not "we have similar audiences" but "your episodes on RevOps tech stack consistently get 3-4x your normal engagement — that's exactly what my audience of operations leaders wants to hear")
- Propose a 2-episode arc: you appear on their show first, then they appear on yours 2-3 weeks later
- Suggest a shared topic that creates continuity across both shows — this gives both audiences a reason to seek out the cross-episode
- Both shows promote both episodes simultaneously for maximum audience blending

Target cross-promotion partnerships with shows that have 3-8x your current audience (aspirational enough to matter, close enough to say yes).

#### Module 5: Listener-to-Pipeline Conversion & Revenue Attribution

Convert anonymous listeners into identified leads, and track the revenue impact of your podcast investment.

**THE PODCAST CONVERSION FUNNEL:**

Stage 1 → Discovery (Platform organic, Google search, AI search citation, guest social sharing)
Stage 2 → Listen (Podcast player — the ICP consumes your content)
Stage 3 → Subscribe (In-episode CTA prompts platform subscribe; high completion rates trigger algorithm to show more episodes)
Stage 4 → Email Opt-In (Episode-specific lead magnet on show notes page; newsletter CTA)
Stage 5 → Email Nurture (Consistent high-value content builds relationship between downloads)
Stage 6 → Intent Signal (Contact visits pricing page, downloads a case study, clicks a comparison page)
Stage 7 → Sales Conversation (Marketing automation triggers SDR outreach based on Stage 6 signals)

The goal of podcast SEO and discoverability optimization is to maximize Stage 1-2 throughput. This module focuses on Stage 3-7.

**IN-EPISODE CTA ARCHITECTURE:**

CTA placement follows the engagement curve of a podcast episode:

**Minute 3-5 — Awareness Plant (pre-engagement):**
Purpose: Introduce the resource so listeners know it exists, without asking for anything yet
Script template: "Before we dive in — we put together a one-page version of the exact [framework/checklist/template] that [Guest Name] will walk us through today. Zero fluff. If you want it, it's at [simple URL like show.com/resources/ep-47]. Now, let's get into it."

**Minute 15-25 — Value Bridge (peak engagement window):**
Purpose: Connect the episode insight directly to the lead magnet while the listener is most engaged
Script template: "What [Guest Name] just described is exactly the kind of thing I break down in detail in our [name of lead magnet]. If you work in [ICP role] and want the full [number]-step version, grab it at [URL]. It's the same process [Guest Name]'s team uses — we made it into a practical template you can run in your own [CRM/process/team]."

**Final 2 Minutes — Decision Point (highest intent, listener has earned the ask):**
Purpose: Primary subscription + review ask, secondary pipeline CTA
Script template: "Here's the single most useful thing you can do in the next 60 seconds to help us reach more [ICP roles] like yourself: leave a review on Apple Podcasts. I read every one. And if you're ready to [specific business outcome — not generic], go to [show.com/demo] — we'll show you [specific outcome] in a 20-minute conversation. No slides, no sales theater. Just the thing that [Guest Name] and I just spent 40 minutes talking about, applied to your specific situation."

**EPISODE-SPECIFIC LEAD MAGNETS:**

Generic lead magnets ("Subscribe to our newsletter") convert at 0.5-1.5% from show notes pages.
Episode-specific lead magnets ("The exact [framework/checklist/template] used in this episode") convert at 3-8%.

For each episode, create one of:
- A 1-page framework PDF (the named framework from the episode, visualized)
- A Notion template or Google Sheet implementing the episode's system
- An episode worksheet ("Answer these 5 questions to apply [Guest Name]'s framework to your situation")
- A curated resource list ("The 7 tools, studies, and frameworks [Guest Name] referenced in this episode")

Host on a clean, low-friction URL: `yourcompany.com/podcast/ep-[number]-[short-keyword]`
Require only email (no name, no company, no phone) for maximum conversion rate.

**PODCAST REVENUE ATTRIBUTION IN YOUR CRM:**

HubSpot Setup:
- Create Contact Property: "Podcast Listener" (Checkbox) — set to TRUE when a contact submits a show notes form, mentions the podcast in any form field, or is tagged by sales after mentioning the podcast in a conversation
- Create Lead Source value: "Podcast" — track all contacts sourced via podcast UTMs or show notes forms under this source
- Build Smart List: "Podcast Audience" — all contacts where Podcast Listener = TRUE, filtered by ICP fit score
- Build CRM Report: "Podcast Pipeline Influence" — any opportunity where the contact has Podcast Listener = TRUE, regardless of first-touch source (this captures pipeline influenced by podcast even when first touch was paid or SDR)

UTM Parameter Strategy:
- All show notes CTAs: `?utm_source=podcast&utm_medium=shownotes&utm_campaign=ep-[number]`
- All in-episode URLs (redirected short URLs): `?utm_source=podcast&utm_medium=in-episode&utm_campaign=ep-[number]`
- All podcast newsletter sends: `?utm_source=podcast_newsletter&utm_medium=email&utm_campaign=[YYYY-MM-DD]`

Every podcast-sourced contact should be tracked through HubSpot Campaigns as "Podcast Q[X] [Year]" with lifecycle stage progression tracked at: Subscriber → Lead (email captured) → MQL → SQL → Opportunity → Customer.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Show notes pages are indexed in Google but stuck on page 5-10 for target keywords — not moving despite optimization**
*Cause:* The page has enough authority to be indexed but insufficient topical authority or backlinks to outrank established competitors for the target keyword
*Fix:* (1) Build internal link equity first — identify 5-8 other show notes pages on your domain that discuss related topics and add a contextual sentence linking to the underperforming page. This distributes domain authority to the page you want to rank. Internal links from topically relevant pages on your own domain move rankings faster than most external link building tactics. (2) Earn one high-quality external link — reach out to the guest featured in that specific episode and ask them to add a link to the episode show notes from their company blog, Resources page, or LinkedIn "Featured" section. A single link from a credible B2B company website (DA 40+) can move a page from position 35 to position 12 within 30-60 days. (3) Expand the content — Google re-evaluates pages with visible content updates. Add 400-500 words of new analysis (your own perspective on the episode topic, updated data, a new FAQ), update the publish date, and resubmit in Google Search Console. Pages that grow over time rank higher than static pages.

**Problem: Show notes are getting traffic but generating zero email opt-ins — the lead magnet isn't converting**
*Cause:* The lead magnet is generic (not tied to the specific episode), the CTA is buried below the fold, or the form asks for too much information
*Fix:* (1) Move the CTA above the fold — place your primary email capture within the first visible screen of the show notes page, before the Key Takeaways section. Most visitors don't scroll below 60% of the page; if your CTA is at the bottom, 60-70% of visitors never see it. (2) Make the lead magnet hyper-specific — "Download the exact CRM hygiene checklist Maya Patel uses with every RevOps team she advises" converts 5-8x higher than "Subscribe to our newsletter." The more specific and episode-tied the lead magnet, the higher the conversion rate. (3) Reduce form friction to a single field — email address only. Every additional field (name, company, phone) reduces conversion rate by 20-30%. You can enrich contact data later via Clay or Clearbit; capture the email first.

**Problem: Publishing GEO-optimized show notes for 3+ months but getting zero AI search citations**
*Cause:* The content isn't fully indexed, lacks sufficient E-E-A-T signals, or the frameworks aren't specific and structured enough for AI systems to extract and cite
*Fix:* (1) Verify indexing status — run `site:yourdomain.com/podcast` in Google. If fewer than 80% of your episode pages appear, you have an indexing problem. Check for `noindex` tags in your CMS, missing sitemap entries, or crawl budget issues if you have a large site. Submit your top 10 episode URLs manually via Google Search Console → URL Inspection → Request Indexing. (2) Strengthen E-E-A-T explicitly — add the host's credentials to every episode page (years of experience, notable employers, specific achievements with numbers). Add guest bios with verifiable proof points. AI systems assess expertise from on-page content signals; "Marcus Webb, Director of Revenue Operations at Salesloft for 4 years, where he built the RevOps function from 0 to supporting $180M ARR" is citable. "Marcus is a RevOps expert" is not. (3) Restructure frameworks for extraction — your named framework needs a formal definition that AI can lift verbatim: "The [Name]: a [number]-step [process type] for [specific audience] that [specific outcome]. Step 1: [complete sentence]. Step 2: [complete sentence]..." This structure mirrors how AI engines write their own content, which makes it dramatically easier for them to cite yours.
