**SECTION 2 — VIDEO CONTENT ARCHITECTURE & SERIES FRAMEWORK**

Design a complete content architecture that maps video series to the B2B buying journey. For each series format, include:
- Series name and positioning (how you introduce it in every video)
- Target buyer stage: Problem-Aware / Solution-Aware / Vendor-Evaluating / Existing Customer
- Ideal video length by platform (LinkedIn: 45–90 sec / TikTok: 30–60 sec / YouTube Shorts: 45–60 sec)
- Hook formula specific to this series type
- Core content structure
- CTA strategy matched to buyer stage (awareness CTAs vs. pipeline CTAs vs. expansion CTAs)
- Posting frequency recommendation

The five mandatory series for B2B video demand generation:

**Series 1: The "Painful Truth" Series (Problem-Aware Stage)**
Purpose: Stop the scroll by naming a specific, embarrassing pain your ICP lives with daily. This is your highest-reach series — it attracts buyers who don't know your product but recognize the problem.
Hook formula: "[Specific pain point] is [costing/killing/destroying] [specific metric]. Here's why it keeps happening."
Content structure: Name the problem → Explain the hidden root cause they don't know → Hint at the fix → CTA to learn more
CTA type: Soft — "Follow for the fix" or "Link in bio for our free [diagnostic/assessment/guide]"
Measurement: View-to-profile visit rate (target: 8–15%)

**Series 2: The "Framework" Series (Solution-Aware Stage)**
Purpose: Position your product's methodology as the only logical approach. Buyers in this stage are researching HOW to solve the problem — not yet choosing a vendor.
Hook formula: "The [X-step / 3-part / unconventional] framework [companies like yours] use to [achieve outcome] in [timeframe]."
Content structure: Name the framework → Explain each step with a specific tactic → Call out what most companies get wrong → CTA
CTA type: Medium — "Get the full [framework/playbook/checklist] — link in bio"
Measurement: Save rate (target: 3–8% of views — saves are the highest-intent signal on most platforms)

**Series 3: The "Customer Win" Series (Solution-Aware to Vendor-Evaluating Stage)**
Purpose: Show specific, quantifiable outcomes for buyers similar to your viewer. These are your highest-converting pipeline videos.
Hook formula: "[Customer company type] went from [painful before state] to [specific measurable outcome] in [timeframe]. Here's how."
Content structure: Before state (quantified) → Turning point → Solution approach → After state (quantified with specific metrics) → CTA
CTA type: Hard — "We do this for [company type] — demo in bio"
Measurement: CTA click-through rate (target: 1.5–4% of views) and demo requests tagged to video source

**Series 4: The "Myth Bust" Series (Vendor-Evaluating Stage)**
Purpose: Destroy the objections and misconceptions that cause buyers to rule out your category or stall in evaluation. This is your competitive moat-building series.
Hook formula: "[Common belief about your category] is completely wrong — here's what's actually happening."
Content structure: State the myth boldly → Explain why buyers believe it → Reveal the truth with evidence → Explain the implication → CTA
CTA type: Hard — "Still unsure? Let's talk — link in bio" or "Compare us to [competitor] — link in bio"
Measurement: Comment rate (objection-based myths generate high comment volume — use comments as VOC research)

**Series 5: The "In the Weeds" Series (Technical/Champion Enablement Stage)**
Purpose: Arm your internal champions with the technical content they need to sell your solution internally. This series targets the practitioners and technical evaluators who influence the final decision.
Hook formula: "The [specific technical thing] question your [IT/finance/ops] team will ask about [your category] — and the answer."
Content structure: Name the specific technical question → Explain why it matters to the decision → Provide the precise answer → Explain how to use this in internal conversations → CTA
CTA type: Soft — "Send this to your [IT/security/finance] team" (encourages sharing = organic amplification)
Measurement: Share rate (target: 2–5% of views — shares from this series are highly qualified)

**SECTION 3 — VIDEO SCRIPT PRODUCTION SYSTEM**

Design a repeatable script system that any team member can use to produce scroll-stopping B2B videos consistently. Include:

**The B2B Video Script Formula (applies to all series):**

Hook (0–3 seconds — make or break):
- Rule: The hook must trigger an immediate "that's me" or "wait, what?" response
- Hook types ranked by B2B effectiveness:
  1. The Bold Claim: "Most [job title]s are measuring [metric] wrong." (controversy hook)
  2. The Pain Mirror: "If your [monthly/quarterly close] still takes [X days], this is why." (recognition hook)
  3. The Counter-Intuitive: "Buying [your category] before doing this one thing is a waste of money." (curiosity hook)
  4. The Social Proof Hook: "[Fortune 500 company type] just did this to cut [pain point] by 40% — and you can too." (aspiration hook)
  5. The Question Hook: "What's the difference between [company type] hitting [outcome] vs. missing it?" (intrigue hook)

Bridge (seconds 3–10):
- Transition from the hook to the content with a single sentence that makes a credibility claim
- Formula: "I've [worked with / analyzed / seen] [specific number] [company type] companies solve this — here's the pattern."

Core Content (seconds 10–45 for short-form, up to 75 seconds for LinkedIn):
- Maximum 3 points per video — any more and retention collapses
- Each point follows: State it → Prove it with one specific data point or example → Connect to the viewer's reality
- Use pattern interrupts every 12–15 seconds: change camera angle, add text overlay, cut to screen recording, zoom change

CTA (final 5–10 seconds):
- One CTA only — multiple CTAs kill conversion rate
- CTA must match buyer stage (soft CTAs for awareness series, hard CTAs for evaluation series)
- Say the CTA twice: once verbally, once as text overlay
- Never say "like and subscribe" in a B2B demand gen video — it signals you're optimizing for vanity metrics

**SECTION 4 — ALGORITHM OPTIMIZATION & DISTRIBUTION PLAYBOOK**

Platform-specific distribution strategies designed to maximize organic reach to your ICP:

LinkedIn Video Algorithm Playbook (2025):
- Posting frequency: 3–5 videos per week for maximum algorithmic momentum; below 3/week causes algorithm to deprioritize your content in non-follower feeds
- Optimal posting times for B2B decision-makers: Tuesday–Thursday, 7:30–9:00 AM and 12:00–1:30 PM in your audience's primary timezone
- The 90-minute engagement window: LinkedIn gives every post a 90-minute window to accumulate engagement before deciding whether to amplify to non-follower feeds. Tactics: (1) Notify your team to engage immediately after posting, (2) Reply to every comment within 30 minutes, (3) Ask a specific question in the caption that demands a response
- Native video vs. external links: Native LinkedIn video gets 3–5× more organic reach than posts linking to YouTube or Vimeo. Never post external video links as your primary content.
- Caption structure that maximizes dwell time:
  * Line 1: Hook (must work as a standalone teaser — appears before "See more" cutoff)
  * Line 2: Blank line (forces visual break, increases click on "See more")
  * Lines 3–8: Context, framework, or key takeaway in short punchy paragraphs
  * Line 9: Blank line
  * Lines 10–12: 3–5 hashtags (use niche professional hashtags, not broad ones)
  * Line 13: Soft or hard CTA with link or profile direction
- Hashtag strategy: 3–5 hashtags maximum; use vertical-specific hashtags (e.g., #FinanceLeadership, #CFOLife, #FinancialClose) rather than broad hashtags (#B2B, #SaaS) — niche hashtags have higher decision-maker concentration
- Comment seeding: Post the first comment yourself within 5 minutes with an additional insight or question — this creates a comment thread that signals high engagement to the algorithm
- Creator Mode: Enable Creator Mode on all executive and SME profiles used for video — it unlocks analytics, follower growth features, and Newsletter integration that compound video reach
- Cross-content amplification: LinkedIn Newsletters that reference your video content drive additional impressions and follower growth; repurpose top-performing video insights into text carousel posts to reach different segments of your network

TikTok for Business Algorithm Playbook:
- The For You Page (FYP) advantage: TikTok is the only platform where zero followers gets the same algorithmic test as 100,000 followers. Every video starts with distribution to a small test batch (200–500 viewers); if engagement signals (watch completion rate, likes, shares, saves) exceed threshold, it cascades to larger audiences
- The watch completion rate priority: TikTok's #1 ranking signal is watch completion rate (percentage of viewers who watch to the end). For B2B video, targeting 30–45% completion rate is realistic; above 50% triggers viral distribution
- B2B hook optimization for TikTok: TikTok scroll speed is faster than LinkedIn. The first 1.5 seconds must create pattern interrupt. Tactics: open mid-sentence, open with a counterintuitive claim, open with a visual surprise, open with text overlay that teases the payoff
- Posting frequency: 1–2 videos per day for fastest algorithm training; minimum 5 per week for consistent reach
- Optimal times: B2B TikTok audience is active during commute windows (7:00–8:30 AM) and lunch breaks (12:00–1:00 PM) — post 30 minutes before peak to allow algorithm time to begin distribution
- Hashtag strategy: Mix 3 tiers — (1) niche professional hashtags with <500K posts for high relevance signal, (2) mid-tier industry hashtags with 500K–5M posts for reach, (3) one trending hashtag per video if naturally relevant
- Duet and stitch strategy: Duet with or stitch from industry commentary, news, or competitor content — TikTok's algorithm heavily rewards content that references trending audio or other videos in your niche
- Sound strategy: 70% of TikTok videos are watched with sound; use trending audio for awareness videos, but use original spoken audio for content where your message is the product. Never use copyrighted music for business accounts — use TikTok's commercial music library
- Business Center integration: Any video with >500K organic views should be amplified immediately with TikTok Spark Ads (boosting organic posts) to generate leads from an audience already proven to engage with that content

YouTube Shorts Playbook:
- The search-algorithm hybrid: YouTube Shorts traffic comes from both the Shorts feed (algorithm) and YouTube Search. Title optimization for search is more important on Shorts than on LinkedIn or TikTok.
- Title formula for search discovery: "[Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe] — [How/Without/Even If qualifier]" e.g., "Cut Financial Close from 12 to 4 Days — Without Replacing Your ERP"
- Description and pinned comment: Use the first 100 characters of description for the primary keyword phrase. Pin a comment with your bio link and a specific CTA immediately after publishing.
- Thumbnail (cover frame) optimization: Shorts auto-selects a cover frame, but you can manually select it. Choose a frame with bold text overlay and a human face — face thumbnails increase click-through by 30–40% in the Shorts feed
- Subscriber conversion: Unlike TikTok, YouTube Shorts viewers who become subscribers are highly engaged long-term. Include a "subscribe for [specific value proposition]" CTA in 1 of every 3 Shorts to build subscriber base that amplifies future content.
- Integration with long-form: Repurpose 60-second clips from your best-performing webinars and customer case study videos as Shorts — they often outperform purpose-built Shorts because they contain highly-distilled expert insight
- Shorts to Long-form pipeline: Use Shorts as a preview for longer videos: "Full breakdown in my latest video — link in description" to drive long-form views that convert at higher rates than Shorts-only viewers

**SECTION 5 — VIDEO-TO-PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION SYSTEM**

Design a complete pipeline attribution system for short-form video demand. Video attribution is the "dark funnel" problem of social media marketing.

Layer 1 — Direct Attribution (lowest volume, highest confidence):
- Bio link tracking: Use a dedicated landing page URL (not your homepage) for all video CTAs — e.g., yourdomain.com/video-offer. This isolates video-driven traffic.
- UTM parameter structure for video attribution:
  * utm_source = linkedin_video / tiktok_organic / youtube_shorts / instagram_reels
  * utm_medium = social_video
  * utm_campaign = [series name] — e.g., painful_truth / framework / customer_win
  * utm_content = [video topic] — e.g., close_process_myth / roi_calculator
- Link-in-bio tools: Use a dedicated link aggregator (Linktree, Beacons, Later.com) for TikTok and Instagram where you can't embed links in posts. UTM-tag each link individually.
- Platform native analytics: LinkedIn, TikTok Business, and YouTube Studio all provide click-through data from bio/profile links — cross-reference with UTM data to validate attribution accuracy

Layer 2 — Self-Reported Attribution (medium volume, medium confidence):
- Demo request form: Add the question "How did you first discover us?" with options including "LinkedIn video," "TikTok," "YouTube Shorts," "Instagram Reels," "Other social video." This single question typically captures 20–35% of video-influenced pipeline.
- Sales discovery call script addition: Train SDRs and AEs to ask in the first 3 minutes: "Before you reached out today, where had you seen or heard about us?" — many video-influenced buyers mention a specific video they watched.
- Chat qualification: Add the same "where did you first hear about us?" question to your website chatbot qualification flow for demo requests.

Layer 3 — Correlation and Influence Analysis (highest volume, lowest confidence):
- Content→Pipeline correlation model: Track weekly video views by series, then correlate with inbound demo request volume with a 7-day and 14-day lag. Top-performing video series will show a 10–20% lift in demo requests in the week following their peak distribution.
- Cohort analysis: Compare deal velocity and ACV for customers who self-report video as first touch vs. other first-touch channels — video-first buyers often have shorter sales cycles because they've already been educated and qualified themselves.
- "Influenced pipeline" tagging: For any deal where the contact or account has viewed your video content (trackable via LinkedIn Video Analytics for Company Page videos), tag the deal as "Video Influenced" in Salesforce. This builds the multi-touch case for video investment.

Layer 4 — Attribution Reporting for CRO/CFO:
Report three numbers to your revenue leadership:
1. Video-Sourced Pipeline: Opportunities where first recorded touch was a video platform UTM (direct attribution)
2. Video-Influenced Pipeline: Opportunities where video appeared at any point in the buyer journey (self-reported + multi-touch)
3. Video Content ROI: (Video-Sourced ARR + % of Video-Influenced ARR) ÷ Video Production + Distribution Cost

Content Gap Exploitation Strategy:
- If a competitor is dominating "How to" framework content in your category, pivot your framework series to a more specific, differentiated angle
- If competitors are not making customer proof videos, your Customer Win series has a clear white space advantage
- If competitors are making professional, corporate-style videos — consider a more authentic, founder-forward approach that TikTok and LinkedIn algorithm increasingly rewards over polished production

Competitor Positioning Counter-Narrative:
- For any video topic where a competitor gains significant traction: within 5 business days, produce a response video using the Myth Bust framework — agree with the problem they identify, but position your solution as the superior approach
- Never name competitors directly in organic B2B video — it gives them a reach benefit via comment engagement and creates brand safety risk

**SECTION 7 — 90-DAY VIDEO PIPELINE SPRINT**

A sequenced, week-by-week execution plan. Each item includes specific deliverable, owner role, and success metric.

**Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–2)**

Week 1:
- Deliverable: Platform audit — set up LinkedIn Creator Mode, TikTok Business Account, YouTube channel optimization. Deliverable: all profiles fully optimized with consistent bio CTAs and UTM-tracked bio links.
- Owner: Demand Gen Manager + Marketing Ops
- Success metric: All profiles live and UTM tracking verified in HubSpot/Salesforce

Week 1–2:
- Deliverable: ICP interview sprint — conduct 5 "video research" interviews with current customers asking: "What's the YouTube/TikTok/LinkedIn video content you wish existed about [category]?" and "What would make you watch a 60-second video about [pain point] all the way through?"
- Owner: PMM or Content Strategist
- Success metric: 5 interviews completed; top 10 video topic hooks validated by ICP language

**Phase 2: Production Sprint (Weeks 3–6)**

Week 3:
- Deliverable: Film 10 launch videos (the templates from Section 3). Batch film in a single day — 10 videos × 3–5 minutes filming each = one focused shoot day.
- Owner: Video Producer + Subject Matter Expert (exec or SME on camera)
- Success metric: 10 videos filmed, edited, captioned, and ready for upload

Week 4:
- Deliverable: Launch — begin posting 4 videos per week on LinkedIn + 5 videos per week on TikTok (or the 2 priority platforms selected in Section 1). Do not launch all 10 at once — stagger for algorithm training.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: We've posted 20 videos over 6 weeks with decent views but zero demo requests traced back to video.**
Solution: This is a CTA-to-landing page conversion problem, not a reach problem. Diagnose in this order: (1) Is your bio link pointing to a specific, video-relevant landing page or just your homepage? Video audiences who land on a generic homepage convert at 0.5–1%; a dedicated landing page with a single offer converts at 5–12%. (2) Is your CTA in the video actually asking for the demo, or is it a soft "follow for more" CTA? Awareness series videos should use soft CTAs, but at least 30% of your videos (Customer Win and Myth Bust series) should have a direct demo request CTA. (3) Are you tracking UTMs correctly? Verify in HubSpot or GA4 that the UTM parameters are being captured on form submissions — broken tracking is often mistaken for zero conversion.

**Problem: Our LinkedIn videos get 200–400 views from our own network but never break out to non-followers.**
Solution: You're stuck in the "echo chamber" — LinkedIn's algorithm is only showing your content to people who already follow you. The fix has three parts: (1) Check your engagement velocity in the first 90 minutes post-publish — you need a minimum of 15–25 meaningful engagements (comments are weighted 8× over likes) within the first 90 minutes for LinkedIn to test non-follower distribution. Coordinate with 5–10 internal team members to comment a genuine reaction immediately after each post. (2) Optimize your hook — if your hook doesn't trigger a "that's me" reaction from a decision-maker seeing it in a non-follower feed, they'll scroll past and your completion rate tanks. Test 3 different hooks for the same core content over 3 weeks. (3) Enable Creator Mode on the profile posting the videos and post consistently (minimum 4×/week) for 8 weeks before evaluating — LinkedIn's algorithm requires pattern recognition to trust an account with non-follower distribution.

**Problem: Our CEO loves the video strategy but our CFO is killing the budget because we can't prove pipeline ROI after 90 days.**
Solution: 90 days is too early to prove closed-revenue ROI for video in a 60–90 day sales cycle — but it's enough time to prove video-influenced pipeline value. Shift the conversation to leading indicators: (1) Present the number of video-attributed contacts in your CRM — these are real pipeline records where video was the first recorded touch. Calculate the pipeline value at your average ACV. (2) Pull your existing open opportunities and identify how many had contact with your video content before or during the evaluation (trackable via LinkedIn Video Analytics for company page followers, plus self-reported attribution). Present this as "influenced pipeline." (3) Show cost-per-video-attributed-contact vs. cost-per-MQL from paid search and events — video almost always wins on efficiency. The CFO argument is: "Our cost-per-influenced-opportunity from video is $X vs. $Y from Google Ads. We need 6 more months of data to show closed revenue, but the leading indicator ROI is already stronger than our paid channels."
