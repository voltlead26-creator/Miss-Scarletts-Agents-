## Overview

Diagnoses the operational health of your marketing function by analyzing campaign cycle times, execution bottlenecks, resource-to-output ratios, and automation coverage gaps — then generates a prioritized efficiency roadmap that reduces time-to-launch, increases campaign throughput, and surfaces the process improvements that most directly accelerate pipeline velocity without adding headcount.

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:**
Build a Campaign Velocity custom report: create date properties `campaign_brief_approved_date` and `campaign_live_date` on the Campaign object, then a calculated property for cycle time in days. Filter by campaign type to compare email vs. paid vs. content velocity trends. Set up a workflow alert: if campaign age exceeds channel benchmark by >3 days, notify CMO and campaign owner via Slack.

**Salesforce:**
Build a Lead Response Time report: Lead Created Date vs. First Activity Date, filtered by Lead Source contains "Marketing," grouped by week. Shows SLA compliance trend over time. Add a dashboard tile showing % of MQLs where first activity was >4 hours after creation — this is your SLA violation rate tied directly to pipeline risk.

**Asana / Monday.com:**
Template every campaign type as a project template with a mandatory pre-work checklist (brief fields, audience confirmed, approver named, launch date confirmed) that gates the "assign to creative" task. Use portfolio view to give CMO live visibility into all in-flight campaigns and their stage. Add custom fields for planned launch date and channel — enables weekly velocity reporting without manual tracking.

**Zapier / Make:**
Automate UTM parameter generation: trigger on new Asana campaign task creation → extract campaign name, channel, and source from task fields → build UTM string → post to Asana task comment. Eliminates manual UTM construction and the ~12% error rate from copy-paste mistakes. Separately, automate the weekly MOps digest: scheduled trigger every Monday 8am → query HubSpot API for campaign metrics from prior week → format as Slack message → post to #marketing-ops.

**Notion:**
Create a Marketing Ops wiki with: approved brief template, SLA policy document, revision round rules ("if feedback from more than one person, consolidate before sending to designer"), tool access guides, UTM taxonomy, and campaign naming convention. Link directly from every Asana campaign template so team members always have the standard within reach.

## Troubleshooting

**"We have no historical data on campaign cycle times"**
Start a two-week measurement sprint before running this prompt: ask team to log brief-approval date and go-live date for every campaign in a shared Google Sheet. After 14 days you will have enough data to run a meaningful audit. For permanent tracking, add two date fields to your campaign management tool — `brief_approved` and `launched` — and make them required fields on every campaign project. This takes 20 minutes to configure and will save hundreds of hours of future estimation.

**"Leadership does not see ops efficiency as a strategic priority — they just want more pipeline"**
Reframe entirely in revenue terms using Module 1's pipeline delay calculation. "Our current campaign velocity gap is costing us an estimated $195,000 in delayed pipeline per month. Fixing our brief-to-launch process is a pipeline acceleration initiative. It does not require new budget or headcount — it requires 90 days of process improvement." Make the dollar number specific to your company using your own ACV and campaign volume. Finance and sales leadership will respond immediately when they see the revenue framing.

**"The bottlenecks are mostly in other teams — legal, exec reviews, or IT — not in marketing"**
Document this explicitly in the audit output. Show the revenue cost of each external dependency's delay, then bring the data to a cross-functional alignment meeting. Request formal SLA agreements with legal (48-hour turnaround for standard campaigns, same-day for time-sensitive launches), IT (defined UAT window), and executives (designated backup approver when primary is unavailable). Most dependencies cooperate readily once they understand that a one-week delay on a campaign approval translates to a six-figure pipeline impact.
