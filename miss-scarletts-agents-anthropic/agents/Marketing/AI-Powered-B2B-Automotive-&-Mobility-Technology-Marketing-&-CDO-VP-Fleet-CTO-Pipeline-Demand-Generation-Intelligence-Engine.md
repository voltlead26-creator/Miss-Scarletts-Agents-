## Troubleshooting

**Problem: VP Fleet champions are enthusiastic but deals stall at CFO business case stage — 60% of pilots don't advance to commercial discussion within 90 days**
Solution: This is a business case enablement failure — your champion understands the operational value but cannot translate fleet uptime and charging cost improvements into CFO-credible financial language. Immediately build and deploy three CFO-specific assets: (1) Auto-generated "Fleet Electrification Investment Thesis" from pilot data — inputs are your actual pilot metrics; outputs are 5-year NPV, payback period, IRR, and IRA credit impact in format CFOs expect to see; (2) "Fleet EV vs. ICE TCO Model" template pre-populated with your pilot numbers, industry benchmark fuel cost escalation assumptions (DOE AFDC data), and EV maintenance savings (NREL fleet data) — give this to champions to present internally, not just for your sales calls; (3) Peer CFO reference — identify 2–3 closed-won fleet customers where CFO approved the investment and will speak to another CFO. One 15-minute CFO-to-CFO reference call is worth a 50-page business case. Equip your champion to request this reference proactively when their CFO review is scheduled.

**Problem: Fleet companies in non-CARB states have low urgency — "we're watching electrification but there's no deadline forcing action" objection is stalling outbound response rates**
Solution: Shift the urgency trigger from regulatory compliance to financial and competitive pressure: (1) Diesel price volatility — create a "5-Year Fleet Fuel Cost Sensitivity Model" showing ICE fleet cost under +15%, +25%, and +40% diesel price scenarios vs. EV fleet with fixed electricity contract; fleet CFOs in non-CARB states respond strongly to fuel cost certainty; (2) Insurance risk — cite NHTSA and fleet insurance industry data showing that fleets with AI-powered safety monitoring (driver behavior, vehicle health) qualify for 15–25% fleet insurance premium reductions; (3) Talent retention — the "EV premium" in driver recruiting: CDL driver surveys show 34% of drivers prefer EV fleets; in the current CDL driver shortage, fleet electrification is a talent retention differentiator your champion can use in workforce conversations; (4) IRA credit window — position current IRA credits as a time-sensitive opportunity with legislative uncertainty: "The fleet EV credits that fund your transition ROI may not exist in 24 months — the operators capturing them now are building a structural cost advantage that non-EV fleets can't replicate later."

**Problem: OEM sales cycles are 300+ days and pipeline from automotive OEM accounts isn't converting — large deals stall in procurement and legal review for months**
Solution: OEM procurement requires a fundamentally different approach than fleet operator sales: (1) Build an OEM-specific "design partner" program — identify 2–3 target OEM digital units and propose a co-development or first-mover pilot relationship with exclusive data collaboration, reduced fees, and joint case study rights. OEM procurement accelerates dramatically when framed as a strategic partnership rather than a vendor transaction; (2) Navigate around procurement by building executive relationships at the CTO/CDO level 12–18 months before formal procurement begins — co-speaking at SAE World Congress or CES Mobility, sharing original connected vehicle research, building a personal relationship that turns into executive sponsorship when your product enters formal evaluation; (3) Secure a reference OEM before pursuing other OEMs — one signed OEM customer with a public case study is worth 18 months of OEM outbound prospecting; the automotive industry is a closed peer network where OEM CDOs check each other's references before evaluating any new technology vendor.
