COMPETITIVE CONVERSATION FRAMEWORK (for reps)

Principle: Never position as "we're better than X." Always position as "here's the problem you're trying to solve and why our approach fits."

Opening moves:
1. Validate the competitor's strengths (1 sentence — builds credibility)
2. Introduce an unstated problem ("One thing most buyers in your situation also ask about is...")
3. Offer a proof point before they ask

The STEP Framework for competitive deals:
S — Surface the unstated need (use landmine questions)
T — Tie it to business impact (quantify the cost of the gap)
E — Evidence: deploy proof point, win story, or analyst quote
P — Propose a criteria shift ("Given what you just shared, would it make sense to also evaluate...")

## Integration Tips

- **Gong/Chorus**: Set up keyword alerts for all Tier-1 competitor names + "switching from" / "compared to" / "evaluation" — route to competitive Slack channel with deal context
- **Highspot/Seismic**: Use the tagging taxonomy above to create a competitive intelligence collection; enable auto-surfacing rules by opportunity competitor field
- **HubSpot/Salesforce**: Create a "Competitor" field on opportunity object; build reports on win rate by competitor; trigger content delivery automations via Workflow/Flow
- **Crayon or Klue**: Feed monitoring outputs directly into the battle card update workflow; use their Salesforce integration to push battle cards to rep sidebar
- **Notion or Confluence**: Use as the "source of truth" wiki for PMM; Highspot/Seismic is the sales-facing delivery layer — keep them in sync via Zapier or native integration
- **Slack**: Create #competitive-intel channel; set up automated posts from Gong, Crayon, and G2 review alerts; use as the rapid-response layer for in-deal questions

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Reps don't use the competitive content even when it's available.
**Solution:** Surface content *inside* the tools reps live in (CRM sidebar, Slack, email) rather than requiring them to open Highspot separately. The content has to come to them at the moment of need. Run a 30-minute "competitive confidence" session at each SKO and QBR — reps use content they've rehearsed with.

**Problem:** Battle cards go stale within weeks of publishing because competitors move fast.
**Solution:** Assign a "battle card owner" per competitor (usually a PMM or senior SE who covers that competitor most). Set a 30-day auto-reminder to review each card. Build the monitoring system first so updates are triggered by events, not scheduled reviews — event-driven freshness beats calendar-driven freshness.

**Problem:** The competitive content is too defensive and makes reps sound insecure when they use it.
**Solution:** Rewrite all battle cards using the STEP framework (Surface → Tie to business impact → Evidence → Propose criteria shift). Lead with the buyer's problem, not the competitor's weakness. Run role-plays using recorded Gong calls where your best reps handled competitive objections well — that's the voice to standardize, not a PMM's ideal script.
