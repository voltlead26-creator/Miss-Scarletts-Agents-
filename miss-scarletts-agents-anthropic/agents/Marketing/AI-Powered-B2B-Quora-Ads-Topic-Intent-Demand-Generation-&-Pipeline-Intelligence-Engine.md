## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Very low impressions despite setting up campaigns correctly**
Solution: Quora has limited B2B inventory compared to LinkedIn or Google. Start by targeting broader topic categories (not just specific questions) to build scale. Use "expanded targeting" in Quora's campaign settings to let the algorithm find related questions. Increase bids 20-30% above the suggested range initially to establish delivery, then optimize down once the algorithm learns. Budget minimums of $50-100/day per campaign are needed for meaningful learning periods.

**Problem: High CTR but very low conversion rate on landing pages (<0.5%)**
Solution: Quora traffic is in research mode, not buying mode — they need more education before converting. Align landing page messaging to the exact question they clicked from (use dynamic landing pages with URL parameters, or create dedicated pages per question cluster). Replace hard "book a demo" CTAs for MOFU question traffic with softer offers: "Download the guide," "Take the 2-minute assessment," or "Get the benchmark report." Reserve demo CTAs exclusively for BOFU and retargeting campaigns. Test adding a Quora-specific trust signal ("As seen on Quora by 10,000+ sales leaders") to the landing page.

**Problem: Promoted Answers getting downvoted or poor organic performance**
Solution: Promoted Answers that feel promotional get downvoted, which increases costs and reduces reach. The answer must lead with 80% genuine education and 20% product mention — violation of this ratio is the #1 cause of poor performance. Use a real employee or founder name (not a brand account) as the answerer to build credibility. The first 2-3 sentences must directly answer the question with a specific, actionable insight. Product mention should come in the final paragraph as a natural extension of the advice, not a pivot. Review Quora's content policies for Promoted Answers and ensure zero policy violations (no comparative claims without citation, no ROI guarantees).
