## Troubleshooting

**Problem**: The output is too generic because I don't have deep intelligence on the specific competitor's angle for this prospect.
**Solution**: Add the competitor's most recent LinkedIn posts, their homepage headline, and 2-3 G2 reviews from customers in the same industry as your prospect to the prompt. The AI will synthesize these into a much more specific pitch prediction. If you have Gong access, also paste the section of the call transcript where the competitor was first mentioned.

**Problem**: The AE says the 14-day sequence is too aggressive for this relationship and they want to "let it breathe."
**Solution**: Include the deal's relationship temperature in your input ("champion is highly engaged / lukewarm / going dark") and the prompt will calibrate intensity accordingly. Also, add: "AE relationship context: The champion is cautious and prefers to feel like he's making his own decision. Adjust all touchpoints to be pull-oriented, not push-oriented."

**Problem**: The prompt generates great messaging but the AE still defaults to the old generic battlecard.
**Solution**: This is a change management problem, not a prompt problem. The fix is: (1) integrate the output directly into CRM as a task/field so the AE doesn't have to go find it, (2) have frontline managers reference the competitive brief in deal review calls, and (3) measure and publicly celebrate the first deal where an AE used the brief and won — making it a proof point in your own sales motion.
