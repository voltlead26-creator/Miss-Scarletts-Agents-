## Integration Tips

**Salesforce / HubSpot CRM**
- Create a custom "Buying Committee" object or use Contact Roles on the Opportunity
- Map each stakeholder profile to a Contact record with custom fields: Role (Champion/EB/Technical), Engagement Score, Last Touch, Champion Strength (1-5)
- Set up automated tasks/sequences in Outreach.io or Salesloft per stakeholder type
- Pipeline inspection dashboard: filter deals with fewer than 3 active contacts as "at risk"

**Apollo.io / Clay / ZoomInfo**
- Use the stakeholder role list from the map to build targeted contact lists per account
- Clay workflows: trigger committee mapping prompt when an account hits ICP score threshold
- Enrich contact records with LinkedIn activity, job tenure, and tech stack data before personalizing outreach

**Gong / Chorus (Conversation Intelligence)**
- Tag calls by stakeholder role to analyze which personas engage vs. go silent
- Use Gong's "deal at risk" alerts to trigger re-mapping when a key contact goes dark
- Track "MEDDPICC talk ratio" — are reps asking qualifying questions or pitching?

**Notion / Confluence (Deal Rooms)**
- Create a digital deal room per account using the committee map as the foundation
- Share champion-specific asset packages via shared Notion page (they can forward to their stakeholders)
- Use version history to track how committee composition evolves over the sales cycle

**Zapier / Make Automation**
- Trigger: New account enters "Evaluation" stage in CRM → Auto-run buying committee mapping prompt → Push output to deal room template
- Trigger: Contact at target account changes jobs (LinkedIn alert) → Re-run committee map → Flag to AE

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The output stakeholder map doesn't match the actual org structure I find when researching.
**Solution:** Run the prompt a second time with corrections — provide the actual titles and names you've discovered via LinkedIn research, 10-K filings, or G2 reviews. The prompt works best when seeded with real intelligence. Add a "Known Contacts" section to your input to anchor the AI's assumptions.

**Problem:** Outreach sequences feel generic even after customization.
**Solution:** The quality of personalization is directly proportional to the trigger events and company context you provide. Add recent signals: "They just hired a new VP of Operations," "They announced a partnership with FedEx," or "Their CEO posted about supply chain volatility last week." One real signal makes the difference between a deleted email and a reply.

**Problem:** Champion is enthusiastic but can't get us access to the Economic Buyer.
**Solution:** Your champion may be weak (low political capital) or unwilling (protecting territory). Use the MEDDPICC Champion score. If below 3, activate an air cover play: have your CEO send a 3-sentence LinkedIn message to the CFO, or run a targeted LinkedIn ad campaign to the Economic Buyer's title at that company using their company domain. Meanwhile, rerun the champion acceleration program focused specifically on Week 3 (stakeholder expansion).
