## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Exit intent overlay fires too aggressively — visitors report feeling "chased," bounce rate increases on affected pages.**
Fix: Audit your trigger conditions for false positives. Add session-level frequency caps (maximum 1 overlay per visitor per 7 days). Add page-time minimums — require ≥ 30 seconds on page before enabling exit intent detection. Consider replacing mouse-leave triggers with scroll-up-after-scroll-down (more reliable exit intent signal) or tab-blur + 120-second idle. A/B test "passive" micro-conversion bar (sticky bottom bar) vs. overlay — bar generates 40–60% of overlay volume with zero intrusion complaints.

**Problem: Recovery email sequence has 8% open rate but 0.3% reply/click rate — emails are being opened but not acting.**
Fix: Your subject lines are winning attention but your body copy isn't creating urgency or specificity. Audit Email 1 for: (a) Does the opening line reference specific behavior from their session? ("You looked at our pricing for 3 minutes" performs 4× better than "We noticed you visited our site.") (b) Is your CTA a single, specific ask or multiple options? Every additional option in a recovery email reduces CTR by 12–18%. (c) Is your offer right for the segment? Pricing page exits want math, not more reading — lead with the ROI number, not a case study.

**Problem: Account-identified overlay is personalizing for the wrong company — showing "[Competitor Name]" content to an existing customer.**
Fix: Your IP-to-account matching is generating false positives, which is common when large enterprises share IP ranges. Add three suppression layers: (1) HubSpot cookie check for known contacts before firing personalization, (2) Salesforce account status lookup (Customer/Active Opportunity = suppress), (3) Minimum confidence threshold of 85%+ from your intent provider before activating named-account content. Accept that you will miss 10–15% of true target accounts in exchange for zero wrong-company personalization embarrassments.
