## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Proof requests to customers go unanswered — review velocity is stagnant**
Root cause: Generic outreach at wrong timing, competing asks from CS and Sales teams, no clear "what's in it for me" for the customer.
Fix: Redesign review trigger timing to hit NPS Promoters within 24 hours (highest response window). Make the ask specific and low-effort: "It would take you 3 minutes on G2 and directly helps teams like yours find the right solution." Unify all proof requests through a single CS workflow (not Sales and Marketing separately). Test a charitable donation incentive ($10 to a charity of their choice for a completed review).

**Problem: Social proof on website feels generic — buyers don't see themselves in it**
Root cause: Proof assets cover only 1–2 verticals or company sizes, creating an "enterprise only" or "SaaS only" perception.
Fix: Conduct a proof coverage audit (Deliverable 1 matrix). Prioritize collecting proof from the vertical and company size segment with the largest pipeline gap. In the interim, use G2 review filtering — pull industry-tagged reviews from G2 API and display them dynamically based on visitor firmographics to bridge the proof gap before new case studies are produced.

**Problem: A/B tests on proof elements show no statistically significant lift**
Root cause: Most proof CRO tests fail due to underpowering (insufficient traffic), testing too many variables simultaneously, or testing proof format when the real barrier is something else (price, ICP fit, product gap).
Fix: Run proof tests only on pages with ≥3,000 monthly unique visitors. Test one variable at a time (format vs. format, not format + placement + copy). Before running proof tests, validate that proof is actually a top objection in lost deal analysis (Gong call review + win/loss data). If price is the #1 objection, proof optimization will have minimal impact — solve the upstream barrier first.
