## Overview

This prompt deploys an AI agent to design and execute a complete SMB-to-Enterprise upmarket GTM expansion—diagnosing your enterprise readiness, transforming your ICP and positioning, redesigning your packaging and sales motion, building a proof architecture that satisfies enterprise procurement, and producing a phased activation roadmap. Use it when you're an SMB-dominant SaaS company targeting $50K+ ACVs for the first time, when enterprise pilots keep stalling in legal/security review, or when your win rate against enterprise-native competitors is below 20%.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Enterprise deals stall in security review for 6-8 weeks and then die.**
Solution: Build a proactive security review acceleration program. Create a dedicated security review package (SOC 2 Type II report, pentest executive summary, DPA, subprocessor list, data flow diagram) and send it automatically 24 hours after the demo, before the prospect asks. Target a 5-business-day SLA for completing vendor questionnaires. Assign a security review coordinator role (can be a CSM or RevOps person in early stage) to own all incoming security questionnaires. Consider building a Vanta or Drata shared portal so prospects can self-serve the documentation.

**Problem: We're winning the technical evaluation but losing at the Economic Buyer level — deals are stalling or being de-prioritized.**
Solution: Your champion is not adequately enabled to sell upward. Build a champion enablement kit that includes: (1) an internal business case template pre-populated with your ROI data, (2) a financial impact calculator the champion can run for their specific situation, (3) an executive briefing deck (10 slides max) the champion can share with their CFO/CPO, and (4) a list of peer companies the CFO can reference. Coach champions with the line: "The best way I can help you is to help you sell this internally. Here's exactly what your CFO will ask and here are the answers." If the champion can't get Economic Buyer access in 3 weeks, it's a disqualification signal.

**Problem: Enterprise win rate is below 20% and we can't tell why we're losing.**
Solution: Implement a structured win/loss program immediately. After every enterprise deal conclusion (win or loss), conduct a 30-minute debrief interview with the champion (for losses: "What would have had to be true for us to win?"). Use Clozd or Gong's win/loss module, or run interviews manually. Track loss reasons in CRM with standardized categories: product gaps, security/compliance, pricing, competitive loss, internal priority change, champion left, no budget. After 10-15 losses, patterns will emerge. The top 3 loss reasons should drive your next 90-day product and GTM prioritization.
