## Overview
Analyzes your customer base, market data, and competitive landscape to design distinct GTM motions for each customer segment — with differentiated marketing, sales coverage, pipeline targets, and investment allocation per segment. Use it when you're scaling past $10M ARR and a single GTM approach no longer serves all of your buyers, when you're debating whether to move upmarket or downmarket, or when your CAC efficiency varies dramatically by customer type.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: I don't have clean CRM data to calculate LTV:CAC per segment.**
*Fix:* Use the Quick Copy-Paste version with estimated ranges for each segment. Even rough estimates ("SMB ACV is around $5K, churn is probably 25–30%") will produce directionally correct GTM recommendations. Start by manually tagging your most recent 100 closed-won accounts with segment labels in your CRM and calculating exact economics from that sample — then extrapolate to the full customer base. The strategic segmentation decisions cannot wait for perfect data; imperfect data with clear assumptions beats paralysis.

**Problem: My segments overlap and I cannot define clean boundaries — for example, a 200-person company sometimes acts like mid-market and sometimes like enterprise.**
*Fix:* Supplement firmographic criteria (headcount, revenue) with behavioral criteria. Segment by buying committee size (1–2 people = sales-assisted motion, 5+ people = enterprise motion), formal procurement involvement, implementation complexity (simple self-install = SMB/mid-market, multi-month implementation = enterprise), and budget authority (credit card purchase = SMB, legal/finance review = enterprise). A 200-person company that triggers a formal RFP with procurement involvement should be worked as an enterprise motion regardless of headcount.

**Problem: Leadership resists reducing SMB investment because "we need the logo count and market share."**
*Fix:* Reframe the conversation from "exiting SMB" to "transforming the SMB motion." Show the self-serve PLG scenario where SMB customers continue to generate revenue and provide market presence through automated, product-led channels — just with a fundamentally restructured CAC. Build the model showing SMB revenue can actually grow while removing expensive human sales coverage if in-product onboarding, freemium conversion, and community-led acquisition are executed well. Reference PLG success stories from Atlassian, Notion, and Calendly, which built massive SMB presence at scale without direct sales teams.
