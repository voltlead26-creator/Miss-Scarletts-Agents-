# MARKET ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

## 1. CAGE Distance Assessment
Analyze the expansion market across four dimensions vs. home market:
- **Cultural Distance**: Communication style (direct/indirect), risk tolerance, relationship vs. transactional buying, consensus vs. champion-driven decisions, hierarchy in the buying committee
- **Administrative Distance**: Regulatory environment (GDPR, data sovereignty, sector regulations), government procurement rules, tax structure for SaaS, local data residency requirements
- **Geographic Distance**: Time zone overlap with home market CS team, logistics for in-person events and field sales, language coverage needed
- **Economic Distance**: Average deal size delta vs. home market (typically 10-30% lower in EMEA/APAC vs. US), procurement cycle differences, fiscal year timing, currency risk

## 2. LOCALIZED ICP PROFILE
Build a country-specific ICP that differs from the home market profile:
- Job titles (exact local equivalents — "VP Sales" in US is often "Sales Director" or "Commercial Director" in UK/DACH)
- Company size and revenue thresholds that map to similar buying power
- Tech stack differences (e.g., EMEA prefers SAP, not Salesforce; Pipedrive dominates SMB DACH)
- Buying committee composition (procurement involvement, legal review, IT security sign-off differences)
- Budget ownership and approval thresholds
- Preferred evaluation process (POC vs. pilot vs. reference-first)
- Industry verticals that index higher in this market

## 3. MESSAGING TRANSCREATION

- **Keep**: Core value proposition elements that resonate universally

- **Tone Shift**: e.g., UK = understate, Germany = data/precision-first, France = vision/philosophy, Japan = consensus/trust-first
- **Local proof points needed**: Which customer stories, analyst reports (local Gartner/Forrester equivalents), and G2/Capterra categories matter in this market

## 4. CHANNEL & PARTNER STRATEGY

- **Direct sales**: When to hire in-country vs. remote coverage from home market
- **Channel/reseller**: Key system integrators or VARs in this market, evaluation criteria, typical channel economics
- **Technology partnerships**: Local ecosystem partners (e.g., DACH expansion = SAP partner ecosystem, UK = Salesforce/HubSpot SI network)
- **Events & community**: Top 3 in-person events in the target market, local analyst firms, local industry associations
- **Inbound localization**: Country-specific SEO terms, local search intent vs. US equivalents, local social platforms (e.g., XING vs. LinkedIn in Germany)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Outbound open rates and reply rates are 60-70% lower in new market than home market**
Solution: This is almost always a messaging and tone problem, not a targeting problem. Run a native-speaker audit of your outbound sequences — have a local marketing professional (not just a translator) rewrite the sequences in market-appropriate B2B tone. In DACH, use formal Sie address; in UK, be more understated; in APAC, open with relationship context before the pitch. Also check: are you sending from a local domain? A US email domain has 15-20% lower open rates in EU markets.

**Problem: Pipeline is building but deals are stalling in legal/procurement review**
Solution: This is not a sales problem — it's a product and legal readiness problem. Deals stalling in legal review in EU markets 80% of the time come down to: (1) GDPR/DPA not ready, (2) data residency not available, (3) security questionnaire taking weeks to complete. Build a "Security & Compliance Fast Track" package: pre-filled security questionnaire, ready-to-sign DPA, data processing map, and subprocessor list. Deals that have this package close 45 days faster.

**Problem: Win rate against local competitors is below 40%**
Solution: You are likely losing on relationships and references, not product. Local incumbents have existing trust that you don't. Counter-strategy: (1) build a local advisory board of 3-5 credible in-market executives who can be references, (2) get 1-2 in-market case studies published ASAP even if you have to offer significant discounts to secure them, (3) co-sell with a local SI or partner who already has the relationships — channel your first 5-10 deals through a trusted local partner rather than going direct.
