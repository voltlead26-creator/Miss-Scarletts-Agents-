## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Existing customers resist paying more for AI features they see as "should be included."
**Solution:** Anchor the value discussion on the specific output, not the feature. Run a 15-minute live session showing what AI produces vs. manual work. Most resistance dissolves when customers see 90-second report generation vs. their current 4-hour process. If resistance persists, offer a 60-day AI trial included in their current plan — 80% of trialists convert when they experience the value firsthand.

**Problem:** Sales team defaults to discounting AI tier in deals rather than selling value.
**Solution:** The ROI calculator is your primary fix. Build it into the sales demo flow so reps run the math with every prospect. Also audit your compensation structure — if reps are measured on closed deals not plan mix, they'll default to whatever closes fastest. Add an AI attach rate metric to quota and compensate accordingly.

**Problem:** AI feature adoption is low among customers who upgraded — risk of churn at renewal.
**Solution:** Implement an AI activation sequence within 48 hours of upgrade: automated onboarding email with the single most impactful use case, CSM-led "first value" call in week 1, power-user Slack community for AI feature discussions. Track "AI active" status (defined as using AI feature at least once in past 14 days) and trigger proactive outreach for customers who go dark. Low adoption is a pre-churn signal — address it 90+ days before renewal.
