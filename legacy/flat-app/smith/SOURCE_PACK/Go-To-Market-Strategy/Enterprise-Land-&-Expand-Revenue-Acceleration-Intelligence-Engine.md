## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Expansion conversations stall after initial success — champion says "now is not the right time"**
Fix: This usually means you haven't multi-threaded. Your champion may not have budget authority or organizational influence for expansion. Diagnose: Has the AE met the economic buyer directly? Use the EBR as a forcing function to get economic buyer in the room. If champion blocks access, ask directly: "When we expand to [adjacent team], who else needs to be involved in that decision? I want to make sure we're setting up a smooth process." Map the hidden buying committee and go around the roadblock tactfully.

**Problem: NRR is healthy but expansion cycles take 9-12 months instead of 3-6 months**
Fix: The POC success criteria contract is missing or too vague — when you land, you're not pre-selling the expansion vision. At signing, add a line: "90-day review: if we hit [3 specific metrics], we'll evaluate expanding to [adjacent team] in Q[X]." This converts a vague "maybe someday" into a contractual milestone review. Also audit whether your pricing structure creates natural expansion friction — usage-based pricing expands organically vs. seat-based requiring a new negotiation.

**Problem: Champions get promoted and become advocates but don't drive expansion — they go quiet**
Fix: Champion promotion is a high-value signal that often gets wasted. When your champion gets promoted, they're immediately in a "prove myself" mode — your job is to make them look good to their new stakeholders. Send a "congratulations" email within 24 hours with a 1-page impact summary they can share upward ("Here's what your team achieved with Rampfast under your leadership — useful context for your new role"). This repositions the relationship from "customer I supported" to "strategic asset that shows my business impact." Then propose: "In your new role, you probably have visibility into [adjacent team's challenge]. Want to share what we've been working on?"
