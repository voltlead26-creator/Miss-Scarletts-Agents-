## Overview
Systematically audits your product launch readiness across every go-to-market dimension — sales enablement, messaging clarity, competitive timing, market education, and operational execution — to identify gaps that could derail your launch, score risk severity, and generate a prioritized mitigation plan before you go live. Use this 2–3 weeks before a major product or feature launch when you need a go/no-go decision framework backed by data, not gut feel.

- Launch scope: [is this a major platform launch, new product line, feature expansion, pricing change, or market expansion?]
- Existing competition in this space: [list 2-4 competitors who already offer similar functionality]
- Key differentiators: [what makes this fundamentally better or different]
- Launch team: [list teams involved — product, marketing, sales, CS, legal, engineering]
- Current status: [what's done, what's in progress, what hasn't started]

Assess my launch readiness across these 8 dimensions and score each 1–10 (10 = fully ready, 1 = major blocker):

1. **Messaging & Positioning**: Is the value proposition clear, differentiated, and validated with buyers? Do we have a messaging hierarchy that works across all channels?
2. **Sales Enablement**: Is the sales team trained, do they have battlecards, objection handling, demo scripts, and pricing guidance?
3. **Competitive Timing**: Is the market ready? Are we launching into a window or into a competitor's momentum?
4. **Content & Asset Readiness**: Do we have launch assets across web copy, email, social, paid, and PR ready?
5. **Customer Success Readiness**: Can CS answer product questions, handle escalations, and set correct expectations with existing customers?
6. **Market Education Gap**: How much education does the market need before they can buy? Have we built that demand?
7. **Operational & Technical Readiness**: Is the product actually ready for launch-level usage — documentation, support processes, billing integration, analytics instrumentation?
8. **Executive & Cross-Functional Alignment**: Does every team agree on launch date, scope, goals, and accountabilities?

For each dimension, provide:
- Score with reasoning
- Top 2 risks if this dimension isn't addressed before launch
- Specific actions needed to raise the score by at least 2 points
- Estimated effort level: Quick fix (< 2 days), Medium (1 week), Heavy lift (2+ weeks)

Then provide:
- Overall launch readiness score (weighted average)
- Go / Soft Go / No-Go recommendation with rationale
- Top 3 launch killers that must be fixed before launch
- Launch success probability estimate

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Every dimension is scoring 5–7 and nothing looks like a hard blocker, but the launch still doesn't feel ready.**
Solution: The hidden launch killer is often **cross-functional ownership gaps** — dimensions that technically have owners but no one with real authority to make decisions. Pressure-test the DACI: ask each "Driver" to write down in one sentence what they are personally accountable for on launch day. If answers vary wildly or are vague, you have an accountability problem, not just a readiness problem. The fix is a single synchronous war-room alignment session with all Drivers + Approvers before the launch date is locked.

**Problem: Sales is scoring themselves 8/10 on readiness, but marketing and CS are scoring them 4/10.**
Solution: The perception gap between how sales feels about readiness vs. how other teams assess them is a leading indicator of a painful first 30 days post-launch. Run a live mock demo and deal review with 2–3 AEs before launch. Score them on: can they explain the value prop in 60 seconds? Can they handle the top 3 objections? Do they know the competitive differentiation cold? This surfaces real skill gaps that self-reported scores hide.

**Problem: Launch date is fixed due to an external commitment (conference, board announcement, investor expectation) and multiple dimensions are in the RED.**
Solution: This is the "Soft-Go" scenario. The launch still happens, but you change its scope. Instead of a full GA launch, consider: (1) an invitation-only early access program for 10–25 pre-selected accounts, (2) a "waitlist launch" that creates demand without requiring full operational readiness, or (3) separating the "announcement" from the "general availability" by 3–4 weeks. This protects the marketing moment while buying time to close readiness gaps before real revenue pressure begins.
