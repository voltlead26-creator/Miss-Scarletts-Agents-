# AI-Powered B2B SaaS Price Increase Communication & Customer Retention Revenue Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30 min | **Tags:** pricing, retention, customer-marketing, b2b, saas, revenue-expansion, churn-prevention

## Overview
This prompt architects a complete price increase program for B2B SaaS companies — from customer segmentation and message sequencing to objection handling and churn risk mitigation. Use it when you need to raise prices on existing customers while maximizing NRR, minimizing churn, and converting the announcement into an expansion conversation rather than a cancellation trigger.

Company context:

- Current pricing: [Current price tiers/amounts]
- New pricing: [New price tiers/amounts — % increase]
- Effective date: [When the new pricing kicks in for existing customers]
- Reason for increase: [Honest rationale — cost inflation, major new features, market repositioning, etc.]
- Customer base: [Number of customers, rough segmentation if available]
- Average contract: [$X ACV, annual vs monthly]

   Segment customers by (a) contract value tier and (b) health score / engagement level, then assign a communication strategy and churn risk rating to each segment.

2. COMMUNICATION SEQUENCE (per segment)
   - Email 1: Advance notice (8 weeks before effective date) — subject line, full body copy
   - Email 2: Value reinforcement (4 weeks before) — highlight new features and ROI they've received
   - Email 3: Final notice (2 weeks before) — clear, direct, offer lock-in or annual upgrade option
   - In-app notification copy (if applicable)

3. OBJECTION HANDLING PLAYBOOK
   Top 5 objections customers will raise with exact response scripts for each.

4. RETENTION OFFER FRAMEWORK
   Tiered options (annual lock-in discount, feature upgrades, payment terms) to offer at-risk accounts without undermining the overall pricing integrity.

5. SUCCESS METRICS
   KPIs to track at 30, 60, and 90 days post-announcement.

Write all email copy as ready-to-send drafts, not outlines. Be direct, value-first, and never apologetic about the increase.

## Success Metrics

- **NRR at 90 days:** Target ≥ base case scenario NRR (typically ≥110%) as modeled in Section 1
- **Gross logo churn rate:** Measure against optimistic/base/downside thresholds; flag if approaching downside boundary before Day 30
- **Annual plan conversion rate:** % of monthly customers who lock in annual pricing during the campaign window — target ≥15% of monthly cohort
- **Email engagement:** Email 1 open rate ≥45%, Email 3 open rate ≥55% (transactional urgency typically drives higher opens); reply rate to Email 1 ≥3% signals active engagement
- **CSM call completion:** 100% of accounts >$25K ACV receive a proactive CSM call before effective date
- **Offer acceptance rate:** % of at-risk accounts that accept a retention offer — target ≥60% of accounts flagged as Critical churn risk
- **Revenue protected:** Total ARR secured through annual lock-ins and retention offers vs. total ARR at risk — target ≥70% retention of at-risk ARR

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: A wave of cancellation requests comes in within 48 hours of Email 1, far higher than the modeled downside scenario.**
Solution: Don't panic-offer discounts immediately — that signals price weakness and will spread by word of mouth in customer communities. Instead, activate the CSM rapid response protocol for only the highest-value accounts in the first 72 hours. For lower-value accounts, let the Email 2 value sequence complete before offering any concessions. Pull the Gong/call data from the first 20 cancellation conversations and identify whether the objection is about the amount, timing, or competitive threat — the root cause determines the response. If it's uniformly about amount, consider whether the Tier 2 loyalty offer needs to be expanded to a broader segment.

**Problem: The CSM team is uncomfortable with the script and is going off-message, making unauthorized offers to avoid difficult conversations.**
Solution: Run a 60-minute role-play session with the full CSM team before the campaign launches. The most common CSM mistake is leading with the offer instead of leading with value — practice the "value anchor first, objection second, offer third" sequence. Create a simple one-page quick-reference card with the objection scripts and offer tiers. Set a Slack channel specifically for price increase escalations so CSMs have real-time support without going rogue with unauthorized discounts.

**Problem: Several enterprise accounts are invoking price protection clauses in their contracts that you weren't aware of.**
Solution: Pull a legal review of all contracts >$25K ACV before launching the communication campaign — this should happen in Week 1 of the 10-week timeline, not after the emails go out. For accounts with legitimate price-protection clauses, honor them fully, flag the renewal date in the CRM, and assign a CSM task 90 days before renewal to begin the conversion conversation. Use these accounts as your "delayed pricing cohort" — they become the test group for your next pricing iteration once protection expires.
