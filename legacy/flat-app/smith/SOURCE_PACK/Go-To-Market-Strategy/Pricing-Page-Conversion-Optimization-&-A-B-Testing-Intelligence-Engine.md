## Integration Tips

- **Webflow/Framer:** Export the CTA microcopy and plan names directly into your no-code pricing page components. Use the A/B test roadmap to prioritize Webflow A/B variants (or integrate Statsig/Optimizely).
- **HubSpot:** Create smart content rules to show different social proof elements on the pricing page based on visitor industry, company size (from IP enrichment via Clearbit/6sense), or lifecycle stage. Pull FAQ answers into HubSpot chatbot flows.
- **Segment + Amplitude:** Track pricing page events — toggle interactions (monthly↔annual), plan hover time, CTA clicks per tier, FAQ accordion opens. Build a pricing engagement score to identify high-intent visitors for sales follow-up.
- **Stripe:** Map plan names in this engine to Stripe product/price IDs. Use Stripe's built-in annual discount percentage to match the framing recommended here. Trigger Zapier flows on annual plan selection to notify CS for proactive onboarding.
- **Notion/Linear:** Paste the A/B test roadmap directly into a Notion database or Linear project. Each test becomes a ticket with hypothesis, metrics, and effort estimate already populated.
- **Gong/Chorus:** Feed the FAQ section into your conversation intelligence tool as a "pricing objection library." Coach reps to use the same language on sales calls that you use on the pricing page — this creates buyer confidence through message consistency.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The output plan names feel generic or don't match our brand voice.
**Solution:** Add a brand voice example to the context (paste 2–3 sentences of existing copy you love). Also specify what the brand is NOT ("not enterprise-y, not startup-cute") — negative constraints often unlock better output than positive ones.

**Problem:** The A/B test sample sizes are larger than our monthly pricing page traffic.
**Solution:** Rerun the prompt with your actual monthly visitor count and request the engine prioritize "low-traffic-friendly" tests — specifically toggle position, button microcopy, and FAQ order, which require 500–800 visitors per variant rather than 2,000+. Alternatively, use Bayesian A/B testing tools (AB Tasty, Convert) that reach significance faster at lower traffic.

**Problem:** The feature matrix output includes features we haven't built yet.
**Solution:** Add a "feature constraints" field to the context with your current feature list. The engine will work with what exists. You can also ask it to produce a "roadmap teaser" approach — showing locked/coming-soon features in the matrix as a conversion and retention play for buyers who care about the roadmap.
