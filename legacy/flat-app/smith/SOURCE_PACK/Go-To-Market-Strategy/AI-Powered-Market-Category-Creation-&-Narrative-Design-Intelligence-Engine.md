- **Notion/Confluence:** Store your Category POV as the company's "north star" narrative document. Link it to your messaging matrix, content calendar, analyst briefing decks, and sales pitch templates so all functions reference the same language.
- **Salesforce/HubSpot:** Add a custom field "Buyer Used Category Language (Y/N)" to opportunity records. Track it as a leading indicator of category adoption — run a correlation analysis between this field and win rate quarterly.
- **Gong/Chorus:** Create a Tracker for your new category label. Monitor how frequently prospects use the term unprompted — this is the most powerful real-world category adoption signal you can measure.
- **Google Search Console + Ahrefs/Semrush:** Set up keyword tracking for your proposed category term before launch to capture baseline (likely zero). Chart monthly search volume growth as your primary long-term category adoption KPI.

## Troubleshooting
- **Problem:** Category name is too generic and competitors immediately claim the same label, diluting your ownership.
  **Solution:** Add a mechanism modifier that reflects your specific technical approach or outcome (e.g., not just "Revenue Intelligence" but "Signal-to-Action Revenue Intelligence" or "Lifecycle Revenue Intelligence"). Pre-test exclusivity: could your top 3 competitors claim this label credibly without changing their product? If yes, the name is too broad. Narrow the label to the specific mechanism or outcome only you deliver.

- **Problem:** Analysts refuse to recognize the category, citing insufficient market evidence or overlap with existing coverage areas.
  **Solution:** Pivot from category creation to category redefinition. Brief analysts on why the existing category label is actively limiting market growth — quantify the TAM gap. Lead with 5+ customer quotes explaining why they couldn't find a solution in the current category definition. Commission a 300-respondent survey showing the problem your category solves is widespread and that existing solutions are failing. Data beats assertions with analysts.

- **Problem:** Sales team reverts to old category language because prospects respond better to familiar terms, undermining the category launch.
  **Solution:** This is a leading indicator that the category is immature — not a failure signal. In months 1–6, buyers will still search and evaluate using old categories. Provide sales with a "translation guide" that maps old language to new and trains them to bridge both: "What many companies call X, we call Y because..." Track category language usage in Gong over time. As awareness builds through content and analyst coverage, adoption will shift. Do not abandon the category because sales prefers the path of least resistance short-term.
