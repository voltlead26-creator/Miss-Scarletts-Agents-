## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Business customers aren't deploying the consumer-facing features to their own customers — adoption flatlines after onboarding**
Solution: This is the #1 B2B2C GTM failure mode, and it's almost always a product design and CS problem disguised as a marketing problem. The fix requires three simultaneous interventions: (1) Build a "Consumer Launch Wizard" directly into your product that guides operators through sending their first consumer campaign with one click — remove all friction. (2) Make the consumer adoption score visible to the operator on their dashboard ("Your loyalty activation rate: 28%. Industry average: 41%. Here's how to improve it."). (3) Assign your first 90 days of CS resources specifically to co-executing the consumer launch alongside the operator, not just advising them to do it themselves.

**Problem: Consumer adoption is high but it's not visibly translating into business customer retention or referrals**
Solution: The causal link between consumer engagement and business revenue isn't explicit to your operators. Build and send a monthly "Consumer Impact Report" to every business customer — one page showing: digital orders processed, consumer retention rate, estimated revenue from loyalty-driven repeat visits vs. first-time visitors, and their consumer adoption score vs. platform average. When a restaurant owner sees that 38% of their monthly revenue came from Branchly-acquired repeat visitors, renewal is a non-event and referrals become natural.

**Problem: Messaging resonates with consumer-forward operators but falls flat with traditional or tech-skeptical business owners**
Solution: Lead exclusively with the business outcome and use consumer metrics only as proof, never as the pitch. Wrong approach: "Our consumer app has a 54 NPS and 2.3 monthly transactions per user." Right approach: "Operators on our platform see 19% more repeat customer revenue within 6 months. Here's exactly how one restaurant similar to yours achieved that — and how long it took." The skeptical operator doesn't care about your consumer metrics; they care about their revenue and their customers' happiness. Consumer data is your evidence; their business result is your headline.
