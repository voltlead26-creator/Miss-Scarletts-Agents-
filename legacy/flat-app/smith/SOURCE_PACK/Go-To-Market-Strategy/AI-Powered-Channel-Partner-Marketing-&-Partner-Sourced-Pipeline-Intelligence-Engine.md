## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** Partners sign up but don't activate — they complete onboarding but never register a deal.
**Solution:** Activation failure almost always means the partner's reps don't know how to identify your buyer in their client base. Add a "30-minute Deal Spotting Workshop" in week 3 of onboarding: live session walking partner reps through 5 real client scenarios where your product would apply. Partners who attend this workshop activate at 2.3x the rate of those who don't.

**Problem:** MDF is being spent on low-ROI activities (branded merchandise, trade show giveaways) and not generating pipeline.
**Solution:** Shift from a reimbursement model to a pre-approved activity menu. Replace open-ended MDF requests with a menu of 15 specific, pre-approved co-marketing plays (joint webinar = $3K, co-authored whitepaper = $5K, paid LinkedIn campaign = $2.5K). Include required pipeline targets for each activity as a condition of approval. Partners who don't submit a post-activity pipeline report within 90 days forfeit their next MDF allocation.

**Problem:** Channel conflict — partner deals competing with direct AE territory, creating internal tension and partner frustration.
**Solution:** Implement a clear deal registration policy with 30-day protection windows and an explicit co-sell playbook: when a partner registers a deal, assign a named AE as co-sell partner (not competition). Track co-sell win rates separately and celebrate partner-influenced closes in your revenue meetings. Direct AEs who work partner deals should receive partial quota credit to eliminate the "partners steal my deals" narrative.
