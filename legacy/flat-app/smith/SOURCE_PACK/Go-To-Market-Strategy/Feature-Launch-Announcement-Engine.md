# Feature Launch Announcement Engine - Multi-Channel Release Communications Generator

**Difficulty:** Intermediate | **Time:** 15-20 min | **Tags:** product-launch, feature-release, saas, announcements, email, social, changelog, sales-enablement

## Overview
Generates a complete, ready-to-deploy communications package for a product feature launch — covering email, in-app messaging, changelog, social posts, and sales talking points. Use this any time you ship a feature that deserves more than a silent deploy: new capabilities, major UX changes, integrations, or anything a customer would actually care about.

## Integration Tips

**HubSpot / Marketo:**
- Use the email body output as a template in your ESP. Map [Company Name] and [Feature Access Link] to contact properties for personalization tokens.
- Set up a smart list filter: "Plan = Professional OR Enterprise" AND "Feature Activated = False" to trigger a re-engagement email at Day 14.

**Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:**
- Add AE talking points as a pinned note on the Opportunity record or as a Product Release task in the activity timeline.
- Create a custom field "Feature Discussed in Call" to track sales adoption of the messaging.

**Notion / Confluence:**
- Paste the internal Slack announcement and CS briefing into your product wiki as the canonical launch communication record.
- Link the changelog entry to the relevant Jira epic or product roadmap item.

**Zapier / Make:**
- Trigger: New row in Google Sheets (feature ship date reached) → Action: Send draft email to marketing manager in Gmail for final review → Action: Post internal Slack announcement to #gtm-launches channel.
- Automate changelog publishing: Zap from Linear/Jira "Done" status → append to Notion changelog database with the AI-generated entry.

**Intercom / Pendo:**
- Use the in-app modal copy directly in an Intercom Product Tour or Pendo Guide. Trigger based on user segment (plan type) and recency (hasn't visited the feature URL in 7 days).

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The email copy sounds like every other SaaS announcement email.**
Fix: Replace the opening line with the customer's specific pain described in visceral, concrete terms. Avoid "we're thrilled" or "introducing." Start with a customer situation: "Your pipeline doesn't send you a text when something goes wrong."

**Problem: Sales team isn't using the talking points.**
Fix: The talking points are probably too long or too feature-centric. Rerun the Advanced prompt and add this constraint: "Each talking point must be deliverable in one breath — 25 words max. The customer pain must come first, not the feature description."

**Problem: Low feature activation despite high email open rates.**
Fix: The CTA is probably landing on a generic dashboard instead of the feature directly. Make sure the link deep-links to the feature entry point. Also add the in-app modal as a secondary touch for users who opened the email but didn't click.
