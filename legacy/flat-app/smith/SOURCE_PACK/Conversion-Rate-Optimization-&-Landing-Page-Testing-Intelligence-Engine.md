## Integration Tips

- **Unbounce / Instapage / Webflow:** Export the variant copy from this prompt directly into your page builder. Use the "duplicate page" feature to create your control and variant as separate URLs, then use your builder's native A/B testing or split test via Google Optimize/VWO at the URL level.
- **VWO / Optimizely / Convert:** Use the hypothesis statements from Section 3 as your test names in your testing platform. Paste the full variant copy into the WYSIWYG editor. Set the primary goal as "form submission" and add the secondary metrics as additional goals.
- **HubSpot:** Create variant landing pages in HubSpot's Landing Page tool. Use the A/B test feature for pages with 1,000+ monthly visitors; for lower traffic, run 50/50 redirects using a URL shortener or your CMS routing.
- **Google Analytics 4:** Create a custom event for each CTA click. Build a Looker Studio report that segments CVR by variant, traffic source, device, and ICP segment. Connect to BigQuery for cohort analysis to verify quality of CRO-sourced leads.
- **Hotjar / FullStory:** Before running tests, record 50 sessions on the control page and review heatmaps. This qualitative validation strengthens test hypotheses and often surfaces friction points the AI analysis didn't catch.
- **Salesforce / HubSpot CRM:** Add a hidden form field that captures the landing page variant. Build a report showing demo-to-opportunity rate and win rate by variant. This closes the loop between CVR optimization and revenue impact.
- **Zapier automation:** Build a workflow: new form submission → enrich lead with Clearbit → score with your ICP criteria → if ICP match score > 80, trigger immediate sales alert in Slack with the enriched profile.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Test doesn't reach statistical significance after 4 weeks.**
Solution: You have two options. (1) Reduce the number of variants — if you launched a 3-way test, kill the lowest performer and run a cleaner A/B. (2) Raise the minimum detectable effect — you may be testing too small a change. Switch to testing the hero section headline + CTA combination (larger impact surface) rather than button color or a single word change. For pages under 500 monthly visitors, abandon A/B testing and use heuristic optimization: implement the AI's top recommended changes directly based on confidence score.

**Problem: CVR improves but lead quality drops (lower SQL rate from new variant).**
Solution: The variant is likely attracting more top-of-funnel visitors who aren't ready to buy. Add qualification friction intentionally: include a company size selector, ask about current tool usage, or change the CTA from "Free Trial" to "See It For [Job Title]s At [Company Size] Companies" to self-select the ICP. Alternatively, add a disqualifying statement in the hero (e.g., "Built for teams of 50+") to reduce unqualified form fills.

**Problem: The AI-generated variant copy sounds too similar to every other SaaS landing page.**
Solution: Inject specificity. Go to your Gong call recordings or G2 reviews and pull 3-5 verbatim phrases your best customers use when describing their pain. Replace AI-generated generic phrases with those exact customer words. Specific, idiosyncratic language outperforms polished generic copy in almost every B2B A/B test because it signals deep audience understanding.
