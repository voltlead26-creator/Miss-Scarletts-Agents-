# Community-Led Growth Demand Generation Engine - From Community Builder to Pipeline Machine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 30-40 min | **Tags:** community-led-growth, clg, b2b-community, demand-generation, slack-community, discord, circle, member-activation, pipeline, advocacy, saas, b2b, growth

## Overview
Transforms raw community membership data and engagement signals into a ranked pipeline of community-qualified leads (CQLs), generates member activation sequences that convert passive lurkers into active champions, and builds a community content calendar that drives consistent inbound demand. Use it when your Slack, Discord, or Circle community has members but isn't generating predictable revenue — or when you want to build a community from scratch as a primary demand generation channel.

LAYER 1 — COMMUNITY QUALIFIED LEAD (CQL) SCORING
Score each member using the PULSE framework:

- P — Persona Match (0-20): Does this member's title, company size, and industry match your ICP?

- U — Usage & Product Signal (0-25): What is their relationship with your product?
  - 20-25: Active paying customer who is also community-engaged (highest-value advocate)
  - 12-19: Free trial or recent sign-up from community membership
  - 5-11: Has visited pricing page or attended a product demo/webinar
  - 0-4: No known product interaction

- L — Level of Engagement (0-25): How actively are they participating in community conversations?
  - 20-25: Original poster in last 14 days, replies to others, asks product-adjacent questions, shares their own work/case studies
  - 12-19: Regular reactor, occasional reply, attends community events
  - 5-11: Joined channels, read-only, no visible engagement

- S — Social & Professional Authority (0-15): Do they have influence that amplifies your brand?

  - 6-11: Moderate LinkedIn presence, company-level recognition in their space

- E — Expansion & Network Signal (0-15): Do they show signs of company-level buying potential or peer network referrals?

  - 6-11: Engaged peers at similar companies, mentions team context in posts

## Integration Tips
- **Common Room / Orbit**: Export your member engagement data (post count, topics, join date, product status) directly from Common Room's "Members" view filtered to last 30 days. Map Common Room's "Funnel Stage" field to the PULSE scoring tiers. Set up Common Room automations to notify your community manager in Slack whenever a member's score crosses a tier threshold.
- **HubSpot**: Create a "Community Status" property on the Contact object (values: Champion / Active / Lurker / Dormant / Non-Member). Build a workflow that enrolls ACTIVE-tier contacts into your product nurture sequence and CHAMPION-tier contacts into a manual task sequence for co-creation outreach. Use UTM parameters (utm_source=community, utm_medium=slack) on all links shared in the community to track product sign-ups attributed to community.
- **Salesforce**: Add "Community Influenced" as a Campaign Type and log each CQL as a Campaign Member when they reach the ACTIVE tier. Use the Campaign Influence model (Einstein Attribution or custom) to attribute pipeline to community for board reporting. Set up a Salesforce Flow that auto-creates a Task for the community manager when a community contact is added to an Opportunity.
- **Zapier / Make**: Build an automation: new Slack message in community → parse for ICP keywords (your product category, competitor names, pain-point phrases) → add to a Google Sheet "Hot Signals" log → notify community manager in Slack with one-click "start activation sequence" button. Run this keyword monitoring continuously to catch in-market buying signals in real time without manual channel monitoring.
- **Circle**: Use Circle's Member Tags to replicate the PULSE tier system natively. Circle's API allows you to pull member activity data (last active, posts, reactions) into a Google Sheet on a scheduled basis for CQL scoring. Use Circle's DM automation to trigger Touch 1 of the activation sequence automatically when a member reaches a defined engagement threshold (e.g., 3 posts in first 30 days).
- **Beehiiv / Substack**: If your community is newsletter-anchored, use open rate and click data as a proxy for the Level of Engagement (L) score. Members who click community-related links are LURKER-minimum; members who reply to newsletters or share posts are ACTIVE-minimum. Feed this data into the PULSE model via a Zapier integration connecting your newsletter platform to your CRM.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The content calendar generates engagement (reactions, replies) but no product sign-ups or demo requests.**
The gap is almost always in the bridge from "value consumer" to "product curious." Audit whether your content calendar includes enough Outcome Posts — content that shows a specific, quantified result achieved by a community member using your product. If 100% of your posts are educational and 0% are outcome-driven, members have no reason to connect your community with your product. Add at least one outcome or case study post per week. Also check whether your CTAs are too soft — "let me know if you want to chat" converts at 3-5x lower rates than "I'm opening 3 spots for a 20-minute technical walkthrough this Thursday — drop a reply to grab one."

**Problem: The DM activation sequences are getting ignored or generating "not interested" responses.**
The most common cause is that Touch 1 feels templated even when it's technically personalized. Review whether your opening line actually references something specific the member wrote — the post title is not enough, you need to reference a specific point or question they raised. If members are unresponsive after Touch 2, do not send Touch 3. Mark them as DORMANT and remove from active outreach for 60 days. Over-messaging in a community setting destroys trust publicly and personally. Ensure DMs are coming from a named person (your community manager) not a brand handle — person-to-person DMs convert at 4-6x the rate of brand account outreach in community platforms.

**Problem: Community members are engaging but CQL scores are too low because ICP match is weak — most active members are not buyers.**
This is a community positioning problem, not a scoring problem. If your most active members are students, freelancers, or practitioners at companies too small to buy, the community's content positioning attracted the wrong ICP. Reposition the community around outcomes and titles that matter to your buyer: change channel names, topic descriptions, and onboarding questions to filter for the right persona. Add a join application or onboarding survey that routes non-ICP members to a read-only experience and ICP members to the full community. Within 60 days of repositioning, your PULSE scores should shift meaningfully toward ACTIVE and CHAMPION tiers.
