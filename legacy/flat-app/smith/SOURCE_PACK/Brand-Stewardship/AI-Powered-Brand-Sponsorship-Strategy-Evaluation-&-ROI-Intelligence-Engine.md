# AI-Powered Brand Sponsorship Strategy, Evaluation & ROI Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25-30 min | **Tags:** brand, sponsorship, roi, cmo, b2b, b2c, brand-equity, partnership, measurement, budget-optimization

## Overview
Evaluates incoming sponsorship proposals and existing sponsorship portfolios using a structured scoring framework, then designs high-ROI activation playbooks and measurement systems to prove and maximize sponsorship return. Use this when you're drowning in sponsorship pitches with no objective evaluation framework, when the CFO is questioning whether conference and media sponsorships actually move the needle, or when you're renewing a major sponsorship and need to negotiate smarter and activate harder.

SYSTEM DESIGN: BRAND SPONSORSHIP INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

MODULE 1: SPONSORSHIP EVALUATION & SCORING FRAMEWORK

Step 1.1 — Multi-Dimensional Scoring Model
Score the sponsorship opportunity on 6 weighted dimensions (100 points total):

DIMENSION 1: AUDIENCE FIT (25 points)
- Exact overlap between property audience and your ICP: what % of attendees/readers match your buyer profile?
- Scoring rubric:
  - 23-25: >60% ICP match with decision-maker seniority
  - 18-22: 40-60% ICP match or heavy influencer-level audience
  - 12-17: 25-40% ICP match
  - 0-11: <25% ICP match or wrong buyer profile
- Data sources to verify audience fit: request audience demographic report, compare with LinkedIn Matched Audience sizes, benchmark against peer companies who sponsor the property

DIMENSION 2: STRATEGIC REACH & FREQUENCY (20 points)
- Total unduplicated reach (how many unique ICP members will see your brand across all placements in the package)
- Frequency of impressions (how many times per year will your ICP see your brand through this property)
- Scoring rubric: calculate effective CPM and compare to:
  - B2B conference sponsorship benchmark: $80-200 CPM for qualified enterprise audience
  - Trade publication digital: $40-100 CPM for verified-role targeting
  - Newsletter sponsorship: $60-150 CPM for engaged subscriber base
  - If calculated CPM is below benchmark AND audience quality is verified: higher score

DIMENSION 3: ACTIVATION POTENTIAL (20 points)
- Can you do more than put your logo on a banner? Score based on:
  - Speaking/content opportunity available: +8 points
  - Owned experience (booth, roundtable, dinner, workshop): +5 points
  - Content co-creation rights (bylines, interviews, research): +4 points
  - Data capture rights (badge scan, registration data access): +3 points
- A logo-only sponsorship with no activation potential should score 0-5 here and likely fails the overall evaluation

DIMENSION 4: COMPETITIVE DYNAMICS (20 points)
- Does sponsoring this property create competitive advantage or just table stakes?
- Scoring rubric:
  - 18-20: You would be the ONLY player in your category sponsoring this (genuine differentiation)
  - 14-17: 1-2 competitors sponsor it; you can out-activate them
  - 8-13: 3+ competitors sponsor it; this is category hygiene, not advantage
  - 0-7: Your primary competitor is a headline sponsor; attending gives them undue authority boost
- If a competitor is the title/headline sponsor, reduce this score by 5 points regardless of other factors

DIMENSION 5: BRAND SAFETY & ALIGNMENT (10 points)
- Reputation risk assessment: Is this property associated with any controversial figures, brands, or viewpoints that could create brand risk?
- Audience attitude: Does this property's audience skew toward or against your brand values?
- Longevity: Is this an established property (>3 years) or a new/unproven one?
- Scoring rubric: binary flags (each reduces score) — controversy risk: -5, audience misalignment: -3, unproven property: -2

DIMENSION 6: MEASUREMENT QUALITY (5 points)
- Will you be able to actually measure impact from this sponsorship?
- Scoring rubric:
  - 5: Property provides audience data, scan data, unique UTMs, verified attribution
  - 3: Some data available, requires manual tracking effort
  - 1: Logo-only with no trackable data — pure brand play with unprovable ROI

SCORING THRESHOLDS:
- 80-100: Strong GO — negotiate aggressively, prioritize activation budget
- 65-79: Conditional GO — negotiate down in price or up in activation rights before committing
- 50-64: Yellow flag — only proceed if you can negotiate significant improvements OR if strategic rationale overrides (e.g., category presence is table stakes)
- Below 50: NO-GO — pass unless this is a legacy relationship or there's a specific strategic exception

Step 1.2 — Pricing & Market Benchmark Analysis
Evaluate whether the quoted price represents fair market value:

a) CPM CALCULATION:
- Formula: (Annual sponsorship cost) ÷ (Verified audience reach × average impressions per year)
- B2B CPM benchmarks by property type (2025-2026 market rates):
  - Tier 1 industry conference (Dreamforce, AWS re:Invent, Gartner Symposium): $150-350 CPM for qualified attendee audience
  - Mid-market B2B conference (10,000-50,000 attendees): $80-200 CPM
  - Niche B2B conference (<10,000 attendees, high ICP density): $200-500 CPM (worth paying if audience is extremely targeted)
  - Trade publication digital package: $50-120 CPM
  - Executive-focused newsletter (10K-50K subscribers): $100-300 CPM
  - Industry podcast (audio ad mid-roll): $25-80 CPM
  - Webinar sponsorship: $300-800 CPM (small but highly engaged)

b) VALUE-BEYOND-REACH CALCULATION:
- Add premium to CPM for: exclusive speaking opportunities (+20%), data/scan access (+15%), executive dinner co-host (+25%), content co-creation (+10%)
- Total value-adjusted CPM should be at or below benchmark for a favorable deal

c) NEGOTIATION FLOOR:
- Establish your maximum willingness-to-pay based on expected pipeline contribution
- Formula: (Target pipeline from sponsorship) × (Your marketing-influenced pipeline ROI multiple) = Maximum budget
- Example: If you need $500K in pipeline and your events generate $5 return per $1 invested → maximum budget = $100K

Step 1.3 — Information Request Protocol
Before signing any sponsorship over $20K, request the following from the property:

TIER 1 (required before any decision):
- Audience demographics report (job titles, seniority, company size, industry) — ask for last year's attendee/subscriber survey data
- Verified attendance/subscriber/listener numbers (not projections) — request third-party audit or media kit with source
- List of current sponsors (to assess competitive landscape and exclusivity zones)
- Previous sponsor performance data (anonymized): average lead volume, engagement rates, any case studies

TIER 2 (required before contracts):
- Data rights and usage policy: who owns badge scan data, newsletter subscriber data, registrant lists
- Cancellation and force majeure terms
- Measurement and reporting cadence: what data will they deliver, when, in what format
- Competitive exclusivity zones: what are you protected from? (e.g., "no other CRM vendor at your sponsorship tier")

RED FLAGS that indicate a bad sponsorship regardless of price:
- Property refuses to share verified audience data and only offers "estimates"
- No exclusivity of any kind even at premium tiers
- No data access or lead retrieval capability
- Property has fewer than 3 years of continuous operation (brand new events carry high cancellation/attendance risk)
- Sponsor list shows 5+ direct competitors at the same tier (you'll be one of many, not a standout)

MODULE 2: ACTIVATION PLAYBOOK DESIGN

Step 2.1 — Activation Investment Principle
Rule: Never allocate less than 50% of the sponsorship fee to activation. A $100K sponsorship with zero activation budget produces $0 in pipeline regardless of audience quality. Best-in-class sponsors invest $0.75-$1.50 in activation for every $1.00 in sponsorship fees.

Step 2.2 — Pre-Event Activation Calendar (90 days before)

90 DAYS BEFORE:
- Content and creative brief for all event-specific assets (deadline: 90 days before event)
- Speakers/executives confirmed and briefed on messaging (align to one clear message theme, not company overview)
- Account-based pre-registration targeting: identify 50 target accounts whose employees are registered; begin custom outreach sequence 8 weeks pre-event

60 DAYS BEFORE:
- 3-part email sequence to prospects mentioning your presence: "Will you be at [Event]?" (week 1) → "Here's what we're showing at [Event]" with specific value hook (week 3) → "Your personalized event guide from [Your Company]" with pre-scheduled meeting link (week 5)
- LinkedIn content series: 3 thought leadership posts connecting your sponsorship topic to ICP pain points — no event promotion, pure value
- Executive LinkedIn activation: company executives begin posting about [Event] topic with branded hashtag (not just "excited to be at X")
- Media outreach: if you have speaking slot, pitch 2-3 trade media journalists covering the event for pre-coverage

30 DAYS BEFORE:
- Private event website or Calendly page for scheduling meetings at the event — link goes in all outreach
- 1:1 personalized outreach to top 20 target accounts confirmed attending: propose specific agenda items, bring a relevant insight
- Booth staff briefing: each person knows their role, the conversation they should drive, the qualification questions to ask, and what to do with leads (scan + fill custom field in real time)

Step 2.3 — At-Event Activation Tactics

EXPERIENCE DESIGN (beyond the booth):
- If you have a speaking slot: structure as 70% customer insight / 30% product reference. Speakers who lead with product get walked out on. Speakers who lead with a surprising data point or customer story fill the room and get crowd questions.
- Private VIP experience: breakfast or dinner for 8-12 senior prospects/customers with an industry expert facilitator. Cost: $5K-$15K. Pipeline generated per event: typically $200K-$1M for enterprise companies. The ratio of peer conversation to pitch should be 80%/20%.
- Interactive demo or workshop: hands-on experience generates 3-5× more qualified conversations than passive booth demos
- Content capture: record a "man on the street" style video series with attendees discussing the topic your product solves — instant social content that reinforces your thought leadership without feeling like an ad

LEAD CAPTURE PROTOCOL:
- Every badge scan must be accompanied by one custom qualifying field recorded immediately: "What's the #1 thing you're trying to solve in [relevant area] this year?"
- Tier every scan in real time: HOT (mentioned our product, asked for follow-up), WARM (ICP profile, positive conversation), COLD (took swag, no real engagement)
- For HOT leads: same-day email from the rep they met, with specific reference to their answer to the qualifying question
- For WARM leads: within 24 hours, personalized follow-up with event-specific content offer
- For COLD leads: add to 5-touch post-event nurture sequence (no cold pitch)

Step 2.4 — Post-Event Follow-Up Sequence (30 days after)

DAY 1-2 POST EVENT:
- Personal follow-up from booth rep or executive to every HOT lead
- Email to ALL scanned contacts: "What resonated most from [Event] for you?" — conversational, not promotional
- LinkedIn connection requests from attending executives to senior contacts met at event

DAY 3-7 POST EVENT:
- Hot lead sequence: proposal or demo scheduled by AE
- Warm lead sequence: 3-email nurture with event-specific content (summary of key insights from your speaking session if applicable, plus relevant proof point for their stated pain)
- Company-wide post-event LinkedIn content: "Here's what we learned from 3 days of conversations with [ICP role]" — synthesizes real conversations into thought leadership

DAY 8-30 POST EVENT:
- All event leads entered into CRM with "Event: [Event Name]" campaign source for attribution tracking
- 30-day pipeline review: which event leads have converted to opportunity?
- Account-level engagement tracking: did target accounts that attended increase intent signals post-event?

MODULE 3: ROI MEASUREMENT FRAMEWORK

Step 3.1 — Pre-Sponsorship Baseline Measurement (before the event/activation)
Establish baselines for all metrics you intend to move so you can prove impact:

BRAND METRICS (measure 30 days before event):
- Unaided awareness in target segment: ideally surveyed, or proxy via branded search volume from target geos/company sizes
- Share of voice in event-relevant topic cluster: track your mentions vs. competitors in trade media, social, and community forums in the 30-day period before the event
- Sentiment score: baseline NPS, LinkedIn brand engagement rate, press coverage sentiment

DEMAND METRICS (measure 30 days before event):
- Website traffic from target companies (use ZoomInfo/Clearbit Reveal or G2 Buyer Intent to track company-level site visits)
- Pipeline from conference/event historically (what did previous year's event generate in pipeline within 90 days? Set that as baseline)
- Open rate and response rate for cold outreach to ICP contacts (control group benchmark)

Step 3.2 — Core Sponsorship KPI Dashboard
Track these 8 metrics for every major sponsorship:

1. COST PER QUALIFIED CONVERSATION (CPQC): Total sponsorship + activation cost ÷ Number of hot/warm qualified conversations
   - Target: Below $500 for SMB-focused events, below $1,500 for enterprise events
   
2. COST PER PIPELINE DOLLAR INFLUENCED: Total sponsorship + activation cost ÷ Sponsorship-sourced pipeline (90 days)
   - Target: 1:5 or better (every $1 spent generates $5 in pipeline)
   
3. CONTACT-TO-OPPORTUNITY CONVERSION RATE: HOT leads that become CRM opportunities within 30 days
   - Target: >15% for enterprise events

4. SHARE OF VOICE AT EVENT: Your branded mentions vs. competitor mentions in event social posts, media coverage, attendee discussions
   - Track with Brandwatch, Mention, or manual LinkedIn search

5. BRAND LIFT (awareness + preference): Change in branded search volume from target company segments in the 30 days after the event vs. 30-day baseline
   - Track with Google Trends (geographic targeting) or Google Search Console

6. EXECUTIVE ENGAGEMENT RATE: % of target accounts (from your pre-event list) that engaged with your brand at the event (booth visit, session attendance, private event)
   - Target: >30% for accounts where you had pre-event outreach

7. CONTENT AMPLIFICATION: Reach and engagement of event-specific content published within 30 days of event
   - LinkedIn posts about the event, live content, post-event insights
   - Target: 3× your average post reach

8. 90-DAY PIPELINE ATTRIBUTION: All opportunities created within 90 days where the event is a touchpoint in the buyer journey (use MTA model: event gets credit if it appears in the 30 days before opportunity creation)

Step 3.3 — Renewal Decision Framework
Build a clear, bias-free decision matrix for renewal:

STRONG RENEW (if all three are true):
- Cost per pipeline dollar influenced ≤ 1:5
- At least one named account in your top 50 account list engaged meaningfully
- Share of voice at the property is growing year-over-year (you're being seen as more central to the community)

CONDITIONAL RENEW (if two of three are true, negotiate first):
- Cost per pipeline dollar influenced is 1:3 to 1:5 (borderline)
- Strong brand lift metrics even if pipeline is lower than target
- Strategic reasons to maintain presence (new category entry, competitive pressure to maintain presence)

DO NOT RENEW (if two or more are true):
- Cost per pipeline dollar influenced is worse than 1:3 after two consecutive years
- No engagement from target accounts despite pre-event outreach
- Your primary competitor is gaining more mindshare at the property year-over-year
- Declining audience quality: ICP% in attendee demographics dropping

MODULE 4: NEGOTIATION PLAYBOOK

Step 4.1 — Beyond-Price Negotiation Items
Never negotiate just on price. These 8 items often have more value than a 10% price reduction:

1. AUDIENCE DATA RIGHTS: Full attendee registration data (name, title, company, email) for your sponsorship tier — this alone can be worth $20-50K in list value
2. SPEAKING SLOT UPGRADE: Mainstage or premium timeslot vs. breakout — the difference in audience size is often 10-100×
3. COMPETITIVE EXCLUSIVITY ZONE: "No other [category] company at your tier or above" — prevents direct competitors from neutralizing your investment
4. CONTENT CO-CREATION: Rights to publish a co-branded research report, bylined article in the property's publication, or guest episode on their podcast — earned media value of $15-50K
5. YEAR-ROUND DIGITAL INTEGRATION: Newsletter mentions, website listing, social media features outside the main event period
6. VIP EXPERIENCE HOSTING RIGHTS: Access to attendee list to invite to your private dinner/roundtable — the highest-converting activation
7. MEASUREMENT REPORTING: Commitment to provide verified metrics (not just reach estimates) — session attendance data, engagement data, unique visitor counts to your booth session
8. MULTI-YEAR OPTIONALITY: Lock in year 1 price with right-of-first-refusal for year 2-3 at same price, without committing to multi-year spend

Step 4.2 — Walk-Away Terms
No deal regardless of price if:

- No audience data: "We can't share registrant data for privacy reasons" — walk away. You're buying access to an audience you can't activate.
- No exclusivity of any kind even at your investment level: You're a commodity sponsor with no differentiation.
- No cancellation clause: Market shifts, competitor changes, event cancellations, and format changes happen. Any sponsorship over $25K should have a 60-90 day cancellation window with partial refund.
- Required logo placement on products you don't use: Co-marketing that requires false testimonials or product association — legal/brand risk.

MODULE 5: SPONSORSHIP PORTFOLIO MANAGEMENT

Step 5.1 — Portfolio Architecture
Structure your annual sponsorship portfolio across three tiers:

SIGNATURE SPONSORSHIPS (40-50% of budget, 1-2 properties):
- One primary conference where your ICP concentrates most densely
- Full activation budget deployed: speaking, VIP dinner, full staff
- Executive presence: CEO or relevant C-suite attends and activates
- Target: $1M+ in pipeline influence per year

CATEGORY PRESENCE SPONSORSHIPS (30-40% of budget, 3-5 properties):
- Industry publications, second-tier conferences, and community events where your ICP participates
- Selective activation: thought leadership content, digital placements, targeted outreach
- Target: $200K-500K in pipeline influence per year per property

EXPERIMENTAL BUDGET (10-20% of budget, 3-10 small bets):
- Emerging communities, new conferences, niche media properties
- Minimal activation: test the audience quality with digital placements before committing to physical presence
- Kill quickly: 6-month review, cut anything not hitting 1:3 pipeline ratio

Step 5.2 — Annual Portfolio Review Process
Review at Q3 planning for following year:

1. Score each current sponsorship against the 6-dimension framework
2. Calculate actual cost per pipeline dollar for each property (TTM)
3. Eliminate bottom 20% by ROI score
4. Reallocate freed budget to: (a) deepen activation on top performers, (b) one new experimental bet
5. Negotiate all renewals in Q4 (before the property's own sales cycle pressures pricing)

OUTPUT REQUIREMENTS:
- Deliver evaluation scores with explicit data sources and assumptions for each dimension
- All negotiation items formatted as specific asks with fallback positions
- Activation calendar in a format that can be dropped directly into Asana, Monday.com, or Notion
- ROI dashboard specified as a template that can be built in Google Sheets or Salesforce
- 30/60/90-day implementation roadmap prioritized by ROI impact
- All recommendations specific enough to act on without a follow-up strategy session

## Example Input/Output

**Input Example:**
Company: Meridian — AI-powered financial planning software for mid-market CFOs and controllers (100-2,000 employees)
Brand objective: Establish Meridian as the go-to solution for CFOs moving from Excel-based planning to AI forecasting
Budget: $750K annual sponsorship budget
ICP: CFO, VP Finance, Corporate Controller at tech/manufacturing/professional services companies, 200-1,500 employees
Sponsorship opportunity: CFO Leadership Summit (annual conference, 3,000 attendees, finance-focused, Chicago)
Package offered: $75K for: 10×20 booth, 1 breakout session (45 min), 3 pre-event email blasts to full list, logo on website/app/signage, 25 badge scan credits
Audience claim from property: "80% of attendees are CFOs, VPs Finance, or Controllers"
Competitors sponsoring: NetSuite (Platinum), Anaplan (Gold), Workday (Gold)

**Output Example (Evaluation Scoring):**

SPONSORSHIP SCORE: 71/100 — CONDITIONAL GO

DIMENSION SCORES:
1. Audience Fit: 24/25 — Finance leadership event with 80% claimed C-suite/VP Finance is near-perfect ICP match. Verify: request last year's attendee demographic report from the property. If <70% verified finance leadership, reduce this score to 18.

2. Strategic Reach: 15/20 — 3,000 attendees at 80% ICP = 2,400 ICP contacts. With $75K investment + package (booth, email, session), effective CPM: $75,000 ÷ (2,400 × 3 estimated impressions) = $10.4 CPM. This is far below the $80-200 CPM benchmark for B2B finance conferences. Either the audience quality is overstated or this is genuinely underpriced — investigate before committing.

3. Activation Potential: 16/20 — Breakout session (+8) and booth (+5) provide strong activation foundation. Missing: no VIP dinner hosting rights in base package (+0). Negotiate this as a top priority.

4. Competitive Dynamics: 8/20 — Three direct competitors already sponsor at higher tiers. NetSuite at Platinum, Anaplan and Workday at Gold means Meridian enters as a fourth AI planning vendor fighting for the same audience. Out-activate or find an angle that differentiates you (Meridian for mid-market specifically vs. NetSuite enterprise positioning). Score docked for commodity-level differentiation.

5. Brand Safety: 8/10 — Established conference, 7-year history, reputable organizer. Minor dock for generic finance content that sometimes attracts competing thought leadership.

6. Measurement Quality: 0/5 — Package includes only "25 badge scan credits" not full scan access. 25 scans for 3,000 attendees is woefully inadequate. This is a HARD NEGOTIATION PRIORITY before signing.

RECOMMENDATION: Conditional GO at $75K only if: (1) badge scan limit is removed or raised to unlimited/500+, (2) VIP dinner hosting rights secured for 12 of your target accounts, (3) breakout session confirmed for peak-time slot (not 7am or post-lunch). Without these, the competitive noise from Anaplan/NetSuite/Workday drowns your investment.

COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION ANGLE:
Your only path to owning mindshare at this event against three larger competitors: COMMIT TO THE MID-MARKET ANGLE OTHERS WON'T. NetSuite targets $50M+ revenue. Workday is enterprise-only. Anaplan is complex and expensive. Meridian for CFOs at 200-1,500 employees is a genuine blue ocean at this conference. Every piece of content, every booth conversation, every session slide should lead with "built for CFO teams without an army of consultants." This is a positioning differentiation strategy, not just an event tactic.

## Success Metrics

- **Sponsorship evaluation accuracy:** Over 12 months, 80%+ of GO decisions should generate positive ROI; post-hoc scoring should validate pre-decision scores within 15 points
- **Pipeline ROI:** Minimum 1:5 cost-to-pipeline ratio for Signature sponsorships; 1:3 for Category Presence sponsorships; track in CRM within 90-day attribution window
- **Activation rate:** At minimum 30% of scanned contacts (HOT + WARM) convert to active CRM engagement (email reply, meeting accepted, or content download) within 30 days of the event
- **Share of voice at property:** Your branded mention count at/around the event should be at least equal to each individual competitor's count (you don't need to beat the pack, just match each player)
- **VIP experience pipeline:** Executive roundtable/dinner attendees should convert to opportunity at >25% rate within 60 days
- **Renewal decision clarity:** Every annual sponsorship should have a documented ROI score by November 1 that enables a GO/NO-GO renewal decision without additional stakeholder debate

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The sponsorship generated leads but CFO says we have nothing to show for the $150K investment**
Solution: This is a measurement failure, not a sponsorship failure. The root cause is almost always one of three things: (1) Leads were captured but not entered into CRM with correct campaign attribution — fix by auditing all contacts added in the 30 days after the event and re-tagging those who attended or mentioned the event. Build a retro-attribution model. (2) Leads are in CRM but opportunities weren't created or sourced correctly — review all opportunities created in 90 days after the event and check if any first/key touch was the event. Sales often creates opportunities without logging the event touchpoint. (3) Real ROI exists but isn't visible because your attribution model is first-touch and the event was a mid-funnel touch — switch to a weighted multi-touch model for events, where any event touch within 90 days of an opportunity creation receives 20-30% pipeline credit. Run this retro-model on your last 12 months of events and you'll likely find 2-4× more "event-influenced" pipeline than first-touch attribution shows.

**Problem: We're one of 8 sponsors in our category and our booth is always empty while a competitor's is mobbed**
Solution: The competitor with the busy booth is doing one or more of: (a) pre-scheduling meetings — their reps reached out to every registered attendee in their ICP weeks before the event and booked 20-30 meetings, so the booth looks busy because it IS busy with pre-scheduled conversations, not walk-by traffic; (b) running an attention-getting demo/experience vs. passive standing presentation — if your booth is a banner and a table with brochures, you will lose to any competitor with a live product demo, a game, an interactive assessment, or a visible customer conversation; (c) offering something attendees want — charging stations, a private meeting room within the booth, a relevant giveaway, or a live expert session. Match their pre-scheduling discipline first (this alone closes 60% of the gap), then redesign your booth experience.

**Problem: Our private dinner VIP experience generates great conversation but no pipeline**
Solution: The most common failure mode in executive dinners is confusing a relationship dinner (valuable over time) with a pipeline dinner (requires clear next steps). Two specific fixes: (1) Brief the facilitator to close with a structured next-step question for every attendee: "Based on what we discussed tonight, would it make sense to have [Your Company] run a quick diagnostic on [specific topic] for your team next month?" This is a low-pressure ask with a specific time frame, not a sales pitch. (2) Assign every attendee to a named follow-up owner (your AE or executive) before the dinner starts, so the morning after the event, that person sends a personal note referencing a specific thing discussed at the table. Generic "Great to meet you" follow-ups convert at <5%. Personalized, specific follow-ups that reference a real conversation convert at 20-40%.
