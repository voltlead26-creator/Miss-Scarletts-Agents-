# Brand Health Monitoring - Real-Time Brand Sentiment & Perception Tracker

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** b2b, b2c, brand, strategy, analysis, awareness

## Overview
Generate a comprehensive brand health audit by synthesizing social listening data, NPS scores, share of voice metrics, and competitive sentiment — giving CMOs an actionable dashboard of where brand perception stands and what to fix. Use this monthly or after any major campaign, product launch, or PR event.

**Input Example:**

**Output Example (excerpt):**

**Executive Summary**

Vaultly's brand health score is 54/100 — stable but fragile. Enterprise customers are strong advocates (NPS 51) while SMB churn risk is elevated by a perception of complexity. The 8% drop in branded search combined with DocuSign's AI launch is eroding mindshare among evaluation-stage buyers. Top priority: reposition onboarding as a competitive strength (not a liability) and publish a direct response to the AI contract analysis trend before competitors define that narrative for us.

**Perception Gap Analysis**

- Key gap: "Efficient" is not landing. The dated UI and long onboarding directly contradict this pillar.
- Root cause: Product investment has prioritized security features over UX; marketing hasn't compensated with proof points around speed-to-value.

## Integration Tips
- **Brandwatch / Sprout Social / Mention**: Export your 30-day social listening report as CSV, paste top themes and sentiment breakdown directly into the Advanced prompt's qualitative signals section
- **G2 / Trustpilot**: Use their review export or analytics dashboard to pull top positive/negative themes; paste verbatim snippets for the most accurate perception gap analysis
- **HubSpot CRM**: Pull NPS survey results by customer segment (plan tier, company size, industry) to populate the segmented NPS fields — segment-level data dramatically improves the diagnosis
- **Google Search Console**: Use the branded query trend to populate the branded search volume field; export 90-day comparison to spot acceleration or deceleration
- **Notion / Confluence**: Run this prompt monthly and paste the Brand Health Scorecard into a running tracker page — build a trend line across quarters to show the board brand equity movement over time
- **Salesforce**: Pull "lost deal" reason codes and closed-won survey data to populate the "sales team intel" field; loss-to-competitor reasons often reveal brand perception issues before they show up in reviews

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: I don't have formal brand tracking data — just a gut feeling.**
Solution: Start with what you have. Pull your G2/Trustpilot reviews (even 20-30 are enough to pattern-match), run a quick LinkedIn poll asking your audience to describe your brand in one word, and check Google Trends for your brand vs. 2 competitors. Feed these into the Quick Version — the output will still surface 80% of the insight and tell you exactly what formal data to collect next.

**Problem: The prompt output is too generic and doesn't feel specific to my company.**
Solution: The quality of output scales directly with input specificity. Replace vague descriptors ("sentiment is mixed") with actual data points and verbatim quotes. Paste 5-10 real customer review snippets instead of summarizing them — the AI will pattern-match on language the same way a human analyst would.

**Problem: The report flags too many problems and I don't know where to start.**
Solution: Use the Reputation Risk Register severity + velocity matrix as your triage tool. Only "High severity + Growing velocity" risks require immediate action. Everything else is a backlog item. Have the AI re-run just the 30-Day Action Plan section with the constraint: "Give me only the top 2 actions that will have the biggest impact on brand perception within 30 days if we execute them well."
