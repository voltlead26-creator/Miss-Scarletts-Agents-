**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 25-30 min | **Tags:** integrated-marketing, brand-demand, campaign-architecture, cmo-strategy, full-funnel, binet-field, brand-building, demand-generation, campaign-planning, b2b-brand

## Overview
Design integrated marketing campaigns that simultaneously build long-term brand equity and generate short-term pipeline — applying the Binet & Field "long and short of it" framework to B2B SaaS contexts. Use this when planning a major campaign, justifying brand investment to the CFO, integrating scattered channels into a coherent architecture, or launching a new product with both awareness and pipeline goals.

**Matched account cohort design:**
- Build two cohorts of target accounts matched on firmographic and technographic signals
- Serve brand campaign to cohort A, no brand impressions to cohort B
- Compare: demo request rate, pipeline creation rate, deal velocity, CAC, win rate

**Campaign Name:** "The Cost of Certainty"

**Budget Split:** 52% Brand ($312K) / 48% Demand ($288K)

The 52/48 split prioritizes awareness building without abandoning near-term pipeline.*

## Integration Tips
- **HubSpot:** Create a custom contact property "Brand_Campaign_Touchpoints" (number field) and a workflow that increments it each time a contact engages with campaign-tagged content; build a report comparing pipeline velocity and close rate by touchpoint count quartile
- **Salesforce:** Set up Campaign objects for each channel; use Salesforce Campaign Influence (First Touch + Multitouch hybrid) to attribute brand impressions to Opportunities; train reps to log a "Brand Mention" activity when a prospect references the campaign on a call — enables win rate correlation analysis
- **LinkedIn Campaign Manager:** Activate the Brand Lift Study feature (requires $90K+ spend threshold) to measure aided recall lift, message association, and consideration rate change vs. unexposed holdout — export quarterly for board reporting
- **Google Search Console + SEMrush:** Baseline branded search volume before campaign launch; track monthly branded query impressions as a leading indicator of awareness momentum; create a Looker Studio dashboard combining branded search trend with campaign impression data
- **Bombora / 6sense:** Use surge data to identify target accounts showing category intent signals; route these to SDR team as "warm inbound" regardless of whether they filled out a form — brand campaign has already primed them
- **Notion:** Build a campaign war room with real-time dashboard embed, weekly update log from each channel owner, creative asset library accessible to all agency partners, and a running log of "brand mentions" reported by sales team

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** CFO won't approve brand budget because "we cannot measure it" and demands performance-only spending.
**Solution:** Propose a 6-month pilot with a geographic holdout test — run the integrated campaign in 3 markets, hold one comparable market back as a control group. At Month 6, compare pipeline metrics, controlling for seasonality and sales capacity differences. Use LinkedIn Brand Lift Study data as interim proof at Month 3. Frame the ROI math specifically: "Our current CPO is $8,200. Brand-aware prospects typically convert at 2.1x the rate of cold outbound — that's an effective CPO of $3,900 for brand-exposed accounts. The brand investment pays back when we convert more than [X] incremental deals that wouldn't have converted without awareness."

**Problem:** Brand and demand creative executions feel like they're from different companies — the brand ads are emotional and abstract while demand ads are completely different in tone and look.
**Solution:** Run a "message spine" exercise before briefing creative: write the single sentence that is true in both executions. For Solvari, it was: *"You deserve certainty, not guesswork."* Brand ads dramatize the emotional consequence of guesswork; demand ads show Solvari delivering certainty with specific proof. The same truth, two executions. Require all creative briefs to include this sentence as the unifying principle. Review all executions against it before production.

**Problem:** Sales team pushes back on brand investment, arguing it doesn't generate enough immediate leads and they need more pipeline now.
**Solution:** Train sales on the compounding flywheel with data they can see in the CRM. Pull the win rate for deals where the prospect mentioned hearing about the brand before the SDR outreach vs. deals with no prior brand exposure. In most B2B SaaS companies, this delta is 15–25 percentage points. Quantify that in revenue terms: "At our current pipeline volume, a 20-point win rate improvement on brand-aware accounts is worth $[X] in additional closed revenue per quarter." Then show them how to use the brand campaign as an outbound trigger — accounts receiving brand impressions get prioritized in SDR sequences, with personalized outreach referencing the campaign: "You may have seen our recent campaign about [topic] — I wanted to connect it to what [customer] achieved."
