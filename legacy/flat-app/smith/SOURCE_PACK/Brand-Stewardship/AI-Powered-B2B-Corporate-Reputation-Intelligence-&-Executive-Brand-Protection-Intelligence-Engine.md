# AI-Powered B2B Corporate Reputation Intelligence & Executive Brand Protection Intelligence Engine

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20-30 min | **Tags:** corporate-reputation, brand-protection, executive-brand, reputation-intelligence, b2b, cmo-leadership, stakeholder-management

## Overview

Builds a proactive corporate reputation intelligence system that continuously monitors reputation signals across every buyer-relevant channel, scores reputation health by stakeholder audience (customers, prospects, employees, investors, press, analysts), and generates targeted reputation capital programs — before crises occur. Use this when entering new enterprise market segments, preparing for a fundraise or M&A process, managing executive transitions, or recovering from a reputation event.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "The prompt produces generic reputation advice that doesn't reflect our specific situation."**
Solution: Add more context to the competitor and current reputation profile sections. Specifically: paste in the last 5 Glassdoor reviews verbatim, list the exact searches a prospect would do to research you (run them yourself first), and include any specific deals where reputation was a deal blocker. The prompt produces generic output only when given generic input.

**Problem: "Our executives refuse to post on LinkedIn — the content calendar isn't actionable."**
Solution: Use the Advanced Version's Executive Brand Architecture section to build the *business case* for executive posting first. Present data: show the CEO the correlation between their post impressions among ICP titles and pipeline conversion rate (pull from LinkedIn analytics + CRM). If they won't post themselves, use a ghostwriting workflow: 15-minute voice memo from exec → AI-structured draft → exec reviews and approves in 5 minutes. Start with 1 post/week, not 2.

**Problem: "We don't have enough G2 reviews to establish a credible reputation score."**
Solution: Run the G2 review generation prompt from [Customer Advocacy & Referral section](../../06_Customer-Success-&-Retention/Customer-Advocacy-&-Referral/G2-Review-Social-Proof-Automation-Engine.md) in parallel with this program. As an immediate stopgap: prioritize getting reviews from recognizable company names in your target ICP verticals — 20 reviews from Fortune 1000 companies outweighs 200 reviews from SMBs in enterprise procurement contexts.
