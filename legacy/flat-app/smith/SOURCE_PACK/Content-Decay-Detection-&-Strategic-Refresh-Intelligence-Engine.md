# ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

## Phase 1: Decay Signal Detection
Analyze content across four decay vectors:

**1. Search Performance Decay**
- Traffic velocity: Month-over-month trajectory over 12 months
- Ranking drift: Position changes for primary and semantic keywords
- CTR degradation: Click-through rate decline vs. impression stability
- Cannibalization signals: Multiple URLs competing for same keyword cluster

**2. Content Freshness Decay**
- Age-relevance gap: Time since last substantive update vs. topic velocity
- Factual staleness indicators: Statistics, pricing, product features, regulations
- Competitor freshness advantage: When top-ranking competitors last updated
- E-E-A-T signals: Author credentials, citations, expert quotes, original research

**3. Audience Intent Drift**
- Search intent evolution: Has the user intent for primary keyword shifted (informational → commercial)?
- Engagement pattern shifts: Time-on-page decline, scroll depth reduction, conversion rate drop
- SERP format mismatch: Does current content format match what Google now rewards (listicle vs. deep guide vs. tool)?
- Semantic gap expansion: New related questions and subtopics competitors cover that you don't

**4. Technical & UX Decay**
- Core Web Vitals degradation
- Internal linking equity erosion
- Structured data obsolescence
- Mobile experience regression

## Phase 2: Revenue Impact Scoring
For each decaying asset, calculate:

**Content Asset Value Score (CAVS) = (Historical Peak Traffic × Conversion Rate × ACV) × Recovery Probability**

Score components:
- Peak Traffic Potential: Maximum monthly sessions achieved historically
- Conversion Rate: % of visitors who converted to MQL/SQL/customer
- ACV Impact: Average contract value influenced by this content
- Recovery Probability: 1.0 (strong backlinks, low competition) to 0.2 (high competition, intent shifted)

## Phase 3: Competitive Gap Analysis
For each high-priority decay candidate:
1. Pull current top 3 ranking URLs for primary keyword
2. Analyze what they cover that your content doesn't (word count, subtopics, media, data freshness)
3. Identify "refresh moat" opportunities — ways to create durable competitive advantage through original data, expert quotes, or interactive elements

## Phase 4: Refresh Strategy Classification
Assign each content piece a refresh strategy:

**Type A — Deep Refresh** (4-8 hours): Complete rewrite preserving URL equity
- When: >50% traffic decline, intent drift confirmed, competitor coverage gap >30%
- Includes: New research, updated stats, expanded structure, new CTAs, schema markup

**Type B — Surgical Update** (1-3 hours): Targeted section updates
- When: 20-50% traffic decline, specific sections outdated, quick competitive gap
- Includes: Updated statistics, new examples, refreshed intro/conclusion, internal link updates

**Type C — Structural Enhancement** (2-4 hours): Format and UX improvement
- When: Content still accurate but engagement metrics declining
- Includes: Add FAQ schema, table of contents, comparison tables, embed tools, improve media

**Type D — Consolidation** (3-6 hours): Merge cannibalizing content
- When: 2+ URLs competing for same keyword cluster, neither ranking well
- Includes: 301 redirect strategy, combined content, updated canonical signals

**Type E — Retire & Redirect** (<1 hour): Remove and redirect
- When: Zero traffic, no backlinks, superseded by better content, no recovery path

## Integration Tips

**Google Search Console + GA4:**
Export the "Pages" report from GSC filtered to 16-month date comparison. Export GA4 "Pages and Screens" with goal completions. Join on URL using Google Sheets VLOOKUP or a BigQuery query. Feed the combined CSV directly into the Quick Copy-Paste Version above.

**HubSpot:**
Use HubSpot's Content Analytics > Blog Performance report to extract traffic, CTA clicks, and contact attribution. Export as CSV and paste into the prompt. HubSpot's "Optimize" tab also flags declining posts — use those as your seed list.

**Ahrefs / SEMrush Automation:**
Set up weekly rank tracking exports for your top 50 content URLs. Create a Google Sheets automation that flags any URL with >10% traffic decline MoM or >5 position drop. This becomes your automatic refresh queue fed into the prompt monthly.

**Notion Integration:**
Ask the AI to format the output as a Notion-compatible table (pipe-delimited). Import directly using Notion's CSV import or paste into a Database. Build a Notion template with the refresh brief structure for your content team to execute against.

**Zapier Workflow:**
Trigger: Weekly GSC data export via Google Sheets → Filter: URLs with >15% traffic decline → Action: Create Asana/Notion task with AI-generated refresh brief → Assign to content team member based on workload.

**CMS Automation:**
For WordPress/Webflow/HubSpot CMS users: Add a "Last Substantive Update" custom field to all posts. Build a Zapier or Make.com workflow that queries posts where this date is >180 days old AND GSC traffic is declining. Auto-create refresh tickets in your project management tool.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: "I don't have 12 months of historical data — my content library is new."**
Solution: Shorten the analysis window to 3-6 months and focus on pages where growth has stalled (not necessarily declined). For pages under 6 months old, focus on pages that peaked immediately post-publish and then plateaued rather than continued growing. Use competitor traffic estimates from SEMrush/Ahrefs as a benchmark for recovery potential.

**Problem: "The AI recommends refreshing too many pages — I don't have the bandwidth."**
Solution: Add a budget constraint to the prompt: "I have 20 hours of content team time per month for refreshes. Prioritize only the pieces where the recovery potential justifies the investment. Ignore any page with fewer than 200 monthly sessions even at its peak." This forces the model to apply the CAVS scoring more aggressively and give you a tighter shortlist.

**Problem: "After refreshing, rankings still haven't recovered after 60 days."**
Solution: This usually indicates one of three issues: (1) Intent mismatch — the SERP has fundamentally shifted formats and your content type no longer matches what Google wants to show; rerun the analysis with explicit SERP format analysis. (2) Authority gap — competitors have significantly more backlinks; the refresh needs to be paired with a link-building campaign. (3) Cannibalization — another page on your site is competing for the same keyword; use the prompt with cannibalization analysis enabled and merge the competing pages.
