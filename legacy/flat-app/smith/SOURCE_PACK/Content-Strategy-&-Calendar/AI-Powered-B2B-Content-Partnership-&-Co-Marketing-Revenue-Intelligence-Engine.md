## Overview
This engine identifies, designs, and operationalizes strategic content partnerships — joint research reports, co-hosted webinars, co-authored guides, content syndication deals, and integrated campaign programs with complementary brands — that expand your reach to qualified audiences without increasing headcount or media spend. Use it when CAC is rising, content distribution has plateaued, or when you want to generate net-new pipeline by borrowing credibility and audience from non-competing B2B brands.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Partner agrees verbally but deal stalls at legal review for 6+ weeks — momentum is lost**
Solution: Front-load the legal conversation in week 1 by sharing a lightweight 1-page "Co-Marketing Agreement" template covering: content ownership (each company owns content on their own domain; joint assets are jointly owned), lead data handling (each company collects their own leads; no raw data sharing without dual-consent), brand usage rights (logo use limited to the specific co-marketing program), and exit clause (either party can withdraw with 30 days notice). Avoid multi-page MSAs for content-only programs — most legal teams will approve a 1-pager in 5-7 days vs. 6 weeks for a full vendor agreement.

**Problem: Co-authored research report produces low MQL conversion because content is too broad to drive product intent**
Solution: Add a "self-assessment" or "benchmark calculator" as a content upgrade gated within the report — e.g., "How does your T&E spend compare to peers? Enter 3 data points for your personalized benchmark." This converts passive readers into active leads with declared intent. The calculator data also segments leads by maturity (above/below benchmark) so sales can prioritize outreach. Aim for 15-25% of report downloaders completing the assessment.

**Problem: Partner's audience doesn't convert at the same rate as your owned-channel leads**
Solution: This is normal — partner audiences haven't yet developed trust with your brand. Implement a "warm introduction" nurture sequence (5-email track over 21 days) specifically for partner-sourced leads that leads with social proof from companies similar to the partner's customer base, emphasizes the shared integration story, and delays the hard product pitch until email 3. Partner-sourced leads typically need 2-3× the nurture touches of direct-acquired leads before they're sales-ready.
