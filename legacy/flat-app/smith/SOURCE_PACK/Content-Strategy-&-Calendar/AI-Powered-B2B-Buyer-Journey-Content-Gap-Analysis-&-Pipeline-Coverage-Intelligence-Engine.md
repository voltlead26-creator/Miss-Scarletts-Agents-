## ANALYSIS FRAMEWORK

### PART 1: BUYER JOURNEY CONTENT MATRIX

Build a complete content matrix using this buyer journey framework:

**STAGE 0 — PROBLEM UNAWARE (Dark Funnel)**
Goal: Create category demand before the buyer knows they need you
Questions: What trend/pain should trigger awareness?
Content needs: Thought leadership, original research, industry frameworks, executive POV
Distribution: LinkedIn organic, podcasts, press, speaking, dark social communities

**STAGE 1 — PROBLEM AWARE**
Goal: Capture attention of buyers who recognize the problem but haven't prioritized it
Questions: Is this problem worth solving? What's the cost of inaction?
Content needs: Educational blog posts, industry benchmarks, "State of X" reports, cold email hooks
Distribution: SEO, LinkedIn ads, outbound email, content syndication

**STAGE 2 — SOLUTION AWARE**
Goal: Establish your category and solution approach as the right methodology
Questions: How do others solve this? What are the options? What does good look like?
Content needs: Comparison guides, methodology whitepapers, case studies (outcome-focused), webinars, ROI frameworks
Distribution: Retargeting, email nurture, ABM content syndication, review sites

**STAGE 3 — PRODUCT AWARE**
Goal: Differentiate your specific product from alternatives
Questions: How does [Product] specifically solve my problem? Is it worth evaluating?
Content needs: Product demos, feature comparisons, integration guides, security/compliance docs, pricing context
Distribution: Website, demo landing pages, SDR sequences, LinkedIn retargeting

**STAGE 4 — TECHNICAL EVALUATION**
Goal: Eliminate technical objections and enable the champion to win internally
Questions: Will this work in our environment? Can we integrate it? What's the implementation like?
Content needs: Technical docs, sandbox environments, integration partner content, POC playbooks, security questionnaires, reference calls
Distribution: Sales enablement, dev portal, email sequences, community (Slack/Discord)

**STAGE 5 — BUSINESS EVALUATION / ECONOMIC BUYER**
Goal: Build the business case and justify the investment
Questions: What's the ROI? How does this compare to building vs. buying? Can we get board approval?
Content needs: ROI calculators, CFO-ready business cases, executive testimonials, analyst validation, total cost of ownership models
Distribution: Sales-delivered, champion enablement, exec briefing content

**STAGE 6 — DECISION / LEGAL / PROCUREMENT**
Goal: Compress legal/procurement cycle and remove last-mile friction
Questions: Is the contract fair? What's the vendor risk? Can we negotiate terms?
Content needs: Security/compliance certifications, DPA/MSA templates, vendor risk questionnaires, NDA-safe one-pagers, procurement playbooks
Distribution: Sales-delivered, legal team, InfoSec team

**STAGE 7 — POST-SALE / EXPANSION**
Goal: Drive adoption, prove value, and create upsell/cross-sell opportunities
Questions: Are we getting ROI? What features should we use next? Can we expand?
Content needs: Onboarding guides, feature adoption campaigns, QBR templates, expansion case studies, customer benchmarking
Distribution: CSM-delivered, in-app, email lifecycle, customer community

## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Create custom "Buyer Stage" properties on contacts; map content engagement (clicks, downloads, page views) to stage properties using workflows. Build a content-influence dashboard in HubSpot Reports showing pipeline attribution by content asset
- **Salesforce**: Add "Last Content Touchpoint" and "Content Stage" fields to Opportunity objects. Use Pardot or Marketing Cloud to track content engagement and push to Salesforce with a "Content Gap Fill Date" timestamp
- **Highspot / Seismic**: Upload priority content into the correct stage-mapped "Playbook" within your sales enablement platform. Set up usage analytics to track which AEs are using gap-filling content and in which deal stages
- **Google Looker Studio**: Build a Content Coverage Dashboard showing stage coverage scores over time, content publish date vs. conversion rate change, and deal velocity segmented by content engagement
- **Notion / Airtable**: Use this as a living content gap tracker — add columns for Status (Not Started / In Progress / Published), Owner, Publish Date, and 30-day performance metrics
- **Zapier**: Automate content gap reporting — trigger a Slack alert to the content team when a deal has spent 10+ days in a stage with a Critical gap score, signaling a content-first intervention may help

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The coverage scores feel subjective and I'm not sure where to start.
**Solution:** Anchor coverage scores to pipeline data, not content volume. A stage with 20 blog posts but a 15% conversion rate has low effective coverage. Start by pulling your CRM stage-conversion report and mapping the 3 worst-converting stages — those are your Critical gaps regardless of content volume.

**Problem:** Sales team isn't using the new gap-filling content I create.
**Solution:** The content wasn't created with sales workflow in mind. Run a 30-minute call with 3 AEs BEFORE creating Tier 1 content. Ask: "In the last 5 deals that stalled at [Stage 4], what question did the buyer ask that you didn't have a great answer for?" Build directly from those answers. Then load into Highspot/Seismic tagged by deal stage so it surfaces automatically in deal workflows.

**Problem:** Content gap analysis reveals 30+ gaps — the roadmap feels overwhelming.
**Solution:** Apply the "pipeline multiplier" filter: only prioritize content gaps in stages where you have active deals stalled right now. Open your CRM, find all deals that have been in the same stage for 2+ weeks, and identify which gap-filling content would immediately move those 10-20 deals. That's your Week 1 sprint list. Everything else is the 90-day backlog.
