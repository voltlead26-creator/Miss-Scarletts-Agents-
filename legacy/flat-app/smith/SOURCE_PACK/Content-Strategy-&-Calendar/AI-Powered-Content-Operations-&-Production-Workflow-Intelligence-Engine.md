## Integration Tips

- **HubSpot**: Connect UTM parameters from the Distribution Playbook to campaign reporting. Use smart content rules triggered by content type tags to personalize CTAs. Create a content-influenced pipeline report using the campaign attribution model.

- **Semrush / Ahrefs**: Pipe the Performance Intelligence Dashboard metrics into a weekly automated report. Connect keyword ranking data to the content calendar to auto-surface refresh candidates when pieces drop from page 1.
- **Google Sheets**: Export the 8-week calendar as a shared stakeholder view. Add conditional formatting to visualize pipeline by funnel stage and flag weeks where TOFU/MOFU/BOFU balance is off.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: Writers consistently miss quality gate scores on first submission (avg < 70)**
Solution: The brief is the problem, not the writer. Audit your Content Intelligence Briefs — if the "unique angle" section is vague or the competitor gap analysis is missing, writers default to generic structures. Run the Brief Generator prompt first, ensure the brief scores the piece's differentiation before any writing starts. Add a 15-minute brief review call between Content Manager and writer before drafting begins.

**Issue: Distribution playbook isn't being executed past Phase 1 (publish day)**
Solution: Automate Phase 2 and 3 triggers instead of relying on manual calendaring. Connect your CMS publish webhook to a Zapier sequence that schedules Phase 2 social posts automatically for Day 3. For Phase 3 Day 14 actions, create a Notion reminder database that fires when 14 days have elapsed since publish date. Remove human discretion from timing — only content selection should require judgment.

**Issue: The editorial calendar looks good on paper but falls apart in week 3 when unexpected priorities hit**
Solution: Reserve 20% of monthly capacity as "flex allocation" — don't schedule this in the calendar. When a sudden product announcement, customer win, or trending topic requires fast-turn content, you draw from flex capacity rather than bumping scheduled pieces. Additionally, build the calendar with explicit "sacrifice order" — rank every piece by strategic importance so if a piece must be cut, the team knows which one without debate.
