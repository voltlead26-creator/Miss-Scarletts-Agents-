## Overview
Generates a full competitor-displacement campaign system — identifying which competitor accounts to target, what intelligence to gather, which displacement messages to run, and what sequences to deploy — for any B2B company looking to convert competitor customers into their own pipeline. Use it when you have a clear competitive differentiation story and want to turn competitor dissatisfaction into qualified pipeline.

### 5. Objection Handling Playbook — Competitive Displacement Edition

**"We just renewed our contract"**
> Acknowledge: "Totally understand — renewal timing is the biggest factor in these decisions." Reframe: "The good news is that evaluation doesn't have to align with renewal. Most of our customers start the process 6–9 months early so the transition is zero-stress when renewal hits. Would it make sense to do a lightweight evaluation now so you're ready?" Bridge: "What's your current renewal date?"

**"Migration is too complex / too risky"**
> Acknowledge: "That's the #1 concern we hear — and it's legitimate." Reframe: "We've built a migration playbook specifically for teams coming from [Competitor]. [Company] completed their full migration in [X] days with [specific risk mitigation: zero downtime / parallel running / dedicated migration engineer]." Proof: "I can share the full playbook so you can stress-test it before we ever talk contract." CTA: "Would seeing a realistic migration timeline change how you're thinking about this?"

**"We've invested heavily in customizations"**
> Acknowledge: "Custom configurations represent real IP — I'd never ask you to throw that away." Reframe: "We have a configuration-import process for the most common [Competitor] setups. And our professional services team has done this migration [X] times." CTA: "Can you tell me what your top 3 customizations are? I can tell you in 5 minutes whether they transfer cleanly."

**"Our team is trained on [Competitor]"**
> Acknowledge: "Training investment is real — and I don't want to underestimate it." Reframe: "The question is whether [Competitor] is the best tool for the next 3 years, or whether your team has learned to work around its limitations. Our onboarding program gets teams productive in [X] days — we'll even run it alongside your existing setup." CTA: "How long did your team take to ramp on [Competitor]? We can likely beat that."

**"[Competitor] is cheaper"**
> Acknowledge: "On list price, that may be true." Reframe: "Total cost of ownership is where the math flips — most customers we work with spend [X] hours/week on manual workarounds that [Competitor] doesn't automate. At [average loaded cost per hour], that's $[X]/year in hidden cost." Proof: "I have an ROI model built for [ICP] companies — takes 10 minutes to run through it. Would that be useful?"

### 6. Win/Loss Intelligence Loop

**Post-Win Interview Framework (10 questions for AE to complete within 48 hours of close):**
1. What was the primary trigger that caused [prospect] to start evaluating alternatives to [Competitor]?
2. Which of our messages or proof points landed hardest in the sales process?
3. What objections came up that we almost lost the deal on? How did we overcome them?
4. Who was the internal champion, and what was their motivation?
5. What did the economic buyer care most about?
6. How long had they been on [Competitor], and what finally pushed them over the edge?
7. What did [Competitor] do (or not do) in the sales process to try to retain them?
8. What migration concern almost stopped the deal?
9. What could we add to our competitive deck that would have shortened the sales cycle?
10. Would this customer be willing to be a public reference for others leaving [Competitor]?

**Post-Loss Interview Framework (10 questions, complete within 48 hours of loss):**
1. What was the primary reason we lost? (Be specific — not just "price" but what price story didn't work)
2. Did the prospect choose to stay with [Competitor] or go with another competitor?
3. Which of our messages failed to resonate and why?
4. What did [Competitor] say or do that turned the deal in their favor?
5. Did we identify the right champion? Did they have enough internal power?
6. Was the economic buyer properly engaged? At what stage?
7. Was the loss about product, price, trust, timing, or relationships?
8. What would we need to change — product, pricing, process, or message — to win this category of deal next time?
9. Is this account a future re-engage (e.g., at renewal)? If so, what trigger would we watch for?
10. What competitor intelligence did we learn that should update our battle cards?

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Sequences get low reply rates despite strong messaging**
Fix: Competitor displacement outreach is highest-intent when timed to switching triggers. Without a trigger (renewal, price increase, outage, leadership change, negative review), displacement cold outreach performs like any cold sequence. Add an intent layer — use Bombora, G2 Buyer Intent, or LinkedIn Sales Insights to prioritize only accounts showing active competitor-evaluation signals. Sequence performance typically 2–3x when trigger-timed.

**Problem: Prospects say "we're happy with [Competitor]" and disengage**
Fix: Never challenge their satisfaction directly — it triggers defensiveness. Instead, shift to future-state questions: "Most of our customers said the same thing right up until [specific trigger]. What would have to be true 12 months from now for you to wish you'd explored alternatives sooner?" Then enroll in a long-cycle nurture that delivers [Competitor] benchmark reports and competitive news quarterly until a trigger event fires.

**Problem: Migration concerns kill deals that should close**
Fix: Migration anxiety is the #1 late-stage killer for displacement deals. Institutionalize a Migration Risk Assessment as a formal step in the sales process — have a solutions engineer map their current [Competitor] configuration in a 30-minute call and produce a migration complexity score (Low/Medium/High) with a written timeline. Presenting a concrete plan eliminates the unknown, which is what actually drives fear. Offer a migration SLA with penalty clauses if it matters to enterprise buyers.
