## Integration Tips
- **HubSpot / Salesforce:** Export a "Deals by original source" report filtered to the last 12 months closed-won. This provides revenue-by-channel, the single most important input to the allocation model. Paste it directly into the channel performance table in the Advanced Version.
- **Google Sheets:** Build a live budget tracker with one tab per channel. Paste monthly actuals into each tab and use IMPORTRANGE to pull spend vs. plan into a summary dashboard. Set conditional formatting to flag channels where CPL exceeds budget assumptions by more than 15%.
- **Looker / Tableau:** Connect your BI attribution model to the channel performance table. Use the AI allocation output as a hypothesis layer — compare AI recommendations against your attribution model's efficiency rankings to surface disagreements worth investigating.
- **Zip / Coupa / NetSuite:** Once allocations are finalized, create budget line items by channel in your procurement or finance system. Use the quarterly phasing plan as the purchase order release schedule.
- **Zapier / Make automation:** Set a monthly webhook: export HubSpot channel performance data, format as CSV, send to Claude API with this prompt, post updated efficiency rankings to a Slack #marketing-ops channel, and tag the CMO if any channel's CAC exceeds its budget-plan threshold by more than 20%.
- **Gartner Benchmarking:** Cross-reference your recommended allocation percentage against Gartner's annual CMO Spend Survey. For B2B tech at Series B stage, typical program budget runs 7-12% of revenue — use this as an anchor for CFO conversations about whether the ask is reasonable.

## Troubleshooting

**Issue: I don't have clean historical CAC data by channel — attribution is murky**
Fix: Use simplified inputs instead: provide total spend per channel and total leads/MQLs per channel for the past 12 months. Ask the model to calculate blended CAC and flag where attribution uncertainty is highest. Then request a sensitivity analysis: "If SEO's true CAC is 20% higher than estimated, how does that change the allocation?" This produces a range rather than a false-precision point estimate.

**Issue: The recommended allocation looks strategically correct but the CFO will reject it because it increases spend on brand and content with long payback cycles**
Fix: Add this instruction to the prompt: "For every channel with a CAC payback period exceeding 12 months, provide a bridge narrative — a specific explanation of how this investment reduces CAC in paid channels over an 18-month horizon." Brand investment lowers paid CPL over time; the model will quantify the expected efficiency gain to make the case defensible with a finance audience.

**Issue: Scenario outputs are too similar across conservative, base, and aggressive plans — not useful for decision-making**
Fix: Provide explicit constraints for each scenario: "Conservative = fund only channels with proven sub-12-month CAC payback. Aggressive = include two unproven channels at $50K test budget each and model an optimistic ramp curve." Forcing hard constraints per scenario creates meaningfully differentiated outcomes that give leadership a real choice.
