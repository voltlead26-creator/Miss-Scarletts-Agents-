## Integration Tips

**HubSpot:** Use HubSpot's Lists and Workflows to segment contacts by "Company Source" (Acquirer vs. Target) and trigger differentiated communication sequences. Merge duplicate contacts using HubSpot's deduplication tool before Day 1 emails.

**Salesforce:** Create a custom "Integration Cohort" field on the Account object to track which customers came from which company. Use this for separate pipeline reporting, cross-sell opportunity tagging, and health scoring.

**Notion:** Build the integration playbook as a shared Notion database with assignees, due dates, and status columns. Share with both marketing teams on Day 1 to create shared accountability.

**Slack:** Create a dedicated #marketing-integration channel with both teams joined on Day 1. Use a Slack workflow to post daily integration standup updates (connected to Notion database status).

**Google Analytics / GA4:** Create a new property or data stream for the combined web presence. Maintain historical data from both websites in separate views for at least 12 months to preserve attribution history.

**Zapier:** Automate the cross-sell lead routing: when a DataPulse customer opens 3+ emails about Nexus Analytics features → create a Salesforce opportunity → notify the account owner → enroll in high-intent cross-sell sequence.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Customers feel blindsided despite Day 1 communications**
*Fix:* Tier your outreach. Don't send a mass email and call it done. Your top 20% of accounts (by ARR) should receive a personal call from their CSM or executive sponsor BEFORE the public announcement. Build the call list in your CRM 5 days before close.

**Problem: Two marketing teams with incompatible brand voices and processes**
*Fix:* Within Week 1, run a joint brand voice workshop (2 hours, facilitated). Extract 10-15 actual content samples from each team and collaboratively rate them on a shared rubric. Create a unified style guide with examples from both companies' best work. Don't mandate — co-create.

**Problem: Martech migration stalls due to data quality issues in the acquired company's CRM**
*Fix:* Do not attempt a full data migration at Day 1. Instead: freeze the Target's CRM as read-only, build a lightweight integration layer (Zapier or native connector) for net-new leads, and run a 30-day data audit before any migration. Import only what you've validated.
