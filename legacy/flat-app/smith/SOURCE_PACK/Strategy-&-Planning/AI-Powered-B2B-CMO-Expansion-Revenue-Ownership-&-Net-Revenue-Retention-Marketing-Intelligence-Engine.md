### PART 1: EXPANSION REVENUE DIAGNOSTIC

**A. Expansion Revenue Opportunity Map**
Analyze the company's expansion opportunity across four dimensions:

1. **White Space Analysis:** For each customer segment (SMB / Mid-Market / Enterprise), calculate the theoretical expansion ceiling vs. current penetration rate. Identify the segments with the largest gap between current ACV and theoretical max ACV.

2. **Time-to-Expand Distribution:** Given the stated average time to first expansion, identify the "expansion-ready cohort" — customers who have been live for X months and have not yet expanded. Estimate the dollar value of this cohort.

3. **Product-Led Expansion Signals:** Identify which product usage behaviors correlate with expansion readiness (based on stated top expansion triggers). These become the marketing automation triggers.

4. **Expansion Velocity Comparison:** Compare current expansion ARR run rate to NRR target. Calculate the required monthly expansion ARR to hit the marketing-owned target, and compare to current monthly run rate — this is the "gap" marketing must close.

**B. Marketing's Expansion Lever Diagnosis**
Assess the current state and gap for each of marketing's four expansion levers:
- **Awareness Lever:** Do customers know about features/modules they haven't purchased? (Assess based on product newsletter engagement and usage data)
- **Education Lever:** Do customers understand how to drive ROI from unused capabilities? (Assess based on help center traffic, onboarding completion, training attendance)
- **Social Proof Lever:** Are customers seeing peer-validated evidence of expansion ROI from companies like theirs? (Assess case study coverage by segment and use case)
- **Trigger Lever:** Is marketing receiving and acting on expansion-ready signals from the product and CRM in real time? (Assess data flow between Gainsight/Mixpanel and Marketing automation)

Rate each lever on a 1-5 scale and prioritize the 2 with the largest gap.

### PART 2: EXPANSION REVENUE PROGRAM ARCHITECTURE

For each of the following six program types, build a complete program brief:

**Program 1: Expansion-Ready Account Nurture Sequence**
The highest-ROI expansion program marketing can own. Build the full architecture:
- Trigger: Specific product usage event or health score threshold that qualifies an account as "expansion-ready"
- Audience: Describe the target contact within the account (not the original buyer — often a power user or champion who can make the business case internally)
- Sequence: 5-touch email + LinkedIn sequence over 14 days
  - Touch 1 (Day 1 — Email): Subject line, value angle, specific CTA
  - Touch 2 (Day 3 — LinkedIn connection/message): Text of message
  - Touch 3 (Day 7 — Email): Subject line, social proof angle (peer company at same stage expanded and achieved X)
  - Touch 4 (Day 10 — Email): Subject line, ROI calculator or self-service expansion demo CTA
  - Touch 5 (Day 14 — Alert to CSM): Marketing auto-creates a Gainsight task for CSM to follow up, with all engagement data attached
- Attribution: How this sequence gets credited in the expansion pipeline report
- Expected conversion rate: Pipeline created per 100 accounts entered into sequence

**Program 2: Customer Advisory Board (CAB) as Expansion Pipeline**
CABs are the highest-engagement customer touchpoint — most companies run them for product feedback. Build a CAB-as-expansion-engine program:
- CAB structure for maximum expansion yield: Invitation criteria (revenue tier, expansion potential, not just "friendliest customers"), ideal size (12-18 members), meeting cadence (quarterly virtual + 1 annual in-person)
- Pre-CAB preparation: Executive sponsor assignment, account expansion audit before each CAB meeting, marketing materials to brief CAB members on expansion capabilities
- CAB session design: How to structure CAB agendas so expansion conversations happen naturally (not as a sales pitch)
- Post-CAB follow-up: Within 72 hours — specific actions that turn CAB engagement into expansion pipeline
- Metrics: Expansion ARR from CAB accounts vs. non-CAB accounts (expected 40-60% higher NRR)

**Program 3: Product-Led Content & In-App Expansion Campaigns**
Create a content and in-app messaging program that reaches customers at the moment of expansion readiness:
- Content types that drive expansion (not customer success content — expansion-specific):
  - "You've unlocked X — here's what customers do next" milestone email series
  - Module-specific ROI calculators embedded in product (not in marketing site)
  - Champion enablement kits: Content that helps a power user build the internal business case for expansion to their VP/CFO
  - Competitive comparison content for customers considering point solutions that compete with your expansion module
- In-app expansion triggers: At what usage milestones should in-app messages appear? What is the message, and what does it link to?
- Collaboration model with Product team: How does marketing access in-app messaging infrastructure without owning it?

**Program 4: Annual Customer Marketing Event (Expansion Pipeline Machine)**
Transform the user conference from a "nice to have" into a measurable expansion pipeline event:
- Pre-event: Account selection strategy (invite customers who are expansion-ready, not just happy — use Gainsight health scores + white space data to build the invite list); pre-event executive briefing to CSMs on expansion talking points per account
- Event design: How to structure sessions to naturally create expansion interest (not a product pitch — show customer-led expansion success stories by use case)
- During event: Marketing's role in capturing expansion intent signals (session attendance, booth visits, 1:1 meeting requests)
- Post-event: 48-hour post-event sequence to follow up on every captured expansion signal; pipeline attribution model
- ROI benchmark: Target expansion pipeline generated per $1 of event spend (industry benchmark: $3-5 pipeline per $1 spent for well-executed B2B user conferences)

**Program 5: Competitive Displacement Prevention (Anti-Churn Marketing)**
Expansion revenue is only additive if churn doesn't offset it. Build the marketing program to protect GRR:
- At-risk account identification: Which marketing signals predict churn before CS identifies it? (Declining email engagement, negative sentiment in support tickets, stopped attending webinars, champion left the company)
- Competitive alert program: When a competitor launches a new feature or price cut that targets your customer base, how does marketing respond within 48 hours? Build the communication sequence.
- Renewal marketing: 90 days before renewal, marketing triggers a "renewal readiness" content sequence — not sales pressure, but ROI reinforcement. Design the 3-touch sequence.
- Champion replacement program: When a champion leaves a customer account (job change detected via LinkedIn or intent data), what does marketing do in the first 7 days to re-anchor the account relationship?

**Program 6: Expansion Pipeline Reporting & Marketing Attribution Model**
The program that makes everything else credible with the CFO:
- Define "marketing-influenced expansion pipeline" — the specific criteria that qualifies a deal as having marketing attribution (e.g., "account received at least 2 marketing touches in the 90 days before expansion opportunity was created in Salesforce")
- Tier the attribution model: First-touch (marketing identified expansion opportunity before CS), co-touch (marketing and CS collaborated), assist (marketing provided content used in expansion conversation)
- Build the expansion dashboard: What metrics does marketing report weekly/monthly to the CEO? (Expansion-ready accounts in nurture, marketing-influenced pipeline created, expansion ARR closed with marketing attribution, NRR by marketing-touch vs. no-touch cohort)

## Integration Tips

- **Gainsight / Totango / ChurnZero:** Set up a custom marketing automation trigger in your CS platform that fires when health score crosses a threshold (e.g., ≥7 AND no expansion opportunity in Salesforce AND account age ≥6 months). Route these triggers to your MAP (HubSpot/Marketo) to auto-enroll into the expansion nurture sequence. Do NOT let CS manually select which accounts get marketed to — the trigger must be algorithmic to scale.
- **Salesforce CRM:** Add a custom picklist field "Marketing Expansion Source" to the Opportunity object with values: (1) Marketing Outbound — Triggered Sequence, (2) Marketing Outbound — CAB, (3) Marketing Outbound — Event, (4) Marketing Assist — Content, (5) None. Map this in your attribution dashboard. Without this field, the CFO cannot audit the attribution claim.
- **HubSpot / Marketo:** Build a dedicated "Customer Expansion" contact list that suppresses all acquisition nurture flows. Existing customers should NEVER receive new-logo demand gen content — this is the most common and most damaging mistake in customer marketing. Set this suppression list as a mandatory exclusion on every demand gen campaign workflow.
- **Pendo / WalkMe / Appcues:** Implement in-app expansion prompts triggered by product milestones. Marketing writes the copy; Product owns the deployment. Set up a shared Notion doc where Marketing can submit in-app message requests to Product, with a 2-week SLA for deployment. This is the highest-conversion channel in the expansion program — users seeing a message in-product at the moment of relevance convert 3-5x better than email.
- **Gong / Chorus:** Train Trackers on expansion-related language in CS calls ("we're not using that module," "we've outgrown the current plan," "could we add more seats"). Route Gong alerts to Marketing Slack channel — every time a customer says they've outgrown their plan in a CS call and Marketing isn't running a sequence to that account, that's a missed expansion opportunity. This closes the signal loop.
- **Notion / Google Slides:** Build the Marketing-CS Expansion Charter as a shared Notion page, not a slide deck — it needs to be a living document that both teams update quarterly. Version history in Notion creates accountability: when attribution disputes arise, the charter is the reference document.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** CS team refuses to accept Marketing's expansion attribution — "You sent two emails, we closed a $90K expansion deal after 8 months of relationship work. Don't take credit for that."
**Solution:** This is a design problem, not a metrics problem. The solution is a jointly-agreed attribution charter (Part 3B) signed off by CS leadership and CMO *before* the program launches, not after deals close. The charter should include: (1) clear definition of "marketing-influenced" that CS helped write, (2) a cap on what marketing claims (e.g., "marketing takes co-credit, not full credit, on any deal where a CSM was actively engaged"), (3) a monthly attribution review meeting where both teams look at the same data. Most attribution disputes happen because Marketing claimed credit unilaterally. If CS co-designs the attribution model, they defend it instead of attacking it.

**Problem:** The expansion-ready cohort is smaller than expected — too few accounts qualify after applying health score, tenure, and white space filters.
**Solution:** This is data quality revealing itself. Run the expansion prompt with this constraint explicitly stated. The AI will help you identify the most likely reason: either health scores are inaccurate (most common — Gainsight scores are often based on incomplete data), or the definition of "expansion-ready" is too conservative. Start by lowering the health score threshold from 7 to 6 and recount. If the cohort is still small, the real issue is that the company has fewer expansion-ready accounts than the board's revenue target assumes — this is a business problem to surface to the CEO now, not to paper over with aggressive marketing.

**Problem:** 90-day results are weaker than projected — the nurture sequence ran, but expansion pipeline created is well below target.
**Solution:** The most common causes are: (1) wrong contacts in the sequence — if you're emailing the original buyer instead of the current power user/champion, conversion will be near-zero; pull Salesforce contact records and confirm you're reaching the person who would actually drive an expansion conversation internally. (2) wrong trigger — the accounts you targeted aren't actually ready to expand, the health score was a false positive; interview 3-5 CSMs to calibrate what "expansion-ready" really looks like in practice. (3) wrong message — the content assumes awareness of the expansion product that customers don't have; add an "education first" touch before the conversion ask. Rerun the Advanced prompt with "diagnose why this expansion sequence underperformed" and the actual metrics data.
