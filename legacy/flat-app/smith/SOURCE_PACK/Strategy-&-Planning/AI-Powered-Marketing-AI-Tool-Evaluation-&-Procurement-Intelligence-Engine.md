## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Vendor refuses to answer data training policy questions or gives vague answers**
Solution: This is a red flag, not a negotiating tactic. Any reputable AI vendor should be able to answer "does our data train your model?" with a clear yes or no within 24 hours. If they can't, escalate to their legal team and request the answer in writing. If they still can't commit in writing, treat the answer as "yes, your data trains our model" and make your decision accordingly. Many CMOs skip this step and discover 18 months later that their proprietary customer data has been used to improve a tool that their competitors also use. Require the answer contractually in the DPA — vague verbal commitments are unenforceable.

**Problem: The ROI model looks great but the tool fails in the 30-day trial**
Solution: Most AI tool failures during pilots are caused by one of three things: (1) The use case was broader than scoped — the demo showed ideal conditions, but real workflow has edge cases the AI handles poorly; (2) Brand voice calibration takes longer than expected — plan for 4-6 weeks of output review and feedback before quality stabilizes; (3) Integration issues weren't surfaced in the demo — always test the actual integration with a live HubSpot/Salesforce sandbox during the pilot, not just in the vendor's demo environment. Build these three checks into your pilot success criteria explicitly. If the tool fails on any of these, use the pilot failure to negotiate a better price or exit rather than signing under pressure.

**Problem: The procurement process is taking too long and the team has already started using a free version or workaround**
Solution: Shadow usage of unapproved AI tools is a growing data governance risk — your team is already sharing company data with a vendor that has no DPA, no security review, and no contract. Address this immediately: (1) Formalize the existing usage into an expedited procurement review — compress the timeline to 5 business days for low-risk tools; (2) Build a self-service intake form that lets marketing team members request AI tool evaluations with the key information pre-collected; (3) Establish a pre-approved vendor list for common low-risk categories (AI writing assistants, image generators, social scheduling) so repeat requests don't require full due diligence. The goal is to make the compliant path faster than the shadow path.
