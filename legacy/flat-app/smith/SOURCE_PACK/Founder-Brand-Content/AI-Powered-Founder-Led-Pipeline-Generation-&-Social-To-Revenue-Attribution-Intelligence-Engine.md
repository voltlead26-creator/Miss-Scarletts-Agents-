## Success Metrics

- **Pipeline conversion rate:** % of engaged ICP prospects who convert to a booked meeting (target: 15–25% of Tier 1 prospects)
- **Content-sourced pipeline:** $ value of pipeline where initial engagement touchpoint was a founder LinkedIn post (track monthly)
- **Engagement-to-meeting time:** Average days from post engagement → meeting booked (benchmark: under 7 days for Tier 1)
- **ICP engagement rate:** % of post engagements from ICP-matching profiles vs. total (target: 20%+ ICP-matched)
- **Sequence response rate:** % of cold DMs receiving a reply within 14 days (benchmark: 30–45% for well-personalized founder outreach)
- **Revenue attribution:** Monthly and quarterly revenue where founder content was first-touch or multi-touch attributed
- **Content ROI per post:** (Pipeline created from post engagement) / (time invested in creating + engaging on post)

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Post engagement is high but ICP match rate is low (mostly peers, not buyers)**
Solution: Audit your content framing. Posts that lead with practitioner tactics ("here's how I built X") attract peers; posts that lead with business outcomes ("our clients reduced X by Y%") attract buyers. Shift the hook to the buyer's language: revenue, risk, cost, compliance. Also check your LinkedIn connection base — if it's mostly marketers and founders, your content distribution needs adjustment via hashtags, tagging customer logos (with permission), or engagement pods with ICP-adjacent accounts.

**Problem: DM response rates are below 15%**
Solution: Check three failure modes: (1) DMs are too long — cut to 100 words max for the first message. (2) The CTA is too high-commitment — replace "book a call" with "happy to share the template" or "curious what you're seeing." (3) The personalization isn't specific enough — if your DM could be sent to anyone who liked a post, it won't convert. Reference exactly what they said and connect it to a specific outcome you've achieved for similar companies.

**Problem: Difficulty attributing revenue to specific posts — sales cycle is 3-6 months**
Solution: Use first-touch attribution only for pipeline reporting (the post that triggered the first human interaction). Log this immediately when the DM is sent, not when the deal closes. For multi-touch, tag every founder content interaction in your CRM throughout the sales cycle. Use a 90-day rolling "content-influenced pipeline" metric rather than trying to attribute closed revenue directly to individual posts during long sales cycles.
