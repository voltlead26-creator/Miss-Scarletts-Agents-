## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Speaking pitches are getting ignored or rejected by conference organizers**
Solution: Conference committees reject pitches that read like product demos or abstract thought leadership with no proof. Fix three things: (1) Lead with data — your abstract should cite a specific, recent statistic that creates urgency around the topic. (2) Show the room what they'll leave with — the committee evaluates pitches based on attendee value, not speaker credentials. Make the deliverable concrete ("attendees leave with a board-ready risk scorecard template"). (3) Reference past proof — include one sentence about a previous talk with audience size and any measurable outcome (standing room only, 4.8/5 rating, 200 resource downloads). If you have none yet, speak at smaller events and meetups first to build a demo reel. You can also ask podcast hosts for a testimonial quote after appearing that you can include in conference pitches.

**Problem: Appearances generate LinkedIn engagement but not meetings or pipeline**
Solution: This is a CTA and follow-up failure, not an audience quality failure. Check: (1) Is your resource handout collecting contact information? A QR code to a gated one-pager converts 3-5× better than just a LinkedIn URL. (2) Are you actively DMing attendees who engage with your post-talk LinkedIn content? Engagement without outreach generates no pipeline. (3) Is your talk closing with a specific, low-friction next action? "Visit our website" generates 0 meetings. "Download this 5-minute risk scorecard and reply to me with your score" generates conversations. Retrofit your closing CTA to match this pattern.

**Problem: Difficulty managing speaking pipeline alongside running the company**
Solution: Systemize into a 3-hour/week operating cadence: Monday (30 min): Review speaking pipeline Notion board, approve any new pitches drafted by AI or a coordinator. Wednesday (60 min): Pre-appearance prep or post-appearance content activation. Friday (90 min): Record any podcast episodes scheduled for the week. The pitch research, email drafting, content repurposing, and CRM logging should be delegated to an EA or junior marketer using the templates from this engine. The founder's irreplaceable time should go only to the actual talk prep and delivery.
