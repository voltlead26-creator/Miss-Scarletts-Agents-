## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Posts consistently receive low impressions despite quality content**
Solution: LinkedIn's algorithm rewards early engagement velocity — specifically in the first 60-90 minutes after posting. Build a micro-engagement group of 8-12 trusted peers (not at your company) who agree to comment meaningfully within the first hour of each other's posts. Critically, reply to every comment within that same 90-minute window. Also audit your posting time: most B2B executives get maximum reach by posting Tuesday-Thursday between 7:30-9:00 AM in their ICP's primary time zone. Avoid posting after noon on any day.

**Problem: Growing follower count but no pipeline conversations materializing**
Solution: Your content is building brand awareness but your CTAs are too passive. Audit your last 20 posts — if your calls to action are "What do you think?" or "Drop a comment below," your conversion rate will be near zero. Replace with specific, low-friction, offer-based CTAs: "If you're a CHRO dealing with [specific scenario], DM me the word 'predict' and I'll send you the 3-question diagnostic we use with clients." Specific keyword triggers convert 4-6x better than open-ended invitations. Also implement the "soft pitch post" format: one post per week that tells a customer transformation story and ends with a direct but non-salesy invitation to connect.

**Problem: Can't maintain consistent posting cadence despite best intentions**
Solution: The root cause is always the same — writing posts the day they're supposed to go live. Implement the Monthly Batch Production system: dedicate 2 hours on the first Monday of each month to produce 16-20 complete post drafts using the AI drafting workflow. Use the 5-pillar architecture to batch by type (produce all 4 Pillar 1 posts for the month, then all Pillar 2 posts, etc.). Schedule everything in advance using Taplio or Buffer. Never write a post the day it goes live. This single change eliminates 90% of consistency failures.
