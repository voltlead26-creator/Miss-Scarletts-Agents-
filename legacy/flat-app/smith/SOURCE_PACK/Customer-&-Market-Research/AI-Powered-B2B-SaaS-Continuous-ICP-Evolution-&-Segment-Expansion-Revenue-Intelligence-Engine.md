## Overview

Use this when win rates are declining, sales cycles are lengthening, or you're approaching the ceiling of your current addressable universe.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: Win rates are declining but leadership insists the ICP is still correct.**
Solution: Separate the ICP question from the execution question by running a structured win/loss analysis (see Related Prompts). Present data showing whether loss reasons are primarily competitive (ICP may be right, positioning is off), capability-related (product gaps in current ICP), or fit-related (wrong target). Decision-makers are more receptive to ICP evolution when you show it's evidence-based, not a hunch.

**Problem: The validation sprint generates some pipeline but it's hard to tell if it's signal or noise with only 6-8 deals.**
Solution: Extend the sprint by 30 days with a clear commitment to a specific pipeline threshold. Use win rate as the primary signal, not total deal count — even 2-3 deals with 40%+ win rate is strong validation signal if they came from deliberate targeting. Cross-reference with qualitative signals: Are buyers in this segment using the same language to describe their problem? Are demos running shorter because there's higher pre-existing pain awareness? Pattern quality matters as much as quantity.

**Problem: The sales team starts pitching the new segment to every prospect because they're excited about the new story, diluting core ICP focus.**
Solution: Implement segment-gated territory rules immediately. New segment leads should be routed to a dedicated SDR/AE pod (even if it's just 1-2 reps). Create a separate pipeline in CRM with its own stage definitions and forecast categories. Compensation credit for new-segment deals should be tracked separately in the first 6 months to prevent misattribution and to protect core ICP reps' quotas. Weekly pipeline reviews should show segment breakdown explicitly.
