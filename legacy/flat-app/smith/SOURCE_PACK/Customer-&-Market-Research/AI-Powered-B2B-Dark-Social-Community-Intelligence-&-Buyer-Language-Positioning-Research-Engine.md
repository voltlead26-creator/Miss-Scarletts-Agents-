### PART 3: BUYER LANGUAGE EXTRACTION PROTOCOL

**AI Analysis Prompt for Raw Conversation Mining**

When you've collected raw community posts, comments, or threads, run them through this extraction protocol:

BUYER CONVERSATION ANALYSIS PROTOCOL

You are analyzing [X] buyer conversations from [community/platform] to extract positioning intelligence for [product category].

For each conversation batch, identify and categorize:

1. PAIN LANGUAGE INVENTORY
   - Exact phrases used to describe the core problem (quote verbatim, do not paraphrase)

   - Implicit criteria revealed by frustrations ("I hate when tools require [thing]")

   - Nice-to-haves vs. must-haves distinction

3. COMPETITOR SIGNAL EXTRACTION
   - For each competitor mentioned: sentiment (Positive/Neutral/Negative), specific feature or issue cited, whether the buyer would recommend, whether they switched from/to this competitor
   - Competitor weaknesses named by actual users (quote directly)
   - Competitor strengths acknowledged (even by switchers)

4. BUYING PROCESS INTELLIGENCE
   - Who else they're involving in the decision (names titles, not just "stakeholders")
   - What internal obstacles they're navigating ("my CFO keeps asking about...")
   - What proof or evidence they needed to get buy-in
   - Timeline pressure signals

5. LANGUAGE PATTERN ANALYSIS
   - Top 10 most frequently used words/phrases to describe the core problem
   - Power words that appear in high-engagement posts (posts with many upvotes/replies)
   - The metaphors or analogies buyers reach for
   - Industry jargon vs. plain language usage rate

OUTPUT FORMAT: Produce a structured intelligence brief with: (a) Top 5 positioning insights with supporting quotes, (b) 10 verbatim phrases to test in copy, (c) 3 competitive vulnerabilities to exploit, (d) 2 messaging hypotheses to test.

**Conversation Quality Scoring**

Before running extraction, score each conversation:
- ICP Match Score (0-3): Does the person match your ICP exactly (3), partially (2), or is it unclear (1) or a poor fit (0)?
- Authenticity Score (0-3): First-hand experience (3), referencing someone else's experience (2), speculative/hypothetical (1), marketing-influenced (0)
- Recency Score (0-3): Last 30 days (3), last 90 days (2), last 12 months (1), older than 12 months (0)

Only extract from conversations scoring 6+. Low-scoring conversations go to a "weak signal" archive for quarterly review.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem:** The communities you find are low-quality — full of vendors and consultants selling to your ICP, not actual buyers talking honestly.
**Solution:** Filter ruthlessly for first-hand experience. Look for posts that include specifics (vendor names, dollar figures, time periods, team sizes) — these are authentic. Ignore posts that are vague or read like marketing copy. The best communities are often not the biggest ones: a 2,000-member Slack community of operations leaders often produces better intelligence than a 500K Reddit community dominated by marketers and job seekers. Look for communities with high reply rates and multi-person conversations, not broadcast posts.

**Problem:** You're finding lots of competitor complaints but no clear signal on what YOUR company should say differently.
**Solution:** The competitor intelligence is only half the job. Run a specific "switch story" mining exercise: search for posts where buyers describe *switching away from a competitor* — the language they use to explain why they left tells you what they were looking for that wasn't delivered. That gap is your positioning opportunity. Also mine "what did you buy instead?" threads for the exact language buyers used to evaluate alternatives.

**Problem:** Your team runs the research once but doesn't sustain the weekly monitoring habit.
**Solution:** The bottleneck is almost always the "so what" step — raw data sits in a doc but nobody converts it to action. Fix this by (a) assigning one person as "intelligence owner" with protected time, (b) creating a pull mechanism instead of a push mechanism — instead of sending the weekly brief, hold a 20-minute "buyer intelligence standup" where the team reviews it together and immediately assigns 1-2 action items, and (c) making the impact visible by tracking which community-sourced phrases get used in actual campaigns and what happened to their performance metrics.
