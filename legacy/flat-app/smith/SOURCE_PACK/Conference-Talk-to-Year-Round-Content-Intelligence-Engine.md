# Conference Talk to Year-Round Content Intelligence Engine - Transform a Single Stage Appearance into 90 Days of Pipeline-Driving Content

**Difficulty:** Advanced | **Time:** 20 min | **Tags:** b2b, content-repurposing, thought-leadership, events, pipeline, social, email, automation, brand

## Overview
Takes a completed conference presentation — slides, transcript, or rough notes — and generates a complete, channel-specific content machine that runs for 90+ days. Use this immediately after any speaking engagement (external conference, industry summit, podcast appearance, or panel) to extract maximum brand, pipeline, and SEO value from work you've already done.

## Integration Tips

**Content Publishing:**
- **Notion/Coda:** Create a "Talk Content Hub" database with one row per piece, linked to the calendar. Use the 90-day calendar output as your source of truth.
- **Buffer / Hootsuite:** Load all social content in Week 1 of post-conference — scheduling ahead eliminates the drop in output that happens when post-conference energy fades.
- **HubSpot Campaigns:** Create a single campaign called "[Conference] [Year] Speaking Amplification" and tag all content, emails, and CTA links to it. This enables multi-touch influence reporting in HubSpot Revenue Attribution.

**Sales Enablement:**
- **Salesforce / HubSpot:** Upload the talk summary one-pager as a sales asset. Tag it with the conference, quarter, and speaker. Track opens via Highspot, Seismic, or HubSpot Content Library.
- **Gong / Outreach:** Build a sequence template for SDRs using the sales outreach email. Track reply rates to measure if talk-derived content outperforms generic outreach (it will).
- **Notion / Guru:** Add the one-pager and key stats to your sales wiki so reps can reference the talk when prospects bring up industry challenges.

**SEO and Analytics:**
- **Google Search Console:** Set a reminder to check branded search queries 30 and 60 days post-conference. Speaking at a major event often creates a spike in "speaker name + company" searches.
- **UTM Parameters:** Use a consistent UTM structure — utm_source=[conference-name], utm_medium=speaking, utm_campaign=[talk-title-slug] — across all links in talk-derived content.
- **Google Analytics 4:** Set up a "Speaking Content" content group to segment traffic from talk-derived blog posts and landing pages.

**Video Distribution:**
- **Descript / Opus Clip:** If you have a recording, use AI to auto-clip the best 60-90 second segments — then match them to the video scripts above for talking head context.
- **LinkedIn Video:** Upload short-form videos natively to LinkedIn (not YouTube links) — native video gets 5-7x more reach than external links.
- **YouTube:** Create a playlist called "[Conference] [Year] Talks" — upload the full recording as unlisted and link to it from the blog post for SEO + conversion.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: No transcript or recording available (common with in-person conferences)**
Solution: Use the Advanced Version with a detailed bullet-point outline instead of a transcript. Immediately after the talk, record a 10-minute "debrief" voice memo on your phone while the content is fresh — this becomes your transcript source. Otter.ai can transcribe it in minutes. For slides-only situations, use the slide deck + speaker notes as the primary input.

**Problem: The AI output sounds like a generic conference recap, not expert thought leadership**
Solution: In your prompt, explicitly instruct: "Do NOT write a recap of what was said. Write as if the speaker is sharing their most hard-won professional insight for the first time, not summarizing a presentation." Also include 3-5 direct quotes from the actual talk — verbatim language dramatically improves output authenticity.

**Problem: Content feels repetitive across the 90-day calendar (same insight repeated)**
Solution: In your initial prompt, explicitly map each content piece to a DIFFERENT sub-insight from the talk: "Piece 1 uses the thesis. Piece 2 uses the framework. Piece 3 uses the data. Piece 4 uses the contrarian claim. Piece 5 uses the Q&A insight." Assign one unique angle per piece before generation. If the talk has fewer than 5 distinct insights, you need to run the Advanced Version on a deeper framework expansion step first.
