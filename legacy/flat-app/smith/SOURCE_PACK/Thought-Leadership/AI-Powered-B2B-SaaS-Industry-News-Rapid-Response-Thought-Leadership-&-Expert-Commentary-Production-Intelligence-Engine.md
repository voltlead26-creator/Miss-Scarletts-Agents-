## Overview
Transforms breaking industry news — analyst reports, competitor IPOs, regulatory changes, major acquisitions, economic shifts — into expert commentary and credible thought leadership content within hours, not days. Use this when industry-defining news drops and your executives need to own the narrative before competitors do, establish category authority in real time, and capture the attention of in-market buyers who are actively researching the implications of that news.

## Troubleshooting

**Problem: The LinkedIn post gets great engagement but zero pipeline impact**
*Cause:* The angle resonates with peers (other founders/marketers) but not with actual buyers. *Fix:* Audit who is commenting — if it's mostly marketing professionals, the content is too "inside baseball." Rewrite Module 1C to explicitly pass the test: "Would a VP Engineering at a 500-person e-commerce company care about this take, or only marketing/startup Twitter?" Adjust the angle, not just the distribution.

**Problem: The executive doesn't feel comfortable publishing such a strong opinion quickly**
*Cause:* Missing executive buy-in process. *Fix:* Build a "pre-brief" step into the workflow: 20 minutes before the event, walk the executive through Module 1B and agree on the primary angle IN ADVANCE of writing the full content. Getting alignment on the POV first (rather than presenting finished content for approval) reduces revision cycles from 3 rounds to 0-1.

**Problem: Competitor publishes a nearly identical take within 1-2 hours**
*Cause:* The "non-obvious" angle was actually the second-most-obvious angle. *Fix:* When this happens, don't pull the content — instead, add a comment on their post that explicitly differentiates your perspective ("[Name] raises a fair point — here's where we see it differently based on our customer data..."). This converts a near-miss into a visible competitive debate that earns more attention than either standalone post would have.
